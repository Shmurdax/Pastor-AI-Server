KINGDOM TAXATION

All kingdoms incorporate a taxation system, which allows its citizens to
participate in the process of maintaining the kingdom infrastructure.
"Bring all the tithe into the storehouse that there may be resources
at my house."

Tithe = 10%

10% of what? Everything

Net vs. gross

In essence, everything in a kingdom already belongs to the king,
including the taxes required from the citizen, therefore taxation is
simply the government's allowing its resources to pass through the hands
of the citizen. 

The pastor or the church is not the ones who levy the Kingdom taxation
on the citizens of the Kingdom -- The King does! If one has an issue
with tithing, their issue is not with the pastor or the church, their
issue is with the King of the Kingdom!

The King ALWAYS reserves a portion for Himself. This has been true from
the beginning! Genesis 2:16-17 "Every tree in the garden is yours to
eat from, BUT don't touch the tree of knowledge of good and evil." He
is saying, "Do not eat of that tree, it is mine!" This is properly
termed the law of divine portion.

A good rule of thumb:

10% to the King

10% to savings

33% to Uncle Sam

47% to live on and support Kingdom Projects

God gives two kinds of seed:

Sowing seed

Eating seed

We should never be guilty of eating His portion because He always
reserves a portion for Himself -- Don't sow your eating seed, and don't
eat your sowing seed...

 8b-12).

19:29-30 (NKJV) "^ ^Everyone who has left houses or brothers or

No farmer sows seed unless and until he knows what kind of soil, he is
sowing it into! Giving is one thing, but to give to a King, is a
completely different level altogether! When we give we should give to
the King:

- Not the church...

- Not the ministry...

- Not the program...

- Not the person...

When we give to something, we become a part of it! When we give to a
King, we become a part of all that His Kingdom entails.

Giving to a King activates the King's obligation to demonstrate his
glory and power to the giver.

When one gives to a King, that King automatically attempts to
demonstrate to the giver that He is a greater King than all other Kings.

When a citizen gives to a King, that citizen is acknowledging that all
things belong to that King, and the citizen is grateful.

Something one is never to do, is to come before a King
empty-handed. Often, we have come before the King to get what we have
need of, and we do so empty-handed, offering noting of worth. This
should never be. We must NEVER come before our King with empty hands!

This is perfectly illustrated when the Queen of Sheba made an appearance
before Solomon. 1 Kings 10:10 (NKJV) "And she gave the king 120
talents of gold, large quantities of spices, and precious stones. Never
again were so many spices brought in as those the queen of Sheba gave to
King Solomon." We shouldn't just bring a gift to the King we should
bring the best and highest. (This is a real estate term)
