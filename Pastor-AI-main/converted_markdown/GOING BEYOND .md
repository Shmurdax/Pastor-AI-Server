THE POWER SOURCE

As many of you know, I have worked for many years in home building. My
father worked as a homebuilder to support our family while working as a
pastor and I followed in his footsteps, and over the years did the same.
I can assure you that much efficiency is to be gained when one can
employ electric or gas powered tools as opposed to hand tools:

- Have you ever tried to remove a tree limb with an axe versus a
 chainsaw?

- Have you ever tried to cut lumber with a handsaw versus a skill saw?

- Have you ever tried to sand wood with a piece of sandpaper versus as
 power sander?

- Have you ever tried to install drywall screws with a screwdriver
 versus a power drill?

I could provide example after example, but I think you get the picture.

When plugged into power, and with the right tool in hand to connect to
that power, we become exponentially more effective in completing the
task at hand. We must be properly empowered if we are to become
effective disciples for the Lord Jesus Christ.

A handsaw, though useful, is not configured to receive electrical power.
Until we are born-again and surrendered to the Lordship of Jesus Christ,
we are not configured to receive His power. Once we have been
born-again, we must get plugged into His "power that is at work in
us." \[Ephesians 3:20\] Zechariah 4:6 states so well, "It is not
by might nor by power, but by My Spirit,' Says the Lord of hosts."

THE POWER METHOD

We must ask ourselves the question "How did Jesus empower the twelve
disciples?" Mark 3:13-15 (NKJV) "And He went up on the mountain and
called to Him those He Himself wanted. And they came to Him. Then He
appointed twelve, that they might [be with Him]{.underline} and that He
might send them out to preach, and to [have power]{.underline} to heal
sicknesses and to cast out demons." Two things must be considered
here:

- To be empowered, they had to "Be with Him." If we are to be
 empowered we must: (1) Spend time with Jesus -- Have a personal
 devotional life. What time do you pray and read your Bible each day?
 If you could not answer that within five seconds you probably don't
 have one. We will never have power without spending time with Him. (2)
 We must spend time being in the presence of our leaders being mentored
 by them. When we submit to be mentored by leaders we are agreeing that
 they can say what they want to, when they want to, how they want to,
 and we give up the right to be offended. This year CT Church is going
 to offer us the opportunity to be empowered through training. Will we
 take advantage of the opportunity?

- To be empowered we must receive power that is not a part of our human
 strength. Notice, Jesus gave His disciples "Power to heal
 sicknesses and to cast out demons." How do we receive this power?
 Acts 1:8 (NKJV) "But you shall receive power when the Holy Spirit
 has come upon you; and you shall be witnesses to Me in Jerusalem, and
 in all Judea and Samaria, and to the end of the earth." When we
 accept Christ we are SEALED with the Holy Spirit; when we become
 baptized in the Holy Spirit we are FILLED with the Holy Ghost.

THE RESULT OF POWER

This power comes to us through the indwelling of the Holy Spirit. Acts
2:1-4 (NKJV) "When the Day of Pentecost had fully come, they were all
with one accord in one place. And suddenly there came a sound from
heaven, as of a rushing mighty wind, and it filled the whole house where
they were sitting. Then there appeared to them divided tongues, as of
fire, and one sat upon each of them. And they were all filled with the
Holy Spirit and began to speak with other tongues, as the Spirit gave
them utterance."

From Acts two forward, the book of Acts is a highlight reel of:

- The exploits of the Spirit... (Signs, miracles & wonders)

- The perseverance of the believers... (Before the crucifixion the
 disciples denied Jesus -- After Pentecost they endured hardship,
 persecution and death but NEVER denied Him)

- The expansion of the Gospel... (Acts 2 is the birthday of the church
 -- The first day 3,000 accepted Christ -- The soul winning enterprise
 of the First Century Church continued until the counting stopped and
 the Scripture say's "He added to the church daily, such as should
 be saved.")

Soul winning is the initial trait of a church empowered by the Spirit!
"When you receive the Holy Ghost -- You SHALL become witnesses."
Signs miracles and wonders are hallmarks of empowered churches and
empowered believers, but soul winning is the benchmark.

One of the definitions of serve on dictionary.com is: "To be of use."
If we serve others, we are in fact being of use to them.

INDIVIDUAL SERVICE

Just notice, the next time you drive down any street, the variety of
establishments you will pass by:

- Schools...

- Restaurants...

- Auto repair shops...

- Clothing stores...

- Hardware stores...

- Fire stations...

- Law offices...

- Construction crews...

All these are providing a service! If we think about it, serving is all
around us!

One of the greatest examples of corporate service is found in the First
Century Church when the office of Deacon was established.

We've discussed the past two Sundays the powerful reach and impact of
the ministry of Jesus' Twelve Disciples. Through His initiative and
their stewardship, the greatest enterprise in the history of mankind was
launched -- the Church! But even these great apostles could not
accomplish the task of spreading the gospel and ministering to the
people of God without a team around them to help meet needs so they
appointed Deacons. (Acts 6:1-7)

The word "deacon" means "servant." Notice the qualifications of the
deacon/servant:

- A person full of the Holy Spirit...

- A person filled with wisdom...

- A person of good reputation...

- A person whose attitude models the fruit of the Spirit...

Some would say, "I must wait until I am qualified to serve," but the
key is to start somewhere and work your way into the place your gifts
and anointing suit you to serve.

Are you ready to "Go Beyond" in serving in 2017? Are you sure?

THE POWER TO SERVE

Our Scripture text, reminds us that God acts through the power that is
at work in us!

Titus 2:14 (NKJV) reminds us that Jesus, "... gave Himself for us,
that He might redeem us from every lawless deed and purify for Himself
His own special people, zealous for good works." Notice this, Jesus:

- Redeemed us from every lawless deed... (I'm saved and I know that I
 am)

- Redeemed us to purity... (Sanctified us)

- Redeemed us to good works... (We are not saved by works - We will be
 judged for our works sake -- We are redeemed from a sinful state to
 our original destiny)

It is true that Jesus redeemed us to save us eternally but His
redemption is also intended to realign us with His initial purpose for
our lives.

His power is at work in us and this results in the zeal to serve. Babe
Ruth said, "It\'s hard to beat a person who never gives up."

The fullest expression of Christianity is in fact to serve God and
others with zeal. The power working in us drives us to serve Him and to
advance His kingdom interests with passion and fervency.

How can we say that we are redeemed by Jesus, the greatest servant in
human history, and are indwelt by His Holy Spirit whose power is at work
in us, and not ourselves be servants? Philippians 2:5-11 (NKJV) "Let
this mind be in you which was also in Christ Jesus, who, being in the
form of God, did not consider it robbery to be equal with God, but made
Himself of no reputation, taking the form of a bondservant, and coming
in the likeness of men. And being found in appearance as a man, He
humbled Himself and became obedient to the point of death, even the
death of the cross. Therefore, God also has highly exalted Him and given
Him the name which is above every name, that at the name of Jesus every
knee should bow, of those in heaven, and of those on earth, and of those
under the earth, and that every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ
is Lord, to the glory of God the Father." Jesus modeled servant-hood
in the following ways:

- He made Himself of no reputation... (He laid aside his Kingly robe to
 put on an earth suit)

- He took upon Himself the role of a servant... (He humbled Himself to
 become useful to others -- He washed the feet of the disciples)

- He took upon Himself human form... (Traded an eternal priesthood for a
 body subject to human maladies)

- He was obedient unto death, even the death of the cross... (In the
 face of the most horrible suffering He still continued forward in
 accomplishing the Father's plan -- Serving all of us)

The Scripture admonished us, "Let this mind be in you." "Let..."
means that this is something I must submit my will to, not what He is
going to make me do! Servant-hood is as much an attitude as an activity!

Are you ready to allow the Holy Spirit to empower you to serve in 2017?
Are you sure?

SERVING ON A TEAM

Today is Super bowl Sunday, which brings me to the idea of
team-servant-hood. Football is not an individual sport it is a team
sport:

- How much success do you think a quarterback would have if he had no
 offensive line?

- How many games do you think a high-powered offense would win without a
 defense?

- How many games do you think a stingy defense would win if they had no
 offense?

- What good would it be to have an offense and defense without a coach
 to put it all together?

Runners will gain no significant yardage if linemen do not block.
Quarterbacks will fail to complete passes if receivers drop the ball.

Who knows, the entire game's final outcome might rest upon the foot of a
kicker! The greatest kicker in the world cannot complete his task
without a proper snap, a proper hold and solid blocking up front.

I am going to tell you who will win the Super bowl today! The franchise
that plays with the best team effort will win!

The same is true of the church. It is through serving others, through
making ourselves useful to others, that we find group and individual
success and joy.

An anonymous author once said, "Teamwork divides the task and
multiplies the success." John Maxwell said, "Teamwork makes the dream
work."

Founding Father Benjamin Franklin has been quoted as saying concerning
the risks they took in revolting against England, "We must all hang
together or most assuredly we shall hang separately."

One unknown author said, "Teamwork is essential --- it allows you to
blame someone else." People who serve with a team mentality are quick
to accept blame and share the credit.

Think of the ingredients of a great recipe:

- Some are bitter...

- Some are sweet...

- Some smell good...

- Some smell bad...

- Some look delicious...

- Some look repulsive...

In the hands of a master chef, mixed together and exposed to the heat of
the stove or the oven, they become blended together and their
differences can become complimentary and produce something that sustains
life and brings pleasure. Great spaghetti has many ingredients and is
better as a leftover because its ingredients blend over time.

If we have the mind of Christ -- and be willing to serve and endure the
heat together, He will blend us together into a sweet smelling and
flavorful offering!

If we, as a team, will pull together toward a common goal of serving
others, Christ will coach us through to the championship!

If we are going to fulfill the will of the Father, we must make
ourselves useful to others.

Are you ready to "Go Beyond" in serving in 2017? Are you sure?
