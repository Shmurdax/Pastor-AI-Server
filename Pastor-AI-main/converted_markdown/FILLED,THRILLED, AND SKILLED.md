## I. RIGHT ANALOGY, WRONG COMPARISON .

separate occurrences namely being filled with the Spirit or being

filled with alcohol. He tells us that there are similarities yet they
are

not the same. The difference? One has excess, (asotia) one has

power.

Asotia is only used once in the bible and it\'s here. Not like the

meaning of the word EXCESS in Matthew 23:25 when Jesus

reprimanded the Pharisees who were full of excess which means a

"Want of self restraint."

Asotia means riot, profligacy (completely be given to the excess),

outrageous conduct. It basically means when a person gets sloppy

drunk, he loses all control not to mention his honor, character,

health, brain cells.

But nonetheless, when a person is FILLED that person has the

similarities of (Asotia) but no (Asotia). Drunk without excess.

Holy Spirit, he never said they were not drunk. He simply said they

... are not drunk "as you suppose." (ACTS 2:15) In other words they

weren\'t cussing, throwing up, and awaiting a migraine, but they

were drunk with joy, love, peace, and power. Let me explain.

## I. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE WE HAVE BEEN COMMANDED TO
DO SO!

No doubt, every believer would agree that to disobey one of the Ten
Commandments would be to sin. What I am about to say will more than
likely be viewed as a very narrow interpretation of Scripture. I believe
all disobedience is sin.

We are commanded in Scripture to be filled with the Spirit. Paul said to
the Ephesians "And be not drunk with wine, wherein is excess; but [be
filled with the Spirit]{.underline};" (Eph. 5:18)

Listen to what Jesus has to say on the subject! Luke 24:49 (NKJV)
"Behold, I send the Promise of My Father upon you; but [tarry in the
city of Jerusalem until you are endued with power from on
high]{.underline}." Jesus deemed the infilling of the Holy Spirit so
significant in the lives of the first century Apostles that he demanded
they place their lives and schedules on hold to join into a ten day
prayer meeting!

The people Jesus is speaking to here are busy people! They were not
unemployed and sitting around hoping someone would assign them a
responsibility for ten days and nights!

At the conclusion of this ten day prayer meeting they received the
infilling of the Holy Spirit! Acts 2:1-4 (NKJV) "And when the day of
Pentecost was fully come, they were all with one accord in one place. 2
And suddenly there came a sound from heaven as of a rushing mighty wind,
and it filled all the house where they were sitting. 3 And there
appeared unto them cloven tongues like as of fire, and it sat upon each
of them. 4 And [they were all filled with the Holy Ghost, and began to
speak with other tongues, as the Spirit gave them
utterance]{.underline}."

The reason Jesus commanded these busy people to spend ten days in a
prayer meeting is because he knew they were going to need the power the
Holy Spirit could furnish! He looked down the road ahead of them to a
day when they would need power for life and service!

The same thing applies to all of us twenty one centuries later! Someone
asked, "Must I be filled with the Holy Spirit to go to Heaven?" My
answer, "I would not want to go to the post office without the Holy
Spirit!"

God never commands us to do anything simply for the spiritual exercise!
When He gives us a command there are always a host of reasons behind His
commands!

We should receive the Holy Spirit because we have been commanded to do
so!

## II. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE HAS BEEN APPOINTED AS
OUR TEACHER!

In our lifetime all of us have had many teachers. Very few of us recall
specific profound lessons our teachers taught us but we do remember the
lessons they taught us through the relationships they built with us!

An evangelist friend and I graduated from the same High School. A few
years ago I was serving as the District Christian Education Director for
the Assemblies of God in Arkansas. One day this evangelist friend and I
were passing through the town where we graduated, so we decided to stop
in for a visit and talk to a few teachers we had not seen since High
School. As we were visiting with our former English teacher she asked
what we were doing for a living, when we told her she hesitated for a
minute and said, "That is a little frightening!"

Teachers oftentimes become mentors to their students! Jesus tells us
that by design, the Holy Spirit is to become the instructor of each
believer! John 14:26 (NKJV) "But the Helper, the Holy Spirit, whom the
Father will send in My name, [He will teach you all things]{.underline},
and bring to your remembrance all things that I said to you."

When we receive the infilling of the Holy Spirit we receive a heightened
awareness of the will of God, as well as a heightened sensitivity to the
voice of the Holy Spirit.

When a person has received the infilling of the Holy Spirit, this
teacher opens the Word of God as never before! The truths of God\'s Word
seem to come alive to the believer!

Because this teacher lives within us, He is able to bring back to our
remembrance the Word of God at a moments notice! This teacher speaks to
us from within, when we are doing right, and when we are doing wrong!
It's like having an English teacher whispering in your ear 24/7.

The closer the relationship we develop with the teacher, the more we are
capable of learning! It is not the responsibility of the mentor to make
time for the mentored; it is the responsibility of the mentored to carve
out time to spend at the feet of the mentor! The more shallow the
relationships with the teacher, the less we are capable of learning! We
are as close to Him as we want to be!

We should receive the Holy Spirit to receive Godly instruction!

## III. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE HAS BEEN APPOINTED
AS OUR GUIDE!

A guide is defined as one who knows the way, usually because they have
already been there. If I am going to follow a guide, I want to know that
they know what they are doing and where they are going! (My experience
in a helicopter at Mt. Rushmore)

There is a terrific difference between a pioneer and a guide. A pioneer
is blazing a trail in hopes of developing a path for others to follow. A
guide is one who already knows the way and leads those who are less
knowledgeable along the path.

John 16:12-15 (NKJV) "I still have many things to say to you, but you
cannot bear them now.13 "However, when He, the Spirit of truth, has

The work of the Holy Spirit in the life of the believer is that of
revelation. He has no agenda of His own; He is simply a guide who
discovers what the Father desires in our lives and reveals the path of
travel to each believer.

A portion of the work of the Holy Spirit consists of revealing the will
of God for ministry to each believer! Acts 13:2-4 (NKJV) "As they
ministered to the Lord and fasted, [the Holy Spirit said]{.underline},

which He does; He then reveals it to the Holy Spirit; who in turn
reveals it to the believer.

The Holy Spirit is our guide we must follow His instructions implicitly!

We should receive the Holy Spirit because He can reveal the path of
life!

- Doing the work of Christ...

- Walking the walk of Christ...

The three things every believer must do in order to please God is to:
(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Have the mind of Christ!

- Do the work of Christ!

- Walk the walk of Christ!

letter but of the Spirit]{.underline}; for the letter kills, but the
Spirit gives life."

If Jesus and Paul needed the empowerment of the Holy Spirit, we will
surely need His help!

We should receive the Holy Spirit to be empowered for service!

## V. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE WILL PRAY THROUGH US
WHEN WE DO NOT KNOW WHAT TO PRAY FOR!

As we have already learned in this series on "Practical
Christianity" the prayer life of each believer is vital to his or her
survival and success as a Christian.

One of the works of the Holy Spirit in the life of the believer is to
enhance and make more effective his or her prayer life. Romans 8:26-27
(NKJV) "Likewise the Spirit also helps in our weaknesses. For we do
not know what we should pray for as we ought, but the Spirit Himself
makes intercession for us [with groanings which cannot be
uttered]{.underline}.27 Now He who searches the hearts knows what the
mind of the Spirit is, because [He makes intercession for the saints
according to the will of God]{.underline}."

Think of this: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- When we do not know what to pray for, the Holy Spirit knows exactly
 what to pray for!

- When we do not know how to pray because we do not know what the will
 of the Father is, the Holy Spirit is never in question about the will
 of God!

- When we have exhausted our vocabulary, the Spirit has not exhausted
 His!

The Holy Spirit anoints us to pray in two very distinct ways: 1
Corinthians 14:15 (NKJV) "I will [pray with the spirit]{.underline},
and I will also [pray with the understanding]{.underline}. I will sing
with the spirit, and I will also sing with the understanding." (PLACE
ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- In the Spirit - In Tongues!

- In the Spirit - In our native language! (Being asked to pray in the
 Arkansas Governors mansion) (Praying over a house where some strange
 activity had been going on)

I am sure all of us have felt at times that our prayer life was somewhat
deficient and inadequate. The Holy Spirit has been appointed to be our
helper in prayer. What we lack, He makes up for. The most effective
praying is Holy Spirit anointed praying!

We should receive the Holy Spirit and allow Him to pray through us!

## VI. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE IS TO BE OUR
COMFORTER!

John 15:26 (KJV) "But when the [Comforter]{.underline} is come, whom
I will send unto you from the Father, even the Spirit of truth, which
proceedeth from the Father, he shall testify of me:"

On numerous ocassions in the Bible, the Holy Spirit is referred to as
the comforter. Because of the times we are living in, when we use the
word comforter, all kinds of images begin to manifest themselves, most
of which do not lend themselves to a true assessment of what is being
portrayed in Scripture.

The image we come up with is probably a person who is weak and frail but
willing to sympathize with our every need and concern. We might even
visualize a weak frail person who is content to hang around a funeral
home and cry with everyone who comes through the door.

When the Scripture uses the word comforter there is an attempt to say
something far more significant.

The Greek word for Comforter is Parakletos which means: (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- One called alongside for the purpose of assistance (Like a big
 brother)

- One who pleads another\'s cause before a judge (He is our legal
 representation)

- A counselor for the purpose of a defense (He is our defense lawyer
 when we stand accused) (Talk about pre-paid legal fees)

- A legal assistant (He researches all similar cases and furnishes the
 information to the attorney)

- An intercessor (He is our most able advocate at Heavens court)

- A helper (Someone to assist in all situations)

Do you get the picture? The comforter is a constant abiding presence
which is capable of assisting the believer in all situations!

Life can be extremely difficult not to mention complicated! All of us
experience times in life when we need the help of others. Though some
people attempt to isolate themselves, we are not engineered to deal with
life\'s problems on our own! God said of Adam, Genesis 2:18, 21-23
(NKJV) "And the [Lord]{.smallcaps} God said, "[It is not good
that man should be alone]{.underline}; I will make him [a
helper]{.underline} comparable to him."21 And the [Lord]{.smallcaps} God
caused a deep sleep to fall on Adam, and he slept; and He took one of
his ribs, and closed up the flesh in its place.22 Then the rib which the
[Lord]{.smallcaps} God had taken from man [He made into a
woman]{.underline}, and He brought her to the man.23 And Adam said:
"This is now bone of my bones And flesh of my flesh; She shall be
called Woman, Because she was taken out of Man."

It is a wonderful thing to have family or friends who are closely enough
connected that we feel free to enlist their help in difficult times.

To walk through an extremely difficult time in life is bad enough, but
to walk it out alone is even worse.

Why would anyone resist such help?

We should receive the Holy Spirit to access maximum assistance from the
Heavenly Father!

## VII. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE CONVICTS US OF
SIN!

Most Christians would agree that a person cannot come to Christ apart
from the conviction of the Holy Spirit. John 6:44 (NKJV) "No one can
come to Me unless [the Father who sent Me draws him]{.underline}..."
How does the Father draw us? John 16:5-8 (LB) "But now I am going away
to the one who sent me; and none of you seems interested in the purpose
of my going; none wonders why. 6 Instead you are only filled with
sorrow. 7 But the fact of the matter is that it is best for you that I
go away, for if I don't, the Comforter won't come. If I do, he
will---for I will send him to you. 8 "And when he has come he will
[convince the world of its sin]{.underline}, and of the availability of
God's goodness, and of deliverance from judgment." A great portion of
the work of the Holy Spirit is to make the unrepentant sinner fully
aware of his hopeless eternal condition.

Another aspect of the work of the Holy Spirit is to make Christians
aware of their failures and sins! That's right, when we who are
believers, fail, the Holy Spirit becomes the still small voice inside us
which causes us to feel guilty concerning our sin. His purpose is not to
make us feel like a failure but to bring us to repentance.

Listen to the importance of walking in the Spirit of God each day of our
lives. Galatians 5:16 (NKJV) "I say then: Walk in the Spirit, and
[you shall not fulfill the lust of the flesh]{.underline}." Galatians
5:24-25 (NKJV) "And [those who are Christ's have crucified the
flesh with its passions and desires]{.underline}.25 If we live in the
Spirit, let us also walk in the Spirit."

If it is true that a portion of the work of the Holy Spirit is to help
us walk out our life in Christ, why would we not want as much of the
influence of the Spirit in our lives as possible?

Sometimes what the Word does not say is almost as important as what it
does say. If it say\'s that "If we walk in the Spirit we will not
fulfill the lusts of the flesh", then it also infers that "If we do
not walk in the Spirit we will fulfill the lusts of the flesh."

Those who have a passion to walk as close to Jesus as possible will
eventually find the path to Holy Spirit baptism. Once they step into
this new dimension many things will change in their lives but one of the
most significant changes will be the heightened awareness, of what
pleases God and what does not.

We should receive the Holy Spirit to allow Him to convict us of our sin!
