Day 101 April 11

David's Mighty Men

📖

David had developed a group of 30 men that were the core of his fighting
force. Joab's brother, Abishai, led the group because of his bravery.
All 30 of these fighting men were proven warriors with skill and
courage. But the mightiest of them were an inspiration to the entire
army---they were called "The Three."

Adino -- A man who was outnumbered in battle 800 to one.

Eleazar -- A man with a sword

Shammah -- He stood when others fled

•

Eleazar stood firm when the rest of the army retreated. While they fell
back, he turned and attacked the Philistines by himself. He fought so
hard and so long that his hand stuck to his sword. The others finally
came back, but only to plunder the dead bodies around him. 2nd Samuel
23:9 describes Eleazar like this: "And after him was Eleazar the son
of Dodo the Ahohite, one of the three mighty men with David when they
defied the Philistines that were there gathered together to battle, and
the men of Israel were gone away: He arose, and smote the Philistines
until his hand was weary, and HIS HAND CLAVE UNTO THE SWORD: and the
LORD wrought a great victory that day; and the people returned after him
only to spoil."

"His hand clave unto the sword" means... "He was one with his
sword" Are we one with our sword? The greatest weapon we have is the
Book -- The Bible!!! Psalm 149:6 "Let the high praises of God be in
their throats and a two-edged sword in their hands." WE SHOULD use
God's Word to remind the DEVIL of the CROSS and his DEFEAT at Calvary!
WE SHOULD:

Know the word

Believe the word

Obey the word

Love the word

Memorize the word

Incorporate the Word in our prayers

Declare the Word

Speak the Word

Or like Eleazar, become one with the Word!

As we do, we will find that He always honor what He says.

Day 102 April 12

Amnon & Tamar

📖

David was an awesome worshipper -- He was the second King of Israel --
He was a giant killer -- He was a great military conqueror -- His
parenting skills were very weak.

•

David had a least eight wives plus "more" and some 20 children
plus "other sons." There was no way he could emotionally supply
what they all independently needed. Polygamy was not God's perfect plan.
David was soft and indulgent as a parent and thus the inner character of
David's life failed to be passed onto his children.

When his eldest son Amnon raped his beautiful half-sister Tamar (2
Sam ), her brother, Absalom, was outraged, and the hatred for his
half-brother turned to a burning desire for revenge, and eventually to
murder. David became very angry with Amnon for raping his half-sister (2
Samuel 13:21), yet surprisingly, failed to take any action against
Amnon.

Tamar means palm tree, signifying fruitfulness.

Absalom means his father's peace.

Amnon means faithful or stable.

Because of David's failure to rule his own house well, none of them
lived up to their names.

•

Dr. Wade Horn, who served as president of the National Fatherhood
Initiative, made this very gripping statement, "If America continues on
its present course, it will be known as the nation of the founding
fathers, with no fathers to be found."

•

In Ephesians 6:4, Paul instructs fathers to not "Provoke their
children to wrath, but to bring them up in the discipline and
instruction of the Lord." The Greek word for father used in this
verse is "pater". It literally means father, not parents. Patḗr (Greek
#:3962) -- "Father; one who imparts life and is committed to it; a
progenitor, bringing into being to pass on the potential for likeness."
Ted Tripp said, "A child's heart is the world's smallest battleground,
and the conquering of it calls out for all-out, hand-to-hand combat
against the enemy."

Day 103 April 13

📖

1 Samuel 16:5-13 "As David and his men went along the road, Shimei
went along the hillside opposite him and cursed as he went, threw stones
at him and kicked up the dust." In this passage of Scripture King
David has been forced to temporarily abandon the throne. He, his family,
and his servants were fleeing Jerusalem to live in exile. The reason
they were going into exile is tragic. David's son Absalom had conspired
against his father, gathered to himself a group of malcontents, and was
bent on overthrowing his own father, hijacking his Father's kingdom and
assuming the role of King. David fears for his safety, that of his
family, friends, and servants. He knows that at this point it is best to
flee, and fight later! On his way out of the city David encounters a guy
named Shimei. Shimei, thinking that David\'s days as king were over,
vented his rage toward David. David could have killed this guy, or had
him killed. In fact, one of his faithful servants, Abishai, volunteered
to do it for him. (V9)

Yet, David allows Shimei to live. For every Shimei in your life, God
will give you an Abishai. Shimei is the original dust devil.

A dust devil forms when hot air near the surface rises quickly through a
small pocket of cooler, low- pressure air above it. If conditions are
just right, the air may begin to rotate. The good news is that most dust
devils are very small and weak, and they often dissipate less than a
minute after forming. Remember, all it takes to settle a little dust is
some rain!!! Zechariah 10:1 "Ask of the Lord rain in the time of the
latter or spring rain. It is the Lord Who makes lightnings which usher
in the rain and give men showers, and grass to everyone in the
field." The enemy might be kicking up dust, throwing stones and
shouting insults at you. You may have a dust devil that's shown up in
your finances, your relationships, your professions and your
confessions. You may be spiritually dehydrated right now. You just need
to know this one thing - the drought is about to be over!!! Jeremiah
31:25 "For I will fully satisfy the weary soul, and I will replenish
every languishing and sorrowful person." Start waving bye-bye to
Shimei! Your drought is over. Bye, bye Shimei!
