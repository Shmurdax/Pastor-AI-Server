INTRO: Immediately preceding the scriptures in our text we find
Judas, the treasurer, and disciple of Christ, betraying Jesus into the
hands of the chief priests.

Judas sold Jesus to the chief priests for \$19.20, and I must ask you,
what will you sell him for?

Jesus has his last supper with his disciples. It is at this supper that
Jesus reveals Judas as a traitor. Jesus makes his disciples aware of the
fact that they also will deny him. Notice what Peter says in verse 35
"Though I should die with thee, yet will I not deny thee."

Verse 36 says, "Then Jesus came with them to a place called
Gethsemane." The meaning of the word Gethsemane is "Oil press." The
very meaning of the word Gethsemane, suggests a place of intense
struggle. If Gethsemane is a place of intense struggle, what benefit
could there possibly be in a place like that?

Three benefits of Gethsemane which we will look at today: (PLACE ON

- Gethsemane is a place of choice....

- Gethsemane is a place of intense struggle...

- Gethsemane is a place of great victory...

## I. GETHSEMANE IS A PLACE OF CHOICE... It was at Gethsemane that
Jesus had to make a decision, to take the easy road or submit His will
to the will of the Father. Even though Jesus was the Son of God, He had
to bring His human will under subjection to the will of the Father, just
like all other sons of God!

When teaching his disciples to pray, Jesus said, "Thy kingdom come,
thy will be done in earth as it is in Heaven." When He comes to the
place called Gethsemane he is given the opportunity for practical
application! We all have opportunities for practical application of
submitting our will to the will of the Father! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN
UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) This is not easy but it is necessary!

I am sure that if it had been possible for sin to have been forgiven
another way, other than Jesus giving his life, the human part of Jesus
would have preferred it. But it was not possible so Jesus had to come to
the place of allowing the will of the Father to be the authority.
Hebrews 4:14-15 (NKJV) "Seeing then that we have a great High Priest
who has passed through the heavens, Jesus the Son of God, let us hold
fast our confession. For we do not have a High Priest who cannot
sympathize with our weaknesses, but was [in all points tempted as we
are, yet without sin]{.underline}."

For those of you who do not know Christ, this service has become a
Gethsemane for you. You are going to be called upon to make a decision
about your eternal destiny! Will you make a good choice or a bad choice?
(LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

Today is the day of salvation, now is the time your decision must be
made. What will it be? You have reached Gethsemane!

In making your choice at Gethsemane, if you choose to make Jesus your
Lord, there is something you must consider that Jesus said about your
decision. Matthew 16:24 (NKJV) "If anyone desires to come after Me,
let him [deny himself]{.underline}, and [take up his cross]{.underline},
and [follow Me]{.underline}."

- Deny, according to Webster, is "To refuse, to give in." Deny in the
 Greek means "To disown or abstain."

- Cross in the Greek means "A pole as an instrument of capital
 punishment."

- Follow in the Greek means "Exactly, circumspectly, diligently, or
 perfectly."

The Don Nordin translation is: "If any man will come after me, let
him refuse to give in to the flesh, let him disown himself, let him
crucify the flesh and let him walk circumspectly or perfectly after
me." (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

It is certainly possible, but not always easy to do what Jesus is
suggesting here. But when you fall in love with Jesus, your desire will
be to please him! It's not nearly as difficult to live for God, when you
love Him and have a made up mind to serve Him!

Every person in the Kingdom of God has been in the place you are walking
through today, a place of decision, please make a good choice!

## II. GETHSEMANE IS A PLACE OF INTENSE STRUGGLE... Jesus himself found
Gethsemane to be a place of intense struggle. Luke 22:44 (NKJV)
"Being in agony, He prayed more earnestly. Then [His sweat became
like great drops of blood]{.underline} falling down to the ground."
The very meaning of the word "Gethsemane" depicts a struggle; the
meaning is "oil press." (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

The world is reeling from an intense struggle as we sit in this house of
worship this morning. As Christians, we are in warfare as well.
Ephesians 6:12 (NKJV) "For we do not wrestle against flesh and blood,
but against [principalities]{.underline}, against [powers]{.underline},
against the [rulers of the darkness of this age]{.underline}, against
[spiritual hosts of wickedness in the heavenly places]{.underline}."
A closer study of this verse reveals the writer is speaking of four
classifications of demonic forces which are at work in the world today.
We are in the struggle of our life, this is not a game! This is war!

On that night in Gethsemane, if the disciples could have peered into the
spiritual world, they would not have gone to sleep; they would have been
frightened out of their minds! Our lord was wrestling against all kinds
of evil forces! He was in the true sense of the word, having an intense
struggle!

In our Christian walk we will encounter times when we will walk through
Gethsemane, that place of intense struggle! We have all been there when
the light at the end of the tunnel turned out to be an oncoming train!

David described his Gethsemane this way, Psalm 23:4 (NKJV) "Yea,
though I walk through [the valley of the shadow of death]{.underline}, I
will fear no evil; For You are with me." We should always remember
that when we are walking through a personal Gethsemane, the Lord is with
us! David continues on. Psalms 23:5 (KJV) "Thou [prepares a table
before me in the presence of mine enemies]{.underline}: Thou [anointest
my head with oi]{.underline}l; my [cup runneth over.]{.underline}"

Remember God can turn our wilderness into a place where water is
standing in pools! (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) Don't fret
because of the struggle at Gethsemane; God is working on our behalf! 1
John 4:4 (KJV) "Ye are of God, little children, and have overcome
them: because [greater is he that is in you, than he that is in the
world]{.underline}."

lives, we can rest assured there is a struggle ahead. It will not always

## III. GETHSEMANE IS A PLACE OF GREAT VICTORY... At Gethsemane Jesus
won the victory over the flesh, so that he could submit to the agony of
the cross and severe torture at the hands of cruel men! When Jesus was
on the cross, all He had to do was to say the word and God would have
sent angels to set Him free!

Jesus knew what He would face on crucifixion day so He chose to confront
the flesh ahead of time. It was at Gethsemane, even before the real
battle was fought, that the victory was won. This is the reason he was
able to endure the things that befell him.

So it is with us many times, we have to make a choice, struggle with the
flesh; get the victory over these things; then fight the battle which
has already been won. Yes, the battle against evil has already been won
for us, all we have to do is decide to follow Jesus, (LEAVE ON THE
SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) overcome the flesh and we are winners, not
losers-Winners!
