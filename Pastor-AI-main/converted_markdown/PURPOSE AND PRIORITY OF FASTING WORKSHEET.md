## III. WHEN TO FAST...

The [timing]{.underline} of a fast is probably as crucial as the
[decision]{.underline} to fast. James 4:5-10

The most effective [method]{.underline} of crucifying the flesh is
through fasting. Galatians 5:16-26

We should fast:

- When God lays a fast on our [hearts]{.underline}...

- When our prayer life needs to be [strengthened]{.underline}...

- When we need to prepare ourselves to receive God\'s
 [message]{.underline}... Luke 4:13

- When we become [cold]{.underline} in our spirit...

- When we are experiencing [crisis]{.underline} in our life...

GUIDELINES FOR AN EXTENDED FAST

Before beginning a fast of more than three days:

- Receive divine direction - Don\'t copy others; be sure you are led of
 the Lord as to what type of fast you are to do and how long it should
 last.

- No caffeine -- Discontinue the use of caffeine a few days before the
 fast.

- Restrict your food intake several days before the fast - Eat fruit as
 a last meal before the fast.

During the fast:

- Dress warmly - You can expect to feel colder during a fast, especially
 in your hands and feet.

- Use warm, not hot, water for showers and baths.

- Avoid ice cold beverages.

- Give extra time to prayer and Bible reading.

After the fast:

- Avoid starches for a few days.

- Chew your food well.

- Stop eating when you feel full.

- Don\'t become physically active too soon.

- Eat healthy food.

Here is a suggested schedule for reintroducing food after a complete
fast:

- DAY 1: Drink liquids every couple of hours in small amounts. Mix water
 with fruit juices.

- DAY 2: Eat chicken noodle soup or something similar. Yogurt in small
 quantities will be fine.

- DAY 3 & 4: Salads, soups without grease and cooked vegetables.

- After a few days you can begin to resume a normal diet.

- The last thing you should begin consuming is fish and meats.
