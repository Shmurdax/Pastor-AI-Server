# OUT OF THE WHIRLWIND

TEXT: Job 1:1-3 "There was a man in the land of Uz, whose name was
Job; and that man was [blameless]{.underline} and [upright]{.underline},
and one who [feared God]{.underline} and [shunned evil]{.underline}. 2
And seven sons and three daughters were born to him. 3 Also, his
possessions were seven thousand sheep, three thousand camels, five
hundred yoke of oxen, five hundred female donkeys, and a very large
household, so that this man was the greatest of all the people of the
East."

Job 38:1-4 "Then the Lord answered Job [out of the
whirlwind]{.underline}, and said: 2 "Who is this who darkens counsel by
words without knowledge? 3 Now prepare yourself like a man; I will
question you, and you shall answer Me. 4 "Where were you when I laid the
foundations of the earth? Tell Me, if you have understanding."

INTRO: Whirlwinds represent times of:

- Chaos...

- Struggle...

- Disorder...

- Resistance...

- Confusion...

- Bewilderment...

Everyone faces whirlwinds! Bad people along with good people face the
whirlwind! Religious people as well as heathens face the whirlwind! Rich
people and poor people face the whirlwind! Jesus said, "In this world
you SHALL have tribulation, but be of good cheer, I have overcome the
world."

In Houston, I shouldn't have to spend a lot of time describing
whirlwinds! We are in the middle of hurricane season, and hurricanes
have a way of disrupting literally everything and causing untold damage!
I told a previous Music Director to stop singing the song "Loves like a
hurricane..." This is the wrong city for that song!

Throughout life and ministry, we are all going to experience some
whirlwinds, the question becomes, what will be our response to the
whirlwind?

It\'s man\'s nature in his suffering to ask the question, "Why?"

It is our God-given nature:

- To reflect on right and wrong...

- To consider what is our purpose...

- To question and to contemplate...

Most often, when we face seasons of difficulty, we question "Why?" We
do this because we are trying to get a handle on the meaning, purpose,
or reason for it.

For example, if someone society considers evil dies, we may feel they
had it coming and that justice is served.

Or if we do something evil and face hardship as a result, at least it
makes sense to us.

But what are we to think, and how are we to feel when we suffer, or when
we witness others who suffer "undeservedly?"

The Word of God has an answer for us!

Job 1:1-3 "There was a man in the land of Uz, whose name was Job; and
that man was [blameless]{.underline} and [upright]{.underline}, and one
who [feared God]{.underline} and [shunned evil]{.underline}. 2 And seven
sons and three daughters were born to him. 3 Also, his possessions were
seven thousand sheep, three thousand camels, five hundred yoke of oxen,
five hundred female donkeys, and a very large household, so that this
man was the greatest of all the people of the East."

- Blameless - Complete, innocent, sincere

- Upright - Straight, level, just

- Feared - Reverent, honor

- Shunned evil - Turned away from evil.

In other words, "Job was a man who lived a straight, innocent life,
filled with honor and avoided evil." When the Holy Spirit declares
this about you, it is a good report! You can\'t do any better!

Please note, the fact that Job lived such a life did not prevent him
from experiencing the whirlwind. In fact, the life that Job lived made
him a target for the whirlwind. Job 1:6-12 "Now there was a day when
the sons of God came to present themselves before the Lord, and Satan
also came among them. 7 And the Lord said to Satan, "From where do you
come?" So Satan answered the Lord and said, "From going to and fro
on the earth, and from walking back and forth on it." 8 Then the
Lord said to Satan, "Have you considered My servant Job, that there is
none like him on the earth, a blameless and upright man, one who fears
God and shuns evil?" 9 So Satan answered the Lord and said, "Does
Job fear God for nothing? 10 Have You not made a hedge around him,
around his household, and around all that he has on every side? You have
blessed the work of his hands, and his possessions have increased in the
land. 11 But now, stretch out Your hand and touch all that he has, and
he will surely curse You to Your face!" 12 And the Lord said to
Satan, "Behold, all that he has is in your power; only do not lay a hand
on his person." So Satan went out from the presence of the Lord."

The Word of God declares Satan to be "The Accuser of the
brethren." His name, the "devil," means in the Greek -- "One who
casts himself between two persons to divide them."

Satan is criminally insane. He seeks to inflict suffering for
suffering\'s sake. He is a murderer and a thief.

Notice the accusation of Satan: "Job only serves You and worships You
because you have blessed him." What a jerk!

This was not just an attack on the character of Job; it was an insult to
the dignity and worth of Almighty God!

This challenge could not go unanswered. Job\'s devotion to God was to be
proven in the fire of testing also known as the whirlwind.

Consider Job\'s trials relayed to us in the forthcoming chapters:

- He lost his livestock

- He lost his servants

- He lost his children

- He lost his health

- He lost his prestige

- His wife lost her wits - "Curse God and die!" (Who can blame
 her? Finances gone ... Kids gone... Health gone.)

Between chapter 1 and chapter 38 we find cycles of conversation between
Job and his "comforters."

Their theology is simple:

- Good things happen to good people...

- Bad things happen to bad people...

- Bad things are happening to you...

- You must have done something bad...

But do you recall what we learned in the beginning? Job was considered,
BY GOD, to be perfect, upright, righteous!

In the end of his season of trial - and trials have a season -- God
answers Job with a seventy-four-question quiz! Job 38:1-4 "Then the
Lord answered Job out of the whirlwind, and said: 2 "Who is this who
darkens counsel by words without knowledge? 3 Now prepare yourself like
a man; I will question you, and you shall answer Me. 4 "Where were you
when I laid the foundations of the earth? Tell Me, if you have
understanding.

Beginning with verse 4 and carrying on to chapter 42 are 74 questions
God asks Job. Questions aimed at reminding Job of God\'s:

- Wisdom...

- Creative power...

- Sovereignty... (He can do what He wants to, when He wants to, how He
 wants to, where He wants to!)

I like one commentator\'s notes on this passage:

"It must be noted that God never told Job about the reason for his
pain, about the conflict between Himself and Satan, which was the reason
for Job\'s suffering. He never gave Job any explanation at all about the
circumstances of his trouble. He did one thing in all that He said. He
asked Job if he was as eternal, great, powerful, wise and perfect as
God. If not, Job would have been better off to be quiet and to trust
Him."

Though God did not reveal an explanation for Job's whirlwind, He did
release a blessing! Rest assured, when God does not answer your
questions it is because He is about to send a blessing that will render
your questions insignificant!

God commended the faithfulness of Job and brought about healing and
restoration to his life.

Job 42:16 (NKJV) "After this lived Job [an hundred and forty
years]{.underline}, and saw his sons, and his sons\' sons, even four
generations."  Job was 70 years old when his trial began, and he
lived to be 210 years old! Job lived to see his great, great
grandchildren! God gave him twice as much life following his head-on
collision with the devil, than he had before his problems!

CONCLUSION:

Can we stand in the midst of the whirlwind:

- To declare the goodness of God?

- To declare His worth?

- To declare His mighty power?

- To declare His righteous acts?

Are we ready to declare to the devil, that he will not persuade us that
God has forgotten us ... that God does not care about us ... that God
is not fair to us?

Are we ready to lift a sacrifice of praise from sincere hearts that will
be a sweet-smelling fragrance before the throne of Almighty God?
