INTRO: The phrase "In Between" automatically triggers a thought
process that let's the hearer know that something is unsettled about the
situation, or a transition is going on. As individuals, we often grapple
with a plethora of transitions throughout life. We have all known the
agony of being in between with: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Relationships...

- Health issues...

- Financial decisions...

- Educational processes...

- Employment concerns...

Being in between suggests that change is in the air and when this is the
case, we are always hopeful the modifications will prove to be
beneficial to our future. The greatest transition in the history of the
world occurred on the first Easter and the modifications proved to be
beneficial to our future and eternity.

Easter is without a doubt the greatest day in the history of the world.
1 Corinthians 15:17 (NKJV) "And if Christ is not risen, your faith is
futile; you are still in your sins!"

Today I want to take a little different look at the events surrounding
the Easter story. The three contiguous days of the first Easter were of
epic proportions, they: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Changed the paradigm of religion...

- Changed the course of human history...

- Changed the delivery of the Gospel message...

- Changed the eternal destiny of billions of souls...

Most Easter sermons deal with the final day of the story, but I want to
focus on all three day's beginning with the events in the Garden of
Gethsemane.

DAY ONE:

(PLAY CLIPS OF THE PASSION OF THE CHRIST AS THE FOLLOWING BULLET
POINTS ARE MADE)

- Jesus had supper with His disciples... This meal occurred in the Upper
 Room in Southwest Jerusalem and it was here He revealed that one of
 His own would betray Him.

- He faced a sleepless night in the Garden... He prayed while His
 closest friends slept.

- He agonized before the Father in prayer until His sweat became as
 great drops of blood... He prayed three times, "If it be possible
 let this cup pass from me." But finally He prayed, "Not my will
 but thine be done."

- He was betrayed by Judas and then deserted by all the disciples...

- He was arrested in the Garden...

- He was brought back to the palace of the High Priest... It was here
 that He was interrogated by Annas a former High Priest and Caiaphas,
 Annas\' son-in-law.

- He was tried by the Sanhedrin, and found guilty of blasphemy...

- He was sentenced to die for a crime He never committed...

- He was sent to Pontius Pilate at the Antonia Fortress...

- Pilate did not find fault with Him...

- Pilate sent Jesus to Herod who did not want to get involved...

- Herod sent Jesus back to Pilate...

- Pilate caved in to the pressure of the crowd and ordered Jesus to be
 tortured and crucified...

- Jesus was beaten with thirty-nine stripes...

- Jesus was required to carry His cross from Pilates Judgment Hall to
 Golgotha... It is believed the cross of Jesus weighed one hundred
 twenty-five pounds and He carried it for two and one half miles!

Jesus was placed in the position of either calling for relief from his
temporal circumstances which would have short circuited the redemption
process, or setting an example of faith for road weary pilgrims to
follow when the spiritual night is so dark one cannot see the next step
to take.

If Jesus had caved under the pressure: (PLAC ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A
TIME)

- The enemy would have defeated the Fathers answer for sin...

- The redemption process would have been back to square one...

- Mankind would have continued in sin without a Savior to call on...

- Evil would have continued to dominate Gods crowing creation,
 mankind...

- The world would probably have self destructed in a cesspool of sinful
 behavior...

The good news is that nothing of the sort transpired!

The will of the father was for Jesus to subdue the flesh and finish the
redemption process.

We must NEVER forget; Jesus was crucified as the sinless son of man
rather than the sinless Son of God.

Jesus cried from the cross, Eli, Eli lama-sa-bach-tha-ni. Which being
interpreted is My God, My God, why has thou forsaken Me?" We know the
Father did not forsake Jesus and we know God is not going to forsake us,
but it sure feels that way from time to time!

DAY TWO -- THE DAY IN BETWEEN:

Day two is a day that is often overlooked in this story. Day two is a
period of time that accomplished immeasurable results. (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The disciples of Jesus forsake Him...

- Joseph of Arimithea buries the body of Jesus in his own tomb...

- The tomb is sealed to insure His interment would be final... (The
 governmental leaders were concerned that someone would steal His body
 and claim that He had risen... They were listening more closely than
 the Disciples... The soldiers were told "Make it as secure as you
 can.")

- As soon as the tomb was sealed Jesus laid aside His humanity and
 dawned His divinity... (The soldiers would have felt quite foolish if
 they had known they were guarding an empty tomb)

- Jesus paid a visit to the halls of Hell... (He conquered the keys of
 death and hell)

- He preached to the captives in paradise... (Those who died under law
 who were anticipating a coming Messiah)

DAY 3:

Matthew 28:1-6 (NKJV) "Now after the Sabbath, as the first day of the
week began to dawn, Mary Magdalene and the other Mary came to see the
tomb. And behold, there was a great earthquake; for an angel of the
Lord descended from heaven, and came and rolled back the stone from the
door, and sat on it. His countenance was like lightning, and his
clothing as white as snow. And the guards shook for fear of him, and
became like dead men. But the angel answered and said to the women,
"Do not be afraid, for I know that you seek Jesus who was crucified.
"He is not here; for He is risen, as He said. Come, see the place
where the Lord lay."

Day three begins in a cemetery, at sunrise, on the first Easter morning.
A cemetery is not a place where you find a lot of hope, quite the
contrary is true: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- A cemetery is a burial ground for the dead...

- A cemetery is a place where dreams are laid to rest...

- A cemetery is a place where hope dims, flickers and dies...

- A cemetery is a place for reflecting on what could have been...

- A cemetery is a place for beautiful flowers to wither and die...

- A cemetery is a place where grief deadens the tune of victorious
 songs...

- A cemetery is a place where money loses its power to buy its way
 out...

- A cemetery is a place where we are forced to accept the finality of
 fate...

In this particular cemetery there were no doubt old tombs that were well
marked by the weathering streaks, of Father Time. Others were recently
occupied.

On the third day, two women could be seen making their way into the
cemetery through the gray dawn shadows of a spring day. The women who
were coming to this cemetery were obviously emotionally distraught and
with painstaking effort, they made their way to the tomb of Jesus.

Tenderly and gently, as only a mother can, one of these ladies had come
to give her son a decent and proper burial. A mother and her trusted
friend were paying their last respects to a son whose life held such
promise. But now, at only thirty-three and one half years of age, the
beloved son who had given so much would be nothing but a faint memory,
and a short inscription on a tomb marker!

There is no pain that can equal the pain of a mother who has lost her
child.

Matthew draws our attention to another group of people in the cemetery,
and they were a group of soldiers, who were frightened out of their
minds. (Guy who fell into an open grave late one night not knowing a
drunk had fallen in there earlier in the evening...)

Remember, these guys were calloused, emotionally unattached monsters,
but they were frozen by fear when a man from outer space showed up with
the keys to a sepulcher housing a political and religious martyr!

This cemetery on the outskirts of Jerusalem appeared to be the most
unlikely place on the earth to experience a miracle. But the greatest
miracle in human history was about to transpire before the unsuspecting
eyes of the soldiers and the women!

It shouldn't surprise me, but it does: God performs some of His most
incredible activities in some of the most inconspicuous places! (LEAVE
ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE)

- God chose the backside of the desert to defy the law of conflagration
 by allowing a bush to burn but not be consumed...

- God chose a group of shepherds and a Judean hillside to display
 heavens choir of angels...

- God chose a stable in Bethlehem to be the birthplace of His only
 Son...

- God chose a hillside in Israel to contradict the law of diminishing
 returns by feeding 5,000 men with a little boy's lunch...

Based on what we already know, it should not come as a great surprise to
us that God stages the miracle of all miracles in a cemetery!

This miracle was not performed in space, a science lab, a palace, or
before an important crowd! This miracle was performed in a cemetery!

God chose this cemetery, which represents man's extremity, to display
the awesome power of his Divinity!

There is another incident that occurred in this cemetery which must not
be overlooked, Matthew says "Behold, there was a great
earthquake."

This incident represents a natural phenomenon. An earthquake is normally
referred to as an "Act of God" by the modern liberal media. In other
words, an earthquake is something that is beyond our control. The
capacity of an earthquake for destruction is almost without limits. An
earthquake:

- Bends steel...

- Busts boulders...

- Buries buildings...

- Breaks structures into splinters, effortlessly...

- Bounces mountains around like a rubber ball...

- Breaks holes in the surface of the earth like tearing a piece of
 paper...

This natural phenomenon is a circumstance of major proportion. Two such
quakes had been observed in the previous seventy-two hours, one when
Jesus died, the earth shook like a bowl of jelly to express its horror,
and now it shakes again to celebrate the resurrection of the Rock of
Ages!

Just minutes before the resurrection the circumstances could not have
been worse; Jesus was not sick or comatose, He was dead! Jesus was not
only dead He had been buried and in the grave three days!

The circumstances could not have been worse! God chose the very worst
circumstances to let us know that nothing that happens to us is too
difficult for Him to handle!

God takes the darkest night of despair and turns it into a miracle for
us! Psalms 30:5 (NKJV) "For His anger is but for a moment, His favor
is for life; Weeping may endure for a night, But [joy comes in the
morning]{.underline}."

Matthew 28:2-4 (NKJV) "Behold, there was a great earthquake; for [an
angel of the Lord descended from heaven, and came and rolled back the
stone from the door, and sat on it]{.underline}. His countenance was
like lightning, and his clothing as white as snow. And the guards
shook for fear of him, and became like dead men."

Let me tell you, there were more than two emotionally distraught women
and a couple of frightened soldiers present when Jesus was resurrected!
The Scripture say's "The angel of the Lord came down!" Everywhere
one looks there are angels in this story: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT
A TIME)

- Angels celebrated His advent...

- Angels sympathized with Him in His agony...

- Angels comforted bereaved believers at His ascension...

- Angels are schedule to share the triumph with Him in the apocalypse
 that is soon to come...

The events of the third day called for something beyond human resources,
so it should be no wonder that angels broadcast the news, when His
abolition was aborted.

Napoleon once said, "Extraordinary problems call for extraordinary
remedies."

The Resurrection of Jesus meets that criterion. It proceeded beyond the
ordinary! This was an extraordinary remedy, prescribed by an
extraordinary God, who thinks nothing of going beyond finite
limitations!

Because of the events of the third day, the tomb that Jesus occupied is
now empty, no longer needed, and can be returned to its owner! Jesus did
not buy a tomb, He just borrowed one; He was only going to use it for
three day's! The Lord of Glory moved out via the Resurrection!

The resurrection is the greatest miracle in the history of the world,
and tells me that there's nothing that God can't do! (PLACE ON THE

- God has never seen a hurt He could not heal...

- God has never seen a battle He could not win...

- God has never seen a question He could not answer...

- God has never seen an obligation He could not meet...

- God has never seen a failure He could not fix...

- God has never seen a loss He could not restore...

The reason that God can do all of this for us is because of the events
of the three days surrounding Easter, and now the tomb is empty!

He's alive! Because He lives, you and I can live as well!
