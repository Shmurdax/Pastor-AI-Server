# FIRST FRUIT

There has been much preaching in the Church on tithing, but precious
little teaching on the principle of first fruit. (How many of you truly
know what is meant by the term "First fruit"?)

If we are going to live the God life, we must learn to put first things
first. In so doing, God must occupy the number one slot in our life.

The Bible has much to say about money:

- There are 500 Scriptures in the Bible concerning prayer...

- There are 500 verses concerning faith...

- There are more than 2,000 Scriptures concerning money and
 possessions...

- Jesus talked about money in 16 of His 38 parables...

Money is a test from God. It is a priority test! Are we passing or
failing? (Be honest with yourself)

According to Scripture the way we handle our money speaks volumes about
where our priorities have been placed. Matthew 6:19-21 (NLT) "Don't
store up treasures here on earth, where they can be eaten by moths and
get rusty, and where thieves break in and steal. 20 Store your treasures
in heaven, where they will never become moth-eaten or rusty and where
they will be safe from thieves. 21 [Wherever your treasure is, there
your heart and thoughts will also be]{.underline}."

The principle of first fruit was established in the Old Testament book
of Exodus. Exodus 13:2,12,13 (NLT) "Dedicate to me all [the firstborn
sons]{.underline} of Israel and every firstborn male animal. [They are
mine]{.underline}." 12 All [firstborn sons]{.underline} and [firstborn
male animals must be presented to the [Lord]{.smallcaps}]{.underline}.
13 A [firstborn male donkey]{.underline} may be redeemed from the
[Lord]{.smallcaps} by presenting a lamb in its place. But if you decide
not to make the exchange, the donkey must be killed by breaking its
neck. However, you must redeem every firstborn son."

God declared the firstborn belonged to Him 16 times in Scripture! When
an animal delivered its firstborn, it was to be sacrificed and if for
some reason it was considered unclean, the person was to take a clean
animal from their herd and sacrifice it, thus redeeming the firstborn.

Jesus was Gods firstborn and He offered him as a sacrifice. He was a
real first fruit offering, in that sense He was Gods tithe! God gave His
tithe in faith before we ever believed. Romans 8:29 (NLT) "For God
knew his people in advance, and he chose them to become like his Son, so
that his Son would be [the firstborn]{.underline}, with many brothers
and sisters."

The Scripture is clear, God not only requires the firstborn or the first
fruit, but He set the example by giving His firstborn Son, for the sins
of His other sons.

In the same way, we are to bring the first fruit -and THEN we will see
the blessing.

Some people want to wait until they can afford to start tithing, but the
curse and the devourer will never allow them to be in a financial
position to do so. I recently had a conversation with a person who was
not tithing, and I told them, "It does not matter how many resources
you are able to attract, you will never have enough, because you are
placing your resources in a sack that has holes in it!"

It is interesting to note that God required the firstborn. He did not
say to allow the ewe to bear nine lambs and then give number ten to Him.
It is possible to be a tither without adhering to the principle of first
fruit. First fruit is giving Him a minimum of 10% BEFORE we access any
of the 100%!

It doesn't take faith to give one out of ten, but it requires great
faith to give the first of ten!

Have you ever wondered why God respected Abel's offering but rejected
Cain's? Genesis 4:3-5 (KJV) "And [in process of time]{.underline} it
came to pass, that Cain brought of the fruit of the ground an offering
unto the LORD. 4 And Abel, he also brought [of the firstlings of his
flock]{.underline} and of the fat thereof. And the LORD had respect unto
Abel and to his offering: 5 But unto Cain and to his offering he had not
respect. And Cain was very wroth, and his countenance fell." Abel
brought the firstborn; Cain brought his offering in the process of time.
When he decided to. When he got around to it!

What we give to God is never lost because He redeems it for us, but what
we withhold from him will always be lost. The reason for the loss is
that our possessions no longer have the redemptive power of God working
for them. Matthew 16:25 (NLT) "If you try to keep your life for
yourself, [you will lose it]{.underline}. But if you give up your life
for me, [you will find true life]{.underline}."

What are we to do with the first fruit? Proverbs 3:9-10 (NKJV) "Honor
the [Lord]{.smallcaps} with your possessions, And [with the first-fruits
of all your increase]{.underline}; 10 So your barns will be filled with
plenty, And your vats will overflow with new wine." These verses
point out the following principles:

- Tithing is a priority of God... "Honor the Lord with your
 possessions..."

- The first fruit is required... Honor him, "...With the first fruits
 of your increase..."

- We will experience Gods provision... "Your barns will be filled
 with plenty..."

- We can expect Him to bless us in a spiritual sense... "Your vats
 will overflow with new wine..."

Just as the firstborn and first fruit belong to God, the tithe belongs
to Him as well. Leviticus 27:30 (NKJV) "And all the [tithe of the
land]{.underline}, whether of the seed of the land or of the fruit of
the tree, [is the [Lord]{.smallcaps}'s. It is holy to the
[Lord]{.smallcaps}]{.underline}."

Some will say that tithing is a principle of the Law, but tithing
actually predates the Law by 400 years and according to Jesus, postdates
the Law! Malachi 3:6 (NKJV) "For I am the [Lord,]{.smallcaps} [I do
not change]{.underline}..." Romans 11:16 (NKJV) "For if the [first
fruit is holy]{.underline}, [the lump is also holy]{.underline}; and if
the root is holy, so are the branches." Matthew 23:23 (NLT) "How
terrible it will be for you teachers of religious law and you Pharisees.
Hypocrites! For you are careful to tithe even the tiniest part of your
income, but you ignore the important things of the law---justice, mercy,
and faith. [You should tithe, yes]{.underline}, but you should not leave
undone the more important things."

Many blessings are promised to those who become tithers, but the first
fruit offering is the trigger on the blessing. I hope you will pull the
trigger today!

CONCLUSION: The following principles should be followed when
engaging in tithing the first fruit:

- The tithe of first fruit is 10% of total family income...

<!-- -->

- The tithe of first fruit belongs in the local church...

- The tithe of first fruit should never be directed or earmarked...

- The tithe of first fruit should be in God's house before one dime of
 the income is spent...
