# SPIRITUAL AUTHORITY

(Graduation Sunday, 2022)

TEXT: Romans 13:1-7 (NKJV) "Let every soul be subject to the
governing authorities. For there is no authority except from God, and
the authorities that exist are appointed by God. ^2 ^Therefore whoever
resists the authority resists the ordinance of God, and those who resist
will bring judgment on themselves. ^3 ^For rulers are not a terror to
good works, but to evil. Do you want to be unafraid of the authority? Do
what is good, and you will have praise from the same. ^4 ^For he is
God's minister to you for good. But if you do evil, be afraid; for he
does not bear the sword in vain; for he is God's minister, an avenger
to execute wrath on him who practices evil. ^5 ^Therefore you must be
subject, not only because of wrath but also for conscience'

INTRO: Today we are honoring graduates, and as such I asked myself
two questions:

"What should I say to these people who have applied themselves and
achieved this milestone in their lives?"

"What could I say that would be most impactful to them?"

While I am speaking directly to the graduates, it is my hope that
everyone who hears this, will listen intently and collect some
takeaway's.

Most of the things I am going to address today, I wish someone would
have told me when I was as young as you are.

CHOOSE SPIRITUAL PATERNITY WISELY

1 Corinthians 4:14-18 (NKJV) "I do not write these things to shame
you, but as [my beloved children]{.underline} I warn you. ^15 ^For
though [you might have ten thousand instructors in Christ, yet you
do not have many fathers]{.underline}; for in Christ Jesus [I have
begotten you]{.underline} through the gospel. ^16 ^Therefore I urge
you, [imitate me]{.underline}. ^17 ^For this reason I have sent Timothy
to you, who is my beloved and faithful son in the Lord, who will remind
you of my ways in Christ, as I teach everywhere in every church."

The most glaring problem in our culture is the lack of parental guidance
-- More than 90% of the males incarcerated in America have no father
involved in their life. 80% of identity that comes from the father --
20% of the identity of a child comes from the nurturing of the mother.

This is not just a problem in the culture, this is a problem in the
church -- We are living in a fatherless culture and a fatherless church
culture. (How many of you have identified a spiritual mother and father?
If you haven't, you should)

Everyone needs a spiritual mother and/or father. But everyone needs a
spiritual mother and/or father who actually knows what it means to be in
a place of Spiritual Paternity. (We will teach on this soon)

Our culture has gone mentor crazy, and a mentor has a place in our lives
for sure. (A mentor can say what they want to, when they want to, how
they want to, where they want to, and we give up the right to be
offended)

Spiritual paternity is on a completely different level from having a
mentor. Listen to what Paul say's to the Corinthians about Spiritual
paternity:

- "You are my beloved children." Beloved = Esteemed, favorite,
 worthy of love. Graduates you need to make it a priority to find a
 person who is way down the road ahead of your spiritually and in
 maturity -- and become their spiritual child -- Behave in such a way
 that you become their favorite -- How do you do this? Obey -- Submit
 -- Honor -- Take their advice over your own experience -- If you are
 not going to follow the advice, don't waste the parents time asking
 for it

- "You can have many instructors in Christ, yet you
 cannot have multiple fathers." Instructors/teachers are plentiful,
 paternity is rare. You can change teachers/mentors -- Paternity is for
 a lifetime -- Once you choose spiritual paternity it is your
 responsibility to maintain that relationship in good order going
 forward

- "I have begotten you through the gospel..." When choosing a
 spiritual parent, it should be someone who has helped lead you to
 Christ, or someone who has had a big hand in your spiritual
 development

- "I urge you, to imitate me." Imitate = Mimic -- To become like
 -- When choosing a spiritual mother/father you should choose someone
 whose character above reproach - Paul wasn't supposed to become like
 the Corinthians, they were to become like him -- Too many times the
 parents are trying to be like the children, rather than the children
 desiring to be like the parents -- You should choose a spiritual
 parent whose character traits are worth mimicking - You should be able
 to follow a spiritual parent as they follow Christ

CHOOSE A PASTOR CAREFULLY

Hebrews 13:17 (KJV) "Obey them that have the rule over you, and
submit yourselves: for they watch for your souls, as they that must give
account, that they may do it with joy, and not with grief: for that is
unprofitable for you."

This passage is pointing directly to the choice of a spiritual
authority, namely a Pastor. There should never be a time in your life
when you do not have a pastor. There is a big difference between having
a preacher and a pastor. Look at what is being said here:

- Obey spiritual authority... (Believe them -- Have confidence in them)

- Spiritual authority is to rule over us... (the word "rule" here
 possesses the connotation of a governor or judge -- In other words,
 spiritual authority is to have the same kind of weight a governor or
 judge would have over our lives -- They have the final say!)

- We are to submit ourselves to spiritual authority... (The word submit
 here literally means to surrender to them)

- Our spiritual authorities will give an account for us... (On judgment
 day, a spiritual authority will not have weight in whether a person is
 saved or lost, but they will have influence in the reward a person
 will receive)

<!-- -->

- We must live a life of submission to spiritual authority so that when
 they are called upon to give an account for us, they may do it with
 joy and not with sorrow...

- A spiritual authority giving a good report on our behalf delivers
 great reward for us... ("You have been faithful over a few things,
 I will make you ruler over many things.")

- A spiritual authority giving a bad report on our behalf will have
 devastating results... (It is after the Judgment Seat of Christ that
 all tears are wiped away from our eyes)

When choosing a spiritual authority, we must be careful because
everything flows down from headship. (Lady who wanted to see the Pastors
new car because she wanted to know what her next new car was going to
look like)

I want to always make sure that those I place myself under are doing
more than I am, because all things flow down in the spiritual realm.

CHOOSE A SPOUSE SLOWLY

One of the most important decisions you are going to make is the choice
of a spouse (Lifetime partner)

When you repeat marriage vows, you are not making a promise to the
preacher or audience, you are making a lifetime promise to your spouse
and more importantly to God.

The choice of a spouse is a lifetime choice:

- For better or worse

- For richer or poorer

- In sickness and in health

This choice goes far beyond having chemistry -- It is a promise that
outlasts everything else. You can fall out of love but you have to
intentionally leave a marriage. (I've never heard vows that say, I will
stay with you as long as I am in love with you -- In marriage, sometimes
you do and sometimes you don't)

Here are some Don Nordin words of wisdom on picking a spouse:

- Don't place too much emphasis on looks... Susan's seeing eye dog --
 Beauty is skin deep, but ugly is to the bone -- Do an age
 enhancement - Beauty often fades -- I am marrying next time for
 money - A commitment to love is unending

- Don't choose a spouse unless you have compatible purpose statements...
 (Susan can help you with this -- Has your potential spouse been on a
 parallel spiritual and professional path or are they, or you
 completely switching gears because of love, better known as
 infatuation?)

- Choose a spouse that you are on the same page with -- Theologically --
 Morally -- Politically - (Differences here make strange bedfellows)

- Children will change everything in your marriage -- Baby Jesus changed
 everything and so has every other baby -- A baby changes the schedule
 -- A baby changes the intimacy in the marriage -- A baby changes the
 finances -- A baby changes the future, forever
