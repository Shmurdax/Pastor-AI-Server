# COURTSHIP AND MARRIAGE

TEXT: Revelation 19:8 "And to her was granted that she should be
arrayed in fine linen, clean and white: for the fine linen is the
righteousness of saints."

INTRO: Today is Valentine's Day. Happy Valentine's Day to you all.

I want to use the steps of a Jewish wedding to challenge each of us
about our relationships with our spouses as well as our relationship to
Jesus.

At the time of Christ, there were Seven stages to a Jewish wedding:

## I. THE CONTRACT

The Contract or legal covenant was an agreement by which the young man
proposed marriage.

Any young man could show up with a contract, but typically, the
marriages were arranged and the contracts drawn up by the parents long
before the youngsters ever got the news. (I would like to bring back
this custom before my Grandchildren get married)

Jesus is the groom and we are the selected bride. We should love our
spouses as "Christ loved the church, and gave Himself for it."

The Church is called the "Bride of Christ" in the New Testament
for good reason.

It is The Church who has a covenant relationship with the One Who
forgives sins - Jesus Christ.

The Old Covenant (Law), which was given to Moses was totally inflexible.
But the New Covenant is a relationship based on LOVE, not fear -- This
is a matter of the heart!

The relationship between a man and woman should be based on love, not
fear. No one should be walking around on egg shells!

The New Covenant is signed, dated, sealed and delivered.

## II. THE PRICE

The family of each bride had a price for the dowry of their daughter,
and the prospective bridegroom was expected to pay it.

Jesus paid the ultimate price. We should love our spouse in the same
way.

The bride's father received money, in effect, because he had raised a
daughter so wisely and well, that someone desired to marry her. The
Price that was placed on the daughter allowed the father to recover some
of his prior losses, and even make a profit on the marriage of his
daughter, to a wealthy groom. (I need to send an invoice to Jeremy)

The Price also served as "earnest money", which is a valuable aspect
of any legal transaction to this very day.

The Price is obvious - Jesus Christ came to offer Himself upon the Cross
of Calvary for our redemption. There was never a greater price paid for
a bride than Jesus Christ paid for the Church!

We should be willing to make whatever sacrifice is necessary for our
spouse.

## III. THE CUP

In those days it was traditional for the groom to pour a Cup of wine for
his intended bride, and then he would propose a toast to the audience.
This served also to establish that both parties agreed to the terms of
the contract -- this became a legal and binding agreement.

Matthew 26:27-28 (NKJV) "^ ^Then He took the cup, and gave thanks,
and gave it to them, saying, "Drink from it, all of you. ^28 ^For this
is My blood of the new covenant, which is shed for many for
the remission of sins." This was a portrait of Jesus drinking the Cup
of covenant with His Bride. His Blood is what has made us His bride, and
sealed the Marriage Covenant.

His blood was shed for the remission of sins, fulfilling Jeremiah's
announcement of the New Covenant - The covenant would not just roll
sin's ahead, it would Forgive Sin!!! The New Testament that we hold in
front of us, is OUR copy of this Marriage Contract. We have been bought
with a Price - The Blood of Jesus! The Apostle Peter said, "We were
not redeemed with corruptible things such as silver and gold; but with
the PRECIOUS BLOOD OF JESUS!"

We should be willing to shed our blood, if necessary, for our spouse.

## IV. THE DEPARTURE OF THE BRIDEGROOM

Tradition says that once the engagement had been agreed to by all
parties, the groom would go back to his home for an undisclosed period
of time. When he made his exit, he would say something on the order of,
"I go to prepare a place for you."

The goal of every spouse should be to build a better life for their
companion. We should be intent each day on improving their lives, not
making each day more miserable.

Before Jesus left earth for Heaven, He said, John 14:1-3 (NKJV)
"Let not your heart be troubled; you believe in God, believe also in
Me. ^2 ^In My Father's house are many mansions; if it were not so, I
would have told you. I go to prepare a place for you.^3 ^And if I go and
prepare a place for you, I will come again and receive you to Myself;
that where I am, there you may be also."

The Lord is now preparing the Bridal Chamber for the Church.

Like the Jewish bride, we must watch and wait for His return - For
"We know not the day or the hour of His return."

In the meantime, let us keep our veil on, as a symbol of our
engagement - our testimony of betrothal. Let us not commit adultery with
the world, for the Bridegroom is coming.

## V. THE RETURN OF THE BRIDEGROOM

When the bridegroom left his prospective bride's home, he then returned
to his father's house to build his bride a "Bridal Chamber" - A
little mansion - For their honeymoon and future home.

During this period of time, which usually took between 1-2 years, the
bride would spend her time gathering her things and making herself ready
for the bridegrooms return. One thing she was to do was to keep her veil
on wherever she went. This let everyone know she was engaged, and also
kept her in a state of readiness, should the bridegroom return
unexpectedly.

As a matter of fact, the groom did not know when he would come back!
Only his father knew.

The bride was literally "Stolen" by her bridegroom! Once the
engagement was agreed upon, the bridegroom could literally appear at any
time. The only thing necessary for the reappearance of the bridegroom
was that he had made all things ready for the bride.

Tradition says that the groom usually came for his bride in the middle
of the night, to seize her out of her bed.

She did not know when or where she was going, and only with lamp in hand
could she be led along by her groom.

We should keep our lamps (Spiritual lives) full of oil, trimmed and
burning!

Jewish brides were Stolen, if you will, in the night, by their
perspective grooms. It was customary that the Groom announce with a
shout when he was near the bride's home, so that she would momentarily
realize that her groom had come for her.

Once the bridegroom arrived and stole his bride, she became his total
responsibility.

We should see the happiness and comfort of our spouses as our sole
responsibility.

1 Thessalonians 4:16-17 (NKJV) "For the Lord Himself will descend

are alive and remain shall be caught up together with them in the clouds
to meet the Lord in the air. And thus we shall always be with the
Lord. ^18 ^Therefore comfort one another with these words." 1 Cor.

this mortal has put on immortality, then shall be brought to pass the
saying that is written: "Death is swallowed up in victory."

## VI. THE BRIDAL CHAMBER

Once the bride had been stolen away by the bridegroom, the bride and
groom would go to the bridal chamber and shut the door behind them.

The best-man would stay near the door until the marriage had been
consummated. Once the marriage was consummated, the best man would let
the other guests know that the wedding party was about to begin.

The bride and the groom would remain in the chamber for up to seven
days, at last emerging for the marriage supper.

Marriage partners have a responsibility toward each other to make sure
the physical and sexual needs of their partner is taken care of. Once
you get married, "Your body is not your own." Once you get
married, you owe your spouse, "Due benevolence."

When Jesus returns certain things will happen on earth and in heaven for
the next 7 years.

The Judgment Seat of Christ, which will be the reward of the righteous,
will occur in heaven while the great tribulation will be getting into
full swing on the earth.

This can be likened to the removing of the Veil from the Bride, which
occurred during the Honeymoon! All of our Secrets will be made known at
the Judgment Seat of Christ! It is here that the bride of Christ will be
rewarded for faithfulness. "Well done, you good and faithful servant.
You have been faithful over a few things, so I will make you ruler over
many things."

## VII. THE MARRIAGE SUPPER

This is the last aspect of the Jewish Custom of Marriage - The Marriage
Supper. This is what we call the wedding reception, in effect, a banquet
for the newly married couple.

The Church will be arrayed in white. Revelation 19:7-8 "Fine Linen,
Clean, and White: for the fine linen is the righteousness of the
saints."

After the marriage supper, the Bride and Groom would typically leave his
father's house and go to the housing that the groom had arranged for
them.

This speaks of the finality of the relationship.

Our marriages should be:

- For better or worse...

- For richer or poorer...

- In sickness and in health...

- So long as we both shall live...

I'm married to Jesus and it is forever. I'm married to Susan and it is
forever.
