# HONOR

KLA

During his earthly ministry, Jesus healed the sick, set the oppressed
free, and was a blessing to many people. He also performed amazing
miracles. Yet, when He traveled to His hometown, the Bible says that He
could not do many miracles there because of their lack of faith.

The people of Nazareth missed out on receiving everything that was
possible for them, because they did not [recognize]{.underline} Jesus
for who He was. They did not honor Him, and treat Him as the prophet,
priest and king that He was.

In fact, they showed disrespect, which means they dishonored Him. The
result? He performed fewer miracles there than anywhere he ministered.
Nazareth wasn't a place that did not [need]{.underline} miracles, it was
a place that did not [receive]{.underline} them, simply because they
failed to show honor.

To receive [honor]{.underline} in any area of our lives, we must first
show [honor]{.underline}. The law of sowing and reaping is in affect in
all aspects of our lives, including in showing honor.

To receive a blessing - a reward - from any man or woman of God, we must
first honor them. This is why Jesus said that "Anyone who receives a
prophet because he is a prophet will receive a prophet's reward."

In Hebrew, the word for honor means to weigh down (with respect and
esteem); the opposite is to treat lightly. [Honor]{.underline} is more
important than [fame]{.underline}.

Every group develops a culture, a way of doing things together. And that
happens either accidentally or deliberately. It's not enough just to
know where we're going, we need to know who we're becoming along the
way.

Honor has fallen on hard times in our culture. Independence is
worshiped. We focus on our private relationship with God, and have a
hard time recognizing spiritual authority, and considering others as
more important than ourselves. The result is that we are cut off from
the flow of Heaven and its signs, miracles, and wonders.

The Greek word for honor is "[ti-me]{.underline}" (tee-MAY). It means
"to [value]{.underline}, to [respect]{.underline}, or to [highly
esteem]{.underline}." It was usually associated with a way to assign
value.

The Bible tells us that there are people and things that we are to give
a place of honor, because of their great value. Today we will look at
these valuable items and learn how we can show proper honor where honor
is due.

HONOR PARENTS

Deuteronomy 5:16 "Honor your father and mother, as the Lord your God
commanded you. Then you will live a long, full life in the land the Lord
your God is giving you."

We are to recognize the [great value]{.underline} that God has placed on
our parents. As a child, we often think our parents are being harsh and
unreasonable. It is amazing how smart and compassionate our parents
seem, when we have children of our own.

There was an old curse that would be placed on each generation of
children in the Nordin household - "I hope one day you grow up and have
a child just like you." This could be a good thing or bad thing.

Reality is that we feel some parents don't deserve honor. Some parents
have been abusive or neglectful, and it affects our lives and spiritual
well-being. That does not change the fact that God
[commanded]{.underline} that we honor our parents.

So how can we honor an abusive parent?

Here are some practical tips:

- If there is a disconnect, call or visit the parent for the purpose of
 listening.

- We should listen rather than defend ourselves, when hurtful things are
 said.

- Let go of expectations that the parent will ever be the parent you
 want him or her to be; accept them for who they are.

- Cultivate an attitude of compassion for the things your parent did
 right, and express gratitude for even slight efforts to show love.

- Refrain from making disparaging remarks about your parent to other
 family members.

Giving honor to parents is a commandment that carries with it a [great
reward]{.underline} for obedience. "Things will go well with you, and
your day's will be long on the earth."

HONOR EACH OTHER

Psalm 8:4-5 "What are mere mortals that you should think about them,
human beings that you should care for them? Yet you made them only a
little lower than God and crowned them with glory and honor."\

We are to recognize the great value that God has placed on
[humanity]{.underline}. This society has devalued the
[human]{.underline} being. God highly values every [person]{.underline}.
"He is not willing that ANYONE should perish, but that EVERYONE would
come to repentance."

The word tells us here that people have been crowned with great honor.
Not just the rich people... Not just the intelligent people... Not just
the talented people... But all people! All human beings have been
crowned with great [honor]{.underline}!

The Hebrew word used here means, " The object is beautiful and
instills awe."

- When God looks at us, our beauty awes him.

- When God looks at a righteous person, their beauty awes Him.

- When God looks at a homeless person, his/her beauty awes him.

- When God looks at a person with a sinful lifestyle, their beauty awes
 him.

What do we do with something that is beautiful and instills awe?

- We [treasure]{.underline} it!

- We [protect]{.underline} it!

- We [highly value]{.underline} it!

- We position it in a place of [honor]{.underline}!

Some things we may even be willing to lay our lives down for.

This is exactly why Jesus laid down His life for

- Me...

- You...

- The righteous person...

- The homeless person...

- The person living a sinful lifestyle...

Because He was awed by our beauty and value, He wanted to protect us
from the thief. And He died doing so.

Romans 13:7 "Give to everyone what you owe them: Pay your taxes and
government fees to those who collect them, and give respect and honor to
those who are in authority."

Remember: We have never locked eyes with anyone that God doesn't love!

HONOR THOSE IN AUTHORITY

We are to recognize the great value that God has placed on those in
[authority]{.underline}. The concept is to give those in authority over
us, special treatment. This is a difficult concept for us.

We live in a world that highly values individuality. We do not want
anyone telling us what to do, or making demands of us. But God commands
us to treat our [bosses]{.underline}, [governmental
officials]{.underline}, and [spiritual authorities]{.underline} with the
honor that their position deserves.

As Christians, we are to be respectful in our relationship with them,
realizing that no one holds a position of authority that God has not
allowed.

1 Thessalonians 5:12 "Dear brothers and sisters, honor those who are
your leaders in the Lord's work. They work hard among you and give you
spiritual guidance."

We are to recognize the great value that God has placed on those in
spiritual authority.

Have you ever been a participant in bringing dishonor to a leader? This
can occur in the following ways:

- Not recognizing them for their hard work...

- Not accepting their experienced guidance... (Story about Joe Lyons?)

- Rebelling against their authority...

- Leading a coup against them...

When people refuse to submit to, or follow the the directions of, those
in authority, they normally think, "If I was in charge I would do
things differently." Guess what? We are not in charge, and for good
reason.

The reason is that we have yet to submit to those in authority, or
follow the directions of a person in charge. In this scenario, if God
allowed us to be in charge we would soon implode, bringing harm to the
organization and everyone involved.

One thing is for sure, disobedience in following the principal of
honoring those in authority will [exact a high price]{.underline}, from
the person who refuses to submit and honor.

We must learn to respect those that are over us. We must honor our
supervisors, governmental leaders and spiritual authorities, and as we
do, we bring them honor and bring blessings into our lives.

HONOR MARRIAGE

Hebrews 13:4 "Give honor to marriage, and remain faithful to one
another in marriage. God will surely judge people who are immoral and
those who commit adultery."

We are to honor the [institution]{.underline} of marriage.

We are to recognize the great value that God has placed upon marriage.
Not just our marriage but also all marriages. We are not to behave in
any manner that would bring dishonor to another person's marriage, or to
bring dishonor our own marriage.

We must be careful that our actions or words not lead to the termination
of someone's marriage, including our own.

If we are married, we must remain [faithful]{.underline} to one another,
so that honor will be shown to the institution of marriage.

- For [better]{.underline} or [worse]{.underline}...

- For [richer]{.underline} or [poorer]{.underline}...

- In [sickness]{.underline} and in [health]{.underline}...

- Until [death]{.underline} do us [part]{.underline}...

God does not take it lightly when a sacred institution that He
established is dishonored. To do so is to bring judgment upon ourselves.

1 Corinthians 6:18-20 (NKJV) "Flee from sexual immorality. All other

1 Peter 3:7 "In the same way, you husbands must give honor to your

Men, we are to honor their wives. We are to recognize the great value
that God has placed on our wives. We are to treat them with
understanding and great respect.

ILLUSTRATION:

A man walking along a California beach was deep in prayer when all of a
sudden he said aloud, "Lord grant me one wish". The sky clouded and

can drive over anytime I want to."

The Lord answered, "Your request is very materialistic. Think of the

how I can make her truly happy?"

After a few minutes God said, "How many lanes do you want on that
bridge?"

Ladies, honestly, there are times when we really don't understand. But
God has told us to treat you with special treatment.

[prayers]{.underline} will be hindered.

does this kind of honor look like?

- They are to [submit]{.underline} to their husbands as the spiritual
 priest of their homes.

- They are to [obey]{.underline} the direction of their husbands, as
 long as that direction does not lead them to dishonor the Lord.

- They are to [dress]{.underline} and [behave]{.underline} in a way that
 brings honor to their husbands and families.

HONOR GOD

We are to recognize the great value that God places upon himself, and we
should [reciprocate]{.underline} by valuing Him above all else.

God is valuable and He realizes it. He says of himself, "You will
have no other God's above me, because I am a jealous God."

He is the creator of everything. He is in control of everything around
us.

He gave us the life and health that we enjoy. He paid a high price so
that you and I can honor him with [service]{.underline} and
[righteous]{.underline} living.

To live a [corrupt]{.underline} lifestyle brings [dishonor]{.underline}
to him.

God doesn't deserve honor, He deserves double honor.

Honoring God begins with our finances.

Proverbs 3:9 "Honor the Lord with your wealth and with the best part

tithe.

to the [church]{.underline} you attend. The average Christian gives

2.56% of their income. A staggering 25% give nothing at all. The average
tip given to a server at a restaurant is 16%. (Write these stats on a
white board)

We have apps on our phones to assist us in tallying what the proper
amount the tip should be. Almost everyone feels obligated to leave a tip
of somekind.

In a survey done by Deal News, 56% of those surveyed tipped in spite of

We worry about what a person who brought us food and filled our glasses

- Has God [delivered]{.underline} us?

- Has God ever [served]{.underline} us?

56% of the population felt that even bad service, deserved something.
What about God?

The tithe is a baseline requirement. Tithing doesn't make us generous;
it makes us compliant; our offerings make us generous.

Paul wrote "You must each decide in your heart how much to give. And
don't give reluctantly or in response to pressure. "For God loves a
person who gives cheerfully." (2 Corinthians 9:7)

We are to give to God, not out of sorrow or guilt; not under distress,

HONOR OURSELVES

It sounds strange indeed to say that we are to honor ourselves! This
simply means, that we are to recognize the [great value]{.underline}
that God places on each of us.

God has an order to everything He does. When we begin to honor those
things required of us; parents, humanity, authorities, church leaders,
marriage, wives, husbands, and God; He begins to exalt us in the
heavens! And whatever, or whoever, He [exalts]{.underline} in the
heavens will most certainly be [exalted]{.underline} in the earth.

Mankind is the crowning creation of God. We cannot deface or devalue His
greatest creation and expect to receive His blessing.

We must be careful about:

- Inferior thoughts... (Self-hate)

- Inferior actions... (Low self-esteem

- Habits that are harmful... ("We are the temple of the Holy
 Spirit... Whoever destroys the temple, him will God destroy.")

 life!)

- Talking down His creation... ("Life and death is in the power of
 the tongue.")

The world has devalued us but God is going to highly exalt us, [IN DUE

- God actually brags about us to the enemy. (Job)

hinder our appointed season, but rather live with expectations of its
