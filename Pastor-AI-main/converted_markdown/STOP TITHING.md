INTOR: Most people simply don't understand tithing. To many
Christians it seems like just another bill, in the long list of monthly
expenses.

Giving is supposed to be an act of worship that draws us closer to the
heart of God. Giving should be to the Christian what intimacy is to a
married couple.

When there is constant intimacy in a marriage the couple is brought
together in a way that nothing else can reproduce.

When there is consistent intimacy in a marriage the souls of the
individuals are merged. There is a term called "Alienation of
affection," and in most states, until recently this has been a
prosecutable offense. This law can be enacted when someone has an affair
with a married person. This law allows for the "other woman/man" can
be prosecuted for "Alienation of affection."

When intimacy is lost from a marriage there is a danger that one or both
parties in the marriage will cheat. This is why we are instructed in the
Word of God to not withhold intimacy from our marriage partner for
extended periods of time, "Lest we be tempted."

Giving builds a trust between God and His child that cannot be
reproduced by any other means. When we fail to live up to our financial
responsibilities toward God we are cheating on Him!

Trust produces intimacy. Intimacy takes us to a place in God that cannot
be produced in any other way. If this is true, then why is it that the
concept of tithing, giving the first ten percent of one's income, is so
difficult for Christian's to start and maintain?

Christians debate questions like: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A
TIME)

- Is tithing still required? (The only good thing Jesus ever said about
 the Pharisees is that they paid tithe)

- Isn't tithing an Old Testament law? (Tithing predates the law of Moses
 when Abraham paid tithe to Melchizadec)

- Didn't Christ abolish the law with His death and resurrection?
 (Christ's death fulfilled the sacrificial part of the law, it did not
 abolish the commandments)

- Should I tithe off the gross or net of my income? (Are we expecting a
 net blessing or a gross blessing?)

- Does God really expect me to tithe if I'm struggling in my personal
 finances? (To cease being generous with God when one is struggling
 would be to complicate the situation)

These questions all have the same recurring theme, "What's the least I
can give and still receive God's blessings." Let me ask all the married
people in the house today, how many of you would be excited about a
physical relationship with a spouse that has the attitude of duty rather
than excitement and expectation?

When you debate these questions, you totally miss the point. Biblical
generosity is not about seeing how little one can give; it is about a
spirit of generosity. How can we feel good about being anything less
than generous when we are serving a God who "So loved the world that
He gave His only begtten Son?"

[Randy Alcorn](http://www.epm.org/about-randy/) said, "Giving affirms
Christ's lordship. It dethrones me and exalts Him." (LEAVE ON THE
SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

Tithers write checks like, \$115.26. People who stop tithing and step
into a spirit of generosity write checks like \$200.00!

When we write a check for \$115.26 our attitude becomes, "Here's my ten
percent, hopefully that will cover the upkeep in Heaven until my next
paycheck. Now bless me."

God doesn't need our money! He is building a city where the cheapest
building material is gold! He is making streets out of the stuff! He is
looking for proof that He is first in our lives. He wants me to step
into such an intimate relationship with Him that I will stop tithing and
begin a journey of generosity.

A person who is tied to tithing writes checks for \$115.26 because they
believe they are the owner! They have not stepped into the belief that
everything belongs to God. Psalm 24:1 (KJV) "The earth is the
[Lord]{.smallcaps}\'s, and the fulness thereof; the world, and they that
dwell therein."

God wants us to stop tithing and start trusting Him completely. The more
we trust Him with our finances, the more He can use us to reveal His
glory. When we stop tithing and step into a spirit of generosity we
become a conduit for His miracles!

The more my faith gets tested on a financial level and I go beyond my
ten percent the more likely it is that He can reveal the miraculous
through me!

People who have stopped tithing and stepped off into generosity have
quit asking "How much should I give" and have started asking, "How
much should I keep?"
