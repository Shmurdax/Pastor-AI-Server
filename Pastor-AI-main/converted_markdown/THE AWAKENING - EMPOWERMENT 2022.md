# THE AWAKENING

INTRO: I have literally been in and around church my whole life
(Explain this).

I had the privilege of spending my teenage years in a church that was in
the midst of a red hot, Holy Ghost revival. (Welcome Hill -- It has
never been a big church but it has been a powerful church -- Chris
Bradley -- Myself -- Many Pastors, missionaries, evangelists, business
people, and lay people, all around the world doing the work of the
Kingdom)

This experience impacted my life in a positive direction, and gave me an
ongoing insatiable desire to see a real move of God in the earth,
otherwise known in church circles, as revival.

I have had the opportunity of being a part of some of the great revivals
of the past (Malvern uly -- Brownsville June 1995). I could
literally morph into a revival junkie with little to no effort
whatsoever. The revivals of the past have been more or less soaking
events. They featured:

- Protracted services... (Weeks or even months in duration)

- Passionate worship...

- Sermons filled with conviction...

- Strong altar calls... (Sometimes multiple altar calls, in one
 service - Preaching a sermon and not giving an altar call makes about
 as much sense as a professional fisherman getting his nets full and
 cutting them lose rather than dealing with the catch)

- Extended altar services... (Sometimes hours in duration -- I got
 filled with the Holy Ghost at daylight, in an all-night prayer
 meeting)

There is certainly nothing wrong with any of this, in fact, I could live
like this indefinitely.

One of the things I remember growing up, is the prediction, if not
prophecy, that there would be an end time revival. Based on this, we
(The church) have waited with baited breath, for this tsunami of the
presence of God to hit the church -- Some people desire such an event,
so they can all sit in circles, hold hands, and soak in the presence of
the Lord. If this is all revival produces, I must question the
effectiveness of such an event.

But I must ask the question, is this what the world needs, and is it in
reality what an end time revival will look like?

I hope an end time Great Awakening will have many of these
characteristics, but I think the world needs something far more
consequential. (What the Lord said to me about the Brownsville revival
-- "That is why it is not happening in your church")

So, the question needs to be asked, "What will this next Great
Awakening look like?"

The Biblical prediction is, that there will be an end time move of God,
and a great falling away simultaneously. How can such a thing be? Here
is what I am seeing happen:

- The love of many has waxed cold... We are seeing this now -- People
 can go everywhere but to church -- Covid

- Those who seek another soaking event, have become discontented with
 organized religion, because they are not experiencing what they have
 witnessed in the past... Many of these people have intentionally
 absented themselves not only from church services, but from the body
 of Christ altogether -- A few years ago I was praying for God to show
 this next generation His power, and He said to me, "Can you embrace
 it if it looks different than what you have imagined?" I rise to ask
 you the same question tonight. Some folks in this room desire His
 presence more than they desire anything else, but are in danger of
 missing it when it happens, if it looks different than the great
 revivals of the past.

- The expectation of my generation for a move of God for the next
 generation, has simply not come to fruition - I think this is because
 we are looking for something that God never intended to send - What if
 what the Lord is intending to send, and what we are expecting, is two
 different things?

- When our expectation, and what the Lord is trying to do is different,
 is it possible for those who are hungry for the next great revival to
 miss it altogether? I believe the answer to this is YES!

I believe what God is going to do in this next season is going to be
