INTRO: We are entering the Christmas season. You know what that
means! Right? Everyone is preoccupied with what they are going to give
to those who are closest to them. We want to give the best gifts we can
afford to give! Right?

Some of you are trying to figure out how to top what we gave last year.
We become our own competition in gift giving. We don't want to give a
lesser gift; we want to give a greater gift. (Guy who was trying to
figure out what to give his wife for their 50^th^ Anniversary)

Scientist J. Robert Oppenheimer once said "The best way to send an
idea is to wrap it up in a person." The sad truth is that many times
we spend enormous amounts of time and money trying to give "The best
gift" to those we love and care about, when what they really want is
us! Our time! Our attention! Our affection! They want to become a
stakeholder in our lives!

So, my advice to each of us is that in all our gift giving and getting,
let's not forget to open ourselves up to those around us and allow
others to do life with us.

THE BEST GIFT IN THEOLOGICAL TERMS

In Biblical terms, God did exactly what scientist Oppenheimer suggested
when He gave "The best gift," by wrapping Himself in flesh and blood,
and appearing as Jesus, in a manger in Bethlehem. The theological word
for what God did in giving "The Best Gift," is "incarnation."

Incarnation means, "in the flesh." Jesus was the incarnation of God.
Jesus was the way God sent "The best gift" to earth, and ultimately to
the human race. When man sinned in the Garden, God's response was that
He would send a sinless human to atone for the sins of man. Because the
sin man committed was a fleshly sin, it would require a flesh and blood
sacrifice to atone for the sins of the human race.

A little girl said, "Some people couldn\'t hear God\'s inside whisper,
so He sent Jesus to tell them out loud." That is what Christmas is all
about, God talking to us about His great love for us, and reminding us
that He put His money where His mouth is, and gave Himself in a flesh
and blood body, whom we know as Jesus!  

Notice three very important things:

- The Holy Ghost was to come upon Mary...

- The power of the Highest was to overshadow Mary...

- The Son of God would be the result of the arrangement between Heaven
 and earth...

Galatians 4:4 (KJV) "But when the fullness of time was come, God sent
forth his Son, made of a woman, made under the law."

The miracle of the incarnation of Christ is that He was 100% God and
100% man simultaneously.

Think of this:

- On His Mother's side He wept at the tomb of His friend Lazarus, on His
 Father's side He called Lazarus back to life...

- On His Mother's side it was necessary to maintain hydration, on His
 Father's side He offered water that would quench thirst for time and
 eternity...

- On His Mother's side He was thirty years old when He entered public
 ministry, on His Father's side He was present when the morning stars
 sang together and the sons of God shouted for joy... (Where was Jesus
 from 12-30?)

- On His Mother's side He was thirty-three and one-half years old, on
 His Father's side He was the Ancient of Days...

- On His Mother's side He died an early death, on His Father's side He
 abolished death...

- On His Mother's side He was buried in a borrowed tomb, on His Father's
 side He is the resurrection and the life...

- On His Mother's side He was subject to His parents, on His Father's
 side God gave Him a name above all other names...

- On His Mother's side He rode a donkey or walked everywhere He went. On
 His Father's side He rides on the wings of the wind...

- On His Mother's side He used a candle to dispel the darkness of night,
 on His Father's side He lit the world three days before He created the
 sun, moon and stars...

- On His Mother's side He could not light one room at a time, on His
 Father's side He will be the light of a city that is built two
 thousand miles square...

- On His Mother's side He could only be in one place at a time, on His
 Father's side He is omnipresent...

- On His Mother's side He had to rely upon the facts He could see, on
 His Father's side He is omniscient...

- On His Mother's side He broke under the weight of the cross, on His
 Father's side He is omnipotent...

- On His Mother's side He was a lamb led to His own slaughter, on His
 Father's side He was the Lion of the Tribe of Judah...

- On His Mother's side He did not open His mouth in His own defense, on
 His Father's side He possessed the power to call twelve legions of
 angels to His own rescue...

- On His Mother's side He tolerated injustice and aggression, on His
 Father's side He treads out the wine press of the wrath of God and
 breaks mighty armies across His knees...

- On His Mother's side He was Jesus, Mary's boy, on His Father's side He
 is King of Kings and Lord of Lords...

- On His Mother's side He never had his name in lights, on His Father's
 side He will reign forever...

- On His Mother's side He was never elected to high office, on His
 Father's side He will rule over Heaven and Earth...

- On His Mother's side He was of no reputation, on His Father's side
 every knee shall bow and every tongue shall confess He is Lord to the
 glory of God the Father...

THE BEST GIFT IN PRACTICAL TERMS

I want us to focus today on "The best gift" ever given -- God became
Jesus and lived among us!

What does that really look like in practical terms?

The ultimate in human impossibility looked like this:

- God\'s Son became Mary's Baby...

- A child was born, but a son man was given....

- The Lion of the Tribe of Judah became a lamb slain from the foundation
 of the world... (From everlasting to everlasting -- Eternal Priesthood
 after the order -- Before Abraham was, I am)

- An equal to God became a lowly servant of all mankind...

- The Ancient of Days became an infant born in Bethlehem's stable...

- The Omnipotent became a dependent life to be fed, clothed and
 nourished by human hands...

- He placed himself in the hands of man, that man might place himself in
 the hands of God... (Cast yourselves upon the mercies of God -- His
 mercies are new every morning)

- He became sin, so the sinful could be forgiven...

- He was bruised, so we could be healed...

- He endured turmoil, so we could have peace...

- He was rejected, so we could be accepted...

- He came to earth, so we could bring heaven to earth...

- He became hungry, so the hungry could be fed...

- He became thirsty, so the thirsty could be satisfied...

CONCLUSION

If we want to give "The best gift" this Christmas, there are two ways
to do it:

- Give ourselves to those closest to us...

- Give Jesus to everyone in our circle of influence...
