INTRO: We live in a generation which is enamored with having their
voice heard and their ideas taken seriously. Any leader who does not
recognize this is doomed to fail. The reason for this seems to be that
the younger generation is concerned that those in leadership are unaware
of the challenges of the future. It appears to them that many leaders
are asleep at the wheel, or at least intoxicated with the systems of the
past.

The expectations of this generation are much more demanding than some
other generations. Their need to be heard stems from several factors:

- They feel that their parent's generation has lost its courage.

- They believe their parent's generation has a consumer mentality rather
 than a pioneer mentality.

- They believe their parent's generation has charted a course which has
 a date with disaster.

- They believe their parent's generation has placed its loyalty in
 organizations which have lost their way.

In short, they believe being heard is a matter of life and death! For
them, being heard will have much to do with whether or not they have a
future to pass on to their children.

Leadership is not a sprint it is a relay race. Each leader is eternally
connected to their predecessor and successor. Anything I build should be
built on the shoulders of my predecessor and handed gracefully to my
successor. Leadership which operates otherwise is misguided and selfish.

How should followers go about having their voice heard by those who are
presently in authority? For young people who have a mantle of leadership
being developed in their lives, awaiting a time when they will be in
charge seems to be a never-ending process. Young people can have their
voices heard by following some very simple discipleship rules.

# ALLOW YOURSELF TO BE HUMBLED

In the story of Elijah and Elisha, the younger prophet knew the older
man of God would soon be taken away. He did not attempt to seize a
mantle which was not being offered; he humbled himself to become a
servant and patiently waited on a time when his opinion would be asked
for.

It is believed that Elisha faithfully served Elijah for approximately
ten years. During this ten-year period, Elisha:

- Mowed the grass

- Cleaned the restrooms

- Changed the church sign

- Locked the doors

- Turned the heat and air on and off

- Drove the man of God to the next meeting

- Carried water to the podium

- Ran the product table

I realize the list of things Elisha did was much different that the list
I just gave you, but you get the picture, he did whatever was necessary
to be mentored by the prophet.

A person placed in authority before traveling the road of humility, will
be required to travel a road of public humiliation. Every effective
leader must first be humbled. If a leader is not humbled, pride and
arrogance will destroy everything he builds.

One of my spiritual sons worked in a full-service gas station while
preparing for ministry. One day a person pulled up to the gas pump and
wanted gasoline, a window wash, the oil checked, and the tire pressures
checked. When the lady drove away my spiritual son was angry, and the
Lord spoke to him and said, "If you can't serve them, you cannot serve
me!"

Dave Thomas, the founder of the Wendy's fast food chain, said he
"Received his MBA (Masters in Business Administration) through Mop
Bucket Attitude before he got his GED!"

Elisha humbled himself before both God and Elijah and it paid off in
great dividends!

BE SUBMISSIVE TO THOSE IN AUTHORITY

When we are in the process of being mentored; when we are on the journey
of being raised up to our destiny; when we are waiting in the wings for
God's timing in placing us in a role to impact direction, we should
always keep in mind - we are not the authority. This is our turn to
submit to the authority God has set over us.

How many times do you think Elijah asked Elisha to do something that was
not necessarily Elisha's cup of tea or in his gifting? What are we to do
when this occurs? We are to submit and do the very best that we can for
our leader! We should do everything "... as unto the Lord!"

When Elijah called Elisha to follow him, he required him to leave
everything behind. Ten years later, when Elisha knew Elijah was going to
be taken away, it was not difficult for him to stay on the trail of the
man of God because he had been doing so for ten years.

Submission is not something you can pick up and lay down at will, it is
not something you do when you feel like it and when you don't; when you
agree and disagree; a follower is to submit to the leader in all cases
except when doing so requires a violation of the written Word of God.
(The dust of the Rabbi)

If we desire to be heard, we will submit to the leadership God has
chosen to place over us.

SERVE THE GENERATION ABOVE YOU

Why was Elisha chosen to receive Elijah's anointing rather than the
"heirs-apparent" -- the sons of the prophets? The answer: Because he
spent ten years serving the man of God.

Serving sounds easy enough, but it requires the following things:

- Time

- Effort

- Resources

- Love

- Patience

One cannot serve a leader without making the investment of time.

One cannot serve a leader without expending selfless effort.

One cannot serve a leader without being willing to commit substantial
resources to the purpose.

One cannot faithfully serve a leader they do not love.

One cannot be devoted to a purpose without exercising patience.

Elisha committed himself to these five things in an effort to serve
Elijah. He literally left everything behind for the purpose of serving
his leader!

Elisha served Elijah for ten years and when it came time to pass the
mantle of anointing. The coveted mantle did not go the heirs-apparent;
it went to the man who had served his leader. Elisha received exactly
what he asked for; he performed twice as many miracles as his mentor.
Service eventually pays off in a big way!

CONCLUSION:

William Jennings Bryan said, "Destiny is not a matter of chance, it is
a matter of choice. It is not a thing to be waited for; it is a thing to
be achieved." Our destiny will only be achieved through humility,
submission, and service.
