# PRAYERS THAT PREVAIL

FOR THE LOST

TEXT: Hosea 1:1-3 (NLT) "The [Lord]{.smallcaps} gave these
messages to Hosea son of Beeri during the years when Uzziah, Jotham,
Ahaz, and Hezekiah were kings of Judah, and Jeroboam son of Jehoash was
king of Israel. 2 When the [Lord]{.smallcaps} first began speaking to
Israel through Hosea, he said to him, "[Go and marry a
prostitute]{.underline}, so some of her children will be born to you
from other men. This will illustrate the way my people have been untrue
to me, openly committing adultery against the [Lord]{.smallcaps} by
worshiping other gods." 3 So Hosea married Gomer, the daughter of
Diblaim, and she became pregnant and gave Hosea a son."

In this series we will be learning how to pray for our loved ones more
effectively. In my opinion the book of Hosea gives some tremendous
lessons on this subject. This book is not at all the kind of plot you
would expect to find in the Bible. Hosea is a prophet to Israel and God
instructs him to marry a harlot!

I don't know about you but if God were to speak a thing like that to me
I would want to be absolutely sure I had my ears on correctly! I would
want to place some fleeces before the Lord and give Him ample
opportunity to straiten me out or change His mind!

Always remember, when God speaks a thing to us He has a perfectly good
reason for doing so. Isaiah 55:6-9 (NLT) "Seek the [Lord]{.smallcaps}
while you can find him. Call on him now while he is near. 7 Let the
people turn from their wicked deeds. Let them banish from their minds
the very thought of doing wrong! Let them turn to the [Lord]{.smallcaps}
that he may have mercy on them. Yes, turn to our God, for he will
abundantly pardon. 8 "[My thoughts are completely different from
yours]{.underline}," says the [Lord]{.smallcaps}. "And [my ways are far
beyond anything you could imagine]{.underline}. 9 For just as the
heavens are higher than the earth, so are [my ways higher than your ways
and my thoughts higher than your thoughts]{.underline}." Psalm 77:13
(CEV) "[Everything you do is right]{.underline}, and no other god
compares with you." Daniel repeats this same phrase in Daniel 9:7.

Even though His ways are superior to our ways and He never makes a
mistake, this set of instructions seems to fall into the unreasonable
category! This is without a doubt, unreasonable but He is oftentimes an
unreasonable God. God has proven Himself to be unreasonable in the
following ways:

- He was unreasonable about reconciling man to Himself!

- He was unreasonable regarding sending His Son Jesus!

- He was unreasonable concerning the sacrifice for sin!

- He will be unreasonable with regard to the judgment for sin!

God's instructions to Hosea are not just edgy they are completely off
the page!

Think of this:

- The Pastor's wife is a harlot!

- Can you imagine the dizzying volume of the rumor mill?

- Can you see her sashaying down the isle on Sunday morning dressed
 inappropriately?

- Can you imagine the caution of the other ladies with regard to their
 husband's relationship with the pastor and his family?

- Can you imagine the ridicule their children received?

Looking at this purely from a logical perspective it would appear that
nothing good could flow out of this relationship. Therefore, this surely
could not be the will of God! Try explaining that theological philosophy
to Abraham who was asked to take his son Isaac to the mountain!

Here God speaks to the prophet to marry this lady of ill repute! Hosea
1:2 (NLT) "When the [Lord]{.smallcaps} first began speaking to Israel
through Hosea, he said to him, "[Go and marry a
prostitute]{.underline}...""

What could God possibly be thinking? What good could come out of this?
How would He sustain Hosea's ministry? How could God get glory from the
obedience of the prophet in this scenario?

Always remember, every time God speaks He has a way designed for His
directives to turn out for the good. In this case He was going to use
the marriage relationship between the prophet and the prostitute to
teach the prophet what God desired to do to restore the relationship
between Jehovah and the wayward nation of Israel. Hosea 1:2 (NLT)
"When the [Lord]{.smallcaps} first began speaking to Israel through
Hosea, he said to him, "Go and marry a prostitute, so some of her
children will be born to you from other men. [This will illustrate the
way my people have been untrue to me, openly committing adultery against
the [Lord]{.smallcaps} by worshiping other gods]{.underline}."

As this book begins to unfold and the storyline becomes more apparent
the student begins to see the love of God for fallen man.

O love of God, how rich and pure! How measureless and strong! It shall

Make no mistake about it God's love calls upon His church to be obedient
to the Heavenly calling of winning others to Christ. John 3:16 (KJV)
"For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that
whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting
life."

In This story of the prophet and the prostitute we can gain some
wonderful insight into what we can do to free our loved ones from
Satan's power. Do you have family members or friends who are bound by
Satan's power? Would you like to see them freed? Would you like to be
able to participate in the process of their emancipation from Satan's
power?

Over the next few weeks I am going to give you some insights that the
Lord has revealed to me from the book of Hosea. I am confident these
truths will change the theory of your life as it pertains to your faith
for your family and friends, as well as your own personal activity with
regard to their release.

I believe the Word of God teaches the concept of household salvation.
Joshua 24:14-15 (NLT) "So honor the [Lord]{.smallcaps} and serve him
wholeheartedly. Put away forever the idols your ancestors worshiped when
they lived beyond the Euphrates River and in Egypt. Serve the
[Lord]{.smallcaps} alone. 15 But if you are unwilling to serve the
[Lord]{.smallcaps}, then choose today whom you will serve. Would you
prefer the gods your ancestors served beyond the Euphrates? Or will it
be the gods of the Amorites in whose land you now live? But [as for me
and my family, we will serve the [Lord]{.smallcaps}]{.underline}." At
times when my children have not been living like they should, I have
told both of them "Everyone is going to Heaven from this house; no-one
is going to hell from here."

Nelsons Bible Dictionary makes the following observation with regard to
the Biblical definition of a "Household": "All the people who lived
together in the same family in Bible times. Israelite families were
larger than ours today. The family included the immediate family and all
who lived together in the same house. This included slaves, concubines,
foreign residents, and servants. When Abraham circumcised all the males
in his household, he included his servants and slaves. The household and
their possessions moved as a unit. When Obed--Edom kept the ark, he and
his household were given a special blessing by David. In the New
Testament, entire households were sometimes baptized together."

We need to get our minds made up that everyone connected to us will be
going to Heaven and then work on developing a prayer life that reflects
our intentions!

It is my desire that every family connected to Christian Temple will
experience household salvation! I pray that your family circle will not
be broken on the Day of Judgment. I also want this to be a church whose
growth is primarily driven by salvations rather than transferees from
other churches.

If we are to accomplish these goals we must get serious about reaching
the lost. The first step to reaching the lost is to pray for the lost! I
want to remind us that the lost will not be saved unless they have been
prayed for! Ezekiel 22:29-31 (NKJV) "The people of the land have used
oppressions, committed robbery, and mistreated the poor and needy; and
they wrongfully oppress the stranger.30 "So I sought for a man among
them who would make a wall, and stand in the gap before Me on behalf of
the land, that I should not destroy it; but [I found no
one]{.underline}.31 "[Therefore]{.underline} I have [poured out My
indignation on them]{.underline}; I have consumed them with the fire of
My wrath; and I have recompensed their deeds on their own heads," says
the Lord [God]{.smallcaps}." 1 Timothy 2:1-4 (NLT) "I urge you,
first of all, to [pray for all people]{.underline}. As you make your
requests, plead for God's mercy upon them, and give thanks. 2 Pray this
way for kings and all others who are in authority, so that we can live
in peace and quietness, in godliness and dignity. 3 This is good and
pleases God our Savior, 4 for [he wants everyone to be saved and to
understand the truth]{.underline}."

We are not going to just pray for the lost we are going to build
ourselves a "Hit List" which includes the name of every person with
whom the people of this church are concerned. We will also keep a "Ten
Most Wanted List" which will be driven by the amount of times the
persons name appears on the request cards over the next four Sunday
evenings.

Do not place a persons name on a card unless you are willing to take
personal responsibility to pray for that individual everyday; agree to
do everything in your power to personally bring that individual to
Christ!
