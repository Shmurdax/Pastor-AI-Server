# THREE INDESPENSABLE ELEMENTS OF EFFECTIVENESS

INTRO: I think I could safely say that everyone in the room wants to
be effective as opposed to being ineffective. Even though most people
desire to be highly effective many of those people eventually settle for
mediocrity. I must ask myself the question what would cause a life full
of promise to settle for anything less than the best? I think several
factors contribute to a person settling for less than ultimate
effectiveness:

- Prayerlessness: If you are a person of prayer, you will pray through a
 lot of problems and a lot of false reports in your life. If you are a
 person who has to be required to have a prayer time you will
 eventually believe a false report, and will make a string of bad
 decisions that will render your ministry ineffective.

- A deficiency of the Word of God: The Word of God is not only our
 spiritual nourishment but is the roadmap to a highly effective life.
 If you are going somewhere you must follow the map; if you are going
 to follow the map you must first familiarize yourself with it.
 (Experience in Portland Oregon)

- Loss of faith in the dream: Everyone begins life with a dream. (I
 began life with a dream & at 43 I am actually living part of my dream)
 The loss of faith in the dream is sometimes due to a string of
 temporary setbacks; a lack of persistence; believing the lie of the
 enemy.

I think it is a tragedy for a life full of promise to settle for
ineffectiveness or mediocrity.

There are two things people will never forgive you for:

- Success: If you succeed the general public will be uncomfortable with
 you because it points up their ineffectiveness or mediocrity. When you
 begin to experience success people will line up around the block in an
 attempt to bring you back down to their level. If you continue to make
 a move toward effectiveness many of your friends will begin to
 distance themselves from you.

- Failure: If you fail they will be like piranhas attacking the prey.
 (Experience with chickens on the farm) Most people mask their own
 unhappiness with their unfulfilled dreams by tearing other people
 down.

In my opinion the very idea of being average should make everyone in
this room extremely uncomfortable. If you can be happy being mediocre or
ineffective you should submit a resignation today, you are in the wrong
place.

The question should be asked what is the difference between being
effective, ineffective, or average?

## I. LISTENING: The subject I am speaking on today is extremely
important to the overall goal, which is effective ministry. I want to
look at three aspects of listening.

- Listen to God: When speaking to the seven churches of Asia in the book
 of Revelation the Lord said "... he that hath an ear, let him hear
 what the Spirit is saying to the churches." If you are going to
 have any measure of effectiveness you must train the ear of your
 Spirit not only to hear what God is saying but also to be able to
 sense when He is wanting to say something. A major component of
 listening to God is sensitivity. During your private prayer time don't
 fill the entire time with your words, set aside some time to listen to
 what God is saying.

- Listen to those closest to you: Don't go through life barking orders,
 learn to listen to some trusted advisers. The Scripture say's there is
 "...wisdom in a multitude of counselors". Rather than
 intimidating those around you, release them to tell you the truth.
 Sometimes the truth can be somewhat painful. Even though the truth can
 cut pretty deep it is capable of making you better, thus more
 effective.

- Listen to what others are saying about you: Believe it or not,
 everyone who steps into a leadership role will attract detractors. As
 tempted as you will be to do so you cannot totally discount what your
 detractors have to say about you. If you take everything they say to
 heart you will be so discouraged you will not be able to continue in
 ministry; on the other hand, you must always remember there is an
 element of truth to what they are saying or it would be totally
 unbelievable. In such cases you must extract the truth, evaluate it,
 and improve your serve as a result of that analysis.

If you train yourself to listen to God, listen to those closest to you,
and listen to others you will continually be in possession of enough
truth about yourself that a personal growth plan will automatically
emerge.

Let me ask you a question; are you more inclined to talk or listen?
Those who lead are often called on to speak; give a statement; asked for
an opinion; give direction; give advice; the amount of time required for
listening will be determined by the amount of time required in speaking!
The biggest six letter word in the vocabulary of an effective person is
LISTEN!

## II. ACCOUNTABILITY: For some people accountability is a difficult
word to pronounce, and even more difficult to accept. The only reason I
know of that a person would reject accountability is because they have
something to hide.

Accountability is the friend of effectiveness. The more accountability
you set up in your life, the better the chances of effectiveness.
Someone once said "Power corrupts, and absolute power absolutely
corrupts." Those of us who are in leadership positions need to be
accountable in the following ways:

- Accountable to God! Never forget "There is an all seeing eye watching
 you."

- Accountable to your mate and family! If you fail to be accountable in
 this area your effectiveness will never be what it could have been.
 God created the family to produce and facilitate the human race. In
 addition to those things the family was established to create close
 fellowship between human beings, and provide a very natural system of
 accountability.

- Accountable to others! Government, laws, church, and organization you
 work for. (Song: When we all pull together) Everything you do affects
 me; everything I do affects you; you are my business. (Achan)

The lack of accountability often restricts effectiveness, and keeps
goals from being realized. 28 years ago Jean Nidetch was an overweight
homemaker desperate to lose weight. She had tried almost everything with
very little success. After two months of attempting a diet prescribed by
her doctor, she still needed to lose in excess of 50 pounds. Her
desperation caused her to call on a real life experience from her
childhood. She remembered walking through a park as a teenager and
seeing children setting in swings as their mothers were huddled together
gossiping. She would give the children a push and they would use their
effort to do the rest. Jean assembled a group of six overweight friends
and together they accomplished their goal. Now 28 years later more than
one million people meet in 25 countries of the world in what has become
known as Weight Watchers meetings.

Accountability is not your enemy it is your friend. In fact: It is one
of the few keys to your effectiveness.

## III. CREATIVITY

God has gifted each one of us in a very unique way. His gifting is not a
random act of kindness, but a gift of purpose. The Scriptures tell us
that we were "Fearfully and wonderfully made"; that verse means
that we were "thoughtfully and reverently made".

In the parable of the talents everyone had at least one talent. Everyone
has a gift; some have more than others! Everyone has more than they
initially know!

It is my opinion that with the gifting God has placed within each of us
comes with a fringe benefit of creativity. Some people never find a way
to release their creativity and therefore live a very frustrated life.
Creativity is the voice of the gift; it is the action component of the
gift; it is the method of expression without which the gift will remain
virtually silent. A gift without creativity equals a life without
purpose.

When you allow your gift to begin to express itself with creativity it
will develop a ministry with meaning. Someone said a persons "Gift
will make a place for them". Undiscovered talent is often revealed
through the release of creativity.

Massive amounts of incubation time are required in prayer, meditation,
and planning before the gift can give birth to creativity. (It is like a
chicken sitting on eggs in her nest -- A pregnant woman)

The gift alone will not open doors for you and will not allow for
effective ministry. Effective ministry is initiated when the gift begins
to speak out through creativity. Creativity will only manifest itself
when all the other ingredients are in place; and only when the recipient
of the gift steps up to the plate and overcomes the fear of failure and
begins to implement the ideas God has placed within their hearts.

Why do you suppose God cared enough to uniquely gift each of us? He had
a purpose! Are you on your way to fulfilling that purpose? If not, why
not? I release you in the name of Jesus to operate in your giftedness
and to begin to create things that will have value to this church and to
the Kingdom of God.

History reveals that most things of real value were invented by the most
unlikely of people.

- Thomas Edison invented the light bulb!

- Henry Ford invented the automobile!

- The Wright brothers gave us the gift of aviation!

None of these people succeeded on their first attempt!

If at first you don't succeed, try, try again! When you fail, evaluate
your actions keep the good and rid yourself of the bad! I did not quit
eating watermelon just because it has seeds! I eat the watermelon and
spit out the seeds!

I am convinced that no-one in this room knows what they are really
capable of; God will reveal it little by little as we release
creativity, and overcome the fear of failure.
