Though we are people of faith, we realize and readily accept the fact
that there are many things about our system of religion that cannot be
explained or accepted through logical thinking. There are many paradoxes
that populate the Bible, which is the book we accept as our rule of
faith and practice.

I thought a paradox was two Doctor's! As it turns out, A Paradox is
defined as "A statement that is seemingly contradictory or opposed to
common sense and yet is, perhaps, true." (Webster) The Word of God has
many paradoxes, yet nowhere in Scripture is the contrast between heaven
and earth more evident than in the Christmas story.  Luke takes us from
the power and fame of Caesar to the weakness and obscurity of a baby in
a stable, and then declares the baby in a manger to be a superior to
Caesar.

While one of the greatest events in the history of the planet is
occurring, the world was paying no attention to what was going on in the
manger. For sure, God was paying attention, and Dr. Luke gives a vivid
snapshot of the events surrounding this wonderful story.

 

Paul speaks of this great paradox in this way, "For you know the
grace of our Lord Jesus Christ, that, [though He was rich, yet for your
sakes He became poor]{.underline}, that you [through His poverty might
become rich]{.underline}." Talk about role reversals. This is costly
to God but beneficial to us!

## I. JESUS EXPERIENCED A HUMAN BIRTH SO THAT WE COULD EXPERIENCE A
SPIRITUAL BIRTH...

Luke 2:4- 7 (NKJV) "Joseph also went up from Galilee, out of the city
of Nazareth, into Judea, to the city of David, which is called
Bethlehem, because he was of the house and lineage of David, to be
registered with Mary, his betrothed wife, who was with child. So it
was, that while they were there, the days were completed for her to be
delivered. And [she brought forth her firstborn Son]{.underline}, and
wrapped Him in swaddling cloths, and laid Him in a manger, because there
was no room for them in the inn." Think of it, God becoming a human
being. This is a plan so complex that it boggles the human mind, yet it
is a plan so simple that the person with the lowest IQ can understand
it.

The thought of God becoming one of us is absolutely stunning to the
human mind. If God becomes a human being He voluntarily accepts all the
weaknesses, restrictions, and limitations experienced by mere mortals.
How could a God so powerful that He constructed the world, created human
life, and spoke the planets into place, condescend to become one of His
own creatures? Yet, Doctor Luke describes such an event with great
detail, right down to the: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Date and time... Tax time!

- City... Bethlehem!

- Place... A manger!

- Clothing... Swaddling clothes!

If God were going to do such a thing as to become a human being would He
not be born to a royal family in a palace? Luke tells us He was born
into the family of a carpenter and a peasant woman who would raise Him
in the small out-of-the-way place called Nazareth. This in-and-of-itself
is a paradox so astounding that it overwhelms the human imagination. If
you were writing a movie script for God to become one of us, never in a
million years would you imagine such a thing.

What does it mean for God to experience the birth and life of a human
being? Man was created perfect and placed in a perfect world called the
Garden of Eden. As perfect as his world was, mankind found a way to mess
up his own world. Adam and Eve sinned, thus offending the holiness of
God. As soon as Adam and Eve sinned, God unveiled a plan of
reconciliation for a fallen race. Genesis 3:15 (NKJV) "And I will put
enmity Between you and the woman, And between your seed and her Seed;
[He shall bruise your head]{.underline}, And you [shall bruise His
heel]{.underline}."

The only way sin could be atoned for was the sacrifice of a perfect
human being. Wait a minute -- a perfect human being? How could such a
thing ever come to pass? From the time Adam and Eve sinned, decades and
centuries came and went, yet not one human being could rise to the point
of perfection in order to qualify as the sacrifice for sin. Romans 3:23
(NKJV) "For [all have sinned]{.underline} and fall short of the glory
of God."

In the Old Testament, the only way a sacrifice could satisfy a Holy God
who had been offended by sin, was for that sacrifice to be spotless,
blameless, and perfect. Leviticus 1:1-3 (NKJV) "Now the

[Lord]{.smallcaps}."

In order to be the supreme sacrifice, this God-man would need to take

0.78 miles per household, a total trip of 71,604,000 miles, not counting

fastest man-made vehicle on earth, the Ulysses space probe, moves at a

miles per hour.

The payload on the sleigh adds another interesting element. Assuming
that each child gets nothing more than a medium-sized Lego set (2
pounds), the sleigh is carrying 321,300 tons or 642,600,000 pounds, not
counting Santa, who is invariably described as overweight. On land,
conventional reindeer can pull no more than 300 pounds. Even granting
that "flying reindeer" (see point #1) could pull TEN TIMES the normal
amount, the job cannot be done with eight, or even nine reindeer, Santa
will need 214,200 reindeer. This increases the payload - not even
counting the weight of the sleigh - to 706,860,000 pounds. Again, for
comparison - this is four times the weight of the Queen Elizabeth.

706,860,000 pounds traveling at 650 miles per second creates enormous
air resistance - this will heat the reindeer up in the same fashion as

[energy](http://www.short-funny-stories.com/funnystories/s-funny-christmas-stories/funny-christmas-stories-08.html)
per second each. In short, they will burst into flame almost
instantaneously, exposing the reindeer behind them, and create deafening
sonic booms in their wake. The entire reindeer team will be vaporized
within 4.26 thousandths of a second. Santa, meanwhile, will be subjected

[gravity](http://www.short-funny-stories.com/funnystories/s-funny-christmas-stories/funny-christmas-stories-08.html).
A 250-pound Santa (which seems ludicrously slim) would be pinned to the
