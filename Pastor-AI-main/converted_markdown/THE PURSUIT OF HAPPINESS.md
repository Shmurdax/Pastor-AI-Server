## I. HAPPYNESS DEFINED...

Galatians 5:22 (NKJV) "But the fruit of the Spirit is love,
[joy]{.underline}, peace, longsuffering, kindness, goodness,
faithfulness, 23 gentleness, self-control. Against such there is no
law." The topic being discussed here is the joy of the Lord. The word
joy comes from the Greek word Chara (car-a) which means:

- Inner gladness...

- Deep seated pleasure...

Joy as it is used here is a depth of assurance and confidence that
ignites a cheerful, rejoicing heart. A cheerful heart leads to cheerful,
rejoicing behavior!

This kind of life and behavior brings with it health and completeness in
life. Proverbs 15:13 (NKJV) "A merry heart makes a cheerful
countenance, But by sorrow of the heart the spirit is broken."

Happyness is revealed:

- In our words... If we are happy we will speak happy words...

- In our conduct... If we are happy we will do happy deeds...

- In our lifestyle... If we are happy it will manifest itself in every
 area of our life...

- In our countenance... If we are happy we should notify our face...

Happyness is not something we stumble upon by accident; it is something
we should pursue with every fiber of our beings. Happyness cannot be:

- Bought...

- Sold...

- Traded...

Happyness can only come from the Lord and must be acquired through a
personal, intimate relationship with Him. Every person has a God shaped
hole in their spiritual make-up and this hole can only be filled by
Jesus.

Proverbs 17:22 (NKJV) "A merry heart does good, like medicine, But a
broken spirit dries the bones." Happyness is capable of causing our
health to be restored. A broken spirit manifests itself through broken
health and many other maladies:

- Grief...

- Hatred...

- Malice...

- Anxiety...

- Bad tempers...

- Continual worry...

- Negative thinking...

These things do not promote happyness they promote a broken spirit which
brings with it an array of health and relational issues. Money can buy
medicine but not health. Money can buy momentary joy but not happyness.
(Shop until you drop... A woman's place is in the mall... Ecclesiastes -
The children gather the wood, the fathers kindle the fire, the women
knead (need) their dough...)

It is a scientific fact that happy people are healthier than unhappy
people. Being happy is simply more fun than being unhappy. Almost
everything we do is done with the hope of experiencing happyness.

Happyness brings with it strength. When the people in Nehemiah's day
finished the task of rebuilding the walls and gates of Jerusalem, the
people discovered that the strength to do such a thing began with a
little word called joy. Nehemiah 8:10 (NKJV) "Then he said to them,
"Go your way, eat the fat, drink the sweet, and send portions to those
for whom nothing is prepared; for this day is holy to our
[Lord]{.smallcaps}. Do not sorrow, for the joy of the [Lord]{.smallcaps}
is your strength."

In a very real sense the pursuit of happyness is the pursuit of the
strength of the Lord. The joy of the Lord is in the truest sense of the
word:

- Spiritual strength...

- Physical strength...

- Relational strength...

Happyness is a state of mind and we are entitled to it as Christians.
The pursuit of happyness is well worth the effort.

## II. HAPPYNESS THE WORLD'S WAY...

It must be pointed out; there is a vast difference between the joy of
the Lord and the joy of the world. The happyness of the world is driven
by an excitement brought on by an emotional high. The joy of the world
is always followed by:

- A nagging sense of incompleteness...

- A feeling that something is lacking...

- A feeling that there is missing ingredients...

- A feeling that something has been left unfulfilled...

- A feeling that a piece of the puzzle has been misplaced...

The happyness which the world offers lack's the ability to deliver a
feeling of completion. Somehow there is not a complete sense of
assurance, confidence or satisfaction. The most complete sense of joy
the world can offer leaves one feeling empty on the inside.

The joy the world offers leaves behind it, a constant awareness that
something can, and probably will go wrong. With this world's happyness
we are constantly aware that circumstances can change or some situation
can arise and disturb our happyness. The joy of the world is temporary,
at its best. The things which affect the joy of the world are
exhaustive, but some of the most common interruptions to the peace which
the world offers are:

- Sickness...

- The death of a loved one...

- A sudden financial reversal...

- The loss of secure employment...

- The onslaught of family problems...

If a person is living on the strength of the happyness this world
offers, when these things develop, their happyness is short lived.
Living by this worlds system can cause a persons life to emulate a
roller-coaster, experiencing exhilarating highs one day and
self-defeating lows the next. A person living on this emotional plane
can jump the moon with a single bound today, and tomorrow they can stand
up-right and walk under a snake's belly.

Who would want to live on such an emotional see-saw? Even if this joy
were consistent it is incomplete. It can never fulfill the individual;
as a result, the world is in constant need of things and accomplishments
to keep their happyness going.

## III. HAPPYNESS GOD'S WAY...

I would like to now focus on the joy available to the believer. Some
very important facts about the joy of the lord are:

- This joy is divine...

- This joy is given by God...

- This joy can only be acquired by true believers...

- This joy does not depend on circumstances as its source...

The joy of the Lord has nothing to do with earthly or material things;
it never rides on cheap triumphs; its source is deep seated peace, which
flows out of a life lived according to the path which the Spirit has
chosen. Romans 14:17 (NKJV) "For the kingdom of God is not eating and
drinking, but righteousness and peace and [joy in the Holy
Spirit]{.underline}." 1 Thessalonians 1:6 (NKJV) "And you became
followers of us and of the Lord, having received the word in much
affliction, with [joy of the Holy Spirit]{.underline}."

## IV. ASSIMILATING HAPPYNESS INTO THE LIFE OF THE BELIEVER...

The joy of the Lord is not dependent on the circumstances of the life of
the believer; it is a joy which transcends circumstances and can endure
in good times as well as difficult times.

The happyness of the world depends upon certain events coming to pass in
ones life, but the joy which God imparts to the believer overrides all
things, even matters of life and death. Psalms 5:11-12 (NKJV) "But
let all those [rejoice]{.underline} who put their trust in You; Let them
ever [shout for joy]{.underline}, because You defend them; Let those
also who love Your name Be joyful in You. 12 For You, O Lord, will bless
the righteous; With favor You will surround him as with a shield." 2
Corinthians 7:4 (NKJV) "Great is my boldness of speech toward you,
great is my boasting on your behalf. [I am filled with
comfort]{.underline}. [I am exceedingly joyful in all our
tribulation]{.underline}."

The reason the happyness which God imparts is able to rise above the
circumstances is because it has as its seed, faith. Romans 15:13 (NKJV)
"Great is my boldness of speech toward you, great is my boasting on
your behalf. [I am filled with comfort. I am exceedingly joyful in all
our tribulation]{.underline}." Philippians 1:25 (NKJV) "And being
confident of this, I know that I shall remain and continue with you all
for your progress and [joy of faith]{.underline}."

The believer has a responsibility in the process of the assimilation of
happyness into our lives. Two major responsibilities the believer faces:

- Controlling our thought life... Philippians 4:8 (KJV) "Finally,
 brethren, whatever things are true, whatever things are noble,
 whatever things are just, whatever things are pure, whatever things
 are lovely, whatever things are of good report, if there is any virtue
 and if there is anything praiseworthy\--meditate on these things."
 It is the responsibility of each believer to control our own thought
 life. God is not going to do it for us it is our responsibility to set
 a guard at the door of our mind! My mother used to say, "You can't
 keep a bird from flying over your head, but you can keep him from
 building a nest in your hair!"

- Guarding the words of our lips... Proverbs 18:21 (NKJV) "Death and
 life are in the power of the tongue..."

The future reward of a life filled with happyness keeps one faithful.
Acts 20:24 (NKJV) "But none of these things move me; nor do I count
my life dear to myself, so that I may finish my race with
[joy]{.underline}, and the ministry which I received from the Lord
Jesus, to testify to the gospel of the grace of God." Hebrews 12:1-2
(NKJV) "Therefore we also, since we are surrounded by so great a
cloud of witnesses, let us lay aside every weight, and the sin which so
easily ensnares us, and let us run with endurance the race that is set
before us, 2 looking unto Jesus, the author and finisher of our faith,
who [for the joy that was set before Him]{.underline} endured the cross,
despising the shame, and has sat down at the right hand of the throne of
God."

CONCLUSION:

Every spirit filled believer should be living a life filled with
happyness. This happyness should manifest itself in our life and walk.
If we will allow the Holy Spirit He can cause us to live a life of
happyness, even in times when things may be less than favorable. This
walk of happyness should be a tremendous witness to all who do not have
it. Unlike Will Smith, this happyness is not dependent on wealth and
human relationships.
