# GOD WAS WITH HIM

(Acts 10:34-38 NKJV)

"34 ¶ Then Peter opened \[his\] mouth and said: "In truth I perceive
that God shows no partiality. 35 "But in every nation whoever fears Him
and works righteousness is accepted by Him. 36 "The word which \[God\]
sent to the children of Israel, preaching peace through Jesus
Christ----He is Lord of all---- 37 "that word you know, which was
proclaimed throughout all Judea, and began from Galilee after the
baptism which John preached: 38 "how God anointed Jesus of Nazareth
with the Holy Spirit and with power, who went about doing good and
healing all who were oppressed by the devil, for God was with Him."

INTRO: This is part of Peter's sermon in the house of Cornelius the
Centurion. Verse 38 is in the very heart of the sermon. It is a summary
of the life of Jesus and is beautiful and brief. Look at it again...

(Acts 10:38 NKJV)

""how God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Spirit and with
power, who went about doing good and healing all who were oppressed by
the devil, for God was with Him."

Let's look at this verse this morning and in so doing we look at the
life of Jesus Christ.

## I. [ANOINTED BY GOD WITH THE HOLY SPIRIT]{.underline}

A. Peter is making it very clear that Jesus did not perform his miracles
in the power of his innate deity.

B. Jesus emptied Himself of his deity when he was born of Mary, and
lived His whole life as a man... FULL OF THE HOLY SPIRIT...

(Philippians 2:5-8 NKJV)

"5 Let this mind be in you which was also in Christ Jesus, 6 who, being
in the form of God, did not consider it robbery to be equal with God, 7
but made Himself of no reputation, taking the form of a bondservant,
\[and\] coming in the likeness of men. 8 And being found in appearance
as a man, He humbled Himself and became obedient to \[the point of\]
death, even the death of the cross."

## C. If Jesus met his temptations and did his mighty works as God, his
incarnation loses its meaning for us.

D. The power of the Spirit in Jesus' life was released in response to
the PRAYER OF FAITH. Where there was NO FAITH... there were no mighty
deeds. Where there was faith, EVERYTHING WAS POSSIBLE.

"Now He did not do many mighty works there because of their unbelief."
(Matthew 13:58 NKJV)

E. Jesus was Anointed by God with the Holy Sprit... He was FULL of the
Holy Spirit.

## II. [HE WENT ABOUT DOING GOOD]{.underline}

A. Jesus was totally unselfish in everything he did and said... ""just
as the Son of Man did not come to be served, but to serve, and to give
His life a ransom for many."" (Matthew 20:28 NKJV)

B. Jesus CARED. He believed that good needed to be done. He did not
ignore sin, sickness and suffering...

ILL: "Jesus Christ Superstar"- Shows a tired disgusted Jesus who is
trying to get away from the sick people who are constantly coming to
Him.

## C. Jesus was convinced that good could be done. Sin is an invader in
God's world. It is not a permanent part of the divine plan. The popular
view of sin is that it is the natural order of things and nothing can be
done about it. Christ did not view sin as such... He said... "Thy
Kingdom come... Thy will be done... IN EARTH as it is in heaven..."

D. Jesus believed that man was lost, but he could be saved... sick but
could be healed... oppressed of the devil, but could be delivered...

This is only possible through our cooperation with God. We will never
DRIFT into salvation.

With this conviction, Jesus left the carpenter's shop and went about
DOING GOOD...

## III. [AND HEALING ALL...]{.underline}

A. Peter zeros in on one way Jesus did go "... HEALING ALL that were
oppressed of the devil."

B. The mighty deeds of Jesus were one of the thrilling joys of the early
saints. They gloried in them. They saw in them unmistakable evidences
that the life of heaven was already beginning on earth.

CONC: [GOD WAS WITH HIM]{.underline}

There are those who reject the miracles altogether. They believe nature
is a rigid affair, a closed system, where only physical causes can bring
about physical effects. MIRACLES ARE IMPOSSIBLE.

There are those who accept the miracles, but only as evidences of the
unique deity of Jesus. They believe Jesus did miracles to prove He was
divine and having done that, they are no longer useful.,,

1\) The writers of the New Testament do not take this view.

2\) Jesus never once worked a miracle just for display. When asked for a
sign He answered, "A wicked and adulterous generation seeketh after a
sign."

3\) Just when did the miracles cease? Not when Jesus died (Look at the
book of Acts).

4\) If power to do the impossible was needed 2000 years ago, HOW MUCH
MORE TODAY?

GOD WAS WITH HIM...

GOD IS WITH US...

May it be said of us... "...God anointed him/her with the Holy Spirit
and with power, who went about doing good and healing all who were
oppressed by the devil, for God was with Him."
