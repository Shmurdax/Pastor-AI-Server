INTRO: Most people, who call themselves Christian, believe in the
person of the Holy Spirit, at least in some limited dimension. The
extent of these beliefs has made for many interesting conversations as
well as quite a large number of heated religious debates.

Following conversion, those who have received the infilling of the Holy
Spirit as a second definite work of grace are widely known as
Pentecostal or Charismatic.

It is estimated that Pentecostals/Charasmatics world-wide number more
that 500,000,000!

To begin with, I want to point out what I believe to be several general
Scriptural facts concerning the person of the Holy Spirit: (PLACE ON
THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The Holy Spirit is the third person of the Holy trinity

- The Holy Spirit works in unity with the Father and the Son (The best
 example is that of husband and wife, they are two people yet they work
 together as one)

- The Holy Spirit is the power behind the implementation of Gods purpose
 in the earth

Before we decide to write the Holy Spirit off as unimportant or
insignificant we should consider the words of Jesus as it relates to the
importance of the Holy Spirit. John 16:7 (KJV) "Nevertheless I tell
you the truth; [It is expedient for you]{.underline} that I go away: for
[if I go not away, the Comforter will not come]{.underline} unto you;
but [if I depart, I will send him unto you]{.underline}." In this
Scripture, Jesus is stressing to His disciples the necessity of His
departure. As long as He lived in a flesh and blood body He could only
be in one place at a time. He is attempting to explain to them that the
reason it was so necessary for Him to go to the Father was so that the
Holy Spirit would come and live in each believer, thus empowering each
believer to carry on the work of Christ in the world. If Jesus thought
it necessary to depart so that the Holy Spirit could indwell and empower
believers, where do we get off promoting the Holy Spirit as optional
equipment?

I want us to look at seven Scriptural reasons for being filled with the
Spiri.

## I. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE WE HAVE BEEN COMMANDED TO
DO SO!

No doubt, every believer would agree that to disobey one of the Ten
Commandments would be to sin. What I am about to say will more than
likely be viewed as a very narrow interpretation of Scripture. I believe
all disobedience is sin.

We are commanded in Scripture to be filled with the Spirit. (LEAVE ON
THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) Paul said to the Ephesians "And
be not drunk with wine, wherein is excess; but [be filled with the
Spirit]{.underline};" (Eph. 5:18)

Listen to what Jesus has to say on the subject! Luke 24:49 (NKJV)
"Behold, I send the Promise of My Father upon you; but [tarry in the
city of Jerusalem until you are endued with power from on
high]{.underline}."

## II. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE HAS BEEN APPOINTED AS
OUR TEACHER!

In our lifetime all of us have had many teachers. Very few of us recall
specific profound lessons our teachers taught us but we do remember the
lessons they taught us through the relationships they built with us!

Teachers oftentimes become mentors to their students! Jesus tells us
that by design, the Holy Spirit is to become the instructor of each
believer! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) John 14:26
(NKJV) "But the Helper, the Holy Spirit, whom the Father will send in
My name, [He will teach you all things]{.underline}, and bring to your
remembrance all things that I said to you." When we receive the
infilling of the Holy Spirit we receive a heightened awareness of the
will of God, as well as a heightened sensitivity to the voice of the
Holy Spirit.

## III. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE HAS BEEN APPOINTED
AS OUR GUIDE!

A guide is defined as one who knows the way, usually because they have
already been there. If I am going to follow a guide, I want to know that
they know what they are doing and where they are going!

There is a terrific difference between a pioneer and a guide. A pioneer
is blazing a trail in hopes of developing a path for others to follow. A
guide is one who already knows the way and leads those who are less
knowledgeable along the path. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT
SLIDE)

John 16:12-15 (NKJV) "I still have many things to say to you, but you
cannot bear them now.13 "However, when He, the Spirit of truth, has
come, [He will guide you into all truth]{.underline}; for He will not
speak on His own authority, but whatever He hears He will speak; and [He
will tell you things to come]{.underline}.14 "He will glorify Me, for
[He will take of what is Mine and declare it to you]{.underline}.15 "All
things that the Father has are Mine. Therefore I said that [He will take
of Mine and declare it to you]{.underline}." The work of the Holy
Spirit in the life of the believer is that of revelation. He has no
agenda of His own; He is simply a guide who discovers what the Father
desires in our lives and reveals the path of travel to each believer.

## IV. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE EMPOWERS US FOR
SERVICE!

It is my opinion that all who have received Jesus as Lord and Savior
will have a desire to serve Him and others. In the Old Testament the
priest would anoint his right ear, right thumb and right great toe. This
symbolized: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Having the mind of Christ...

- Doing the work of Christ...

- Walking the walk of Christ...

The three things every believer must do in order to please God is to:
(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Have the mind of Christ!

- Do the work of Christ!

- Walk the walk of Christ!

Serving strictly from duty or obligation runs a little thin pretty
quick. If we are to serve effectively over the long haul of our
Christian lives we must operate in the power of the Spirit, rather than
functioning within our own abilities! Acts 1:8 (NKJV) "But [you shall
receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you]{.underline}; and
you shall be witnesses to Me in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria,
and to the end of the earth."

## V. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE WILL PRAY THROUGH US
WHEN WE DO NOT KNOW WHAT TO PRAY FOR! One of the works of the Holy
Spirit in the life of the believer is to enhance and make more effective
his or her prayer life. Romans 8:26-27 (NKJV) "Likewise the Spirit
also helps in our weaknesses. For we do not know what we should pray for
as we ought, but the Spirit Himself makes intercession for us [with
groanings which cannot be uttered]{.underline}.27 Now He who searches
the hearts knows what the mind of the Spirit is, because [He makes
intercession for the saints according to the will of
God]{.underline}."

Think of this: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- When we do not know what to pray for, the Holy Spirit knows exactly
 what to pray for!

- When we do not know how to pray because we do not know what the will
 of the Father is, the Holy Spirit is never in question about the will
 of God!

- When we have exhausted our vocabulary, the Spirit has not exhausted
 His!

## VI. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE IS TO BE OUR
COMFORTER! John 15:26 (KJV) "But when the [Comforter]{.underline}
is come, whom I will send unto you from the Father, even the Spirit of
truth, which proceedeth from the Father, he shall testify of me:" On
numerous occasions in the Bible, the Holy Spirit is referred to as the
comforter. Because of the times we are living in, when we use the word
comforter, all kinds of images begin to manifest themselves, most of
which do not lend themselves to a true assessment of what is being
portrayed in Scripture. The image we come up with is probably a person
who is weak and frail but willing to sympathize with our every need and
concern. We might even visualize a weak frail person who is content to
hang around a funeral home and cry with everyone who comes through the
door.

When the Scripture uses the word comforter there is an attempt to say
something far more significant.

The Greek word for Comforter is Paraklete which means: (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- One called alongside for the purpose of assistance (Like a big
 brother)

- One who pleads another\'s cause before a judge (He is our legal
 representation)

- A counselor for the purpose of a defense (He is our defense lawyer
 when we stand accused) (Talk about pre-paid legal fees)

- A legal assistant (He researches all similar cases and furnishes the
 information to the judge)

- An intercessor (He is our most able advocate at Heavens court)

- A helper (Someone to assist in all situations)

## VII. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE CONVICTS US OF
SIN!

Most Christians would agree that a person cannot come to Christ apart
from the conviction of the Holy Spirit. John 6:44 (NKJV) "No one can
come to Me unless [the Father who sent Me draws him]{.underline}..."
How does the Father draw us? John 16:5-8 (LB) "But now I am going
away to the one who sent me; and none of you seems interested in the
purpose of my going; none wonders why. 6 Instead you are only filled
with sorrow. 7 But the fact of the matter is that it is best for you
that I go away, for if I don't, the Comforter won't come. If I do, he
will---for I will send him to you. 8 "And when he has come he will
[convince the world of its sin]{.underline}, and of the availability of
God's goodness, and of deliverance from judgment." A great portion
of the work of the Holy Spirit is to make the unrepentant sinner fully
aware of his hopeless eternal condition. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL
THE NEXT SLIDE)

Another aspect of the work of the Holy Spirit is to make Christians
aware of their failures and sins! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT
SLIDE) That's right, when we who are believers, fail, the Holy Spirit
becomes the still small voice inside us which causes us to feel guilty
concerning our sin. His purpose is not to make us feel like a failure
but to bring us to repentance.

Listen to the importance of walking in the Spirit of God each day of our
lives. Galatians 5:16 (NKJV) "I say then: Walk in the Spirit, and
[you shall not fulfill the lust of the flesh]{.underline}."
Galatians 5:24-25 (NKJV) "And [those who are Christ's have crucified
the flesh with its passions and desires]{.underline}.25 If we live in
the Spirit, let us also walk in the Spirit."
