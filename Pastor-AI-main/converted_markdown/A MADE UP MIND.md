In our text David had been rejected, and during his rejection, David
needed people who not only admired him at a distance and believed in him
secretly, but people who would come out boldly and follow him. As a
pastor I have been there when people said "All we need is someone to
stand up ..." When you do, you are often the only one who is! People
will say "We are behind you brother..." They are so far behind you;
they are only a faint speck in the rear view mirror! (Story of the guy
on a bicycle who pulled up beside the corvette at the stoplight and was

Pastors and church leaders greatly appreciate those who will stand with
them in difficult times! The reward of such people will be great in
Heaven. The loyal support of Gods people for His leaders does not escape
the attention of eternal reward.

David needed people who would not turn their backs on the day of battle!
This is exactly what our King is looking for today, people with a made

## I. TOTAL COMMITMENT

1 Chronicles 12:8 (KJV) "And of the Gadites there [separated
themselves]{.underline} unto David into the hold to the
wilderness...." Please remember, when these people made the
decision to support David and his effort, they could never retreat!
Retreat would not be tolerated; in fact, to do so would almost certainly
be a signature on their death warrant! This decision was a total
commitment to David and his cause!

The same thing happens to you and I; Christ followers have no real place
of retreat! Proverbs 13:15 (KJV) "Good understanding giveth favour:
but [the way of transgressors is hard]{.underline}." If one is going
to live a victorious life the best thing that we can do is to burn the
bridges of retreat behind us!

ILLUSTRATION: When Cortez landed at Vera Cruz in 1519 to begin his
conquest of Mexico, he had a small band of soldiers, which consisted of
about 700 men. Once he landed it is said that he purposely set fire to
his small fleet of 11 ships. His small army watched as their only means
of retreat sank to the bottom of the Gulf of Mexico. With the only means
of retreat destroyed, the only way to move was forward, into the Mexican
interior to meet whatever might come their way!

So it is with being a soldier in the army of God! If one is to be
effective in the Christian walk a total commitment to Christ and His
cause must be made in advance! 2 Timothy 2:4 (KJV) "No man that
warreth entangleth himself with the affairs of this life; [that he may
please him who hath chosen him to be a soldier]{.underline}."
Hebrews 13:12-14 (KJV) "Wherefore Jesus also, that he might sanctify
the people [with his own blood, suffered without the gate]{.underline}.
(13) [Let us go forth therefore unto him without the camp, bearing his
reproach]{.underline}. (14) For here have we no continuing city, but we
seek one to come."

We live in perilous times, and it is sad when those who are supposed to
be Soldiers in the army of God side with those who have rejected Christ
as king! It is most difficult in these times to ascertain the difference
between the world and the church on many fronts: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN
ONE AT A TIME)

- Political issues...

- Character issues...

- Lifestyle issues...

- Doctrinal issues...

As you read this chapter you will soon find out that David required men
of quality, selected, separated men! Men who had made an all-out
commitment to his cause and vision, men who would get the job done no
matter the difficulty! Are we ready to stand up to the world and sell
out to Jesus and His cause?

Are we totally committed to Christ to the point that we have already
determined that nothing can separate us from Him or His cause? WHO CAN
SEPARATE US FROM THE LOVE OF CHRIST?

## III. COST COUNTING

The warfare David's men were engaged in could prove to be very costly,
but they had decided that this was a cause worth dying for! 1 Chronicles
12:15 (KJV) "These are they that went over Jordan in the first
month, [when it had overflown all its banks]{.underline}: and they put
to flight all them of the valleys, both toward the east, and toward the
west." These were men of grit, gall, and backbone, crossing a river
at flood-stage to fight a battle that could mean their life!

What kind of hardship are we willing to endure in order to win this good
fight of faith? If we hope to be victorious, we must have our mind made
up to persevere despite the odds.

ILLUSTRATION: When James Calvert went out as a Missionary to the
cannibals of the Fiji Islands, the captain of the ship sought to turn
him back. "You will lose your life and the lives of those with you if
you go among such savages," he said. Calvert only replied, "We died
before we came..." God give us some more James Calvert's!

Have we...

- Counted the cost of friendships?

- Counted the cost of material possessions?

- Counted the cost of worldly popularity?

- Counted the cost of surrender?

- Counted the cost of submission to authority?

The cost of selling out to Jesus in pursuit of the victorious life can
be very high, but the juice is worth the squeeze! Are we willing to pay
the price of victory?

1 Chronicles 12:8 (KJV) "And of the Gadites there separated

battle]{.underline}, that could [handle shield and buckler]{.underline},
whose [faces were like the faces of lions]{.underline}, and were as
[swift as the roes upon the mountains]{.underline};"(21) And they
helped David against the band of the rovers: for they were all [mighty
men of valor]{.underline}, and were [captains in the
host]{.underline}."

These men were:

1. Men of strength... "Men of might..."

2. Men who were experienced fighters... "Men of war..."

3. Men who were battle ready... "Fit for battle..."

4. Men who knew how to get the most out of their weapons... "Handle
 shield and buckler..."

5. Men in whose faces only courage could be seen... "Faces were like
 the faces of lions..."

6. Men who were sure-footed in the day of battle... "Swift as roes
 (goats) upon the mountains..."

7. Men who were full of faith rather than fear... "Mighty men of
 valor...."

8. Men whose leadership skills had been tested and proven...
 "Captains in the host..."

The men who were following David were probably not physical specimens of
strength and power but they had their minds made up to stay the course
and support what they believed to be right. They were men whose total
reliance was upon God.

Let me remind you that none of us win the victory in and of ourselves!
We must have supernatural strength and anointing! When we walk in the
anointing, 300 men with pitchers and lanterns can defeat every imp in
hell, or Houston!

- The anointing will provide an ark for the floodwaters...

- The anointing will rob the heat from our fiery furnaces...

- The anointing will give the lions lockjaw when we are called upon to
 spend a night in their den...

- The anointing will cause us to come back from the battle dragging a
 bloody giants head...

- The anointing will neutralize the venom of a deadly viper...

- The anointing will cause the improbable to become probable...

- The anointing will cause the impossible to become possible...

unquestioned loyalty!
