INTRO: I think we can all agree that the First Century Church was a
healthy, effective and powerful church. I hope we can all agree that we
want to attend a healthy, effective and powerful church.

Though times and culture have changed over the past twenty-one century's
it is my opinion that the words of the Apostle Paul to the Ephesians is
still relevant in the twenty first century church. What it took to build
a healthy, effective and powerful church in the first century, the same
will be required in the twenty first century.

The Apostle Paul makes his case that the only way for the body of Christ
to be healthy, effective and powerful is to operate in the five-fold
ministry model. Listen to what he says will be the outgrowth of such a
church:

- Unity in the faith... ("It is a good and pleasant thing for
 brethren to dwell together in unity")

- A more complete knowledge of Jesus... (The reason this occurs? In a
 five-fold ministry model we do life together and you can know what I
 know, and I can know what you know)

- Steady movement toward perfection... (We do not know what He is like,
 but when we see Him, we shall be like Him)

- We will no longer be driven about by fads and heresy... ("Believe
 His Word and you will be established -- Believe His prophets and you
 will prosper." -- In the five-fold model we are submitted to our
 head -- Jesus! Ecclesia -- A community of called believers who are
 submitted to no King but Jesus - We are submitted to those whom He has
 placed over us -- The apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, and
 teachers)

- Learn to speak the truth in love... (Jesus came "Full of grace and
 truth.")

When we get up under the authority of Jesus and His delegated
authorities, the apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, and teachers,
we become equipped to do the work of the ministry! Sounds like a
healthy, effective and powerful church to me!

APOSTLE

(Describe the five-fold ministry gifts using the hand -- Start with the
thumb -- Apostle -- Prophet -- Evangelist -- Pastor - Teacher)

Jesus named and commissioned the first Christian apostles, and they in
turn named and commission others. For all those who believe the only
apostles are those who Jesus chose and commissioned while on earth, let
me remind us there is a New Testament apostle that most of us are
familiar with by the name of Paul.

If Paul can be an apostle who was anointed and commissioned after the
earthly life of Jesus, others can be called and anointed to the same
calling and office.

In the contemporary church the office of apostle, at worst has been
ignored or denied. At best, it is sparingly and hesitantly applied.

Apostle! The word itself stirs up all kinds of theological and emotional
implications. What is an Apostle? Who can truly be considered to be, or
to have been an apostle? Are there apostles in the world today? And if
there are apostles, who are they? How can they be identified? How do
they function? And in what way are they relevant to God's purposes in
the earth today?

News Flash:

Initially, from Matthew 10:1-4 through Acts 6:2, there were no deacons
or elders or any other officers within the Church; only the five-fold
ministry. It was the initial five-fold ministry gifts who established
the governmental order of the first century Church. Of course, all of us
believe the Apostles were moved upon by the Holy spirit to appoint
elders and deacons in response to a church conflict.

Please know that I am not diminishing or minimizing the importance of
these offices, I'm just making the point that Jesus appointed apostles,
the apostles appointed deacons and elders. Why is it then that we
readily embrace the office of deacon or elder and get queasy when the
office of apostle is brought forward?

Apostles are as relevant today as they were in the first century.

The word apostle is a transliteration of the Greek word apostolos. This
is a compound word using apo---away from; and stellos---one sent as an
authorized representative; an ambassador. In other words, an apostle is
one who is consecrated (set apart) from the larger community of people
to fulfill a specific commission.

A person who truly functions in the office of an apostle has a primary
role and concern to establish kingdom order. They are set in place, by
call and commission, to bring order out of chaos. Apostles lead, not
merely by words, but by executive action and administration.

Apostles have unusual authority to direct, command, encourage, and
establish God's purposes within the Church, and by extension, within the
community at large. They are given significant revelation (rhema) that
allows them to see what others often cannot see.

The voice of an apostle is about profound insight, clear and concise
revelation from God's throne. It is insight and direction received in
times of communion with the Holy Spirit, delivered with the confidence
of one prepared to die for the faith.

Perhaps the most significant way in which apostles establish a righteous
response to the leading of the Holy Spirit is through order.

- Apostles establish ministries using biblical parameters to govern.

- Apostles appoint elders and deacons.

- Apostles ground their ministry in biblical order, setting sound,
 reasonable, and understandable policies that give stability to the
 organism called the Church (Ekklesia).

In so many ways, apostles embody the perspective prescribed to the sons
of Issachar when they joined themselves to King David at Ziklag (Note: 1
Chronicles 12:32). "They are men (and women) who understand the times
and know what the Church is about, what it should do, and how it should
be done."

Apostles, from their earliest commissioning in the Gospels, were
assigned the responsibility of founding the Church, and by extension
many, many churches. It should be noted that not one church in the New
Testament was planted by a pastor! The planting and building of churches
was, and is an apostolic function, not a pastoral one. It takes a
pioneering anointing to plant churches. There is a grace upon apostles
and apostolic teams to pioneer and breakthrough.

Because much of the Church does not believe in present-day apostles, the
bulk of the apostolic work has been assigned to the pastor. Therefore,
we have many small, weak, struggling churches.

In the New Testament, the Apostles planted the churches and then
appointed people with pastoral gifts to pastor them! (CALL PASTOR ROY
UP)

The apostolic ministry has been set in the Church to oversee and release
all believers into their callings and destinies. The apostle should not
think only in terms of maintaining, but also in terms and areas of
expanding.

There is room for all of the gifts to operate in an apostolic church.
There is no limit to the numbers of ministries released when the leaders
embrace and walk in an apostolic call.

In the church that embraces the five-fold, especially the apostolic, the
Antioch church becomes our model church. The Church begins to migrate
from a pastoral position to an apostolic one, without losing the
pastoral dimension.

The church that embraces and celebrates the apostolic, will move beyond
being just a local parish to having a tremendous impact in different
regions and territories.

This includes planting churches and building apostolically, in the
places where teams are sent.

The five-fold ministry gifts were intended to function within the
context of the (Ecclesia) church as we know it, but I believe they were
also intended to function in the world (Marketplace).
