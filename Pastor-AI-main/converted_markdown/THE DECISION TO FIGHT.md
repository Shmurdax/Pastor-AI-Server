INTRO: I have come here to start a fight! My purpose today will be
to walk you through a process that will help you to decide whether or
not you should start a fight with the devil. The two responses most of
us have with regard to hardship, danger and controversy is...

- Flight... "Run if you want to, run if you will, but I came here to
 stay -- It's a battlefield brother, not a recreation room, it's a
 fight and not a game -- Run if you want to, run if you will, but I
 came here to stay."

- Fight... This could very well be the moment that everything in your
 life turns on a dime and you make the decision to no longer roll over
 and play dead for the enemy.

So, how do I know when to flee and when to fight? I want us to look at
three determining factors that assist us in making a good decision about
whether or not to fight.

 

## I. PURPOSE

In the text that I read to you, we are introduced to a farmer named
Joash and his son Gideon. The story of Gideon is a very familiar story
but the background of the story is somewhat forgotten. 

 

It seems the Midianites and Amalekites have been oppressing Israel for
several years. The Israelites would plant, tend, irrigate, and do all
the things for bringing a crop to maturity. When it came harvest time
the Midianites and Amalekites would come upon them and take the harvest
for themselves. The Bible say's "They encamped against Israel."
They had become so comfortable with harvesting the crops that they did
not sow or work, that they fully expected to come into the harvest
without resistance. (They brought their motor-coaches, RV's, travel
trailers and tents)

They didn't come with tanks and weapons, they came with equipment for
hauling off the harvest. And, the Israelites just stood idly by and let
it happen! (Sound familiar?)  

 

Gideon had watched this scene being repeated year after year. This
activity had no doubt become a source of irritation for him. Verse
eleven say's "Gideon threshed wheat in the winepress, in order to
hide it from the Midianites." Gideon and his family were so
intimidated by the enemy they had to hide out just to thresh enough
wheat to keep starvation from becoming a reality.      

 

Th e very attitude with which the Midianites and Amalekites eventually
irritated Gideon to the point of considering putting up a fight. Being
irritated to action is the stuff that miracles and deliverance are made
of! (Sheer adrenaline can do amazing things)

 

What kinds of things has the enemy been doing repeatedly in your life
that has you irritated enough that maybe you can see the purpose for a
fight?

 

While farming is a noble profession, it is not the place that you would
normally look for a new hero in a time of national crisis. (You would
normally look in the White House, through the halls of Congress, the
universities and best think tanks; wouldn't you?)

 

When the purpose is important enough, the fight for a turnaround can
begin is very ordinary circumstances and surroundings. 

 

In the winepress Gideon discovered his purpose, and that purpose would
lead him down a very precarious path of danger and victory. By the way,
you will probably never have victory without picking a fight that will
be dangerous but nonetheless victorious.

The second factor that will determine whether you should take flight, or
stand and fight, is... 

## II. COURAGE

Gideon knew the task would not be easy. The things we will be called
upon to do in the middle of a fight, are never simple:

- The Three Hebrew Children...

- Daniel in the lion's den...

- David killing Goliath...

 

Gideon was not sure the task was even do-able! This is what faith looks
like... "God can do anything, I believe (Hope) He will do this!" 

 

None of the heroes of the faith had any way of knowing the final outcome
of their mission beforehand, they only knew the purpose demanded
courage!

There is a song by Bethel Music that talks about:

- The greatness of His renown...

- The waves of Heaven crash over us...

- The greatness of the God of Heaven...

- The Lord drawing us out beyond the shore...

- The Lord is for us and not against us...

- The Lord making us brave...

Up until this point Gideon was a chicken! He was a coward, along with
his father, his family, and everyone else in Israel! He was "Hiding
in the winepress while threshing wheat."

While considering whether to flee or fight, our purpose makes us brave
enough to spring into action...  

 

## III. ACTION

Judges 6:11-12 NKJV "Now the Angel of the [Lord]{.smallcaps} came and
sat under the terebinth tree which was in Ophrah, which belonged to
Joash the Abiezrite, while his son Gideon threshed wheat in the
winepress, in order to hide it from the Midianites.12 And the Angel of
the [Lord]{.smallcaps} appeared to him, and said to
him, [The [Lord]{.smallcaps} is with you, you mighty man of
valor]{.underline}!" The word "valor" as it is used here means:

- Strong...

- Mighty...

- Forceful...

- Efficient...

I want us to get a good visual here: Gideon was hiding out, because he
was afraid of the Midianites and the Amalekites. He was threshing wheat,
and the Angel say's "The Lord is with you, you mighty man of
valor"!

I believe while Gideon was threshing wheat he was trying to decide
whether to keep hiding or to take action and fight!

During WWII England was being attacked by Germany. With London being
bombed around the clock the British people began to lose heart, then one
man, Sir Winston Churchill stepped to a microphone and uttered these
heartening words: "We shall fight on the seas and the oceans, we
shall defend our Islands, we shall fight on the beaches, we shall fight
on the landing grounds, we shall fight on the hills; but we shall never
surrender." This must be how Gideon felt that day! Gideon knew it was
time for someone to step up to the plate of action, and he may not have
even known that someone would be him! 

Naivety is the one quality that allows for greatness without supporting
superficial arrogance. 

Here is what I think happened in Judges Chapter six: As a young boy
Gideon had played Israelites and Midianites with his brothers, cousins,
and friends. This was done in the same way we Texans would play cowboys
and Indians. The cowboys and Israelites always win, not without a
battle, but they always win. 

In his own imaginary world Gideon had fought the Midianites and
Amalekites a hundred times or more, and had never lost one battle! He
imagined coming home to a hero's welcome, being the grand Marshall of
the parade, etc...  

When Gideon became a young man, he quit playing Israelites and
Midianites, but in his mind he continued to fight that battle and win
that war! In his mind he kept coming home to a hero's welcome! 

On this day, while Gideon was hiding out, threshing wheat for his
father; in his subconscious he had just returned from destroying the
Midianites and Amalekites, and was being carried through the streets of
the capital city; unbeknown to Gideon the Angel of the Lord was sitting
close enough to read his thoughts; and the angel of the Lord
said "The Lord is with you, you mighty man of valor"! In other
words: "stir yourself; do what is in your heart and God will be with
you and will prosper your way". (Talk about angels here -- We command
angels -- They are awaiting our instructions -- Randy Lowe -- Mr.
Stanton)

Gideon knew, and you know, winning the war would not be as easy as all
that; but it never is, is it? Look at what Gideon had to overcome to
make the dream a reality, and become Israel's hero: 

- He single-handedly destroyed Baal worship... (Throwing down the altars
 and cutting down the groves)

- He outlasted threats on his life... (After coming back from the war,
 Sir Winston Churchill was confronted by an irate lady who said, "If
 I were your wife I would put poison in my tea". Churchill calmly
 replied "Madam if you were my wife I would put poison in my own
 tea".

- He overcame the loss of 22,000 professional quitters...

- He patiently endured the loss of 9,700 short-sighted soldiers...

- He courageously confronted an innumerable host of Midianites and
 Amalekites with a pitcher, a lantern, and a shout of praise to God...

 

The decision was finally made that day to fight and the victory was won!
Gideon went from being a ranch hand to judging the people of Israel. God
anointed an unlikely person who decided to get in the fight, and he made
God's FAITH HALL OF FAME in Hebrews 11. 

 

CONCLUSION:

Who knows how many of God's un-developed and under-developed heroes are
in this place today? Only time and opportunity will tell the final
story! Don't allow the hero that is within you to go unrevealed because
you;

- Failed to see tShe purpose...

- Lacked the courage...

- Failed to take action...
