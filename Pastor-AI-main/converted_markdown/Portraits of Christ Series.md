TEXT: 1 Peter 1:18-21 (PLACE REFERENCE ONLY ON SCREEN) (NIV)
"For you know that it was not with perishable things such as silver
or gold that you were redeemed from the empty way of life handed down to
you from your forefathers, 19 but [with the precious blood of Christ, a
lamb without blemish or defect]{.underline}. 20 He was chosen before the
creation of the world, but was revealed in these last times for your
sake. 21 Through him you believe in God, who raised him from the dead
and glorified him, and so your faith and hope are in God."

INTRO: When we think of a lamb, many pictures come to mind:

- A meek, mild and gentle animal...

- A quiet and defenseless creature...

- An obedient and submissive being...

- A sacrifice slain on an altar...

In our text Peter declares that Jesus the Lamb of God was not just
another ordinary lamb, but a perfect lamb "Without blemish or
spot." This lamb was "Chosen before the creation of the world and
has been revealed in these last times." The offering of a lamb for
sin was not the result of a surprised God attempting to find a way to
fix the blundering ways of a fallen race. The sacrifice of Jesus was
actually preplanned: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Before the world was created...

- Before there was a human being...

- Before the first sin was ever committed...

- Before subsequent sins were committed...

This preplanned lamb was also counted as slain before the worlds were
formed. Think of the extent of the love of a Holy God reaching down to
fallen man. We cannot fathom His devotion, dedication, and love, as He
preplanned His only begotten sons journey from heaven to earth for the
purpose of death by crucifixion! All this for one reason, to redeem a
fallen race!

It is most difficult for us to get our minds around the significance of
a sacrificial lamb because we have never known or participated (Thank
God) in a sacrificial paradigm of worship.

Today I want to attempt to help make the Portrait of Christ as The Lamb
of God clearer than it has ever been.

## I. THE HISTORY OF THE LAMB...

I think it fitting, the first animal sacrifice in the Bible was offered
by Jehovah Himself. Genesis 3:21 (CEV) "The LORD God made clothes out
of [animal skins]{.underline} for the man and his wife." I find it
very interesting that immediately after the fall, God killed some of the
animals He had created to cover the nakedness (sin) of the fallen race.
Adam and Eve made a vain attempt to cover themselves through their own
efforts by sewing together fig leaves that they hoped would cover their
nakedness. Seeing man's futile effort at covering sinfulness, God
provided covering by means of a sacrifice. Sin: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN
ONE AT A TIME)

- Has never been covered without a sacrifice...

- Is never covered without a sacrifice...

- Will never be covered without a sacrifice...

The animals being slain by Jehovah to cover the nakedness of Adam and
Eve is a portrait of what Jesus would do for the entire human race!

Mohandas Gandhi, said, "A man who was completely innocent, offered
himself as a sacrifice for the good of others, including his enemies,
and became the ransom of the world. It was a perfect act."

Under the New Covenant, we are required to be clothed by Christ rather
than our own good works. Galatians 3:27 (CEV) "When you were
baptized, it was as though you had [put on Christ]{.underline} in the
same way you put on new clothes." Here we have Jesus as the Lamb of
God, covering our sinfulness.

In Genesis chapter four Abel offered animal sacrifices to the Lord of
the first of His flock. Again, we have a portrait of Christ as the Lamb
of God.

Abraham was constantly offering animal sacrifices for himself and his
family, all of which were a type of what Jesus would as The Lamb of God!
Nowhere is this picture more vivid than in Genesis chapter twenty-two
when a ram took the place of Isaac upon the altar at Moriah. What a
picture of Christ as The Lamb of God! (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A
TIME)

- Isaac was a miracle son, so was Jesus...

- Isaac was an only son, so was Jesus...

- Isaac was an innocent party, so was Jesus...

- Isaac was a willing sacrifice, so was Jesus...

That's where the similarities end and the differences begin. Isaac and
Jesus were different in the following ways: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN NE AT
A TIME)

- Isaac was spared as a sacrifice; Jesus was made a sacrifice...

- Isaac was allowed to live; Jesus died...

- Isaac was rescued by a ram caught in a thicket; Jesus died at the will
 of His Father...

- Isaac continued to live a normal life; Jesus was resurrected as an
 eternal God...

Another great portrait of Christ as The Lamb of God is the Passover lamb
in Exodus.

The Passover lamb:

- Had to be spotless... (Sinless life)

- Fed a strict diet over a prescribed period of time... (Doing the will
 of His Father)

- Became both a sacrifice and a meal... (Bread that has come down from
 Heaven)

- Was to be killed at exactly the right time... (At twilight -- Jesus
 came to earth to die)

- The blood was to be applied to the doorway of their homes... (Nothing
 but the blood)

They were to consume the sacrifice:

- With their loins girded... (Sanctified life)

- With their sandals on... (Prepared to follow)

- With their staff in their hand... (Leave in haste)

The sacrifice of the Passover lamb became so personal that: (PLACE ON
THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- It was first referred to as "A lamb." (Jesus was certainly a
 lamb among many lambs)

- It was then referred to as "The lamb." (Jesus was the exclusive
 Lamb of God)

- Finally it was referred to as "Your lamb." (Jesus is a personal
 Lord of Glory)

## II. JESUS THE LAMB...

We have seen the Old Testament accounts of the foreshadowing of the Lamb
but the New Testament presents to us a very clear picture of how Jesus
fulfills every part of the lamb in sacrificial worship.

I find it very interesting that God chose to use a lamb as the
representation of what His Son would become. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN
UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) The reason it seems peculiar to me is because:

- A lamb is a very small animal... (God is big)

- A lamb is weak in the animal kingdom... (God is all-powerful)

- A lamb is not a very wise animal... (God has all wisdom)

The Scripture declares, God wanted to take the "Weak things of this
world and use them to confound the wise."

John the Baptist declared, "Behold the Lamb of God that takes away
the sins of the world." By employing the word "Behold" John the
Baptist was pointing to something of immeasurable spiritual and eternal
significance. He was saying: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The Lamb of God is here...

- This Lamb is the Messiah...

- He will take away the sin of the world...

Until this moment, the best a lamb could do was to roll sin ahead for
one year at a time. Previous lambs could only cover sin up for a season!
The blood of millions of sacrificial lambs could not forgive a single
sin; just roll it forward looking like a giant snowball of sin in
anticipation of the coming of the Messiah.

Colossians 2:13-14 (NASB) "When you were dead in your transgression,
He made you alive together with Him, [having forgiven us all our
transgressions]{.underline}, 14 [Having canceled out the certificate of
debt]{.underline} consisting of decrees against us, which was hostile to
us; and He has taken it out of the way, having nailed it to the
cross." The Old Testament offerings only covered sin for a time, like
a credit card covering a debt, it only postpones the inevitable!
Eventually someone MUST pay the debt in full!

Because of His sinless life, He alone was qualified to forgive sin. He
took my sin away! Jesus paid the debt of sin that had been charged to
the max, and he cut the card up! (Do visual of this)

Psalm 103:12 (NASB) "As far as the east is from the west, so far has
He [removed our transgressions]{.underline} from us." How far is the
East from the West? One can start traveling east and continue forever
without coming to the end of east! The same is true if he tries to find
the end of west.

## III. JESUS THE LAMB CHANGES EVERYTHING...

Consider what Jesus the Lamb has done for us, we were: (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Thought of by Him before time began...

- Sought out by Him in eternity past...

- Fought for by Him on an old rugged cross...

- Brought to Him by the Holy Spirit...

- Bought by Him through a horrific death...

- Taught by Him through His Word...

- Caught by Him through a sanctified life...

- Wrought by Him as His very own bride...

Jesus the Lamb of God: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Gave His life for us...

- Gave His life to us...

- Lives His life through us...

- Delivers on eternal life with Him...

When one comes into relationship with Him, nothing about us remains the
same. "Old things pass away and all things become new.

CONCLUSION:

The ancient lambs of Old Testament worship and sacrifice, point us to a
day when A LAMB would do what they could never do. Jesus was the
fulfillment of the types and foreshadows those lambs provided, and He
accomplished everything they typified.

The very best the ancient lambs could ever provide was a stay of
execution for sins committed, but this Lamb of God expunged the record
and erased not only the offense, but also the penalty for the offence.
This Lamb of God has taken the process one step further; He makes all
things new! 2 Corinthians 5:17 (NKJV) "If anyone is in Christ, [he is
a new creation]{.underline}; old things have passed away; behold, [all
things have become new]{.underline}."

If we see, understand and accept the portrait of Christ as the Lamb of
God, He will change everything about us!

INTRO: In our text Moses was tending the flock of Jethro his
father-in-law, who was the priest of Midian. On the day in question
Moses led the flock to the far side of the desert and came to Horeb,
which is known as "The mountain of God." At Horeb the LORD
appeared to Moses in flames of fire from within a bush. To fully
understand this passage of Scripture we must first discover who is in
the bush. When the voice from within the bush identifies Himself to
Moses as "I AM" the original words used are:

- Ego -- Meaning "I or I myself."

- Eimi -- Meaning "I Am."

In scripture Eimi is often used alone and still means "I am"
without emphasis, but when "Ego" is added as a prefix, the "I
AM" is meant to be emphatic. Such is the case in the incident of the
burning bush experience of Moses. This use of "I AM" is employed
four other times in the Old Testament and each time the person being
referred to, is none other than Jesus. (Isaiah 41:4; Isaiah 41:10;
Isaiah 43:10; Isaiah 48:12)

Most Bible scholars would agree that the name Yahweh or Jehovah, as it
is sometimes translated, would be the proper name of God. All other
name's ascribed to Him, including the compound names, provided further
revelation of His character and His activity!

Jehovah, or Yahweh, occurs 6,823 times in the Bible! Most English
translations of the Bible translate this name with the word LORD, in all
capital letters. Conversely, Adonai (ownership) is often translated
Lord, using upper and lower case letters.

Easton\'s Bible Dictionary gives us the following insight into the name
Jehovah, our English name for Yahweh: "This name was held by the later
Jews to be so sacred that it was never pronounced except by the High
Priest on the Day of Atonement, when he entered into the most Holy
place. "

The Hebrew name in Hebrew means, "To be and so implies the absolute
existence of God!"

I want us to look at the I AM in 3-D.

## I. I AM WHO I AM...

Smith\'s Bible Dictionary describes the difference between Elohim and
Jehovah, or the difference between LORD and Lord: "While Elohim
exhibits God displayed in his power as the creator and governor of the
physical universe, the name Jehovah designates his nature as he stands
in relation to man, as the only almighty, true, personal, holy Being, a
spirit and 'the father of spirits,' (John 4:24) who revealed himself
to his people, made a covenant with them, and became their lawgiver, and
to whom all honor and worship are due."

In our text, Jehovah transitioned from just being the creator of the
universe, to being a personal God with whom Moses would interact over
the next forty years.

The sight of a bush that refused to be reduced to ashes, by a white-hot
fire, intrigued Moses!

The visitor in the burning bush was about to change his life forever.
The person in the bush was Jesus and He changes the lives of all who
dare to dialogue with Him.

The LORD called to Moses out of the bush and Moses responded, "Here I
am!" With Moses attention securely fastened on the burning bush, the
LORD said four things: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Do not come any closer... A mere mortal can only tolerate a portion of
 the presence of the great I AM! Nonetheless, we should get as close as
 possible.

<!-- -->

- Take off your shoes... In Eastern culture this was a sign of respect!
 One cannot build a relationship with the LORD until they have
 developed respect for Him.

- You are standing on Holy ground... The ground became Holy because of
 the presence of the great I AM! His presence creates holiness wherever
 it goes!

- Go deliver My people... The constant activity of God is that of
 deliverance! Paul said, "He hath delivered... He doth deliver... He
 will yet deliver..."

To this point in the life of Moses, Jehovah was little more than a God
of History that he learned about when Jochabed rocked him on her lap
while he was but a child! He knew that in the past Jehovah had: (PLACE
ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Created and populated the universe...

- Called and equipped his ancestors...

- Saved and delivered the Hebrews...

- Protected and provided for the Israelites...

Yet, to Moses Jehovah was little more than a God of History! For many
people God has never been more than a God of history!

He wants to be more to each of us than a God of history! Jesus declared
Himself to be the God of seven distinct designations in the book of
John: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- John 6:35 -- "I am the bread of life..."

- John 8:12 -- "I am the light of the world..."

- John 10:7 -- "I am the door of the sheepfold."

- John 10:11 -- "I am the good shepherd."

- John 11:25 -- "I am the resurrection and the life."

- John 14:6 -- "I am the way, the truth, and the life."

- John 15:1 -- "I am the true vine."

Jehovah engaged extreme measures in an attempt to become a God of the
present to Moses, and He is doing the same for someone in this room
today!

## II. I AM WHAT I AM...

When God first identifies Himself in this passage, He does so in terms
of His historic relationship to Israel's forefathers. Exodus 3:6 (KJV)
"I am the God [of your father]{.underline}, the God of Abraham, the
God of Isaac and the God of Jacob." At this, Moses hid his face,
because he was afraid to look upon God.

The same God, who had acted through the unmistakable power of redemptive
grace in the past, was speaking to Moses in real time.

Moses knew the history; Yahweh had delivered them, cared for them and
sustained them in the past. Moses was about to discover that a very
personal God was going to use him to redeem His people from Egyptian
captivity!

The fact that Moses was engaged in a conversation with Jehovah was off
the chain in the mind of Moses. When the voice from the burning bush
suggested that Moses would be used as an instrument of deliverance,
Moses lost control of all reason!

Moses was not thrilled with the news that he was going to be a chosen
vessel in the hands of the great I AM! Moses immediately objects and his
objections were baseless:

- He proceeds to tell I AM how unworthy he is...

- He then proceeds to explain to I AM how incapable he is...

- He even suggests someone else might be a better fit...

Exodus 3:11 (KJV) "Who am I, that I should go to Pharaoh and bring
the Israelites out of Egypt?" This question is wrong on a lot of
levels but it is most certainly irreverent and irrelevant: Irreverent --
This question calls into question God's judgment in His choice of Moses.

Here you have an example of the creation arguing with the creator!
Really?

Have you ever found yourself in this position?

Have you ever made excuses about your ability or inability? When we do
this we are being irreverent! This questioning of God's choice of
laborers is couched in the mistaken idea that we can do God's will and
work in our own strength! No such thing is possible! We must not only
have the call of God, but the presence of God to do His work! Moses
said, "If your presence is not going with us, do not carry us up
hence."

Irrelevant: Whom God calls He qualifies! When we are called by God to
perform a certain responsibility, He has not chosen us because we are
the sharpest knives in the drawer; He selected us because He intends to
anoint us for His task! To argue with the call of God, based on
perceived ability is irrelevant! His call is not dependent on natural
ability or skill; it is based upon anointing and obedience!

Besides, His gifts and callings are without repentance! Romans
11:29 (Amplified Bible) "For [God\'s gifts and His call are
irrevocabl]{.underline}e. \[He never withdraws them when once they are
given, and He does not change His mind about those to whom He gives His
grace or to whom He sends His call.\]"

## III. I AM WHO I AM GOING TO BE...

Have your children ever asked, "Mommy, where did I come from?" And you
replied, "From God! They then ask, "Where did God come from?"

Ken Hemphill in his book "Names Of God" makes this statement: "When
such a question is asked, it is obvious that we are thinking in a
scientific fashion in terms of cause and effect. We have been taught
that everything that exists had to have a prior cause. That is the point
of the name Yahweh. God is the uncaused cause! (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL
NEXT SLIDE) When God states that He is the 'self-existing' One, this
means He is the first cause and before Him there was no other and after
Him there will be no other. Life is found in Him. He is the first
cause that you may have been searching for all your life!"

Moses suffered from a problem that continues to plague humanity. Until
his rendezvous with Jehovah at the burning bush; He was only a God of
history to Moses! Jehovah will have little to no effect on our lives as
long as we relegate him to historical time frame!

We have no problem with the historical accuracy of the Bible, we
believe:

- God created the world...

- God parted the Red Sea...

- God delivered the Three Hebrew Children...

- Jesus died on a cross for our sin...

- Jesus ascended from Bethany's launching pad...

- Jesus promised to return for individual believers...

This is all well and good but if that is as far as we go, we have placed
God in a historical box!

Moses finished his interrogation of Jehovah with one final question:
Exodus 3:13 (KJV) "Suppose I go to the Israelites and say to them,
\'The God of your fathers has sent me to you,\' and they ask me, 'What
is His name' then [what shall I tell them]{.underline}?" This
question is both reverent and relevant! Moses finally placed his focus
in the right place! His question suggests that if the person in the
burning bush was who He claimed to be, He could bring His will to pass
irrespective of the odds! This should be the focus of our attention as
we grow in our relationship to God and attempt to do His will!

Notice God's answer to Moses: Exodus 3:14 (AMP) "And God said to
Moses, I AM WHO I AM and WHAT I AM, and I WILL BE WHAT I WILL BE; and He
said, You shall say this to the Israelites: I AM has sent me to you!"

The name Jehovah or Yahweh declares that God is: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN
ONE AT A TIME)

- Personal

God's revelation to man. There are no references that begin with,

The voice in the bush identifies Himself as "I AM WHO I AM..."

- His divine nature...

- His divine character...

- His divine attributes...

- Him to be the God of History...

- Him to be the God of real time...

- Him to be the God of eternity to come...

Moses' question and God's response turns the attention from who Moses
is, to who was promising to be with Moses!

Notice what the LORD say's to Moses: Exodus 3:15 (KJV) "...This is my
name for ever, and this is my [memoria]{.underline}l unto all
generations." The LORD is telling Moses that the name of Yahweh is to
be a memorial to all generations:

- Generations past...

- In real time...

- Generations yet to come...

But really, what is the significance of His memorial name that is to be
remembered forever? The word "Remember" is more than a mental act
of memorization, it means, "To recall and then act accordingly!" So
when God says that all generations are to "Remember" Him as
Yahweh, this means that all who know God are to see Him as being present
with them and to act accordingly! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL NEXT

What should this look like in 2011?

- When all hell is breaking loose in our lives, we are to recall His
 name, realize He is with us and allow peace to rule 24/7!

- When all hope has been lost, we are to recall whose we are and allow
 hope to anchor us!

- When our faith has been attacked, we are supposed to recall who is
 with us then stand fast on faith!

- When defeat seems to be lurking at the door, we must get our eyes off
 the circumstances, remember that He is the captain of the LORD'S host,
 and remain engaged on the field of battle until the victory comes!

The person that lives in the awareness that God is present at every
moment will live a very different life from the person who is reliant
upon his or her own ability!

INTRO: In the Bible, names are significant. People were intentional
in choosing the names of their children and the meaning of those names
would often choose the trajectory of the life of the child! Names were
chosen for a variety of reasons:

- A family name sake...

- A family occupation...

- A joyous occasion...

- A traumatic event...

- A great victory...

- A devastating defeat...

In our culture names are no big deal but titles are looked upon as very
significant! Titles in our culture somewhat demonstrate the significance
of names in scriptural context.

One common denominator between titles in our culture and names in the
Old Testament is that the bearer has the power to honor or demean the
title or name. Proverbs 22:1 (NKJV) "[A good name is to be chosen

and gold." There are many Biblical and contemporary examples of
individuals who have honored and demeaned a name. We will only have one
name and we would do well to manage it responsibly.

far-reaching impact; in fact, our current culture is being shaped by the

 perhaps I shall obtain children by her."

 voice of Sarai."
