BAR-MITZVAH/BAT-MITZVAH

The Barmitzvah ceremony that celebrates a Jewish young man transitioning
from childhood to manhood. A Batmitzvah is the same ceremony for a young
lady transitioning from childhood to womanhood.

In essence, once a son had passed through bar-mitzvah, he enters
APPRENTICESHIP with his father, or with whomever his father has
selected. This apprenticeship was to be his period of training, a
maturation process by which his newly recognized adult status grew, in
both competence and confidence.

At the time of a bar-mitzvah, a boy was established as a joint-heir with
every other inheriting male in his family. He becomes a joint-heir of
his father's possessions, and is entitled to all the rights and
privileges that pertain to that position.

It is also to bar-mitzvah that Paul refers when he

says of Timothy, "you know the proof of him, that, as a son with the
father, he hath served with me in the gospel." (Philippians 2:22).
This is the apprenticeship that qualifies a son to become an heir.

No-one gets a double portion without following a vision to the end of
the road. (Elijah/Elisha -- 10 years of serving -- Not one request until
he was asked)

Pauls relationship with Timothy provides a candid look into the core of
a Father/Son relationship. During the course of his ministry Paul had
several spiritual Sons and Daughters. The youngest was Timothy.
Father/Son relationships is unlike any other relationships between
humans.

When Paul needed to send someone to Philippi, as an extension of his own
authority and responsibility, he said this about Timothy:

"But I trust in the Lord Jesus to send Timotheus shortly unto you,
that I also may be of good comfort, when I know your state. For I have
no man likeminded, who will naturally care for your state. For all seek
their own, not the things which are Jesus Christ's. But ye know the
proof of him, that, as a son with the father, he hath served with me in
the gospel. Him therefore I hope to send presently, so soon as I shall
see how it will go with me. But I trust in the Lord that I also myself
shall come shortly." (Philippians 2:19-24)

Two distinct words jump off the page in this passage. The first is send.
It is the Greek word "apostello", from which we derive the term
apostle. Paul was intent upon sending, or "apostelling" Timothy to
Philippi as an ambassador and representative of Paul's authority.

The second word is like-minded. This is the Greek word "opsuche,"
which means "same-souled." Obviously, the relationship between Paul
and Timothy had become intensely powerful. Paul could confidently send
Timothy because he knew that their mutual identity was clear, their
perception was aligned, their agenda was not conflicted, and Timothy
operated in perfect harmony with Paul's judgment.

Paul describes this phenomenon as being "same-souled." It is the
ultimate result of a deeply held covenantal connection between a father
and a son. Paul's endorsement of Timothy was utterly unrestrained. "I
have no other man \[same-souled\] who will naturally care for your
state."

Timothy had developed a Father/Son relationship with Paul that had
become a way of life. He would naturally, without a second thought,
deliver Paul's heart and spirit to the Philippian church. Nothing could
more accurately illustrate the Father/Son connection.

# REWARDS AND RESPONSIBILITIES

Benefits to Son/Daughter:

A spiritual Mother/Father will:

- Invest in you...

- Walk with you...

- Help you discover who you are...

- Help you discover and accomplish your purpose...

- Find intimacy with the Holy Spirit...

- Establish Kingdom in your life...

- Pass what we know on to you so you do not have to relearn what we have
 learned through trial, error and success...

- Have you to walk with them in a common pursuit of a few initiatives
 that they feel have the power of legacy attached to them...

This covenant relationship establishes alignment in:

- Vision...

- Purpose...

- Ministry...

- Opportunity...

Rightly entered, this covenant relationship is profoundly advantageous
to both the spiritual parent and the child, and ultimately to the
Kingdom of God as well. This creates not only a win-win situation, but a
win-win-win situation!

Reciprocity permeates this kind of agreement, and provides benefits to
everyone involved. Each is blessed by the other and both are blessed by
the Lord.

There are six major benefits which accrue to the Son/Daughter from the
Parent/Child, relationship:

- Identification... From the beginning, the Son/Daughter becomes
 identified with the Parent. Identification is so very important. It
 validates the birthright of the child. It marks him/her as an heir of
 their parents possessions, and a joint-heir with all those who are
 Sons/Daughters. "This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well
 pleased." (Matthew 3:17) "...hear him." (Luke 9:35)
 Sons/Daughters have a connection to the greater family of the parent
 and become known by their parent's name.

- Information... The spiritual parent brings knowledge and wisdom,
 insight and understanding to the relationship. They bring hard won
 principles and core values to the relationship which can, and must be
 instilled into the life of the Son/Daughter.

- Inspiration... In addition to expanding the insight and vision of a
 spiritual Son/ Daughter, the spiritual parent must also motivate and
 inspire their children. This is not an easy task. Often, the child
 comes to the parent in a state of personal disarray. They may be
 filled with vision, but lack clarity. They may come with emotional or
 moral baggage which must be unpacked and corrected. It is incumbent on
 the spiritual parent to be an exemplary model for the spiritual
 Son/Daughter to follow. The parent becomes your biggest fan. They
 become the ones who cheers you on when things go awry. A Father/Mother
 will cover their child, should that child fall short in their life
 and/or ministry. The spiritual parent will not abandon their child or
 leave them as an orphan in times of crisis. Spiritual Fathers/Mothers
 inspire their children to victory!

#### Impartation... When a Son/Daughter is received by a Spiritual Parent, they come under the Parent's anointing and influence. As a result, the Parent begins to impart their own spiritual personality and character to the child. Where the Parent goes, the Child goes. What the Parent does, the Child becomes engaged in. In other words, the Child becomes aligned with the Parents spirit and essence, so that their streams of ministry begin to flow together. Some would say the Child receives the Spiritual Parents DNA. The Spiritual Parent inspires the Child to take on similar attributes, similar values, and a common purpose and vision. God puts the spirit of the father on the son, much as he put the spirit of Moses on the seventy elders of Israel (Note: Numbers 11:25). Before he became a prophet, Elisha was a spiritual son to Elijah. As Elijah was about to be carried away into heaven, he offered a last request to Elisha and Elisha received a double anointing. (2 Kings 2:9-10) The most significant thing a father can impart to his son is his anointing. Yet, it is impossible to do so unless the Lord makes it so, for anointing comes from the Lord (Note: 1 John 2:27), not from the father. A A spiritual Father/Mother has the responsibility to release his anointing over his Son(s), but only as they qualify and as God directs or permits. 

#### Inheritance... Spiritual Fathers/Mothers bring a heritage, a legacy of ministry, to the relationship. Immediately upon becoming a Son/Daughter, the spiritual child becomes a partaker of the full inheritance of their parents. The child doesn't take over the parent's ministry. Rather, they partake of their parent's ministry in ways others cannot. With time, their identity with their parent's, influences others to see them much as they see the parent (Mom and Dad Goodwin's spiritual children are easily identifiable). The child enjoys the rich reward of standing in their parent's shadow, as it were, and enjoy the blessing that comes with being the parent's child. 

#### Intimacy... The bond between a spiritual Father/Son Mother/Daughter grows into total trust and transparency. It is not bounded by geography or time. It is, a result of being together at every opportunity, in any setting---whether in intense ministry opportunities, or in casual times of mutual recreation. There is a vulnerability that comes to the surface, a kind of open honesty that allows secrets to evaporate as both strive to bless, encourage, and advance the other. Weaknesses are confronted with pathways to strength. Mistakes are adjusted with compassion and consideration. 

Benefits to the Father/Mother:

Three benefits that accrue to the Father/Mother include:

- Assistance... As the relationship takes on the quality of a working
 covenant, the Father/Mother begins to receive meaningful assistance in
 accomplishing their vision. The Bible gives encounter after encounter,
 and story after story demonstrating how being in a Father/Son
 Mother/Daughter relationship is so advantageous. Most notably, those
 Sons/Daughters enter into the Father/ Mothers vision and receive it as
 their own.

Elisha washed the hands of Elijah. (Note: 2 Kings 3:1)

Joshua served Moses faithfully, and at the time of Moses passing, he was
positioned to become the leader of the nation.

Spiritual Sons/Daughters have the privilege of entering into the
Father/Mothers vision as no others can. They are drawn to the heart of
their parents, and completely engage in assisting the parent in
achieving the destiny God has set for them. In the process, they become
the inheritors of the mantle, the ones God uses to extend and expand the
vision.

#### Perspective... Fathers/Mothers who turn to their spiritual children are blessed with perspective otherwise unavailable to them as they grow older. There is a freshness that comes with youth, a relevance to changing times which is often lost or overlooked by an aging parent. Being close to a fresh perspective, allows the vision to transition into more than if the Father/Mother had held it alone. 

#### Continuity... The presence of a true Son/Daughter lends permanence and stability. There accrues to a wise and connected Father/Mother a kind of influence that cannot be achieved otherwise. The parent can rest in the fact that if he/she is incapacitated or dies, their vision will continue through their spiritual children. Remember, in the final analysis, it's not what a person learns, or even what they do that matters. It is only what a person leaves in the next generation that truly makes a difference. The best way to insure continuity is through Sons/Daughters.
