Today we are going to talk about resurrection in a historic sense (2,000
plus years ago) and what resurrection looks like in the 21^st^ century.

Most Christians have the resurrection tucked away in a nice, neat,
little historical package that they can reflect on once a year, and then
move on to more pressing and significant issues.

It is my hope that when you walk out of this church today, you will
never view resurrection in the same way! I pray that the lights will
come on for all of us and that we will not only believe in the
resurrection, but walk in its enormous power every day for the rest of
our lives.

Luke 24:1-9 (NKJV) "Now on the first day of the week, very early in
the morning, they, and certain other women with them, came to the tomb
bringing the spices which they had prepared. 2 But they found the stone
rolled away from the tomb. 3 Then they went in and did not find the body
of the Lord Jesus. 4 And it happened, as they were greatly perplexed
about this, that behold, two men stood by them in shining garments.
5 Then, as they were afraid and bowed their faces to the earth, they
said to them, "Why do you seek the living among the dead? 6 He is not
here, but is risen! Remember how He spoke to you when He was still in
Galilee, 7 saying, 'The Son of Man must be delivered into the hands of
sinful men, and be crucified, and the third day rise again.'" 8 And they
remembered His words. 9 Then they returned from the tomb and told all
these things to the eleven and to all the rest."

The resurrection of Christ from the dead is the most important event in
the history of the world. This is true because the resurrection of
Christ from the dead validates: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- His virgin birth...

- His sinless life...

- His brutal death...

- His claim to be the Messiah...

If Christ had not been raised from the dead, His death would have been
nothing more than another (among many) brutal execution via crucifixion.
The good news is that He did in fact rise from the dead on the third
day!

You may be able to bury Jesus in a compartment of your life but sooner
or later He is going to resurrect and you will have to deal with Him.
You can banish Him from:

- Your lifestyle - He will eventually challenge how you are choosing to
 live...

- Your relationships -- Sooner or later He will be the last one standing
 and you must answer for who He is...

- Your finances -- In time, you will have to admit "All good and
 perfect things come from above."

- Your home -- You will discover He is the only real source of peace...

- Your eternity -- "Every knee shall bow..."

The resurrection of Jesus from the dead is an historical fact. What
significance, if any, does His resurrection have on those of us who are
alive and making our way through the twenty-first century?

- God has never seen a question He could not answer...
