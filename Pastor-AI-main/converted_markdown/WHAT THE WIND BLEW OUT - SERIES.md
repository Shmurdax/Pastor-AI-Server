# WHAT THE WIND BLEW OUT

WHAT THE WIND BLEW IN

TEXT: Acts 2:1-4 (NKJV) "When the Day of Pentecost had fully
come, they were all with one accord in one place. ^2 ^And suddenly there
came a sound from heaven, as of a [rushing mighty wind]{.underline}, and
it filled the whole house where they were sitting. ^3 ^Then there
appeared to them divided tongues, as of fire, and one sat upon each of
them. ^4 ^And they were all filled with the Holy Spirit and began to
speak with other tongues, as the Spirit gave them utterance."

INTRO: The phenomenon in the Upper Room occurred exactly fifty days
after the resurrection and is still celebrated and known as the Day of
Pentecost. Those who have received the Upper Room experience and speak
in other tongues are commonly called Charismatics or Pentecostals. Those
who have received such an experience are not some small fringe group -
it is believed that there are in excess of 750,000,000 Spirit filled
believers on this globe right now!

It is against this backdrop that I want to talk about the initial
outpouring of Holy Spirit, and what happened when He blew into the Upper
Room in the form of a wind, on the Day of Pentecost.

THE WIND BLEW OUT NATIONAL RELIGION AND BLEW IN GLOBAL SALVATION...

Until the outpouring of Holy Spirit in the Upper Room in Jerusalem, only
a very small, elite group of people, in comparison to the population of
the world were able to experience a personal relationship with the Lord.
Jesus Himself said "Salvation is of the Jews" (Jn. 4:22), meaning
they had the corner on the market of religion. In fact, Messiah would
come through this very lineage.

The trouble with this is that, "He came unto His own (first)
and his own received him not." (Jn. 1:11)

By the time Christ came, the Jewish faith had become so ingrown that
very few could meet the tremendous rules and regulations imposed upon
the people. Judaism had morphed into an elite group of people that
happened to be born into a certain lineage, and a very minor percentage
of the global population were proselytized and brought into the Jewish
faith. Rather than operating as THE answer and evangelizing the world,
this system of religion operated as an exclusive group of up-and-outers!
(Sound familiar?)

Rabbis, Sadducees, and Pharisees had complicated this system of religion
to the point, that men and women were bowed down with the pain and
suffering of religion, with its forms and rituals.

When this wind blew into the Upper Room the exclusive, national religion
of the Jews, went out with the wind - John 1:12 "As many as received
him, to them gave he power to become the sons of God even to them that
believe on his name."

This wind blew out the exclusivity of the Gospel and blew in the
salvation of tens of millions of people from the four corners of the
earth. In fact, when the curtains open in the book of Revelation an
innumerable host are worshipping the King of Kings and Lord of Lords
from every kindred, tongue, people, and nation!

[]{.mark}

Old ceremonial, legalistic religion was gone with the wind---it was
replaced with a global salvation of "Whosoever will let him come and
drink freely!"

[]{.mark}

Because of the wind that blew into the upper room, salvation was no
longer restricted to a nationality, tribe, dialect, or denomination!
From the outpouring of Holy Spirit to this present day, "God rides on
the wings of the wind."

This wind:

- Blows where He wants to blow...

- Moves where He wants to move...

- Rests where He wants to rest...

- Revives what He wants to revive...

This wind can:

- Blow behind enemy lines...

- Blow behind iron curtains...

- Blow behind bamboo curtains...

- Blow into communist countries...

- Blow through Muslim strongholds...

- Blow on Pentecostal churches...

- Blow into non-Pentecostal churches...

- Blow in spite of your mother in law...

Because the wind blew out national religion and blew in global
salvation, any heart that cries to Him can find salvation! "Give the
wind a mighty voice, Jesus saves! Jesus saves! Let the nations (all of
them) now rejoice, Jesus saves, Jesus saves!"

THE WIND BLEW GOD OUT AS A PLACE AND BLEW HIM IN AS A PRESENCE...

Throughout the Old Testament, one had to go to a certain place to
worship God. The woman at the well made this very clear. John 4:20
(NKJV) "Our fathers worshiped on this mountain, and you Jews say that
in Jerusalem is the place where one ought to worship." Jesus makes it
clear that until this time, salvation was pretty much a national thing,
and geographically God was believed to live in one place, behind the
curtain, in the Temple. He hastens on to reveal that things were about
to change! John 4:22-23 (NKJV) "Salvation is of the Jews. ^23 ^But
the hour is coming, and now is, when the true worshipers will worship
the Father in spirit and truth; for the Father is seeking such to
worship Him."

Under the dispensation of law, one was considered very fortunate to live
in close proximity to the Temple. If the worship place was within
traveling distance one could go often, but the bulk of the people could
only make the journey once a year. For many people, a trip to the Temple
to meet with God was a pilgrimage of several days journey.

The reason the people were willing to make such a trip is because the
Temple was where God lived, and if one desired to commune with Him or
have their sins rolled ahead another year, the Temple was the only place
that could accommodate such an occurrence!

On the Day of Pentecost, the wind blew God out as a place, and blew Him
in as a presence!

The result of God being a presence rather than a place allows Him to be:

- Universal...

- Interpersonal...

- Omnipresent...

When the wind blew God out as a place, it blew Him in as a presence! No
longer does He dwell merely in temples made with hands, He dwells in the
hearts of all those who believe and call upon Him.

1 Corinthians 6:19 (NKJV) "Do you not know that your body is the
temple of the Holy Spirit who is in you, whom you have from God, and you
are not your own?" We are the house of God! God lives here! If we
confine His presence to a house of worship, that allows us to live any
old way we want to, when we are not on His turf or in His house!

We are no longer our own because God lives here!

The idea of God as a place was blown out with the wind on the Day of
Pentecost, and the presence of God abides within!

THE WIND BLEW OUT THE PRIESTHOOD OF A FEW AND BLEW IN THE PRIESTHOOD
OF EVERYONE...

In order to be a Priest during the dispensation of Law one had to:

- Be from a particular family...

- Live by a certain code...

- Be trained in a certain discipline...

On the Day of Pentecost everything changed! Each believer became:

- A Holy Priesthood... Peter 2:25

- A Royal Priesthood... 1 Peter 2:9

- Kings and Priests... Revelation 5:10

Because of the Upper Room, we no longer need to go to a human being to
confess our sins or speak with God; we can come to Him on our own! (We
should have accountability partners)

The Word says that on the merits of the shed blood of Jesus, I have a
right to be at the throne of God. We are a living priesthood, and anyone
born of His Spirit has a right to come to the throne of God. Hebrews
4:15-16 (NKJV) "For we do not have a High Priest who cannot
sympathize with our weaknesses, but was in all points tempted as we are,
yet without sin. ^16 ^Let us therefore come boldly to the throne of
grace, that we may obtain mercy and find grace to help in time of
need." James 5:16 (NKJV) "The effective, fervent prayer of a
righteous man avails much."

As believer's, we're all priests unto God. The wind blew out the
priesthood of a few and blew in the priesthood of many!

In order to properly operate in the Priesthood of our own lives and
families, we should:

- Never approach God on our record, we should come on the record of His
 Son...

- Never approach God on our history, we should come on the sinless life
 of His Son...

- Never approach God on our works, we should come through the finished
 work of His Son...

When we come to Him, we come by faith that the wind blew out the
Priesthood of a few and blew in the Priesthood of every believer!

The wind blew out the weakness of man and blew in the power of the Holy
Ghost! When the wind blows upon a believer our lives are no longer lived
"By might, nor by power, but by my Spirit saith the Lord of
hosts." (Zechariah. 4:6).

Out of four hundred silent years between the Old Testament and the New
Testament Dr. Luke pens, "And suddenly there came a sound from heaven
as of a rushing, mighty, wind, (GR-violent, tornadic blast) and it
filled all the house where they were sitting."

Joel 2:28 (KJV) "And it shall come to pass afterward, that I will
pour out (Gr. Gush -- run greedily) my Spirit upon all flesh."

Concerning the wind in the Upper Room, Peter said, "This is that
which was spoken of by the prophet Joel; in the last days, saith God, I
will pour out of my Spirit upon all flesh." (Acts 2:17, 18).

The western church has marginalized the person and work of Holy Spirit.
In today's church Holy Spirit has been determined to be too messy and
confined to the backroom, or a small group. Others have barred him all
together, believing He is useless and antiquated. But make no mistake
about it:

- He is the life of the Church...

- He is the power of the Church...

- He is the builder of the Church...

- He is the direction of the Church...

- He is the protection of the Church...

- He is the revelation of the Church...

- He is the enablement of the Church...

Let me sound an alarm: THE WIND IS BLOWING AGAIN! What will we let Him
blow out? What will we let Him blow in?
