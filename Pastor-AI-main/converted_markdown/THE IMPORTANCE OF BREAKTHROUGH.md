# THE IMPORTANCE OF BREAKTHROUGH

IN THE FREEDOM PROCESS

(Sermon for 7/9/23)

Community Transformation Church is all about the Freedom Process in the
lives of individuals, but we do not have a magic wand that we can wave
and get people free. The individual must want their Freedom more than we
want it for them. Unless this is the case, everything we offer is pretty
much useless.

Our Freedom Process includes the following:

- Freedom Weekends...

- The Sozo Process...

- Healing Teams...

- Individual Deliverance if needed...

None of this will work unless the individual is completely honest,
sincere, and sold out to being Free.

While all this is important in its own place, the next couple of weeks I
want to talk about the importance of Breakthrough in the Freedom
Process. Anyone who experiences Freedom will experience Breakthrough at
some point in their Freedom Process. Breakthrough happens at different
times in the Process, depending on the individual's sincerity, honesty,
and willingness to follow the instructions of those who have prepared
themselves to lead them through the Freedom Process.

Breakthrough is an interesting term.

Breakthrough is more than incremental advancement. It is that moment in
time where everything changes in one critical moment!

Breakthrough means:

- Anointed increase...

- Radical expansion...

- A turning point...

- A turnaround...

- Exceptional maximization...

- Exponential enlargement...

The Psalmist describes breakthrough like this; It is that moment where
one could "Run through a troop and leap over a wall." It is my
prayer that everyone who attends one of the eight Community
Transformation Campuses will experience Breakthrough, followed by a life
of Freedom.

In considering Breakthrough as a part of the Freedom Process, I want to
look at an incident in the life of David.

II Samuel 5:17-25 (NKJV) "Now when the Philistines heard that they
had anointed David king over Israel, all the Philistines went up to
search for David. And David heard of it and went down to the stronghold.
18 The Philistines also went and deployed themselves in the Valley of
Rephaim. 19 So David inquired of the Lord, saying, "Shall I go up
against the Philistines? Will You deliver them into my hand?" And the
Lord said to David, "Go up, for I will doubtless deliver the Philistines
into your hand." 20 So David went to Baal Perazim, and David defeated
them there; and he said, "The Lord has broken through my enemies before
me, like a breakthrough of water." Therefore he called the name of that
place Baal Perazim. 21 And they left their images there, and David and
his men carried them away. 22 Then the Philistines went up once again
and deployed themselves in the Valley of Rephaim. 23 Therefore David
inquired of the Lord, and He said, "You shall not go up; circle around
behind them, and come upon them in front of the mulberry trees. 24 And
it shall be, when you hear the sound of marching in the tops of the
mulberry trees, then you shall advance quickly. For then the Lord will
go out before you to strike the camp of the Philistines." 25 And David
did so, as the Lord commanded him; and he drove back the Philistines
from Geba as far as Gezer."

News Flash:

All Breakthrough is prefaced by sacrifice. The pattern of sacrifice
begins with Abel's sacrifice and ends with the cries of the saints from
beneath the altar in Revelation. None of us will experience
Breakthrough, or Freedom, without sacrifice. For us to experience
Breakthrough that leads to Freedom we must be willing to "Lay aside
every [weight]{.underline}, and the [sin]{.underline} that continually
sidetracks us." Not every weight is sin, but every sin adds weight --
and the weight that sin adds, prevents Breakthrough, and aborts the
Freedom Process. Unloading the weights and the sin, prepares us for
Breakthrough, and eventually our own Freedom.

IN order for David to solidify his hold on Israel he would have to
conquer Jerusalem. Jerusalem was not only filled with Jebusites, the
Jebusites were full of insults! 2 Samuel 5:6 (LB) "David now led his
troops to Jerusalem to fight against the Jebusites who lived there.
"You'll never come in here," they told him. "Even the blind and lame
could keep you out!" For they thought they were safe." Have you ever
met a Jebusite?

- In your home...

- On your job...

- In your finances...

- With your health...

- Among your children...

Notice, David did not allow the conditions or the insults of the
Jebusites to dissuade him!

What are we allowing to dissuade us?

The Bible says the Philistines assembled in the valley of Rephaim.
Rephaim means the valley of the giants! Ever had one of those?

If we desire Breakthrough and Freedom, we will probably have to follow
David's pattern for victory! Look at what David did to get to
Breakthrough, and ultimately, Freedom:

## I. PRAY...

2 Samuel 5:19 (NKJV) "David inquired of the Lord, saying, "Shall I go
up against the Philistines? Will You deliver them into my hand?" And the
Lord said to David, "Go up, for I will doubtless deliver the Philistines
into your hand."

No Breakthrough occurs without prayer! When we are in need of a
Breakthrough, we must never assume we know what is in the mind of God.
Prayer is necessary to know the mind of God, and to chart a course of
victory!

When I mention that we must pray, I am not suggesting that one must pray
hours each day, but one must establish a daily appointment to meet with
God. We must make it a routine. When we pray, we must be careful not to
do all the talking! We must allow time for listening. Holy Spirit may
actually want to respond! He most often responds through God's written
Word! So, allow some time for looking into His Word!

Missionary Jim Elliot, who in the 1950's bravely pioneered outreach to
the Indians of Ecuador, said, "The saint who advances on his knees
never retreats!"

Francios (Fran-swah) Fenelon (Fee-Knee-Lone), a 17^th^ century French
Catholic Archbishop advised: "Tell God all that is in your heart, as
one unloads one's heart to a dear friend:

- Tell Him your troubles, that He may comfort you...

- Tell Him your joys, that He may sober you...

- Tell Him your longings, that He may purify them...

- Tell Him your dislikes, that He may help you to conquer them...

- Talk to Him of your temptations, that He may shield you from them...

- Show Him the wounds of your heart, that He may heal them...

Before the first battle David prayed and in the second battle he prayed.
Never make a major decision without committing time to fasting and
prayer.

President Abraham Lincoln said, "I know that the Lord is always on the
side of the right; but it is my constant anxiety and prayer that I and
this nation may be on the Lord's side."

David needed a Breakthrough therefore he could not afford to get it
wrong! The only way to make sure we don't get it wrong is to bathe our
decisions in prayer.

THE IMPORTANCE OF BREAKTHROUGH

IN THE FREEDOM PROCESS

(Sermon for 7/16/23)

Last week we used King David's experience with the Jebusites as the
basis for our study. I would like to continue on that topic today. Last
week we learned that as powerful as David was, he found himself in need
of Breakthrough. Breakthrough is a part of every Freedom Process.
Breakthrough comes at various intervals, depending on the sincerity and
willingness of the individual to be honest, and to follow the prescribed
Freedom Process.

As familiar as David was with the Lord, He prayed before facing down the
Jebusites, and proceeding with a Freedom Process that involved
Breakthrough. Look at what David did to get to Breakthrough, and
ultimately, Freedom:

## I. PRAY...

## II. OBEY...

David was obedient in the first battle and won a great victory. 2 Samuel
5:20-21 (NKJV) "So David went to Baal Perazim, and David defeated
them there; and he said, "The Lord has broken through my enemies before
me, like a breakthrough of water." Therefore he called the name of that
place Baal Perazim. 21 And they left their images there, and David and
his men carried them away."

Because of David's Prayer and Obedience, the valley of the giants became
the place of Breakthrough! Notice, the victory was so great, "the
Philistines left their god's behind!" That is what I call
Breakthrough!

Though the victory was great, the Philistines were not going to roll
over and play dead! Your adversaries are not going to give up easy!
Freedom is not a one-time event; it is a Process that can actually take
a lifetime -- But it must become a lifestyle! Paul said, "God hath
delivered, He doth deliver, and will yet deliver."

David's enemies said, "Even the blind and lame could keep you
out." Be assured, your enemy will:

- Mock you...

- Stand in defiance against you...

- Scream into your face, that you should give up...

British Prime Minister Winston Churchill, whose words of courage
strengthened his countrymen and led to victory in the face of ferocious
bombing by the Nazis, said, "You have enemies? Good! That means you've
stood up for something at some time in your life!"

Breakthrough and the Freedom Process require Prayer and Obedience -- But
it also requires Faith! We must learn to see what we can't see, before
we can see it! That is what Faith looks like!

Look at what David did to get to Breakthrough, and ultimately, Freedom:

## I. PRAY...

## II. OBEY...

## III. HAVE FAITH...

It is one thing to Pray and Obey, but it is another thing to have Faith
enough to transition when the battle plan changes! 2 Samuel 5:22-24
(NKJV) "Then the Philistines went up once again and deployed
themselves in the Valley of Rephaim. 23 Therefore David inquired of the
Lord, and He said, "You shall not go up; circle around behind them, and
come upon them in front of the mulberry trees. 24 And it shall be, when
you hear the sound of marching in the tops of the mulberry trees, then
you shall advance quickly. For then the Lord will go out before you to
strike the camp of the Philistines."

In the first battle David was told to "Attack" but in the second
battle he was told to, "wait". I like "Attack" better
than "Wait"! The writer of Hebrews said, "You have need of
patience..."

Patience in the battle proves one has Faith! Are we walking or running
through "The valley of the shadow of death"?

Remember, the enemy is not fazed by our previous victories! The enemy
will persevere in the face of continuous defeats! The enemy is
clinically insane!

If David had followed the strategy of yesterday in the battle of today,
he would have missed his divine resources and would not have received
his Breakthrough. If we fail to implement a GOD strategy (Pray, Obey &
have Faith), we will no doubt miss the Breakthrough!

It will no doubt take Faith to win the battle we face.

Look at what David did to get to Breakthrough, and ultimately, Freedom:

## I. PRAY...

## II. OBEY...

## III. HAVE FAITH FAITH...

## IV. WAGE WAR...

One thing is an absolute certainly -- If one is to have a Breakthrough
and own their Freedom, they must be prepared to fight for it! Every day!

Corrrie Ten Boom said, "The first step on the way to victory is to
recognize the enemy." George Washington said, "There is nothing so
likely to produce peace as to be well prepared to meet the enemy."

Here is the strategy for the breakthrough against the Philistines:

- Sneak around behind them...

- Position themselves in the mulberry trees...

- When marching occurs in the tops of the trees -- wage war...

Have you ever executed a ridiculous strategy to receive a major
Breakthrough? Sometimes we must do the ridiculous while expecting the
miraculous.

If we want an extraordinary Breakthrough, we must be willing to
implement a less than orthodox strategy.

David and his men had to fight the Jebusites -- They beat the brakes off
of them the first time around but the next battle had to be waged in a
different dimension! When we approach every battle the same way, we
rarely see victory, because every incident has a different battle
strategy.

Sally Kempton said, "It is hard to fight an enemy with outposts in your
head."

David's Breakthrough was totally dependent on a miraculous action. The
word "marching" or "going" in verse 24 is very interesting
indeed. The Aramic version say's "The sound of horses hooves." The
Lord said to David, "When you hear the sound of horses running
through the tops of the trees, attack!" the last time I checked,
horses can't run through the tops of the trees!

I can almost see the young king telling his men there will be the sound
of horse and horsemen in the tops of the mulberry trees!!!

Breakthrough is limited for folks who go out on a limb expecting the
miraculous!

The Lord was saying to David, "you will know by the sound that the
God of the Battle, is present to do battle."

I hear the sound of Breakthrough and Freedom in this house!

The Philistines assumed that David\'s army was in front of them, so they
reasoned that some mighty army was suddenly appearing behind them, to
help David\'s army! So, they fled immediately and were pursued.

I have a word for some folks in this house: "God is about to turn your
Rephaim (Valley of Giants) into Baal-Perazim (Breakthrough)."
