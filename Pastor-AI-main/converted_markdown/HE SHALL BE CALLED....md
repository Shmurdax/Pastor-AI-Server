The decision to name a child is one that will likely follow the child
the rest of their lives! Unless the child decides to go through the
legal process of changing the name, they are stuck with the negative or
positive consequences of a decision they had no influence over! I could
spend the rest of this service calling the names of people who have

represents. Every so often we run across a person with multiple names,

you discover its Prince Charles! Such a name is quite long for a child
to bear but Prince Charles is royalty and royalty calls for such a name.

Jesus was and is royalty. Even before his birth, he was a child with
many names. The prophet Isaiah, writing seven hundred years before Jesus
was born, prophesied that Messiah would have at least four names:
"For to us a child is born, to us a son is given, and the government

Counselor]{.underline}, [Mighty God]{.underline}, [Everlasting
Father]{.underline}, [Prince of Peace]{.underline}." This verse
reveals four names for Jesus and interestingly enough each one unlocks
an aspect of his character. The names given to the Prophet Isaiah seven
hundred years before Jesus' birth actually teach us who he is, and how
His character impacts us 2,700 years later! Isaiah's prophecy came to
pass, and the names of the Messiah have an impact upon us today. These
four names speak to us about wisdom, power, security, and assurance.
