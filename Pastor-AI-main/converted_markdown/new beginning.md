Can you believe it? Winter is in full swing, the trees and landscape are
dead, the weather leaves much to be desired, the year 2000 has come to a
conclusion, and 2001 is about to begin. Even though most of us do not
like winter, we patiently endure it because we know spring is just
around the corner, and with spring there will be "A NEW BEGINNING".
The year 2001 is the beginning of the twenty first century. It is my
opinion that as a church we should look upon this year as "A NEW
# BEGINNING".

God's word is full of examples of people who were afforded the
opportunity for "A NEW BEGINNING". After more than 400 years of
slavery in Egypt, Israel was given a new lease on life in the form of
"A NEW BEGINNING". Exodus 12:1-2 "And the Lord spake unto
Moses and Aaron in the land of Egypt, saying, This month shall be
unto you [the beginning of months]{.underline}: it shall be [the first
month of the year to you]{.underline}."

In order to bring this "NEW BEGINNING" about for the Israelites, God
sets a bush on fire in the desert and speaks to a senior citizen by the
name of Moses to lead them from Egypt to Caanan. For Israel the
visitation of the death angel meant the "BEGINNING" of a new epoch,
a new era, this would be a new starting point. God is no respector of
persons, what he did for Israel, he will do for you and I! Isaiah
43:19 "Behold, I will do [a new thing]{.underline}; [now it shall
spring forth]{.underline}; shall ye not know it? I will even make a way
in the wilderness, and rivers in the desert."

If you were to research the history of Israel, you would find a path
strewn with the wreckage of centuries of exile and servitude. For Israel
this "NEW BEGINNING" was to be an emancipation, a redemption, an
exodus! I'm quite sure that many of the Israelites wondered if they
would ever be able to completely shake the monkey of enslavement and
servitude, and experience "A NEW BEGINNING". The answer to that
question is that God would provide the grace and tools necessary for
them to live free from the past, but the real test would be whether they
would do what was necessary to facilitate this "NEW BEGINNING".

God desires the same for us! He wants to wipe the slate clean, forgive
the past, and make bright the future! The real question is, can you
forget the past and by faith lay hold of the future? As with Israel,
repentance and forgiveness are the foundational stones upon which "A
NEW BEGINNING" is built. To repent means to say you are sorry for the
wrong you have done, and make a firm committment never to repeat the
action. To forgive is to allow God by the power of his Holy Spirit to
take the pain out of a past experience, to the point that you have no
desire to even the score. If you truly desire to have "A NEW
BEGINNING" you must firmly plant your spiritual feet upon these two
foundational stones.

Responsibility comes with "A NEW BEGINNING". When Israel left Egypt
they were confronted with the hardships of the wildnernness, and finally
with the responsibility of driving the giants out of Caanan. There comes
a time for those who have experienced "A NEW BEGINNING" to be
schooled and discipled by processes that are less than desireable. It is
in these difficult times that the characteristics of our Heavenly Father
are developed within us. These processes cannot be hurried, rather
patiently and faithfully endured. I believe that the year of 2000, as
difficult as it was, has set the stage for God to do a new thing within
this church. I can hardly wait to see what God is going to accomplish in
2001 as we prepare for "A NEW BEGINNING'!
