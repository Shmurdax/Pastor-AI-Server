INTRO: Today we are going to be talking about a commitment to serve.
Many Christians only have a vague idea of what is involved in serving
God. Someone said, "It is true that most people would like to serve
God, but only in an advisory capacity!"\
\
Do you know anyone like that? I am not talking about anyone here today
but I'm sure most of you know people who serve God and the church in an
advisory capacity! (Anonymous letter)

There are two non-negotiable elements to serving: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN
ONE AT A TIME)

- Serving requires hands-on activity...

- Serving requires commitment...

God and this church already have enough advisors; the only openings that
still remain are hands-on responsibilities that require a commitment in
the good times and the difficult times.

When making a commitment to serve some people want to know what the
minimum requirement will be.

At times, many of us attempt to avoid a Commitment to serve the Lord,
especially:

- If it is not easy for us to do...

- If we feel inadequate for the task...

- If we are afraid of success or failure...

There are at least three things that go into finding our place to serve
Lord and others: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Gift Giving...

- Gift Discovery...

- Gift Deployment...

## I. GIFT GIVING...

1 Peter 4:10 (CEV) "Each of you has been blessed with one [of God\'s
many wonderful gifts]{.underline} to be used in the service of others.
So use your gift well." Every human being has been given at least one
gift by God to use in His service. Many people say, "I don't have any
gifts or talents." This is not a scriptural point of view. Many times
Scripture references the fact that each individual has been given one or
more gifts that are to be used in serving God and others.

The reason some people do not recognize there God given gifts is because
they have never been developed. At other times, gifts are overlooked
because they do not seem very spiritual. For example: (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- A person who is gifted to work with their hands...

- A person who is gifted with administration...

- A person who is gifted in the area of helping...

- A person who is gifted with compassion...

The aforementioned gifts are often overlooked because we do not see them
in a spiritual sense.\
Romans 12:6-8 (NKJV) "Having then [gifts differing according to the

let us prophesy in proportion to our faith; or ministry, let us use

 But [the manifestation of the Spirit is given to each one for the

## II. GIFT DISCOVERY...

In our Commitment to serve, we must be constantly on the lookout for
ways that God has gifted us. When it comes to gifts, it is the
responsibility of the believer to: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A
TIME)

- Discover the gift...

- Develop the gift...

- Properly use the gift...

How can we know what abilities we have individually been given? (PLACE
ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Pray to discover the gifts...

- Find out what your passions are...

- Look into what you are naturally good at...

This next truth is going to set some of you free today; don't be afraid
to try out some ways of serving God by volunteering for short term
assignments. If you volunteer for something and discover you are not
good at it or passionate about it, give proper notice and move on to
something else, but don't quit and sit idly by doing nothing!

## III. GIFT DEPLOYMENT...

I Peter 4:10 (CEV) "Each of you has been blessed with one of God\'s
many wonderful gifts to be used in the service of others. So [use your
gift well]{.underline}."

Charles Kingsley said, "Have your tools ready and God will find you
some work." The Lord told J. Hudson Taylor, "I am going to evangelize
China, and if you will walk with me, I will do it through you."

Ecclesiastes 9:10 (NKJV) "Whatever your hand finds to do, do it with
your might; for [there is no work or device or knowledge or wisdom in
the grave where you are going]{.underline}."

gifts! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE ED OF THE SERVICE) They need

of all nations..."
