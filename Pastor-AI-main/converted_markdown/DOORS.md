any man will hear my voice and open the door, I will come in and sup
with him, and he with me."

important. No matter where you go in the world, all buildings have doors
and no one has to explain what they are.

- Allow for privacy...

- Deny access...

- Allow for safety...

- Protect from the elements...

Literally, everywhere we go, we deal with doors:

- Some are open...

- Some are closed...

- Some are locked...

- Some are unlocked...

- Some take effort to open...

- Some open automatically...

people. The Jewish New Year which began in September, 2018 is the year

For the next few Sunday's I want to talk about several doors that are
mentioned in the Bible.

First, let's consider...

THE DOOR OF INVITATION

From the dawn of creation, it would seem that God was continually
seeking to invite man to have a closer relationship with him. Whether it
was Abraham being called out of his homeland, or it was Joseph being
obedient to the visions that God gave him. The door of invitation was
constantly left open; not that man was seeking God, but rather that God
was seeking after man.

We see this even in the parable that Jesus told of the prodigal son. The
beauty of the story is the picture of the father running to the son and
opening the door of invitation for restoration and reconciliation.
Notice the son did not demand restoration or reconciliation; it was the
father who was willing to give it freely and without apprehension. This
truly demonstrates the heart of our heavenly Father. It would seem that
the door of invitation has intentionally been left standing open.

In Revelation 3:20 the scripture records, "Behold, I stand at
the door and knock. If anyone hears My voice and opens the door, I will
come in to him and dine with him, and he with Me." Notice in this
text that it was not man knocking on the door trying to get to Jesus, it
was Jesus knocking on the door trying to get the man to open the door.

The Door of Invitation always comes with Initiation. The door of
invitation is always initiated by the Heavenly Father and never by man.

Jesus said in John chapter 10 verse nine, "I am the door. If anyone
enters by me, he will be saved and will go in and out and find
pasture." Jesus is not just the initiator but he is the very door of
invitation itself. Many have claimed that they "found" Jesus, but the
truth is that we did not find Jesus; Jesus found us. We cannot cleanse
ourselves; Jesus cleanses us.

Jesus declared in John :7, "Truly, I say to you, [I am the
door]{.underline} of the sheep." Simply put, the door of invitation
is Jesus himself! The one knocking on the door of our hearts is Jesus
himself.

Think of this:

- Jesus is the door...

- Jesus is knocking on the door... (With His voice)

- Some have opened the door... (Have been brought into fellowship)

- Some have ignored the knocking of His voice... (Story of the
 preacher's son saying, "Y'all come out, we know you're in there."

- Some are contemplating opening the door... (Make a great decision
 today and FLING the door open)

If you decide to open the door to the voice of the Savior, He will
reveal the hidden goodness that sin and the enemy have had locked
outside of your reach.

Another door I would like us to consider is...

THE DOOR OF CLEANSING

Those who have opened the door of invitation have found it to be a door
of cleansing.

In the story of the Exodus from Egypt the Israelites were commanded to
"Apply the blood to the sides and tops of the doors of their
homes." The inference is that the only thing that cleanses a sinful
person, an unholy family, or lost world, is the blood of Jesus. The
blood of the lamb referred to here is a type and symbol of the blood
Jesus would shed on Golgotha hundreds of years later:

- Oh the blood of Jesus...

- What can wash away my sin?

- His blood is precious blood...

- There is room at the cross for you...

Have you accessed the Door of Cleansing called Jesus?

Notice in Exodus 12:22, "And you shall take a bunch of hyssop,
dip it in the blood that is in the basin, and strike the lintel and the
two door posts with the blood that is in the basin. And none of you
shall go out of the door of his house until morning."

Now pay close attention to this text. After the blood was applied, they
could not do as they pleased. The last part of verse 22 records, "and
none of you shall go out of the door of his house until morning."
Many Christians get upset with Pastors who preach that the expectations
of God demand clean living. No matter how upset people become, God still
expects holiness of His people, both in word and in deed. Holiness is
still Right!

Almost everything in today's church culture is driven by the hyper-grace
message. This theological heresy says that the Christian can do anything
he or she wants because, "We are no longer under the law but we are now
under Grace. Therefore, God is no longer judging people's sins." This
is simply not true. If you were to canvas the entire Bible and look at
every instruction that God gave to man, it was not to keep anything from
man, but rather to keep things from destroying man. The door of
invitation and cleansing have been initiated, not to keep us from
enjoying our lives, but to keep us from destroying ourselves, and
everyone, and everything around us.

Notice how the text reads in Exodus 12:23, "For the Lord will pass
through to strike the Egyptians; and when He sees the blood on the
lintel and on the two doorposts, the Lord will pass over the door and
not allow the destroyer to come into your houses to strike you."

The reason God gave instruction in verse 22 for the Israelites to stay
in their homes was simple; to stay hidden behind the protection of the
blood of the Lamb! Judgment was (and is) coming on the land! The blood
of the lamb was the only thing that could protect them from judgement!
The blood is still the only thing that can save us from judgement.

CONCLUSION:

Two voices are knocking at your door today:

- The voice of sin...

- The voice of the savior...

In Genesis chapter 4, we find the story of Cain and Abel. Verse seven of
that chapter records, "If you do well, will you not be accepted? And
if you do not do well, sin lies at the door. And its desire is for you,
but you should rule over it."

- If we open the door to the voice of the savior -- We will do well...
 (Hidden goodness will be revealed)

- If we open the door to the voice of sin -- We will not do well... (The
 desire of sin is to have us and to rule over us)

Sadly, Cain answered the call of sin and paid an awful price. Once Cain
answered the door, its instruction was to kill his brother!

The implications of answering the door of sin rather than the door to
the savior, were far reaching for Cain. His decision brought a lifetime
of pain and misery, not only to him, but to his descendants.

The same is true today, the implications of which door we decide to
open, will affect not only us, but our descendant's after us.

We can either open the door to the hidden goodness of God or to the
not-to-hidden-agenda of the enemy. Revelation 3:20 "Behold I stand at
the door and knock. If any man will hear my voice and open the door, I
will come in and sup with him, and he with me."
