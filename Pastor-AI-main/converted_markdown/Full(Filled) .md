In the Twenty Sixth chapter of the book of Acts we find the Apostle Paul
standing before Agrippa about to answer questions concerning his
behavior within the religious community. He had been accused of all
kinds of blasphemous acts when in reality there was absolutely no
evidence to support the charges. In a very short period of time Paul had
gone from being a rising star, to being a shooting star, in religious
politics. By the way, that's not as difficult to do as you might think!

In this passage, Paul had been arrested and tried by the religious
authorities of his day, and because no one could find anything within
his character worthy of prison or death, they passed him around like a
political hot potato, hoping someone would be devious enough to convict
him without evidence.

Everyone in jail and prison declares themselves to be innocent, when the
evidence points to their guilt. Yet, there are circumstances where
people are wrongly convicted, incarcerated, and sometimes executed. We
are absolutely taken back by such things.

This kind of thing happens in religious circles far more often than it
happens in the world. The church is the only army in the world that
kills its wounded. In church politics, you don't have to be guilty -- If
you are accused - You are guilty until you prove yourself innocent -
Beyond all doubt.

Paul was an innocent prisoner who was about to lose his life because he
had crossed the wrong people within the religious system.

While Paul was in Ephesus for his final time he spoke of his
incarceration in this manner: Acts 20:22-23 "And now, behold, I go
bound in the spirit unto Jerusalem, not knowing the things that shall
befall me there: Save that the Holy Ghost witnesseth in every city,
saying that bonds and afflictions abide me."

Paul had every opportunity to become angry, and develop a sour
disposition and attitude. (Have you ever known anyone with a sour
disposition and attitude? Don't say amen or look around) 2 Corinthians
11:23-28 "... I have worked much harder, been in prison more
frequently, been flogged more severely, and been exposed to death again
and again. 24 Five times I received from the Jews the forty lashes minus
one. 25 Three times I was beaten with rods, once I was stoned, three
times I was shipwrecked, I spent a night and a day in the open sea, 26 I
have been constantly on the move. I have been in danger from rivers, in

Would these problems be enough to cause us to be discouraged or quit?
Would they make us lose our joy or become unhappy? All of us must admit
that we have had the sweet licked off of our candy by far less!

We must stop allowing our disposition to sour, and our attitudes to
become bad, because of the circumstances we find ourselves in. "The
joy of the Lord is our strength." We need to man up and woman up and
live a Full(Filled) life and maintain our joy in difficult times.

Listen to what Paul had to say about living a Full(Filled) life in
difficult circumstances. Acts 20:24 "But none of these things move
me, neither count I my life dear unto myself, so that I might finish my
course with joy, and the ministry, which I have received of the Lord
Jesus, to testify the gospel of the grace of God."

How could Paul possibly maintain an attitude of victory in such a
turbulent time in his life? I believe he lets us in on the secret to his
attitude of victory, even though he knew he was staring death in the
whites of the eyes. Listen to the first four words he says to Agrippa,
Acts 26:2 "I think myself happy..." I am aware that Paul was
saying to Agrippa that he was happy for the opportunity to finally tell
his side of the story. However, I believe these four words, "I think
myself happy," characterize the life and ministry of the Apostle
Paul.

He had served and ministered in places where he had to figure out how to
live a Full(Filled) life and maintain his joy in difficult
circumstances. Otherwise he could not have continued on. If we do not
figure out how to do the same -- We cannot continue on! The devil will
surely steal our joy and drive us into a pit of depression.

Paul practiced the art of living a Full(Filled) life and maintain joy in
difficult circumstances to the point that he was able to say, Philip.
4:11 "... I have learned, in whatsoever state I am, therewith to be
content." The ability to live a Full(Filled) life and maintain joy
and contentment in difficult circumstances is a level of Christian
maturity that most people never attain. There is no such thing as
Full(Fillment) without contentment:

- To be filled with joy, we must be content...

 lives...

No matter what the place or the circumstances look like that surround
us:

- The safest place on earth is the will of God...

- The place of greatest supply is the will of God...

- The place of greatest joy on earth, is the will of God...

Given the circumstances of his life, how could Paul be Full(Filled) and

Please understand, this is not mind over matter, this is about girding

joy that confuses the enemy and causes others to question why we are so

## I. GIRD UP THE LOINS OF YOUR MIND

The word "gird" as it is used here, comes from the Greek word
"anazonnumi" (anna -zone'-noo-mee). This word comes from two Greek
words -- "ana" (anna) which means "to prop up by repetition" and
"zonnumi" (zone'-noo-mi) meaning "to bind about as with a belt." The
Apostle Peter is saying that the person who wins the battlefield of the
mind will win the war.

Unless we learn to control our thought life, we have no chance of living
a Full(filled) life and maintain joy in difficult circumstances.

Always remember, the battle will not be fought with boxing gloves of
fisty-cuffs. The real battle will be fought between our ears (this is an
8-12 inch battle ground). It is here that the battle will be won or
lost! Romans 8:5-9 "For they that are after the flesh do mind the
things of the flesh; but they that are after the Spirit the things of
the Spirit. 6 For to be carnally minded is death; but to be spiritually
minded is life and peace. 7 Because the carnal mind is enmity against
God: for it is not subject to the law of God, neither indeed can be. 8
So then they that are in the flesh cannot please God. 9 But ye are not
in the flesh, but in the Spirit, if so be that the Spirit of God dwell
in you. Now if any man have not the Spirit of Christ, he is none of
his." The Apostle is saying that it is up to us to control our own
thought life.

God is not going to control our thoughts and imaginations, it is our
responsibility to set a guard at the door of our mind. My mother used to
say, "Don, you can't keep a bird from flying over your head, but you
can keep him from building a nest in your hair."

We must learn to gird up the loins of our mind by controlling what we
allow ourselves to think about. Our thoughts will provoke the joy of the
Lord in our lives, or they will provoke heaviness and depression. They
will provoke us to righteousness, or to sinful behaviors and patterns.
Philippians 2:5 "[Let]{.underline} this mind be in you, which was
also in Christ Jesus." "Let" suggests that we are in control of what
we allow in and out of our lives. We are to bind good thoughts to our
minds and release evil thoughts from our minds. (Illustrate this by
cinching and releasing a belt around something)

Philippians 4:7-8 "And the peace of God, which passeth all
understanding, shall keep your hearts and minds through Christ Jesus.
(Talk about this -- The peace of God supersedes -- Trumps -- every
problem we encounter) 8 Finally, brethren, whatsoever things are
true, whatsoever things are honest, whatsoever things are just,
whatsoever things are pure, whatsoever things are lovely, whatsoever
things are of good report; if there be any virtue, and if there be any

If we are going to live a Full(filled life and maintain joy in difficult
circumstances, we must learn to "Put on the garment of praise for the
spirit of heaviness..."

The only way we will ever be able to live a Full(Filled) life and
maintain joy in difficult circumstances is by controlling our mind
through repetition. The patterns of thought that have been worn into our
brains are of our own doing.

Are we going to think on good things or evil things? If we spend most of
our time reflecting on the goodness of God, we will without question be
able to live a Full(Filled) life and maintain joy in difficult
circumstances. Defeat or victory is decided by the way we have trained
ourselves to think!

If we allow our minds to dwell of what could have been, what should have
been, or what might have been, we will not have a snowballs chance in
Houston of living a Full(Filled) life and maintain joy in difficult
circumstances.

The word "sober" comes from the Greek word "nepho (nay'-fo), and
means "to abstain from wine. Be alert!"

The Apostle Peter say's four chapters later, 1 Peter 5:8 "Be sober,
be vigilant; because your adversary the devil, as a roaring lion,
walketh about, seeking whom he may devour."

There is a major difference between the word "may" and the word
"can". The word "may" denotes that the devil must get
permission. The word "can" denotes he is able and does not need
permission! I am here to inform you that while the devil "may"
defeat us, it is not a foregone conclusion. I rise to remind us today
that he "cannot" devour us, unless we give him permission to do so!

Peter is saying that our ability to remain "sober" will directly
affect our ability to win battles, and live a Full(Filled) life. (Song:
This is how I fight my battles)

You may be thinking, "how could sobriety affect my ability to live a
Full(Filled)life, while maintaining joy in difficult circumstances?"

Sobriety is not the ability to frown or maintain a gruff exterior, nor
the denial of circumstances in a quest for happiness. The sobriety that
Peter was speaking of is a sane estimation of the circumstances that
surround our lives, coupled with a faith that God is bigger than the
problem!

When we begin to look at our circumstances through God's looking
glasses, it won't take us long to understand that we can live a
Full(Filled) life while maintaining joy, despite difficult
circumstances.

If we cannot see a thing, we must give up, or get a more powerful
magnifier. (My vision when I was finishing up the book, The Audacity of
Prayer -- I had to use a magnifying glass -- Had to have cataract
surgery on both eyes)

What is in our magnifier?

- Our problems?

- Our difficulty?

- Our failures?

- Our all sufficient, omnipotent, omnipresent, omniscient God?

Our joy is not determined by the circumstances, it is determined by what
we are focused on!

If we are intoxicated by this world, and what it has to offer, our
circumstances will never be good enough to make us truly happy. In fact,
our circumstances will get the best of us!

Look at the Children of Israel. The spies went into Canaan to look the
land over. Caleb and Joshua came back believing they were able to
conquer the land. They said "Let us go up at once, for we are well
able to take the land."

But notice the report of the other ten goobers. Numbers 13:31-33 "But
the men that went up with him said, We be not able to go up against the
people; for they are stronger than we. 32 And they brought up an evil
report of the land which they had searched unto the children of Israel,
saying, The land, through which we have gone to search it, is a land

hearts of the people melt." They were:

- Wet blankets...

- Joy drainers...

- Dream killers...

- Faith exterminators...

- Stick-in-the-muds...

Caleb and Joshua were focused on the fact that the land was everything
God had said it would be, therefore they believed the Lord would deliver
it into their hands! (The ten spies focused on the giants -- Caleb &

They were still operating under a slave mentality -- They believed

Are we going to live in the wilderness of doubt and unbelief, or are we

When we begin to sober up from the intoxication of this world, and see

stop seeing the enormity of the battle, and begin to see the vastness of

In first-grade we all read a book about a little train that was carrying
a very heavy load. This little train was approaching a hill that seemed
too steep to climb. As the little train approached the hill he could be
heard to say "I think I can. I think I can. I think I can. I t-h-i-n-k
I c-a-n. I t\--h\--i\--n\--k\-- I c\--a\--n. I t\-\--h\-\--i\-\--n\-\--k

We will either think ourselves happy or think ourselves sad, the

In a nutshell, the Apostle Peter is saying that if we get focused on the
end of the race all the setbacks along the journey will have little to
no effect on us.

When building a house, the frame goes up quick and it's not too
difficult to keep the morale up during this stage of construction.
However, once this stage is complete things slow down and some day's it
seems as though nothing at all is being accomplished. It is during these
times that one must keep the final product in focus. The builder must
focus on giving the keys to the home owner and collecting final payment.
The homeowner must stay focused on the house warming party.

This is true of the Christian life. There is no question, we will meet
with discouraging times and circumstances but we must never fail to
"Hope to the end."

- Patience... Hebrews 10:35-36 "Cast not away therefore your
 confidence, which hath great recompence of reward. 36 For ye have need
 of patience, that, after ye have done the will of God, ye might
 receive the promise."

- The Word of God... Pentecostal people are notorious for wanting to
 live on a fuzzy feeling. I like to shout and praise the Lord as much
 as the next person, but we cannot afford to base our joy on our
 feelings -- Our joy is derived from what the Word of God has to say on
 the matter! The highest form of reality is not what the facts
 indicate, but what the Word say's. The Word of God say's we are
 redeemed! That we are accepted! That we are victorious! That we will
 be glorified! That despite our suffering here, we will reign with our
 Lord!

- Keeping our focus on the end result... 1 John 3:1-3 "Behold, what
 manner of love the Father hath bestowed upon us, that we should be
 called the sons of God: therefore the world knoweth us not, because it
 knew him not. 2 Beloved, now are we the sons of God, and it doth not
 yet appear what we shall be: but we know that, when he shall appear,
 we shall be like him; for we shall see him as he is. 3 And every man
 that hath this hope in him purifieth himself, even as he is pure."

CONCLUSION:

Living a Full(Filled) life and maintaining joy in difficult
circumstances is directly tied to hoping for this end:

- Romans 8:18 "For I reckon that the sufferings of this present time
 are not worthy to be compared with the glory which shall be revealed
 in us."
