Even though we are doing the acrostic using the word FAMILY, we will not
necessarily be going in order as my assignment is Yielding To Authority.

Our Scripture text for today points to the fact that there can be no
authority, spiritual or otherwise, without submission on the part of
those who are being led by an authority.

For starters, all of us must be under the authority of God, but we also
must place ourselves under authority to a human being. This is where it
gets a little dicey for us. We willingly submit ourselves to God, but we
have issues submitting ourselves to one another.

Submission to authority must be transacted except when such submission
would cause us to violate a biblical mandate. (Explain what that is and
is not)

Unless submission causes one to violate the Word of God, we are all
called upon to submit to all authority, and especially to spiritual
authority. Yet, I must hasten to add, those who misuse authority will
most certainly place themselves in the path of the judgement of God. The
hand of the Lord is often swift and stern, but sometimes it is slow and
sure. One thing is for certain, it grinds to powder.

YEILDING TO HUMAN AUTHHORITY

What does it look like to be yielded to human authority? (Yeilding vs.
Rebelling -- Rebellion is usually an attitude before it is an activity)

First of all, there can never be a healthy human family, a healthy
spiritual family, or a healthy local church, without being clear who the
leader is going to be, and what the responsibility of the individual
members of that family is going to involve.

To have a healthy family of any kind, a leader (Not a dictator & not the
children) will have to be identified, and the role of everyone else will
have to be established and communicated.

In the human family it looks like this:

- The husband is to be the Spiritual Authority... Sadly there are men
 who won't step up to this responsibly and their wives must do so. I
 congratulate you ladies who must fulfill this role -- I mourn with the
 men who refuse to do so -- all of us will stand accountable for the
 leadership we provided in our families -- The opinion of everyone in
 the family must not only be heard but welcomed. At the end of the day,
 the leader must lead, and everyone else must follow.

- In the Spiritual family, God is in charge... He is the Father of the
 family. We are His children -- He leads -- We follow.

- In the local Church family, God is in charge... He has a "Set"
 person in the house, called the Pastor who receives vision from God,
 and leads the family accordingly. God gives "Gifts" of leadership in
 the church -- Elders -- Apostles -- Prophets -- Evangelists --
 Pastor's -- Teachers -- Helps -- Administration -- Etc...

To answer this question, "What does it look like to be yielded to
authority?" I have chosen to use the words of Jesus. Matthew 10:40
(NASB) "He who receives you receives Me, and he who receives Me
receives Him who sent Me." Luke 10:16 (NASB) "The one who listens
to you listens to Me, and the one who rejects you rejects Me; and he who
rejects Me rejects the One who sent Me." Let us go a little deeper
to see what Jesus is saying here:

- If we receive the instructions of a delegated authority (Parent -
 Pastor), we are receiving the words of Jesus and ultimately of God...

- If we reject the instructions of a delegated authority (Parent -
 Pastor), we are rejecting the instructions of Jesus and ultimately of
 God...

In the process of yielding to authority, our attitude in submission is
vitally important. (People will say things to leaders that I have
appointed, that they would never say to me! If you disrespect the
leaders I have appointed, you have disrespected me -- If you disrespect
me and/or the leaders God has set in the church, you are disrespecting
God. What is true in the church is true at home and at work!

YEILDING TO SPIRITUAL AUTHORITY When we speak of spiritual
authority, we are speaking of those that God has chosen to place over us
in the context of His church. Hebrews 13:17 (KJV) "Obey them that
have the rule over you, and submit yourselves: for they watch for your
souls, as they that must give account, that they may do it with joy, and
not with grief: for that is unprofitable for you." Look at what is
being said here:

- Obey spiritual authority... Believe them -- Have confidence in them.

- Spiritual authority is to rule over us... The word "rule" here
 possesses the connotation of a governor or judge -- In other words,
 spiritual authority is to have the same kind of weight a governor or
 judge would have over our lives -- they have the final say!

- We are to submit ourselves to spiritual authority... The word submit
 here literally means to surrender to them.

- Our spiritual authorities will give an account for us... Here is what
 I believe this means - On judgment day, a spiritual authority will not
 have weight in whether a person is saved or lost, but they will have
 influence in the eternal reward of those they have been assigned to
 lead..

<!-- -->

- We must live a life of submission to spiritual authority, so that when
 they our leaders are called upon to give an account for us, they may
 do it with joy and not with sorrow...

- A spiritual authority giving a good report on our behalf delivers
 great reward for us... ("You have been faithful over a few things,
 I will make you ruler over many things.")

- A spiritual authority giving a bad report on our behalf will have
 devastating results... It is after the Judgment Seat of Christ that
 all tears are wiped away from our eyes.

When choosing a spiritual authority, we must be careful because
everything flows down from headship. I want to always make sure that
those I place myself under are doing more than I am, because all things
flow down in the spiritual realm.

People choose churches for:

- Proximity to their home...

- The preaching style of the pastor...

- The style of music...

- Youth Ministry...

- Children's Ministry...

- Social prestige...

A family should choose a church based on the following things:

- The whole Bible is being preached...

- Jesus is being proclaimed and followed...

- The Holy Spirit is esteemed and highly valued...

- Missions is being promoted both at home and abroad...

- Discipleship is front and center...
