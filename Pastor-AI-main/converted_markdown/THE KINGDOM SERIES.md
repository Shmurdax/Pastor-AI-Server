# THE KINGDOM

(Sermon for July 11, 2021)

TEXT: GENESIS 1:26 "Then God said, 'Let Us make man in Our image,
according to Our likeness; let them have dominion over the fish of the
sea, over the birds of the air, and over the cattle, over all the Earth
and over every creeping thing that creeps on the Earth.'"

INTRO: The first thing man was given by his Creator was a Kingdom to
have dominion over. Adam was given power to name everything on the
earth. You can only name what you own. (My experience at Hill Country
Camp "You can only control what you can afford to buy.")

Dominion sets the framework for all the desires, passions, and
activities of mankind. The desire to rule and reign is a feature within
all of us. More so in some, than others. This desire was divinely
granted in the Garden of Eden, but has been perverted by the enemy and
misguided people.

The third chapter of Genesis explains the fall of humanity as sin
entered the world, and it shows that while Adam had the authority, Eve
had the influence. (Some things never change!) Genesis 3:16-17 "To
the woman He said: 'I will greatly multiply your sorrow and your
conception; In pain you shall bring forth children; Your desire shall be
for your husband, And he shall rule over you.' Then to Adam He said,
'Because you have heeded the voice of your wife, and have eaten from the
tree of which I commanded you, saying, 'You shall not eat of it'"

Mankind's disobedience resulted in the loss of his dominion and Kingdom,
so religion became the search vehicle man created to find that Kingdom.
Religion is humankinds search for dominance.

Religion is the most powerful force on Earth. Despite the claims of many
to the contrary, everyone on Earth is religious. Religion is defined as
"The adherence to a set of beliefs that regulate the moral, social, and
ritualistic behavior of the individual."

SEVEN WAYS RELIGIONS ARE THE SAME

1. They all promise power to control life and circumstances.

2. They all explain life and death.

3. They all claim to have the Truth.

4. They all claim superiority over all others.

5. They all compare and compete with each other.

6. They all demand adherence to their particular belief system while
 denying others.

7. They are all motivated by contention and usually thrive in an
 isolated culture, that excludes other segments of humanity. They
 thrive in the spirit of segregation.

THE SEARCH FOR POWER AND PURPOSE

Every person on Earth ultimately searches for two things in life: Power
and purpose. All of us look for:

- Meaning for our existence...

- Power to control our lives...

- Power to control our circumstances...

- Power to determine the future...

- Power to predict the unknown...

- Power over life and death...

Six ways we seek power and purpose:

1. Religion - Having influence through traditional laws and theories.

2. Politics - Using the power of influence.

3. Money - Gaining control through material things.

4. Fame and Notoriety - Being widely known by the general public.

5. Recognition - Easily being recognized and feeling important.

6. Influence -- Having our voices, opinions, and views to matter.

THE ANSWER IS KINGDOM RATHER THAN RELIGION

Myles Munroe said, "90% of all the problems facing the world today are
the result of Government or religion."

Jesus's first announcement was the arrival of the Kingdom of Heaven. His
solution to the malnourished and bankrupt human spirit was not a
religion but the Kingdom of Heaven. Matthew 4:17 "From that time
Jesus began to preach and to say, 'Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is
at hand.'" Matthew 5:3 "Blessed are the poor in spirit, For theirs
is the kingdom of heaven."

- Religion preoccupies man until he finds the Kingdom.

- Religion prepares man to leave Earth; the Kingdom empowers man to
 dominate the Earth.

- Religion focuses on Heaven; the Kingdom focuses on Earth.

- Religion is reaching up to God; the Kingdom is coming down to man.

- Religion wants to escape Earth; the Kingdom seeks to bring Heaven to
 Earth.

Listen to what Jesus had to say about religion:

Matthew 23:13, 15 "But woe to you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites!
For you shut up the kingdom of heaven against men; for you neither go in
yourselves, nor do you allow those who are entering to go in... Woe to
you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! For you travel land and sea to
win one proselyte, and when he is won, you make him twice as much a son
of hell as yourselves." Matthew 15:1,3 & 6b "Then the scribes and
Pharisees who were from Jerusalem came to Jesus, saying, 'Why do Your
disciples transgress the tradition of the elders? For they do not wash
their hands when they eat bread.' He answered and said to them, 'Why do
you also transgress the commandment of God because of your
tradition?' Thus you have made the commandment of God of no effect
by your tradition."

CONCLUSION:

Christianity as a religion is:

- Well-known...

- Well-established...

- Well-studied...

- Well-researched...

- Well-recorded...

- Well-distributed...

... but little or nothing is known about the Kingdom. The priority is in
propagating the Christian religion rather than the message and concepts
of the Kingdom of God.

This perpetuation of the Christian religion and its rituals, customs,
and writs, have left a great vacuum in the world. This void can and must
be filled with only one thing: The understanding of the Kingdom. How we
are to bring Heaven to Earth!

This is the Fathers business and we should make it our business! "We
must be about the Fathers business."

(End of sermon #1)

KINGDOM PRIORITIES

(Sermon for July 18, 2021)

TEXT: Matthew 6:33-34 "But seek first his kingdom and his
righteousness, and all these things will be given to you as well.
Therefore do not worry about tomorrow, for tomorrow will worry about
itself. Each day has enough trouble of its own."

INTRO: The most basic priority driving most humans is simply
survival. We naturally prioritize:

- Water...

- Food...

- Clothes...

- Housing...

- Protection...

- Security...

- Preservation...

- Significance...

Our confidence in our Creator's obligation and commitment to sustain His
creation, should lead us to transfer our priority from our basic human
needs, to the priority of cultivating and maintaining a healthy
relationship with His Kingdom, and with Himself.

SEEK THE KINGDOM

Our instructions from Jesus is to "Seek first the Kingdom and His
righteousness." To "seek" means to:

- Study first the Kingdom...

- Learn first the Kingdom...

- Understand first the Kingdom...

- Explore first the Kingdom...

- Pursue first the Kingdom...

Seekers must have a desire to know as much about Kingdom life as is
possible. This is what we're told to do first. In other words, the
pursuit of the Kingdom must be our top priority.

The word "Kingdom" in this verse is the Greek word, "basileia."
The Hebrew word is "Mamlakah," and is translated in Genesis 1:26 as
"dominion." Think of it in this manner:

- Study first Kingdom dominion...

- Learn first Kingdom dominion...

- Understand first Kingdom dominion...

- Explore first Kingdom dominion...

- Pursue first Kingdom dominion...

The greatest secret to living effectively on Earth is understanding the
principle and power of priorities. Our lives are the sum total of all
the decisions we make every day. The decisions we make are determined by
the priorities we set.

Will the Kingdom be an add on or will it be a priority? Is it 1^st^?
2^nd^? 3^rd^? ???

SEEK RIGHTEOUSNESS

Jesus's also instructs us to "Seek the righteousness of the
Kingdom." This is another vitally important concept that has been
diluted in the waters of religion. This is a point that must be
recovered if we are to understand the Kingdom, and experience the
abundant life that was intended for all.

The word "righteousness" comes from the discipline of law, not
religion, and implies right positioning. To be righteous means:

- To be in alignment with authority...

- To be in right standing with authority...

- To have correct fellowship with authority...

- To be in right relationship with authority...

- To be in legal or lawful alignment with authority...

- To fulfill the requirements of authority...

Let's take a quick look at six areas of Spiritual alignment in the
Kingdom:

1. Spiritual alignment -- This is vertical and horizontal.\

2. Physical alignment -- Our bodies are supposed to line up with the
Word of God and be healed.\

3. Social alignment -- We are to obey the laws and those in authority
UNLESS doing so causes one to violate the Word of God.\

4. Emotional alignment -- We are to live consistent lives. We are not to
live on an emotional roller coaster, with exhilarating highs and
self-defeating lows.\

5. Psychological alignment -- "He will keep in perfect peace those
who's mind is stayed upon Him." 6. Financial alignment -- Tithing --
Generosity -- Financial abundance -- I've never seen a Prince or
Princess that was broke, busted and disgusted! David said as much! "I
once was young but now I am old, but I have never seen the RIGHTEOUS
forsaken nor his seed begging for bread."

CONCLUSION:

The secret to a full and fulfilled life is the discovery, understanding,
and application of Kingdom principles in one's life.

Religion causes us to become so future focused on the sweet bye and bye,
that we fail to make any significant Kingdom conquests. Religion causes
us to become enamored with the final disposition of the journey, rather
than squeezing the good out of the trip. We must realize that God didn't
save us just to get us to Heaven, or He would have killed us at the
altar! He saved us in part so we could cooperate with His plan of
bringing Heaven to Earth.

Religion postpones the Kingdom of God to a future event when Earth will
be taken to Heaven. I believe with all my heart that Jesus is soon to
return.

But if we are going to "Seek first the kingdom of God, and His
righteousness (Dominion), we must find a way to get focused on
bringing Heaven to Earth!

- What can we do to bring Heaven to Earth in our homes? (Live at peace)

- What can we do to bring Heaven to Earth on our jobs? (Show forth the
 goodness of God)

- What can we do to bring Heaven to Earth in our businesses? (Begin
 everything with Jesus and end everything with Jesus -- Run a Jesus
 (Kingdom) business)

- What can we do to bring Heaven to Earth in our neighborhoods? (Host a
 life group)

- What can we do to bring Heaven to Earth in our relationships? (Treat
 others as we would like to be treated)

- What can we do to bring Heaven to Earth in our church? (Serve with a
 selfless heart)

- What can we do to bring Heaven to Earth in our Government? (Run for
 office)

- What can we do to bring Heaven to Earth in the world? (Give and go)

(End of sermon #2)

CHARACTERISTICS OF THE KINGDOM

(Sermon for July 25, 2021)

TEXT: Isaiah 9:6-7 "For unto us a Child is born, Unto us a Son is
given; and the government will be upon His Shoulder.\
And His name will be called Wonderful, Counselor, Mighty God,
Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace. Of the increase of His government
and peace there will be no end, upon the throne of David and over His
kingdom, to order it and establish it with judg- ment and justice From
that time forward, even forever. The zeal of the Lord of hosts will
perform this."

INTRO: The Kingdom concept is the only one presented, preached, and
promoted by Jesus Christ throughout his ministry. Jesus repeatedly
declared the Kingdom of God and Heaven, as His principal message.
Thirty-two times in four Gospels Jesus mentions the Kingdom, yet there
is very little preaching or teaching about it in the modern-day church.

A Kingdom is the sovereign rulership and governing influence of a King
over His Kingdom. The rulership of a King causes His will, intent, and
His purposes to be carried out within that culture. Inside a Kingdom the
nature, morals, and values of the King are the only thing that matters.
The will of the King rules supreme.

John 18:37 "Pilate asked Jesus, 'Are You a king?' Jesus answered,
'You say rightly that I am a king. For this cause I was born, and for
this cause I have come into the world, that I should bear witness to the
truth. Everyone who is of the truth hears My voice.'"

The truth Jesus came to testify of, was that He was a King with a
Kingdom. Essentially, Jesus said to Pilate:

- A King is here...

- A Kingdom is here...

- The Kingdom is now available...

Three things we need to understand about this Kingdom concept:

1. A King is the central component of his Kingdom.\

2. A King is the ultimate and only source of authority in his Kingdom.

3. The sovereignty of a King is inherent in his royal authority.

Let's look at the characteristics of the Kingdom of God.

THE KING IS THE KINGDOM

Without the King there is no Kingdom. In a Kingdom, the King is the
constitution. His word is the law. His word is the government.

In the Kingdom of God, the authority of God is exclusive and absolute.
His word is law, and His will is carried out even to the farthest
reaches of His realm. And God's realm is infinite.

While this may seem restrictive or even despotic to someone raised in a
democratic environment, in many ways it actually relieves a lot of
pressure. If we are under a King, we can defer to the King's authority
and not our own. Matthew 7:28-29 "And so it was, when Jesus had ended
these sayings, that the people were astonished at His teaching, for He
taught them as one having authority, and not as the scribes."

The king is sovereign! Sovereignty means freedom from external control.
As sovereign, the King is free to do as he pleases, with no
accountability to anyone else in the Kingdom. In short, we are not the
boss over God! He is not waiting for us to agree with Him! We must bring
our wills under subjection to the King of the Kingdom!

Let's look at some of the characteristics of the Kingdom:

A KING IS NOT VOTED IN

- Democratic leaders are elected to power...

- Totalitarian dictators seize power...

- A king is born into power...

As Americans, we LOVE elections! We want to make our voices heard, and
the way we do that is, we vote! We vote on:

- Presidents...

- Federal officials...

- State officials...

- Local officials...

- School officials...

- Pastors...

- Church leaders...

In church we used to vote on everything! (Describe this -- Churches used
to vote on their pastors every year! I didn't know whether to hang my
clothes in the closet or in the car!)

The church was never intended to be a democratic process -- It was
designed to be a Theocracy -- And Jesus is Theo!

We didn't vote this King in and we can't vote Him out! Kings aren't up
for elections, and they never take public opinion polls! Kings
sovereignly rule over a Kingdom!

A KING IS A KING BY BIRTHRIGHT

A Kings Kingship is not conferred by men. A King does not rule by the
will of the people, or power of force. This is because a King is born
into it.

A King is born into power, and cannot be voted out of power. Because
Kingship is by birth, a King rules for life. Revelation 11:15 "The
kingdoms of this world have become the kingdoms of our Lord and of His
Christ, and

He shall reign forever and ever!" The Angel said to Mary, Luke
1:31-33 "You will conceive and bring forth a son, and you shall call
His name Jesus. 32 He will be great, and will be called the son of the
Highest; and the Lord God will give Him the throne of His Father David.
33 And He will reign over the house of Jacob forever, and of His Kingdom
there shall be no end."

A KING'S AUTHORITY IS ABSOLUTE

When a king speaks, he speaks with absolute authority. He speaks with an
authority that is inherent to his Kingship. He cannot be challenged by a
senate, parliament, media, or the will of the people. King Jesus is the
King of His Kingdom, and His Word is final! "His Word is forever
settled in Heaven." If it is settled in Heaven, it is certainly
settled in earth. "The Word of God is quick and powerful and sharper
than any two-edged sword..."

A KING'S WORD IS THE LAW

Because a King's authority is absolute, his word IS the law. Psalm
19:7-9, 11 "The law of the Lord is perfect, converting the soul; the
testimony of the Lord is sure, making wise the simple; the statutes of
the Lord are right, rejoicing the heart; the commandment of the Lord is
pure, enlightening the eyes; the fear of the Lord is clean, enduring
forever; the judgments of the Lord are true and righteous altogether...
Moreover by them Your servant is warned, and in keeping them there is
great reward."

A KING PERSONALLY OWNS EVERYTHING IN HIS KINGDOM

In fact, a Kingdom is the only form of government where the ruler owns
everything and everyone. Psalm 24:1 "The Earth is the Lord's, and all
its fullness, the world and those who dwell therein." Psalm 50:10
"For every beast of the forest is Mine, and the cattle on a thousand
hills."

(End of Sermon #3)

CHARACTERISTICS OF THE KINGDOM

(Sermon for August 8, 2021)

TEXT: Isaiah 9:6-7 "For unto us a Child is born, Unto us a Son is
given; and the government will be upon His Shoulder.\
And His name will be called Wonderful, Counselor, Mighty God,
Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace. Of the increase of His government
and peace there will be no end, upon the throne of David and over His
kingdom, to order it and establish it with judg- ment and justice From
that time forward, even forever. The zeal of the Lord of hosts will
perform this."

INTRO: Last week we covered the following characteristics of the
Kingdom:

- The King is the Kingdom...

- A King is not voted in...

- A King is a King by birthright...

- A Kings authority is absolute...

- A Kings word is the law...

- A King personally owns everything in His Kingdom...

Let's continue looking at characteristics of the Kingdom:

A KING'S DECREE IS UNCHANGING

In a democratic system, laws can be amended, revised, or revoked.
Dictators change and even reverse their own decrees whenever it suits
them. Leaders go back on their word all the time. But a King's word is
the law. Once a King issues a decree, it cannot be changed. Isaiah 40:8
"The grass withers, the flower fades, but the word of our God stands
forever." Matthew 24:35 "Heaven and Earth will pass away, but My
words will by no means pass away."

A KING CHOOSES WHO WILL BE A CITIZEN

Because of his absolute authority, a King determines the standards of
citizenship in his Kingdom. John 15:16-19 "You did not choose Me, but
I chose you and appointed you that you should go and bear fruit, and
that your fruit should remain, that whatever you ask the Father in My
name He may give you. These things I command you, that you love one
another. If the world hates you, you know that it hated Me before it
hated you. If you were of the world, the world would love its own. Yet
because you are not of the world, but I chose you out of the world,
therefore the world hates you."

A KING EMBODIES THE GOVERNMENT OF HIS KINGDOM

Wherever a King is, his entire government is present. Whenever a King
speaks, his whole government is speaking. Wherever a King moves, the
government moves with him because he embodies the government; the King
is the government. Matthew :19-20 "Again I say to you that if two of
you agree on Earth concerning anything that they ask, it will be done
for them by My Father in heaven. For where two or three are gathered
together in My name, I am there in the midst of them." Matthew
28:18-20 "And Jesus came and spoke to them, saying, 'All authority
has been given to Me in heaven and on Earth. Go therefore and make
disciples of all the nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father
and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all
things that I have commanded you; and lo, I am with you always, even to
the end of the age.' Amen."

The Kingdom of Heaven is here because the King of Heaven is here! He
rules in the hearts and lives of those who populate His Kingdom!

A KING'S PRESENCE IS THE PRESENCE OF HIS AUTHORITY

When a King shows up, his full authority is present. His authority does
not reside in a place or in a document; it resides in him personally.
This is why citizens of God's Kingdom in the Earth can act with Kingly
authority. Matthew 18:18 "Assuredly, I say to you, whatever you bind
on Earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on Earth will
be loosed in heaven." John 14:13-14 "And whatever you ask in My
name, that I will do, that the Father may be glorified in the Son. If
you ask anything in My name, I will do it."

A KING'S WEALTH IS MEASURED BY HIS PROPERTY

The larger and richer in resources a Kingdom is, the wealthier the King,
because the King owns everything in his Kingdom. This is one of the
major distinctions between a King and other government leaders. Kings
own everything in their domain by right of birth and Kingship. As a
matter of fact, property is so tied up with a King's identity that
without it a King is not a King.

Why is wealth so important in a Kingdom? So the King can take care of
his citizens. A righteous and benevolent King does not amass wealth for
himself, but for the welfare of his citizens. Have you ever heard of a
poor Prince or Princess?

This is why it is only in a Kingdom where we truly find commonwealth;
that is, the wealth is common to all the people.

A KING'S PROSPERITY IS MEASURED BY THE STATUS OF HIS CITIZENS

If the citizens are poor, the king is seen as a poor king. If the
citizens are prosperous, how- ever, the king is seen as a wealthy king.

Jesus never preached prosperity. Why not? Because prosperity is a matter
of Kingdom business. Anyone who becomes a citizen of the Kingdom of
Heaven automatically prospers, because the King of Heaven is a wealthy
King---the wealthiest of all.

A KING'S NAME IS THE ESSENCE OF HIS AUTHORITY

A King can delegate authority to anyone he pleases, to act in his name
or on his behalf. This is often done by issuing a "King's letter,"
which is a royal edict signed by the King, and bearing his official
seal, that authorizes the bearer to act on his authority. That is why
the New Testament says that Kingdom citizens are to pray in the name of
Jesus.

A KING'S CITIZENRY REPRESENTS HIS GLORY

Any conscientious king wants his citizens to be happy, prosperous, and
content because their status and quality of life reflect on him.
Citizens of God's Kingdom are supposed to show what their King is like
by the way they:

- Live...

- Act...

- Dress...

- Walk...

- Talk...

Kingdom citizens are to reflect the nature and character of their King,
who is:

- Righteous...

- Just...

- Benevolent...

- Compassionate...

- Full of glory...

Psalm 37:17, 25 "But the LORD upholds the righteous... I have been
young, and now am old; yet I have not seen the righteous forsaken, nor
his descendants begging bread."
