# WE ARE

COMMUNITY TRANSFORMATION

In Matthew 13 Jesus taught the disciples and the multitudes utilizing
parables. A parable is a simple, life-like story, that is used to teach
a moral or spiritual lesson. In this chapter Jesus taught on:

- The parable of the sower

- The parable of the wheat and tares

- The parable of the mustard seed

- The parable of the leaven

The shortest, yet most complex parable He taught on, was the parable of
the leaven. Matthew 1:33 (NKJV) "Another parable He spoke to
them: "The kingdom of heaven is like leaven, which a woman took and hid
in three measures of meal till it was all leavened."

The New Living Translation states it like this, "The Kingdom of
Heaven is like the yeast a woman used in making bread. Even though she
put only a little yeast in three measures of flour, it permeated every
part of the dough."

Today we are focusing on the parable of the yeast. In thinking through
our focus of each of us becoming Community Transformation, I would say,
that this church has become the yeast of Heaven to Southeast Houston and
the world.

I want to take you on a journey in the next few minutes that will cover
the last 100 years.

Community Transformation Church is the result of two churches coming
together in a merger, in the early 1970's. Oaklawn and Magnolia Park
(Place pics of these two churches on the screen -- Talk about these two
churches) (Have people to stand who were a part of either of those
churches)

In 1976 Rev. O.C. and Jackie Bates became the Pastors of Oaklawn, and
soon after Rev. E.M. Yates, who had been the pastor of Magnolia Park for
52 years, retired from the pastorate. These two churches merged, and
formed Christian Temple. In 1983 the facility we worship in today was
completed and opened on Easter of that year. (Place the original picture
of CT church building on the screen) (Have everyone to stand who were a
part of the church at that time)

In the spring of 2001 Brother and Sister Bates resigned as the Pastors
after serving 25 years. In November of 2001, Susan and I were elected as
the pastors of this great church.

Sometime after being elected to this church, I was approached by some
leaders, with the idea of selling this property and relocating to a
different part of the city. I went on a 21 day fast and the Lord spoke
to me and said, "Do not move this church, reach this community." So
for the last 21 years, that is what we have attempted to do. As a
result, we have gone from a predominantly white, older, commuter church,
to a very multi-ethnic, multi-generational, multi-site church.
Corporately, this church has become the yeast in this area, and to some
degree around the nation and the world.

YEAST LOOKS WEAK

Yeast actually looks like sand. If one were to judge yeast based on its
appearance, one would not give it a second thought, with regard to its
ability to change anything.

going downhill at an alarming rate? I am about to share the answer with
you.

start doing outreach into the community, praying over the community,

doors in this area than anyone)

a steeple -- To the church known for its outreaches." In the event of a
natural disaster, this community will automatically come to this
property before they go anywhere else. That is what the yeast of the
Gospel does. SHOUT -- "I AM COMMUNITY TRANFORMATION!"
