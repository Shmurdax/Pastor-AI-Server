# SPIRITUAL AUTHORITY

Coat of Many Colors

Gershon & Sandra Suan

25^th^ Anniversary Celebration

INTRO: Today we come to celebrate The Coat of Many Colors 25^th^
Anniversary as a church, and the Gershon and Sandra Suan family's 25^th^
Anniversary as the Pastors of this great church! (Give Honor to whom
Honor is due)

I have come to say that with the help of the Lord, "You did it" as a
church, and to this family in particular, "You did it!"

Dreams are easy, staying in place through the mountain peaks and valleys
of leadership, are the things that make dreams become reality! There are
three things that I want to acknowledge:

1) You took it one day at a time, and won more day's than you lost!

2) You pushed yourself when you didn't know whether you could
 physically or relationally stand the push!

3) You stayed strong, even though at times you had to fake it until you
 made it!

We are here to celebrate the past 25 years of life and ministry! Did you
hear that? 25 years, a quarter of a century!

Yes, we are going to celebrate the past, but I want to speak into the
future of this Pastoral family, and the future of this great church!

To do this I want to exegete a couple of passages of Scripture.

1. The first one is Romans 13:1-7 (NKJV) "Let every soul
be subject to the governing authorities. For there is no authority
except from God, and the authorities that exist are appointed by God.
^2 ^Therefore whoever resists the authority resists the ordinance of
God, and those who resist will bring judgment on themselves. ^3 ^For
rulers are not a terror to good works, but to evil. Do you want to be
unafraid of the authority? Do what is good, and you will have praise
from the same. ^4 ^For he is God's minister to you for good. But if you
do evil, be afraid; for he does not bear the sword in vain; for he is
God's minister, an avenger to execute wrath on him who practices
evil. ^5 ^Therefore you must be subject, not only because of wrath but
also for conscience' sake. ^6 ^For because of this you also pay taxes,
for they are God's ministers attending continually to this very thing.
7^ ^Render therefore to all their due: taxes to whom taxes are
due, customs to whom customs, fear to whom fear, honor to whom
honor."

Please allow me to bullet point and paraphrase this passage for us.

A) There can be no authority, spiritual or otherwise, without
 submission on the part of those who are being led by an authority.
 For starters, all of us must be under the authority of God but we
 also must place ourselves under the authority to a human being.

B) Submission to authority must ALWAYS be transacted EXCEPT when such
 submission would cause us to violate a biblical mandate. (Explain
 what that is and is not)

C) Every person must be subject to governing authorities... (This means
 to be in obedience to)

D) All authority is appointed by God... (There are times that God
 allows for ungodly rulership because of the sin of the people --
 Israel wanted a King)

E) Anyone who resist authority resist the government of God...

F) Those in authority are not a threat to those who do good but to
 those who do evil...

G) Anyone who stands against authority that has been appointed by God
 will reap the negative consequences of their actions...

H) If we do good, we need not fear those in authority...

I) If we do evil, we are right to fear those in authority because both
 they and God have a sword that can be turned against evildoers...

J) If we do good, we will receive praise from those who are in
 authority...

K) We are to be doers of good deeds, not simply because we are afraid
 of the wrath of God or our authorities, but because we want to live
 with a good conscience...

L) We are to pay honor and respect to those to whom honor is due... (We
 are to respect the office even when we do not respect the person who
 holds the office

M) The opposite of submission is rebellion. Rebellion brings about a
 spirit of lawlessness.

N) Lawlessness is the spirit of antichrist and is alive and well in the
 world and in the church... (Lawlessness is making major decisions in
 our lives without consulting those who are in authority over us --
 Pastor Vs. preacher)

O) Rebellion is usually an attitude before it is an activity... (One
 can be in rebellion without saying a word or taking an action)

P) Obedience to those in authority is an expectation that God has
 placed upon all Christ followers... (Many people claim to be
 submitted to authority, but their actions say otherwise -- We won't
 know if we are submitted until we disagree -- My service to Bro.
 Granberry at the camp) We were not born with the ability to obey;
 obedience is a trait that must be developed over time and through
 circumstances... child doesn't have to be taught to misbehave, they
 have to be taught to obey -- As human beings, submission to
 authority is contrary to our nature -- The lack of submission was
 the sin of Nadab and Abihu -- The ground opened up and swallowed
 them and all those who sided with them -- I seen the equivalent of
 that within the church)

Q) Each time we come to a place of disagreement with authority, we must
 make a conscience decision of whether to submit or rebel... (We are
 either in submission to authority or we are in rebellion -- Most
 people would say, "I am not rebelling, I am disagreeing." Look at
 this - The word submit means to "Stand under" but the word resist
 means to "Stand against" -- The decision to stand under or to stand
 against belongs to the individual believer -- The sum total of those
 decisions will determine whether we are UNDER authority or we are in
 REBELLION against the authorities in our lives, and ultimately to
 God)

R) Unless submission causes one to violate the Word of God, we are all
 called upon to submit to all authority, and especially to spiritual
 authority.

Yet, I must hasten to add, those who misuse authority will most
certainly place themselves in the path of the judgement of God. The hand
of the Lord is often swift and stern but sometimes it is slow and sure.
One thing is for certain, it grinds to powder.

2. Hebrews 13:17 (KJV) "Obey them that have the rule over you,
and submit yourselves: for they watch for your souls, as they that must
give account, that they may do it with joy, and not with grief: for that
is unprofitable for you." Look at what is being said here:

1) Obey spiritual authority... (Believe them -- Have confidence in
 them)

2) Spiritual authority is to rule over us... (the word "rule" here
 possesses the connotation of a governor or judge -- In other words,
 spiritual authority is to have the same kind of weight a governor or
 judge would have over our lives -- They have the final say!)

3) We are to submit ourselves to spiritual authority... (The word
 submit here literally means to surrender to them)

4) Our spiritual authorities will give an account for us... (On
 judgment day, a spiritual authority will not have weight in whether
 a person is saved or lost, but they will have influence in the
 reward a person will receive)

5) We must live a life of submission to spiritual authority so that
 when they are called upon to give an account for us, they may do it
 with joy and not with sorrow...

6) A spiritual authority giving a good report on our behalf delivers
 great reward for us... ("You have been faithful over a few
 things, I will make you ruler over many things.")

7) A spiritual authority giving a bad report on our behalf will have
 devastating results... (It is after the Judgment Seat of Christ that
 all tears are wiped away from our eyes)

8) When choosing a spiritual authority, we must be careful because
 everything flows down from headship. (Lady who wanted to see the
 Pastors new car because she wanted to know what her next new car was
 going to look like)

9) I want to always make sure that those I place myself under are doing
 more than I am, because all things flow down in the spiritual realm.
