foreigner, living in a tent. And so did Isaac and Jacob, to whom God

 All these faithful ones died without receiving what God had

ILLUSTRATION: A young man was listening as the great Boston preacher
Phillips Brooks talked of the great days of the past when the United
States was a growing nation. He told about the time when brave explorers
traveled into areas that had not been explored. He told of those who
risked and sometimes gave their lives as they helped develop the
country. The young man heard Bishop Brooks tell of those who gave their
lives that others might have liberty to study the Bible and obey God.
These stories thrilled this young man and he said to the bishop, "I
wish I had lived in those far-off days; I think I too could have been a
hero." The bishop replied, "My boy, every age brings its opportunity,
and if you cannot be a hero now, you would not have been a hero then."

God is still in the hero making business! You and I can be heroic if we
choose.

What kind of stuff are heroes made of? There are many ingredients of
course that go into making a hero but we are going to talk about the
three most important ingredients.

## I. HEROES ARE PEOPLE OF COURAGE

ILLUSTRATION: Two small boys walked into Dr. Mike Holmes dentist's
office and one of them said bravely, "I want a tooth taken out and I
don't want any gas and I don't want it deadened because we're in a
hurry!" Dr. Mike said, "You're a brave young man. Which tooth is it?"
The boy turned to his smaller friend and said, "Show him your tooth,
Tommy."

The biggest problem with courage is that it is only apparent in the face
of hardship.

Most of us prefer comfort to courage. Maybe that's why there are few
heroes today.

The heroes of the Bible faced a lot of hardship and when they were
facing such difficulty, God seems to model tough love: (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- When Job complained, God told him, "Stand up on your feet; I have
 something to show you."

- When Elijah was hiding from Jezebel, God said "Get up, I have
 things for you to do."

- When Moses was hiding on Jethro's ranch, God said, "Quit making
 excuses, I will have someone to talk for you."

- When Paul complained about a "Thorn in the flesh," God said,
 "Stop griping. My grace will be sufficient."

Being a hero is not easy but it is rewarding. One cannot be a hero
without courage. Courage is the resource God gives us when we are
fighting giants.

If we never go out to fight giants, we have no need for courage. Our
giants sometimes take on the following forms: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE
AT A TIME)

- A fatal disease

- The loss of a loved one

- The end of a dream or a career

- The test of our ethics or character

Some of the most courageous people I know will never make the front
cover of People Magazine, CNN, or FOX News. They are quiet people,
modest people. But they stand for what is right, they refuse to let go
of their faith in times of great stress; they hang in there when the
going gets tough. In their own quiet way, they are heroic. People of
great courage are: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Pastors who refuse to compromise.

- Lay members who fill a post of duty faithfully.

- Dads who stand against the enemy for their family. Joshua said, "As
 for me and my house, we will serve the Lord."

Dads, the enemy is out to destroy your family and tear apart our homes.
Will you be a hero?

## II. HEROES ARE PEOPLE OF GREAT CHARACTER

Proverbs 22:1 (NKJV) "A good name is to be chosen rather than great
riches." Ecclesiastes 7:1 (NKJV) "A good reputation is more
valuable than the most expensive perfume."

When the First Century Church was being set in order, character was one
of the qualifications of the ministers and deacons: (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Ministers -- 1 Timothy 3:1-4 (NKJV) "If a man desires the position
 of a bishop, he desires a good work. [A bishop then must be
 blameless]{.underline}, the husband of one wife, temperate,
 sober-minded, of good behavior, hospitable, able to teach; not
 given to wine, not violent, not greedy for money, but gentle, not
 quarrelsome, not covetous; one who rules his own house well, having
 his children in submission with all reverence."

- Deacons -- Acts 6:3 (NKJV) "Now look around among yourselves,
 brothers, and select seven [men who are well respected]{.underline}
 and are full of the Holy Spirit and wisdom. We will put them in charge
 of this business."

Character is a quality which seems to be woefully lacking in our
society. America has lost its way to a point where:

- More than half of the marriages end in divorce.

- Couples live together without being married.

- Drug and alcohol addiction are destroying a generation of people.

- Politicians are no longer penalized for bad morals.

- Business people raid the resources of their company and then hire
 high-priced attorneys to help them keep what they have stolen.

- Ministers and church members live like the world rather than living
 like Jesus.

I have good news and bad news: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The good news is that America has finally reached a point where it is
 no longer an immoral country.

- The bad news is that America has reached a point where it has NO
 morals.

Christian Dads must stand against the tide of darkness and become a hero
in their own homes by being men of good character.

Let's ask ourselves this question, "Do we want our children to grow up
to live like us?"

## III. HEROES ARE PEOPLE WHO HAVE PLACED THEIR CONFIDENCE IN GOD

Hebrews 10:35-36 (NKJV) "[Do not throw away this confident trust in
the Lord]{.underline}, no matter what happens. [Remember the great
reward it brings you]{.underline}! Patient endurance is what you
need now, so you will continue to do God's will. Then you will receive
all that he has promised."

The amazing thing about the people listed in Hebrews eleven is not how
heroic they were, but how human, how ordinary, how very much like us
they were: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Noah -- Got drunk

- Abraham -- Lied

- Isaac -- Was in charge of a dysfunctional family -- His wife aided and
 abetted her son in deceiving his brother.

- Jacob -- Deceived his brother Esau

- Moses -- Committed murder -- Got mad and broke the Ten Commandments

- Rahab -- Was a prostitute

These people had some issues, but their courage was not placed in their
own abilities. The writer of Hebrews lets us know that these people were
the essence of what faith is all about!

Many years ago Mary Lou Redding wrote, "I've grown up in a world of
superheroes. Wonder Woman, Superman, Captain Marvel, The Fantastic Four.
Ordinary people like me, without super-powers or limbs that stretch for
miles or the ability to change form. Well, they can't be heroes. Or can
they? That long list in Hebrews chapter eleven contains a telling
phrase: "They are weak, but became strong." It seems that strength
and superpowers are not prerequisites to hero-ness. God's heroes are
weak people who have simply made themselves available. Being a hero is
not ducking into a phone booth and putting on tights and a flashy
costume. It is acknowledging our weakness and limitations and making all
that we have available to God."

That is how God's heroes are born. We have the potential for greatness
in us because we have a great God. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE END
OF THE SERMON) Greatness is not about us, it is about God! Can we find
the confidence to become a hero?

We can all be heroes by faith in Christ. He will give us the courage and
the character we need in the time of testing.

CONCLUSION:

The Heroes of faith in Hebrews eleven were not perfect by any stretch of
the imagination but they had courage beyond belief.

It is time for weak-willed, pink fingered, tip-toe-through-the-tulip,
sissy men to look to God and become infused with the courage of a God
who holds the world in His hands; to step forward and become the heroes
our children are so desperately searching for.
