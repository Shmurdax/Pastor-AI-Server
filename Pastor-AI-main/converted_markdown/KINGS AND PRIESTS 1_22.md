The number of able-bodied men totaled 583,550, averaging 48,630 men per
tribe. We know the tribe of Levi (who were designated to be the priests)
was smaller than any of the other tribes. Based on the sheer numbers we
know there were more than [twelve]{.underline} kings for every priest in
Israel. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) The Tribe of Levi was not to
be numbered. As a result, we are not quite sure of the exact ratio, but
some believe the number could be as much as twenty-seven to one. If this

somewhere in the range of [twelve-to-one]{.underline} and
[twenty-seven-to-one]{.underline}! (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

Remember, the tribe of Levi was smaller than the other tribes, which
meant, in the giving of tithes and offerings, there were approximately
twelve to twenty-seven men tithing for every priest serving in spiritual
matters. This structure provided ample supply for the priests to care
for their responsibilities without needing to concern themselves with
material things for livelihood or ministry purposes.

Much of the frustration in the body of Christ stems from
well-intentioned priests who feel the pressure to grow a church capable
of providing first-class facilities, paying salaries and conducting
outreach, when the focus of the priest is to be on ministry and ministry
alone. Frustrated priests have functioned outside their call as
high-pressure fundraisers, in turn sowing their frustrated spirit
throughout the entire body. When frustration is sown throughout the body
tempers flare, offense is common, unity and harmony are almost
non-existent, and the focus of the group is never solidified behind a
common cause. A fractured vision always results in goals being modified
downward. When goals are modified downward the enemy wins and the
Kingdom of God loses.

tribe fancied themselves to be priests?

- Their enemies would have consumed them...

- The military barracks would have been empty and the sanctuary full...

- They never would have lived in Canaan... (Prayer is a must against

created subtle pressure to make priests out of every member, all the

the kings.

Priests have performed the ministry of a king in the modern church but
they are uncomfortable in such a role. People rarely succeed unless they
are comfortable with what they are doing.

Consequently, we have become a kingdom of priests, not kings and
priests. If Israel is any indication of the numerical division between

Kings cannot seem to find their place in the body. They are made to feel
