# WHEN REALITY INTERSECTS

WITH FAITH

TEXT: Hebrews 11:1 (NKJV) "Now faith is the substance of things
hoped for, the evidence of things not seen." Hebrews 11:1 (AMP)
"Now faith is the assurance (the confirmation, the title deed) of the
things \[we\] hope for, being the proof of things \[we\] do not see and
the conviction of their reality \[faith perceiving as real fact what is
not revealed to the senses\]."

INTRO: The way God sees things and the way we see things are very
different. God's thoughts and our thoughts are more times than not, on
two different wave lengths:

- God functions in truth, we operate in perception.

- God functions in actuality, we operate in observation.

- God functions in accuracy, we operate by facts.

- God functions in faith, we walk by sight.

When we see things differently than God sees them we often speak out of
what we see rather than what we are supposed to know.

This kind of living causes us to say one thing while God is saying
something completely different. This causes us to go against faith and
operate in doubt.

Operating in doubt introduces dysfunction into our lives and causes
faith to plummet, allowing our problems to eclipse our promise.

Prayer and faith carry with them the ability to move what we need, from
the Heavenly realm to the natural realm. "Life and death is in the
power of the tongue."

I want us to look at some instances where reality and faith intersect,
and the believer speaks out of reality, when we should be speaking words
of faith over the situation.

When confronted with difficulty, reality say's "It is impossible."
Listen to what faith say's... Luke 18:27 (NLT) "What is impossible
from a human perspective is possible with God."

When we are weary, reality say's "I am too tired to go on." Listen to
what faith say's... Matthew 11:28-30 (NLT) "Come to me, all of you
who are weary and carry heavy burdens, and I will give you rest. 
Take my yoke upon you. Let me teach you, because I am humble and gentle,
and you will find rest for your souls. For my yoke fits perfectly,
and the burden I give you is light."

When we have been betrayed in a relationship, reality say's "Nobody
really loves or cares about me. I must not be good enough." Listen to
what faith say's... John 3:16 (NLT) "For God so loved the world that
he gave his only Son, so that everyone who believes in him will not
perish but have eternal life." John 13:34-35 (NLT) "I am giving
you a new commandment: Love each other. Just as I have loved you, you
should love each other. Your love for one another will prove to the
world that you are my disciples."

When we have exhausted our resources,

reality say's, "I can't go on." Listen to

what faith say's... 2 Corinthians 12:9

(NLT) "My gracious favor is all you need.

My power works best in your weakness."

Psalms 91:15 (NLT) "When they call on

me, I will answer; I will be with them in

trouble. I will rescue them and honor

them."

When we do not know which way to turn, reality say's, "What should I
do?" Look at what faith say's... Proverbs 3:5-6 (NLT) "Trust in the
[Lord]{.smallcaps} with all your heart; do not depend on your own
understanding. Seek his will in all you do, and he will direct your
paths."

When we become weary with doing the right thing, reality say's, "It's
not worth it." Look at what faith say's... Galatians 6:9 (NLT)
"Don't get tired of doing what is good. Don't get discouraged and
give up, for we will reap a harvest of blessing at the appropriate
time."

When we have failed, reality say's, "I can't forgive myself." Look at
what faith say's... 1 John 1:9 (NLT) "If we confess our sins to him,
he is faithful and just to forgive us and to cleanse us from every
wrong." Romans 8:1 (NLT) "There is no condemnation for those who
belong to Christ Jesus."

When we face financial hardships, reality say's, "I can't see a way
out." Look at what faith say's... Philippians 4:19 (NLT) "God will
supply all your needs from his glorious riches, which have been given to
us in Christ Jesus."

When fear presses in on us, reality say's, "I am afraid of what the
future holds." Look at what faith say's... 2 Timothy 1:7 (NLT) "God
has not given us a spirit of fear and timidity, but of power, love, and
self-discipline."

When we face situations that are much bigger than we are, reality say's,
"I am not wise enough to negotiate this mess." Look at what faith
say's... James 1:5 (NLT) "If you need wisdom---if you want to know
what God wants you to do---ask him, and he will gladly tell you. He will
not resent your asking."
