We have established that the Word say's that everywhere the Lord shows
up there is an overflow of His abundance! As believers, does it
automatically follow that we will participate in, and be able to access,
the benefits of the abundance that His presence produces?

Not necessarily! There are certain keys that are described in the Word
of God, that if properly followed and practiced, lead to abundance.

Keys are used to unlock doors that grant access to otherwise
inaccessible spaces. Webster defines a key in this manner - "An
instrument used for moving the bolt of a lock thus locking or unlocking
something."

I want to quickly point to two keys, that if used by individual
believers, will lead to abundance. If these keys are used as a corporate
body the results will be exponential.

Three keys to abundance:

- The key of giving...

- The key to multiplication...

Many well-meaning Christians use the lame excuse that tithing is
required under the Law, but the New Testament does not mandate it. The
truth is:

- Tithing predates the law... Tithing was initiated 430 years before the
 law was given. According to the book of Galatians Abraham is our
 spiritual father, and Melchizedek is a type of Christ. Abraham paid
 tithe to Melchizedek 430 years before the Law of Moses was written.

- Tithing became a part of the Law... Tithing is 10% - Tithing enacts
 trust and faith. Faith moves the hand of God. (Illustrate this) God
 commands us to tithe but He doesn't need our tithe. He said through
 Malachi that if we do not tithe, we have "robbed God."
 (Difference between theft and aggravated robbery) If God doesn't need
 our money, why would He consider the failure to tithe as robbery?
 Because it robs Him of the opportunity to bless us!

Who would prefer prosperity over poverty? (Story of person who asked me
if I was a prosperity preacher)

Tithing is the first key to breaking the cycle of poverty and placing
ourselves and our families in the pathway of a God who "Drips with
abundance."

## II. THE KEY TO MULTIPLICATION... Have you ever wanted, or felt that
you needed to multiply something?

- Multiply ourselves to get all our work done?

- Multiply the hours in a day? (I used to wish there were more hour in a
 day -- At 65 I struggle to fully use the ones I have)

- Multiply money to pay our bills?

Webster defines multiplication in the following way: "A method used to
find the result of adding a specified quantity repeatedly a specific
number of times." I am convinced that if we desire to live in the
abundance of God, we must find a way to abandon addition and embrace
multiplication.

The Bible is full of instances of the miracle of multiplication:

- The strength of Israelei soldiers was multiplied on many occasions
 when they were hopelessly outnumbered...

- The meal and oil of the widow and her son was multiplied...

- The widow's oil was multiplied... Her only limitation was how many
 vessels she borrowed!

- The multiplication of a little boy's lunch...

I want us to zero in on this miracle of multiplication of the little
boy's lunch in Luke 9. This miracle outlines how Jesus took a meal of
fish and chips from Long John Silvers and fed a multitude of people.

Jesus blessed the little boy's lunch and told the disciples to give it
away! Can you imagine what Peter or for that part, Thomas must have been
thinking as they walked into the crowd with a half a handful of fish and
bread and 5,000 men to feed? I would give anything to be able to record
the thoughts of the disciples...

- Peter -- Walking on water is one thing, but this is ridiculous...

- Thomas -- I doubt if this is going to work...

- John -- I love Jesus, but we've never done it like this before...

- Andrew -- How is this going to work, there are more people than there
 is lunch...

- Luke -- My medical education tells me there is not enough here to feed
 this crowd...

- Judas -- All He is going to do is stir up these hungry folks and I
 will have to bankrupt the treasury to settle them down...

- James -- I know He has performed a lot of miracles, but I think He has
 overloaded himself this time...

The thing that Jesus knew, which the disciples were not aware of, was
the key to multiplication! Jesus may have fed upwards of 20,000 people
that day with five loaves and two fish. But that is not the end of the
story! They took up twelve baskets of fragments! They ended up with more
than they started with! That is because "His path drips with
abundance!"

The story of the loaves and fish points to at least two keys that unlock
the miracle of multiplication in our lives:

- Key #1 - A thing must be blessed before it can multiply! Jesus blessed
 the fish and bread before He distributed it to the multitudes. If we
 desire to use the key to multiplication in our finances, we must first
 bless them. How do we bless them? Pray over them dedicating the first
 fruit to God, and then follow through to complete our part of the
 covenant. Describe what first fruit means to us) Romans 11:16 (NKJV)
 "For if the first fruit is holy, the lump is also holy; and if the
 root is holy, so are the branches."

- Key #2 - Only what is given away can multiply! The disciples were
 handling blessed bread which possessed the power of multiplication! If
 the disciples had decided to eat the meal themselves, it would not
 have been enough for twelve people! But when they started to give it
 away, they witnessed the miracle of multiplication. If they had used
 the lunch for themselves, they would have had a couple of bites each,
 instead of full stomachs and twelve baskets of leftovers! We should
 never eat our sowing seed or sow our eating seed! We do not work for
 our living -- We work for our giving, and give for our living! "Let
 him who stole steal no more, but rather let him labor with his hands
 that he may have to give." Guy who told me, "We pay you x amount
 per week to preach here three times a week."

Even though the food that Jesus gave to the disciples was blessed, it
had to be given away for it to multiply.

I am here to remind us today that only what we give away can be
multiplied! (I am going to lean on the Campus Pastors to preach, teach
and emphasize these principles throughout this year)

Tithing begins the process of multiplication but where we get into the
miracle of multiplication is when we begin to practice first fruits and
give above the tithe!

Tithing is where we remove the curse. Tithing does not make one
generous, tithing makes one compliant! Doing first fruit, and giving
above our tithe, is where multiplication is enacted.

King Hezekiah witnessed the key of multiplication when he required the
