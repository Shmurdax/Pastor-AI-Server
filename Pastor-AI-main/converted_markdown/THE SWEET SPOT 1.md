INTRO: The sweet spot is defined as "The point or area on a bat,
club, or racket at which it makes most effective contact with the
ball."

Recently I was in Foley Alabama with my family. We went into a Krispy
Kreme donut shop while the hot sign was on. When the hot sign is on, you
can watch the process of the dough becoming some of the sweetest and
best donuts to ever make contact with taste buds! (PLAY KRISPY CRÈME
VIDEO)

Before the donuts become a finished product:

- The ingredients must be mixed together...

- The dough must be given time to rise...

- The dough must be rolled into one solid sheet...

- The round template must cut through the dough...

- Holes must be punched in the dough...

- The donuts must be fried to a golden brown...

This whole process must be arduously followed before the donuts are
ready to have the icing applied. The icing is what we are really after,
but the Krispy Kreme donut would not be up to Krispy Kreme standards
without following the entire process.

All of us would like to live life in the sweet spot, but a process is
required to get us there. Circumventing any part of the process is a
guarantee that the purpose for our lives will be aborted and the sweet
spot will probably never occur.

For the believer, the sweet spot is the place of greatest effectiveness.

## I. PATMOS IS A PREREQUISITE TO THE SWEET SPOT...

The Book of Revelation was revealed to the Apostle John on an island
called Patmos. He was banished there for his testimony of Jesus. For
most folks, a little dab of Patmos will do you!

The very definition of Patmos is "My killing."

Because of its rocky, barren and desolate nature, the Roman government
used the island as a place of banishment for criminals. The prisoners
were compelled to work the mines of the island. The Emperor Domitian
banished John to this island, about A.D. 95.

Patmos was a place of banishment for the purpose of punishment. John was
not there because of any desire of his own; he was there because he was
being persecuted for proclaiming the powerful message of Jesus Christ!

How interesting that God would use such a place to bring to John the
Revelation of Jesus Christ! John already knew Jesus, but his experience
on Patmos was about to take their relationship to a new level.

God chooses some of the most peculiar places and circumstances to reveal
the sweet spots in our lives. Are we willing to go anywhere or do
anything to get in the sweet spot? How about Patmos? Are we willing to
live on Patmos for God to reveal His purpose through us? Are we willing
to give up our dream to pursue His?

Patmos was no picnic! Patmos is never a picnic! It means "My Killing!"

On Patmos, John was subject to the wicked taunts of evil and
Christ-rejecting men! Men in authority had banished him to that island
to make him suffer for his pricking their consciences through the
preaching of the mighty Word of God!

On Patmos John was suffering, but he discovered the value of living in
the patience of Jesus Christ, not his own!

## II. THE SWEET SPOT CAN ONLY BE REVEALED THROUGH DIFFICULTY...

Think of the difficulty John had endured not just at Patmos, but leading
up to his banishment. I am sure the devil was playing tricks in his mind
with such things as:

- Where is your God now?

- Are you sure your message is right?

- If you were on target, wouldn't your God rescue you?

- Don't you think it is time to admit your mistake?

John was not only in difficulty; he was surrounded by difficult
circumstances. One of the most amazing things about this passage is
verse 10. "I was in the Spirit on the Lord's Day..."

Despite the difficulty, John had created an atmosphere for God to reveal
Himself.

I am convinced the sweet spot in life can only be revealed through
difficulty. A pearl is not developed in a mall! It is developed in the
life of an oyster and is the result of months, if not years, of
suffering and irritation.

Most of us expend a lot of time, energy and resources attempting to
avoid difficult spots altogether. For every difficult spot we are able
to avoid, we run the risk of missing the icing of life and losing the
opportunities that can only be discovered in the sweet spot.

At Krispi Kreme:

- The dough must be kneaded...

- The dough must be given time to rise...

- The donuts get punched out of the dough...

- The doughnuts pass through a frying process...

- Only then is the icing added...

No kneading - no time to rise - no cutting - no frying - no sweet spot!

Nothing could have been more difficult for John than Patmos, but listen
to his words: "I was in the Spirit on the Lord's Day." Not only was
John in the Spirit on the Lord's Day, He received revelation that no one
before or after him ever received. In fact the revelation was so strong
that he described portions of it as "not lawful to utter."

When we can bring ourselves to trust God enough to bravely walk into a
personal Patmos, we will likely see a greater revelation of Jesus
through our lives than we could have anticipated.

Sometimes:

- Rejection is protection...

- Division is multiplication...

- Separation is preparation...

- Mortification is fortification...

- Dejection determines destiny...

- Disappointment is really the appointment...

When walking through a personal Patmos, if we create an atmosphere for
God to move, we will see miraculous things take place! We will see Him
in His glory!

## III. THE SWEET SPOT IS WHERE GOD'S POWER IS REVEALED...

At Patmos, John saw Jesus! Patmos sure didn't look like a sweet spot.
But for John, Jesus took the stick of life and pushed him under the
avalanche of icing, right into the sweet spot!

(Use a stick to push a couple people around - into the sweet spot of
life)

Admiral Jim Stockdale who spent eight years in the Hanoi Hilton (POW
Camp) said, "What separates people is not the presence or absence of
difficulty but how they deal with the inevitable difficulties of life."

Most of us never get to a place where God can get through to us without
a personal Patmos! Oftentimes the only way we can receive a true,
first-hand revelation from God is to be pushed out of our comfort zone.
We need to quit struggling against the stick of life and accept what
comes our way with contentment. Philippians 4:11 (NKJV) "I have
learned in whatever state I am, to be content."

The difficulty we face could very easily be the stick of life being held
by a nail-scarred hand attempting to push us under the avalanche of
icing!

As close as John was to Jesus, it was only at Patmos that John saw Jesus
on a completely different level. Revelation 1:17 (NKJV) "When I saw
Him, I fell at His feet as dead." John had never responded to Jesus
in this way before! The stick of life pushed John under the avalanche of
icing on Patmos.

CONCLUSION:

In the sweet spot called Patmos, John wrote the last few chapters of the
Bible and they stand in stark contrast to the first few books of the
Bible:

- Genesis speaks of the creation of the sun - Revelation tells of a
 place that needs no sun

- Genesis speaks of the entrance of sin into this world - Revelation
 tells of the banishment of all sin

- Genesis speaks of the curse of sin that is pronounced upon mankind -
 Revelation declares the curse of sin to be broken

- Genesis speaks of Satan's first triumphant victory over mankind -
 Revelation speaks of Satan's demise

- Genesis speaks of the exclusion from the Tree of Life - Revelation
 speaks of the admission to the Tree of Life

If we want to live in the sweet spot we must be willing to:

- Patiently endure hardships...

- Be content in all circumstances...

- Be willing to get shoved around a little...

- Walk in the Spirit in spite of all circumstances...
