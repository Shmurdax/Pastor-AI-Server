DOMINION COMES FROM SUBMISSION TO SPIRITUAL AUTHORITY

All of us were born with a natural desire to conquer and rule over
things, property, and even people.

Think of Adam...

God placed Adam under His authority and gave him one commandment:
"Do not eat of the tree in the midst of the Garden." Then, God
gave Adam spiritual authority to rule over all created things. Three
things happened here:

- God delegated authority to mankind...

- Mankind received authority from God...

- The connection between the Kingdom of God and the Kingdom of man is a
 thing called obedience...

As long as Adam remained under spiritual authority, he had
[governmental]{.underline} authority over everything else.

Adam surrendered his right to have dominion (Rule), when he stepped out
from under spiritual authority and disobeyed the Lord. At this point he
lost his governmental authority over created things.

The desire to have dominion and rule has been given to us by God. But
God will only allow our [dominion]{.underline} to go as far as our
[submission]{.underline} will take us. Adam's rulership would only
last as long as he was submitted to the purposes of God. The same God
who gives authority can also retract it. (Saul -- The people didn't
reject Saul -- God did -- In fact the people supported Saul long after
God rejected him)

When Adam fell, the desire to rule did not leave him, but the ability to
rule was gone. In fact, from that very time his entire life was an
attempt to regain his original dominion, which couldn't be completely
retrieved. The Kingdom life is one of attempting to reach the level of
[submission]{.underline} that allows us to have
[dominion]{.underline}. "He who is greatest in the Kingdom must be
servant to all."

Even Jesus submitted Himself to the authority of man. At twelve years
old Jesus had a conflict with His parents over remaining in the Temple
when He was expected to return home with them. Luke 2:51-52 (NKJV)
"Then He went down with them and came to Nazareth, and was subject to
them, but His mother kept all these things in her heart." He obeyed
His parents and returned to Nazareth with them at their direction.
Listen to the results... (Vs. 52) "And Jesus increased in wisdom and
stature, and in favor with God and man."

Won't He do it...?

POWER GRABBING IS A PARTNERSHIP WITH SATAN

Since the fall of Adam, the [acquisition]{.underline} of authority is
an attempt to take power, rather than waiting for it to be given. When
one takes power rather than it being given, the alliance of such an
activity is from Satan, rather than from God. Awaiting the gifting of
authority flows out of a right relationship with God and His
authorities, a power grab is an agreement with the spirit of antichrist.

A person who awaits the moment that authority is conferred upon them
operates in the spirit of Christ, a person who attempts to seize control
is in alliance with the devil.

Notice, in the fall of Satan, the Bible declares that his attempt was to
make himself equal to God. The spirit of antichrist says, "I don't have
to obey rules or rulers, I know as well as anyone else how things should
be done." This was not only the downfall of Lucifer, it is the downfall
of multitudes of would be, could be, Kingdom authorities.
