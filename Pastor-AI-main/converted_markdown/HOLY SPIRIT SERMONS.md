INTRO: Most people, who call themselves Christian, believe in the
person of the Holy Spirit, at least in some limited dimension. The
extent of these beliefs has made for many interesting conversations as
well as quite a large number of heated religious debates.

Following conversion, those who have received the infilling of the Holy
Spirit as a second definite work of grace are widely known as
Pentecostal or Charismatic.

It is estimated that Pentecostals/Charasmatics world-wide number more
that 500,000,000!

To begin with, I want to point out what I believe to be several general
Scriptural facts concerning the person of the Holy Spirit: (PLACE ON
THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The Holy Spirit is the third person of the Holy trinity

- The Holy Spirit works in unity with the Father and the Son

- The Holy Spirit is the power behind the implementation of Gods purpose
 in the earth

answer the following questions: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Why was tongues chosen as the initial physical evidence of Holy Spirit
 Baptism? The tongue is the most unruly member of the body. James 3:6-8
 (NKJV) "And [the tongue is a fire, a world of
 iniquity]{.underline}. The tongue is so set among our members that [it

Before we decide to write the Holy Spirit off as unimportant or
insignificant we should consider the words of Jesus as it relates to the
importance of the Holy Spirit. John 16:7 (KJV) "Nevertheless I tell
you the truth; [It is expedient for you]{.underline} that I go away: for
[if I go not away, the Comforter will not come]{.underline} unto you;
but [if I depart, I will send him unto you]{.underline}."

In this Scripture, Jesus is stressing to His disciples the necessity of
His departure. As long as He lived in a flesh and blood body He could
only be in one place at a time. He is attempting to explain to them that
the reason it was so necessary for Him to go to the Father was so that
the Holy Spirit would come and live in each believer, thus empowering
each believer to carry on the work of Christ in the world. If Jesus
thought it necessary to depart so that the Holy Spirit could indwell and
empower believers, where do we get off promoting the Holy Spirit as
optional equipment?

I want us to look at seven Scriptural reasons for being filled with the
Spirit...

## I. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE WE HAVE BEEN COMMANDED TO
DO SO!

No doubt, every believer would agree that to disobey one of the Ten
Commandments would be to sin. What I am about to say will more than
likely be viewed as a very narrow interpretation of Scripture. I believe
all disobedience is sin.

We are commanded in Scripture to be filled with the Spirit. (LEAVE ON
THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) Paul said to the Ephesians "And
be not drunk with wine, wherein is excess; but [be filled with the
Spirit]{.underline};" (Eph. 5:18)

Listen to what Jesus has to say on the subject! Luke 24:49 (NKJV)
"Behold, I send the Promise of My Father upon you; but [tarry in the
city of Jerusalem until you are endued with power from on
high]{.underline}." Jesus deemed the infilling of the Holy Spirit so
significant in the lives of the first century Apostles that He demanded
they place their lives and schedules on hold to join in a ten day prayer
meeting! The people Jesus is speaking to here were busy people! They
were not unemployed and sitting around hoping someone would assign them
a responsibility for ten days and nights! At the conclusion of this ten
day prayer meeting they received the infilling of the Holy Spirit! Acts
2:1-4 (NKJV) "And when the day of Pentecost was fully come, they
were all with one accord in one place. 2 And suddenly there came a sound
from heaven as of a rushing mighty wind, and it filled all the house
where they were sitting. 3 And there appeared unto them cloven tongues
like as of fire, and it sat upon each of them. 4 And [they were all
filled with the Holy Ghost, and began to speak with other tongues, as
the Spirit gave them utterance]{.underline}."

The reason Jesus commanded these busy people to spend ten days in a
prayer meeting is because He knew they were going to need the power the
Holy Spirit could furnish! Jesus looked down the road ahead of the first
century disciples to a day when they would need power for life and
service! The same thing applies to all of us twenty one centuries later!
(LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) Someone asked, "Must I
be filled with the Holy Spirit to go to Heaven?" My answer, "I would
not want to go to the post office without the Holy Spirit!"

God never commands us to do anything simply for the spiritual exercise!
When He gives us a command there are always a host of reasons behind His
commands!

We should receive the Holy Spirit because we have been commanded to do
so!

## II. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE HAS BEEN APPOINTED AS
OUR TEACHER!

In our lifetime all of us have had many teachers. Very few of us recall
specific profound lessons our teachers taught us but we do remember the
lessons they taught us through the relationships they built with us!

Teachers oftentimes become mentors to their students! Jesus tells us
that by design, the Holy Spirit is to become the instructor of each
believer! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) John 14:26
(NKJV) "But the Helper, the Holy Spirit, whom the Father will send in
My name, [He will teach you all things]{.underline}, and bring to your
remembrance all things that I said to you."

When we receive the infilling of the Holy Spirit we receive a heightened
awareness of the will of God, as well as a heightened sensitivity to the
voice of the Holy Spirit.

When a person has received the infilling of the Holy Spirit, this
teacher opens the Word of God as never before! The truths of God\'s Word
seem to come alive to the believer!

Because this teacher lives within us, He is able to bring back to our
remembrance the Word of God at a moment's notice! This teacher speaks to
us from within, when we are doing right, and when we are doing wrong!

The closer the relationship we develop with the teacher, the more we are
capable of learning! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) It
is not the responsibility of the mentor to make time for the disciple;
it is the responsibility of the disciple to carve out time to spend at
the feet of the mentor! The more shallow the relationships with the
teacher, the less we are capable of learning! We are as close to Him as
we want to be!

We should receive the Holy Spirit to receive Godly instruction!

## III. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE HAS BEEN APPOINTED
AS OUR GUIDE!

A guide is defined as one who knows the way, usually because they have
already been there. If I am going to follow a guide, I want to know that
they know what they are doing and where they are going! (My experience
in a helicopter at Mt. Rushmore)

There is a terrific difference between a pioneer and a guide. A pioneer
is blazing a trail in hopes of developing a path for others to follow. A
guide is one who already knows the way and leads those who are less
knowledgeable along the path. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT
SLIDE)

John 16:12-15 (NKJV) "I still have many things to say to you, but you
cannot bear them now.13 "However, when He, the Spirit of truth, has
come, [He will guide you into all truth]{.underline}; for He will not
speak on His own authority, but whatever He hears He will speak; and [He
will tell you things to come]{.underline}.14 "He will glorify Me, for
[He will take of what is Mine and declare it to you]{.underline}.15 "All
things that the Father has are Mine. Therefore I said that [He will take
of Mine and declare it to you]{.underline}."

The work of the Holy Spirit in the life of the believer is that of
revelation. He has no agenda of His own; He is simply a guide who
discovers what the Father desires in our lives and reveals the path of
travel to each believer.

A portion of the work of the Holy Spirit consists of revealing the will
of God for ministry to each believer! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE
NEXT SLIDE) Acts 13:2-4 (NKJV) "As they ministered to the Lord and
fasted, [the Holy Spirit said]{.underline}, "Now [separate to Me
Barnabas and Saul for the work to which I have called
them]{.underline}."3 Then, having fasted and prayed, and laid hands on
them, they sent them away. 4 So, [being sent out by the Holy
Spirit]{.underline}, they went down to Seleucia, and from there they
sailed to Cyprus."

There is an old song that say\'s "The Lord knows the way through the
wilderness, all I have to do is follow." If the Lord knows the way,
which He does; He then reveals it to the Holy Spirit; who in turn
reveals it to the believer.

The Holy Spirit is our guide we must follow His instructions implicitly!

We should receive the Holy Spirit because He can reveal the path of
life!

## IV. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE EMPOWERS US FOR
SERVICE!

It is my opinion that all who have received Jesus as Lord and Savior
will have a desire to serve Him and others. In the Old Testament the
priest would anoint his right ear, right thumb and right great toe. This
symbolized: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Having the mind of Christ...

- Doing the work of Christ...

- Walking the walk of Christ...

The three things every believer must do in order to please God is to:
(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Have the mind of Christ!

- Do the work of Christ!

- Walk the walk of Christ!

Serving strictly from duty or obligation runs a little thin pretty
quick. If we are to serve effectively over the long haul of our
Christian lives we must operate in the power of the Spirit, rather than
functioning within our own abilities! Acts 1:8 (NKJV) "But [you shall
receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you]{.underline}; and
you shall be witnesses to Me in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria,
and to the end of the earth."

Some people think they can do the work of God through their own ability!
This is impossible over the long haul! Jesus needed the empowerment of
the Holy Spirit to do what He was able to do! Acts 10:38 (NKJV) "How
[God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Spirit and with
power]{.underline}, who went about doing good and healing all who were
oppressed by the devil, for God was with Him."

The Apostle Paul was aware of his need for the anointing and empowerment
of the Holy Spirit to do the work of God. 2 Corinthians 3:2-6 (NKJV)
"You are our epistle written in our hearts, known and read by all
men;3 clearly you are an epistle of Christ, ministered by us, written
not with ink but [by the Spirit of the living God]{.underline}, not on
tablets of stone but on tablets of flesh, that is, of the heart. 4 And
we have such trust through Christ toward God.5 [Not that we are
sufficient of ourselves]{.underline} to think of anything as being from
ourselves, but [our sufficiency is from God]{.underline},6 who also made
us sufficient as ministers of the new covenant, [not of the letter but
of the Spirit]{.underline}; for the letter kills, but the Spirit gives
life."

If Jesus and Paul needed the empowerment of the Holy Spirit, we will
surely need His help! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE)

We should receive the Holy Spirit to be empowered for service!

## V. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE WILL PRAY THROUGH US
WHEN WE DO NOT KNOW WHAT TO PRAY FOR! One of the works of the Holy
Spirit in the life of the believer is to enhance and make more effective
his or her prayer life. Romans 8:26-27 (NKJV) "Likewise the Spirit
also helps in our weaknesses. For we do not know what we should pray for
as we ought, but the Spirit Himself makes intercession for us [with
groanings which cannot be uttered]{.underline}.27 Now He who searches
the hearts knows what the mind of the Spirit is, because [He makes
intercession for the saints according to the will of
God]{.underline}."

Think of this: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- When we do not know what to pray for, the Holy Spirit knows exactly
 what to pray for!

- When we do not know how to pray because we do not know what the will
 of the Father is, the Holy Spirit is never in question about the will
 of God!

- When we have exhausted our vocabulary, the Spirit has not exhausted
 His!

The Holy Spirit anoints us to pray in two very distinct ways: 1
Corinthians 14:15 (NKJV) "I will pray with the spirit, and I will
also pray with the understanding. I will sing with the spirit, and I
will also sing with the understanding." (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A
TIME)

- In the Spirit - In Tongues!

- In the Spirit - In our native language! (Being asked to pray in the
 Arkansas Governors mansion) (Praying over a house where some strange
 activity had been going on)

I am sure all of us have felt at times that our prayer life was somewhat
deficient and inadequate. The Holy Spirit has been appointed to be our
helper in prayer. What we lack, He makes up for. The most effective
praying is Holy Spirit anointed praying!

We should receive the Holy Spirit and allow Him to pray through us!

## VI. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE IS TO BE OUR
COMFORTER! John 15:26 (KJV) "But when the [Comforter]{.underline}
is come, whom I will send unto you from the Father, even the Spirit of
truth, which proceedeth from the Father, he shall testify of me:"

On numerous occasions in the Bible, the Holy Spirit is referred to as
the comforter. Because of the times we are living in, when we use the
word comforter, all kinds of images begin to manifest themselves, most
of which do not lend themselves to a true assessment of what is being
portrayed in Scripture. The image we come up with is probably a person
who is weak and frail but willing to sympathize with our every need and
concern. We might even visualize a weak frail person who is content to
hang around a funeral home and cry with everyone who comes through the
door.

When the Scripture uses the word comforter there is an attempt to say
something far more significant.

The Greek word for Comforter is Paraklete which means: (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- One called alongside for the purpose of assistance (Like a big
 brother)

- One who pleads another\'s cause before a judge (He is our legal
 representation)

- A counselor for the purpose of a defense (He is our defense lawyer
 when we stand accused) (Talk about pre-paid legal fees)

- A legal assistant (He researches all similar cases and furnishes the
 information to the judge)

- An intercessor (He is our most able advocate at Heavens court)

- A helper (Someone to assist in all situations)

Do you get the picture? The comforter is a constant abiding presence
which is capable of assisting the believer in all situations!

Life can be extremely difficult not to mention complicated! All of us
experience times in life when we need the help of others. Though some
people attempt to isolate themselves, we are not engineered to deal with
life\'s problems on our own! God said of Adam, Genesis 2:18, 21-23
(NKJV) "And the [Lord]{.smallcaps} God said, "[It is not good that
man should be alone]{.underline}; I will make him [a helper]{.underline}
comparable to him."21 And the [Lord]{.smallcaps} God caused a deep sleep
to fall on Adam, and he slept; and He took one of his ribs, and closed
up the flesh in its place.22 Then the rib which the [Lord]{.smallcaps}
God had taken from man [He made into a woman]{.underline}, and He
brought her to the man.23 And Adam said: "This is now bone of my bones
And flesh of my flesh; She shall be called Woman, Because she was taken
out of Man."

It is a wonderful thing to have family or friends who are closely enough
connected that we feel free to enlist their help in difficult times. To
walk through an extremely difficult time in life is bad enough, but to
walk it out alone is even worse.

Why would anyone resist such help?

We should receive the Holy Spirit to access maximum assistance from the
Heavenly Father!

## VII. WE SHOULD RECEIVE THE HOLY SPIRIT BECAUSE HE CONVICTS US OF
SIN!

Most Christians would agree that a person cannot come to Christ apart
from the conviction of the Holy Spirit. John 6:44 (NKJV) "No one can
come to Me unless [the Father who sent Me draws him]{.underline}..."
How does the Father draw us? John 16:5-8 (LB) "But now I am going
away to the one who sent me; and none of you seems interested in the
purpose of my going; none wonders why. 6 Instead you are only filled
with sorrow. 7 But the fact of the matter is that it is best for you
that I go away, for if I don't, the Comforter won't come. If I do, he
will---for I will send him to you. 8 "And when he has come he will
[convince the world of its sin]{.underline}, and of the availability of
God's goodness, and of deliverance from judgment." A great portion
of the work of the Holy Spirit is to make the unrepentant sinner fully
aware of his hopeless eternal condition. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL
THE NEXT SLIDE)

Another aspect of the work of the Holy Spirit is to make Christians
aware of their failures and sins! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT
SLIDE) That's right, when we who are believers, fail, the Holy Spirit
becomes the still small voice inside us which causes us to feel guilty
concerning our sin. His purpose is not to make us feel like a failure
but to bring us to repentance.

Listen to the importance of walking in the Spirit of God each day of our
lives. Galatians 5:16 (NKJV) "I say then: Walk in the Spirit, and
[you shall not fulfill the lust of the flesh]{.underline}."
Galatians 5:24-25 (NKJV) "And [those who are Christ's have crucified
the flesh with its passions and desires]{.underline}.25 If we live in
the Spirit, let us also walk in the Spirit."

If it is true that a portion of the work of the Holy Spirit is to help
us walk out our life in Christ, why would we not want as much of the
influence of the Spirit in our lives as possible?

Sometimes what the Word does not say is almost as important as what it
does say. If it say\'s that "If we walk in the Spirit we will not
fulfill the lusts of the flesh", then it also infers that "If we
do not walk in the Spirit we will fulfill the lusts of the flesh."

Those who have a passion to walk as close to Jesus as possible will
eventually find the path to Holy Spirit baptism. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN
UNTIL THE END OF THE SERVICE) Once they step into this new dimension
many things will change in their lives but one of the most significant
changes will be the heightened awareness, of what pleases God and what
does not.

We should receive the Holy Spirit to allow Him to convict us of our sin!
