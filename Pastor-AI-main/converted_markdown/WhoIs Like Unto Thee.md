## V. THEY HAVE NOSES, BUT THEY SMELL NOT!

A. Of all these comparisons to our God this one seems to be the
strangest of them all! What does a "NOSE", and the sense of
"SMELL" have to do with God anyway?

Forty Four times the Old Testament uses the word "SAVOUR" when
describing the flesh of a burnt offering burning before the Lord. The
same word is used when describing incense burning upon the Altar of God.

Old Testament worship was characterized by unique odors. (Flesh burning
upon the Altars of Israel, Incense burning in the worship place.)

Almost always the word "SAVOUR" is preceded by the word "SWEET"!
(Only four of Forty Four times does the word "SWEET" fail to appear
before the word "SAVOUR"!)

The word "SAVOUR" is used on a very limited basis in the New
Testament. It appears only Seven times. Of that Seven times, Five times
it refers to the sense of "SMELL", and is preceded by the word
"SWEET" only Twice.

The word "SAVOUR" comes from the Hebrew word "REYACH"
(ray'-akh), and means an odor, scent, or smell, as if being blown
through the air!

The word "SWEET" comes from the Hebrew word "NIYCHOWACH"
(nee-kho'-akh), and means a pleasant odor!

B. In Old Testament worship the burning of flesh upon the altars,
and the offering of incense in the worship was said to be a "SWEET"

"SWEET SMELLING SAVOUR" in the "NOSTRILS" of God!

knowledge of God in this earth we are sending up a "SWEET" smelling
"SAVOUR" into the "NOSTRILS" of God! "But thanks be to God,
who always leads us in triumphal procession in Christ and through us

to God the aroma of Christ (A sweet savour of Christ)among those who are

According to Ephesians 5:1-2 the sacrifice of Jesus was a "SWEET
SMELLING SAVOUR" before God! The way we stir up a "SMELL" that God
