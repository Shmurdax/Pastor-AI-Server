ever sinned, God developed a plan to forgive the sin!

The bottom line is this, sin carries a high cost but before the cost was

## II. THE SINNER AND GRACE

Romans 5:20 (NKJV) "... But where sin abounded, grace abounded much
more," In the case of the woman caught in the act of adultery, sin
abounded in her life! This was probably not her first affair! Almost
everyone I know who has been caught in such a sin claims this was their
first offense. If this is the case they are either the most inauspicious
person on the earth or they are as dumb as a box of rocks!

A person who gets caught in such a failure usually has developed a
lifestyle which has become very careless. God ALWAYS sends mercy before
he sends judgment but you can rest assured, if you do not take His
mercy, you will get His judgment. The Judgment of God is a last ditch
effort to extend grace! Matthew 5:27-30 (KJV) "Ye have heard that it
was said by them of old time, Thou shalt not commit adultery: But I
say unto you, That whosoever looketh on a woman to lust after her hath
committed adultery with her already in his heart. And if thy right
eye offend thee, pluck it out, and cast it from thee: for it is
profitable for thee that one of thy members should perish, and not that
thy whole body should be cast into hell. And if thy right hand
offend thee, cut it off, and cast it from thee: for [it is profitable
for thee that one of thy members should perish, and not that thy whole
body should be cast into hell]{.underline}."

The good news for the sinner is that where sin abounds, grace is more
prevalent. Show me a place where sin abounds and I will show you a place
where grace is capable of making a major difference.

Romans 5:20 (NKJV) "... But where sin abounded, grace abounded much
more," Look at the word abounds in Scripture. There are two Greek
words for this one English word.:

- Sin abounded - pleonazo (pleh-on-ad'-zo -- flay-on-odd-zo) To exist in
 abundance. To increase.

- Grace abounded - huperperisseuo (hoop-er-per-is-syoo'-o --
 hooper-per-is-o) To abound beyond measure. To abound exceedingly. To
 overflow.

In other words, where sin abounds it is restricted by certain limits.
Where Grace abounds there is absolutely no limitations.

## III. THE BELIEVER, SIN AND GRACE

1 John 1:9 (AV) "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to
forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness." We
have talked about the sinner and law, the sinner and grace, now I want
us to focus on what happens when a saint sins! What? Can there be such a
thing as a sinning saint?

I want to ask a question "Is there anyone present who has not committed
a sin since you were converted?" I rest my case, the fact is, all of us
have sinned, both before and after conversion. So what can be done when
a believer sins? 1 John 2:1-2 (CEV) "My children, I am writing this
so that you won't sin. But [if you do sin]{.underline}, Jesus Christ
always does the right thing, [and he will speak to the Father for
us]{.underline}. [Christ is the sacrifice that takes away our
sins]{.underline} and [the sins of all the world's
people]{.underline}." There are five things in this Scripture worth
paying attention to:

- We are instructed not to sin -- The Christian should be attempting to
 defeat sin at all times. (There are more ways to live above sin than
 to rent a room above a bar!)

- If we sin a path to purity has been provided -- There is forgiveness
 for the believer who finds his/her self having failed.

- Jesus pleads our case before the Father -- He is our attorney! "He
 ever lives to make intercession for us."

- The sacrifice of Jesus is for the believer -- John is writing to
 believers when He say's "Christ is the sacrifice that takes away
 our sins." Here he states that forgiveness is for every believer
 and even includes himself in the equation.

- The sacrifice of Jesus is for the lost - "...and [the sins of all
 the world's people]{.underline}."

The forgiveness which Christ provides is not just a matter of expunging
the record of our offences but actually "...cleanses us from all
unrighteousness."

Other instructions are given for what should happen when a saint sins.
Galatians 6:1 (CEV) "Brethren, if a man is [overtaken in any
trespass]{.underline}, [you who are spiritual]{.underline} [restore such
a one]{.underline} [in a spirit of gentleness]{.underline}, considering
yourself [lest you also be tempted]{.underline}." The writer of
Galatians gives us a five point outline for dealing with the sins of the
saints:

- The sinning saint must be confronted -- Allowing people to continue in
 sin is neither profitable to them nor the Kingdom of God.

- Restoration is the order of the day -- When a saint sins restoration
 and redemption should be the intention of the body of Christ.

- Spiritual people should provide a path to restoration -- Usually this
 should be church leaders or their designees. The people who know about
 the failure should be kept to a minimum. This was not the case in the
 woman caught in the act of adultery. Mercy demands confidentiality;
 religion demands public humiliation!

- Restoration should be accomplished through gentleness -- Nothing is to
 be gained with harshness. A sinning saint is already dealing with
 enough condemnation, the last thing they need is blame being assigned
 by fellow believers.

- Exercise caution -- Great caution should be exercised when the
 restoration process is in operation. Compromise is not what is needed;
 bringing justice and mercy together is what is desired. The tendency
 is to spin the failure so that it does not appear to be so bad; the
 danger is that even spiritual people can fall into the same trap as
 the sinning saint has fallen into.

The sacrifice of Jesus does not just provide forgiveness; it provides
complete pardon from sins activities! The woman caught in the act of
adultery in John eight experienced this very thing. John 8:9-10 (CEV)
"Finally, Jesus and the woman were there alone. Jesus stood up
and asked her, "Where is everyone? Isn't there anyone left to accuse
you?" "No sir," the woman answered. Then Jesus told her, "[I am not
going to accuse you either. You may go now, but don't sin
anymore]{.underline}."
