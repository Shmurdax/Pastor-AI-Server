INTRO: The dwelling place of God, among the Israeli people, was
within the Holy of Holies. Inside the Holy of Holies was an interesting
piece of furniture called the Ark of the Covenant of the Lord.

Everywhere the Israelites went the Ark went first. Moses said, "If
Your presence goes not with us, carry us not up hence."

The Ark was to be carried by the Priests and no one was to touch it. It
was to be carried on the shoulders of the Priests utilizing staves that
were fitted into the rings on each end of the Ark.

Everywhere the Ark traveled there was great victory. So much so that the
Philistines recognized the supernatural power that surrounded the Ark
and decided that if they could capture it, they might become the
beneficiaries of its blessings.

 

When Israel engaged in battle with the Philistines at Ebenezer and
Aphek, they found themselves on the losing end of the conflict, and as
messed up as they were, they called for the Ark.

They likely called for it because it had brought great victory in the
past. Please allow me to point out an often overlooked fact. 1 Samuel
4:5 (KJV) "And when the ark of the covenant of the [Lord]{.smallcaps}
came into the camp, all Israel shouted with a great shout, so that the
earth rang again."

Just because we are excited about the presence of God is no indication
that we are ready for it. No amount of shouting can compensate for sin
in the camp.

Just because there is a lot of noise in a church is no indication that
everything is kosher.

 

This time the presence of the Ark did not convey victory, and Israel
lost thirty thousand more men in the process. When the dust settled, the
dead included Hophni and Phinehas, the evil sons of Eli who were playing
a priestly role.

When Eli heard the news of his sons' death, his only concern was for the
status of the Ark. When he heard of its loss, he passed out, fell
backwards, and broke his neck in the fall.

The final victim on that infamous day was Phinehas' unnamed wife, who
used her final breath to name her child Ichabod, meaning, "The glory
of the Lord has departed."

The capture of the Ark of the Covenant by the Philistines was the
fulfillment of prophecies spoken against the house of Eli.

 

What we allow at God's house, He will allow at our house!

The Philistines assumed the capture of the Ark would acquire the favor
they had witnessed happening to the Israelites. Rather than favor, it
brought curses on them.

The Ark was taken to Ashdod and was put in the temple of the Philistine
god, Dagon. However, this synthetic god could not stand in the presence
of the Ark.

In the seven months that it resided in the land of Philistia, the cities
Ashdod, Gath, and Ekron experienced what was likely the bubonic plague
and was overrun with rats.

After seven months of calamity, the

Philistines decided to see if the presence of the Ark was the culprit to
their calamity. They had a brand new cart made and decided to have it
pulled by never-before-

harnessed cows that had young calves that they placed in a stall. They
placed the Ark on the cart, and disgustingly, they also included
replicas of the rats and tumors made of gold.

The Philistine experts in the supernatural made this a litmus test to
determine whether the Ark was the problem or if their problems were just
coincidental.

The test would be that if the cows head straight back to where the Ark
came from, toward Beth-she-mesh, rather than going to where their calves
were locked up, they would conclude this catastrophe was indeed a divine
judgment. But if not, they would know that God had nothing to do with
it---it was just a coincidence.

God, in His wonderful response to queries, caused two un-driven cows
that had never pulled a cart, who were mooing with hunger

and leaving their calves behind, to walk straight from Ek-ron to
Beth-she-mesh, a border town of Israel. Amazingly, the cows never left
the road.

From Beth-she-mesh, the Ark was moved to Kir-jath-je-a-rim, where it
stayed in the house Abinadab. (About 50 miles from the Tabernacle of
Moses)

During this time, a priest from the line of Aaron did not attend the
Ark, but an appointed priest, Eleazar, Abinadab's son, attended the Ark.
(From the time the Ark was captured until it arrived in Jerusalem - 92
years)

Though not much is said about blessings or curses during this ninety-two
year span, the fact is that during this time there was a widespread,
fearful movement toward God.

 

While the Ark rested in the quiet, insignificant, obscure house of
Abinadab:

- Samuel grew old...

- The people demanded a King...

- Saul was anointed king...

- Saul was defeated by the Philistines...

- David was anointed king...

- David reigned for seven years from

Hebron...

All the while the Ark had been relegated to the backroom, I mean the
house of Abinadab.

It was not until David finally conquered Jerusalem in the seventh year
of his reign that he called for them to bring the ark to Jerusalem, the
city of David.

In preparation for the trip from the house of Abinadab to Jerusalem, a
new cart was constructed! The nation had witnessed the Philistines
getting by with carrying the Ark

on a cart and they thought they could do the same thing! Since when does
the church take its cues from the world?

This is where the modern church is at practically, operationally, and
theologically.

The church was never intended to adapt to the culture, it was intended
to establish the culture.

Just because the world is doing it does not mean that we should!

They built a new cart and loaded the Ark onto it and a group of very
capable men were assigned to attend it.

At the threshing floor of Nachon, the oxen stumbled and it appeared that
the Ark would be tossed to the ground.

Abinadab's son Uzzah, who had been assigned to accompany the ark, put
out his

hand to steady it. To the shock and dismay of everyone involved, Uzzah
was immediately struck dead.

Not even those whose duty it was to bear the ark were allowed to touch
it. The history of improper conveyance of the Ark should have been
enough to warn them of the danger that lurked nearby. On one occasion
fifty thousand men had perished because they looked into the Ark.

Uzzah, in his zeal and hot, impatient eagerness, tried to steady the ark
and as a result he was struck dead.

I believe this is a warning for all those in this day who are making
attempts to steady the Ark. Here is what an attempt to steady the Ark
looks like today:

- A failure to preach the whole counsel of God...

- A determination to engage people with no plan for real life change...

- A failure to introduce people to the Holiness of God...

- Believing that methods don't matter, motives are the important
 thing...

- Relegating the work of the Holy Spirit to a back room or beyond the
 property boundary... (This is done so that givers won't be offended)

- Pentecostal denominations being led into ecumenicalism at the expense
 of who they are...

Think about Uzzah's life. He was the son of Abinadab, in whose house the
Ark had been stored for so many years. He had grown up with the Ark in
his house. He and his brother were charged with taking care of it as it
made its way from their home to its new resting place in Jerusalem.

If we put ourselves in the place of Uzzah, it's easy to understand why
he would do so. He was used to having it around -- although he knew it
was holy, it was also a familiar thing. It seemed perfectly natural to
try to steady it, to keep it from falling.

Could it be that those of us in this room who have contended for
Pentecost are just as guilty of a lack of respect for the presence of

God as those who have relegated His presence to a back room or abandoned
it altogether?

Being a champion of Pentecost is not enough! Believing in the
supernatural is not adequate! We cannot allow for anything to become a
substitute for the miracle working power of God to be ushered back into
His church!

Like Uzzah, we are often guilty of reaching out with our hands instead
of reaching up with our hearts. We are guilty of trying to do spiritual
work in the power of the flesh.

 

Could it be that we have become so enamored with defending His presence
that in reality we are not experiencing His presence? What does His
presence look like in a ministry?

- Souls saved...

- Believers filled...

- The bound delivered...

- The broken-hearted healed...

- The poor having the Gospel preached to them...

Quite frankly, David was terrified by the power of the Ark after it
killed Uzzah and he suspended its journey for three months.

When we realize we have strayed from His presence and His power is no
longer present, we should suspend everything and call a fast; declare a
solemn assembly; and pray heaven down in our town.

 

When Uzzah died, David asked Obed-Edom if he could leave the Ark at his
house. David wasn't just asking for a place to park the Ark, he was
asking if Obed would do what needed to be done to attend the Ark. In
effect he was saying "Can we trust you with this Ark?"

Against the backdrop of the death of Uzzah, wouldn't we be a little more
than reluctant to have the Ark parked at our house? There is no sign of
hesitation on the part of Obed; he was not afraid to receive the Ark.

Obed knew that a man with clean hands and a pure heart had nothing to
fear from the presence of the Lord.

I believe the lack of hesitation on the part of Obed is because he
already had his house in order. Before the Ark ever arrived, Obed feared
and served the God of the Ark.

 

I ask every preacher and church member in this house, can we trust this
Ark to you? Can we leave this Ark with you and trust you to treat it in
a proper manner? Jude 1:3 (KJV) "Beloved, when I gave all diligence
to write unto you of the common salvation, it was needful for me to
write unto you, and exhort you that ye should earnestly contend for the
faith which was once delivered unto the saints."

Remember, while the Ark was in the possession of the Philistines it
brought curses; when it was in the custody of Abinadab it never seemed
to bring a blessing or a curse; but when Obed-Edom hosted the Ark, it
blessed him and his entire house!

The ark of God, that overthrew Dagon and smote Uzzah, was nothing but a
fountain of blessing in the household of Obed-Edom.

 

What was the difference? The attitude and priority with which Obed-Edom
cared for the Ark released the blessing from the storehouse of God. Can
we leave this Ark with you? Will you give it priority?

In a three-month period the fortunes of Obed's house took a drastic
turn:

- His house turned from poverty to prominence... (God is El Shaddai not
 El Get-by!)

- He became a man of honor rather than being despised... (Obed means
 slave -- Every time someone called him by his name it was a painful
 reminder of who he was -- The name Edom was assigned to him because he
 was an Edomite and a descendent of Esau - He was a "Gittite" this
 means that he was from the city of Gath, the place of David's greatest
 enemy, Goliath.)

 

In a mere three months, Obed and his descendants became addicted to the
presence of God.

Because he gave the Ark proper respect, Obed reversed the curse and
found his destiny.

The day came that David returned to retrieve the Ark. Obed had a choice
to make:

- Go back to his old way of life...

- Grieve the absence of the Ark...

- Pack up and follow the Ark...

When David came to take the Ark to Jerusalem, Obed and his house left
all and followed the Ark!

This was an easy choice for Obed; he would give up his life and
livelihood and follow the presence of God.

 

The proof of desire is pursuit! Can we leave this Ark with you?

In so doing, Obed-Edom established a legacy of generational blessings.

As a result:

- He became the keeper of the gate at the Temple...

- He became a singer and musician in the house of the Lord...

- He became the keeper of the treasury...

- He had more than 62 offspring that were all called blessed of God and
 served in the temple...

It doesn't matter what sort of background, education, or family history
you have. When you have the good sense to give His presence the honor it
deserves, you are going to be blessed.

 

When David retrieved the Ark from Obed's house he did it the right way.
