# IT IS TIME TO LOOK WITHIN

INTRO: There are two ordinances that we observe: Water Baptism and
Communion.

Within 1500 years after that very simple beginning of our Lord's
celebration of Passover with His disciples, the Lord's Table became a
controversial and divisive subject in the institutional church. The
following has divided us on this subject:

- There are those who teach that with each celebration of the Lord's
 Supper, the body of Christ is broken again and the blood of Christ is
 spilled again. They believe that during the act of blessing the
 elements at each celebration of the Eucharist, the bread literally
 becomes the body of Christ and the juice/fruit of the vine literally
 becomes the blood of Christ. This position is called
 transubstantiation.

- Many from the Reformation argued that the Communion bread does not
 become the literal body of Jesus, but the body of Christ is present
 within the bread. And, the communion cup does not become the literal
 blood of the Lord, but the blood of the Lord is present within the
 cup. Theologians refer to this doctrine as consubstantiation.

- Others believe that the bread is not the physical body of Christ, and
 the body of Christ is also not present within the bread; the cup is
 not the blood of the Lord, and the blood of the Lord is not present
 within the cup. These reformers saw the bread and cup simply as
 emblems -Symbols meant to remind participants of the Last Supper and
 its significance for believers throughout their earthly life.

This debate over the elements of Communion continues today. There is
further dispute among us as to whether we should eat from one loaf, or
each have a separate wafer; whether we should drink from one cup, or
each have a separate cup; whether we should serve communion once a week,
once a month, or a couple of times a year.

It would do us well to remember that Jesus just said, "As often as we
observe this ordinance to do it in remembrance of Him" - and what these
emblems represent.

We must pay special attention to the text: 1 Corinthians 11:28 (NKJV)
^"^But let a man examine himself, and so let him eat of the bread and
drink of the cup." The Message Bible says it like this, "Examine
your motives, test your heart, come to this meal in holy awe."

This is a command and, therefore, not to be taken lightly. When we
approach the Lord's Table one of the most important exercises, we do
there is self-examination, looking within.

Many times, as it pertains to our physical body, a self-examination is
what brings about concern that takes us to the doctor for a professional
exam. (The doctor told me, "You should be thankful for the
diverticulitis.")

It's safe to say that no one is more familiar with our body/ mind that
we are. We often know when something is not exactly right before anyone
else does.

The same is true in our spiritual lives. We know something isn't right
and we go to the expert, (the One who made us) to get things right.

Paul is telling these Corinthian believers, and us, to look within our
lives and honestly assess our moral and spiritual condition.

Looking within affords us the opportunity to make sure that all is well
and, if not, to correct it. This is much like looking into a mirror
before going somewhere.

As we approach the Lords Table, we are to pray for God to reveal to us
anything in our lives that is not in line with His will that would bring
disgrace to Him or to us.

Looking within demands that I do three things:

- Looking at our past...

- Looking at our present...

- Looking at our future...

LOOKING WITHIN DEMANDS THAT WE TAKE A SERIOUS LOOK AT OUR PAST

Take the time today to look at your past. Think of all the past failures
and successes. We all have failures and successes that are a part of our
past, don't we? While much of our past has the propensity to cause
discouragement and even depression, we cannot afford to allow our past
to dictate our present and future.

I challenge us to view our past in light of the work of the cross.
Romans 8:1 (NKJV) ^"^There is therefore now no condemnation to those
who are in Christ Jesus, who do not walk according to the flesh, but
according to the Spirit."

The conviction of the Holy Spirit in the life of the believer is a
terrific thing but I want to remind us today that the Holy Spirit does
not convict us of forgiven sin. That's the oppression of the enemy. God
doesn't remember forgiven sin! "As far as the east is from the west,
so far has He removed our transgressions from us."

As we look at our past, be must make sure that every part of our past
has been brought under the blood of Jesus. We must be sure there is
nothing hidden. Remember, He doesn't force His way in. We must invite
Him in. This is called Looking Within.

LOOKING WITHIN DFEMANDS THAT WE TAKE AN HONEST LOOK AT OUR PRESENT

We need to resolve the issues that exist between us and the Lord, before
an embarrassing rooster-crowing incident makes our failures public, as
it did for Peter.

Paul reminds us that if we judge ourselves, others won't have to do it
for us. 1 Corinthians 11:31 (NKJV) "For if we would judge ourselves,
we would not be judged."

As we look within, we must be sure that everything has been brought
under the blood of Jesus. We must be sure there is nothing hidden.

As we approach the Lord's Table the timing is perfect to bring those
difficult spiritual and emotional issues of our lives to Christ for
cleansing by His blood.

LOOKING WITHIN DEMANDS THAT WE EVALUATE OUR FUTURE

The enemy would like to convince us that our future is not all that and
a bag of chips. He wants us to have a pessimistic view of the road
forward, but Jesus said in John 10:10 "The thief has come to steal,
kill and destroy. But I have come that you might have life and have it
more abundantly."

As we celebrate at the Lord's Table today, it would do us well to
remember that the bread and the cup link us with a family of brothers
and sisters in Christ, that has been growing for some 2,000 years. We
are a part of something bigger than we are. (The redeemed church is a
part of the church triumphant)

Being a part of the Body of Christ means that I not only have the help
of the Lord available, I have the support of His Church behind me.

As I look into my future, I must remember, I have a support network that
will not let me down. Philippians 4:13 "I can do all things through
Christ who gives me strength."

No matter how daunting our futures appear to be, we must remember, "If
the Lord is our partner, we should make our plans big."

CONCLUION:

As we look within to see our future, we must remember, more of this
family is in heaven than on earth at this moment. This is why the Lord
said through Paul here in 1 Corinthians 11, "^ ^For as often as you
eat this bread and drink this cup, you proclaim the Lord's death [till
He comes]{.underline}."

Those who have gone before us are among that "Great cloud of
witnesses" where time is no more. There is no past, present, or
future for them. The race is run! The battle is over! They are not in
time, they are in eternity!

As we come to the Lords Table this morning we are reminded:

- Our past is gone...

- Our successes are temporary...

- Our failures have been forgotten...

- We are looking for a City...

- This world is not our home...

- The treasures of Heaven are waiting...
