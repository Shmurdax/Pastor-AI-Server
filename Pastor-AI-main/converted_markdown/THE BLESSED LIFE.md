THE LAW OF THE HARVEST

INTRO: God has set into order some laws, one of which is the Law of
Harvest. According to the Word of God, this law is irrefutable.
Galatians 6:7-10 (NLT) "Don't be misled. Remember that you can't
ignore God and get away with it. You will always [reap what you
sow]{.underline}! 8 Those who live only to satisfy their own sinful

But those who [live to please the Spirit]{.underline} will [harvest

doing what is good. Don't get discouraged and give up, for [we will reap
a harvest of blessing at the appropriate time]{.underline}. 10 Whenever

Any good farmer understands and respects this simple principle. A farmer
who makes his living from the soil:

- Prepares the soil...

- Plows...

- Plants...

- Fertilizes...

- Carefully oversees...

- Seeks to protect...

## I. THE HEART OF THE SOWER...

I Pastored for a number of years in a farming community, and the lessons
I learned from those farmers has greatly enhanced my ability and
willingness to give to the Lord. I have yet to meet a farmer who sows
his fields without expecting the ground to produce a return. I disagree
with people who say we should not sow with an expectation to receive.
That is absolutely foolishness. Every farmer expects an increase in due
season. The farmer who sows without expecting to receive will be out of
business in short order.

Thought the farmer plants with an expectation of reaping, a true farmer
makes his living from the ground because he has a love for farming:

- He maintains his equipment... During the winter the true farmer does
 not spend his days recklessly, he is in the shop getting the equipment
 ready for the next farming cycle.

- He plows the ground... He is preparing it to receive his seed.

- He Plants the crop... He understands that unless he makes an
 investment in sowing there will be no harvest.

- He tends the crop... He fertilizes, plows, sprays pesticides, etc...

- He reaps his reward... At the right time he goes into the field and
 gathers the harvest into the silo.

This is the pattern that is repeated year after year, however if the
farmer gets hailed out; flooded out; stormed out; droughted out, he is
still there the next season because his heart is to farm, he has a
connection to the land. The smell of the soil gets him going in the
morning! The sound of the equipment working causes the blood to flow
properly through his veins! The sound of the combine thrashing the
harvest causes his heart to beat a little faster

## II. THE MOTIVE OF THE SOWER... Even though we reap what we sow and
expectantly await our harvest, our motivation for sowing should not
necessarily be reaping but giving and sharing. If our motivation for
sowing is reaping alone, if we do not reap what we expected our giving
comes to a screeching halt! If the farmer is in the farming business
strickly for the harvest, he would quit the first year he made a bad
crop!

Many Christians never discover the secret of joyous giving or the
blessing of sharing. The Word plainly say's the Father has uniquely
designed us to experience the joy of giving and the blessing of sharing.
Acts 20:35 (NLT) "And I have been a constant example of how you can
help the poor by working hard. You should remember the words of the Lord
Jesus: '[It is more blessed to give than to receive]{.underline}."

When a person begins to experience the exuberance of this kind of giving
it is much easier for them to enter this sphere in the future. The
condition of the heart determines whether a person is capable of this
kind of giving. Matthew 6:20-21 (NLT) "[Store your treasures in
heaven]{.underline}, where they will never become moth-eaten or rusty
and where they will be safe from thieves. 21 [Wherever your treasure is,
there your heart and thoughts will also be]{.underline}."

A foundational truth for all Christ centered givers is this: Everything
we have belongs to God; we are the steward (Business Managers) of His
possessions. If we truly view ourselves merely as stewards, it makes it
much easier to distribute His wealth in a responsible manner. However if
we view the possessions we have been blessed with as our own we tend to:

- Guard it...

- Hoard it...

- Protect it...

- Keep it...

In such a scenario, we tend to think only of what is good for us, never
allowing the heart of God to enter the picture.

## III. THE REWARD OF THE SOWER...

When the farmer takes the bob trucks and combines into the field, he
knows that months of work are going to be rewarded with a harvest. The
resources the farmer receives from months of labor will be:

- Tallied... He will total up his increase.

- Taken to the market... He will sell his harvest for as much as
 possible.

- Distributed... He will lay aside enough money to sow next year's crop
 and then everyone he owes will get their share.

- Budgeted... He will determine his bills and living expenses until the
 next harvest.

The heart of God is to make stewards of us so He can direct His
resources, and when this occurs we experience the completion of the
cycle of giving, which is receiving, so we can give again. Luke 6:38
(NLT) "[If you give, you will receive]{.underline}. Your gift will
return to you in [full measure, pressed down, shaken together to make
room for more, and running over]{.underline}. [Whatever measure you use
in giving---large or small---it will be used to measure what is given
back to you]{.underline}."

Once a person develops this kind of a heart, they are no longer a
reservoir which threatens to run dry, they become a river which has many
tributaries and no matter how great the outflow, the input just seems to
keep pace. When a person is a reservoir he is controlled by what he has
and grieved by what he gives, because he continually measures his
success and effectiveness by what he has accumulated.

When we have the heart of God and truly have become a steward we measure
our success by what we have given rather than by what we have received
or kept. Giving truly becomes an investment in His kingdom. Investing in
His kingdom produces eternal results.

The reward of the true giver is not the harvest, but the opportunity to
sow.

INTRO: An adventure is an exploration of new territory, the
discovery of new horizons, and the discovery of new things. I am a very
adventurous person; I enjoy forging ahead in uncharted waters and
discovering new and improved ways of doing things. I thrive on a
challenge. My dream vacation is to float the Colorado river through the
Grand Canyon!

Giving is an adventure that if taken seriously can lead to blessings yet
untapped. The reason I decided to title this lesson "Adventurous
Giving" is because it is my opinion that most of us have never pressed
beyond the norm in our giving to discover what is available to those who
take the next step.

As a human being we are natural born takers rather than givers. As soon
as we are born we learn to make our presence known through crying and
develop as an adolescent through selfish antics. The spiritual
development of each believer is much like that of the human being. There
are four steps of human and spiritual development:

- The baby stage... A baby thinks only of self, and if denied it will
 raise a ruckus. It seeks its own way. Its feelings are easily hurt,
 and it is often jealous. It cries but never sings. It tries to talk
 but never makes sense. A baby loves to be served but it never serves.
 It drinks milk and cannot digest solid foods.

- The little child stage... Children are often untruthful, envious, and
 cruel. If rebuked they become martyrs. If crossed they become
 resentful and often make a scene. They are talebearers repeating
 everything they hear (In adults it is known as gossip). They are given
 to emotional outbursts and are easily offended. They love praise and
 will accept it from any source. They seek only the things that appeal
 to self.

- The young adult stage... They are strong and agile and well able to
 overcome most any enemy. They have vision and faith for the future,
 along with the courage and tenacity to tackle it. They are in
 preparation for the productive years of their lives.

- The parent stage... They have peace with God; rejoice in their
 children; have learned to be content with the circumstances; know the
 source of real strength; do not brood over the past but look with
 faith to the future; know that all things work together for the good;
 they enjoy life now and look expectantly to eternal life.

While we are natural born takers, the nature of God is to give. John
3:16 (KJV) "For God so loved the world, [that he gave]{.underline}
his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not
perish, but have everlasting life." I cannot refrain from asking this
question, "If the nature of God is to give and we have been born again
by His spirit, shouldn't we begin to love to give as well?"

One aspect of the adventure of giving is the fact that we cannot
out-give God. To begin with, how would we ever purpose to give a greater
gift than He gave when He sent His son to this earth to die for our
sins?

God will be indebted to no man. Mark 10:29-30 (KJV) "And Jesus
answered and said, Verily I say unto you, There is no man that hath left

children, or lands, for my sake, and the gospel's, 30 But [he shall

## III. ADVENTUROUS GIVING ALWAYS MAKES GOD THE PRIORITY... If we are
going to live the blessed life, we must put first things first. In so
doing, God must occupy the number one slot in our life.

It is my opinion that the way we handle our money speaks volumes about
where our priorities have been placed. Matthew 6:19-21 (NLT) "Don't

I know there will be people who will say that I should have used some

did you know that there are, 500 Scriptures in the Bible concerning

38 parables! Money is actually a test from God. It is a priority test!
Are we passing or failing?

In order to fully understand where our priorities have been placed I
want to talk to you about the principle of first-fruits. This precedent
was established in the Old Testament book of Exodus. Exodus 13:2,12,13
(NLT) "Dedicate to me all [the firstborn sons]{.underline} of Israel

God declared the firstborn belonged to Him 16 times in Scripture!
According to Old Testament Law the firstborn was to be sacrificed or
redeemed. There was no third choice! When an animal delivered its
firstborn it was to be sacrificed and if for some reason it was
considered unclean, the person was to take a clean animal from their
herd and sacrifice it, thus redeeming the firstborn. Jesus was Gods
firstborn and He offered him as a sacrifice. He was a real first fruits
offering, in that sense He was Gods tithe! God gave His tithe in faith
before we ever believed. Romans 5:8 (NLT) "But God showed his great
love for us by sending Christ to die for us [while we were still
sinners]{.underline}." Romans 8:29 (NLT) "For God knew his people
in advance, and he chose them to become like his Son, so that his Son
would be [the firstborn]{.underline}, with many brothers and
sisters."

The Scripture is clear, God not only requires the firstborn or the
first-fruit, but He set the example by giving His firstborn Son, for the
sins of His other sons. In the same way, we are to bring the first-fruit
and THEN we will see the blessing. The first-fruits allow us to
participate in adventurous giving!

It is interesting to note that God required the firstborn. He did not
say to allow the ewe to bear nine lambs and then give number ten to God.
He said give the firstborn to Him. The prophet told the widow to bake
him a cake first and when she did, the meal in the barrel never failed
to be present. It is possible to be a tither without adhering to the
principle of first-fruits.

Why did God go beyond requiring the tithe to requiring the first-fruits?
Because it doesn't take faith to give one out of ten, but it requires
great faith to give the first-fruit and believe Him to multiply it ten
times! Giving the last tenth allows for no adventure, but giving the
first tenth brings the tight rope of adventure into play.

belongs to Him; if I release it to Him I get blessed; if I refuse to
release it to Him He will get His another way and I place myself under a

The first born in Israel didn't die that night did they? Why not?

want to make sure I am very clear; I in no way want to leave the

Many well meaning Christians use the excuse that tithing is required
under the law but the New Testament does not mandate it. Tithing was

living under grace, therefore I am not affected by the law?" Every
person here would no doubt point out to me that the principle of the
sanctity of human life is a common thread which predates and surpasses
the Law of Moses. That's right, and so does the principle of tithing...

Grace actually requires more than law...

The Law said not to commit murder! (Exodus 20:13, Deuteronomy 5:17)
Jesus said, "Don't even be angry with your brother." (Matthew 5:22)

The Law said not to commit adultery! (Exodus 20:14, Deuteronomy 5:18)
Jesus said, "If you look at a woman with lust in your heart you have
already committed adultery."

The demands of grace are greater than that of the Law, so if we prefer
to give according to grace rather than Law we will go beyond the
expectations of the Law...

## I. THE KEY OF GIVING... I would tend to believe that everyone in my
audience today would prefer to live in abundance rather than live with
lack in our life. This is probably not going to sound very spiritual but
I have tried poverty and I have tried a little bit of prosperity and my
personal preference is prosperity.

The first key to abundance is giving. I should probably hasten on to say
that the key to giving is attitude rather than ability. We cannot talk
about giving without talking about stewardship. We are stewards for God;
we are his business managers; everything we have belongs to the Lord.
When we realize who owns it all it is not nearly as difficult to follow
His will in giving. If I give it all away at His direction, it is
alright because there is more where that came from.

ILLUSTRATION: One day Larissa had a \$20.00 bill and could not get
change and had need of two dollars. I offered to trade her two one
dollar bills for her twenty and she readily agreed. I wanted to use this
as a teaching opportunity on the value of the dollar and so I explained
to her what kind of trade she had just made. She had reduced her net
worth by \$18.00 to meet a momentary need. Her response was "It's OK
Dad, there's more where that came from!"

I am Gods steward, what I have belongs to Him, He can direct it as He
pleases, and when He asks for it all I must remember "It's OK Dad,
there's more where that came from." The stewardship principle is as old
as humankind. Adam and Eve were stewards (caretakers) of the garden.
They were told not to eat of one tree in the garden. Genesis 2:15-17
(NLT) "The [Lord]{.smallcaps} God placed the man in the Garden of
Eden [to tend and care for it]{.underline}. 16 But the
[Lord]{.smallcaps} God gave him this warning: "[You may freely eat any
fruit in the garden 17 except fruit from the tree of the knowledge of
good and evil]{.underline}. If you eat of its fruit, you will surely
die." Why did God get so ticked when they decided to disobey and eat
of the forbidden tree? The answer is, they were acting like owners
rather than stewards. This attitude points to a conflict between
ownership and stewardship. An attitude of ownership will cause you to be
upset when the Pastor preaches on finances. An attitude of stewardship
will cause us to set up and pay attention to any teaching which allows
us a little more information on how to manage the assets of the Master
who has placed us in charge of his things. While we are stewards, He
allows us to enjoy the fruit of His increase in this life. 1 Timothy
6:17 (NLT) "Tell those who are rich in this world not to be proud and
not to trust in their money, which will soon be gone. But their trust
should be in the living God, [who richly gives us all we need for our
enjoyment]{.underline}."

When it comes to giving, God has given us stewardship responsibilities
over our lives. While we have stewardship responsibilities, we must not
touch anything He has placed as off limits for us. We have established
that the tithe belongs to the lord and the person who touches the tithe
is doing exactly what Adam and Eve did in the garden.

The key of giving and tithing is a test for all up and coming stewards.
Jesus reveals the acid test of stewardship in Matthew 6:21 (NLT)
"Wherever your treasure is, there your heart and thoughts will also
be."

Did you know the word translated "Tithe" in the Bible literally
means "Tenth" or "Tenth part?" Throughout the Bible the number ten
represents testing...

- How many plagues came upon Egypt? Ten...

- How many commandments are there? Ten...

- How many times did God test Israel in the wilderness? Ten...

- How many times did God test Jacob's heart while working for Laban?
 Ten...

- How many day's was Daniel tested in Daniel chapter one? Ten...

- The preparedness of the virgins was tested in Matthew 25. How many
 virgins were there? Ten...

- How many days of testing are mentioned in Revelation 2:10? Ten...

Tithing and giving is the defining test of all would-be-stewards. This
is also the only area in which we are admonished to test God. Malachi
3:10 (NKJV) "Bring all the tithes into the storehouse, That there may
be food in My house, And [try Me now in this," Says the
[Lord]{.smallcaps} of hosts]{.underline}, "If I will not open for you
the windows of heaven And pour out for you such blessing That there will
not be room enough to receive it."

ILLUSTRATION: Suppose I am going to be gone on a trip for several
months. I choose three guys to which I commit to send \$10,000.00 per
week and my only requirement is that they give Susan \$1,000.00 per man
per week. They keep \$9,000.00 and give her \$1,000.00. I suppose I
could get any number of participants for that scenario! Suppose after
one month I check back with her and discover the first guy has been
giving her \$2,000.00 each week, the second guy is giving her \$1,000.00
per week, but the third guy gave her \$800.00 the first week, \$500.00
the next week, and nothing for the final two weeks? What would you
expect me to do? I would fire the third guy and send the first guy
\$20,000.00 per week! Right? Sure, it just makes good business sense!
The third guy getting mad and throwing a fit because he was fired makes
about as much sense as a Christian refusing to tithe and expecting to
reap the blessings of the Lord! Let me give you a Scripture for this.
Matthew 25:29 (NLT) "To those who [use well what they are given, even
more will be given]{.underline}, and [they will have an
abundance]{.underline}. But from [those who are unfaithful, even what
little they have will be taken away]{.underline}."

Before we move on from this subject I want to digress to the
relationship between Abraham and Melchizedek. The writer of Hebrews
reveals an all important truth concerning this relationship as well as
the relationship of our tithe and who we are giving them to. Hebrews 7:8
(NKJV) "Here [mortal men receive tithes]{.underline}, but [there he
receives them]{.underline}, of whom it is witnessed that he lives."
We may think we are returning our tithe through the auspices of the
local church but according to this New Testament Scripture when they are
placed on deposit in our local church a far greater entity is keeping up
with our giving record. We should return our tithe with the same
intensity as we would if we had to stand at the pearly gates and return
them to Jesus himself!

- What a privilege...

- What an opportunity...

- What a Holy act of worship...

Tithing is not a grim duty to be performed by the Christian. It is not a
dry religious exercise which kills the Spirit of a service. It is not
punishment intended to cost the believer hard earned cash. It is a
wonderful opportunity to invest in the kingdom of God and in doing so;
it brings unbelievable benefits to the tither not to mention protection
from the devourer.

The first key to abundance is without question, tithing! If we cannot
pass the tithing test with a good attitude we can forget being in
possession of any of the other keys. It is really this simple; if we
tithe with a good attitude we are blessed; if we do not tithe with a
good attitude we are cursed.

## II. KEY OF MULTIPLICATION... Have we ever wanted or felt that we
needed to multiply something?

- Multiply ourselves to get all our work done?

- Multiply the hours in a day?

- Multiply money to pay our bills?

Webster defines multiplication in the following way: "A method used to
find the result of adding a specified quantity repeatedly a specific
number of times."

The Bible is full of instances of the miracle of multiplication:

- The strength of Israelite soldiers was multiplied on many occasions
 when they were hopelessly outnumbered...

- The meal and oil of the widow and her son was multiplied...

- The multiplication of a little boy's lunch...

I want us to zero in on this miracle of multiplication here in Luke 9
which outlines how Jesus took a meal of fish and chips from Long John
Silvers and fed a multitude of people.

Jesus blessed the little boys lunch and told the disciples to give it
away! Can you imagine what Peter or for that part, Thomas must have been
thinking as they walked into the crowd with a half a handful of fish and
bread and 5,000 men to feed? I would give anything to be able to record
the thoughts of the disciples...

- Peter -- Walking on water is one thing, but this is ridiculous...

- Thomas -- I doubt if this is going to work...

- John -- I love Jesus but we've never done it like this before...

- Andrew -- How is this going to work, there are more people than there
 is lunch...

- Luke -- My medical education tells me there is not enough here to feed
 this crowd...

- Judas -- All He is going to do is stir up these hungry folks and I
 will have to bankrupt the treasury to settle them down...

- James -- I know He has performed a lot of miracles but I think He has
 over loaded himself this time...

The thing that Jesus knew that the disciples were not aware of was the
key of multiplication! Jesus may have fed upwards of 20,000 people that
day with five loaves and two fish and took up twelve baskets of
fragments!

There are at least two components to utilizing the key of
multiplication. The first key: A thing must be blessed before it can
multiply! Jesus blessed the fish and bread before he distributed it to
the multitudes.

If we desire to use the key of multiplication in our finances we must
first bless them. How do we bless them? Pray over them dedicating the
first-fruit to God, then following through to complete our part of the
covenant. Romans 11:16 (NKJV) "For if the first-fruit is holy, the
lump is also holy; and if the root is holy, so are the branches."
What this Scripture does not say is almost as important as what it does
say. If the first-fruit is misapplied everything that follows after it
is also cursed.

There are many sweet well-intentioned Christians who have never
personally witnessed the miracle of multiplication where their resources
are concerned. It is not that they are bad people; they have just never
understood the principle of blessing the first-fruit by giving it to the
Lord.

Remember, we are not giving our tithes to the preacher or the church,
but to the Lord. If we dedicate our first-fruit to Him it makes the rest
of our finances Holy and then the remainder is available for the miracle
of multiplication!

The second component of the key of multiplication is as simple as the
first. Only what is given away can multiply! The disciples were handling
blessed bread which now had the power of multiplication on it! It had
been blessed so it had the potential for multiplication! If the
disciples had decided to eat the meal themselves, it would not have been
enough for twelve people but when they started to give it away they
witnessed the miracle of multiplication. If they used the lunch for
themselves they would have had a couple of bites each instead of full
stomachs and twelve baskets of leftovers!

Even though the food was blessed it had to be given away in order for it
to multiply. I am here to remind us today that only what we give away
can be multiplied! If we need a financial blessing we must find a way to
sow a financial seed!

Tithing is not giving it is returning what belongs to God to begin with.
Tithing begins the process of multiplication but where we get into the
miracle of multiplication is what we give away above our tithe! A
portion of our finances over-and-above our tithe must be shared if they
are to be multiplied. Tithing is where we remove the curse, giving above
our tithe is where multiplication begins.

King Hezekiah witnesses the key of multiplication when he required the
people to tithe of the first-fruit and the first-born. 2 Chronicles
31:4-10 (NLT) "In addition, [he required the people in Jerusalem to
bring the prescribed portion of their income to the priests and Levites,
so they could devote themselves fully to the law of the
[Lord]{.smallcaps}]{.underline}. 5 [The people responded immediately and
generously with the first of their crops and grain, new wine, olive oil,
honey, and all the produce of their fields]{.underline}. [They brought a
tithe of all they owned]{.underline}. 6 The people who had moved to
Judah from Israel, and the people of Judah themselves, [brought in the
tithes]{.underline} of their cattle and sheep and a tithe of the things
that had been dedicated to the [Lord]{.smallcaps} their God, and they
piled them up in great heaps. 7 The first of these [tithes]{.underline}
was brought in late spring, and the heaps continued to grow until early
autumn. 8 [When Hezekiah and his officials came and saw these huge
piles, they thanked the [Lord]{.smallcaps} and his people
Israel]{.underline}! 9 "Where did all this come from?" Hezekiah asked
the priests and Levites.10 And Azariah the high priest, from the family
of Zadok, replied, "[Since the people began bringing their gifts to the
[Lord]{.smallcaps}'s Temple, we have had enough to eat and plenty to
spare, for the [Lord]{.smallcaps} has blessed his
people]{.underline}."

The key of multiplication is inserted into the lock of life when the
individual Christian tithes. Giving above our tithe is turning the key
in the lock of multiplication. It would be well to note that when
EVERYONE in a local church exercises the key of multiplication it
catapults the people and the church into a place of "Not enough room
to receive it." I believe the "Not enough room to receive it"
principle is enacted first corporately, then individually.

Can you imagine what one congregation the size of this one could do if
everyone tithed every week? There would not be one person in this
congregation who would be in lack because the resources of the church
would be sufficient to meet the needs of the hurting until they could
stand on their own two feet and become a part of the multiplication
process.

## III. KEY OF BREAKING THE SPIRIT OF MAMMON...

Mammon I not really a word we go around using everyday but Jesus used
this word in Matthew 6:24 (NKJV) "No one can serve two masters; for
either he will hate the one and love the other, or else he will be loyal
to the one and despise the other. You cannot serve God and
[mammon]{.underline}." The fact that Jesus so clearly states that it
is just as possible to serve mammon as it is to serve God, and even
states that no one can serve both mammon and God at the same time,
suggests to me that He is clearly defining mammon as an idol which
people worship, serve and bow down to.

Mammon is an Aramaic word that essentially means riches. Jesus draws a
striking contrast between the spirit of mammon and the spirit of God. He
is saying that they are so diametrically opposed that it is not possible
to serve both at once. Look at what Jesus say's:

- No one can serve two masters...

- He will hate one and love the other...

- He will be loyal to one and despise the other...

There is no middle of the road here; no half-in-half; no neutrality; no
fence straddling will be permitted on this subject. The spirit of mammon
is founded on pride and arrogance. The attitude of the spirit of mammon
is:

- Man does not need God...

- Man is a little god himself...

- Man is self-sufficient...

- Man should trust in his own riches...

In the spiritual sense mammon is a spirit which trusts in riches. If I
am being seduced by the spirit of mammon my security is judged by the
size of my bank account. All money has a spirit attached to it. It
either has the spirit of God working in it or the spirit of mammon
working through it. Money which has been submitted to God and His
purposes has the spirit of God on it. This is why it multiplies and
cannot be affected by the devourer. Money which is devoted to God and
His purposes rather than making an attempt to replace Him is blessed by
God.

Money which has not been submitted to God has the spirit of mammon on it
by default. To not pay our tithes is an automatic invitation for the
spirit of mammon to rule over our finances and our lives. The spirit of
mammon:

- Makes an attempt to buy happiness... Things cannot satisfy...

- Makes an attempt to control others with the almighty dollar... Always
 paying the check then causing others to feel as if they are in debt...

- Makes an attempt to manipulate others by proving I am more prosperous
 than you... Because of my things I should be listened to...

- Makes a person believe they are a self-made man... Everything we have
 comes from, and belongs to God...

The spirit of mammon is looking for servants! It is seeking worshippers!
It will promise you everything but deliver emptiness! The spirit of
mammon is alive and well both inside and outside the church.

The spirit of mammon uses the oldest trick in the world, a trick which
was used on Adam and Eve and even on Jesus in the wilderness, it
promises to deliver on the things that only God can deliver on:

- Security...

- Significance...

- Identity...

- Independence...

- Power...

- Freedom...

When you think about it, mammon is nothing more than the system of this
fallen world that stands in sharp opposition to God and his ways:

- Mammon say's to buy and sell; God say's to sow and reap...

- Mammon say's to take; God say's to give...

- Mammon say's to cheat and steal; God say's to give and receive...

- Mammon is selfish; God is generous...

- Mammon say's to hoard everything you can; God say's to give it away
 cheerfully...

I want you to hear what I am about to say; money and mammon are not
synonymous; money is not inherently evil. 1 Timothy 6:10 (NKJV) "For
[the love of money is a root of all kinds of evil]{.underline}, for
which some have strayed from the faith in their greediness, and pierced
themselves through with many sorrows." You can love money without
having it and you can have it without loving it. I know more people who
love it and do not have it than I do which have it and are in love with
it. It is the love of money which perverts the owner.

Greed, covetousness and selfishness are all manifestations of the spirit
of mammon. Because mammon is a spirit, it is constantly talking to us.
Mammon sounds like this:

- If you have the right car you will be accepted...

- If you live in the right house you will be respected...

- If you get the next promotion you will be esteemed more highly...

- If you have the right credit cards you can get what it takes to get a
 leg up in life...

- If you can get acquainted with the right people you can get where you
 have always wanted to be...

The bottom line is that the spirit of mammon will tell you that "if you
can somehow beg, borrow, steal or manipulate your way to the top you
will be happy." The spirit of mammon will tell you that "if God does
not provide a miracle right away, you are going to need a lot of money
to overcome this setback." This is a lie, what we need is God and
nothing else! If we have God, He will take care of everything else we
need because He alone is our source!

The spirit of mammon has two constant companions:

- The spirit of poverty -- This spirit keeps a person in poverty by
 making them believe they cannot be spiritual and blessed at the same
 time! This spirit will cause a believer say things like "Wealth is
 not spiritual" or when they get blessed they will say, "I don't want
 this blessing because I don't deserve this blessing." When a person
 operating in a spirit of poverty gets blessed with a better house and
 someone notices their blessing, rather than saying "This is such a
 blessing from the Lord" they will say things like, "Yes, it was
 repossession and we were able to acquire it far below market value,
 otherwise we could not have afforded it." Poverty say's, "I bought
 this outfit at Wal-Mart." It is time for God's children to quit
 apologizing for their blessings, recognize where the blessings are
 coming from, and give God the full credit He deserves for what is
 going right in our lives.

- The spirit of pride -- The spirit of pride causes a person to keep
 running into debt attempting to keep up their public image, while
 living beyond their means! When our outgo exceeds our income, our
 upkeep becomes our downfall! The spirit of pride also say's, "Wealth
 comes from hard work." The spirit of pride causes a person to flaunt
 their wealth. A spirit of pride say's "This outfit was imported from
 Europe." This is no time for God's children to be running to debt to
 keep up their public image. It is time to do what is right with our
 finances and then sit back and await the blessings of the Lord. When
 we receive a blessing, we should give God the glory rather than
 attempting to flaunt our new found success.

Matthew 6:24 (NKJV) "No one can serve two masters; for either he will
hate the one and love the other, or else he will be loyal to the one and
despise the other. You cannot serve God and [mammon]{.underline}." 1
Timothy 6:10 (NKJV) "For [the love of money is a root of all kinds of
evil]{.underline}, for which some have strayed from the faith in their
greediness, and pierced themselves through with many sorrows." We are
not being told here to hate money, we are being told to hate the spirit
of mammon! The spirit of mammon is always accompanied by a greedy,
selfish, lying, deceiving, Antichrist like spirit which flourishes in
the atmosphere f money worship. I am not one to fight other ministries
but I see this same spirit raising its ugly head in many national and
international ministries. It is all about money. I am not presenting
this as a get-rich-quick-scheme, I am presenting it as a
lay-down-our-life and take-up-His practice!

The bottom line is we cannot worship God and mammon; we will hate one
and love the other! When we begin to worship mammon we will step farther
and farther from the will of God.

Matthew 6:19-21 (NKJV) "Do not lay up for yourselves treasures on
earth, where moth and rust destroy and where thieves break in and
steal;20 "[but lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven, where neither
moth nor rust destroys and where thieves do not break in and
steal]{.underline}.21 "For where your treasure is, there your heart will
be also." When we use money for unrighteous or greedy purposes we are
placing it at great risk of destruction. When we use money for righteous
purposes we are placing it in the safest investment in the universe and
it will gain interest and dividends which can only be measured in
eternity! This is a wonderful investment opportunity!

The reason the enemy fights us so hard in the area of our finances is
because he knows that dollars can be turned into souls if it is rightly
handled. Hell is plundered by our offerings, and Satan knows it! One
other reason Satan hates Spirit led giving is because it simultaneously
diminishes his kingdom and makes us more like our heavenly Father.

Church leaders have a responsibility to be people of integrity in the
area of finances and they will give an account to God for what they did
with the offerings! How would you like to be a church leader who stands
before God and gives a report that hundreds of thousands of dollars were
left in the bank at the rapture? How would you like to be a church
leader who stands before God and must give an account for negligent
spending practices?

Before I leave the subject of mammon I want to point out one more
scripture. Luke 16:11-12 (NLT) "And if you are [untrustworthy about
worldly wealth, who will trust you with the true riches of
heaven]{.underline}? 12 And [if you are not faithful with other people's
money, why should you be trusted with money of your
own]{.underline}?" Some people would look at our subject today and
say, this does not affect me; I don't have enough money to worry about
it. It does not matter how much money you have, what matters is whose it
is! If all we have belongs to God, then it is His money, not ours! If
all that I have belongs to God then each new day brings a brand new test
of stewardship! Will my resources have the spirit of God resting on them
or will they be directed by the spirit of mammon?

The Bible is saying that we must prove ourselves faithful with a little
before we will be given more! If you have a little to nothing you are a
perfect candidate for the blessing of God because if you will prove to
Him you can be faithful with a little He will entrust more to you! A
person who is unjust with a little will be unjust with a whole lot more!
Christians who cheat with a little will not become faithful when they
are rich!

CONCLUSION: Is God speaking to us about money or is the spirit of
mammon calling the shots? One or the other is happening! If we say
neither is happening then the spirit of mammon has already rocked us to
sleep and is in control of our finances by default!

The golden rule for our finances is "We are Gods stewards and we are
managing his resources for Him." God reminded Israel of this before
they went into Canaan and embarked upon one of the most extraordinary
times of national wealth in their history. Deuteronomy 8:18 (NLT)
"Always remember that [it is the [Lord]{.smallcaps} your God who
gives you power to become rich]{.underline}, and he does it to fulfill
the covenant he made with your ancestors."
