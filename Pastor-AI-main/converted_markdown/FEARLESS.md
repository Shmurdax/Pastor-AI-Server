Paul refers to fear in this way, "the spirit of fear". This is a
very interesting statement indeed. Fear is a spirit?

Two very important facts about fear:

- Fear is an emotion... Fear as an emotion is when someone scares you,
 or you are frightened by a sudden circumstance.

- Fear is a spirit... Fear as a spirit is when there is constant ongoing
 fear with regard to a thing.

If fear is a spirit we know it is not a spirit from God, "God has not
given us the spirit of fear". If God does not give us the spirit of
fear, where does this spirit originate? It comes from hell and is of the
devil!

Fear is referred to as a specific spirit, "the spirit of fear". At
one time or another we have all been the target and victim of the spirit
of fear.

FEARLESS FAITH

Hebrews 11:1 (KJV) "Now faith is the substance of things hoped for,
the evidence of things not seen." Hebrews 11:1 (AMP) "Now faith is
the assurance (the confirmation, the title deed) of the things \[we\]
hope for, being the proof of things \[we\] do not see and the conviction
of their reality \[faith perceiving as real fact what is not revealed to
the senses\]."

The way God sees things and the way we see things are very different.
God's thoughts and our thoughts are more times than not, on two
different wavelengths. We have a propensity to operate in what we can
see and perceive, but God operates at a much higher level. Fearless
faith occurs when we see a thing the way God sees them. (Example of
driving in a car or flying in a helicopter)

We often make decisions on our natural observation but God operates in
the supernatural.

Our decisions and choices are rooted in the facts but God decides a
thing based on truth.

We walk by sight (Though we are told not to), God functions in faith.

When we see things differently than God sees them we often speak out of
what we see rather than what we are supposed to know.

This kind of living causes us to say one thing while God is saying
something completely different. This causes us to go against faith and
operate in doubt.

Operating in doubt introduces dysfunction into our lives and causes
faith to plummet, allowing our problems to eclipse our promise.

If we are going to live a life of fearless faith and receive all His
benefits and see signs, miracles, and wonders we must learn to live a
life of fearless faith.

The best examples of fearless faith are found in Hebrews eleven:

- Because of fearless faith "the elders obtained a good report."

- Because of fearless faith "Able offered unto God a more excellent
 sacrifice than Cain."

- Because of fearless faith Ables sacrifice is preaching to us today.

 became heir of the blessings of God.

- Because of fearless faith Abraham left all and sought a better country
 and a better promise.

- Because of fearless faith Sarah gave birth to the promised son at
 ninety years old. (She conceived this promised son with a husband who

- Because of fearless faith Abraham offered up his promised son Isaac as
 a sacrifice.

- Because of fearless faith Isaac blessed Jacob and Esau with regard to
 their future.

- Because of fearless faith Jacob blessed Joseph's sons.

- Because of fearless faith when he was dying, Joseph prophesied
 Israel's deliverance from Egypt.

- Because of fearless faith Jochebed hid Moses three months despite the
 Kings commandment.

- Because of fearless faith Moses "refused to be called the son of
 Pharaoh's daughter. Choosing to suffer affliction with the people of
 God rather than to enjoy the pleasure of sin for a season."

- Because of fearless faith Moses "forsook Egypt, not fearing the
 wrath of the King."

- Because of fearless faith Moses walked through the sea on dry ground

that the path will be easy. Mark 6:45-52 (KJV) "And straightway [he

Jesus did not respond to fear or desperation, He responded to fearless
faith. The disciples believed He was an Angel and they knew this was
their only hope so they riveted their faith to the supernatural and
Jesus responded by going to them and calming the storm.

Fearless faith carries with it the ability to move what we need, from
the Heavenly realm to the natural realm. "Life and death is in the
power of the tongue." (O be careful little mouth what you say)

Fearless faith is that faith that goes beyond saying that God is able to
believing and saying, "He will do this..."

Here is what fearless faith looks like in our lives:

- When confronted with difficulty, we refuse to say, "It is
 impossible". We choose to confess... Luke 18:27 (NLT) "What is
 impossible from a human perspective is possible with God."

- When we are weary, we refuse to say, "I am too tired to go on". We
 choose to confess... Matthew 11:28-30 (NLT) "Come to me, all of you
 who are weary and carry heavy burdens, and I will give you rest. 
 Take my yoke upon you. Let me teach you, because I am humble and
 gentle, and you will find rest for your souls. For my yoke fits

- When we have been betrayed in a relationship, we refuse to say,
 "Nobody really loves or cares about me. I must not be good enough".

 not perish but have eternal life."

 on". We choose to confess... 2 Corinthians 12:9 (NLT) "My

 best in your weakness." Psalms 91:15 (NLT) "When they
 call on me, I will answer; I will be with them in
 trouble. I will rescue them and honor them."

- When we do not know which way to turn, we refuse to say, "What should

- When fear presses in on us, we refuse to say, "I am afraid of what

 "God has not given us a spirit of fear and timidity, but of power,
 love, and self-discipline."

- When we face situations that are much bigger than we are, we refuse to
 say, "I am not wise enough to negotiate this mess". We choose to
 confess... James 1:5 (NLT) "If you need wisdom---if you want to
 know what God wants you to do---ask him, and he will gladly tell you.
 He will not resent your asking."

God's will for everyone in this place is to move us from believing faith
to fearless faith! I ask each of you are you ready to step out on faith
and believe God for the unthinkable?

In our text Joshua was laying the ground rules for possessing Canaan.
Moses had just died and Joshua had been saddled with the dubious task of
challenging the Israelites to actually engage in the battle for Canaan.
Joshua knew that the battle for Canaan was just the first step in
occupying the land for generations to come.

Joshua fully understood that the only way they could conquer the land
and then occupy it over the long haul was to raise up a generation who
valued righteousness and followed the commands of Jehovah.

Joshua knew that if they were able to conquer Canaan, the only thing
that could take the Promised Land from them was sin.

He was painfully aware that the land, and the gains they had made, would
never be more than one generation from being lost. This is true in our
lives. The heritage we are attempting to pass to our children is always
one generation from extinction. This is precisely why we must raise
fearless families who are intent on passing this faith to the next
generation.

The task of passing our faith along to our children and grandchildren is
not as simple as it seems because all families have elements of
dysfunction in them. ALL families! (Adam & Eve -- The first family --
Cain murdered Able)

The psalmist wrote to his music director and tells him that only God can
bring unity to families. Psalm 68:6 (NKJV) "God sets [the solitary in
families]{.underline}; He brings out those who are bound into
prosperity; But the rebellious dwell in a dry land." Notice the two
other things he points to here:

- When a family embraces unity, prosperity occurs.

- When unity is spurned by a family drought conditions will persist.
 Nothing works right when there is conflict in a family. James 3:16
 (KJV) "Where there is strife, there is every evil work." To
 allow strife to persist in our homes is like standing on the front
 porch and waving evil spirits from the streets to come live with us.

My prayer for CT'ers is that our families will march in lock step, and
move forward shoulder to shoulder in pursuit of being a fearless family.

Characteristics of fearless families:

- Everyone is serving Jesus...

- Everyone is filled with the Spirit...

- Family devotions are a matter of routine...

- The tithe is joyfully returned to the church...

- Faithful church attendance in non-negotiable...

- Serving in ministry through the church is a matter of course...

- Being involved in outreach is a family activity...

In the Old Testament farmers were instructed to leave some grain in the

harvest-time there were two sets of harvesters in the field, the primary
harvesters which worked the middle of the field were actually hired by
the landowner to help bring in the harvest; and the poor people in the
corners of the field which were harvesting grain to sustain their
families.

The harvesters in the middle of the field would fill their baskets and
walk to the barn or wagon and empty the grain and go back to the middle
of the field to repeat the process. Because they were being paid by the
hour it did not matter to this group of harvesters how full their basket
was, they were working until the harvest was reaped. They just needed to
stay busy until the job was done. The next set of harvesters had a
completely different motivation, they were poor and had probably
traveled a great distance to be in the field and all they would be able
to bring home for their families is what they could get in their basket!

The contents of the basket were a matter of life and death to them!
Because this was a matter of survival the poor would not just fill their
basket half full, and leave the field, they would press-it-down,
shake-it-together to make room for more, and heap-it-up again.
