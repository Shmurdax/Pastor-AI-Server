TEXT: Psalms 77:11-20

INTRO: The author of this Psalm is Asaph, who is described in
Scripture as the son of Berechiah of the Gershonite family and was
appointed by David and later by Solomon to be over the service of song.
In this Scripture, Asaph is probably meditating on the direction the
music should take for the upcoming services and in this deliberation he
begins to think of God's mighty power in helping Israel to negotiate the
difficulty of the Red Sea passage. He then begins to write to the chief

The Red Sea passage is one of the great miracles of the Bible. Someone
said that it was not a miracle at all because the Red Sea was only a
very small body of water that could have easily been forded by a child.
IF that were so, this instance becomes an even greater miracle because
God drowned the host of the Egyptians in the Red Sea!

This miracle is laid out before us in the fourteenth chapter of Exodus.
The Israelites have been freed from 400 years of slavery in Egypt and
they march triumphantly out of the land. When the reality of what the
burden the Israelites have been carrying for the Egyptian economy begins
to settle in on the Egyptians they change their minds and pursue Israel.
Now the Israelites face the dilemma of the Red Sea in front of them and
Pharaoh's army behind them! They are trapped! There is no way out! Have
you ever been there?

In your marriage

On your job

With your children

In your finances

In your spiritual walk

The feeling of being trapped is hopelessness; there is no way out! But
wait a minute, there is still a thing called a miracle! God still
performs miracles! The day of miracles is not over because there never
was a day of miracles; this is a God of miracles! It is Gods nature to
do a miracle! He is more apt to do a miracle than not to do one!

Charles Spurgeon said, "The Lord will make a way for you where no foot
has been before. That which, like a sea, threatens to drown you, shall
be a highway for your escape."

Being trapped and feeling hopeless calls for a miracle! Over the next
few weeks I want us to look at this instance of the Red Sea passage and
take from it at least ten lessons! The ten strategies we will be
considering are as follows:

God means for you to be where you are!

Be more concerned for God's glory than for your relief!

Acknowledge your enemy but keep your eyes on the Lord!

Pray!

Give God time to work!

When unsure, just take the next logical step by faith!

Envision God\' enveloping presence!

Trust God to deliver in His own unique way!

View your current crisis as a faith builder for the future!

Don\'t forget to praise Him!

## I. STRATEGY \# 1: REALIZE, GOD MEANS FOR US TO BE WHERE WE ARE!

Exodus 14:1-4 (NKJV) "Now the [Lord]{.smallcaps} spoke to Moses,
saying:2 "Speak to the children of Israel, that they [turn and camp
before Pi Hahiroth, between Migdol and the sea, opposite Baal Zephon;
you shall camp before it by the sea]{.underline}.3 "For Pharaoh will say
of the children of Israel, 'They are bewildered by the land; the
wilderness has closed them in.'4 "Then I will harden Pharaoh's heart, so
that he will pursue them; and I will gain honor over Pharaoh and over
all his army, that the Egyptians may know that I am the
[Lord]{.smallcaps}." And they did so."

The Lord is so specific with Moses that He actually tells him to turn
back and camp at a specific place. What I am about to say will sound
somewhat sacrilegious but I do not mean it that way! God's instructions
very seldom make logical sense. And for that reason, those who pursue
His will understand that they must receive His instructions with a
spiritual mind, rather than a logical mind.

The place where they camped really hemmed them in! The sea was before
them, Pharaoh\'s hosts behind them, and the mountains around them. And
all this, at Gods instruction!

Three things for us to remember when we face difficulty:

- We are able to control the outcome of some difficulties!

- Some circumstances are beyond our control!

- Whether or not we have any control, God will guide every circumstance
 to a satisfactory conclusion, if we will trust and obey Him!

When we face difficulty it is easy for us to allow ourselves to begin to
explore the world of worry! Someone described worry like this: "Worry
is the trickle of fear that meanders through the mind, cutting a channel
into that which all other thoughts flow."

John R. Rice said, "Worry is putting question marks where God has put
Periods."

Bishop Fulton J. Sheen called worry, "A form of atheism, for it
portrays a lack of faith and trust in God."

But for some of us, worry seems as inherent as breathing. Most of us do
not feel that we have done our due diligence until we have worried
sufficiently in difficult times. Worry is the enemy of faith! Look at
what the Jesus had to say about worry. Matthew 6:25-34 (CEV) "I tell
you [not to worry]{.underline} about your life. [Don't
worry]{.underline} about having something to eat, drink, or wear. Isn't
life more than food or clothing? 26 Look at the birds in the sky! They

- Our outgo exceeds our income and it appears our upkeep is going to be
 our downfall?

- The creditors are calling?

- Our financial portfolio is collapsing?

- When we are terminated from a job we intended to retire from?

- When a loved one is diagnosed with an incurable disease?

- When a child is living in a rebellious state, doing who knows what
 with who knows who? (Fox News: After the New Orleans flood a reporter

How could Moses and Aaron keep from worrying when the Red Sea was facing
them? The mountains were beside them? The soldiers of Egypt were bearing
down on them with swords drawn?

What helped create faith in a moment of terror was the fact that they
knew Jehovah delivered them from Egypt and that it was His will for them
to go to Canaan! Moses determined that they were where they were not by
their own will, but according to the will of God!

When we determine that we are where we are by the will of God, we should
begin converting our worries into prayers and our fears into faith. God
will never send you to a place without furnishing the grace to do His
will while maintaining your sanity and integrity!

In the story of the Red Sea the Israelites followed the pillar of cloud
and fire as carefully as possible; they were no doubt thrilled with
their new freedom and were full of excitement about the future. They
were leaving 400 years of slavery behind them! They were marching
toward:

- A new country where they would experience freedom for the first time!

- A new opportunity for upward mobility!

- A new home, one of their choosing!

Yet as they followed Him, God deliberately led them into a cul-de-sac
between hostile hills, to the edge of a sea too deep to be forded and
too wide to he crossed. The unmistakable indication of Exodus 14:1-4 is
that the Lord took responsibility for leading them into peril. He gave
them specific, step-by-step instructions, leading them down a route to
apparent ruin: "Turn and camp; camp there; there, before the
entrapping sea; yes, right there in that impossible place."

Please understand that God was not being disingenuous to Israel:

- He was about to deal with Israel's enemies once and for all!

- He was about to allow them to see Him in a way they had never seen Him

- He was about to provide them with weapons to protect themselves,
 during the forty plus years of wilderness wandering!

The Lord occasionally does the same with us, testing our faith, leading
us into hardship, teaching us wisdom, showing us His ways. Our first
reaction may be a surge of panic and a sense of alarm, but we must learn

- Make sure we are in His will!

- Make sure we have not sinned!

- Express faith rather than fear!

We must be patient while He brings the components necessary for our

## II. STRATEGY #2: BE MORE CONCERNED FOR GODS GLORY THAN FOR OUR
RELIEF!

As we begin to deal with the temptation of seeking our relief above
everything else, let me just say that this is a very natural reaction to
difficult circumstances. At the same time we must be aware that the
natural mind is the enemy of faith and of Gods will. When we encounter
difficult times we must always remember that God is using this to set us
up for victory while setting the enemy up for defeat.

In Scripture, God often allowed His people to endure hardship while he
was collecting their enemies for a major disaster! Such is the case here
at the Red Sea! Notice Exodus 14:3-4, (KJV) "For Pharaoh will say of
the children of Israel, They are entangled in the land, the wilderness
hath shut them in. 4 And I will harden Pharaoh's heart, that he shall
follow after them; and [I will be honored]{.underline} upon Pharaoh, and
upon all his host; that [the Egyptians may know that I am the
LORD]{.underline}. And they did so."

When we encounter impossible odds we should always ask ourselves, "What
is God up to in this situation?" When we come upon a difficult crisis we
must always remember that if we operate in faith rather than fear God
will ultimately reveal His:

- Divine grace!

- Divine sufficiency!

- Divine will!

Every trial is meant to enable the believer to preserve the balance of
the Christian life and to bring glory to God in the most difficult of
times.

In difficult times we are often perplexed because the trial seems to be
without rhyme or reason and we cannot find the answers to our questions.
This is mostly because we are asking the wrong questions. We ask
questions like;

- How did I get into this mess?

- Why did this happen to me?

- How quickly can I solve this problem?

These are natural questions, but they may be the wrong ones to ask. The
children of Israel made this mistake, Exodus 14:10-12 (CEV) "When the
Israelites saw the king coming with his army, they were frightened and
begged the [Lord]{.smallcaps} for help. 11 They also complained to
Moses, "[Wasn't there enough room in Egypt to bury us?]{.underline} [Is
that why you brought us out here to die in the desert?]{.underline} [Why
did you bring us out of Egypt anyway?]{.underline} 12 [While we were
there, didn't we tell you to leave us alone?]{.underline} We had rather
be slaves in Egypt than die in this desert!"

There is a better approach, one that results in an entirely new way of
looking at difficulties; one that puts our problems into a different
context and creates a new paradigm for dealing with tough situations.

## III. STRATEGY #3: ACKNOWLEDGE YOUR ENEMY, BUT KEEP YOUR EYES ON THE
LORD!

There is no question that Israel had an enemy. The design of the enemy
is consistently bent on destruction and demise.

Look at what Israel was facing. Exodus 14:5-9 (CEV) "When the king of
Egypt heard that the Israelites had finally left, he and his officials
[changed their minds]{.underline} and said, "Look what we have done! We
let them get away, and they will no longer be our slaves."6 The king got
his war chariot and army ready. 7 He commanded his officers in charge of
his six hundred best chariots and all his other chariots to [start after
the Israelites]{.underline}. 8 The [Lord]{.smallcaps} made the king so
stubborn that he [went after them]{.underline}, even though the
Israelites proudly went on their way."

The Israelites could have denied the existence of an enemy but that
would not have changed the fact that a hostile king was in hot pursuit
of this group of emancipated slaves.

The denial of an enemy never calms his hostilities but the
acknowledgement of his existence demands readiness. Charles Spurgeon
paraphrases this Scripture in this way, "The great tyrant has not
forgotten you, and he designs your capture and re-enslavement."

The devil sometimes launches direct, frontal attacks against us. But
Satan is usually more conniving and insidious. In Ephesians 6:12-13
(CEV) "We are not fighting against humans. We are fighting against
forces and authorities and against rulers of darkness and powers in the
spiritual world. 13 So put on all the armor that God gives. Then when
that evil day comes, you will be able to defend yourself. And when the
battle is over, you will still be standing firm."

Whenever the enemy attacks, whether covertly or overtly, he is normally
closer and cruder than we first imagined, much like the Pharaoh of the
Exodus.

Have you ever felt pursued? Oppressed? Sensed the devil nipping at your
heels? Ever wondered if your simultaneous troubles were orchestrated by
a brutal, invisible hand? Suspected that your depression or anger
stemmed from a malicious source?

While dealing with difficulty we should always acknowledge our enemy,
while keeping our eyes on the Lord.

I want us to consider the parallels between Pharaoh and Satan:

- Both are unyielding enemies!

- Both covet power over their subjects!

- Both are enraged beyond reason!

- Both have assembled vast armies meant for destruction!

- Both will eventually be plundered by the Almighty!

The Bible likens Satan to five different animals:

- In Genesis 3, he\'s a serpent trying to deceive God\'s people!

- In Matthew 13, he's a bird trying to defile God\'s harvest!

- In John 10, He's a wolf attacking God\'s flock!

- In First Peter, he\'s a lion trying to devour God\'s children!

- In Revelation 12, he\'s a dragon wanting to destroy God\'s Son!

- The weapon of discouragement!

- Family members!

- A hypocrite in the church!

- Believing the maker of the promise to be faithful...

- Being fully persuaded that God has the power to do what He has

- Believing that things will happen just as He has told us.

Every one of us live our lives by faith!

When we take our morning shower, we assume there\'s going to be water,
preferably hot...

When we eat our cereal, we expect it to be healthy and wholesome...

When we drive to work, we proceed through green lights, assum­ing they
are red for intersecting traffic...

When we step on the brake we expect the vehicle to stop...

When we press the accelerator we expect the vehicle to go...

Even the most sincere atheist lives by faith, not only in his athe­istic
philosophy, but in the very processes and procedures of everyday life.
God created this universe so that the faith principle is always at work.

For Christians, faith is making certain assumptions about God\'s care
and control over our lives, based on His scriptural promises. We may not
understand every circum­stance or appreciate every event. Sometimes
we\'re backed up to the Red Sea with the Egyptians in pursuit. But God
has given us promises, and we disappoint Him when we question His
ability to keep His Word. This is proven by the experience of the
disciples on the Sea of Galilee. Mark 4:39-40 (NLT) "When he woke up,

The level of faith differs from person to person! One person\'s faith is
turbocharged while another\'s sputters on one cylinder. The reason for
this discrepancy of faith is not that one person was given more than the
other. In fact the Scripture tells us, everyone started out with the
same amount of faith. (TO EVERY MAN WAS GIVEN THE MEASURE OF FAITH)

Several factors determine the level of our faith:

- The level of intimacy between the individual and the maker of the
 promise...

- The level of the practical knowledge of the Word of God...

- The amount of exercise our faith has received over the months and
 years...

- Our willingness to endure hardship without whimpering...

As Jesus wandered through Palestine, He had a sort of X-ray vision that
penetrated hearts and measured faith. He seemed intensely interested in
the quantity of faith being exercised by those who crossed His path. He
made various statements with regard to those whom he met:

The Centurion of Capernaum whose servant was at the point of death told
Jesus He did not need to come to his house to heal his servant. He said
"Just speak the word and he will be healed." Jesus said, Matthew
8:10 (NLT "I tell you the truth, I haven't seen faith like this in
all the land of Israel!"

The Canaanite woman whose daughter was demon possessed would not take no
for an answer said "Even the dogs get crumbs from the Masters
table!" Jesus said, Matthew 15:28 "Woman, your faith is great.
Your request is granted."

To the Disciples on the Sea of Galilee He said, "[Why are you so
afraid? Do you still not have faith in me]{.underline}?" Matthew 8:26
(NLT)

Several Friends brought a paralyzed man to Jesus in Matthew 9:2 (NLT)
"Seeing their faith, Jesus said to the paralyzed man, "Take heart,
son! Your sins are forgiven."

Without question, it takes faith to move the hand of God and when He
sees our faith He rewards those who fully trust Him. Hebrews 11:6 (NKJV)
"But [without faith it is impossible to please Him]{.underline}, for
he who comes to God must believe that He is, and that [He is a rewarder
of those who diligently seek Him]{.underline}."

Faith isn\'t something I can muster up at will; it must be given by God
and developed according to the processes of life which He has ordained.
He is, after all, a God who grows things. Physically, each of us began
life as a tiny speck. It\'s a marvelous cell, for all the
characteristics of each person; the sex, eye color, shoe size,
intelligence, and so forth are determined at fertilization by the
baby\'s genetic code that resides in the forty-six human chromosomes in
that one cell.

We begin micro­scopically, and then grow into a mature human being. In
the same way faith begins small and grows mighty! God intends to develop
us spiritually. How? Like any good teacher, He bestows truth; then
devises tests to review and reinforce that truth, to trans­fer and
translate it into lasting, life changing experiences.

Like any good coach, He sits down with His players, using a game book of
Scripture, gives us information, explains the rules, and reviews the
plays. Then comes the scrimmage; the video tapes; the correction; more
information and another scrimmage. It is virtually an endless cycle.

In the process:

- Good players become skilled professionals...

- Good people become growing disciples...

- Little faith grows into great faith...

- Mountains become molehills...

- Difficulties become opportunities...

This process is how God chose to deal with the Children of Israel and
with the disciples. With the Israelites, He gave instructions through
Moses, then brought them to the edge of the Red Sea. He provided a
miracle in the Red Sea passage then led them to Canaan. Because of their
unbelief He led them from Canaan to the wilderness where He allowed them
to be tested. The wilderness is the place where the Children of Israel
were permitted to apply their promises to their problems.

Jesus taught the disciples on the mountainside, then loaded them into a
boat and sent them into a terrific storm designed to help them apply the
promise to the problem.

The same is true for us. At church we hear the Word of God, and in our
devotions we feed on the Scriptures. The Lord then sends a trial into
our lives to give us an oppor­tunity to put His teachings into practice.
As we trust Him and pass the test, we\'re strengthened for the future.

This, then, is the principle: our faith grows when we choose to apply
God\'s promises to today\'s problems and use the experiences to mature
us for tomorrow\'s challenges. In a sense, we are storing up faith for
times ahead. That\'s why the Bible is so full of promises. We never
encounter any situation for which God has not provided a precious
promise for our perplexing problem.

The Puritan Thomas Watson said, "Faith lives in a promise, as the fish
lives in the water. The promises are both comforting and quickening. The
promises of God are flotation devices to keep us from sinking when we
come to the waters of affliction!"

J. I. Packer stated, "In the days when the Bible was uni­versally
acknowledged in the churches as \'God\'s Word writ­ten,\' it was clearly
understood that the promises recorded in Scripture were the proper,
God-given basis for all our life of faith, and that the way to
strengthen ones faith was to focus it upon particular promises that
spoke to one\'s condition."

Faith, then, is simply finding and claiming the promises of God in every
situation, and, based on those promises, making consistent decisions,
being fully persuaded that God has the power to do what He has promised.
