INTRO: Here in Matthew five Jesus lays out eight markers for
believers to self judge ones on spiritual maturity. Each of the eight
are immediately followed by a promised blessing.

The beatitudes are:

- Proverb like...

- Without narrative...

- Cryptic...

- Precise...

- Full of meaning...

Each of the beatitudes mirrors a major Biblical theme. The theme I want
to point to today is "Blessed are the peacemakers: for they shall be
called the children of God." (Vs. 9)

All of us have either had situations, now have situations, or will have
situations in our lives where we will need to exercise our right to act
like daddy God and be a peacemaker.

Note: There is a big difference between being a peacekeeper and being a
peacemaker! Peacekeepers are people pleasers and are willing to give up
their core values to keep the peace! (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)
(Example: A woman willing to quit going to church and serving Jesus to
please an unsaved husband)

Peacemakers find a way to make peace without compromising their core
values. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) (Example: Jesus faced the
pressure to conform to the worlds system but rather than caving in to
keep the peace He went to the cross and made the peace!)

I would like to point out four things a peacemaker will be required to
do in the peacemaking process. If we do these things we will make peace
wherever we serve and people will begin to recognize we are the children
of God!

## I. CHOOSE YOUR BATTLES CAREFULLY!

In this world criticism comes with the territory. People often
misunderstand your intentions, develop false assumptions from less than
limited information, and question your character.

Sometimes it\'s not necessary to come down off the wall and speak to
everything. Your friends don\'t need an explanation and your enemies
won\'t believe you anyway. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

You may have the footing, the right and the ammunition to take care of
business but that isn\'t always the right course to take.

A peacemaker knows, nothing is worth a broken relationship. Because we
understand this, we choose to react in a way that allows the
relationship to remain, while creating a path to resolution.

Peacemakers are fully aware of the value of people...

## II. CAREFULLY CHOOSE YOUR REACTION...

When the need for peacemaking presents itself it is almost always at a
time when emotions are running high, and hostilities are everywhere.

Sure, you could set people straight, set the record straight, demand
your way, etc...

Peacemakers carefully guard:

- Their words...

- Their reactions...

- Their body language...

- Their facial expressions...

Peacemakers understand that a wrong step in any one of these disciplines
can ruin the peace process by shutting down the engagement of the
individual(s) involved. This is true in:

- Marriage...

- Child rearing...

- Professional careers...

Sometimes peacemakers have to hold their peace and allow the Lord to
fight their battles. (Story about this)

Peacemakers know that once words are spilled into the atmosphere they
can never truly be taken back. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)
They\'re like a feather pillow that explodes. Months later feathers are
turning up out of nowhere!

Peacemakers choose to react with Christian respect to everyone because
they know that what really counts are the people who are involved.

## III. TREAT PEOPLE WITH DIGNITY...

Regardless of what people do to you, keep a sweet spirit. Love everyone!
(That doesn\'t mean you have to go on vacation with them)

Don't just take the high road! Take the highest road. (LEAVE ON SCREEN
UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) (Example: Staff members who have left throwing sand in
the air)

Peacemakers know two wrongs never make a right, and always bear in mind,
the people are what really counts!

## IV. PEOPLE NEVER DEPRECIATE...

(Buy a new car -- Drive it 30 day's -- Have it appraised)

Things depreciate but people never do. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL NEXT
SLIDE) The worlds system depreciates people based on the following
things:

- Net worth...

- Color of skin...

- How old they are...

- How young they are...

- Connections or lack of them...

In the God world two things last forever:

- God's Word...

- The human soul...

CONCLUSION:

Matthew 5:9 say's "Blessed are the peacemakers: for they shall be
called the children of God." In other words people will recognize us
as the children of our Father because we have gone beyond keeping the
peace to making the peace!

Think of this:

- Jesus served...

- Jesus healed...

- Jesus comforted

- Jesus fed the hungry...

- Jesus ministered to the people...

- Jesus redeemed the multitudes...

He was:

- Cursed...

- Beaten...

- Mocked...

- Misquoted...

- Misunderstood...

- Ultimately murdered...

Yet, He continued with the peace process! Peacemakers never give up on
the situation! (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL END OF SERVICE)
