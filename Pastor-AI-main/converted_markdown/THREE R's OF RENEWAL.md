THREE R\'s OF RENEWAL

INTRO: Set the scene... Vs. 17-21

The taste of victory is tremendous, but victory is only temporary. As
soon as today's victory is won we must be in the process of preparation
for tomorrow's battle.

Almost everything has need of periodic renewal. People - houses -
vehicles - organizations - ministries - churches - etc. Organizations
fail for lack of renewal. Swiss watchmakers refused to adjust to the
advent of the invention of the quartz watch and they lost their
domination of the world watch-making market.

Our fellowship is in need of renewal from top to bottom:

- Average A.M. attendance -- 143

- Median age of A/G minister -- 51

- In 1995 we opened 274 churches and 215 churches were closed. Net gin
 of 59 churches...

- In 2005 258 churches were opened and 237 churches were closed. Net
 gain 21 churches...

- Between the years of 1995 and 2005 the U.S. A/G church grew from
 2,387,982 to 2,830,861 adherents. A net gain of 442,969. This sounds
 pretty good on the surface but in ten years we only had an 18.55%
 growth rate extrapolated out over a ten year period! This is 1.85% per
 year...

- During the same period the worldwide fellowship more than doubled,
 growing from 25,219,790 to 54,717,677.

The worst thing that can happen to any organization is a lack of
introspection and a failure to [adjust]{.underline} to the times. Our
message must never change but our methods must be in a constant state of
metamorphosis.

When renewal does not occur the following things happen:

- [Decay]{.underline}

- [Disrepair]{.underline}

- [Decline]{.underline}

- Lack of [motivation]{.underline}

- Loss of [interest]{.underline}

- [Reversal]{.underline}

The shelf life of Christian leaders is growing shorter every day due to
burnout, disillusionment, and lack of tangible success. 86 ministers
leave the ministry every day! How do we prevent this? By knowing we are
called and embarking on a voyage of development that does not cease
until they throw dirt in our face.

In short; if renewal does not occur pretty often, the leader will
burnout and the organization will [crash]{.underline} and
[burn]{.underline}! No-one wants to leave a legacy or epitaph of
failure. Everyone wants to succeed. The difference between success and
failure is times of renewal and adjustment. (Larissa told her
grandparents that she was going to make the WNBA and whether or not they
came to her games would determine if they were to go to a good nursing
home or a bad one!)

If we are to make our church and individual ministries relevant and
effective into the future we can ill afford to rest on the pinnacle of
yesterday\'s victory. (David - When he attacked the enemy and won - A
few days\' later they were back again - He enquired of the Lord and the
Lord told him not to do things the same way!) I know he was not Assembly
of God! Things went so well before, there would be no need to pray!

The seven last words of a dying church are, "We've never done it that
way before..."

Our spiritual walk, our church and individual ministries are in constant
need of renewal! The following are three R's of renewal.

# I. REINVENT...

2 Samuel 5:22-23 (NKJV) "Then the Philistines went up once again and
deployed themselves in the Valley of Rephaim (The valley of the
giants).23 Therefore [David inquired of the [Lord,]{.smallcaps} and
He said, "You shall not go up]{.underline}..."

One of the most important lessons the church needs to learn is, just
because it worked a decade or two ago is no reason to believe it will
work today! Our message must remain consistent but our methods must
constantly be undergoing evaluation and change.

Reinvent [Programs]{.underline} -- Determine what is working for you and
be in a constant state of fine tuning. In the process of fine tuning, if
you make a decision that is destructive rather than constructive, STOP
IT! (Two service Sunday morning schedule with SS in between)

Reinvent [People]{.underline} - Continually evaluate the strengths of
those in your down-line -- Adjust their responsibilities to compliment
their strengths. (PLACE)

Reinvent [Ourselves]{.underline} - Continually evaluate what you are
doing and how you are doing it. (Listened to my own tapes -- I told
Susan, "I sounded angry." She said, "You were!")

Even though David and his men had experienced enormous success in the
battle of yesterday they had to reinvent a battle plan for tomorrow's
victory! Each task calls for different gifts. Don\'t be afraid to change
you!

## II. REAFFIRM...

2 Samuel 5:22-24 (NKJV) " Then the Philistines went up once again and
deployed themselves in the Valley of Rephaim (The valley of the
giants).23 Therefore David inquired of the [Lord,]{.smallcaps} and He
said, "You shall not go up; circle around behind them, and come upon
them in front of the mulberry trees.24 "And it shall be, when you hear
the sound of marching in the tops of the mulberry trees (The sound of
horses running through the tops of the trees), then [you shall
advance quickly]{.underline}..." I am pretty sure David was not
Assembly of God because any Assembly of God leader knows if it worked
once it will work eternally!

We must remember, God is a God of variety and he rarely does anything
the same way twice. David recognized this and if he had failed to
consult with God concerning today's battle he would have lost all the
momentum he had gained in yesterday's victory. Rather than running the
risk of losing ground he had gained he asked God about His strategy for
today's victory! He has a strategy for victory for each of our battles!

Reaffirm [Programs]{.underline} - Constantly evaluate programs for
effectiveness. If a ministry has grown obsolete or ineffective do one of
three things:

- [Adjust it!]{.underline} Don't be afraid to tweak what you are doing!
 The larger you grow the more important this becomes!

<!-- -->

- [Repackage]{.underline} it! Call it something besides Sunday school if
 necessary... (Christian Education, Discipleship Training, etc...
 Believe it or not, it does not have to happen on Sunday morning at
 9:45!)

- [Kill]{.underline} it! Riding a dead horse carries with it three
 devastating results; The seat is too low to the ground to be
 comfortable; the scenery looks much the same at all times; the smell
 will eventually get to you! Don't be afraid to bury a dead horse! The
 only people who will be offended are those who have never known any
 thing other than the smell of death!

Reaffirm [People]{.underline} -- We must be constantly reaffirming and
encouraging people! I must force myself to do this. I am a very driven
person, no-one has to pat me on the back to encourage me to do my job,
and do it well -- this is what I do and I pour everything into it! I
expect everyone else to do the same! (When I held my first staff meeting
at CT I told the staff, "It does not matter how much I like you or how
close I get to you, there is one word you must remember; PRODUCE -- when
you fail to PRODUCE, you will be working elsewhere.") Because I am a
work-a-holic and a perfectionist I must constantly remind myself to
reaffirm people. People need to be reaffirmed in the following ways:

- [Cards]{.underline}...

- [Calls]{.underline}...

- A [pat]{.underline} on the back...

- Public [recognition]{.underline}...

Do you think David needed to reaffirm the soldiers when he told them
they were going to "Sneak around behind the enemy and wait until they
heard horses running through the tops of the mulberry trees"?

Reaffirm [Ourselves]{.underline} -- Sometimes we must encourage
ourselves in the Lord! This can be accomplished with a dedicated time of
prayer and the Word each day. Other things we must do to reaffirm
ourselves:

- Ongoing [evaluation]{.underline}...

- Ongoing [education]{.underline} ...

- Work on our [strengths]{.underline}...

- Overcome our [weaknesses]{.underline}...

Do you think David needed any affirmation when God spoke to him about
horses running through the tops of trees? He probably said something
like, "If you want them to know, tell them yourself!" (Shane Gore)

## III. RELEASE...

2 Samuel 5:22-25 (NKJV) "Then the Philistines went up once again and
deployed themselves in the Valley of Rephaim (The valley of the
giants).23 Therefore David inquired of the [Lord,]{.smallcaps} and He
said, "You shall not go up; circle around behind them, and come upon
them in front of the mulberry trees.24 "And it shall be, when you hear
the sound of marching in the tops of the mulberry trees (The sound of
horses running through the tops of the trees), [then you shall
advance quickly. For then the [Lord]{.smallcaps} will go out before you
to strike the camp of the Philistines."25 And David did so, as the
[Lord]{.smallcaps} commanded him; and he drove back the Philistines from
Geba]{.underline} (Gee-baw) [as far as Gezer]{.underline}
(Gay-zer)."

The moment arrived when David had to release his men to go out and do
the will of God! He could no do it all himself, he had to trust them to
do what he had trained and instructed them to do! Would they do it
exactly as he would do it? Probably not, but they would certainly do it
within the framework of the instructions he had given them!

This is something that does not come naturally to me because I am a
perfectionist, I want things done right! Everything!!! My theme for
years was, "It's not what you expect but what you inspect"!!! When we
release a thing, we must allow for the task to be accomplished with the
[personality]{.underline} of the leader being [dominant]{.underline}. No
two personalities are alike. The final product is the ultimate thing not
the process!

We must:

- Release [Programs]{.underline}... Working programs & failing programs!

- Release [People]{.underline}... Assign responsibility - Grant
 authority -- Support publicly, correct privately - Give credit!

- Release [Ourselves]{.underline} -- Release yourself to do what you are
 called to -- Release yourself to do what you are good at -- Release
 yourself from responsibilities that consume large amounts of time and
 have little or no return!

David could lead his men into [battle]{.underline} but he could not
fight the battle [alone]{.underline}! We are not superman; we cannot
succeed solely on our own ability! Together, these men were successful!
Any one of them standing alone would have failed. Three things that made
them successful were;

- [God]{.underline}...

- The [Leader]{.underline}...

- Obedient [Followers]{.underline}...

When David heard from God and released his men to do the will of God the
victory was secured. 2 Samuel 5:24-25 (NKJV) "And it shall be, when
you hear the sound of marching in the tops of the mulberry trees (The
sound of horses running through the tops of the trees), [then you
shall advance quickly. For then the [Lord]{.smallcaps} will go out
before you to strike the camp of the Philistines."25 And David did so,
as the [Lord]{.smallcaps} commanded him; and he drove back the
Philistines from Geba]{.underline} (Gee-baw) [as far as
Gezer]{.underline} (Gay-zer)."

David was successful in leading his men from the valley of giants to a
breakthrough because he; reinvented; reaffirmed and released his men to
do the will of God!
