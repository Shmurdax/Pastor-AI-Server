# ANCHORS

Hebrews 6:13-20 "For when God made a promise to Abraham, because He
could swear by no one greater, He swore by Himself, 14 saying, "Surely
blessing I will bless you, and multiplying I will multiply you." 15 And
so, after he had patiently endured, he obtained the promise. 16 For men
indeed swear by the greater, and an oath for confirmation is for them an
end of all dispute. 17 Thus God, determining to show more abundantly to
the heirs of promise the immutability of His counsel, confirmed it by an
oath, 18 that by two immutable things, in which it is impossible for God
to lie, we might have strong consolation, who have fled for refuge to
lay hold of the hope set before us. 19 [Which hope we have as an anchor

INTRO: Hope enters into the "THAT" which is in the veil! The
only thing that matters is inside the veil -- And that is Jesus!

(TALK ABOUT THE HOLY OF HOLIES -- THIS IS WHERE GOD LIVED -- ARK OF THE
COVENANT - MERCY SEAT -- TABLES OF THE LAW -- AARONS ROD THAT BUDDED)

(TALK ABOUT ANCHORS AND HOW THEY WORK)

When an anchor is dropped it goes through the water and fastens itself
to the seabed! The solidity at the bottom of the sea moves through the
anchor -- Through the chain -- To the boat -- And ultimately to all of
us!

The writer of Hebrews declares that our anchor is hope! I am anchored to
hope in the:

- Forgiveness that is available through the blood of Jesus...

- Fellowship I have in the body of Christ...

- Fathers loving embrace...

- Word of God...

ANCHORED TO FORGIVENESS

In the Old Testament God spoke in types and shadows to the people who
were living in the moment, as well as all of us who would come along at
a later time and dispensation. (TALK ABOUT TYPES AND SHADOWS)

One type and shadow is spelled out in the Book of Exodus. It is the
account of the death of the firstborn in the land of Egypt. The death
angel passed through the land of Egypt and took the life of the
firstborn of every house who had not applied the blood of a lamb to
their doorway.

Exodus 12:1-4 The Israelites were instructed to:

- Choose a lamb...

- The lamb had to be without blemish...

- The lamb was to be fed the right diet...

- The lamb was to be killed at the appropriate time...

- The blood of the dead lamb was to be painted on the doorway of their
 houses...

- Prepare the lamb for serving according to God's recipe...

- Eat the flesh of the lamb with their sandals on and their staff in
 their hand... (EXPLAIN THE SIGNIFICANCE OF THIS)

Until the resurrection of Jesus, this was the most revered event in the
history of Israel. It commemorated the night The Israelites came out of
Egyptian bondage. 

Today we look back to the cross, but in doing so we must go back a few
thousand years more, to the event that foreshadowed and prophesied the
cross.

What is our purpose for being here today? Is it just to spend a couple
of hours together singing songs and hearing a few words from an old book
called a Bible? This is partially true, but our purpose is much greater!

We are here to become more anchored to that which is behind the veil.

In Old Testament worship the High Priest would take the blood of the
sacrifice from the altar and go behind the curtain and sprinkle the
blood of the sacrifice on the Mercy Seat. The moment of truth of whether
or not God would accept the sacrifice was when spotless blood met the
presence of God! We are anchored to that which is behind the veil!

(TALK ABOUT THIS AND PUT ON THE HARNESS)

The walk from the altar of sacrifice to the Holy of Holies say's:

- We are here to declare that we are holding to the anchor of the blood
 of Jesus... Hebrews 9:22 (NKJV) "And according to the law almost
 all things are purified with blood, and without shedding of blood
 there is no remission." Charles Spurgeon said, " [Morality may
 keep you out of jail, but it takes the blood of Jesus Christ to keep
 you out of
 hell.](http://www.azquotes.com/quote/610674?ref=blood-of-jesus)"

<!-- -->

- We are here to continue our move, from death to life -- The only way
 to do this is through the blood... (What can wash away my sin? Nothing
 but the blood of Jesus)

- We are here to continue our transition from bondage to freedom -- The
 only way to affect real life change is through the blood of Jesus...
 (Paul- He doth --Hath -- And will yet deliver) Jesse Duplantis said,
 "The Blood of Jesus washes away our past and the Name of Jesus opens
 up our future."

If my anchor can't move, I can't lose. We are attached to something that
can't:

- Be defeated...

- Be altered...

- Be compromised...

How did the Children of Israel get into bondage to start with?

- Story of Joseph...

- There arose a pharaoh who didn't know Joseph... (They went to Egypt a
 family of 60 something and left a nation of millions -- In the worst
 of times...)

- Egypt was not their home. Israel became slaves to the Egyptians...

- God delivered them by the blood of the lamb... 

Fast forward a few thousand years... People today are living in bondage
and don't know how they got there or how to get out...

CONCLUSION:

Exodus 12:11-13 (NKJV) "And thus you shall eat it: with a belt on
your waist, your sandals on your feet, and your staff in your hand. So
you shall eat it in haste. It is the LORD'S Passover. 12 'For I will
pass through the land of Egypt on that night, and will strike all the
firstborn in the land of Egypt, both man and beast; and against all the
gods of Egypt, I will execute judgment: I am the LORD. 13 'Now the blood
shall be a sign for you on the houses where you are. And when I see the
blood, I will pass over you; and the plague shall not be on you to
destroy you when I strike the land of Egypt." 

- Belt... Finishing touches for the journey (EXPERIENCE IN AIRPORT)

- Sandals... Ready for going the distance

- Staff... Prepared for protection

They were to be prepared - for what God was preparing for them. It is
time for us to get anchored to the forgiveness that God has provided,
and to get ready for what God has ready for us!

At 11:59 p.m. they were slaves... At 12:00 midnight they were free...

What was the difference between what happened to the Egyptians and the
Israelis?

- The Egyptians were shocked by judgment -- The Israelis were struck by
 deliverance!

- The Egyptians were broadsided by loss -- The Israelis were stepping
 into a gateway of a new day...

- The Egyptians were losing their national wealth -- The Israelis were
 collecting on bad debts...

- The Egyptians were planning funerals -- The Israelis were planning to
 celebrate...

What was the difference? The forgiveness provided by the blood that was
behind the veil! This forgiveness is horizontal and verticle! "If you
bring a gift to the altar and discover that your brother has something
against you... Leave the gift at the altar -- Reconcile with your
brother --THEN offer your gift."

In the middle of all the instructions concerning the Passover and
deliverance from Egypt... these words are given... "If the household
be too little for the lamb..." Notice that the Lamb is NEVER too
little for the household... His blood reaches to the highest mountain
and flows to the lowest valley!!!

The fact is, there is too much Jesus for just you or your house -- We
must share the good news of the anchor of forgiveness!

There is no sinner so bad that Jesus cannot save them... "Whosoever
shall call upon the name of the LORD shall be saved." Charles
Spurgeon said, "[Atonement by the blood of Jesus is not an arm of
Christian truth; it is the heart of
it.](http://www.azquotes.com/quote/862921?ref=blood-of-jesus)"

There is no sin so great that Jesus cannot forgive... 1 John 1:9 (AV)
"If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our
sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness."

which is behind the veil... (BEHIND THE VEIL IS WHERE GOD LIVED -- THE

culture if we do not anchor ourselves to the Word of God we will be
pushed all over the map by the culture of the world -- and - the culture

recreation, social norms, and entertainment! Any church built on
"things" such as this cannot stand!
