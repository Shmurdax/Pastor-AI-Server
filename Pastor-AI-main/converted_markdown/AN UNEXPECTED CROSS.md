# AN UNEXPECTED CROSS

\ POWER POINT SLIDES

\\ INSTRUCTIONS TO THE TECHNICIAN

TEXT: Matthew 27:27-32 (KJV) "Then the soldiers of the governor
took Jesus into the common hall, and gathered unto him the whole band of
soldiers. And they stripped him, and put on him a scarlet robe.
And when they had platted a crown of thorns, they put it upon his
head, and a reed in his right hand: and they bowed the knee before him,
and mocked him, saying, Hail, King of the Jews! And they spit upon
him, and took the reed, and smote him on the head. And after that
they had mocked him, they took the robe off from him, and put his own
raiment on him, and led him away to crucify him. And as they came
out, [they found a man of Cyrene, Simon by name: him they compelled to
bear his cross]{.underline}."

INTRO: The word compelled here comes from the Greek word Aggareuo
(Uh-gar-e-o) and means "To employ a courier, dispatch a mounted
messenger, press into public service." In this Scripture it is not
clear whether Simon was forced, encouraged, or volunteered to carry the
cross of Jesus. On the surface it appears he was selected and not given
a choice.

There are nine men named Simon in the New Testament: (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Simon Peter, the Disciple of Jesus

- Simon, who was called Zelotes

- Simon, the father of Judas, who betrayed Jesus

- Simon, the Samaritan wizard

- Simon, the tanner

- Simon, the Pharisee

- Simon, the cousin of Jesus

- Simon, the leper

- Simon of Cyrene, who carried the cross of Jesus

I suppose we could talk about any one of these men and find some pretty
interesting things about their lives. This man Simon who carried the
cross of Jesus is a particularly interesting character. We know very
little about him. In fact, the only thing we know for sure is that he
was called upon to carry an unexpected cross.

It was probably out of curiosity that Simon attended the kangaroo court
that sentenced Jesus to death. He was an innocent bystander who was
called upon to do something he was not expecting to do. He probably did
not get up that morning to attend the hearing of Jesus in hopes of
carrying his cross to Golgotha. Most of us have better things to do than
volunteer to carry a ninety-pound cross up a mountainside.

Most of us either have faced unexpected crosses or will face unexpected
crosses. An unexpected cross is being forced to deal with something we
did not see coming.

Certainly, there are things we can see while they are still at a
distance, and in such a scenario, we can make preparations to deal with
them in an orderly fashion. Other things take us by surprise and we are
forced to deal with them on a moment-to-moment basis.

Some areas we face unexpected crosses:

## II. UNEXPECTED CROSSES WITH OUR HEALTH When we are healthy, we take
our health for granted. None of us ever plan to be sick, but sickness is
in the world and all of us will be called upon to deal with it sooner or
later.

Looking forward to the time of Christ the Prophet Isaiah made this
declarative statement. Isaiah 53:5 (NKJV) "But He was wounded for our

apostle Peter made this observation. 1 Peter 2:24 (NKJV) "Who Himself
bore our sins in His own body on the tree, that we, having died to sins,
might live for righteousness---by whose stripes you were healed."
