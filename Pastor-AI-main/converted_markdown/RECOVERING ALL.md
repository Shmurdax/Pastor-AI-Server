# RECOVERING ALL

TEXT: 1 Samuel 27:1-7 (NIV) "But David thought to himself, 'One
of these days I shall be destroyed by the hand of Saul. The best thing I
can do is to escape to the land of the Philistines. Then Saul will give
up searching for me anywhere in Israel, and I will slip out of his
hand.' 2 So David and the six hundred men with him left and went over to
Achish son of Maok king of Gath. 3 David and his men settled in Gath
with Achish. Each man had his family with him, and David had his two
wives: Ahinoam of Jezreel and Abigail of Carmel, the widow of Nabal.
4 When Saul was told that David had fled to Gath, he no longer searched
for him. 5 Then David said to Achish, 'If I have found favour in your
eyes, let a place be assigned to me in one of the country towns, that I
may live there. Why should your servant live in the royal city with
you?' 6 So on that day Achish gave him Ziklag, and it has belonged to
the kings of Judah ever since. 7 David lived in Philistine territory for
a year and four months." 1 Samuel 30:1-8 (NKJV) "Now it happened,
when David and his men came to Ziklag, on the third day, that the
Amalekites had invaded the South and Ziklag, attacked Ziklag and burned
it with fire, 2 and had taken captive the women and those who were
there, from small to great; they did not kill anyone, but carried them
away and went their way. 3 So David and his men came to the city, and
there it was, burned with fire; and their wives, their sons, and their
daughters had been taken captive. 4 Then David and the people who were
with him lifted up their voices and wept, until they had no more power
to weep. 5 And David's two wives, Ahinoam the Jezreelitess, and Abigail
the widow of Nabal the Carmelite, had been taken captive. 6 Now David
was greatly distressed, for the people spoke of stoning him, because the
soul of all the people was grieved, every man for his sons and his
daughters. But David strengthened himself in the Lord his God. 7 Then
David said to Abiathar the priest, Ahimelech's son, "Please bring the
ephod here to me." And Abiathar brought the ephod to David. 8 So David
inquired of the Lord, saying, "Shall I pursue this troop? Shall I
overtake them?" And He answered him, "Pursue, for you shall surely
overtake them and without fail recover all."

INTRO: (David anointed by Samuel to be king over Israel)

(David killed Goliath -- Saul has killed his thousands but David his ten
thousands)

(Saul's jealousy causes him to hate David, thus he pursues David to kill
him)

David no doubt became very weary with all the pressures of being pursued
and allowed the stress to cause him to make some mistakes.

David took matters into his own hands and made plans to dwell in the
Land of the Philistines. The King of the Philistines gives him a town
called Ziklag for he and his entourage to dwell in.

During this time David had the opportunity to kill Saul but refused to
do so.

Saul couldn't have killed David if he had wanted to. It could be, that
if David had not retreated to Ziklag he would have become King sooner.
(Sometimes we delay our destiny with our decisions to take the path of
least resistance)

While David and his men were out of town, the Amelikites plundered,
robbed, and burned Ziklag. To make matters worse they kidnapped the
women and children! We know these men as David's Mighty Men! Yet, they
are faced with a catastrophic event in their lives. "It rains on the
just and on the unjust."

The philosophy of this world, and quite honestly the philosophy of the
twenty-first-century-church seem to be:

- Good things happen to good people...

- Bad things happen to bad people...

- If good things are happening in your life, you must be a good
 person...

- If bad things are happening in your life you must be a bad person...

I have a Greek word and a Hebrew word for such a philosophy:

- Bologna...

- Hogwash...

Notice the devastation of a David, who killed Goliath and his Mighty
Men: 1 Samuel 30:3-4 "So David and his men came to the city, and,
behold, it was burned with fire; and their wives, and their sons, and
their daughters, were taken captives. 4 Then [David and the people that
were with him lifted up their voice and wept, until they had no more
power to weep.]{.underline}"

Notice this, the MIGHTY MEN have been reduced to WEEPING WARRIORS by a
catastrophe! A Catastrophe has the propensity to turn mighty men into
weeping warriors!

Not only were they weeping warriors, they turned on David! "They
talked of stoning him!" Be careful not to allow your trouble to turn
you against God, or cause you to give up on your destiny!

David and his men lost everything:

- They lost their cattle...

- They lost their possessions...

- They lost their wives...

- They lost their children...

- The enemy burned what they couldn't steal... (Ain't that just like the
 devil?)

Despite the loss of everything David recovered all! How did he do this?
How are we to recover all when it seems the rug has been jerked out from
under us?

## I. NEVER GIVE UP!

David's Mighty Men gave up, but there is no evidence that David
entertained such a thing!

You cannot recover all entertaining thoughts of giving up! On October
29, Winston Churchill gave a speech and uttered the famous words,
"Never give in, never give in, never, never, never, never---in
nothing, great or small, large or petty---never give in except to
convictions of honor and good sense."

If you have lost some things or you have lost everything I am here to
encourage you to not give in to the idea of quitting!

## II. DON'T STOP GOING TO CHURCH!

When tragedy struck, the first place David went was to church. 1 Samuel
30:7 "Then David said to Abiathar the priest, Ahimelech's son,
"Please bring the ephod here to me." And Abiathar brought the ephod to
David.

Some people distance themselves from the church, or quit church in a
crisis! This plays right into the hands of the enemy! Isolation is a
trick as old as Eden! Eve sinned when she was isolated! Don't blame God
for your tragedy, place the blame where it belongs, the Devil is the
dipstick that did it! John 10:10 "The thief comes to steal, kill and
destroy... I have come that you might have life and have it more
abundantly!"

## III. PRAY!

David didn't just go to church he prayed! Tragedy will sometimes cause
us to not want to pray but we need more than ever to pray when the
pressure is on!

If you will pray, God will answer! God can't answer prayers that haven't
been prayed! "You have not, because you ask not..."

1 Samuel 30:8 "So David inquired of the Lord, saying, "Shall I pursue
this troop? Shall I overtake them?"

Be bold in prayer... Be audacious in prayer...

## IV. OBEY THE VOICE OF GOD...

Listen to what the Lord told David, 1 Samuel 30:8 "So David inquired
of the Lord, saying, "Shall I pursue this troop? Shall I overtake them?"
And He answered him, "Pursue..."

David pursued the enemy and in his pursuit he found one of the culprits
who had been left behind because he had fallen sick! There is no honor
among thieves!

David nursed him back to health and the former enemy snitched on his
friends... He rolled over on his former cousins in crime... He turned
states evidence... There is no honor among thieves!

If you will obey the voice of God despite your calamity, God will lead
you into a Holy set up, one step at a time!

## V. RECOVER ALL...

1 Samuel 30:8 "So David inquired of the Lord, saying, "Shall I pursue
this troop? Shall I overtake them?" And He answered him, "Pursue, for
you shall surely overtake them and without fail recover all."

David recovered all, and so will you! If you will keep your faith and
hold to Gods unchanging hand you will recover all!
