INTRO: The Harvest Offering mentioned in Deuteronomy 26 was to take
place at the occasion of the first harvest after entering into the
Promised Land. (The fall is harvest time)

Notice, in verse one the Moses say's, "When (Not if) you come
into the land."

The Harvest offering was to be given in remembrance of where they had
come from and in celebration of where God had brought them.

Where they had come from:

- Theirs Fathers were "Syrian" = Nomad's with no permanent home,
 permanent land or stability. "About to perish, few in number."

- They went to "Egypt" = The place of oppression, bondage and
 frustration. "Mistreated us; afflicted us; laid hard bondage on
 us." But notice, in spite of this they became a great nation!
 "Great, mighty and populous." (Manasseh = Forgetfulness
 (Manasseh was the first son of Joseph) Ephraim = Fruitfulness (Ephraim
 was the second son of Joseph) -- "The Lord made me to be fruitful
 in the land of my affliction.")

- They cried out to "The Lord" = The Lord brought them out with
 "A mighty hand; an outstretched arm; great terror; signs and
 wonders."

- The Lord was going to bring them into "This Land" = Into a place
 of inheritance, permanence and stability. A place "Flowing with
 milk and honey."

The command of Moses was for the Israelites to bring a "Harvest
offering" to the Lord, of the first harvest in Canaan. The offering
being referred to here; was to be an extra special, exceptional
offering. The idea here is that their offering would enact the blessing
of God upon all their other harvests in Canaan!

The Lord had brought them from being lost and oppressed into the
inheritance of Christ and His victory and blessing. The fact is that in
one place in Scripture they were referred to as "Not a people."

The same is true with us! If it were not for the Lord...

Thanking God for our harvest is right and wise and it keeps us properly
focused and centered in His divine favor. It is right because God is our
source.

It is wise because it demonstrates humility, and that ensures God's
grace and favor toward us. It is an act of recognition that we could not
have accomplished these things on our own!

It keeps us properly focused -- We do not become prideful and therefore
avoids God's resistance.

"Promised Land." It is called the "Promised Land" because God had
promised it to their ancestors! He was also promising it to them!
Finally, He was promising it to their descendants!

OFFERING PROCEDURE

- Appropriate offering containers should be pre-placed at the altar
 area. \[Dt. 26:2\] -- "Baskets"

- The congregants should be invited as a group to bring forward their
 offering and present it at the altar, placing it in the
 "baskets" before the pastoral staff & church leaders. \[Dt.
 26:2\]

- The pastor shall take a representative portion of the offering and
 raise it to the Lord.

- The pastor shall call upon the members to raise their hands to the
 Lord and then shall lead them in the following confession.

PRAYER OF CONFESSION (Make sure this is on the screen so the
congregation can join in reciting the prayer with the pastor)

- I was once lost and estranged from God having no spiritual life.

- I lacked direction and divine purpose.

- I lived only for myself and my own pleasures.

- I placed my values on the temporary things of this world.

- I wandered about seeking peace and stability but could not find it.

- I walked in spiritual darkness and ignorance.

- Demonic agents blinded my mind and held sway in my thoughts, tempting
 me and influencing me to commit sin.

- I experienced oppression and mistreatment at the hand of the enemy.

- I found myself in bondage to habits, negative attitudes, fear and
 worry bringing harm to my spirit, body and relationships.

- But God sent His Son, Jesus Christ into this world to teach me, rescue
 me and restore me.

- He shined the light of His glorious gospel into my heart.

- He responded to my cries for mercy and help by stretching forth His
 hand to save me.

- He gave life to my spirit.

- He smote my oppressors, revoked their claims and broke their chains.

- He placed me into His kingdom and authority.

- He became my Father and brought me into His family.

- He committed to me the great and precious promises of His Word.

- He filled me with His Holy Spirit.

- He called me into His service giving me meaning and purpose.

- He blessed the work of my hands.

- He brought peace to my heart, strength to my arms and direction to my
 feet.

- He brought order to my house and peace to my family.

I bring this offering today:

- In remembrance of where He has brought me from.

- In celebration of where He has brought me to.

- In thanks for all He has given me.

As my sacrifice for all He is, all He has done and all He is going to
do:

- I declare the power of the seed I have sown.

- I declare that the enemy will be powerless to kill or diminish its
 effectiveness.

- I release YOU to rebuke the devourer for my sake.

- I declare that both the seed and the harvest will produce maximum
 impact.

- I declare that my family and I will have more than enough because of
 the harvest.

- I declare that the Kingdom of God and CT church will be blessed as a
 result of my harvest.
