[The Center for American Progress recently made the following statement:
"America's families are caught in a perfect storm. Massive amounts of
debt, falling house prices, disappearing jobs, flat wages, lower wages,
and skyrocketing costs for the most important consumer items are quickly
emptying the pockets of middle class America and bringing many families
to the edge of financial ruin." They released the following statistics
in April of 2008: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)]{.mark}

- [Jobs -- Employment declined by 80,000 jobs in March 2008 -- This
 represents the largest loss since March 2003.]{.mark}

- [Wages -- Despite inflation wages remain flat. Compared to March of
 2001 the hourly wage of an individual in February 2008 was up 2.3% and
 the weekly wages were 1.1% higher! This is over a seven year
 period!]{.mark}

- [Pensions and Health Insurance -- The share of private sector workers
 with a pension dropped from 50.3% in 2000 to 43.2% in 2006. In the
 same period the share of employees with employer-provided health
 coverage dropped from 64.2% to 59.7%.]{.mark}

- [Family Debt -- Household debt averaged a record 133.7% of disposable
 income in the fourth quarter of 2007! In the fourth quarter of 2007
 families spent 14.3% of their disposable income to service their debt,
 up from 13% in the first quarter of 2001.]{.mark}

- [Housing Crisis -- New home sales in February 2008 were 28.9% lower
 than a year earlier. The median sales price of a home dropped 2.7%.
 The total values of homes fell by 1.8% in the fourth quarter of 2007.
 This is the largest drop since the third quarter of 1975! Home equity
 as a share of home values also fell to a record low of 47.9%.
 Foreclosures have hit a record high with 2% of all homes in
 foreclosure. The bankruptcy rate is up 85.2% from the first quarter of
 2006 to the third quarter of 2007. Credit card defaults rose to 4.2%
 of all credit card debt by the fourth quarter of 2007, an increase of
 32.6% from the first quarter of 2006!]{.mark}

- [Oil and Gas Prices -- The price of crude oil at the end of March 2008
 which was to be delivered in the next month, was 54.2% higher than a
 year earlier! According to Aaron Wissner, in 2003 the average price
 for a gallon of gas was \$1.50 compared to \$3.47 in April 2008. He
 also predicts we could see \$10.00 per gallon gas in the next seven
 years! Does this sound crazy? In March of 2008 the average price of
 gas in Europe was \$9.30 per gallon!]{.mark}

- [People Paying More for Basics -- Transportation costs 9.5% more in
 January 2008 than a year earlier, college tuition is up 6.2%, fuel and
 utilities is up by 5.6%, medical care is up 4.5%, the cost of food is
 up 4.6%,. Since March 2001 food prices have rose by 21.6%, fuels and
 utilities 36.3%, medical care 34.1%, transportation 26.1% and college
 tuition by 63.5%!]{.mark}

- [National Deficit Increases - In January 2008, the Congressional
 Budget Office estimated that the deficit for 2008 will amount to \$219
 Billion - \$56 Billion more than in 2007 -- This is really frightening
 when you consider that this is not even taking into account the
 economic stimulus package or the wars in Afghanistan or Iraq!]{.mark}

- [The Value of the Dollar is Falling -- By the end of March 2008, a
 dollar bought 44.4% fewer euros, 34.1% fewer Canadian dollars, 39.9%
 fewer British pounds, and 18.2% fewer Japanese Yen than in March 2001.
 After inflation, the dollar has lost 22.2% of its value against its
 trading partner currencies between march 2001 and March 2008!]{.mark}
