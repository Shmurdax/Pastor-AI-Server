THE PROOF OF DESIRE IS PURSUIT

Over the past couple of years a renewed "DESIRE" for the things of God
has become topic number one among the church crowd in America, and quite
possibly worldwide. As I consider the idea of "DESIRE" for God, I must
honestly ask myself what I am willing to surrender in order that I might
"PURSUE" him and his righteousness. Noone will ever be able to "PURSUE"
the things of God as long as the influence of the world is important to
them. The influence of the world is certainly worldiness, and sinful
practices. However I am convinced that the thing that keeps most
Christians from "PURSUING" God is their inability to escape the
perimeters and boundaries, that they themselves have set for God's
Spirit. These boundaries are traditions that they have come under
bondage to, beyond which they have chosen not to "PURSUE" God!

Most Christians, preachers and laity alike, give enormous amounts of lip
service to the fact that they "DESIRE" the thingsof God more than
anything else. It matters little how much lip service we give "THE PROOF
OF DESIRE IS PURSUIT"!!!

In Colossians chapter three we are told that if we are going to "PURSUE"
God there are at least three things we are going to have to do;

First of all we are told that we must "Set our affection on things
above, not on things on the earth". The church has suffered spiritual
famine over the past couple of decades because we have allowed our
attention to be diverted from our cause (PURSUING GOD), to a "PURSUIT"
of the world and the things of the world. As a result we have become as
carnal as a canary and cannot for the life of us figure out why God is
not moving like he used to! The setting of your affection is not
something God is going to do for you, it's something you have been
commanded to do on your own, if you are going to "PURSUE" God!

Secondly, we are told to "Seek those things which are above"! Let me ask
you some questions that might help you determine what you are really
seeking: Would you rather pray,or visit with your friends? Would you
rather read your Bible , or watch television? Would you rather go to
church, or go to a ballgame? Would you rather pay your tithes, or pay
your car payment? Would you rather speak in tongues, or speak in
english? Would you rather forgive someone who has wronged you, or hold a
grudge? You see seeking God is more than just saying "Jesus I want you
to be Lord of my life". Seeking God is a 24 hour a day, seven day a week
"PURSUIT" of God and his righteousness!

Thirdly, the Scripture say's "When Christ, who is your life, shall
appear, then shall ye also appear with him in Glory". Need I remind you,
that when you set your affection where it is supposed to be, and you
seek God with a whole heart, Christ will appear in your life. (In every
area of it!) When he appears you will soar with him! You will soar with
him in the here and now by walking in the Spirit! You will also soar
with him when he comes in the clouds of Heaven! There is a bumper
sticker that has come out of the Pensacola Revival that makes the
statement "YES LORD, WE WILL RIDE!" I think it might be better stated,
"YES LORD, WE WILL SOAR!" I leave you with the following Scripture:
Psalm 42:1 "As the deer pants for streams of water,

so my soul pants for you, O God." Now friends that sounds like an all
out "PURSUIT" of God, thus proving his "DESIRE"!

I THINK MYSELF HAPPY!

In the Twenty Sixth chapter of the book of Acts we find the

Apostle Paul standing before Agrippa about to answer questions
concerning his behavior within the religious community. He has been
accused of all kinds of blasphemous acts when in reality there was
absolutely no evidence to support the charges!

Paul has been arrested and tried by the legal religious authorities of
his day, and because no one can find anything within his character
worthy of prison or death, they pass him around like a political hot
potato!

While Paul was in Ephesus for his final time he spoke of his
incarceration in this manner: Acts 20:22-23 "And now, behold, I go
bound in the spirit unto Jerusalem, not knowing the things that shall
befall me there: Save that the Holy Ghost witnesseth in every city,
saying that bonds and afflictions abide me."

Paul had every opportunity to become angry, and develop a sour
attitude, but listen to what he continues to say: Acts 20:24 "But
none of these things move me, neither count I my life dear unto myself,
so that I might finish my course with joy, and the ministry, which I
have received of the Lord Jesus, to testify the gospel of the grace of
God."

How could he possibly maintain an attitude of victory in such a
turbulent time in his life? I believe he let us in on the secret to his
attitude of victory even though he knew he was staring death in the
whites of the eyes. Listen to the first Four words he says to Agrippa,
Acts 26:2 "I think myself happy..."

Now please understand that I am aware that Paul was saying to Agrippa
that he was happy for the opportunity to finally tell his side of the
story. However I believe that those Four words characterize the life and
ministry of the Apostle Paul! He had Pastored and preached in places
where he had to "THINK HIMSELF HAPPY" in order to continue on!

Paul practiced "THINKING HIMSELF HAPPY" to the point that he was able
to say, Philip. 4:11 "... I have learned, in whatsoever state I am,
therewith to be content."

How could this be? I believe he told us how Three versus earlier:
Philip. 4:8 "Finally, brethren, whatsoever things are true,
whatsoever things are honest, whatsoever things are just, whatsoever
things are pure, whatsoever things are lovely, whatsoever things
are of good report; if there be any virtue, and if there be any
praise, think on these things."

I can tell you that in your marriage, relationships, employment, and
spiritual walk you will either "THINK YOURSELF" happy or sad!

I choose by an act of my own will to "THINK MYSELF HAPPY"!!! Are you
going to overflow with joy and be pleasant to be around, or are you
going to be a sad sack that everyone dreads having to spend time with?

The decision is yours! "THINK YOURSELF HAPPY" for all of our
sakes!!!

The book of Hebrews is one of my favorite books in the Bible. This
Epistle is loaded with tremendous nuggets of spiritual truth that can be
adapted to everyday twenty-first century life. I have recently been
reading in the book of Hebrews in my private devotions. As usual I began
to notice some things I have never noticed before. The sixth chapter
began to come alive to me, and I want to look at it with you in this
article. There are three things I want us to focus our thoughts on
today; Good Works; Hope; Faith & Patience:

Good Works: Hebrews 6:10 "God is not unjust; he will not forget your
work and the love you have shown him as you have helped his people and
continue to help them." When someone blesses us in some way, we try to
find a way to show our gratitude. When someone blesses our children, we
are more grateful than we would have been if they had blessed us. The
writer here is saying that God will bless those who bless his children
This is a promise as old as Abraham. God said "I will bless them that
bless you, I will curse them that curse you." If you want the blessing
of God, find a way to bless one of his children. If you have done your
part to bless God's children, wait patiently because God has a plan in
the works to bless you! If, on the other hand, you have been involved in
tearing down God's family you will no doubt receive punishment if there
is no repentance and restitution. Believe me, I would much rather be
found blessing God's family, rather than fighting his family.

Hope: Verse talks about "...Hope to the end." Then Verse 18 say's
that "...We might have strong consolation, having fled for refuge to
lay hold of the Hope set before us." Verse 19 say's "Hope is the anchor
of the soul, sure and steadfast, and takes us behind the veil." What is
this Hope? The word Hope comes from the Greek word Elpis, and means
confidence. What confidence is capable of anchoring our soul's (Mind,
will & emotions) in the midst of lifes storms? Confidence that the blood
of Jesus is enough to cover all of our sin's, past, present & future!
Confidence in His ability to keep us in the here and now! Confidence in
His ability and willingness to meet all my needs! Confidence in His soon
return!

It is possible to be involved in Good Works, and have your Confidence
rightly directed and still never be able to get to the place in God that
you feel your prayers are going to be answered. Hebrews 6:12 "We do
not want you to become lazy, but to imitate those who through faith and
patience inherit what has been promised." We are strongly admonished
here to follow the example of Faith and Patience set by those who
obtained promises. Speaking of Abraham Hebrews 6:15 say's "And so
after waiting patiently, Abraham received what was promised."

Can you believe it? Winter is in full swing, the trees and landscape are
dead, the weather leaves much to be desired, the year 2000 has come to a
conclusion, and 2001 is about to begin. Even though most of us do not
like winter, we patiently endure it because we know spring is just
around the corner, and with spring there will be "A NEW BEGINNING".
The year 2001 is the beginning of the twenty first century. It is my
opinion that as a church we should look upon this year as "A NEW
BEGINNING".

God's word is full of examples of people who were afforded the
opportunity for "A NEW BEGINNING". After more than 400 years of
slavery in Egypt, Israel was given a new lease on life in the form of
"A NEW BEGINNING". Exodus 12:1-2 "And the Lord spake unto
Moses and Aaron in the land of Egypt, saying, This month shall be
unto you [the beginning of months]{.underline}: it shall be [the first
month of the year to you]{.underline}."

In order to bring this "NEW BEGINNING" about for the Israelites, God
sets a bush on fire in the desert and speaks to a senior citizen by the
name of Moses to lead them from Egypt to Caanan. For Israel the
visitation of the death angel meant the "BEGINNING" of a new epoch,
a new era, this would be a new starting point. God is no respector of
persons, what he did for Israel, he will do for you and I! Isaiah
43:19 "Behold, I will do [a new thing]{.underline}; [now it shall
spring forth]{.underline}; shall ye not know it? I will even make a way
in the wilderness, and rivers in the desert."

If you were to research the history of Israel, you would find a path
strewn with the wreckage of centuries of exile and servitude. For Israel
this "NEW BEGINNING" was to be an emancipation, a redemption, an
exodus! I'm quite sure that many of the Israelites wondered if they
would ever be able to completely shake the monkey of enslavement and
servitude, and experience "A NEW BEGINNING". The answer to that
question is that God would provide the grace and tools necessary for
them to live free from the past, but the real test would be whether they
would do what was necessary to facilitate this "NEW BEGINNING".

God desires the same for us! He wants to wipe the slate clean, forgive
the past, and make bright the future! The real question is, can you
forget the past and by faith lay hold of the future? As with Israel,
repentance and forgiveness are the foundational stones upon which "A
NEW BEGINNING" is built. To repent means to say you are sorry for the
wrong you have done, and make a firm committment never to repeat the
action. To forgive is to allow God by the power of his Holy Spirit to
take the pain out of a past experience, to the point that you have no
desire to even the score. If you truly desire to have "A NEW
BEGINNING" you must firmly plant your spiritual feet upon these two
foundational stones.

Responsibility comes with "A NEW BEGINNING". When Israel left Egypt
they were confronted with the hardships of the wildnernness, and finally
with the responsibility of driving the giants out of Caanan. There comes
a time for those who have experienced "A NEW BEGINNING" to be
schooled and discipled by processes that are less than desireable. It is
in these difficult times that the characteristics of our Heavenly Father
are developed within us. These processes cannot be hurried, rather
patiently and faithfully endured. I believe that the year of 2000, as
difficult as it was, has set the stage for God to do a new thing within
this church. I can hardly wait to see what God is going to accomplish in
2001 as we prepare for "A NEW BEGINNING'!

I can hardly believe it is almost Christmas time again. Christmas should
be one of the most important holidays on the calendar of the Christian.
Christmas has become so commercialized that many Christian people
actually dread the season. It is my opinion that we need to put Christ
back in Christmas, and allow his presence to be the center and
circumference of everything that we do.

I am going to be doing something this year that I have never done since
I have been pastoring. Through the month of December in each service I
am going to be preaching on a Christmas theme. Our theme for the month
will be "CHRISTMAS LESSONS OF WORSHIP".

The second chapter of the book of Matthew is dedicated to describing the
events surrounding the birth of Jesus. In this historical account of the
first Christmas I believe we can find at least five "CHRISTMAS LESSONS
OF WORSHIP".

## I. WORSHIP WILL TAKE YOU ON A SPIRITUAL JOURNEY! Matthew 2:1-2
"Now when Jesus was born in Bethlehem of Judaea in the days of Herod
the king, behold, there came wise men from the east to Jerusalem.
Saying, Where is he that is born King of the Jews? for [we have seen his
star in the east, and are come to worship him]{.underline}."

We are not sure how far these wise men had to travel to get to Bethlehem
to worship Jesus, but we must assume that they traveled a considerable
distance. The worship of the wise men caused them to embark upon a
journey. When you begin to enter into true worship it will take you on a
journey. Jesus told the woman at the well of Samaria, John 4:23
"But the hour cometh, and now is, when the [true worshippers shall
worship the Father in spirit and in truth]{.underline}: for the Father
seeketh such to worship him."

When you begin to worship God in spirit and in truth, your worship will
take you on a journey. This journey will take you from the camp of the
living dead, into the very presence of God, where the joy of living can
be found. Your worship will be the vehicle that transports you from
where you are, to where God wants you to be.

## II. WORSHIP WILL TAKE YOU OUT OF FAMILIAR SURROUNDINGS!

When you examine this story you will discover that every character
involved, is outside their familiar surroundings including Mary, Joseph,
and baby Jesus.

When you begin to embark upon a journey of worship, you will soon
discover that you are being moved beyond your normal comfort zone. The
end result of worship will cause us to sit together in heavenly places
in Christ Jesus.

## III. WORSHIP WILL BRING YOU TO YOUR KNEES AT THE FEET OF JESUS!
Matthew 2:11 "And when they were come into the house, they saw
the young child with Mary his mother, and [fell down, and worshipped
him]{.underline}:..."

The wise men are accustomed to being approached by a King who is seeking
advice. Now they find themselves on their knees, worshipping a newborn
baby! Someone asked the question, "how many wise men came to worship
Jesus?" The answer to that question is, "all of them"!

Our journey of worship will cause us to bow at his feet in worship!

## IV. WORSHIP WILL CAUSE YOU TO GIVE YOUR VERY BEST! Matthew 2:11
"And when they were come into the house, they saw the young child
with Mary his mother, and fell down, and worshipped him: and when they
had opened their treasures, [they presented unto him gifts; gold,
frankincense, and myrrh]{.underline}."

The level of worship that these people experienced, separated them from
their most precious material goods. If you pursue a new level of worship
your journey will likely find you offering him everything you posses.
Romans 12:1-2 "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies
of God, that ye [present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy,
acceptable unto God, which is your reasonable service]{.underline}.
And be not conformed to this world: but be ye transformed by the
renewing of your mind, that ye may prove what is that good, and
acceptable, and perfect, will of God."

## V. WORSHIP WILL CAUSE YOU TO LEAVE ANOTHER WAY! Matthew 2:12
"And being warned of God in a dream that they should not return to
Herod, [they departed into their own country another
way]{.underline}."

Everyone who has ever embarked upon a journey of worship to the King,

blind, but now we can see! We have been translated from the kingdom of
darkness into the kingdom of his dear Son!
