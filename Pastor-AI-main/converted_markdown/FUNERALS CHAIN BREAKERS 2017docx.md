# FUNERALS

Chain-breakers 2017

1) A funeral is about 2 things -- Honoring the deceased and ministering
 to the living

2) I always ask the family to give me 10-12 bullet points of things
 they want the audience to know about their loved one (What is in
 your wallet)

3) I try to build a funeral sermon that comforts the family and friends
 -- Honors the deceased -- and convicts the lost

4) Prepare the ending of your sermon to include an altar call (My
 experience with this)

5) I use the bullet points to weave into a scripture text

6) Always rewrite the obituary to where you are not just reading the
 published version verbatim -- For instance -- John Doe passed on
 10/12/17... John Departed this life to be in the presence of Jesus
 on 10/12/17...

7) Always phonetically spell the names of all family members -- If you
 are not sure about a pronunciation ASK a family member -- DO NOT
 WING IT

8) Never speak more than 20 -30 minutes at a funeral

9) After the sermon and final prayer, stand at the head of the casket
 and shake hands with everyone that comes by -- Be sure to have a
 smile on your face as you greet the guests

10) Lead the casket from the pulpit to the hearse

11) Lead the casket from the hearse to the burial plot

12) Be prepared to call the graveside service to order by asking
 everyone to gather in as close as possible

13) Make sure your remarks are practical -- but be sure to address the
 resurrection -- (Example -- This wife has lost a loving husband,
 these children have lost an awesome dad... Wait a minute! I don't
 think anyone has lost anything... This place seems so final -- this
 is a comma in the middle of a sentence, not a period, exclamation
 point or question mark)

14) Never speak more than 5-10 minutes at the gravesite

15) When you are finished speaking remove your boutonnière and place it
 among the flowers on top of the casket

16) Shake hands with all immediate family seated under the tent,
 expressing your love and condolences
