GROW or GO 
 
For more than 40 years I’ve been a student of 
church growth. I believe the church is 
genetically engineered to grow. If the church is 
not growing something is off and needs the 
attention of church leadership. 
 
The two predominant characteristics of the first 
Century Church: 
• Growth… 
• Multiplication… 
 
Here is the slogan I want you to get in your head 
today… We are going to GROW or something, 
maybe even someone, must GO! There are 
times that leaders must subtract before they can 
multiply. 
 
I am going to point out some things that I 
commonly see as hurdles to church growth: 
 

# NO CULTURE OF INVITATION
As leaders, we pray, we ask, we pressure, we 
motivate, we emphasize, but members still don’t 
bring friends to church. Why? 
 
Are we creating environments and events that 
would naturally motivate our people to invite 
friends and family? All of us must answer this 
question individually and honestly. (I am going 
to attempt to help you do that today) 
 
A culture of invitation does not happen on its 
own, culture is intentionally developed. To 
create a culture of invitation, a couple things 
must occur: 
1) Challenge the people to discover how 
they can best evangelize. (Have Susan and 
Nick to talk about this) 
2) Create BRINGING EVENTS. We should 
have 4-6 BRINGING EVENTS per year. 
ALWAYS follow a bringing event with a 
sermon series, starting the next Sunday, 
that would get the attention of the visitors 

and make them want to return. (Marriage – 
Parenting – Reparenting – Finances – 
Etc…) 
 
NO NETS 
Fishing with a pole can feed a person or two but 
fishing with nets can feed multitudes. Are you 
fishing with a pole or a net? 
 
Pole = Having church two or three times a 
week. Life groups. Business as usual. 
Nets = Evangelism events. 
 
Once we catch them, we must then be prepared 
to process them. Conserving the harvest. 
 
Every church has two growing seasons: 
• First of February – First of May 
• Second week of September – Third week of 
November 
 

Wise leaders invest most of their efforts and 
resources that have the potential for growth, in 
these time frames. 
 

CONCLUSION: 
Please be honest – If you were unchurched, and 
lived in close proximity to your church, would 
you visit your church? Please remember, you 
don’t know anyone at your church – So the 
people are friendly, is not a valid reason. 
 
List 5 reasons you would visit your church – If 
you had trouble listing 5 things without even 
having to think about it, I promise you, you are 
not growing. In which case, we must GROW, or 
something, or someone, must GO! This list 
needs to be considered when strategizing for 
growth. 
 
Now, list 5 reasons why you would not pay your 
church a visit? Which list was easier to come up 
with? 
 

This list must be fixed ASAP!
