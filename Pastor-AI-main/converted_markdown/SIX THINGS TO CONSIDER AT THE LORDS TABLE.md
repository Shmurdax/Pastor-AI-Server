INTRO: When we compare Luke\'s account of the Last Supper to Paul\'s
teaching in First Corinthians eleven, we are struck by how rich the
Scripture\'s teaching about the Lords Supper really is.

The teaching of this ordinance is one of the greatest teachings in the
Word of God. I think we often minimize the importance and spiritual
impact of what we are doing because of how many times we have done it
before, and the sameness of what we say and do when we receive this Holy
Ordinance.

I would remind us this morning that when we come to the Lords Table, we
should focus our attention on the riches of God\'s grace, given to us in
Christ. When we receive communion, we are to sense and celebrate the
many-faceted grace of God, demonstrated in Christ, through the Lord\'s
Supper. This Table teaches us to apply the truth of the good
news---Jesus saves!

When we come to the Lords Table we must be focused on the following
things:

REMEMBRANCE

The Psalmist said it like this: Psalm 77:11-12 (NKJV) "I will
remember the works of the Lord; Surely I will remember Your wonders of
old. ^12 ^I will also meditate on all Your work, And talk of Your
deeds."

When discussing the Lords Table both Jesus and Paul in Luke 22 and 1
Corinthians 11, bring out the fact that when we come to celebrate this
Ordinance, we should do so with our memory fully engaged.

The theme of remembrance is one of the most familiar, yet powerful
topics connected to the Lord\'s Supper. One would hardly think that
those who have been redeemed would need to be reminded of God\'s grace
shown in Jesus Christ. But we do need to be reminded.

One of the most famous phrases that is centered around communion is,
"Do this in remembrance of Me."

How do we do this?

- Retrace the final events of Jesus\' life leading up to Calvary,
 reminding ourselves of His suffering and death on our behalf.

- The bread and cup is the visible reminder of the "Body and blood of
 the Lord."

Remembrance strengthens us. Our faith is strengthened and built up
through eating, drinking, and remembering. Zwingli said, "Eating is
believing and believing is eating."

We are never told to "Do this in remembrance of my death." The
command is "Do this in remembrance of me."

CONCLUSION:

When we come to the Lords Table:

- We remember the pit from which He brought us... "He lifted me up
 out of the HORRIBLE pit. Placed my feet on a solid rock, and
 established my life."

- We remember the manner of persons we once were... Paul said to the
 Corinthian church -- 1 Corinthians 6:9-11 (NKJV) "Do you not know
 that the unrighteous will not inherit the kingdom of God? Do not be
 deceived. Neither fornicators, nor idolaters, nor adulterers,

 God. ^11 ^[And such were some of you]{.underline}. But [you were
 washed]{.underline}, but [you were sanctified]{.underline}, but [you
 were justified]{.underline} in the name of the Lord Jesus and by the
 Spirit of our God."

- We remember how He drew us with lovingkindness and tender mercies...

- We remember how delivered us from sin, self, and this present world...
 "I once was a wrong road choosin, losin, sinner; but Jesus made me a
 winner!" Paul said, "He hath delivered, He doth deliver, He will
 yet deliver."

- We remember how He changed our direction... The old song say's "When
 He reached down His hand for me. When He reached way down for me. I
 was lost and undone, without God or His Son, when He reached down His
 hand for me." (Saul on the road to Damascus)

 As we approach the Lords Table today do you remember?

- Posterity = The Hebrew word is "zeh\'-rah" and means: Offspring;

- Serve = The Hebrew word is "aw-bad" and means: To work; to till the

 vineyard

- Recounted = The Hebrew word is "saw-far" and means: To score
 (scratch) with a mark as a tally; to record; to inscribe, and also to
 enumerate.

- Declare = The Hebrew word is "naw-gad" and means: A messenger who
 stands boldly; to manifest; to announce; to expose; to predict; or to
 explain.

- Righteousness = The Hebrew word is "tsed-aw-kaw" and means:
 Rightness in a moral sense; ethical activity; justice; or
 truthfulness.

FELLOWSHIP

1 John 1:6-7 (NKJV) "If we say that we have fellowship with Him, and

All fellowship flows out of a relationship with Jesus Christ. As we
abide in him, and He in us, we thrive and are fruitful.

This passage gives both the vertical and horizontal focus to fellowship:

Vertical fellowship... Vs. 6 "If we say that we have fellowship
with Him, and walk in darkness, we lie and do not practice the
truth." Our fellowship is with the Father and with his Son, Jesus
Christ. John 14:23 Jesus said, "If anyone loves me, he will obey my
teaching. My Father will love him, and we will come to him and make our

home where they fellowship! That home is IN HIM!

Humankind was built for fellowship with the Heavenly Father.

God walked with Adam and Eve in the Garden every day. When sin was
introduced into the earthly realm it broke the fellowship between God
and mankind.

The fall doomed man to an eternity apart from God which is a terrible,
horrific thought. The idea of eternal separation from God is unthinkable
to most of us.

Almost as devastating, is the broken relationship between God and man in
the earthly realm. Most of us cannot even imagine doing life apart from
God. How people get through some of the challenges of life without
walking in fellowship with the Lord, is more than I can fathom.

Horizontal fellowship... Vs. 7 "...If we walk in the light as He
is in the light, we have fellowship with one another..."

Humankind was not genetically engineered to be alone. Genesis 2:18

SELF-EXAMINATION

Jesus\' simple statement, "I tell you the truth, one of you will
betray me," made each of the twelve disciples uneasy and led them to
self-examination. "Surely not I, Lord" each of them asked at
that Last Supper.

Later, Paul taught that not only the disciples but all Christians must
examine themselves before meeting at the Lord\'s Table. He warned that
those who didn\'t ask "Surely not I, Lord?" were eating and
drinking judgment unto themselves.

1 Corinthians 11:28 (NIV) "A man ought to examine himself before he
eats of the bread and drinks of the cup." Here, we are admonished to
look within. Looking within may be the most important but difficult task
at the Lord\'s Table. As we look within, I think we should do so with
several questions in mind:

- Is it well with my Lord?

- Is it well with my soul?

- Is it well with my family?

- Is it well with other believers?

- Is it well with the world?

If the answer to any of these questions is no, we must make the wrongs
right before we can partake of the meal Jesus provided.

When we participate in the exercise of introspection the job is only 10%
complete; the balance of the project is doing something about what we
see. Introspection is a fruitless exercise unless, and until, we take
action. W. Edwards Deming said, "It is not necessary to change. 
Survival is not mandatory." Robert C. Gallagher said, "Change is
inevitable, except from a vending machine." 

We must look within, but we cannot afford to walk away unchanged.
Remember, God loves us too much to leave us like we are.

Look at 1 Corinthians 11:28 in some other translations:

- The Amplified Version -- "Let a man thoroughly examine himself, and
 only when he has done so, should he eat of the bread and drink of the
 cup."

- The Message Version - 1 Corinthians 11:28 (TMSG) "Examine your
 motives, test your heart, come to this meal in holy awe." Let each man
 look into his own heart."

- The New Century Version - "^ ^Look into your own hearts before you
 eat the bread and drink the cup."

- The New Life Version -- "This is why a man should look into his own
 heart and life before eating the bread and drinking from the cup."

- The Passion Translation -- "So let each individual first evaluate
 his own attitude and only then eat the bread and drink the cup."

- The Voice Translation -- "Examine yourselves first. Then you can
 properly approach the table to eat the bread and drink from the
 cup."

Paul is telling these Corinthian believers (and us) to examine our life.
I think the acid test might be whether we could honestly say with the
Apostle Paul, "For to me, to live is Christ, and to die is gain."
(Philippians 1:21 NKJV)

Self-examination gives us the opportunity to make sure that all is well
and, if not, to correct it. Self-examination is intended to be much like
a person looking into a mirror, before going out in public. (Sometimes I
wonder if some folks own a mirror)

When we come to the Lords Table it is a time to pray for God to reveal
to us anything in our life that is not in line with His will, or
anything that would bring disgrace to Him.

Self- Examination affords us the opportunity to do three things:

1\) It affords us the opportunity to look at our past.

Let's reflect for a few moments on our lives. Our past is our past
because of Calvary!

We shouldn't allow the sins of the past, to ruin our present. Romans
8:1-2 (TMSG) "Those who enter into Christ's being-here-for-us no
longer have to live under a continuous, low- lying black cloud. 2 A new
power is in operation. The Spirit of life in Christ, like a strong wind,
has magnificently cleared the air, freeing you from a fated lifetime of
brutal tyranny at the hands of sin and death."

The Holy Spirit does not convict us of forgiven sin. That's the
oppression of the enemy. God doesn't remember forgiven sin!

As we look at our past, we must be sure that every part of our past has
been brought under the blood of Jesus. We must be sure there is nothing
hidden. We must allow Him to cleanse any lingering effects, or anything
we may have held back. Remember, He doesn't force His way in. We must
invite Him in

2\) It affords us the opportunity to focus on our lives in the present
tense.

No doubt there are those this morning who desperately need to discern
Christ's body in this wafer, and Christ's blood in this cup, to resolve
issues between us and the Lord, before an embarrassing rooster-crowing
begins. (Peter)

If we do not examine (Judge) ourselves we will be publicly judged. We
either embarrass our sin or our sin will embarrass us.

Paul reminds us that if we judge ourselves, others won't have to do it
for us (it's called mercy).

The present is a great time to bring those difficult spiritual and
emotional issues of our lives to Christ for cleansing by His blood.

3\) It affords us the opportunity to look at our future.

And what a future it is! Our future is one of Kingdom participation! We
have the opportunity to bring Heaven to earth in our circles of
influence!

The Lord did not save us just to get us to Heaven. If that was His
immediate intention, He would have killed us at the altar. He saved us
for a lifetime of Kingdom purpose. While at the Lords Table we must
examine whether we are aimed in the right direction for our future.

As we celebrate at the Lord's Table today, it would do as well to
remember that the bread and the cup link us with a family of brothers
and sisters in Christ that has been growing for some 2,000 years. We are
a part of something much bigger than we are individually. We are a part
of the Kingdom globally.

His ultimate intention for our lives is literally out of this world!
More of this family is in heaven than is on earth at this moment. They
are among that "great cloud of witnesses" where time is no more:

- There is no past...

- There is no present...

- There is no future...

There is only eternity! The race is run! The battle is over! They are
not in time; they are in eternity!

If they could speak to us this morning and give us a glimpse of the life
they now enjoy, we might reorder our priorities much more wisely.

Those who come to the Lords Table with a Kingdom mindset, fully
understand the words of the Apostle Paul when he said, "For me to
live is Christ, to die is gain."

CONCLUSION:

Each of us should examine:

- Our ambitions...

- Our motives...

- Our goals...

- Our dreams...

- Our desires...

We must examine ourselves as we approach the Lords Table.

According to Dictionary.com

- Attitude = Manner, disposition, feeling - Tendency or orientation,
 especially of the mind.

- Orientation = Position in relation to some benchmark.

In aeronautics the attitude of an aircraft is determined by its
relationship to the ground.

When flying, the pilot has in front of him in the cockpit an "AI" or
attitude indicator. The attitude indicator is an instrument used to
inform the pilot of the orientation of the aircraft relative to Earth\'s
horizon.

An aircraft can have three basic attitudes:

- When the centerline of the plane is parallel to the ground the plane
 is in a Level Attitude. A level attitude covers more ground over the
 long haul.

- When the nose of the aircraft is above the horizon, it is called a
 Nose High Attitude. An aircraft must have a nose up attitude in order
 to get off the ground. If the aircraft keeps a nose up attitude long
 enough, it will reach an altitude the aircraft cannot sustain.

- If the nose of the aircraft is below the horizon the aircraft is in a
 Nose Low Attitude. At times the aircraft must have a nose down
 attitude to land at a destination. (Humility -- "God blesses the
 humble and resists the proud") If the aircraft maintains a nose
 down attitude for too long, it will crash.

While in flight, the pilot must monitor not just the plane's altitude
but also the plane's attitude.

If we are going to have an attitude of thanksgiving we must never be
concerned with altitude only, but the attitude with which we are flying
at that altitude! When we come to the Lords Table we must do so with the
utmost of thankfulness.

Whether the nose is level, pointed up, or pointed down is going to make
a world of difference to the pilot and any passengers he/she may be
carrying.

It is accurate to say, that the pilot must maintain vigilance concerning
a plane's attitude, and its orientation to the horizon and ground, in
order to stay on course and avoid catastrophe.

Coming to the Lords Table and adjusting our thankfulness is essential to
keeping ourselves on course as we do Kingdom business.
