CHAINBREAKERS

SEXUALITY

We live in a society that is obsessed with sexual activity. This
obsession has led us to believe that sexual intercourse or associated
physical sexual activity is synonymous with intimacy.

Couples feel great pressure to live up to the sexual call our society
sends out. As a result, many feel frustrated or unsuccessful in marriage
if sex is not all it's cracked up to be on TV or in other media forms.
If we hold sexual activity at the center of our plan for intimacy in
marriage we will no doubt be very disappointed at some point in the
marriage. Sex between marriage partners should occupy a high
[priority]{.underline} but it cannot be the thing that everything else
is [judged]{.underline} by.

The sexual relationship between a husband and wife can be a wonderful
expression of the intimacy. In a well-adjusted marriage each partner is
at least as concerned about the other persons needs and growth as we are
about our own pleasure and satisfaction.

If our marriages are put together Biblically and properly, we will have
a very healthy physical relationship with our spouses. (Lady at Fouke
who brought a "Better than sex" cake to a pot- luck dinner)

There is nothing better than good sex and there is nothing worse than
bad sex. Sex is more than a physical act it is an
[emotional]{.underline} and [spiritual]{.underline} interaction between
two people God intentionally put together.

With regard to our sexual relationships with our spouses we should
remember the following things taken from 1 Corinthians 13:

- Love [waits]{.underline}... Lust can\'t wait.

<!-- -->

- Love [shares]{.underline}... Selfishness refuses to sacrifice.
 Selfishness is the root of all sin.

- Love [rejoices]{.underline}... Love rejoices at others success but
 insecurity can\'t recognize anyone but itself.

- Love is constantly [honoring]{.underline}... Selfishness is constantly
 craves attention.

- Love comes from a heart overflowing with [affection]{.underline} for
 God... Selfishness seeks its own best interest.

As married people we have a tremendous biblical responsibility toward
our spouses in the arena of sexuality. God\'s first commandment to man
was a sexual commandment, "Be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish
the earth." (Genesis 1:28) The Bible say's Adam "knew" his
wife.

God is the creator of sex but this culture has hijacked it, distorted it
and perverted it. God brought Eve to Adam. The perversion was not so in
the beginning. Genesis 2:25 "They were both naked, the man and his
wife, and were not ashamed."

Three reasons they were not ashamed:

- A holy and righteous God, who commanded them to make love, introduced
 them...

- Their minds were not preconditioned to guilt...

- No other people were around to observe their intimate relations...
 (Keep everybody out of your bedroom -- Internet, pornography etc...)

A rule that must be honored in all marriages is that only what is
[enjoyable]{.underline} to both partners should be permitted. At the
same time we must keep the needs of our spouse in mind and there are
times we must bench our selfish, prudish ideas. Hebrews 13:4
"Marriage is honorable in all, and the bed undefiled."

Your body is not your own, you gave it to your partner at the altar when
you said your marriage vows. 1 Corinthians 7:2-5 "But since there is
so much immorality, each man should have his own wife, and each woman
her own husband. 3 The husband should fulfill his marital duty to his
wife, and likewise the wife to her husband. 4 The wife\'s body does not
belong to her alone but also to her husband. In the same way, the
husband\'s body does not belong to him alone but also to his wife. 5 Do
not deprive each other except by mutual consent and for a time, so that
you may devote yourselves to prayer. Then come together again so that
Satan will not tempt you because of your lack of self-control." Four
central principles taught in 1 Corinthians 7:2-5 concerning sex:

- Both the husband and the wife have sexual needs and drives that should
 be fulfilled [inside]{.underline} their marriage... If this does not
 happen the person who is withholding themselves from their partner is
 sinning against God, the marriage covenant and their mate.

- When one marries they [forfeit]{.underline} control of their body to
 their partner... Both parties of a marriage relationship should seek
 opportunities to satisfy their mate. A healthy, exciting sexual
 relationship takes work and the individual that fails to work in this
 area is inviting disaster to wreck the marriage. To not do so is a sin
 against God, the marriage covenant and their mate.

- Both partners are [forbidden]{.underline} to refuse to meet the sexual
 needs of their mate... This command is more than being available, this
 is a command to do everything physically, emotionally and sexually
 possible to keep the attention and affection of our spouse focused on
 us. (Alienation of affection) If this does not occur one should not be
 too shocked when one discovers their spouse has a wandering eye or has
 been unfaithful. If sex is not enjoyable (Lack of a sex drive
 --Uncomfortable - Painful) to one of the marriage partners it is up to
 that person to seek every remedy possible. (Doctor -- Marriage
 counselor etc...) Otherwise they leave themselves, their partner and
 the marriage itself at risk of temptation. To not do so is a sin
 against God, the marriage covenant and their mate.

- The act of sex within a marriage is approved by God and must be
 treated as such by all who commit to the marriage
 [covenant]{.underline}... The Bible talks about "Due
 benevolence" inferring that people who enter into Holy marriage
 automatically assume a debt of lovemaking to their spouse. (Gallon jar
 full of beans) We should guard against being a deadbeat husband or
 wife in this area. To not do so is to sin against God, the marriage
 covenant and our mate.

WHAT LOVEMAKING MEANS TO A MAN

1\) It [satisfies]{.underline} his sex drive... (Now lets look at what
lovemaking means to a woman! Ha!) It is generally agreed that the male
in most species of living creatures has the stronger sex drive. So it's
not a problem that your husband has a corner on!

Women have sex drive, but theirs is normally sporadic, whereas, a man's
sex drive is almost continual.

2\) It fulfills his [manhood]{.underline}... A man usually possesses a
stronger ego than a woman and it is a scientifically proven fact that
his sex drive is linked to his ego. This is not abnormal. It is
completely normal.

A husband has a need to know that his wife thoroughly enjoys his
lovemaking. The old Victorian nonsense that a "nice lady" doesn\'t
act as if she enjoys sex conflicts with this.

A secure man becomes a better father, uses better judgment, and improves
his capacity to love the entire family when his wife is meeting his
sexual needs.

3\) It [enhances]{.underline} his ability to show his love for his
wife... Because God has endowed a man with an intense sex drive and a
conscience, the satisfactory release of that drive without provoking his
conscience will enhance his love for the person who makes that possible.

But only one person on earth should do that - his wife. If his wife is
not fully engaged in meeting this need, she is sinning against God, the
marriage covenant and her husband. If the husband is not being the kind
of man that causes his wife to want to meet his needs he is sinning
against God, the marriage covenant and his wife.

4\) It reduces [friction]{.underline} in the home... I have counseled
with women who refuse to make love to their husbands for long periods of
time because they are mad that he treats them better after sex. Let me
ask you a question. If when you performed certain responsibilities in
your place of employment you were treated better by the boss, and
actually received promotions and raises because you went above and
beyond the call of duty, would you not be happy to do a little extra?
What about your husband?

5\) It provides life\'s most [exciting]{.underline} experience...
(Susan asked me one day, "Why do you suppose God made sex?" My
response -- "Because he knew I would not have wanted to live if he
hadn't!") Sex is at the top of the list of things a man would rather
do.

Men let me share the words of Jesus with you about your sex drive and
thought life. Matthew 5:28, "I say unto you that whosoever, looketh
on a woman to lust after her hath committed adultery with her already in
his heart."

Vain imaginations can be very destructive to your marital relationship.

WHAT LOVEMAKING MEANS TO A WOMAN

1\) It [fulfills]{.underline} her womanhood... If a woman considers
herself unsuccessful in bed, she will have a difficult time accepting
her total womanhood.

A wife is more than a mother and a homemaker. She is also a sexual
partner to her mate.

2\) It [reassures]{.underline} her of her husband\'s love... There are
five kinds of love required by a woman.

- [Companionship]{.underline} love. Few women enjoy solitude for long
 periods of time. Have you noticed how few hermits and recluses are
 women?

- [Compassionate]{.underline} love. A woman has a natural bedside
 manner, but only a few men display that kind of compassion.

- [Romantic]{.underline} love. Women are romantics! She is looking for
 her knight in shining armor. For that reason she needs romance,
 flowers, music, soft lights, dinner out, and a host of other things.
 Romance is more than just making sure the door to the bedroom is
 locked! Some men think foreplay is telling their wife to brace
 herself! Ladies, I want you to make a top-ten-list of things you
 consider to be romantic acts then give it to your husband! (Do it
 right now -- Give some time for this) He may need a little
 jump-starting! Just be careful not to kick-start him! (Story about
 some beagles trying to jump-start a rabbit on a cold day)

> Gentlemen, make sure the romantic adventure you have planned is for
> her best interest and not your own.

- [Affectionate]{.underline} love. Most women crave kisses of
 appreciation, which have nothing to do with sex. To women a tender
 touch, a warm embrace, and the closeness of the one they love is
 almost as enjoyable as the more intimate sexual contact. Song of
 Solomon 1:2 "Let him kiss me with the kisses of his mouth: for thy
 love is better than wine, because of the savour of thy good ointments
 thy name is as ointment poured forth..."

- [Passionate]{.underline} love. Impassioned love comes naturally to a
 man because of his stronger sex drive. Most women have to cultivate
 the appetite for passionate loving, but they do have the capacity to
 learn. It is easier for a woman to express passionate love after the
 other four needs for love have been satisfied.

3\) It [satisfies]{.underline} her sex drive... Although a woman may not
possess as strong or consistent a sex drive as a man, she does have a
sex drive. If she doesn't there is either a physical, emotional or
relational problem.

A woman does not seem so readily tempted to fantasize, as does her
husband. However, she does have the capacity to remember romantically
those exciting experiences of her past.

4\) It [relaxes]{.underline} her nervous system... Frigid women are
nervous women. Note: not every nervous woman is frigid.

5\) The [ultimate]{.underline} experience... Sexual involvement and
activity, like every other part of the relationship, will change and
grow with time - whether one pays attention or not - this could be
productive or counterproductive.

One should ask their partner questions to find out if the sexual
experience is pleasurable or not? The best intimate sexual expression is
a well-informed sexual expression. Hebrews 13:4 "Marriage is
honorable, and the bed undefiled...."

Intimate sex recognizes a wealth of opportunities for intimate physical
expression outside of the context of intercourse. Recreation (Doing a
hobby together), a caring back-rub, a foot-rub, a quiet cup of coffee
together, are all opportunities for intimate expression.

Keep, others out of your bedroom! Example - Alcohol, Drugs, Pornography,
Magazines, Books, Violence, Fantasies, etc. The more purified our
character, the more responsive our spouse.
