## I. CALEB HAD A SPIRIT OF COMPOSURE...

Caleb was an ordinary man with an extraordinary purpose. As far as men
go, Caleb would probably not have stood out in the crowd. His name could
have been Smith, Jones, or Harris; he was an ordinary sort of a guy.
What set Caleb apart were his purpose and the stick-to-it-ness. The
Bible declared that "He had another spirit from the Lord..."

In order for us who are in this room today to take a step from the arena
of the ordinary to the world of the extraordinary, it will take the same
things which Caleb had to assimilate into his life.

Caleb was not your average run-of-the-mill, pink-fingered,
tip-toe-through-the-tulips, kind of sissy. He was the guy who was ready
to take on a challenge at a moments notice. No matter what the crisis,
even in the midst of an evil report, Caleb said, "let's go-get- em'!"

Caleb was expressing faith when the tide of public opinion was
diametrically opposed to his point of view. A poll was taken by Moses
and 83.33% of the people were saying just the opposite of what Caleb had
in mind. He was outnumbered 5 to 1! Caleb did not allow the tide of
public opinion to determine his faith, he knew God was able to deliver
and he was ready to do his part in the battle.

Caleb was surrounded by negativity. When public opinion becomes negative
it is easy to just side with the majority and fail to have our voice
heard. This is not the case with Caleb, when others are stating the
reasons why it would be impossible for Israel to prevail; he made
unwavering declarations of faith in Israel and Israel's God!

There's a time to pray, and a time to act! It is easy to get into the
stall mode using the excuse; we are praying about it. What would we say

the answer to? Rhetorical questions can run a little thin after a while.

The majority of the leadership of Israel wanted something-for-nothing!
