# GRACE

BROWN DENOTES INSTRUCTIONS TO TECHNICIAN

BLUE DENOTES POWER POINT SLIDE

TEXT: Titus 3:3-7 (NKJV)

INTRO: The word grace is mentioned one hundred seventy times in the
King James Version of the Bible. This is a very, very, important
subject!

When it comes to the grace of God, we can: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT
A TIME)

- Sing about it and never understand it...

- Discuss it and never exhaust it...

- Debate it and never fully comprehend it...

- Receive it and never fully grasp it...

- Display it and never really have a handle on it...

- Ignore it and never outrun it...

The greatest gift available to mankind is the grace of God!

Throughout the Old Testament grace was rarely seen, Law was in effect!
This is because man chose for it to be that way. Through a stubborn
refusal to do things Gods way, man chose by an act of His own will to
live by Law and not by grace!

Jesus came to this earth to bring about a new dispensation which would
become known as grace! Jesus lived thirty three and a half years but
until He left earth bound for Glory, grace was just a rumor!

Until Christ finished His work on the cross grace was only something
which was a figment of man's wildest imagination! Though Jesus is gone,
grace remains! (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

Jesus ascended to the Father to become the administrator of grace!
Hebrews 7:19-28 (KJV) "For the law made nothing perfect, but the
bringing in of a better hope did; by the which we draw nigh unto God.
And inasmuch as not without an oath he was made priest: (For
those priests were made without an oath; but this with an oath by him
that said unto him, The Lord sware and will not repent, Thou art a
priest for ever after the order of Melchisedec:) By so much was
Jesus made a surety of a better testament. And they truly were many
priests, because they were not suffered to continue by reason of death:
But this man, because he continueth ever, hath an unchangeable
priesthood. Wherefore he is able also to save them to the uttermost
that come unto God by him, seeing [he ever liveth to make intercession
for them]{.underline}. For such an high priest became us, who is
holy, harmless, undefiled, separate from sinners, and made higher than
the heavens; Who needeth not daily, as those high priests, to offer
up sacrifice, first for his own sins, and then for the people's: for
this he did once, when he offered up himself. For the law maketh men
high priests which have infirmity; but the word of the oath, which was
since the law, maketh the Son, who is consecrated for evermore."
Jesus is seated at the right hand of God, doing one thing,
administrating grace in your life and in mine! W-O-W!

Think of grace in this way: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Grace covers me when I deserve it the least...

- Grace releases me from my fiercest bondages...

- Grace opens up doors for me which was permanently shut...

- Grace provides a way of escaping judgment...

- Grace intervenes when sin is present...

- Grace delivers when the enemy persists...

- Grace proves my faith and salvation...

- Grace allows me the chance at heaven...

Mercy is not getting what I do deserve; but grace is getting what I do
not deserve!

In our culture we are conditioned to believe "You get what you pay
for." The thing about grace is: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- We cannot earn it...

- We cannot purchase it...

- We cannot be good enough to deserve it...

- We cannot work hard enough to be worthy of it...

- We cannot be acquainted with enough people to negotiate it...

Grace is a gift which Jesus left behind for us, and He is now
administrating it in our lives!

How do we receive this grace? Ephesians 2:8 (NKJV) "For by grace you
have been saved through faith, and that not of yourselves; [it is the
gift of God]{.underline}, not of works, lest anyone should boast."

We receive grace by accepting it as a gift!

The Scripture tells us it is possible to frustrate the grace of God!
Galatians 2:20-21 (KJV) "I am crucified with Christ: nevertheless I
live; yet not I, but Christ liveth in me: and the life which I now live
in the flesh I live by the faith of the Son of God, who loved me, and
gave himself for me. I do not [frustrate the grace of
God]{.underline}: for if righteousness come by the law, then Christ is
dead in vain." If grace is a gift from God, how could one frustrate
this gift? The grace of God is frustrated if we: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN
ONE AT A TIME)

- Add to it...

- Restrict it...

- Take away from it...

- Delineate from its power...

- Relegate it to an inferior position...

Think of this; Jesus left this grace for us! It belongs to us! It is
here for us!

The only question which remains is whether we will accept it or reject
it?
