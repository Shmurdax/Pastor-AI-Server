TEXT: Acts 1:9-10 (KJV) "And when he had spoken these things,
while they beheld, he was taken up; and a cloud received him out of
their sight. And while they looked stedfastly toward heaven as he went
up, behold, two men stood by them in white apparel; Which also said, Ye
men of Galilee, why stand ye gazing up into heaven? This same [Jesus,
which is taken up from you]{.underline} into heaven, shall so come in
like manner as ye have seen him go into heaven."

INTRO: If I could have only been there to have watched a crucified
but resurrected Jesus leave earth from Bethany's launching pad! What a
pleasure it would have been to stand on the mountain with the other five
hundred believers! To have rubbed shoulders with the apostles, and to
have watched as Jesus was received up into Glory, as the angels replaced
Him as messengers of encouragement! What a sight it must have been!

I'm sure to those standing there it was a very ambivalent sight; they
were no doubt thrilled to be part of such an historic and heavenly
moment; yet, they were saddened by His exit!

He did not choose to keep His ascension a secret, in fact, for three and
one half years His death, burial, resurrection and ascension had been a
theme of His ministry! After all Jesus had told the disciples of His
departure again and again. I am not sure they ever expected this day to
come as quickly as it did, and I am certainly not convinced they thought
it would happen in this way!

Though the crowd of people who were gathered at Bethany's launching pad
was probably astounded at the power and anointing of the moment, it was
not long until reality set in; He is gone; Jesus is gone; He is really
gone!

They no doubt thought that when He told them He would have to go away,
it was just a metaphor or parable. I don't believe they thought it would
literally happen; surely Jesus was speaking in jest for He would almost
certainly not leave this infant church alone! Would He? They were not
ready for the responsibility and probably felt ill equipped.

Just a few days before this epic event, the apparent leaders were
fighting among themselves to decide who would have the loftiest
positions in His Kingdom. When they were called upon to take a public
stand displaying stability, they went into denial and hiding, fearing
for their very lives. Surely this crowd gathered at Bethany was not the
type of leadership Jesus would leave in charge of His infant Church!

Notice the backdrop of this story: (LLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A
TIME)

- Chaos was still the norm in Jerusalem...

- Most of the public did not believe Jesus had risen from the dead...

- The political and religious leaders were determined to quell what they
 felt were rumors of a risen Jesus...

- The explanation of an empty tomb by political and religious leaders
 was that the disciples of Jesus had stolen His body from the tomb...

Most of the people present at Bethany's launching pad that day felt they
could very well be arrested at any time. They still looked behind their
backs and watched the Roman soldiers as they roamed the streets. The
past few days were horrifying to them from the standpoint of Roman law
and Jewish customs.

Every day the disciples left home, they did not know if they would make
it back safely that night. Their anxieties and worries were complicated
by the fact that they had been a part of the ministry of Jesus for the
better part of four years. They were easily identifiable, the public
knew who they were and where they lived and you can bet the Roman
government documented their every move.

Just when it seemed that things could not be in a worse state, He was
gone, Jesus was gone!

Following the events at Bethany, the next morning they went to the
mountain to see if He would return. Wouldn't you know it, nothing but
blue sky and white fluffy clouds were gathered in the sky! The angel
said He would be coming back just like He went away! Where was He? How
were they going to make it? When could they expect His return?

He had talked about giving gifts and leaving promises, but they had not
experienced anything like this, so their minds were fuzzy and puzzled
about the future. The reality of the moment began to set in on them,
He's gone!

But what was that He told them to do just before He left? Go back to
Jerusalem and pray for the promise of the Father? What was that all
about? Just what was going on with this bedraggled, disheveled, unnerved
group of disciples who had been with Jesus for the past three and a half
years?

Just like the disciples, our Lord is no longer physically among us, He's
gone and has been gone from this earth for more than 2,000 years, but
just look what He left behind! (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- An unfinished task...

- An unchallengeable message...

- An unquestionable love...

- An unmerited grace...

- An unstoppable force...

- An unfailing promise...

## I. AN UNFINISHED TASK...

Jesus had told the disciples, "The Son of man is come to seek and to
save that which was lost." This is the bedrock of hope for everyone
here today! We were "That which was lost." Over a two thousand
year period untold millions have come to know Christ as their personal
Savior but as long as there is one person not saved, the task is not
complete.

The song writer was right when he penned the words, "Though millions
have come, there's still room for one; there's room at the cross for
you."

As long as there are people who have not heard the message of the gospel
of Christ, the task is not complete; the work of the church is not done;
He left behind an unfinished job for the church.

Jesus left behind parting instructions for a church with an incomplete
task when He said, "Go ye therefore, and teach all nations, baptizing
them in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost,
teaching them to observe all things whatsoever I have commanded you; and
lo, I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world. Amen."

The task is incomplete, He wants the job finished and it is not!
(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The mission fields cry out "unfinished"...

- The cities and towns cry out "unfinished"...

- The downtrodden cry out "unfinished"...

- The up and coming cry out "unfinished"...

- The ghettos cry out "unfinished"

- The lonely cry out "unfinished"...

- The sin-sick cry out "unfinished"...

- Our neighbors cry out "unfinished"...

- Our unsaved family members cry out "unfinished"...

- Our world cries out "unfinished"...

There is still a call that comes ringing over the restless waves, "Send
the light! Send the light! There are souls to rescue, there are souls to
save, send the light!"

Jesus left a task unfinished which He instructed the church to fulfill,
and it is still there for you and I to finish. And do it we must if we
are going to fulfill His request.

Think of this, if twelve people would commit to winning one person per
year and making that convert into a disciple who brings a person a year
to Christ, over a twenty year period, 12,582,900 people would be won to
the Lord! In the world of the disciples, if the command of Jesus had
been carried out by twelve men over a forty year period the population
of the planet would have been converted.

Isak Dinesen said, "When you have a great and difficult task, something
perhaps almost impossible, if you only work a little at a time, every
day a little, suddenly the work will finish itself." (LEAVE ON SCREEN
UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

The greatest gift available to mankind is the grace of God!

Throughout the Old Testament grace was rarely seen, Law was in effect!
This is because man chose for it to be that way. Through a stubborn
refusal to do things Gods way, man chose by an act of His own will to
live by Law and not by grace!

Jesus came to this earth to bring about a new dispensation which would
become known as grace! Jesus lived thirty three and a half years but
until He left earth bound for Glory, grace was just a rumor! Until
Christ finished His work on the cross grace was only something which was
a figment of man's wildest imagination! Though Jesus is gone, grace
remains! (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

ILLUSTRATION:

"The Wonderful Window."

A London clerk worked in a run-down, drab, depressing, part of the
city. This clerk refused to allow his outlook to determine his happiness
so one day he bought a picture with an inspiring scene and installed it
in the window of his office that overlooked the worst part of the city.
The picture was of beautiful castles and towers, green rolling hills,
beautiful parks and lovely homes. In the picture a Knight rode a
stallion through the streets and watched over the people with pride.

As the clerk worked long hours at the tedious task of making everything
balance he felt as though he was working for the Knight in the picture.
This produced a sense of honor, dignity, and hope. As he looked at the
picture of what could be rather than looking at what is, the picture
changed his life!"

We need not allow our circumstances or surroundings to discourage us!
God has placed us where we are but He assured us He would "Come
again!"
