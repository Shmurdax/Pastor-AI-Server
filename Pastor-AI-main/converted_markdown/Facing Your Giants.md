the same page when we speak of giants in our lives. Giants in our lives
are things which seem almost impossible to beat or conquer.

lives, and these giants will either give us a tremendous opportunity to
step up to the plate and allow God to be God or they will keep us from

they will keep us defeated in our walk. The upside of giants is that

## I. GOLIATH...

The name Goliath means "To reveal or advertise in a disgraceful
sense; reviler; verbal abuser; or gossiper." Think about this, what
weapon did Goliath use against the men of Israel? Words! The tongue! For
forty days he dared them to "send a man that we may fight
together." (This is the old maids prayer!!!)

We could say that Goliath is the giant of the untamed tongue. This is
probably a giant which all of us face from time to time in varying
degrees. It is certainly one of the biggest giants that we will face in
our lifetimes.

The tongue is a powerful weapon and can be used for good or evil.

## II. ISHBI-BENOB... (Ish-be-be-nob)

2 Samuel 21:15-17 (NKJV) "When the Philistines were at war again with

Please note that just because you have dealt with one giant, does not
mean they are all dead! In a perfect world, when you have killed one
giant the rest of them leave you alone but this is not a perfect world
and the giants just keep coming.

In this scenario, David killed Goliath but once he laid that victory to
rest, Ishbi-Benob (Ish-be-be-nob) had to be dealt with. The name
Ishbi-Benob (Ish-be-be-nob) means "My dwelling is on the heights"
or "I belong above." Without doing any injustice to Scripture, I
believe we could say Ishbi-Benob (Ish-be-be-nob) represents pride. As
soon as Ishbi-Benob's (Ish-be-be-nob) name is mentioned, the Scripture
gives this description of him, "...The weight of whose bronze spear
was three hundred shekels, (11 ¾ Pounds) and he was bearing a new
sword." Everything about this guy reeks of pride, from being able to
throw a oversized spear to the need for carrying a new sword. The
epitome of his pride is proven by this statement, "He thought he
could kill David."

lifetime: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

 whether or not they are good for our public persona...

- Pride causes us to run into debt in an attempt to prove we are just as
 financially successful as the next person...

- Pride say's "I had this outfit imported; it is of the finest quality
 available anywhere in the world..."

- Pride will cause us to buy a Rolex knockoff in an attempt to make
 people think we are wearing the real thing... The only person who gets
 deceived in this scenario is the person wearing the watch! (We can see
 the green on your wrist!!!)

Pride is a giant which uses the element of surprise as a masquerade to
gain entrance into our lives. Oftentimes this giant has set up a

accountability partner, we deal with the giant that is creating a place

Pride shows up with an oversized spear and a new sword. It is impressive
and glitters brighter than a diamond. It often veils itself within a
spirit of excellence. A spirit of Excellence is a good thing but if we

unreasonable and is couched in a spirit of pride.

Christian walk. New Christians are not as susceptible to the spirit of
pride as seasoned believers are. Pride often follows a great victory or
it can surface after a string of victories. When pride is at work we
begin to feel superior or invincible.

The battle against pride becomes greater as we get more victories
notched on our spiritual belt. It looms greater as we become more
skilled at firing our rapid firing gospel gun!
