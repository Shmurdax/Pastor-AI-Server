## I. DEFENITION OF THE GIFT...

Everyone I know either needs a miracle or is connected to someone who
does. The first thing I would like to point out is the terminology of
scripture with regard to this wonderful gift. I believe every phrase in
scripture is thoughtfully and purposefully framed, for instance, what we
normally refer to as "The Gift of Miracles", is in fact, "The Gift of
Working of Miracles."

The definition of this gift is as follows: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT
A TIME)

- Working - Energema - (en-erg\'-ay-mah) Active, efficient operation

- Miracles - Dunamis - (doo\'-nam-is) Miraculous power or ability

Based on this information we could define this gift as "The active,
efficient operation of miraculous powers or abilities."

In his book "The Gifts of the Spirit" - Harold Horton defines miracles
in the following ways: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- "A miracle is a supernatural act on the natural plane."

- "Works contrary to nature, accomplishments, so to speak, of the
 impossible, sudden and unaccountable reversals of the order to which
 we are accustomed."

<!-- -->

- "A miracle is a supernatural intervention in the ordinary course of
 nature; a temporary suspension of the accustomed order; an
 interruption of the system of nature as we know it."

- "A miracle is a sovereign act of the Spirit of God irrespective of
 laws or systems."

Before we move on to other things let me add that the gift of the
working of miracles has to do with miracles of a non-physical nature
rather than a physical nature. When we talk about a person having
experienced a miracle of healing it could more correctly be identified
as a gift of healing.

In scripture the gift of the working of miracles would be instances such
as;

- God speaking the world into existence

- God creating man

- Abram\'s ram caught in a thicket

- The plagues of Egypt

- The Red Sea being parted

- The brazen serpent in the wilderness

- The provision of food and water in the wilderness

- The 40 year life span of clothing and sandals in the time of the
 wilderness wandering

- Gideon\'s fleece being wet or dry

- Elijah\'s fire on Carmel

- The widow\'s oil being multiplied

- Jesus turning water into wine

- Tax money being found in the mouth of a fish

- The stormy sea being stilled with three words

- The apostles being freed from prison

We all know God to be a God of miracles. To receive the benefits of this
gift, we must move beyond the belief that He is able; to believing He
wants us to receive a miracle. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT
SLIDE) (Have audience to point finger at themselves and say "God
wants me to have a miracle!")

No doubt about it, God desires to furnish miracles in our midst and
there is certainly an overabundance of need for miracles, so the
question becomes, "How do I get myself into a position to receive a
miracle?"

## II. DYNAMICS OF THE GIFT...

Miracles go beyond the natural to the supernatural. (LEAVE ON THE
SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) Having said that I must also say that God
is not the only one who can operate in the supernatural:

- Pharaoh\'s magicians were able to cause their rods to become snakes

- Pharaoh\'s magicians were able to turn the water to blood

- Pharaoh\'s magicians were able to bring frogs upon the land

- Pharaoh\'s magicians were able to turn dust into lice

Pharaoh\'s magicians had the power to punish the land but they did not
have the power to bless it by ridding it of pestilence. Simply put, the
devil and his cronies can operate in the supernatural but they cannot
complete a work that results in good things happening to God\'s people.
In addition he cannot curse what God has blessed as long as the person
who has been blessed stays within the protection God has provided.

The working of miracles is a result of God setting aside His laws of
nature allowing the higher Spiritual laws to take control. In the
working of miracles, natural laws are not abandoned, but merely set
aside for the time being as the greater laws of God come into operation
to supersede them: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The law of conflagration was set aside in the miracle of the burning
 bush.

- The law of death was set aside in the miracle of the translation of
 Enoch and Elijah.

- The law of process was set aside in the miracle of water being turned
 to wine.

- The law of gravity was set aside in the miracle of the ascension.

These natural laws being circumvented are not only referred to in the
Bible as miracles but also as powers, signs, and wonders. Harold Horton
in his book "The Gifts of the Spirit" gives the following definitions:

- Powers - Explosions of almightiness

- Signs - The visible tokens of invisible power

- Wonders - Impelling staggering astonishment

In God\'s world performing a miracle, no matter how difficult it may
seem, is just His natural and normal way of doing things. To understand
this truth fully one must understand one fundamental, foundational
truth: He is not a God who performs miracles He is a God of miracles. It
was more natural for Jesus to walk on water than not to walk on it!

By His very nature God is more apt to perform a miracle than not to
perform it. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE)

For God, miracles are the natural way of doing things. It does not take
any more ability and power for God to do the miraculous, than for us to
perform the daily duties of our professions.

## III. DEMONSTRATION OF THE GIFT...

From Genesis to Revelation the Bible is full of miracles: (PLACE ON
THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The very act of creation was miraculous

- The final disposition of the earth will be miraculous

- The creation of man was miraculous

- The final fate of man will be miraculous

There is no question that miracles are for today, and the need for
miracles is unprecedented. If miracles are so desperately needed the
importance of this gift becomes crucial to the contemporary church.

Notice the terminology of Scripture with regard to this gift: 1
Corinthians 12:10 (NKJV) "to another the [working of
miracles]{.underline}, to another prophecy, to another discerning of
spirits, to another different kinds of tongues, to another the
interpretation of tongues." 1 Corinthians 12:28-29 (NKJV) "And
God has appointed these in the church: first apostles, second prophets,
third teachers, after that [miracles]{.underline}, then gifts of
healings, helps, administrations, varieties of tongues.29 Are all
apostles? Are all prophets? Are all teachers? Are all [workers of
miracles]{.underline}?"
