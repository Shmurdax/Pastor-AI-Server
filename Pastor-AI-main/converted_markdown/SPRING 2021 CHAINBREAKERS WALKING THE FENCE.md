# SPRING 2021

CHAINBREAKERS WALKING THE FENCE

Recently an elderly minister friend related a story to me out of his
past ministry experience and I would like to share it with you along
with what I believe to be a very practical application for life and
ministry in the 21^st^ Century.

Many years ago my friend spent several years in Scotland doing missions
work, during this time he became personally acquainted with many people
who made their living as shepherds. One day he was helping a shepherd
friend tend to the needs of his flock and the shepherd realized one of
his ewes who was expected to calve any day, was missing. In an effort to
find the missing ewe the shepherd asked the minister to "Walk the Fence"
because the shepherd had not done so in a couple days.

"Walking the Fence" is a normal part of [shepherding]{.underline}, this
is something which is done, if not every day, at least several times a
week.

When a shepherd "Walks the Fence" he does so with several things in
mind:

- He is attempting to discover whether the fence has been
 [breached]{.underline}...

- He is looking to see if anything has [entered]{.underline} or
 [exited]{.underline} the property which would not be in the best
 interest of the sheep...

- He is looking for [hazards]{.underline} which might be detrimental to
 the sheep... (i.e. A fence which is down; a loose fence post; a
 sagging wire; a wire which has been left loose and might get caught in
 the wool of a lamb; etc)

- He is making sure everything inside the fence; and in close proximity
 to the fence in no way poses a [danger]{.underline} to his
 [flock]{.underline}...

"Walking the Fence" might seem at times to be an insignificant part of
shepherding but in reality, it can mean the difference between
[success]{.underline} and [failure]{.underline} for the shepherd, and it
could very well be the difference between [life]{.underline} and
[death]{.underline} for the sheep.

On this particular day, my friend began his journey around the fence and
before long he found the ewe and her new born lamb, both dead, inside
the fence! The ewe had ventured too closely to the barbed wire fence,
which had been repaired a few weeks earlier, the person who had made the
repairs had failed to cut off a small piece of excess wire just a few
inches in length, and the ewe became entrapped when the barbed wire
buried itself in her coat of wool! To no avail, the ewe had obviously
attempted to free herself from the barbed wire. Because of the physical
exhaustion of attempting to free her-self, had no strength left, and
died during the calving process! Not only did she perish but her little
lamb died as well!

As Pastors and Ministers we must be constantly walking the fence,
checking on the ones with which God has [entrusted]{.underline} us! We
cannot afford for the sheep or lambs we are watching over for the Good
shepherd, to perish on our watch!

Shepherding is serious business! As under-shepherds we will eventually
be called upon to give an [account]{.underline} for everyone God has
placed in our care! What does "Walking the Fence" look like for us?

- We "Walk the Fence" by routinely [praying]{.underline} for the people
 who have been entrusted to us... Many times God will reveal certain
 things about certain people as we intercede for them!

- We "Walk the Fence" by [interacting]{.underline} with the people... It
 is very difficult to shepherd people we never interact with. As we
 interact with them we often see warning signs of things which should
 not be in their lives!

<!-- -->

- We "Walk the Fence" by paying attention to who they are [hanging
 out]{.underline} with...

- We "Walk the Fence" by noticing their habit of [church
 attendance]{.underline}... A pattern of declining church attendance is
 always a warning sign that something is just not right!

- We "Walk the Fence" by noticing changes in [work ethic]{.underline}...
 When work habits change it is a flashing neon sign that something is
 going sideways.

As under-shepherds we have been given the wonderful privilege of
oversight of the flock of God! We need to get our hiking boots on and
not fail to "Walk the Fence!"
