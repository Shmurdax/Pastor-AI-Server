# GOOD ATTITUDES = GREAT OUTCOMES

TEXT: Numbers 14:24 (NKJV) "But My servant Caleb, because [he has
a different spirit in him]{.underline} and has followed Me fully, I will
bring into the land where he went, and his descendants shall inherit
it."

INTRO: In our text, we are introduced to one of the Bible's most
prolific and successful characters, Caleb. One of the greatest
compliments in the Word of God is paid to this little known Old
Testament character. Numbers 14:24 (NKJV) "But My servant Caleb,
because [he has a different spirit in him]{.underline} and has followed
Me fully, I will bring into the land where he went, and his descendants
shall inherit it."

Caleb had a good spirit about him. Caleb exhibited a great attitude in a
difficult situation. He had a spirit of:

- Composure...

- Conquest...

- Confidence...

- Conviction...

## I. CALEB EXHIBITED AS ATTITUDE OF COMPOSURE...

Caleb was an ordinary man who CHOSE to live his life in the lane of an
extraordinary purpose.

As far as men go, Caleb would probably not have stood out in the crowd.
His name could have been Smith, Jones, or Harris; he was an ordinary
sort of a guy. What set Caleb apart were his attitude when confronted
with difficulty. The Bible declared that "He had another spirit from
the Lord..."

In order for us who are in this room today to take a step from the arena
of the ordinary to the world of the extraordinary, it will take the same
things that Caleb had to assimilate into his life.

Caleb was ready to take on a challenge at a moments notice and do it
with an attitude of composure. No matter what the crisis, even in the
midst of an evil report, Caleb's attitude was, "This to will turn to
good." Good attitudes = Great outcomes!

Caleb was expressing faith when the tide of public opinion was
diametrically opposed to his point of view. A poll was taken by Moses,
and 83.33% of the people were saying just the opposite of what Caleb had
in mind. He was outnumbered 5 to 1! Caleb did not allow the tide of
public opinion to determine his faith, he knew God was able to deliver
and he was ready to do his part in the battle. Bad attitudes = Bad
outcomes!

Caleb was surrounded by negativity. When things in our whirlwind become
negative it is easy to just be sucked into the vacuum created by the
whirlwind. This is not the case with Caleb, when others are stating the
reasons why it would be impossible for Israel to prevail; he made
unwavering declarations of faith in Israel and Israel's God! Good
attitudes = Great outcomes!

Notice Numbers 13:26-30 (NKJV) "Now they departed and came back to
Moses and Aaron and all the congregation of the children of Israel in
the Wilderness of Paran, at Kadesh; they brought back word to them and
to all the congregation, and showed them the fruit of the land. 27 Then
they told him, and said: "We went to the land where you sent us. It
truly flows with milk and honey, and this is its fruit. 28 Nevertheless

Amalekites dwell in the land of the South; the Hittites, the Jebusites,
and the Amorites dwell in the mountains; and the Canaanites dwell by the
sea and along the banks of the Jordan." Bad attitudes = Bad
outcomes! "30 Then [Caleb quieted the people before Moses, and said,
"Let us go up at once and take possession, for we are well able to
overcome it]{.underline}." Caleb said SHHHH!!!!!!

If we hope to demonstrate a spirit of composure in a faithless situation
we must get our eyes off of the circumstances and get them on the final
outcome!

If we keep our eyes on the finish line and the Lord we can remain calm
cool and collected no matter how difficult the situation becomes. We are
not under the circumstances, but we've got a God who is God in spite of
the circumstances! Good attitudes = Great outcomes!

David said in Psalms 61:1-3 (NKJV) "To the Chief Musician. On a
stringed instrument. A Psalm of David. Hear my cry, O God; Attend to my
prayer. 2 From the end of the earth I will cry to You, When my heart is

In Caleb's case there were 10 grasshoppers and two giant killers! The
difference between grasshoppers and giant killers is the ability to have
a good attitude in difficult times. This can only be achieved through a
spirit of composure!

Good attitudes = Great outcomes!

## II. CALEB EXHIBITED AN ATTITUDE OF CONQUEST...

Notice the words of Caleb, Numbers 13:30 (NKJV) "Then Caleb quieted
the people before Moses, and said, "[Let us go up at once and take
possession, for we are well able to overcome it]{.underline}."

There's a time to pray, and a time to act! It is easy to get into the
stall mode using the excuse; we are praying about it. (There are some
things you don't have to pray about!)

A failure to properly discern whether it is time to pray or time to act
can be costly. In this case I believe Israel could have achieved victory
if they had just taken the advice of Caleb, closed the discussion and
began the process of dividing and conquering the land!

The majority of the leadership of Israel wanted something-for-nothing!
They had lived as slaves for more than four hundred years; because of
this; they did not know how to achieve one thing that was not being
handed to them. Bad attitudes = Bad outcomes!

If we are not constantly on our guard we can very easily fall into the
trap of living in the same kind of slave mentality that kept Israel from
the Promised Land. If a thing is being freely offered without any
challenge, we are willing to accept it. However, if effort is required
on our part, we seem to lose our courage. Bad attitudes = Bad outcomes!

The things worth having in life require a price to be paid!

- Career...

- Marriage...

- Parenthood...

- Spiritual walk...

If we are not willing to pay the price, we will probably never be in
possession of the things we most desire. If we in fact achieve them
there will very likely be a price to pay!

Those who have a spirit of conquest are willing to pay the price and
persevere when the going gets tough! They are willing to move out in
obedience to God through faith! Good attitudes = Great outcomes!

Everything about this man Caleb portrays the fact he is a fiery soldier,
summoned to a conquest, and guided to a glorious, victorious destiny!
Even though he had a spirit of conquest and believed he could conquer
his inheritance did not mean the going would be easy! He still had forty
plus years of faithful endurance and staying in shape for battle, ahead
of him. Good attitudes = Great outcomes!

Those who have a spirit of conquest put their own personal likes and
dislikes on the back burner because they realize there is a greater
destiny to be achieved.

Notice this; for more than forty years we don't hear one negative word
from Caleb about the other 10 guys! He didn't have time to get caught up
in that; he had a mountain to conquer! Good attitudes = Great outcomes!
