# FAITH LIFT

THREE LEVELS OF FAITH

1 Corinthians 12 lists nine gifts of the Holy Spirit. These nine Gifts
can be classified in three categories:

- Revelation Gifts - Word of Wisdom, Word of Knowledge, and Discerning
 of Spirits.

- Vocal Gifts -- Prophecy, Tongues, and Interpretation of Tongues.

- Power Gifts -- Faith, Healing, and miracles.

I would like to begin this book by talking about Three Levels of Faith.

This statement may come as a shock to you, if you have a religious
mindset... One does not have to be saved to possess and exercise faith!
Think of this:

- Every time a person steps on an airplane, they are exercising faith in
 the pilot and the equipment!

- Every time a person steps into an elevator, they are exercising faith
 in the manufacturer and the machine!

- Every time a person gets into an automobile, they are exercising faith
 in their own ability and the ability of the vehicle!

Romans 12:3 (KJV) "God has given to EVERY person, THE measure of
faith." While the abilities and intellect of each person on the Earth
is very different, it is true that initially all people have been given
the same measure of faith to begin life with. God started each of us off
with as much faith as he started the Apostle Paul and Smith Wigglesworth
off with! How we develop the faith we have been given determines what we
can do with it.

There are three ways we receive faith:

- Faith is imparted...

- Faith is given as a fruit of the spirit...

- Faith is given as a gift...

Nothing is more pleasing to the Lord than to see his people walking out
and operating in faith! Hebrews 11:6 (NKJV) "But without faith it is
impossible to please God: for he that comes to God must believe that He
is, and that He is a rewarder of them that diligently seek Him."

IMPARTED FAITH

The impartation of this gift occurs at the moment of conception. It
comes installed in the intellect. Imparted faith gives us the capacity
to receive, possess, exercise, and cultivate faith.

We are commanded to develop our intellect to its maximum potential so we
can offer it to God for His use. 2 Timothy 2:15 (KJV) "Study to shew
thyself approved unto God, a workman that needeth not to be ashamed,
rightly dividing the word of truth." Just as we are responsible as
human beings to develop our intellect, we are responsible to exercise
and increase our faith.

THE FRUIT OF FAITH

Faith is not only imparted at conception; it is planted as a seed at
conversion. In Luke 8 Jesus gives the parable of the sower and the seed.
In verse 11 He explains the parable, "Now the parable is this: The seed
is the word of God." Everyone of us, saved and unsaved have been given
"The Measure of faith." The seed of faith is in us, believers and
unbelievers alike! All of us have the capacity to believe! What we
choose to do with the faith we have been given will largely determine
the impact we make on this planet, as well as the influence we will be
able to exercise over people and in eternity. Faith is a big deal and
most of us need some Faith Lifts from time to time!

Not only have we been given faith to begin our time on this planet, John
1:12 tells us God has given us "The power to become the sons of
God." Every person on earth has the Power to become a son or
daughter of God" which opens a pathway to even more faith! What is
the "Power to become the sons of God?" It is Faith! The faith we
were given at birth is ENOUGH faith to get us to God and ENOUGH faith to
make us into one of His sons and daughters.

At conversion the seed of the fruit of the spirit is deposited in our
lives. Whether or not that fruit ever comes to maturity depends totally
on how the individual responds to it and cultivates it.

Faith is also a component of the fruit of the spirit, listed in
Galatians 5:22. How do we cultivate and grow imparted faith and faith as
a Fruit of the Spirit? Romans 10:17"Faith comes by hearing, and
hearing by the word of God."

Once the seed of faith has been planted at conversion, we begin the
process of developing faith into mature fruit, through the study,
memorization, and practice of the Word of God.

The fruit of faith is what God does through us; walking out His Word,
learning to stand on His promises; the gift of faith is what God does
for us.

We are saved by faith, not by works. But once we are converted, we
continually receive Faith Lifts by mixing faith with works.

THE GIFT OF FAITH

God wants us as His children to have faith, so much so, that He has
provided it for us through as many sources and channels as possible.

Faith is the only ingredient mentioned in both the Galatian list of
fruit and the Corinthian list of gifts.

The gift of faith goes far beyond just believing God is able, to the
point of speaking things into existence that seem totally irrational to
the human mind.

This kind of faith moves us into a place where God honors our word as
his own, and miraculously brings it to pass.

God did this in the Old Testament when the Prophet Ezekiel prophesied to
the dry bones and they came back to life and actually became a living
breathing, functioning army! Faith can bring dry bones to life again!

God did this for Paul and Silas in Acts 16! At midnight they were
praying and singing praises to God! God honored their words by shaking
the Philippian jail with an earthquake, not only were they freed from
prison, the jailor and his entire house were saved. Faith can free us
from whatever has us bound!

The gift of faith produces a confidence in God and His word that defies
human reasoning, satanic obstacles, doubt, and unbelief. It says I will
believe no matter what, simply because God said it! When the gift of
faith is functioning properly, it will produce the following things:

- Supernatural blessing...

- Supernatural protection...

- Supernatural provision...

- Supernatural strength...

Faith is the switch that opens God\'s powerhouse. Faith sees the
invisible, believes the incredible, and claims the impossible. Mark
11:22-24 (NKJV) "So Jesus answered and said to them, "Have faith in
God.23 "For assuredly, I say to you, whoever says to this mountain, 'Be
removed and be cast into the sea,' and does not doubt in his heart, but
believes that those things he says will be done, he will have whatever
he says.24 "Therefore I say to you, whatever things you ask when you
pray, believe that you receive them, and you will have them."

Faith is not a guarantee against problems... Faith is a guarantee
against defeat!

My natural Father has more faith than any person I have ever known. As a
result, I have had a front row seat to see the miraculous my entire
life. I grew up thinking that people of great faith were oblivious to
fear or unbelief. I have learned that people who exercise great faith
probably face more fear than any of us, but they have learned to do what
they do -- Afraid! What conquers fear? Faith! God doesn't respond to
desperation, if He did, there would be no desperate people in the world
-- He responds to faith, and faith alone!

Those who see signs, miracles, and wonders disregard fear and unbelief
and allow their natural faith, the faith they have been given as a fruit
of the Spirit, and the faith they have received in their gift plex to
come together to see the miraculous.

ACKNOWLEDGE THE ENEMY, BUT KEEP YOUR EYES ON THE LORD

In the fourteenth chapter of the book of Exodus, the Children of Israel
followed God into a cul-de-sac between hostile hills and the edge of a
sea too deep to be forded, and too wide to he crossed. The unmistakable
indication of Exodus 14:1-4 is that the Lord took responsibility for
leading them into peril. He gave them specific, step-by-step
instructions, leading them down a route to apparent ruin: "Turn and
camp; camp there; there, before the entrapping sea; yes, right there in
that impossible place."

How could Moses and Aaron keep from worrying when the Red Sea was facing
them; the mountains were beside them; the soldiers of Egypt were bearing
down on them with swords drawn? It was certain that the Egyptians were
not one bit concerned about a confrontation with the Israelites because
these people had spent the last 400 years living as slaves. Not one of
them had been trained in the intricacies of warfare nor did one of them
own a weapon! What the Egyptians did not know is that the Israelites did
not need a weapon; they did not need to know how to fight; they had a
secret weapon, and His name was Jehovah!

What helped create faith in a moment of terror was the fact that they
knew Jehovah delivered them from Egypt and that it was His will for them
to go to Canaan! Moses determined that they were where they were not by
their own will, but according to the will of God!

Please understand that God was not being disingenuous to Israel! He was
about to deal with Israel's enemies once and for all! He was about to
allow them to see Him in a way they had never seen Him before! He was
about to provide them with weapons to protect themselves, during the
forty plus years of wilderness wandering!

Life can be very, very difficult. If we are walking through a difficult
spot, we are not so out of step with the general population, or the most
spiritual people in the Kingdom. The reason life can be so difficult is
because God has appointed a destiny for us, and there is a real, live
enemy who will do everything in his power to sabotage and keep us back
from reaching our ultimate potential.

We must bear in mind, our enemy is not our parents, professors, boss or
the government; our enemy is satan and satan alone! On the other hand,
the Lord has committed Himself to being our constant companion and even
fighting our battles for us.

There is no question that Israel had an enemy, and that enemy was
consistently bent on destruction and demise. Look at what Israel was
facing. Exodus 14:5-9 (CEV) "When the king of Egypt heard that the
Israelites had finally left, he and his officials [changed their
minds]{.underline} and said, "Look what we have done! We let them get
away, and they will no longer be our slaves."6 The king got his war
chariot and army ready. 7 He commanded his officers in charge of his six
hundred best chariots and all his other chariots to [start after the
Israelites]{.underline}. 8 The [Lord]{.smallcaps} made the king so
stubborn that he [went after them]{.underline}, even though the
Israelites proudly went on their way."

The Israelites could have denied the existence of an enemy, but that
would not have changed the fact that a hostile king was in hot pursuit
of this group of emancipated slaves. Denial of the enemy never calms his
hostilities, but the acknowledgement of his existence demands readiness
on the part of Christ followers. Charles Spurgeon once said, "The great
tyrant has not forgotten you, and he designs your capture and
re-enslavement."

The devil sometimes launches direct, frontal attacks against us, but he
is usually more conniving and insidious than picking an overt fight
against Kingdom citizens. Ephesians 6:12 describes in detail what we are
up against. (NKJV) "For we do not wrestle against flesh and blood,
but against [principalities]{.underline}, against [powers]{.underline},
against the [rulers of the darkness of this age]{.underline}, against
[spiritual hosts of wickedness in the heavenly places]{.underline}."
Ladies and Gentlemen, this is not a fight against flesh and blood, this
is spiritual warfare at the highest levels, and it is for the highest
prize -- Our eternal souls!

Whenever the enemy attacks, whether covertly or overtly, he is normally
closer and cruder than we first imagined, much like the Pharaoh of the
Exodus. When we engage our enemy, we should always acknowledge his
existence, while keeping our eyes on the Lord.

I want us to consider the parallels between Pharaoh and satan:

- Both are unyielding enemies!

- Both covet power over their subjects!

- Both are enraged beyond reason!

- Both have assembled vast armies meant for destruction!

- Both will eventually be plundered by the Almighty!

The Bible likens Satan to five different animals:

- In Genesis 3, he\'s a serpent trying to deceive God\'s people.

- In Matthew 13, he's a bird trying to defile God\'s harvest.

- In John 10, He's a wolf attacking God\'s flock.

- In First Peter, he\'s a lion trying to devour God\'s children.

- In Revelation 12, he\'s a dragon wanting to destroy God\'s Son.

There is little question about the intentions of our enemy. He is
malicious, malignant and venomous. He is not tempting us to allow us to
have a good time; he is attempting to destroy everything in our lives
and to drag us down to Hell.

This is not the kind of enemy we can defeat simply with the denial of
his existence or presence. Defeating our enemy will require far more
than confessing we do not have an adversary!

How do we overcome such a persistent and malignant opponent? We would do
well to remember the following things:

- The blood of Jesus Christ forgives our sins and resolves our guilt!

- The resurrection frees us from the fear of death and satisfies our
 need for eternal significance and happi­ness!

- The presence of the Lord surrounds us!

- The promises of the Bible sustain us!

- The grace of God heals the wounds this world inflicts upon us!

- Our testimony becomes our triumphant shout and the final nail in the
 coffin of the enemy!

Jesus said, John 8:36 (NKJV) "Therefore if the Son makes you free, [you
shall be free indeed]{.underline}."

This enemy doesn\'t surrender his prey without a fight. He comes racing
after us, chariot wheels churning the dust, seeking to discourage and
defeat us. He pursues us with the intensity of the Pharaoh of Exodus.

He may use our own friends or family members! He sometimes uses the
weapon of persecution! He often uses the weapon of discouragement! He
tries to use the lack of acceptance as a weapon of discouragement
against us! He will not hesitate to use the trick of personal financial
situations to his advantage! He is not above pointing out hypocrites in
the church to get us off track! He will do everything he can to bring
about an onslaught of temptation! He is such a scum bag that he will
orchestrate a barrage of troubles and trials against us, using the
weakest and most frail people in our lives to do it! We can be assured;
our enemy will attempt to trap us in our troubles; entangle us in our
dilemmas; corner us in an impossible situation; or lure us into
temptation.

We must stop blaming God for all these maladies occurring in our lives!
When we find ourselves in a tough situation suffering pain, worry,
anguish, illness, or temptation, we can rest assured the devil is
undoubtedly behind it. John 10:10 (NKJV) "[The thief]{.underline}
does not come except to steal, and to kill, and to destroy. I have come
that they may have life, and that they may have it more
abundantly."

Here is where we must turn the tables on the enemy! Of course, we should
acknowledge satan\'s activity, but rest assured we will not be
intimidated by him. We can resist him in the power of God and by the
blood of Jesus Christ! In fact, our Commander in chief enjoins us to
such resistance. When threatened by the devil's schemes, we should stand
on the Word. Daniel 11:32 (CEV) "The king will use deceit to win
followers from those who are unfaithful to God, but [those who remain
faithful will do everything possible to oppose him]{.underline}."
James 4:7-8 (CEV) "Surrender to God! [Resist the devil, and he will
run from you]{.underline}. 8 Come near to God, and he will come near to
you. Clean up your lives, you sinners. Purify your hearts, you people
who can't make up your mind." 1 Peter 5:9 (CEV) "But you must
[resist the devil and stay strong in your faith.]{.underline} You know
that all over the world the Lord's followers are suffering just as you
are."

In our effort to resist the enemy we should employ every weapon and
piece of armor that has been made available to us. 2 Corinthians 10:3-6
(NKJV) "For though we walk in the flesh, we do not war according to
the flesh.4 For [the weapons of our warfare are not carnal but mighty in
God]{.underline} for pulling down strongholds,5 casting down arguments
and every high thing that exalts itself against the knowledge of God,
bringing every thought into captivity to the obedience of Christ,6 and
being ready to punish all disobedience when your obedience is
fulfilled."

We should resist the enemy in the name of Jesus Christ, we should stand
our ground; and we should shake off discourage­ment the moment it is
initiated! When we do this, the enemy is doomed to be drowned in the Red
Sea of the blood of Jesus Christ. Every time we resist the slightest
temptation, we honor God. Every time we overcome even the smallest
problem by trusting and obeying our Lord Jesus, God is glorified in our
lives.

We must make the conscious decision that at every tempting point we will
always choose:

- Character over convenience!

- Faithfulness over ease!

- Honesty over deceit!

Do we have an enemy? Yes! Will we experience problems? Yes! Will we be
defeated? No!

Look at the patriarch Job and how he faced his difficulties
courageously! In one terrible season:

- He lost his herds to barbarians!

- He lost his children to a tornado!

- He lost his health to a disease!

- He lost his wealth to misfortune!

- He lost his friends to a critical spirit!

- He lost his wife to doubt!

Job seemed to be oblivious to the fact that as he sat among the ashes
scraping his boils with pottery shards and bemoaning his fate, that an
enemy who was bent on destroying his soul had orchestrated his problems.
This was nonetheless a fact! The enemy was behind every negative thing
that happened to Job! But much to the enemy's chagrin, Job emerged on
the other side of the trial with his faith intact and his attitude
optimistic! The reason he was able to persevere is because he
acknowledged he had an enemy, but he kept his eyes on the Lord. You
don't have to believe me that this was Job's posture, listen to his own
words! "Though God slay me yet I will trust him. "If worms destroy
this body, I intend to see God face to face."

As a result of Job acknowledging the enemy but keeping his eyes on the
Lord, God gave him twice as much as he had before the trial. He was 70
when the trial began, and he lived to be 210!

Since the same devil orchestrates similar attacks against us today, what

our focus on Jesus!

In Exodus 14, Pharaoh could threaten with brute force and protest; he

That cloud which became darkness to the Egyptians was a light to the

Acknowledge the enemy but keep your eyes on the Lord!

If we walk in His will there is nothing that hell can devise which will

We must acknowledge our enemy while keeping our eyes on the Lord.

All of this happened to prove to the Israelites and to us that when we

- Power! He is omnipotent!

Ladies and Gentlemen, the Red Sea may stretch out in front of us; the

 acknowledge the enemy but keep their eyes on the Lord!

twice a day, and the man of God had to leave the brook Cherith. At this
juncture, God sent Elijah to Zarephath and there he engages in a
conversation with a widow who was financially destitute.

If we need a ridiculous blessing we may be called upon to take a
ridiculous action! Trust me, everyone who operated in great faith in
Hebrews eleven knew what the giant of fear looked like, smelled like,
acted like! But they did what they did, despite the fear that had to be
overcome.

Are we going to operate in a Faith Lift an do what we know to do, or are
we going to sit on the sidelines and allow the miracles of God to pass
us by!

MIRACULOUS RESULTS...

1 Kings 17:15-16 (NKJV) "She went away and did according to the word
of Elijah; and she and he and her household ate for many days. The
bin of flour was not used up, nor did the jar of oil run dry, according
to the word of the LORD which He spoke by Elijah."

We are not told exactly how much time went by between the time this
widow made a cake for the man of God, believing she and her son would be
taken care of. The Bible just say's "She and her son did eat many
days." What we are sure of is that the drought lasted 3 ½ years! It
is possible the meal and oil lasted for a thousand days or more! The oil
and meal did not grow; it just stayed!

Sometimes God does not choose to allow us to see the reserves; He just
allows us to see the bare minimum and asks us to trust Him, believing
there is more than enough!

When we step out in obedient faith to do the ridiculous, God has
obligated himself to do the miraculous!

Those who need the miraculous will probably be required to set the
miracle in motion by doing the ridiculous! We are often guilty of
sitting on the sidelines doing nothing, waiting on God to send the right
opportunity for our miracle when God has already given us the raw
material to bring the thing to pass. After all He has given us natural
faith, the fruit of faith, and the gift of faith! What are we waiting
for?

When this lady stepped out in obedient faith, doing the ridiculous, she
set in motion the miraculous for her and her family!

As with most miracles, the miracle itself is a precursor to what God
really wants to do. This lady and her son had sufficient food for as
much as a thousand days, but God was just building her faith to receive
the greatest miracle of her life. I Kings 17:17-24 (NKJV) "Now it
happened after these things that the son of the woman who owned the
house became sick. And his sickness was so serious that there was no
breath left in him. So she said to Elijah, "What have I to do with
you, O man of God? Have you come to me to bring my sin to remembrance,
and to kill my son?" And he said to her, "Give me your son." So he
took him out of her arms and carried him to the upper room where he was
staying, and laid him on his own bed. Then he cried out to the LORD
and said, "O LORD my God, have You also brought tragedy on the widow
with whom I lodge, by killing her son?" And he stretched himself
out on the child three times, and cried out to the LORD and said, "O
LORD my God, I pray, let this child's soul come back to him." Then
the LORD heard the voice of Elijah; and the soul of the child came back
to him, and he revived. And Elijah took the child and brought him
down from the upper room into the house, and gave him to his mother. And
Elijah said, "See, your son lives!" Then the woman said to Elijah,
"Now by this I know that you are a man of God, and that the word of the
LORD in your mouth is the truth."

If this woman had not trusted the man of God with the little things, she
could never have trusted him for her son! The miracle of the meal and
oil was just a starter miracle for what would follow!

What is God getting us ready for? Are we going to pass or fail the
present test? If we fail this test, it will set us up for defeat, later!
If we pass this test, we will be placing ourselves and our families in
the pathway of a blessing at a more crucial time!

The bottom line is, whether we need food or a resurrection, if we need
the miraculous, we must be willing to do the ridiculous!

What does a Faith Lift look like in our lives?

- If we need a financial miracle, we must give a ridiculous offering...

- If we need a miracle of healing, we must trust God in a ridiculous
 way... (Pray for someone else)

- If we need a relational miracle, we must humble ourselves before man,
 taking the high road of humility...

- If we need a spiritual miracle, we must find a place to serve
 others...

- If we need a miracle of cooperation, we must submit ourselves to those
 who have authority over us...
