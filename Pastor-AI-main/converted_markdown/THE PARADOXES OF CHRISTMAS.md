As people of faith, we must accept the fact that there are many
paradoxes that populate the Bible, which is the book we accept as our
rule of faith and practice.

A Paradox is defined as "A statement that is seemingly contradictory or
opposed to common sense and yet is, perhaps, true." (Webster)

Nowhere in Scripture is the contrast between heaven and earth more
evident than in the Christmas story.  Luke takes us from the power and
fame of Caesar to the weakness and obscurity of a baby in a stable, and
then declares the baby in a manger to be a superior to Caesar.

 

Look at how many times a paradox occurs in Dr. Luke's rendition of the
Christmas story:

PARADOX #1 - JESUS EXPERIENCED A HUMAN BIRTH SO THAT WE COULD
EXPERIENCE A SPIRITUAL BIRTH

The thought of God becoming one of us is absolutely stunning to the
human mind. If God became a human being He voluntarily accepted all the
weaknesses, restrictions, and limitations experienced by mere mortals.
How could a God so powerful that He constructed the world, created human
life, and spoke the planets into place, condescend to become one of His
own creatures? Yet, Doctor Luke describes such an event with great
detail, right down to the:

- Date and time... Tax time!

- City... Bethlehem!

- Place... A manger!

- Clothing... Swaddling clothes!

What does it mean for God to experience the birth and life of a human
being? Man was created perfect and placed in a perfect world called the
Garden of Eden. As perfect as his world was, mankind found a way to mess
up his own world. Adam and Eve sinned, thus offending the holiness of
God. As soon as Adam and Eve sinned, God unveiled a plan of
reconciliation for a fallen race. Genesis 3:15 (NKJV) "And I will put
enmity Between you and the woman, And between your seed and her Seed;
[He shall bruise your head]{.underline}, And you [shall bruise His
heel]{.underline}."

The only way sin could be atoned for was the sacrifice of a perfect
human being. Wait a minute -- a perfect human being? How could such a
thing ever come to pass? From the time Adam and Eve sinned, decades and
centuries came and went, yet not one human being could rise to the point
of perfection to qualify as the sacrifice for sin. Romans 3:23 (NKJV)
"For [all have sinned]{.underline} and fall short of the glory of
God."

In the Old Testament, the only way a sacrifice could satisfy a Holy God
who had been offended by sin, was for that sacrifice to be spotless,
blameless, and perfect. Leviticus 1:1-3 (NKJV) "Now the
[Lord]{.smallcaps} called to Moses, and spoke to him from the tabernacle
of meeting, saying, "Speak to the children of Israel, and say to
them: 'When any one of you brings an offering to the [Lord,]{.smallcaps}
you shall bring your offering of the livestock---of the herd and of the
flock. 'If his offering is a burnt sacrifice of the herd, let him
offer [a male without blemish]{.underline}; he shall offer it of his own
free will at the door of the tabernacle of meeting before the
[Lord]{.smallcaps}."

To be the supreme sacrifice, this God man would need to take upon
Himself the form of a human being and the only way to do such a thing
was to be born into the world through the strength of human flesh.

Though Mary was a virgin, she brought Jesus into the world just like all
other human beings are born. (Story of little boy's who stole a statue
of Mary from the Catholic Church and prayed "God if you ever want to
see your mother again...")

While describing the birth of Jesus, Matthew quotes Isaiah. Matthew 1:23
(NKJV) "Behold, the virgin shall [be with child, and bear a
Son]{.underline}, and they shall call His name Immanuel," which is
translated, "God with us."

The bottom line is this... Jesus experienced a human birth so that we
could receive a spiritual birth. Ephesians 2:1-6 (NKJV) "And [you He
made alive]{.underline}, who [were dead]{.underline} in trespasses and
sins, in which [you once walked according to the course of this
world, according to the prince of the power of the air, the spirit who
now works in the sons of disobedience]{.underline}, among whom also
we all once conducted ourselves in the lusts of our flesh, fulfilling

His great love with which He loved us, even [when we were dead in
trespasses, made us alive together with Christ]{.underline} (by grace
you have been saved), and raised us up together, and made us sit
together in the heavenly places in Christ Jesus,"

Mary became the mother of Jesus so that we could have a Heavenly Father.
Jesus became an earthly son so that we could be inducted into the family
of God, thus being adopted by a Heavenly Father.

One of the most overlooked facts about Adam and Eve's sin in the Garden
of Eden is that the sin they committed broke the relationship between
Jehovah God and the human race. Throughout the Old Testament the
relationship between God and man was strained, restricted, and limited
at best.

In the best-case scenario, man was orphaned at Eden.

I realize there are many here today who do not, and never have had, a
close relationship with their earthly father. This is a human tragedy,
not unique to our day. The divine design of the human family is such
that children and their parents share a very close relational bond. If
you do not have this kind of relationship with your parents, it may be a
little difficult to get your hands around the following thoughts.

Matthew 7:11 (NKJV) "If you then, being evil, know how to give good

Though Jesus was a God man, the man side of the scenario brought with it

Because Jesus was a human being He was limited by time and space. As

- He laughed...

- He cried...

- He loved...

- He slept...

- He became fatigued...

- He became disappointed...

- He developed relationships...

- He experienced the pain of rejection...

- He experienced the pain of a human body...

-- He was grounded!) The Scripture clearly states that He went back to
Nazareth and submitted Himself to His parents and the result was, "He
grew in stature and favor with both God and man!"

Why does man suffer all these limitations and restrictions? Because of
the curse of sin! In the Garden of Eden man was neither limited by time
nor space! The entire garden was at their disposal, and when they sinned
God drove them out of the Garden to prevent them from eating of the Tree
of Life and living forever in their sinful condition. Genesis 2:22-24
(NKJV) "Then the [Lord]{.smallcaps} God said, "Behold, the man has

Jesus took upon himself the form of sinful flesh. 2 Corinthians 5:21
(NKJV) "[He made Him who knew no sin to be sin for us]{.underline},

What benefit is it for us, that Jesus would come to the earth and do
such a thing?

- He became flesh so that we could become eternally saved...

- He suffered so that we could live a rejoicing life...

- He denied the enticement to sin so that we could deny it as well...

- He took stripes on His back so that we could be healed...

- He died on that horrible day so that we could live for an eternal
 day...

- He rose from the dead so that death would no longer have dominion over
 us...

- He ascended to OUR Heavenly Father so that He could make intercession
 for us...

How is all this possible? Jesus became subject to the limitations of the
flesh so that we could be free from sin's restrictions!

PARADOX#4 - JESUS WAS RECEIVED BY LOWLY SHEPHERDS SO THAT WE COULD BE
WELCOMED BY HEAVENLY ANGELS...

The Christmas story is replete with meaning but nowhere is the paradox
of Christmas more pronounced that when the shepherds came to see the
Christ child.

Shepherding was a trade that was interwoven into the culture of Old
Testament times, but it was a trade that one performed if he could not
find a way to excel in the socio-economic culture of the day. Shepherds
were not the kind of persons one would expect to see at the event that
would be the fulfillment of the Messianic promise. You would expect to
see:

- Kings...

- Potentates...

- Influencers...

- Political leaders...

- Religious leaders...

- High-society people... (The upper crust is some crumbs held together
 by a little dough!)

Yet, here it is, right here in the Christmas story, shepherds were some
of the first people to welcome this Godchild into the world! There are
many things about this story that on the surface do not make a lot of
sense; in fact, they are downright surprising! One of the shocking
things about this story is who God placed on His invitation list to
attend such an event.

Shepherds! Are you sure, shepherds? Yes shepherds! Why would God invite
such a common group of people to the birth of His Son?

I believe the answer to such questions is multi-faceted;

- God wanted to demonstrate that He was coming to the earth for
 everyone... John 3:16 (NKJV) "For God so loved the world that [He
 gave His only begotten Son]{.underline}, that whoever believes in Him
 should not perish but have everlasting life."

 Testament and the way he chose to deal with them in the New

- God wanted those who would accept Him to know that He would be the

- God wanted to bring us to the understanding that, at the end of the

Jesus was welcomed by shepherds so that we might be welcomed by angels!
