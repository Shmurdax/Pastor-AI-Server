# CONFIDENT

TEXT: 2 Timothy 1:11-12 "I was appointed a preacher, an apostle,
and a teacher of the Gentiles. ^12 ^For this reason I also suffer these
things; nevertheless I am not ashamed, for I know whom I have believed

INTRO: Over the next four Sunday's we are going to be talking about
being confident. This world has a way of shaking our confidence at many
levels. Dictionard.com defines confidence as "Having strong belief or
full assurance."

Most people begin their adult life filled with anticipation and
confidence. Somewhere along the line dreams and reality seem to collide
as the battering ram of life crashes through hopefulness and oftentimes
what's left isn't really worth talking about.

Confidence is a big deal. Obtaining confidence is not easy and retaining
and maintaining it is even more difficult. The only thing that can
guarantee confidence over the entire journey of life is faith in God.
(R.W. Shambach -- "You don't have any trouble. All you need is faith in
God.")

CONFIDENT IN OUR ETERNITY

Many people have trouble believing that one can be confident about their
salvation. They say things like:

- I hope I'm saved... (When He saved me He gave me a know so salvation
 -- Gentleman at Scottsville Arkansas who told me after my sermon that
 we couldn't possibly know for sure whether we were saved or not, until
 we die)

- Time will tell... (Time won't tell anything -- The thing that is going
 to tell is whether our names are written down in Heaven)

- I'm doing everything I can to make it... (One cannot get to Heaven by
 doing good deeds)

Please let me make one thing very clear. There is a real eternity out
there! All of us have a date with eternity! Hebrews 9:27 (NKJV) "It
is appointed for men to die once, but after this the judgment."

Go to the cemetery:

- There are small graves and large graves...

- There are old graves and new graves...

- The residents die young and old...

- The residents die rich and poor... (Someone asked how much money
 Howard Hughes left behind -- "All of it.")

- The residents die educated and uneducated...

There is no escaping it, we are all creatures of eternity. Each of us is
going to spend eternity somewhere:

- Heaven...

- Hell...

Sorry to disappoint you but there is no in between stop over, rest area
kind of place.

I don't think any of us intend to go to hell so how can we be confident
that our eternity has been secured? How can we be assured our ticket has
been punched? How can we be sure we are going to a better place when we
leave this, not so perfect place?

Listen to the dialogue between Jesus and Nicodemus. John 3:1-8 (NKJV)
"There was a man of the Pharisees named Nicodemus, a ruler of the
Jews. ^2 ^This man came to Jesus by night and said to Him, "Rabbi, we
know that You are a teacher come from God; for no one can do these signs
that You do unless God is with him." ^3 ^Jesus answered and said to
him, "Most assuredly, I say to you, unless one is born again, he cannot
see the kingdom of God." ^4 ^Nicodemus said to Him, "How can a man be
born when he is old? Can he enter a second time into his mother's womb
and be born?" ^5 ^Jesus answered, "Most assuredly, I say to you, unless
one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God.
 ^6 ^That which is born of the flesh is flesh, and that which is born of
the Spirit is spirit.  ^7 ^Do not marvel that I said to you, 'You must
be born again."

In this passage of Scripture, Nicodemus makes an observation that Jesus
couldn't do the things he was doing apart from being a man sent from
God. This vote of confidence from Nicodemus (Who was a ruler of the
Jews) must have seemed pretty nice to Jesus.

Jesus decided not to allow the conversation to get bogged down with all
the niceties and mutual admiration comments. He decided to ratchet up
the conversation a little and when He did, Nicodemus was totally
unprepared for what was to come next. Jesus said, "Most assuredly, I
say to you, unless one is born again, he cannot see the kingdom of
God." Please know, one cannot be saved by:

- Church membership...

- Leveraging the relationship of a pastor...

- Generous giving and doing good deeds...

There is only one way to salvation and that is through Jesus Christ and
the sacrifice He offered through the cross.

Nicodemus wondered out loud whether he as a grown man could enter the
second time into his mother's womb and be born again? We must admit, we
do use some interesting phrases around church:

- Washed in the blood... (Couldn't that be a little bothersome?)

- Covered by the blood... (Isn't that a little risky? Sounds like a
 spiritual Waffle House -- Scattered, smothered and covered)

- The lamb has been slain... (Shouldn't someone call the animal rights
 people?)

- On fire for God... (Shouldn't we call the fire department?)

- I'm coming out... (I didn't think we believed in a gay lifestyle)

- I'm going through... (Going through what?)

- Hang on brother... (To what?)

- Turn loose... (Won't I fall?)

The response of Jesus to Nicodemus' inquiry of the prospects of actually
being able to be born again tells the whole story of being confident
about our eternal destiny. "Jesus answered, "Most assuredly, I say to
you, unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the
kingdom of God.  ^6 ^That which is born of the flesh is flesh, and that
which is born of the Spirit is spirit."

When He speaks of the water, He is speaking of the natural birth. When a
baby is born, the mothers water breaks as the baby comes down the birth
canal to discover America. This is the first birth.

One must be born a natural birth before a spiritual birth is possible.
Once one has had a natural birth he/she is now trapped in a place of
decision about a spiritual birth. This process is called being born
again.

One cannot inherit eternal life based on making some good decisions or
being a doer of good deeds. Everything has to change, and the only way
that is going to happen is by being born again!

CONCLUSION:

There is no question, one can be confident about their eternity! On the
cross Jesus made it possible for those of us who have been born once, to
be born again.

The cross is the birth canal of every single person who has ever punched
their ticket to Heaven. There is no other path that leads to the
everlasting Kingdom of Heaven.

Those who are born once die twice -- They die a natural death and a
spiritual death. Those who have been born twice (again) die only once --
The natural death. In this scenario, there is no spiritual death.

If you haven't been born again, you will not find a better time than
right now. 2 Corinthians 6:1-2 (NKJV) "We then, as workers
together with Him also plead with you not to receive the grace of God in
vain. ^2 ^For He says: "In an acceptable time I have heard you, And in
the day of salvation I have helped you." Behold, now is the accepted
time; behold, now is the day of salvation."
