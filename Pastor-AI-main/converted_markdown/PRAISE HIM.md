# PRAISE HIM

1 Chronicles 16:812 - 22-36 (NKJV) "On that day David first delivered
this psalm into the hand of Asaph and his brethren, to thank the Lord:
8 Oh, give thanks to the Lord! Call upon His name; Make known His deeds
among the peoples! 9 Sing to Him, sing psalms to Him; Talk of all His
wondrous works! 10 Glory in His holy name; Let the hearts of those
rejoice who seek the Lord! 11 Seek the Lord and His strength; Seek His
face evermore! 12 Remember His marvelous works which He has done, His
wonders, and the judgments of His mouth. 23 Sing to the Lord, all the
earth; Proclaim the good news of His salvation from day to day.
24 Declare His glory among the nations, His wonders among all peoples.
25 For the Lord is great and greatly to be praised; He is also to be
feared above all gods. 26 For all the gods of the peoples are idols, But
the Lord made the heavens. 27 Honor and majesty are before Him; Strength
and gladness are in His place. 28 Give to the Lord, O families of the
peoples, Give to the Lord glory and strength. 29 Give to the Lord the
glory due His name; Bring an offering, and come before Him. Oh, worship
the Lord in the beauty of holiness! 30 Tremble before Him, all the
earth. The world also is firmly established, It shall not be moved.
31 Let the heavens rejoice, and let the earth be glad; And let them say
among the nations, "The Lord reigns." 32 Let the sea roar, and all its
fullness; Let the field rejoice, and all that is in it. 33 Then the
trees of the woods shall rejoice before the Lord, For He is coming to
judge the earth. 34 Oh, give thanks to the Lord, for He is good! For His
mercy endures forever. 35 And say, "Save us, O God of our salvation;
Gather us together, and deliver us from the Gentiles, To give thanks to
Your holy name, To triumph in Your praise." 36 Blessed be the Lord God
of Israel From everlasting to everlasting! And all the people said,
"Amen!" and praised the Lord."

INTRO: 1 Chronicles 16 is one of the first times the expression
"Give Thanks" is used in the Bible. I stand to echo the words of
the Psalmist who said, "Give thanks."

In this passage David is welcoming the Ark of the Covenant back to its
rightful place in the tabernacle.

I want to give you three reasons from 1 Chronicles 16 that we should
praise Him.

## I. WE SHOULD PRAISE HIM BECAUSE HE IS A GOD OF SECOND CHANCES...

David had just attempted to relocate the Ark of God from the house of
Abinadab to Jerusalem. Remember, in the Old Testament, the Ark was where
God lived among His people.

The Ark was mishandled and David's good intentions ended in disaster
with the death of Uzza. David attempted to do a good thing the wrong
way.

Even though his first attempt ended in a catastrophe, God allowed him
another chance and this time he succeeded. 

David then authors a psalm of thanksgiving in 1 Chronicles 16. You can
tell he is deeply grateful to the Lord, as we should all be, when we
have another shot to get something right. Thomas H. Parker said, "If at
first we don't succeed, try try, again!"

I'm thankful that God doesn't dwell on our short-term failures but
rather, works in us for our long-term potential. God remains steadfastly
at work in us long after we have given up on ourselves.

When we mess up and fess up, God moves on, and so should we! The problem
is that we get high-centered in our mess when God is attempting to coax
us into a new opportunity.

He will extend a second chance, or even a third, fourth, and fifth
chance if necessary. That is a very good reason to praise Him!

## II. WE SHOULD PRAISE HIM BECAUSE HE MAKES BIG THINGS FROM SMALL
BEGINNINGS...

In this Psalm of thanks, David recalls the history of God's people and
God's faithfulness to them.

Let me pause and throw in a principle of gratitude worth remembering:
Remembering God's faithfulness in our PAST should help us REJOICE in
our PRESENT because His PAST RECORD is a good indicator of His FUTURE
ACTIVITY!

In this passage, David recalls just how fragile and small God's people
started out. They went to Egypt a family (66 people) and left a nation
(Of 2 ½ - 3 million people).

In Canaan the story is even greater, they multiplied exponentially and
became a nation of multiplied millions.  

David says in verses 19-20, "When they were but few in number, few
indeed, and strangers in it, they wandered from nation to nation, from
one kingdom to another." David says, "This whole thing started
small, but God did big things to get us to this point!" 

And God still does today:

- He is still able to calm a storm...

- He can still multiply the loaves and the fish...

- He can still stop the mouths of hungry lions...

- He can still rob a furnace of its fire...

- He can still order the steps of good people... 

- He is still a rewarder to those who diligently seek Him... 

God said in Zechariah 4:10 "Do not despise the days of small
beginnings." God made a nation of a family! We should praise Him
because He turns small things into BIG STUFF.

We see the present circumstances for what they are; God sees them for
what they are going to be!

## III. WE SHOULD PRAISE HIM FOR HIS NAME...

David say's in verse 29, "ascribe to the Lord the glory that is due
his name." 

In the beginning of this chapter, after the Ark of the Lord had been
returned, David actually appointed people whose sole job was to
continually praise the Lord. He then encourages EVERYONE to do the
same! 

In other words, David was very intentional in offering gratitude to God,
and so should we. We should ascribe to the Lord the praise that is due
Him.  

To ascribe means to assign or give credit, and that is exactly what we
are doing when we express praise and gratitude to God.

However, there is a powerful principle when it comes to being
intentional in our praise and here it is: Our praise ATTACHES us to the
presence of God, and DETACHES us from the presence of anything else that
would drag us down! 

If we don't have anything else to praise Him for we should praise Him
for His name:

- His name is a saving name...

- His name is a delivering name...

- His name is a healing name...

- His name is a redeeming name...

- His name is above every name...

CONCLUSION:

Here is the bottom line. We must not forget that:

- Our praise is a gateway to God's blessing, and a giant leap forward
 for His glory...

<!-- -->

- Our praise is a trap to the enemy and a cry of triumph against our
 troubles...

- Our praise is poison to our perplexities and a passing lane to
 prosperity...

- Our praise is a weapon in the hands of God and a deathblow to the
 worries that crease our brows...

This truth alone makes me grateful for the privilege of praising Him!
