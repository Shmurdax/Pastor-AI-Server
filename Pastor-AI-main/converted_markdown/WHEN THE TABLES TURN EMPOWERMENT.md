## I. EVERYTHING IS NOT AS IT SEEMS...

David asked one of Saul's unemployed servants of the whereabouts of the
descendants of Saul. Ziba revealed there was one lone descendent of
Saul, who happened to be the son of Jonathan but had been handicapped by
a fall. Ziba revealed this descendant was living in the House of Machir
in Lo Debar.

Mephibosheth, once a prince was now doing life as a pauper!

When Mephibosheth was five years old, his grandfather Saul and his
father Jonathan were killed in a battle against the Philistines.

When the nanny who was caring for Mephibosheth heard of the tragic
results of the battle she picked up five-year-old Mephibosheth, and made
a run-for-it. In her haste to flee she dropped the child and he suffered
a spinal injury. In one tragic day, at the age of five Mephibosheth:

Remember, everything is not as it seems!

Mephibosheth was living with Machir the son of Ammiel in Lo Debar. The
name Ammiel comes from the Hebrew word "ammiyel," meaning, "People of
God." The word Machir comes from the Hebrew word, "makik" (maw-kak)
meaning "To be brought low -- decaying." The place, Lo Debar was a
pasture-less place in Palestine, probably desert and therefore more than
likely it was a poverty stricken place.

Notice the sad trajectory of this once promising family with whom

good could befall him. Once hope is lost, self-destructive behavior sets
in. When one begins to participate in self-destructive behavior:
(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Blessings are turned to rubbish through negligence... (One may not

- Opportunities are obscured by pessimistic behavior... (When personal

 miss any opportunity for success)

- Before his father and David became best friends...

- Before his father and grandfather were killed...

- Before he was orphaned and placed in the house of Machir...

- Before his fortunes turned from promising to pathetic...

- Before he knew who Ziba, the servant of David was...

- Before he was summoned to the palace...

- Before he met the King...

- Before his fortunes were restored...

- Before he was invited to eat at the Kings table...

The story of Mephibosheth reiterates to us the following principles:
