# I WANT MY STUFF BACK

I Samuel 27:1-7

INTRO: (Relate the story of David being anointed by Samuel -- Eliab,
Abinadab - -- Shammah -- 7 sons passed by and the Lord chose none of
them, though some of them looked like Kingly material! - God will NEVER
anoint the flesh -- David fights Goliath)

Saul's jealousy caused him to hate David, thus he pursued David to kill
him. It did not help that the women were in the streets singing "Saul
hath killed his thousands, David his ten thousands."

After a while David became weary with all the pressures of being pursued
and he began to take matters into his own hands, and actually hatched a
plan to move his family and his followers to Ziklag, a city in the land
of the Philistines.

During this time David had opportunity to kill Saul on two different
occasions but refused to do so, saying, "I refuse to touch God's
anointed." (God always has a set person -- They are all flawed -- He
alone decides when to set them up and when to take them down -- We are
to never judge upward or horizontally -- Never judge a person you have
no jurisdiction over -- Leave that to God and the authorities over them)

I personally don't believe that it was within the will of God for David
to be in the land of the Philistines in the first place. (Expound on the
fact that God had already decided David would be the next King of
Israel)

Could it be that if David had not fled from Saul that he would have been
King much sooner?

If one is to do the will of God, there must be a boatload of commitment
in the bad times as well as the good times.

After a proving period, David asks Achish, King of Gath to allow he and
his men to move to a small town called Ziklag. 1 Samuel 27:7 (KJV)
"The time that David dwelt in the country of the Philistines was a
full year and four months."

Ziklag represents a hiding place or refuge outside the known will of
God! Most of the time it represents the path of least resistance.

Notice if you will, that David didn't stay in Ziklag all the time, from
time to time he would go onto Saul's turf and when the pressure was on,
he would return to Ziklag! (Sound familiar?)

After an undetermined amount of time, David was even willing to help
Achish fight Saul!

Remember, before Ziklag, David spared Saul's life on two different
occasions, saying, "I refuse to touch God's anointed!" Sixteen
months of living outside the known will of God causes one to do things
they never thought they were capable of doing! (Beware, the danger of
the gradual -- The little foxes spoil the vines)

Look closely at the results of sixteen months of hiding, retreating,
self-pity and living in fear. Ziklag burns! While David and his men were
out of town:

- The city was raided...

- Their stuff was taken...

- Their wives and children were kidnapped...

- The city was burned to the ground...

Notice the devastation of David and his men. 1 Samuel 30:3-4 (KJV)
"So David and his men came to the city, and, behold, it was burned
with fire; and their wives, and their sons, and their daughters, were
taken captives. 4 Then [David and the people that were with him lifted
up their voice and wept, until they had no more power to
weep]{.underline}." Weeping warriors seems to me to be a misnomer.
Think of this, the fiercest warriors the world had ever seen, were
weeping for their losses.

There are weeping warriors in this room today! The Lord spoke these
words to me - "There is a time for weeping but this is a time for
faith."

David immediately did what he knew would work; he went to God in prayer
about the matter. I Samuel 30:6-8 (NKJV) Now David was greatly
distressed, for the people spoke of stoning him, because the soul of all
the people was grieved, every man for his sons and his daughters. But
David strengthened himself in the LORD his God. Then David said to
Abiathar the priest, Ahimelech's son, "Please bring the ephod here to
me." And Abiathar brought the ephod to David. So [David inquired of
the LORD]{.underline}, saying, "Shall I pursue this troop? Shall I
overtake them?" [And He answered him, "Pursue, for you shall surely
overtake them and without fail recover all]{.underline}."

David decided he wanted his stuff back! The most dangerous person on the
planet is the individual who has decided he/she wants their stuff back
and is willing to do anything to get it back!

Righteous indignation is an attribute the modern-day church needs to
acquire! Passivism is the neutering of the church!

While pursuing this band of criminals David discovered an Egyptian who
had been left behind because he was sick. There is no respect among
thieves!

We must be careful how we treat people who intersect our path in our
darkest seasons, they very well may have to code to our victory!

In our darkest season the Lord will often cause our paths to intersect
with folks who don't:

- Look like us...

- Believe like us...

- Think like us...

- Live like us...

Could it be that something in the way we have done things, and thought
about things, may have contributed to the situation we find ourselves
in?

God may very well be trying to change our thinking so we can go where HE
wants us, not where we had initially planned to go on our own!

David fed and restored this man, and in turn he led them to the enemies,
and David recovered all!

David got his stuff back! What are we going to do? What do we need to
take back?

- Our joy?

- Our peace?

- Our life?

- Our family?

- Our ministry?

- Your health?

- Our wealth?

I felt the Lord prompting me to give us a prescription for success:

- Pray until we have our stuff back...

- Believe until we have our stuff back...

- Pursue until we have our stuff back...
