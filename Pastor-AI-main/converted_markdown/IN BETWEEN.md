INTRO: The phrase “In Between” 
automatically triggers a thought process that 
let’s the hearer know that something is 
unsettled about the situation, or a transition is 
going on. As individuals, we often grapple 
with a plethora of transitions throughout life. 
We have all known the agony of being in 
between with: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN 
ONE AT A TIME) 
• Relationships… 
• Health issues… 
• Financial decisions… 
• Educational processes… 
• Employment concerns… 
 
Being in between suggests that change is in the 
air and when this is the case, we are always 
hopeful the modifications will prove to be 
beneficial to our future. The greatest transition 
in the history of the world occurred on the first 

Easter and the modifications proved to be 
beneficial to our future and eternity. 
 
Easter is without a doubt the greatest day in the 
history of the world. 1 Corinthians 15:17 
(NKJV) “And if Christ is not risen, your faith 
is futile; you are still in your sins!” 
 
Today I want to take a little different look at the 
events surrounding the Easter story. The three 
contiguous days of the first Easter were of epic 
proportions, they: (PLACE ON THE 
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME) 
• Changed the paradigm of religion… 
• Changed the course of human history… 
• Changed the delivery of the Gospel 
message… 
• Changed the eternal destiny of billions of 
souls… 
 
Most Easter sermons deal with the final day of 
the story, but I want to focus on all three day’s 
beginning with the events in the Garden of 
Gethsemane. 

DAY ONE:

• Jesus had supper with His disciples… 
This meal occurred in the Upper Room in 
Southwest Jerusalem and it was here He 
revealed that one of His own would betray 
Him. 
• He faced a sleepless night in the Garden… 
He prayed while His closest friends slept. 
• He agonized before the Father in prayer 
until His sweat became as great drops of 
blood… He prayed three times, “If it be 
possible let this cup pass from me.” But 
finally He prayed, “Not my will but thine 
be done.” 
• He was betrayed by Judas and then 
deserted by all the disciples… 
• He was arrested in the Garden… 
• He was brought back to the palace of the 
High Priest… 
• He was tried by the Sanhedrin, and found 
guilty of blasphemy… 

• He was sentenced to die for a crime He 
never committed… 
• He was sent to Pontius Pilate at the 
Antonia Fortress… 
• Pilate did not find fault with Him… 
• Pilate sent Jesus to Herod who did not 
want to get involved… 
• Herod sent Jesus back to Pilate… 
• Pilate caved in to the pressure of the 
crowd and ordered Jesus to be tortured 
and crucified… 
• Jesus was beaten with thirty-nine 
stripes… 
• Jesus was required to carry His cross from 
Pilates Judgment Hall to Golgotha… It is 
believed the cross of Jesus weighed one 
hundred twenty-five pounds and He 
carried it for two and one half miles! 
 
If Jesus had caved under the pressure: (PLAC

• The enemy would have defeated the 
Fathers answer for sin… 
• The redemption process would have been 
back to square one… 

• Mankind would have continued in sin 
without a Savior to call on… 
• Evil would have continued to dominate 
Gods crowing creation, mankind… 
• The world would probably have self

behavior… 
	
  
DAY TWO – THE DAY IN BETWEEN: 
Day two is a day that is often overlooked in this 
story. Day two is a period of time that 
accomplished immeasurable results. (PLACE 
ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)	
  
• The disciples of Jesus forsook Him… 
• Joseph of Arimithea buried the body of 
Jesus in his own tomb… 
• The tomb was sealed to insure His 
interment would be final… (The 
governmental leaders were concerned that 
someone would steal His body and claim 
that He had risen… They were listening 
more closely than the Disciples… The 
soldiers were told “Make it as secure as 
you can.”) 

• As soon as the tomb was sealed Jesus laid 
aside His humanity and dawned His 
divinity… (The soldiers would have felt 
quite foolish if they had known they were 
guarding an empty tomb) 
• Jesus paid a visit to the halls of Hell… 
(He conquered the keys of death and hell) 
• He preached to the captives in paradise… 
(Those who died under law who were 
anticipating a coming Messiah) 
 
DAY 3: 
Day three begins in a cemetery, at sunrise, on 
the first Easter morning. A cemetery is not a 
place where you find a lot of hope, quite the 
contrary is true: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN 
ONE AT A TIME) 
• A cemetery is a burial ground for the 
dead… 
• A cemetery is a place where dreams are 
laid to rest… 
• A cemetery is a place where hope dims, 
flickers and dies… 
• A cemetery is a place for reflecting on 
what could have been… 

• A cemetery is a place for beautiful flowers 
to wither and die… 
• A cemetery is a place where grief deadens 
the tune of victorious songs… 
• A cemetery is a place where money loses 
its power to buy its way out… 
• A cemetery is a place where we are forced 
to accept the finality of fate… 
 
On the third day, two women could be seen 
making their way into the cemetery through the 
gray dawn shadows of a spring day. The 
women who were coming to this cemetery were 
obviously emotionally distraught and with 
painstaking effort, they made their way to the 
tomb of Jesus. 
 
Matthew draws our attention to another group 
of people in the cemetery, and they were a 
group of soldiers, who were frightened out of 
their minds. (Guy who fell into an open grave 
late one night not knowing a drunk had fallen in 
there earlier in the evening…) 
 
This cemetery on the outskirts of Jerusalem 

appeared to be the most unlikely place on the 
earth to experience a miracle. But the greatest 
miracle in human history was about to transpire 
before the unsuspecting eyes of the soldiers and 
the women! 
 
It shouldn’t surprise me, but it does: God 
performs some of His most incredible activities 
in some of the most inconspicuous places! 
(LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE 
NEXT SLIDE) 
• God chose the backside of the desert to 
defy the law of conflagration by allowing 
a bush to burn but not be consumed… 
• God chose a group of shepherds and a 
Judean hillside to display heavens choir of 
angels… 
• God chose a stable in Bethlehem to be the 
birthplace of His only Son… 
• God chose a hillside in Israel to contradict 
the law of diminishing returns by feeding 
5,000 men with a little boy’s lunch… 

The circumstances could not have been worse! 
God chose the very worst circumstances to let 
us know that nothing that happens to us is too 
difficult for Him to handle! 
 
God takes the darkest night of despair and turns 
it into a miracle for us! Psalms 30:5 (NKJV) 
“For His anger is but for a moment, His favor 
is for life; Weeping may endure for a night, 
But joy comes in the morning.” 
 
Matthew 28:2-4 (NKJV) “Behold, there was a 
great earthquake; for an angel of the Lord 
descended from heaven, and came and rolled 
back the stone from the door, and sat on it. 
 His countenance was like lightning, and his 
clothing as white as snow. And the guards 
shook for fear of him, and became like dead 
men.” 
 
Let me tell you, there were more than two 
emotionally distraught women and a couple of 
frightened soldiers present when Jesus was 
resurrected! The Scripture say’s “The angel of 
the Lord came down!” Everywhere one looks 

there are angels in this story: (PLACE ON 
THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME) 
• Angels celebrated His advent… 
• Angels sympathized with Him in His 
agony… 
• Angels comforted bereaved believers at 
His ascension… 
• Angels are schedule to share the triumph 
with Him in the apocalypse that is soon to 
come… 
 
Napoleon once said, “Extraordinary problems 
call for extraordinary remedies.” 
 
The Resurrection of Jesus meets that criterion. 
It proceeded beyond the ordinary! This was an 
extraordinary remedy, prescribed by an 
extraordinary God, who thinks nothing of going 
beyond finite limitations!
