# BENEFITS OF THE CROSS AND RESURRECTION

TEXT: 1 Corinthians 1:18 (KJV) "For the preaching of the cross
is to them that perish foolishness; but unto us which are saved it is
the power of God."

INTRO: There are two more Sunday's before resurrection Sunday.
Resurrection Sunday is the day that all of Christendom joins together to
celebrate the work Jesus completed through the agony of the cross and
the phenomenon of His resurrection.

The resurrection is not possible without the cross; likewise the cross
is of no effect without the resurrection. This is not an either or
proposition it is a both and proposal.

I want to take the next two Sunday's and talk about the cross and the
resurrection.

The significance of the resurrection is determined by the nature of the
Saviors death. The death of the Savior is elevated in human history
because of the resurrection. Without the resurrection the events of
Golgotha would just have been another execution!

Anytime one highlights the resurrection it points to a brutally beaten
and suffering Savior. The cross leaves us feeling depressed and
brutalized, and that is as far as some people ever go. The resurrection
cancels and conquers the seeming defeat of the cross.

The resurrection is to the Christian what the first sight of a new-born
baby is to a mother laying exhausted on a delivery room bed.

In our text Paul talks about "the preaching of the cross" but I
want to investigate its effects so that we discover its "power".

## I. JESUS BECAME...

2 Corinthians 5:21 (KJV) "For [he hath made him to be sin for
us]{.underline}, who knew no sin; that we might be made the
righteousness of God in him." He who had never known sin, He who had
lived a sinless life for more than thirty-three years "became sin
for us"! Pilate said "I find no fault in Him."

He became:

- Our substitute... Where I should have died, He died in my place... The
 old song say's "I should have been crucified; I should have suffered
 and died; I should have hung on the cross in disgrace; but Jesus God's
 Son took my place."

- Our sacrifice... John 1:29 (KJV) "The next day John seeth Jesus
 coming unto him, and saith, [Behold the Lamb of God, which taketh away
 the sin of the world]{.underline}." Every Jewish person standing
 on the bank of the Jordan River that day knew what the work of the
 sacrificial lamb was on the Day of Atonement. This was not just
 another lamb; this was the Lamb of God which would eradicate sin
 forever!

## II. JESUS OBTAINED...

Last week we talked about what Jesus became. this week I want to talk
about what Jesus obtained and in order to do so I want to look at three
things that occurred between the cross and the throne:

- Jesus obtained the keys of hell and death. Revelation 1:18 (KJV)
 "I am he that liveth, and was dead; and, behold, I am alive for
 evermore, Amen; and have the keys of hell and of death."

- Jesus obtained joy for the masses. From the Garden of Eden to the
 cross of Christ the one thing that seemed to be strangely absent from
 religious rituals and human existence was joy! The cross and
 resurrection changed the face of human history by adding the all
 important ingredients of hope and joy! Hebrews 12:2 (KJV) "Looking
 unto Jesus the author and finisher of our faith; who [for the joy that
 was set before him]{.underline} endured the cross, despising the
 shame, and is set down at the right hand of the throne of God."

- Jesus obtained the right to present the Gospel. 1 Corinthians 15:1-8
 (KJV) "Moreover, brethren, [I declare unto you the
 gospel]{.underline} which I preached unto you, which also [ye have
 received]{.underline}, and [wherein ye stand]{.underline}; {2} [By
 which also ye are saved]{.underline}, if ye keep in memory what I
 preached unto you, unless ye have believed in vain. {3} For I
 delivered unto you first of all that which I also received, how that
 [Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures; {4} And that he
 was buried, and that he rose again the third day according to the
 scriptures]{.underline}: {5} And that he was seen of Cephas, then of
 the twelve: {6} After that, he was seen of above five hundred brethren
 at once; of whom the greater part remain unto this present, but some
 are fallen asleep. {7} After that, he was seen of James; then of all
 the apostles. {8} And last of all he was seen of me also, as of one
 born out of due time."

Can you imagine the Gospel without the death, burial and resurrection of
our Lord? The Gospel devoid of these things would be a powerless Gospel
at its very best. If Jesus had lived His earthly life out to a ripe old
age and died of natural causes, His life would be an interesting history
lesson but nothing more.

If the tomb had remained sealed the demise of this good man would have
been unforgettable to those who witnessed it, but insignificant to
future generations.

In Jesus earthly ministry he healed the sick and raised the dead but
that is not what He came to do; His purpose on this earth was simply to
die on the cross and be raised the third day! John 12:27 (KJV) "Now
is my soul troubled; and what shall I say? Father, save me from this
hour: but [for this cause came I unto this hour]{.underline}. "

You can believe in Jesus earthly ministry and not be saved! In order to
be saved you must embrace the theology of the cross and resurrection.

## III. JESUS REMAINED...

Throughout the ordeal of the cross, tomb, and resurrection it would
appear that Jesus gave up a great deal, and He did, but I want us to
look at some things Jesus did not give up:

- His dignity... Luke 23:28 (KJV) "But Jesus turning unto them said,
 Daughters of Jerusalem, [weep not for me, but weep for yourselves, and
 for your children]{.underline}." Luke 23:34 (KJV) "Then said
 Jesus, [Father, forgive them; for they know not what they
 do]{.underline}..." He was a class act; He kept his dignity!

- His deity... Matthew 27:54 (KJV) "Now when the centurion, and they
 that were with him, watching Jesus, [saw the earthquake]{.underline},
 and those things that were done, they feared greatly, saying, [Truly
 this was the Son of God]{.underline}." Not one time during His
 life, death, burial or resurrection did Jesus lay down His deity; not
 even for one minute.

- His destiny... 1 John 3:8 (KJV) "...For this purpose the Son of
 God was manifested, [that he might destroy the works of the
 devil]{.underline}." Colossians 2:15 (KJV) "And having spoiled
 principalities and powers, [he made a show of them
 openly]{.underline}, triumphing over them in it." Mark 16:19 (KJV)
 "So then after the Lord had spoken unto them, he was received up
 into heaven, and [sat on the right hand of God]{.underline}." Acts
 2:33 (KJV) "Therefore being [by the right hand of God
 exalted]{.underline}, and having received of the Father the promise of
 the Holy Ghost, he hath shed forth this, which ye now see and
 hear." Romans 8:34 (KJV) "Who is he that condemneth? It is
 [Christ that died, yea rather, that is risen again, who is even at the
 right hand of God, who also maketh intercession for
 us]{.underline}."
