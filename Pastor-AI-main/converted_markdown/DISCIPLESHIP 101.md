LESSON 1

REPENT

WHERE IS THIS COMMAND FOUND?

Matthew 4:17 "From that time Jesus began to preach, and to say,
Repent: for the kingdom of heaven is at hand."

Applying This Command

Repentance is more than just a [one-time event]{.underline} that happens
at salvation. A pattern of repentance in a believer's life is key to
obeying the Word of God and reflecting the [character]{.underline} of
God.

Choosing to follow this command involves a daily
[commitment]{.underline} to [address]{.underline} the way we think.

Why is this significant? Our [thoughts]{.underline} determine our
[actions]{.underline}. Our actions do not primarily come from a
[whim]{.underline} of our will or our feelings. Instead, our actions are
a [byproduct]{.underline} of what we have been choosing to think about.

If we leave our thoughts unchecked, our lives will take on the
[character]{.underline} and [attributes]{.underline} of whatever
influences our lives.

As believers, [genuine change]{.underline} happens in our lives when we
fully submit our minds to thinking the thoughts of God. This takes place
as we [daily exchange]{.underline} our thoughts for His throughs,
meditating on His Word.

We should meditate in [focused, set apart times]{.underline}, and we
should meditate throughout the day. The more we soak ourselves in the
living Word of God, the more our lives will reflect His
[life]{.underline} and [character]{.underline}.

Our Heavenly Father, God [delights to supply]{.underline} the needs of

LESSON 3

FOLLOW ME

WHERE IS THIS COMMAND FOUND?

John 4:19 "And he saith unto them, Follow me, and I will make you
fishers of men."

Applying This Command

The command to [Follow Me]{.underline} is not an idea or a suggestion.
Rather, it is an [imperative call]{.underline} that must be followed by
choice.

T

his choice must be made by every person, and it will determine [the
course]{.underline} of his or her life.

While the words of Jesus in Matthew 4:19 are a command, they are also an
[invitation]{.underline} to live the abundant life of Jesus.

Christ's life should fill every area of our lives, as we fully surrender
to [His Lordship]{.underline} and follow Him wholeheartedly.

In this command, Jesus was calling His disciples to exchange their
[identity]{.underline} as fishers of fish, to become fishers of men.

When we choose to follow Christ, He replaces our old
[identity]{.underline} with a new [identity]{.underline} in Him. As we
live in the reality of this new [identity]{.underline}, He empowers us
to live the Christian life by His Spirit dwelling in us.

The key to living a fulfilled life in the Lord, and walking as fishers
of men, is first being [filled with His Life]{.underline}.  As a result,
Christ's Life [within]{.underline} us, blesses and ministers to those
[around]{.underline} us.

The more Christ [increases]{.underline} in our lives, the more we can
invest in the lives of others for [eternity]{.underline}, walking as
fishers of men.

How Can We Follow Jesus Now That He Is in Heaven?

When the disciples followed Jesus, they were with Him 24 hours a day, 7
days a week, for 3 years. They learned how He [thought]{.underline}, how
He [answered questions]{.underline}, how He [lived]{.underline}, and how
He gave Himself for people.

Jesus is now in [Heaven]{.underline}; however, He made provisions for us
to continue to be with Him 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, for the rest
of our lives. First of all; He is within every believer, as the
Scripture states: "Christ in you, the hope of glory" (Colossians 1:27).

He also sent [His Holy Spirit]{.underline} to live within us, and to
guide us in His ways. (See John 14:16--18.) Just as the disciples walked
with the Lord and did only those things that the Lord directed, so we
are to walk in the [guidance]{.underline} and power of the [Holy
Spirit]{.underline}.

The result of this fellowship is that the Lord can accomplish more
through our lives than He accomplished in His [earthly
ministry]{.underline}. "Verily, verily, I say unto you, He that
believeth on me, the works that I do shall he do also; and greater works
than these shall he do; because I go unto my Father" (John 14:12).

The more time we spend with Christ by meditating on His Word, the more
we will be able to [reflect the light]{.underline} of His truth to
others.

It is as believers fully yield control of the right to live and operate
their own life the way they want to, that Christ, through [the power of

perform His will in them.

The Christian life isn't about trying harder; it is about recognizing
our [total inability]{.underline}, and embracing Christ's ability,
through the power of His Spirit!

LESSON 6

FEAR NOT

WHERE IS THIS COMMAND FOUND?

Matthew 10:28 "Fear not them which kill the body, but are not able to
kill the soul: but rather fear him which is able to destroy both soul
and body in hell."

Applying This Command

Hundreds of times in Scripture we are instructed not to fear and not to
be [afraid]{.underline}.

At the same time, we are told to [fear the Lord]{.underline}. The same
[Hebrew and Greek words]{.underline} are used to describe both the fear
that we should not have toward man, and the fear that we must have of
the Lord.

The primary Hebrew word used for fear is [yaré.]{.underline} It
means "to revere, to frighten, to make afraid, and be dreadful."

The Greek word for fear is [phobeo ]{.underline}which means "to be
alarmed, to be frightened, to be in awe of, and to revere."

Fear is extremely important because we [bow down]{.underline} to what we
fear. If we fear man, we will [bow down]{.underline} to man and be
intimidated by him. If we fear our children, we will [bow
down]{.underline} to them, and they will control us.

But if we fear God, we will [worship]{.underline} Him and give Him His
proper [honor and reverence]{.underline}.

When David faced Goliath he had no fear, because he realized that
Goliath was not defying him or even Israel, but the [name of the
Lord]{.underline}. He was, therefore, confident that God would protect
His own [life and reputation]{.underline} by allowing him to conquer
Goliath.

He had previously built his faith by conquering a [lion and a
bear]{.underline}.

In contrast to this, David was fearful when his own son
[Absalom]{.underline} who rose up against him. He fled for his life,
because he knew that Absalom's wrath was against him personally, and
that this was part of the [judgment]{.underline} of God for his [sin
with Bathsheba]{.underline}.

Jesus did not fear people, because He understood their [thoughts and
motives]{.underline}. He did not fear Satan, because He knew He could
overcome anything [thrown in His path]{.underline}. Jesus did not fear
[death]{.underline}, because He had already purposed to give His life
for the salvation of all who believe on Him.

When we offend someone, naturally our first response is to
[justify]{.underline} ourselves rather than seeking
[reconciliation]{.underline}. If we are at fault or not, this type of
attitude is focused on one thing: [self]{.underline}. At its root, this
selfish focus is fueled by a spirit of [pride]{.underline}. It is our

Pride is [self-confident]{.underline}, [self-focused]{.underline}

We don't just need each other, we cannot accomplish [God's

This is why the command [Be Reconciled]{.underline} is so
important.

As believers in Jesus Christ, God's Word says that we are members
["one of another"]{.underline} in the Body of Christ (Romans

Our actions not only affect us; they impact those [around
us]{.underline}---especially those closest to us. It is only as we
humble ourselves and seek to make past offenses right that we will
maintain [close fellowship]{.underline} with God and fulfill His

We are commanded by Jesus to be [disciples]{.underline}, making

John 14:15 "If ye love me, keep my commandments."

Applying This Command

This same word is used to describe the care that the Philippian jailer

LESSON 16

BE BORN AGAIN

WHERE IS THIS COMMAND FOUND?

John 3:5-7 "Jesus answered, Verily, verily, I say unto thee, Except a
man be born of water and of the Spirit, he cannot enter into the kingdom
of God. That which is born of the flesh is flesh; and that which is born
of the Spirit is spirit. Marvel not that I said unto thee, Ye must be
born again."

Applying This Command

Jesus describes our salvation as being [born again]{.underline}.
Nicodemus questioned how one could be born again as an adult. Jesus
explained that we must be born of water and of the spirit to be able to
inherit the Kingdom of God:

- Water -- The [natural]{.underline} birth

- Spirit -- The [spiritual]{.underline} birth

A marvelous transformation occurs in the life of a person who is [born
again]{.underline}. Paul explains the change in this way, "If any man
be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold,
all things are become new." (II Corinthians 5:17).

The word "become" is in the perfect indicative tense, which indicates
that the action has already been completed in the [past]{.underline} but
has continuing results in the [future]{.underline}. This emphasizes two
[aspects]{.underline} of our salvation. The first is our
[position]{.underline} in Christ. The second is our
[experience]{.underline} as we grow in Christ.

A new believer takes on the [righteousness of Christ]{.underline} and is
righteous in Him. However, he is to exercise the energy of the Holy
Spirit in carrying out [righteous deeds]{.underline}. We are holy in our
[position]{.underline} before God, but we are also instructed to
"perfect holiness in the fear of the Lord." (See I Peter 1:16 and
II Corinthians 7:1.)

When God's life flows into us through the [new birth]{.underline}, there
will be several clear indications that something wonderful has taken
place. For some people, the evidence of new birth will be
[immediate]{.underline} and [dramatic]{.underline}; with others, they
may develop [more slowly]{.underline}.

- We become less like the [world]{.underline} and more like
 [Jesus]{.underline}.

- We desire [spiritual]{.underline} things over [temporal]{.underline}
 things.

- We love like [Jesus]{.underline} and stop [hating]{.underline} on

LESSON 17

EAT AND DRINK

WHERE IS THIS COMMAND FOUND?

Mattthew 26:26-28 "As they were eating, Jesus took bread, and blessed
it, and broke it, and gave it to the disciples, and said, Take, eat;
this is my body. And he took the cup, and gave thanks, and gave it to
them, saying, Drink ye all of it; for this is my blood of the new
testament, which is shed for many for the remission of sins."

Applying This Command

It was at the [communion table]{.underline} that Jesus revealed Himself
as the sacrificial Lamb. There is no other [sacrifice]{.underline}.

It was also at this table that Jesus revealed the [true
nature]{.underline} of His disciples: one would [betray
Him]{.underline}, all would [forsake Him]{.underline}. One, Peter, would
deny Him [three times,]{.underline} before the night was over.

Jesus ate the [Passover]{.underline} with His disciples before He was
crucified. The act of [breaking bread]{.underline} is an intimate time
where much can be brought to light. Such was the case in this
[Passover]{.underline} meal.

Jesus set the example for us to come together around the [communion
table]{.underline}, and Paul reminded the Corinthians, and us, to
continue this discipline.

Paul admonishes us to [examine ourselves]{.underline} when we come to
the communion table. The communion table should be viewed as regularly
planned stops, where we take the time to remember what Jesus did for us,
and to have a time of [solitary introspection]{.underline}.

Paul also tells us to continue doing this on a regular basis, "unil
He comes."

LESSON 18

AWAIT HIS RETURN

WHERE IS THIS COMMAND FOUND?

Matthew 24:42-44 "Watch therefore: for ye know not what hour your
Lord doth come. But know this, that if the goodman of the house had
known in what watch the thief would come, he would have watched, and
would not have suffered his house to be broken up. Therefore be ye also
ready: for in such an hour as ye think not the Son of man cometh."

Applying This Command

This command does not instruct us to know when Christ will return, but
rather to be ready for His return, at any [moment]{.underline}.

All the debate and division among believers over the timing of [Christ's
Second Coming]{.underline} detracts attention from the great truths that
we are to learn from this coming event.

This command makes it clear that no one knows the [precise
timing]{.underline} of His return. The Scripture makes it clear that not
only does man not know when Jesus will return, but that the
[angels]{.underline} nor [Christ]{.underline} know either. If the
[angels]{.underline} and [Jesus]{.underline} do not know the timing of
the [Second Coming]{.underline}, one can be assured that we do not know.

The point is, that we should be living as if He is coming today! When we
live as if His coming could occur at any time, it causes us to keep all
matters right with [God]{.underline} and [man]{.underline}.

If we believe His return is [imminent,]{.underline} we should be working
to reach each of our friends and family. The fact that Christ could
return at any moment should [motivate]{.underline} us to make the very
best use of every minute that we have.

There is much to do for the Lord, and we can labor only until the time
He [returns]{.underline}. At that point, we must give an
[account]{.underline} of how we used all the [time and
resources]{.underline} that He entrusted to us.

Based on the shortness of time and the evil in the world, Paul wrote,
"See then that ye walk circumspectly, not as fools, but as wise,
redeeming the time, because the days are evil. Wherefore be ye not
unwise, but understanding what the will of the Lord is." (Ephesians
5:15--17).

Paul repeats this instruction in Colossians 4:5, "Walk in wisdom
toward them that are without, redeeming the time." To redeem a
[coupon]{.underline} is to use it and stop it from going to waste.

In light of His Second Coming, what are we doing to redeem the [time and
reso]{.underline}urces He has placed in our hands?

LESSON 19

LOVE THE LORD WITH ALL YOUR HEART

WHERE IS THIS COMMAND FOUND?

Matthew 22:37-38 "Jesus said unto him, Thou shalt love the Lord thy
God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind.
This is the first and great commandment."

Applying This Command

Jesus identified this command as the [first and greatest]{.underline}
one of all. Nothing will ever be more important to [God]{.underline}
than for us to love Him with all our hearts. Nothing should ever be more
important to us, than [showing]{.underline} our love for God.

Jesus follows the first and greatest commandment by saying, the [second
commandment]{.underline} is to "Love your neighbor as ourselves."
He then states, "On these two commandments hang all the Law and the
prophets."

This is big - every other command or teaching in the Bible is simply an
[application]{.underline} of these two commands. All other Scriptures
must therefore be interpreted on the basis of how they teach us to [love
God]{.underline} and [one another]{.underline}.

First of all; loving God means that we must respond to His marvelous
grace by devoting all our [affections]{.underline}, our
[thoughts]{.underline}, our [wills]{.underline}, and our
[strength]{.underline} to Him. It means that we dedicate our bodies to
Him, and purpose to lay down our lives for Him, and the advancement of
His [kingdom]{.underline}.

It means that we detect and renounce every competing
[affection]{.underline}, such as loving money, loving the world, or
loving our own lives, because we know that to love these things more
than we love God, is to make us, and them an enemy of God.

LESSON 20

LOVE THY NEIGHBOR

WHERE IS THIS COMMAND FOUND?

Matthew 22:39-40 "Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy
heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind" And

the second \[commandment\] is like unto it, Thou shalt love thy
neighbor as thyself. On these two commandments hang all the law and the
prophets."

Applying This Command

In this passage of scripture, a [lawyer]{.underline} asked Jesus to name
the most important commandment, Jesus stated, "Thou shalt love the
Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy
mind" (Matthew 22:37). The previous Scripture stands to reason --
right? Of course!

However, Jesus didn't stop there, He continues by saying, "... the
second commandment is like unto it..." The Greek word translated for
"like unto it" is "homoios", which means "[resembling, such as,
the same as.]{.underline}"

Jesus' statement is a very significant statement, because it means that
when we love our neighbor, we are actually [proving]{.underline} that we
love God.

"Thou shalt love thy neighbor as thyself." There is solid Biblical
support for this concept because we are all made in the [image of
God]{.underline}. Therefore, if we [despise or curse]{.underline} a
fellow man, we are also despising and cursing God.

James also makes this connection when he writes about the
[tongue]{.underline}. James 3:9 "Therewith bless we God, even the
Father; and therewith curse we men, which are made after the similitude
of God."

In Matthew 25:40 Jesus said, "Inasmuch as ye have done it unto one of
the least of these my brethren, ye have done it unto me"

God created every person in the world, and one day everyone will stand
before [God's throne]{.underline} to be [judged]{.underline}. However,
God loves all humans so much that He sent His only begotten Son to die
for our sins, so that they would be [spared His judgment]{.underline}.

If God loves every human being so much that He was willing to give His
only begotten son for us, how then should we [treat one
another]{.underline}?
