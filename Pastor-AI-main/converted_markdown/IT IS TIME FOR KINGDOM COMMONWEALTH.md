INTRO: All kingdoms function on the principle of a commonwealth.
Commonwealth is the king's commitment to see that all of his citizens
have equal access to the wealth and resources of the kingdom.

Equal access to the wealth and resources is not the same as socialism.
The idea of socialism involves wealth distribution: Taking from the
wealthy and giving to the poor. The idea of socialism on the surface
seems plausible or even reasonable. But when you dig into it, many
problems begin the manifest:

- It allows for laziness... Why work? If ever one is going to be given
 everything anyway, why work?

- It taps down the drive of those who are industrious... If one is not
 going to benefit from hard work or witty ideas why bother?

- It eventually brings everyone down and the whole society collapses...
 (This is a well- documented fact)

reputation of the king. When the welfare of the king's citizens is
excellent, then the king's reputation among other kings is honorable.
When the welfare of the citizens of the kingdom is not good, the
reputation of the king is diminished.

In a kingdom the king is personally committed to, and involved in, the

THE KINGDOM TAXATION PRINCIPLE

All kingdoms incorporate a taxation system, which allows its citizens to
participate in the process of maintaining the kingdom infrastructure.
"Bring all the tithe into the storehouse that there may be resources
at my house." The Kingdom taxation principle allows the citizen to
share in the kingdom's commonwealth, and return a set portion of the
king's resources back to the king.

In essence, everything in a kingdom already belongs to the king,
including the taxes required from the citizen, therefore taxation is
simply the government's allowing its resources to pass through the hands
of the citizen. 

Matthew 22:17 (NKJV) "Tell us then, what is Your opinion? Is it right

CREATIVE DEVELOPMENT PROGRAMS FOR KINGDOM CITIZENS

Who we give to matters! No farmer sows seed unless and until he knows
what kind of soil he is sowing it into! Giving is one thing, but to give
to a King is a completely different level altogether! Giving to a King
is the thing creative development programs are made of. When we give to
something, we become a part of it! When we give to a King, we become a
part of all that His Kingdom entails.

Giving to a King activates the King's obligation to demonstrate his
glory and power to the giver. When one gives to a King, that King
automatically attempts to demonstrate to the giver that He is a greater
King than all other Kings.

When a citizen gives to a King, that citizen is acknowledging that all
things belong to that King, and the citizen is grateful.

Because giving to King Jesus is impossible (Since all things already
belong to the king), the act of giving benefits us more than it does the
King.

Something one is never to do, is to come before a King
empty-handed. Often, we have come before the King to get what we have
need of, and we do so empty-handed, offering noting of worth. This
should never be. We must NEVER come before our King with empty hands!

This is perfectly illustrated when the Queen of Sheba made an appearance
before Solomon. 1 Kings 10:10 (NKJV) "And she gave the king 120
talents of gold, large quantities of spices, and precious stones. Never
again were so many spices brought in as those the queen of Sheba gave to
King Solomon." We shouldn't just bring a gift to the King we should
bring the best and highest. (This is a real estate term) 

King Solomon was greater in riches and wisdom than all the other kings
of the earth, including the Queen of Sheba. The whole world sought
audience with Solomon to hear the wisdom God had put in his heart. Year
after year, everyone who came brought a gift, articles of silver, gold,
robes, weapons, spices, horses and livestock. They brought the best they
had! Are we bringing the best we have?

Even though Solomon was richer than the Queen of Sheba, she still
brought the best offering she could manage. Coming before a King with a
great, premeditated offering, is a thing of honor -- To not do so is a
thing of dishonor. (Leave your sick sacrifices outside -- they are
stinking the place up -- David at the threshing floor of Araunah the
Jebusite "I refuse to give to my God of that which cost me
nothing.")
