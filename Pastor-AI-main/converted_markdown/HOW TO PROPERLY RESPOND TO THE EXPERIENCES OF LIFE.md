## I. DURING HAPPY EXPERIENCES WE SHOULD PRAISE GOD!

James 1:17 (NKJV) "[Every good gift and every perfect gift is from
above]{.underline}, and comes down from the Father of lights, with whom
there is no variation or shadow of turning."

Life is filled with many experiences. No roller coaster in the world can
offer the exhilarating highs and the depressing lows, and everything in
between which one will experience during our journey through life. While
there are countless experiences in life, it is my opinion that the good
far outweighs the bad. I choose to be an optimist and see the glass half
full rather than half empty. I want us to look at some of the happy
experiences in life and how we can properly respond during those times.

Happy experiences in life include: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A
TIME)

- Holidays...

- Birthdays...

- Vacations...

- Family times...

- Educational accomplishments...

- Marriage...

- Anniversaries...

- The birth of a child...

- The marriage of a child...

- Retirement...

I realize this is not by any means an exhaustive list, but these events
certainly bring about some happy times. The response we display during
happy times will determine many things about our future: (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- It charts a course going forward...

- It develops a measuring stick for all other experiences...

- It determines the perception of others toward us...

Our lives will be defined by the sum total of the choices we make. When
we die, no minister will be a good enough orator to change the legacy we
have created by our choices. Proverbs 22:1 (NKJV) "A good name is to
be chosen rather than great riches."

Albert Schweitzer said, "Success is not the key to happiness. Happiness
is the key to success. If you love what you are doing, you will be
successful."

Though many happy moments will no doubt occur in our lives, the one
thing we never want to do is gloat or become braggadocios with regard to
these events. The reason we do not want to ever do this is because in so
doing we take the credit, when the glory really belongs to God.

The proper response to happy times is to give God praise. When we give
God praise for the good things which have happened to us, we acknowledge
that we are totally reliant upon Him for all of the blessings of life.
James 1:17 (NKJV) "[Every good gift and every perfect gift is from
above]{.underline}, and comes down from the Father of lights, with whom
there is no variation or shadow of turning." .

Think about this, without the help of the Lord: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN
ONE AT A TIME)

- We would not have a family to celebrate the good times with...

- We would not have the mental capacity to acquire an education which
 would support us in life...

- We would not have a church family to keep us encouraged in the
 difficult times...

- We would be bound for eternal separation from the creator of the
 universe...

- We would have no hope beyond this life...

If it were not for the Lord working in our lives some of the experiences
of life would eliminate the joy of the journey. True joy comes from the
Lord. Benjamin Franklin once said, "The Constitution only guarantees
the American people the right to pursue happiness.  You have to catch it
yourself."

There is an old song which say's "Without Him I could do nothing.
Without Him I'd surely fail. Without Him I would be drifting, like a
ship without a sail."

Think about how devastating it would be to not have a personal
relationship with Jesus! Would there be any true joy at all in our
lives? I seriously doubt that we would!

I'm sure all of us greatly appreciate all the good times, but we need to
be careful to properly respond by giving Him all of the praise!

## III. IN QUITE EXPERIENCES WE SHOULD WORSHIP GOD!

Matthew 11:28-30 (NKJV) "Come to Me, all you who labor and are heavy
laden, and [I will give you rest]{.underline}. "Take My yoke upon
you and learn from Me, for I am gentle and lowly in heart, and [you will
find rest for your souls]{.underline}. "For My yoke is easy and My
burden is light."

In this series of sermons we have been looking at how we should respond
when life comes at us with all kinds of experiences and challenges. The
life experiences we have discussed and the proper responses are:
(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- In happy experiences we should praise God...

- In difficult experiences we should seek God...

Today we are going to talk about worshipping God in the quiet times
experiences of life.

Of all the experiences of life, quiet times may be the times which are
the rarest of all times. I would like to define the quiet times of life
in this way:

"Quiet times are when nothing is going wrong; nothing is going overly
well; everything is on an even keel." (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE
NEXT SLIDE)

One can create times of rest, but God occasionally leads us into
purposeful times of rest. These are periodic stages which we will
identify as quiet places. It is my opinion that if there is not a
certain amount of quiet times in our lives we would self-destruct. The
human body is not capable of continually carrying a tremendous load of
stress forever; and it is not capable of living in a constant state of
euphoria. Quiet times are essential for our physical and spiritual
health and well being. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE)

Jesus was well aware of this and He addresses this in our text. Jesus is
saying: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- If we are carrying a heavy burden we can bring it to Him...

- When we take our burden to the Lord He always provides support...

- When our burdens are cast upon the Lord we find rest for our souls...

- He will bear our burdens and assist us in not over-encumbering
 ourselves...

There is an old song which goes something like this; "Leave it there.
Leave it there. Take your burden to the Lord and leave it there. If
you'll trust and never doubt, He will surely bring you out. Take your
burden to the Lord and leave it there."

I want to take a quick look at what Jesus is saying when He say's;
"You will find rest for your souls."

Man is made up of: (PLACE ON THESCREN ONE AT A TIME)

- Body... This is the physical structure of who we are. It is referred
 to in Scripture as a; house; temple; house of this tabernacle; and
 even a tent.

- Spirit... The spirit of a person is the God conscious part of who we
 are. The spirit is what is made right at conversion, not the body or
 soul. The battle all of us face beginning at conversion is that the
 spirit is made right and a war ensues as the spirit attempts to bring
 the body and soul into line with the Word of God and this new life in
 Christ.

- Soul... The soul is our mind, our will and our emotions. The soul is
 that part of us which possesses the ability to touch; taste; feel;
 see; smell; reason; these are all a part of the soul. We could say
 that the soul is the sensual part of a person.

The truth is; I live in a body; I possess a spirit; I am a living soul.
It is interesting to me that Jesus said, "You will find rest for your
souls." Think of this: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- When the body has had enough it WILL demand rest...

<!-- -->

- When the spirit has become depleted it will drive one to his knees...

- When the soul has been overloaded it usually requires a conscious
 decision on the part of the individual to seek rest...

Trust me, soul rest is not only nice and welcomed, it is a necessary
part of healthy living both physically and spiritually.

Elbert Hubbard said, "The man who doesn\'t relax and hoot a few hoots
voluntarily, now and then, is in great danger of hooting hoots and
standing on his head for the edification of the psychiatrist and trained
nurse, a little later on."

The Lord leads us into places of periodic rest; he has placed some
roadside rest stops on the highway to heaven! The Psalmist phrased it in
this way, Psalms 23:1-3 (CEV) "You, [Lord]{.smallcaps}, are my
shepherd.I will never be in need. [You let me rest]{.underline} in
fields of green grass. [You lead me to streams of peaceful
water]{.underline}, and [you refresh my life]{.underline}.You are
true to your name, and [you lead me along the right
paths]{.underline}." According to this Scripture God intentionally
provides: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Times of rest...

- Times of peace...

- Times of refreshing...

> Why would God provide such things as rest, peace, and refreshing?
> (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- He provides such times in order to re-connect us with what is really
 important in life...

- He makes the rest stops available to keep us on the right path...

Have you ever taken a long trip and because you were driving far more
hours than what would be considered safe, you made a wrong turn or could
not remember certain parts of the trip? This is what can happen to us
without some quiet times. (Florida trip)

Most of us have a tendency to create new activity during quiet times,
simply because we do not know what to do with ourselves during such
times! Jennifer Yane said, "I try to take one day at a time, but
sometimes several days attack me at once."

We should maximize the precious moments of quiet times which come from
the Lord by taking the time to worship Him! A.W. Tozer said, "Without
worship, we go about miserable." (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT
SLIDE."

We should use the quiet time to draw close to God through worship! Jack
Hayford said, "Worship changes the worshipper into the image of the one
worshipped." How can we see to it that this happens?

- During the quiet times we do not have the other competing
 distractions; we should turn our focus on the Lord and allow Him to
 fill our thoughts...

- During the quiet times we are not so encumbered with the cares of life
 and we should take some time to be alone in His presence and just
 communicate with Him through His Word...

- During the quiet times we are not being pulled into every decision
 that is going on in the world, and we should take some time to worship
 through prayer.

- During the quiet times our mind is clear of some of the other
 obstructions of life, and we should take some time to sing to the Lord
 both with songs of inspiration and a new song to the Lord...

Someone said, "The mark of a successful man is one that has spent an
entire day on the bank of a river without feeling guilty about it."

Remember, Jesus took time to rest and we must as well! God even rested
from His labor of building and populating the world! Genesis 2:1-3
(NKJV) "Thus the heavens and the earth, and all the host of them,
were finished. And on the seventh day [God ended His
work]{.underline} which He had done, and [He rested on the seventh day
from all His work]{.underline} which He had done. Then [God blessed
the seventh day and sanctified it]{.underline}, because in it [He rested
from all His work]{.underline} which God had created and made." We
would do well to remember that resting is still a commandment of God; in
fact, it is the fourth commandment to be exact! Exodus 20:8-11 (NKJV)
"[Remember the Sabbath day, to keep it holy]{.underline}. Six days
you shall labor and do all your work, but [the seventh day is the
Sabbath of the [Lord]{.smallcaps} your God]{.underline}. In it [you
shall do no work]{.underline}: you, nor your son, nor your daughter, nor
your male servant, nor your female servant, nor your cattle, nor your
stranger who is within your gates. For in six days the
[Lord]{.smallcaps} made the heavens and the earth, the sea, and all that
is in them, and [rested the seventh day]{.underline}. Therefore [the
[Lord]{.smallcaps} blessed the Sabbath day and hallowed
it.]{.underline}" There is more verbiage with regard to this day of
rest than any of the other commandments!

Man was not genetically engineered for a long shelf life if he does not
have quiet times and times of rest, relaxation, and refreshing! If we
want to be effective over the long haul we must welcome, enjoy, and
learn to worship in the quiet times.

Finally, remember the following things about the quiet times: (PLACE
ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- We should welcome quiet times...

- We should learn to enjoy quiet times...

- We should learn to create quiet times...

- We should resist activity during quiet times...

- We should not forget to worship during quiet times...

## IV. IN PAINFUL EXPERIENCES WE SHOULD TRUST GOD!

Proverbs 3:5-6 (NKJV) "[Trust in the [Lord]{.smallcaps} with all your
heart]{.underline}, And lean not on your own understanding; In all
your ways acknowledge Him, And He shall direct your paths."

In this series of sermons we have been looking at how we should respond
when life comes at us with all kinds of experiences and challenges. The
life experiences we have discussed and the proper responses are:
(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- In happy experiences we should praise God...

- In difficult experiences we should seek God...

- In quiet experiences we should worship God...

Today we are going to talk about trusting God in the painful experiences
of life.

Pain is an emotion which if not properly dealt with can dull the senses
and leave one critical or worse yet, bitter. None of us can afford to
have this happen to us so it is imperative that we learn to properly
respond to pain. The proper response to pain is to trust God. This is
probably the most difficult thing to do of any response we could have,
but we can be assured it is the most beneficial response. Trusting God
in painful experiences is the right thing to do for: (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Ourselves...

- Our family...

- Our church...

- Our acquaintances...

- Our walk with God...

Pain can be brought into our lives from many sources:

- Our family can create pain...

- We can create pain for ourselves...

- Friends and acquaintances can create pain...

- Accidents and health incidents can create pain...

- Unforeseen financial difficulties can create pain...

More times than not, painful experiences come upon us quite suddenly and
unexpectedly.

Because painful experiences seem to come upon us without prior notice
they present challenges which are extremely difficult to handle. The
Word of God addresses the issue of unexpected painful experiences.
Proverbs 3:25-26 (KJV) "Do not be afraid of [sudden
terror]{.underline}, Nor of trouble from the wicked when it comes; 
For the [Lord]{.smallcaps} will be your confidence, And will keep your
foot from being caught."

There are two ways for the child of God to respond in times of
unexpected painful experiences: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Panic... This response helps no one and even serves to complicate the
 matter. 2 Timothy 1:7 (NKJV) "God has not given us [a spirit of
 fear]{.underline}, but of power and of love and of a sound mind."
 (My Mom & Dad's response when my sister was bitten by a poisonous
 snake)

- Trust... It is for certain the situation will not be made better
 through panic, so the proper response is to trust the Lord. Trust is
 the demonstration of faith! (My Dad's response when my sister was
 bitten by a poisonous snake)

The fact is that all of us will deal with a certain amount of pain in
our lives, how we deal with it will determine our future and will also
establish how others see us.

Trusting God is easier to talk about than to do. (PLAY GEICO
COMMERCIAL HERE)

In her book "Gateway to Joy, Elisabeth Elliot said, "God is God.
Because He is God, He is worthy of my trust and obedience. I will find
rest nowhere but in His holy will; a will that is unspeakably beyond my
largest notions of what He is up to." (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE
NEXT SLIDE) \

The reason it is so difficult for us to trust God is because human help
is right at our fingertips! Think of this: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT
A TIME)

- If we are having family problems we can call a counselor...

- If we are having financial problems we can call a banker...

- If we are having emotional problems we can call a psychiatrist or
 psychologist...

- If we are having problems finding a job we can call a head hunter...

I am not personally opposed to human help and resources but I think that
because of its availability and accessibility we often miss out on the
adventure of trusting God! Painful experiences usually place us in a
position of making a decision of trusting man or trusting God! I think I
would rather take my chances on God! Vernon Paterson said, "There are
always uncertainties ahead, but there is always one certainty\--God\'s

## V. IN EVERY EXPERIENCE WE SHOULD THANK GOD!\
1 Thessalonians 5:16-18 (NKJV) "Rejoice always, pray without
ceasing, in everything give thanks; for this is the will of God in
Christ Jesus for you."

In this series of sermons we have been looking at how we should respond
when life comes at us with all kinds of experiences and challenges. The
life experiences we have discussed and the proper responses are:
(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- In happy experiences we should praise God...

- In difficult experiences we should seek God...

- In quiet experiences we should worship God...

Today we are going to talk about trusting God in the painful experiences
of life.

asing, [in everything give thanks]{.underline}; for this is the
will of God in Christ Jesus for you."

While all of these experiences are real and come with their own set of
unique challenges, the one common thread which should run the length and
breadth of them is the fact that we are going to give thanks to God in
all the experiences of life!

In writing to the Thessalonians the Apostle lets them know that they are
to "Rejoice always" and "Give thanks in everything!" What?
Could he really mean what he is saying? What about the difficult
experiences? What about the painful experiences? Surely he did not mean
to "Rejoice" and "Give thanks" during times such as these!
Did he?

The Apostle Paul had full knowledge of the perplexities which life can
throw at us! 2 Corinthians 11:23-28 (NKJV) "I am more: in labors more
abundant, in stripes above measure, in prisons more frequently, in
deaths often. From the Jews five times I received forty stripes
minus one. Three times I was beaten with rods; once I was stoned;
three times I was shipwrecked; a night and a day I have been in the
deep; in journeys often, in perils of waters, in perils of robbers,
in perils of my own countrymen, in perils of the Gentiles, in perils in
the city, in perils in the wilderness, in perils in the sea, in perils
among false brethren; in weariness and toil, in sleeplessness often,
in hunger and thirst, in fastings often, in cold and nakedness---
besides the other things, what comes upon me daily: my deep concern
for all the churches." Anyone want to match experiences with Paul?
Yet he is the same one who said, "Rejoice always... Give thanks in
all things."

After Paul had endured all that the devil, life, the religious
hierarchy, as well as the suspicion which the church had thrown into his
path, how could he say such a thing?

Everything in our lives comes down to two things: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN
ONE AT A TIME)

- Either we believe our lives are in the hand of God or we don't...

- We place our faith in Him or we attempt to control our own destiny...

When we decide to trust God we must come to the place where we are
content to leave the details of the journey to Him and Him alone!

Psalms 37:23-25 (NKJV) "[The steps of a good man are ordered by the
[Lord]{.smallcaps}]{.underline}[,]{.smallcaps} And He delights in his
way. Though he fall, he shall not be utterly cast down; For the
[Lord]{.smallcaps} upholds him with His hand. I have been young,
and now am old; Yet I have not seen the righteous forsaken, Nor his
descendants begging bread."

Life becomes much simpler and less complicated when we come to the
understanding that God is in charge of our lives, and nothing can happen
to us apart from His permission! William Culbertson said, "Keep
praying, but be thankful that God\'s answers are wiser than your
prayers!" (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE)

One cannot reach a place of "Rejoicing always" and "Giving
thanks in everything" until we come to a place where our lives are
resting completely in the hands of the Lord! Isaiah 26:3 (NKJV) "You
will keep him in [perfect peace]{.underline}, whose [mind is stayed on
You,]{.underline} Because he [trusts in You]{.underline}."

Paul gives us a little insight into the secret of reaching a point where
no matter what experience in life we happen to be navigating, we can
"Rejoice always" and "Give thanks in everything."
Philippians 4:11-13 (NKJV) "I have learned in whatever state I am,
[to be content]{.underline}: I know how to be abased, and I know how
to abound. Everywhere and in all things I have learned both to be full
and to be hungry, both to abound and to suffer need. I can do all
things through Christ who strengthens me."

One of the greatest compliments we could ever give to God is to become
so contented with our lives that we never hit the panic button! True
contentment in the life of the Christian expresses: (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Total dependence on God...

- Trust in God to work things out...

- Faith toward God for everything...

When we reach this point in our walk with God, "Rejoicing always"
and "Giving thanks in everything" trusting God is no longer
something one has to work at, but trust and faith flows freely from the
inner person!

Remember everything that Paul endured? Listen to what he say's in Acts
20:24: (NKJV) "But [none of these things move me]{.underline}; nor do
I count my life dear to myself, so that I may finish my race with joy,
and the ministry which I received from the Lord Jesus, to testify to the
gospel of the grace of God."

The recipe for living a life of "Rejoicing always" and "Giving
thanks in everything" is simply: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A
TIME)

- Use an extra large portion of dependence upon God...

- Apply an equal measure of trust in the Lord...

- Stir in a pinch or two of faith for every situation we face...

- Mix thoroughly with enough persistence for good consistency...

- Let all this settle until "Rejoicing" and "Thanksgiving"
 begin to emerge!
