DEFINITION OF MIRACLES, SIGNS AND WONDERS

These three words, "Miracles, Signs and Wonders" are frequently
found together in the New Testament.

The meanings of these three words are different, but because they are so
often used together, sometimes people mistakenly believe their meanings
are interchangeable, and that they are one and the same.

"Miracles, Signs, and Wonders" speak about three different aspects
of the nature and power of God. While the function of these three words
are complementary, together they bring about the full impact of who God
is, and what He does.

The Greek word translated as "miracles" is (δύναμις) dunamis, and
it means "a deed manifesting great power with the implication of some
supernatural force." Oftentimes we find it translated in the New
Testament as "power, mighty works, and strength."

From the Greek word (δύναμις) dunamis, we get our English word dynamite.
The miracle working power of God shows His mighty hand, and his enormous
power.

The Greek word translated as "sign" is (σημεῖον) semeion, and it
means "a sign, a mark; by which a person or thing is distinguished from
others and is known; an event which is regarded as having some special
meaning; not simply a miraculous event but something which points to a
reality of even greater significance."

The concept of the word "sign" means much more than a symbol. The
word embodies a sense of something unique in what it points to, so that
there is no ambiguity in what is being represented.

The Greek word translated as "wonder" is (τέρας) teras, and it
means "a marvel." It speaks to the wonder or awe that is produced in
those who witness the miracle, or the dunamis.

Therefore:

- A miracle is the supernatural power of God being manifest in the
 natural realm...

- A sign points up the fact that no one but God could have done such a
 thing...

- A wonder is the awe that is produced in the hearts of the people as
 they witness the miracle...

All three together, miracles, signs, and wonders give a complete
revelation of the supernatural power of God and are utilized in turning
our hearts, and the hearts of others to God. (Story of Tanaya in India
-- God raised the child to life (Miracle) -- There was no doubting who
was at work (Sign) -- The hearts of the people were turned to God
(Wonder).

produced miracles, signs and wonders, then He was to be believed -- If
He did not produce miracles, signs and wonders, then He was not to be
believed.

Miracles, signs and wonders would be the acid test of whether He had a
supernatural order of ministry that went beyond the dead, ritualistic,
stagnate, ministry seen in the normal synagogue.

Friends, I believe this is also going to be the test of the
modern-day-church! If we have miracles, signs, and wonders then we are

followed.

Acts, Chapter 8, provides a clear and stark contrast between the signs
produced by the Holy Spirit and those produced by Satan.

Samaria. In so doing, he had convinced all the people that he was
operating in "the great power of God." Then Philip came to this
same city, and began to preach the Gospel and performed miracles. He
healed the sick and cast out demons.

 

What is noteworthy in this story is that once Philip arrived and began
to minister by the power of the Holy Spirit and preach the Gospel, the
entire population of the city, including Simon, believed his message.

Both Simon and Philip performed miracles, so how was it that no one
questioned which of the two was right? Everyone immediately knew they
had been deceived by Simon's miracles and that Philip's message,
ministry, and miracles were true.

The key to distinguishing between one operating by the power of the Holy
Spirit, and one operating by demonic powers, is found in the signs.

"He (Simon) had astonished them with his sorceries for a long

Philip's ministry produced freedom, changed lives, and brought great
