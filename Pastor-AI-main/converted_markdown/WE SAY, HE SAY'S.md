# WE SAY, HE SAY'S

TEXT: Proverbs 3:5-6 (NLT) "Trust in the [Lord]{.smallcaps} with
all your heart; do not depend on your own understanding. Seek his
will in all you do, and he will direct your paths." (KJV) "Trust
in the Lord with all thine heart, lean not unto your own understanding,
in all your ways acknowledge Him and He shall direct thy paths."

INTRO: The way God sees things and the way we see things are very
different. God's thoughts and our thoughts are more times than not on
two different wave lengths:

- God functions in reality, we operate in perception.

- God functions in actuality, we operate in observation.

- God functions in truth, we operate by fact.

- God functions in faith, we walk by sight.

When we see things differently than God sees them we often speak out of
what we see rather than what we are supposed to know. This kind of
living causes us to say one thing while God is saying something
completely different which leads to misunderstandings and difficulty in
our lives.

Prayer and faith carry with them the ability to move what we need, from
the Heavenly realm to the natural realm. "Life and death is in the
power of the tongue."

I want us to look at some instances where God and the believer are often
speaking words which are in sharp opposition to each other.

When confronted with difficulty, we say: "It's impossible."

God says: Luke 18:27 (NLT) "What is impossible from a human
perspective is possible with God."

When we are weary, we say: "I am too tired."

God says: Matthew 11:28-30 (NLT) "Come to me, all of you who are
weary and carry heavy burdens, and I will give you rest. Take my
yoke upon you. Let me teach you, because I am humble and gentle, and you
will find rest for your souls. For my yoke fits perfectly, and the
burden I give you is light."

When we have been betrayed in a relationship we say: "Nobody really
loves me."\
God says: John 3:16 (NLT) "For God so loved the world that he gave
his only Son, so that everyone who believes in him will not perish but
have eternal life." John 13:34-35 (NLT) "I am giving you a new
commandment: Love each other. Just as I have loved you, you should love
each other. Your love for one another will prove to the world that
you are my disciples."

When we have exhausted our resources we say: "I

can\'t go on."

God says: 2 Corinthians 12:9 (NLT) "My gracious

favor is all you need. My power works best in your

weakness." Psalms 91:15 (NLT) "When they call

on me, I will answer; I will be with them in trouble. I

will rescue them and honor them."

When we do not know which way to turn we say: "What should I do?"

God says: Proverbs 3:5-6 (NLT) "Trust in the [Lord]{.smallcaps} with
all your heart; do not depend on your own understanding. Seek his
will in all you do, and he will direct your paths."

When we become weary with doing the right thing we say: "It\'s not
worth it."\
God says: Galatians 6:9 (NLT) "Don't get tired of doing what is good.
Don't get discouraged and give up, for we will reap a harvest of
blessing at the appropriate time."

When we have failed we say: "I can\'t forgive myself."\
God says: 1 John 1:9 (NLT) "If we confess our sins to him, he is
faithful and just to forgive us and to cleanse us from every wrong."
Romans 8:1 (NLT) "There is no condemnation for those who belong to
Christ Jesus."

When we face financial hardships we say: "I can\'t manage."\
God says: Philippians 4:19 (NLT) "God will supply all your needs from
his glorious riches, which have been given to us in Christ Jesus."

When fear presses in on us we say: "I\'m afraid."\
God says: 2 Timothy 1:7 (NLT) "God has not given us a spirit of fear
and timidity, but of power, love, and self-discipline."

When we face situations that are much bigger than we are, we say: "I am
not smart enough."

God says: James 1:5 (NLT) "If you need wisdom---if you want to know
what God wants you to do---ask him, and he will gladly tell you. He will
not resent your asking."
