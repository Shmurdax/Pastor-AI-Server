INTRO: Today we are going to continue talking about Habits Of The
House -- and in particular the Habit Of Serving.

Many Christians only have a vague idea of what is involved in serving
God. Someone said, "It is true that most people would like to serve
God, but only in an advisory capacity!"\
\
Do you know anyone like that? I am not talking about anyone here today
but I'm sure most of you know people who want to serve God and the
church, but only in an advisory capacity! (Anonymous letter)

There are two non-negotiable elements to serving:

- Serving requires hands-on activity... (Use a towel here -- You know
 you have become a servant leader when your worship becomes serving
 others)

- Serving requires commitment...

At times, many of us attempt to avoid a Commitment to serve the Lord and
the Church, especially:

- If it is not convenient for us to do...

- If we feel inadequate for the task...

- If we are afraid of success or failure...

There are at least three things that go into finding our place to serve
Lord and others:

- Gift Discovery...

- Gift Giving...

- Gift Deployment...

## I. GIFT DISCOVERY...

A habit of this house is helping people discover their gifts! One of our
verbs is "Disciple" and a part of discipleship is to help you to
discover your gifts.

In our Commitment to serve, we must be constantly on the lookout for
ways that God has gifted us. When it comes to gifts, it is the
responsibility of the believer to:

- Discover the gift...

- Develop the gift...

- Properly use the gift...

How can we know what abilities we have individually been given?

(Susan will furnish information on the process of discovering gifts --
I will speak from the slide)

This is going to set some of you free today; don't be afraid to try out
some ways of serving God by volunteering for short term assignments. If
you volunteer for something and discover you are not good at it or
passionate about it, give proper notice and move on to something else,
but don't quit and sit idly by doing nothing!

Once you discover where you are suited to serve, make a long-term
commitment.

If you have not gone through the process of discovering your gifts,
there is no time like the present. My dad would say, "There is no time
like the present." "We are burning daylight" "We must work while it is
yet day, for the night is coming in which no one will be able to
work."

## II. GIFT DEVELOPMENT

A habit of this house is developing people, and helping people develop
their gifts!

It is one thing to have a gift and to identify what the gift is, it is
something completely different to develop the gift. One of our verbs is
"Empower" and one of the ways we attempt to empower you, is through
helping you develop your gifts.

(Susan will furnish information for a slide showing the way's gifts
can be developed -- I will speak from the slide)

I fly all the time. There are many reasons there is a pilot and a
co-pilot. One of the main reasons is that the co-pilot is logging hours
and being developed so that one day he can be trusted with his own crew.
He is not going to be the captain until his skills have been properly
developed. And for that I say, "Thank you Jesus and United!"

It is one thing to discover our gifts, but it is a completely different
process to have our gifts developed.

I became a Pastor when I was 19 years old! If the opportunity had been
offered to me when I was 19 years old to Pastor CT Church, I would have
undoubtedly said "YES"! AND - I would have destroyed this church!

You guys need to thank all the people who helped develop us!

Gift development:

- Takes time...

- Requires a commitment on the part of the gifted...

- Requires patience on the part of the instructor...

- Involves a process of trial and error... "He who has never made a
 mistake has never done anything -- Which in and of itself is a
 mistake!"

## III. GIFT DEPLOYMENT...

One of the habits of this house is to deploy people into the Kingdom
harvest. One of our verbs is "Send" and we do this by launching people
into a ministry whether it is at home or abroad. (Interns -- Tanaya --
Ronnie -- Richard -- All of you)

I Peter 4:10 (CEV) "Each of you has been blessed with one of God\'s
many wonderful gifts to be used in the service of others. So [use your
gift well]{.underline}."

Charles Kingsley said, "Have your tools ready and God will find you
some work."

The Lord told J. Hudson Taylor, "I am going to evangelize China, and
if you will walk with me, I will do it through you."

For Kingdom expansion to occur, it is urgent for all believers to deploy
their gifts!

We all need to be deployed now! Wilbert Welch chancellor of Grand Rapids

started steadily stepping up the stairs!"

We have already been commissioned, Jesus said, "Go and make disciples
of all nations..."
