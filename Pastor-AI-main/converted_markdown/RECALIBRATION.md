As rewarding as ministry can be, it carries with it a plethora of
challenges. Tonight I want to talk about one specific challenge -
reconnecting with family.

All of us know our priorities in life and ministry should be: (PLACE
ON SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- #1 God...

- #2 Family...

- #3 Ministry...

We are great when it comes to preaching these priorities, but we
struggle with how to live them out in real time. We only have 24 hours
in a day, and how we do all that is required of us in ministry, and keep
the priority of our relationship to God, and the relationship to our
family in proper perspective becomes a mammoth challenge.

When I have my GPS on voice mode and I do not obey it exactly, it will
tell me to make a u-turn, and if I do not heed HER instructions it will
say, "Wait a moment, recalibrating."

In the world of global positioning systems, recalibrating means:
(PLACE ON SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- A turn has been missed...

- The command to make a u-turn has been ignored...

- A new route to the same destination must be created...

There comes a moment in all of our lives, no matter how large or small
the ministry, that we have to recalibrate. (Trip to Disneyworld -- Susan
-- Do you like being a husband? Father? Pastor?)

I want to look at two families whose priorities were polar opposites and
the results are unmistakably different.

King David

David, the son of Jesse, was moved from obscurity to the throne by the
hand of the Lord. David is undisputedly the greatest King to ever sit on
a throne. He was "A man after Gods own heart." The word of God
prophesies that when Christ returns to the earth He will "Sit upon
the throne of David."

Yet when we dig into David's personal and family life, some very ugly
things begin to surface:

- David committed adultery with Bathsheba...

- David murdered Uriah, Bathsheba's husband...

- The son born from the union between David and Bathsheba died as an
 infant...

- David's son, Amnon raped his half-sister Tamar...

- Because of her shame Tamar lived the rest of her life as a recluse in
 the house of her full-brother Absalom...

- David refused to enact any kind of justice against Amnon for Tamar's
 rape... (His inaction was probably a result of guilt for his own sin
 against Bathsheba and Uriah)

- Absalom avenged the rape of his sister Tamar by killing Amnon...

With all of this afoot, it would stand to reason that David would call a
timeout and recalibrate his family! Not so - he chose to ignore the
entire hideous turn of events.

When Absalom killed Amnon, he found himself banished from the king's
presence and living in exile for three years. During this time, the king
refused to see him or speak to him. Eventually Joab worked out a deal
for Absalom to move back to the royal city, but still the king refused
to see or talk to his son. No matter what our children do, they are
still our children.

Eventually Absalom petitions Joab three times to see the king but was
refused. In the course of time, Absalom was invited before the king.
This is where it really gets weird -- The king kissed Abaslom but
refused to speak to him or hold a conversation with him.

At some point we must forgive the infractions of family members and move
on!

From this point the die was cast, Absalom's inability to establish a
relationship with his father would be the impetus for his rebellion that
would lead to an attempted hostile takeover by Absalom. The people we
choose to shut out will eventually hem us in. (LEAVE THIS UP UNTIL
NEXT SLIDE)

We have heard a lot of preaching and teaching about Absalom's rebellion
and people having an Absalom spirit, but what has been largely
overlooked is that this entire scenario was the result of a father
refusing to discipline a son who was guilty of rape, and refusing to
dialogue with a son who engaged in vigilante justice.

Eventually David's refusal to recalibrate his family cost him the lives
of two of his son's.

Because he could not reestablish a relationship with his father, Absalom
sat at the gate of the palace and spoke with the people who were coming
and going and said, "Your claims are good and right but there is no
one appointed by the king to hear you. If I were king all who have a
problem could come to me and I would give them justice." Did you get
that? His pain of not being able to have a relationship with his own
father was the fuel that drove Absalom's rebellion.

When we place ministry above our families, we create a seething anger
within our children that will surely manifest itself against God and
ministry. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL I START TALKING ABOUT OBED EDOM)

No matter what has gone down in our families, we must keep the lines of
communication open. The church can get another pastor, but your family
cannot get another father or mother. I have told CT Church repeatedly,
"I love you dearly, but if there is ever a time that I have to choose
between you and my family, you will lose 100% of the time."

Absalom was killed as a result of his rebellion against king David, and
after Absalom's death, David sat in the chamber over the gate and wept
saying, "Absalom, Absalom, my son Absalom, would to God I had died
for you!" Absalom didn't need a father to die for him, he needed a
father to talk to him and guide him.

The story of David's family could have been a pattern for all to follow,
but the fact that he was a better king than father created a
dysfunctional pattern that all of us would like to avoid rather than
follow.

Where have we placed our family in our list of priorities? What will our
pattern produce? Are we prepared for the results of placing ministry
ahead of family? If we should gain the whole world and lose our own
family, what profit do we have?

Obed Edom

When David decided to move the Ark to Jerusalem, he built a new cart and
began the process of transporting this Holy piece of furniture. The Ark
was supposed to be carried on poles that were carried on the shoulders
of the priests, not on a cart.

Eventually the oxen stumbled and it appeared the cart would fall to the
ground, so a man by the name of Uzzah reached out to steady the Ark and
he was struck dead right then and there for touching the Ark.

When Uzzah died, David asked Obed Edom if he could leave the Ark at his
house. David wasn't just asking for a place to park the Ark, he was
asking if Obed would do what needed to be done to attend the Ark. In
effect he was saying "Can we trust you with this Ark?" (LEAVE ON
SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

Against the backdrop of the death of Uzzah, wouldn't we be a little more
than reluctant to have the Ark parked at our house? There is no sign of
hesitation on the part of Obed, he was not afraid to receive the Ark.

Obed knew that a man with clean hands and a pure heart had nothing to
fear from the Lord. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

While the Ark was in the possession of the Philistines it brought
curses; when it was in the custody of Abinadab it never seemed to bring
a blessing or a curse; but when Obed Edom hosted the Ark, it blessed him
and his entire house!

The ark of God that overthrew Dagon and smote Uzzah was nothing but a
fountain of blessing in the household of Obed Edom.

What was the difference? The attitude and priority with which Obed Edom
cared for the Ark released the blessing from the storehouse of God. Can
we leave this Ark with you? Will you give it priority?

In a three-month period the fortunes of Obed's house took a drastic
turn: (PLACE ON SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- His house turned from poverty to prominence... (God is El Shaddai not
 El Get-by!)

- He became a man of honor rather than being despised... (Obed means
 slave -- Every time someone called him by his name it was a painful
 reminder of who he was -- The name Edom was assigned to him because he
 was an Edomite and a descendent of Esau - He was a "Gittite" - - this
 means that he was from the city of Gath, the place of David's greatest
 enemy, Goliath.)

- Obed and HIS FAMILY became addicted to the presence of God...

When the Ark came to the house of Obed Edom, he recalibrated his
household, gave the Ark the proper respect, and ordered his family
right. In so doing, Obed reversed the curse, found his destiny, and
created a generational blessing for his family.

The day came that David returned to retrieve the Ark. Obed had a choice
to make:

- Go back to his old way of life...

- Grieve the absence of the Ark...

- Pack up and follow the Ark...

When David came to take the Ark to Jerusalem, Obed and his house left
all and followed the Ark! The recalibration of Obed's family paid off in
a big way!

This was an easy choice for Obed; he would give up his life and
livelihood and follow the presence of God. In so doing, his attitude,
actions, and aspirations created a legacy of faith, and relationship
with the Lord. The Lord was faithful back to Obed Edom, establishing a
legacy of generational blessings.

Obed Edom: (PLACE ON SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Became the keeper of the gate at the Temple...

- Became a singer and musician in the house of the Lord...

- Became the keeper of the treasury...

What does each of these responsibilities have in common? Each job change
moved him closer to the Ark!

His addiction to the presence of God caused Obed Edom to pass down a
generational blessing to his offspring. He had 62 descendents that are
all called blessed of God and served in the temple! (PUT ALL THESE
TOGETHER TO MAKE ONE SENTENCE)

CONCLUSION:

If we are going to engage in ministry and raise Godly families we must:

- Make God the priority...

- Become actively involved in leading our families...

- Teach our children to pursue the presence of God...

- Demonstrate that serving God and His Church is an honor rather than an
 obligation... "He has counted us worthy placing us in the
 ministry."

I call every minister and minister's spouse in this place to recalibrate
the priorities of our lives. We know what our priorities should be, but
we must hit the reset button from time to time.
