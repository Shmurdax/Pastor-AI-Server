INTRO: It would be great to think that every time we prayed, the
answer would come immediately. But, truth is, that's not always the
case. In fact, quite the opposite is true; oftentimes God seems to be
silent on the issues we face. I wish I could stand before you today and
confidently declare, every single time you call upon the Lord He will
answer right away. I cannot do that because it simply is not true. We
must face the obvious; sometimes God is all too silent for our comfort!
When this is the case we must exercise our right and responsibility to
wait upon the Lord until He gives us His answer!

During a very difficult part of my life I remember sitting outside about
three o'clock one morning looking up into the starry sky and saying to
God, "Why don't you say something? I don't care what the answer is I
just want to hear you say something! Slap me if you would like, I just
need to know you are actually hearing me!" There was nothing! I don't
think the stars even twinkled! It was deathly silent! I must tell you, I
find those times to be most difficult!

Soon after this experience I walked into a ministers meeting and one of
the ministers whom I had never met came straight to me, never shook my
hand or greeted me, he just started prophesying! Through that brother
the Lord made me to know in unmistakable terms, He was there all the
time and He had already devised a strategy for my victory.

going to explore several of them.

## I. FAITH IS BEING PUT TO THE TEST...

Hebrews 11:1 (NKJV) "Now faith is the substance of things hoped for,
the evidence of things not seen." Hebrews 11:6 (NKJV) "But without
faith it is impossible to please Him, for he who comes to God must
believe that He is, and that He is a rewarder of those who diligently
seek Him."

Faith is not belief without proof it is trust without reservation.
(LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) (My experience meditating Hebrews
11:6) It is true at times when God is silent faith is being put to the
test!

Because God was silent while Jesus was in the Garden, Jesus was placed
in the position of either calling for relief from his temporal
circumstances which would have short circuited the redemption process,
or setting an example of faith for road weary pilgrims to follow when
the spiritual night is so dark one cannot see the next step to take.

If Jesus had caved under the pressure of the silence of the Father on
the night before His crucifixion:

- The enemy would have defeated the Fathers answer for sin...

- The redemption process would have been back to square one...

- Mankind would have continued in sin without a Savior to call on...

- Evil would have continued to dominate Gods crowing creation,
 mankind...

- The world would probably have self destructed in a cesspool of sinful
 behavior...

The good news is that nothing of the sort transpired, Jesus persisted
despite the silence of the Father! Jesus set a wonderful example of
faith for every person who has ever experienced the Silence of God in
moments of great trial.

The Father was not being disingenuous to Jesus in His hour of greatest
trouble; He was allowing Jesus to set the bar of faith for His children
to follow when they are not hearing a booming voice from the sky.

Most of us have programmed ourselves to believe that we should hear the
voice of God before making any decision. I love it when God speaks to me
and leaves no doubt which direction He wants me to take but the truth of
the matter is, it takes no faith to live like this!

Any of us can live a successful Christian life when God is telling us
which socks to wear, what to eat, where to go, what to say! All one must
do to live in this way is to be obedient, no faith needed! The will of
God for His children is the development of faith! God would rather
develop faith in our lives than to give us everything our little hearts
desire. Sure, He wants us to have things but if He can develop faith we
can be faithful when the battle is raging and the things are nowhere to
be found!

Faith is developed when we face earth shaking, life altering, nail
biting decisions, and we do not hear one thing from God! He is silent on
the issue! Have you ever been there? Are you there now? I am certainly
not saying I prefer the silence of God, I don't, but how am I ever going
to learn to live by faith when I allow myself to be paralyzed by the
silence of God?

We often make the mistake of allowing our lives to be benched until we
can get an answer form The Lord! This robs valuable time from the
earthly journey of the child of God! Sure we need to hear from God but
His silence should not automatically cause us to be disengaged while we
wait for the thunder, the lightning, and a booming voice from the sky,
saying, "Wear the blue socks, they will match your blue suit!"

So what is going on when God is silent? Faith is being put to the test!

This is what was going on with Jesus in the garden and this is what is
going on with us when we are not hearing from God! Jesus actually
prayed, "If it is possible, let this cup pass from me." It is here
we see the humanity of Jesus. Jesus did not allow His humanity to stay
engaged indefinitely, He said "Not my will but your will be done!"
When God is silent we have a choice to make; we can allow the flesh to
be engaged, which will be self-defeating; or we can allow faith to rise
to the occasion, which will allow the Jesus in us to be engaged and we
will be able to "Go from faith to faith, and from victory to
victory."

When this happens God will be allowed to make the rough place smooth,
the crooked place straight, and the high place low! Faith is put to the
test and when our faith has been "Tried as by fire, it shall come
forth as gold!" William Newton Clarke said, "Faith is the daring of
the soul to go farther than it can see!"

Patrick Overton said, "When you have come to the edge of all that you
know and are about to drop off into the darkness of the unknown, faith
is knowing one of two things will happen: There will be something solid
to land on; you will be taught to fly." This is exactly what was going
on with Jesus and He was setting an example for us that the Father can
be trusted. In the Jesus world when His children are required to take a
leap of faith He is going to give us a smooth landing or teach us to
fly!

## II. GOD IS WORKING WHILE WE ARE WAITING...

The experience of Jesus in the garden of Gethsemane is disconcerting to
many but we should never mistake the unresponsiveness of God for the
inactivity of God! While Jesus prayed three times, God was certainly not
unresponsive and He was anything but inactive.

Before the Garden experience of Jesus, the Father had set in motion a
plan that was coming to pass even as Jesus was praying and God was
unresponsive! What was God doing while Jesus was praying and not getting
an answer? (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- He was enlisting Simon of Cyrene to carry the cross of Christ...

- He was placing it on the heart of Joseph of Arimithea to loan Jesus'
 family his tomb...

- He was preparing the halls of Hell for a visit from the dead Son of
 God...

- He was getting the people in Paradise ready for a Messiah they had
 anticipated for centuries...

- He was preparing a cemetery for a Sunday morning resurrection
 celebration...

- He was setting up Bethany's launching pad for His Son's departure from
 earth...

- He was making ready an upper room for the outpouring of the Holy
 Spirit that would energize believers with a world changing power...

God was not in a state of inactivity, He was at work bringing to pass
the completion of the plan of redemption!

How does this play out in our lives? Many times we plan our lives out
and expect God to get involved. What we need to do is wait before the
Lord until He gives direction for our lives!

## III. WHEN GOD IS SILENT, WE MAY ALREADY KNOW THE ANSWER...

On the eve of His crucifixion Jesus prayed all night wrestling His flesh
into conformity to the will of the Father. On His Father's side Jesus
knew from the start what must come to pass but on His mother's side He
had to conform the will of the flesh to the will of the Father.

Some would question why Jesus looked for a relief route around the
cross. The reason for this is that while He was one hundred percent God,
He was also one hundred percent man! Look at what Jesus was facing:
(ALLOW SCENES FROM THE PASSION OF THE CHRIST TO PLAY ON THE SCREEN
WHILE THE FOLLOWING BULLET POINTS ARE BEING MADE)

- He had supper with His disciples... This meal occurred in the Upper
 Room in Southwest Jerusalem and it was here He revealed that one of
 His own would betray Him.

- He faced a sleepless night in the Garden... He prayed while His
 closest friends slept.

- He agonized before the Father in prayer until His sweat became as
 great drops of blood... The name "Gethsemane" comes from the
 Hebrew word Gat Shamanim, and means "oil press." I think the name
 of this place is significant because it appears to me that while Jesus
 was agonizing before the Father, His spirit was under the most severe
 attack of His earthly life. This was spiritual warfare! His spirit was
 assaulted to the point that three times He said, "If it be
 possible, let this cup pass from me." It is in this crucial,
 pivotal time that Jesus cries, "Not my will, but thine be
 done." Though some folks would like to argue that Jesus sweat
 becoming like blood drops is figurative, medical science say's this is
 possible but only when a person is encountering a life threatening
 degree of stress. The medical term for this is "Hemohydrosis" or
 "Hematidrosis." This phenomenon occurs when the capillaries around
 the sweat pores become fragile and leak blood into the sweat. Thus,
 human sweat becomes mixed with blood and great sweat-drops of blood
 occur.

- He was betrayed by Judas and then deserted by all the disciples...

- He was arrested in the Garden... Jesus was arrested immediately
 following an all night prayer meeting; no time to freshen up or catch
 His breath He was incarcerated on the heels of an agonizing all night
 prayer meeting.

- Following His arrest, He was brought back to the palace of the High
 Priest... It was here that He was interrogated by Annas, a former High
 Priest and Caiaphas, Annas\' son-in-law.

- He was then tried by the Sanhedrin, and found guilty of blasphemy...
 This trial was based on His proclamation of being the Son of God.

- He was sentenced to die for a crime He never committed... I wonder
 where the ACLU was???

- He was sent to Pontius Pilate at the Antonia Fortress... This was done
 because only the Romans were able to execute criminals.

- Pilate did not find fault with Him... Because Pilate was a people
 pleaser He treated Jesus as the political hot potato He had become.

- Pilate sent Jesus to Herod who did not want to get involved...

- Herod sent Jesus back to Pilate...

- Pilate caved in to the pressure of the crowd and ordered Jesus to be
 tortured and crucified...

- Jesus was beaten with thirty-nine stripes... The whip He was beaten
 with had pieces of metal and broken pieces of glass and pottery sowed
 into the ends of it!

- Jesus was required to carry His cross from Pilates Judgment Hall to
 Golgotha... It is believed the cross of Jesus weighed one hundred
 twenty-five pounds and He carried it for two and one half miles!

Friends, this was what Jesus was dealing with in the Garden, while the
Father was absolutely silent! In fact Jesus thought the Father had
forsaken Him! Have you ever felt that way? Jesus cried from the cross,
Eli, Eli lama-sa-bach-tha-ni. Which being interpreted is My God, My
God, why has thou forsaken Me?" We know the Father did not forsake
Jesus and we know God is not going to forsake us, but it sure feels that
way from time to time!

If the Father said nothing from the Garden to the cross, what was going
on? The Father did not need to say anything because the decision
concerning the cross had long since been settled! There was only one
path to redemption: through the sacrifice of the sinless son of man!

Could it be that if we are not hearing from God, we already know the
answer? It may not be the answer we want to hear, but the silence of God
may very well be the answer of God!

Because of the grave illness of their brother, Mary and Martha called
for their friend Jesus. Jesus did not respond until Lazarus had been
dead four day's! This could really get a person ticked off!!

When Jesus finally showed up, listen to how He addresses the situation.
John 11:14-15 (NKJV) "Then Jesus said to them plainly, "[Lazarus is
dead and I am glad]{.underline} for your sakes that I was not there,
that you may believe. Nevertheless let us go to him."

Martha was not impressed! She said to Jesus "If you had been here,
Lazarus would not have died." This is a scathing accusation against
anyone, but this is Jesus she is talking to!

Remember, when God allows us to take a step of faith into the unknown,
He will create a soft place for our landing or He is going to teach us
to fly! Jesus was not attempting to be disingenuous to the sisters from
Bethany by not showing up when He was needed, He was preparing them for
a faith flight!

Mary and Martha were not in the mood for flying and if they had been
they sure would not have wanted to fly coach which sure seemed like what
they were doing! (How to get a seat to yourself on Southwest)

Have you ever had a situation that was so far out of hand that you felt
God had let you down with His commitment to silence? Sometimes when God
is silent, we already have our answer!

This is not the end of this scandal! While Martha was vocal with her
feelings about the silence of Jesus in the most traumatic time of their
lives, the Bible say's "Mary stayed in the house." Jesus was not
the only one who could be silent. Mary turned the tables on Jesus and
gave Him a little taste of His own medicine: The silent treatment!

Jesus did not come when He was called for because He knew He was going
to resurrect Lazarus from the dark tomb of death he was in. Through the
resurrection of Lazarus, He was going to ratchet up the faith of His
followers! The resurrection of Lazarus was about to launch the followers
of Jesus to a new level of faith where they could take new territory;
whip new devils; and fly at new altitudes!

When God is silent it is often because the outcome has already been
decided and the waiting period is the result of a very deliberate God
working in the lives of intensely desperate, powerfully impatient,
terribly misguided people!

While some of us can accept the fact, God is very deliberate while we
are waiting, the outcome has already been decided, what we really would
like to know is what takes God so long to reveal His answer to us?

I think the answer to this all-encompassing question could be that He
has bound Himself to work with people who possess free moral agency. Why
God allowed for free will is the one question I would like to have an
answer to!

This one thing has caused all of us more trouble than we would like to
admit.

Because God has given humankind the power of choice and our prayers
often involve other individuals, God must work with the free moral
agency of those individuals to bring about our answer and His will. This
takes time, lots of time, even if you are God!

When God is silent He is oftentimes revealing the answer, we must go
through the battle!

In those times when God is silent, we must never mistake his silence for
a lack of interest! We should never assume that because He has not
spoken He does not hear our cry! When God is not talking it is often a
matter of timing!
