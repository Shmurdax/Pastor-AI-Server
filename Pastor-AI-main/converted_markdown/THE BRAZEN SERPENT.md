INTRO: Have you ever heard of the old saying, "You can't teach an
old dog, new tricks?" That's the way the saying goes, now whether it's
true or not is debatable. Whether the statement is true or false, it
points to a very basic flaw in human nature, people do become set in
their ways, and besides that, most people are very predictable! Someone
said, "People can change, but not much." Leave on screen until
next slide) Apart from the redemptive power of the cross and the work of
the Holy Spirit in one's life this is basically a true statement.

The principle of predictability is very true element of the character of
the children of Israel. The story of their departure from Egypt and
wanderings in the wilderness is the story of a rebellious nature born
out of a slave mentality developed over a four hundred year period of
Egyptian slavery! The Children of Israel rebelled against their human
and divine authority and murmured and complained until God decided to
send judgment upon their blasphemous ways!

Some of the darkest moments in Israeli history are the times when this
nation unabashedly wondered out loud if it would be better off to return
to Egyptian slavery rather than forge ahead into the future leaning on
the strong arm of the Lord who delivered them in the first place!

In Numbers chapter twenty one we find the Israelites have reverted to
their old habits of murmuring, complaining, and throwing sand in the
face of Jehovah! They are truly back to their old ways!

Thirty-eight years have passed since God led them out of Egyptian
bondage! They were not permitted entrance into Canaan because of their
unbelief. The Israelites quite literally turned eleven day's
journey into forty plus years of wilderness wandering! (LEAVE ON
SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) Does this sound familiar?

For thirty eight years God had sustained them; fed them; led them (by
supernatural means); and delivered them from every crisis situation that
threatened them, all the while negotiating a wilderness laden with
pitfalls and dangers! They had witnessed the supernatural working of
God's power, been eye-witnesses to God's glory, and had experienced the
loving way in which Jehovah had dealt with them! Now a new crisis has
developed and they stepped right up to the plate and flunked the test!

A group of Canaanites, led by King Arad, had ambushed the Israelites and
captured some of them. As soon as this happened, the Israelites came
running to the Lord with a vow and the enemy was destroyed! Numbers
21:2-3 (NKJV) "So Israel made a vow to the [Lord,]{.smallcaps} and
said, "If You will indeed deliver this people into my hand, then I will
utterly destroy their cities." And the [Lord]{.smallcaps} listened to
the voice of Israel and delivered up the Canaanites, and they [utterly
destroyed them and their cities]{.underline}. So the name of that place
was called Hormah."

As so often happened in the history of Israel, and as too often happens
in our lives, this great victory was followed by a terrible defeat!
(LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) The old problems of
discouragement, disobedience, and rebellion began to surface and proved
to be the downfall of this nomadic nation of emancipated slaves! Numbers
21:4-6 (NKJV) "Then they journeyed from Mount Hor by the Way of the
Red Sea, to go around the land of Edom; and the soul of [the people
became very discouraged on the way]{.underline}. And [the people
spoke against God and against Moses]{.underline}: "Why have you brought
us up out of Egypt to die in the wilderness? For there is no food and no
water, and our soul loathes this worthless bread." So the
[Lord]{.smallcaps} sent fiery serpents among the people, and they bit
the people; and many of the people of Israel died." They became
tired. We should be careful lest Satan would take advantage of us when
we become physically and spiritually fatigued! They were discouraged,
"Because of the way."

I am sure there were many in that company who remembered the miraculous
deliverance from Egypt! There were still a great number in this
multitude that had been stirred by God's power in the opening of the Red
Sea! They had seen it with their eyes; they had been there when it
happened; they were eyewitnesses of one of the greatest miracles in
human history! They had personally marched through the Red Sea on dry
land! Some of these people had stood on the bank of the Red Sea and sang
the song of redemption as Egyptian chariots, soldiers, and paraphernalia
washed up on the shoreline!

This nation of people had seen God's hand of mercy time and time again,
as he provided water in the dry place, rained down manna from heaven,
sent quail when they decided they did not like manna, and gave victory
after victory!

This group of people was now being called upon to skirt the land of Edom
on their journey to the Promised Land! The problem with going around
the border of Edom meant: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- A long, hard walk...

- Many hills and valley...

- Even more burning desert...

Their song of redemption and victory turned to grumbling and
complaining! Sound familiar? Aren't we often just like that? Amid the
problems of Edom it is easy to forget how bad Egypt really was!

Complaining was nothing new to the Israelites but here we find
their murmuring took a new twist. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE
NEXT SLIDE) Before their complaints had been directed at Aaron and
Moses, but now they spoke out blatantly against God! Numbers 21:5 (NKJV)
"And [the people spoke against God and against Moses]{.underline}:
"Why have you brought us up out of Egypt to die in the wilderness? For
there is no food and no water, and our soul loathes this worthless
bread." Can you imagine such ingratitude after nearly forty years of
continuous tender loving care? This was the epitome of rebellion!

Because of the people's rebellion, God sent judgment upon them! Numbers
21:6 (NKJV) "[The [Lord]{.smallcaps} sent fiery serpents]{.underline}
among the people, and they bit the people; and many of the people of
Israel died." Sin always sets in motion some unavoidable
consequences: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Sin must always be punished... Romans 6:23 (NKJV) "[The wages
 of sin is death]{.underline}, but the gift of God is eternal life in
 Christ Jesus our Lord."

- Un-confessed sin must always be punished... As merciful and kind
 as God is, He cannot simply overlook un-confessed sin, He is committed
 to bringing the very nature of sin to justice!

- The law of sowing and reaping is an unchangeable law which also; and
 especially includes sin... Galatians 6:7-8 (NKJV) "Do not be
 deceived, God is not mocked; for whatever a man sows, that [he will
 also reap]{.underline}. For he who [sows to his flesh]{.underline}
 will of the flesh [reap corruption]{.underline}, but he who [sows to
 the Spirit]{.underline} will of the Spirit [reap everlasting
 life]{.underline}."

- Sin is an affront to God's holiness... When the holiness of God is
 insulted, judgment will always follow! Based on what God chose to do
 here to bring justice to the assault on His holiness, I would say God
 was ticked!

Why did God send serpents? From Genesis to Revelation the serpent is a
symbol of Satan. It would seem to me that God was graphically teaching
the Israelites that they would reap what they had sown! If they followed
sin, sin would kill them.

This judgment is a lesson for us too. Paul refers to this account in his
advice to the Christians at Corinth. 1 Corinthians 10:9-11 (NKJV)
"Let us not tempt Christ, as some of them also tempted, and were
destroyed by serpents; nor complain, as some of them also
complained, and were destroyed by the destroyer. Now [all these
things happened to them as examples, and they were written for our
admonition]{.underline}, upon whom the ends of the ages have come."

When we become aware of sin in our lives and are quick to be willing to
deal with it in repentance, we can alleviate the negative consequences
which sin can bring. 1 Corinthians 11:31 (NKJV) "If we would judge
ourselves, [we would not be judged]{.underline}." The lesson of this
scripture is not judgment but salvation.

Numbers 21:7-9 (NKJV) "Therefore the people came to Moses, and said,
"We have sinned, for we have spoken against the [Lord]{.smallcaps} and
against you; pray to the [Lord]{.smallcaps} that He take away the

The serpent of brass was a picture of the Lord Jesus Christ, the
ultimate cure for sin! When the people who had been bitten simply
turned their eyes on the brazen serpent, their life would be
miraculously saved: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- They were not told to prepare a vaccine for healing... they were
 told to follow one simple instruction... Numbers 21:8 (NKJV) "Make
 a fiery serpent, and set it on a pole; and it shall be that everyone
 who is bitten, [when he looks at it, shall live]{.underline}."

- They were not told to fight off the serpents... Humanly speaking,
 that approach probably seemed quite logical or even plausible.

- They were not told to mount a massive "kill the snakes"
 campaign... The fact is, this kind of predicament could not be
 overcome by human ingenuity!

The healing for the snakebite of sin has already been provided! Isaiah
1:18-19 (NKJV) "Come now, and let us reason together," Says the
[Lord,]{.smallcaps} "[Though your sins are like scarlet, They shall be
as white as snow]{.underline}; [Though they are red like crimson, They
shall be as wool]{.underline}. If you are willing and obedient, You
shall eat the good of the land."

You cannot kill enough snakes in your life to please God! You must come
as you are!

God did not ask the people for any kind of repayment, He just asked them
to look by faith, upon the brazen serpent! John 3:16-17 (NKJV) "For
God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that [whoever
believes in Him should not perish but have everlasting
life]{.underline}. "For God did not send His Son into the world to
condemn the world, but that the world through Him might be saved."
What a picture of Grace! Grace does not say "DO", it says "DONE"!

There are several parallels between the Brazen Serpent and the Lord
Jesus Christ: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The serpent was lifted upon the pole... The Lord Jesus Christ was
 lifted upon the cross!

- The requirement for a healing vaccine was to look on the serpent by
 faith... The requirement for salvation is Faith! Romans 10:9-10
 (NKJV) "If you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus and believe
 in your heart that God has raised Him from the dead, you will be
 saved. For with the heart one believes unto righteousness, and
 with the mouth confession is made unto salvation."

- Works could not save them from the poison of the snakes... Works
 will not save us either! Ephesians 2:8-9 (NKJV) "For by grace you
 have been [saved through faith]{.underline}, and that not of
 yourselves; it is the gift of God, [not of works]{.underline}, lest
 anyone should boast."

SKIT: (The props for the skit and the actors get set up as the
following statements are made by the Pastor) Suppose an Israelite had
been bitten by one of those fiery serpents. He knew that if left to his
own resources the snake bite would be fatal! He had watched as others
had succumbed to the venom of the dreaded snakes!

He had heard of the Brazen Serpent! He knew people had simply looked at
it and survived. But he thought it was a foolish idea.

(The pole with the Brazen Serpent is set up, and a snake bite victim is
in position) A friend comes to him and says, "Why don't you look at
Moses' Brazen Serpent?" He gives him his testimony, "I looked and
lived!" The snake bite victim replies, "I cannot bring myself to
believe a snake on a pole can do anything for me!" He is afraid someone
will think he is foolish! His friend explains, "This is your only hope!
No one has survived the snake bite except those who have looked at the
brazen serpent!" The snake bite victim thinks about it for a while and
then decides he will go out and see if he can find some medicine which
will reverse the venom of the snake. He goes through the audience
begging people to tell him where he can get help. He finds no help and
with his strength failing, returns to his friend and lies down. His
friend once again tries to get him to look at the brazen serpent on the
pole, but he is not quite ready yet! He's going to wait until he feels
better. Now he feels that he is too sick for the brazen serpent to do
him any good, besides that, he knew someone who looked at the snake and
lived, who he knew to be a hypocrite! The man dies! (Some people come
in with a stretcher and carry the snake bite victim out.)

The Pastor say's "What would you think of such a man? He was foolish,
yet so many today are doing the exact same thing!"

Another snake bite victim enters and after being convinced the brazen
serpent can help him, he looks upon it and lives! The entire casts of
actors rejoice because of the miracle!

The Pastor say's "The call from the Brazen Serpent was 'look and live,'
the call from the cross is 'look and live!' The serpent was their ONLY
hope and Jesus is our ONLY hope!
