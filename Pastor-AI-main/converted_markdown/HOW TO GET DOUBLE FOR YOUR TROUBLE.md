Joseph was one of the twelve sons of Jacob. At the age of seventeen,
Joseph was employed as a shepherd in his family's ranching business.

Without ever knowing names in this story, one could pretty well predict
a head-on collision in the relationship department within this family:

- Jacob favored Rachel over Leah...

- Joseph was born to Jacob and Rachel late in life...

- Jacob favored Joseph above his other sons...

Joseph seemed to be on a fast track to success. He could convince his
father of anything. His father rolled out the red carpet any time Joseph
came around. It was obvious to the other brothers that Jacob was going
to find a way to resource Joseph above anyone or anything else.

The actions of Jacob created an impasse in his family. More importantly,
it created issues between his sons that were irreconcilable.

While Joseph seemed to be in the passing lane of life, he was about to
hit a traffic jam. This traffic jam was going to set up a detour through
four great tests that would determine who Joseph could become.

THE TEST OF VISION

Because of the situation in his home, his brothers hated Joseph. John
15:18-20 (NKJV) "If the world hates you, you know that it hated Me
before it hated you. ^19 ^If you were of the world, the world would love
its own. Yet because you are not of the world, but I chose you out of

If we are able to live a life that really counts, we will attract some
haters along the way. Shake the haters off! (Song: You Gotta Keep
Walking) (Keep paddling your canoe)

As if the dysfunction in this family was not bad enough, Jacob gave
Joseph a coat of many colors! The gifting of this coat of many colors
was filled with meaning:

- It indicated special favor...

- It denoted covenant relationship...

- It signaled family leadership...

To add insult to injury, Joseph started to dream dreams of exaltation.
(Binding sheaves in the field and the sheaves of his father, mother and
brothers bowing down to his sheave; Dream of the sun, moon and eleven
stars bowing down to him) When we start telling our dream, people are
going to react:

- Some will be excited...

- Some will be supportive...

- Some will be helpful...

- Some will be skeptical...

- Some will exert resistance...

- Some will be absolutely hateful...

When Joseph told his dream, no one was excited, supportive, or helpful!

Jacob, who thought Joseph was "Da Man," was very skeptical.

His brothers, who already had issues with him, did not react very well
to the unveiling of Joseph's dreams. Genesis 37:3-5 (NKJV) "Now
Israel loved Joseph more than all his children, because he was the son
of his old age. Also he made him a tunic of many colors. ^4 ^But when
his brothers saw that their father loved him more than all his brothers,
they hated him and could not speak peaceably to him. ^5 ^Now Joseph had
a dream, and he told it to his brothers; and they hated him even
more."

It is very disappointing, even downright deflating, when we roll our
vision out and the very people we thought would be excited about it,
resist it and even hate us because of it.

No one accomplishes a vision without some pockets of resistance and a
few haters. Mark my word...every God sized vision will be tested.

Just because we hit some resistance does not mean we have the wrong
vision! Just because we acquire some haters along the way is no
indication that we should:

- Give up...

- Throw in the towel...

- Raise the flag of surrender...

- Let the fat lady start singing...

Joseph\'s brothers conspired to kill him, but Reuben said "Let\'s put
him in a pit, and leave him there." Reuben planned to return later
and deliver Joseph unto his father. Reuben was hoping that with a little
time things would calm down.

The brothers of Joseph failed to consider that the tallest tree was once
a sapling. We must be careful how we treat people as we ascend the
ladder of success; we may need their assistance as we come back down.

Every God sized vision is planned through the pit. No dream will be
accomplished until it has been thoroughly tested. This means we will
more than likely experience times when we look at where the vision has
taken us and say, "This is the pits!"

Just because our vision has driven us to the pits, is no excuse for us
to give up on the vision! If we stay with the vision, the very dream we
rode into the pit will be the one that will drive us out of the pit! 2
Corinthians 1:8-10 (NKJV) "For we do not want you to be ignorant,
brethren, of our trouble which came to us in Asia: that we were burdened
beyond measure, above strength, so that we despaired even of life.
9 Yes, we had the sentence of death in ourselves, that we should not
trust in ourselves but in God who raises the dead, 10 who delivered us
from so great a death, and does deliver us; in whom we trust that He
will still deliver us, "

Joseph's brothers took his coat away from him, then dipped it in animal
blood and gave it to Jacob telling him, "Some wild animal probably
killed Joseph." Reuben is the only brother who showed any compassion
toward Joseph. Reuben intended to return to the pit and retrieve Joseph
but it was not to be so.

When our vision is being tested, there will be well-intentioned people
who can see it and believe it, but because of the peer pressure, cannot
muster the courage to break with the crowd and embrace it. How
disappointing! (Chickens)

The rest of the brothers placed Joseph in the pit long enough to find
some slave traders who were willing to buy him. When our vision is being
tested, there will be people we trust in our circle of influence, who
will sell us down the river. Psalm 55:12-14 (CEV) "My enemies are not
the ones who sneer and make fun. I could put up with that or even hide
from them. ^13 ^But it was my closest friend, the one I trusted most.
^14 ^We enjoyed being together, and we went with others to your house,
our God. "

Joseph's vision was being tested to the limit and ours will be as well.
There is no such thing as an untested vision. "If you limit your
choices only to what seems possible or reasonable, you disconnect
yourself from what you truly want, and all that is left is a
compromise." - Robert Fritz (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

We may experience times when we have been sold down the river, but if it
is a God vision, our canoe can withstand the current.

Joseph was brought out of the pit and sold to the Ishmaelite slave
traders for 20 pieces of silver. When our vision is being tested, the
light at the end of the tunnel is often an oncoming train.

If we can ride the test until it becomes a testimony, the vision God has
given us will not only survive, it will thrive! Jeremiah 29:11 (NKJV)
"For I know the thoughts that I think toward you, says the
[Lord]{.smallcaps}, thoughts of peace and not of evil, to give you a
future and a hope."

If we have a vision, it will be tested. What kind of price are we
willing to pay to follow God to reveal our destiny?

To get double for our trouble, we must be able to pass the vision test.
To pass the vision test we must believe:

- The vision is more important than our comfort...

- The vision is more important than public opinion...

- The vision is more important than the opposition...

How important is the vision? Can we pass the vision test? If we want
double for our trouble, the vision will need to be tested. Are we up to
the test? What will we do? Will we rise above the fray or embrace an
inferior idea?

The Ishmaelite slave traders sold Joseph to Potiphar, an officer of
Pharaoh. Potiphar was a very successful Egyptian businessman, and early
on he noticed that the Lord was with Joseph. As Joseph worked for
Potiphar, everything he touched seemed to turn to gold.

In time, Potiphar turned everything he had over to Joseph, and
consequently Joseph became the chief operating officer of his house and
business enterprises. God prospered Joseph as Potiphar's COO, and
Potiphar realized some very hefty profits.

The character test began when Potiphar's wife began to flirt with
Joseph. This flirtation turned to proposition as Joseph steadfastly
resisted her advances. I do not believe Mrs. Potiphar was an ugly woman.
Temptation is always attractive. "There is pleasure in sin for a
season."

In the house of Potiphar, Joseph found himself embroiled in a fight for
his character. This fight was not about mere resistance; it was about
whether Joseph would ever realize his vision.

Joseph refused to give in because he was determined to get double for
his trouble!

THE TEST OF SUCCESS

Genesis 41:39-44 (CEV) "The king told Joseph, "God is the one who has
shown you these things. No one else is as wise as you are or knows as
much as you do. ^40 ^I'm putting you in charge of my palace, and
everybody will have to obey you. No one will be over you except me.
^41 ^You are now governor of all Egypt!" ^42 ^Then the king took off his
royal ring and put it on Joseph's finger. He gave him fine clothes to
wear and placed a gold chain around his neck. ^43 ^He also let him ride
in the chariot next to his own, and people shouted, "Make way for
Joseph!" So Joseph was governor of Egypt. ^44 ^The king told Joseph,
"Although I'm king, no one in Egypt is to do anything without your
permission."

Joseph was about to discover that the test of success would be his
greatest test to date. How we handle success reveals who we really are.
"Nearly all men can stand adversity, but if you want to test a man\'s
character, give him power." --- Abraham Lincoln

Most people think when success comes everything will be smooth sailing.
The truth is the test of success reveals things about us that neither
others, nor we, knew previously.

Once Joseph settled into his new responsibilities, he married and God
gave him two sons. Genesis 41:50-52 (MSG) "Joseph had two sons born
to him before the years of famine came. Asenath, daughter of Potiphera
the priest of On, was their mother. Joseph named the firstborn Manasseh
(Forget), saying, "God made me forget all my hardships and my parental
home." He named his second son Ephraim (Double Prosperity), saying, "God
has prospered me in the land of my sorrow."

In Bible days parents rarely named their children based on what they
thought sounded good, they named them for something of great
significance in their lives. Genesis 41:51 (MSG) "Joseph named the
firstborn Manasseh (Forget), saying, "God made me forget all my
hardships and my parental home." What does Manasseh mean? It means
forgetfulness.

By naming his son Manasseh (Forgetfulness), Joseph is not saying that he
had forgotten about what his brothers had put him through. He had
certainly not lost the memory of his father, mother, or brothers. When
his brothers came to buy corn in Egypt, they did not recognize Joseph,
but he immediately recognized them.

By naming his son Manasseh, he was saying God had taken the pain out of
a past experience for him. If we intend to pass the success test and get
double for our trouble, we must release the pain of the past.

When we fail to release those who have hurt us, we are constantly
tethered to the past. (Illustrate this)

Being tethered to the past causes us to spend more time peering into
what happed at a former intersection of our life rather than following
the GPS (Gods Plan directed by the Spirit) into our future. "If you
limit your choices only to what seems possible or reasonable, you
disconnect yourself from what you truly want, and all that is left is a
compromise."

--- Robert Fritz

If anyone had reason to be bitter and have a critical attitude,
certainly Joseph did. What's our excuse? If we are bitter or critical we
need to ask ourselves:

- Have we been kidnapped or held against our will?

- Did our siblings put us in a pit or sell us into slavery?

- Have we spent years in prison as a result of false charges?

- Have we been separated from our families against our will for thirteen
 years?

When his first son was born, Joseph named him Manasseh, "The Lord
hath made me to forget"

The proof is unmistakable that Joseph had allowed God to take the pain
out of a past experience and allowed forgiveness to replace the hurt:

- He did not retaliate against the butler...

- He did not even the score with Mrs. Potiphar...

- He did not take revenge against his brothers...

- He blessed his father's house when he could have exterminated them...

Un-forgiveness locks the victim in a self- imposed prison. The only
means of escape is for the offended to forgive the offender.

Please allow me to ask some questions about what we plan to do when we
achieve the success we have been working toward:

- Are we going to flaunt the success before our naysayers?

- Are we going to retaliate against those who have opposed us?

- Are we going to exact revenge on those who have caused us pain?

- Are we going to undo the success of those who stepped on our fingers
 while they were climbing the ladder of success?

- Are we going to develop a bad attitude toward those who have treated
 us with a lack of dignity and respect along the way?

The test of success is not as easy as it seems.

Joseph named his first son Manasseh so that every time he referred to
his son by name, it was a constant reminder that he could not afford to
let un-forgiveness and bitterness cloud the vision God had called him
to. Joseph intended to get double for his trouble, and he knew that he
had to forgive to get it!

If we allow the pain of past experiences to become the guiding principle
in our lives, we will fail the success test and become another statistic
on the trash heap of human wreckage. If we refuse to forgive, we will
never know the joy of receiving double for our trouble.

If Joseph could endure all this and forget the past, surely you and I
can forget the past and serve God victoriously. Philippians 3:13-14
(NKJV) "Brethren, I do not count myself to have apprehended; but one
thing I do, forgetting those things which are behind and reaching
forward to those things which are ahead, ^14 ^I press toward the goal
for the prize of the upward call of God in Christ Jesus."

I want us to look at two very important aspects of the test of success:

- What should we forget? We should forget past hurts -- past failures --
 past successes.

- How do I forget? Forgive -- give up on the need to get even -- turn
 retribution over to God -- move on with our lives -- expend our energy
 and resources moving toward the vision, rather than trying to make
 sure the naysayers have been dealt with. Romans 12:19 (KJV) Dearly
 beloved, avenge not yourselves, but rather give place unto wrath: for
 it is written, "Vengeance is mine; I will repay," saith the Lord.

If we want double for our trouble, we should pay close attention to the
actions of Joseph when his brothers came to Egypt to purchase corn:

- He sold them corn and put their money back in their sacks.

- He kept Simeon so that they would bring Benjamin (a brother he had
 never met) back with them. When they returned, he made a feast for
 them.

- He sold them corn and supplies and once again put their money back in
 their sacks.

- He placed his silver cup in Benjamin's sack and let them go.

- After they left, he sent his servants to retrieve them.

- His servants searched their belongings and of course found Joseph's
 silver cup in Benjamin's sack.

His brothers pleaded for mercy and Joseph revealed his true identity.
Genesis 45:7-8 (KJV) "And God sent me before you to preserve you a
posterity in the earth, and to save your lives by a great deliverance.
^8 ^So now it was not you that sent me hither, but God: and he hath made
me a father to Pharaoh, and lord of all his house, and a ruler
throughout all the land of Egypt."

The real story here is the reason for Joseph's ascent to a position of
influence; he forgave his brothers before they asked for forgiveness.

How could he do such a thing? Because "The Lord hath made me to
forget."

Please note: From the time Joseph was named Prime Minister until his
brothers arrived in Egypt to buy corn, was approximately nine years.

We are made aware that Joseph had two sons, the name of his second son
was Ephraim. Ephraim means, "Double fruit."

Genesis 41:52 (KJV) "And the name of the second called he Ephraim:
For God hath caused me to be fruitful in the land of my affliction."

There are 2 things here for us to note:

- God caused Joseph to be fruitful. We must recognize that all success
 comes from God.

- God caused Joseph to be fruitful in the land of his affliction. Anyone
 can succeed when everything is going right! God is looking for people
 who have the tenacity to succeed in the good times as well as the bad
 times.

God is looking for some folks that can blossom despite the circumstances
and surroundings and maintain their integrity throughout the onslaught
of success.

How did God bless him?

- Financially... He became the Prime Minister and received the salary
 that went with such a position!

- Family... A wife and two sons! It doesn't get any better than that!
 Every time Joseph called one of his sons by name, he was reminding
 himself of the miracles of God!

- Position... Joseph's authority was second only to Pharaoh! Not too bad
 for an illegal immigrant!

CONCLUSION:

Though Joseph experienced the zenith of his success in Egypt, it was
never home to Joseph. According to the Scripture, we are also
"pilgrims and strangers" here. We are living in the land of
affliction, so to speak.

Joseph lived in the land of affliction, but he walked uprightly before
God. Because he walked uprightly before God, God blessed him.

No matter how good or difficult the journey becomes, we must keep in
mind that this world is not our end game. Joseph had a good run in
Egypt, but listen to what he says just before his death. Genesis
50:24-25 (KJV) And Joseph said unto his brethren, "I die: and God
will surely visit you, and bring you out of this land unto the land
which he sware to Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob." ^25 ^And Joseph took
an oath of the children of Israel, saying, "God will surely visit you,
and ye shall carry up my bones from hence."
