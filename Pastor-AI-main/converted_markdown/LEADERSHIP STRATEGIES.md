# LEADERSHIP STRATEGIES

Church Models:

There are three basic church models:

- A board led church... (In this model the board calls the shots and the
 pastor is a hireling. Trust issues are rampant)

- A pastor led church... (In this model the pastor/staff develop the
 vision, communicate it to the board and church leaders -- then
 together they sell it to the congregation)

- A combination of the two... (This model is the most dangerous of all
 possible scenarios. The potential for conflict is increased
 exponentially)

A board led church or a combination of the two is not a scriptural model
and I have never witnessed a church operating in any paradigm other than
a pastor led church reach its full potential.

My whole understanding for who should be in charge in the church comes
from the book of Acts. The book of Acts church was a pastor led church.

A few facts to consider:

- The book of Acts church was a pastor led church... (All healthy
 churches are pastor led)

- Pastors are gifted and called to lead the church... (Some people think
 that because they can run a business -- They can run a church -- These
 are two different paradigms)

- Lay leaders are gifted and called to assist the pastor in the
 implementation of the vision... (Lay leaders are predominantly Kings
 -- They are about provision)

- All healthy churches are pastor led... (the church cannot be healthier
 than the leader)

How can a church be transitioned from a Board led church to a Pastor led
church?

- Teach for change... (Expecting what one hasn't taught is unrealistic)

- Invest in people who get the vision... (Some people have been so
 indoctrinated with the systems of the past that they cannot
 transition)

- Remember, Rome wasn't built in a day... (These things take time)

- Be extremely selective in who is allowed onto the leadership team...
 (What are the requirements to get on your team? -- 21^st^ Century
 Leaders -- Second mile leadership

- Clearly communicate the expectations for those who serve in
 leadership... Forms and potential Board Member Meetings)

Church Conflict:

The first-century church had conflict! Acts 6:1(NKJV) "The
Hellenistic Jews (Jews who had adopted the Greek language and
culture) among them complained against the Hebraic Jews because their
widows were being overlooked in the daily distribution of food."
(Everything was not perfect in the First Century Church)

There arose a deep division in the church. If anyone is looking for a
perfect church, let me help you -- it does not exist!

Some strategies of a healthy church include:

- The board should agree to operate on a closed agenda meeting... (The
 pastor & board submit all agenda items for the board meeting two weeks
 in advance)

- The pastor sets the agenda according to the items submitted...

- The board agenda is sent to everyone who is going to be in attendance
 one week prior to the meeting...

- Each person makes the agenda a matter of prayer and listens intently
 for the voice of the Lord...

- All discussion of agenda items should be conducted with everyone
 present so that each leader is fully informed...

- Neither the pastor nor the board should be allowed to caucus
 concerning agenda items...

- The board meets and prayerfully works their way through the agenda...

- When the agenda says adjourn, there should be no more official
 business conducted...

- Under no circumstances should anyone be allowed to bring something
 into the meeting that was not on the agenda... (Including and
 especially the Pastor -- This requires forethought and a great deal of
 planning)

Conflict Resolution:

Please notice the first-century church was being greatly moved upon and
used by the Holy Spirit. Just because the Holy Spirit is working in
one's life or ministry, it does not automatically follow that there will
be no strife or conflict. Quite the contrary is true. The devil is
attracted to the presence of God. (Job -- When the sons of God got
together the devil showed up! I've seen him hanging out with the sons of
God more times than I care to count)

The reason some churches never have conflict is because they have not
done enough in decades to stir up the devil!

Conflict resolution in the church MUST have the pastor's fingerprints on
it, but the conflict should be resolved at the lowest possible level.

The conflict resolution plan should go something like this:

- The pastor gives oversight to all conflict management...

- The pastor delegates as much conflict resolution as possible...

- The leadership team must stand united during conflict...

<!-- -->

- All conflict management should be conducted with the well-being of the
 church as the ultimate goal...

- All conflict management should occur with the restoration and dignity
 of the offenders in mind...

- The board of the church should rise to the defense of the pastor and
 staff except in cases of a moral issue...

- The board should seek an outside entity to resolve all accusations of
 moral misconduct by the pastor...

Selecting Key Leaders:

Look at how the First Century Church resolved conflict. The apostles
assigned a group of individuals to select seven men full of the Holy
Spirit to whom they could delegate, certain responsibilities.

Right here is where far too many churches introduce dysfunction into the
life of the church. These leaders were not chosen to tell the Apostles
what to do. They were chosen so that the pastor and staff would have
qualified lay people to whom they could delegate practical and
ministerial responsibilities.

Acts 6:6 (NIV) "They presented these men to [the apostles, who prayed
and laid their hands on them]{.underline}." Please notice this church
did not choose people who were incompatible with the apostles, nor did
they have a different view of ministry than the apostles; they chose men
that the apostles readily accepted and upon whom they felt completely
comfortable laying their hand of approval.

In the minds of the First-Century church member, when the apostles laid
their hands upon these men, this person became an extension of the
ministry of the apostle.

Lay leaders must be:

- Scripturally qualified...

- Thoroughly vetted... (Know those who labor among you)

- Ministry minded... (Are they engaged in ministry BEFORE their
 selection?)

- Completely compatible with the pastor and his team...

- Representatives of the leader not of the people... (Disagreement is
 unavoidable -- Disrespect is unacceptable)

- Team players... (Disloyalty is unforgettable -- Dishonor is
 untreatable)
