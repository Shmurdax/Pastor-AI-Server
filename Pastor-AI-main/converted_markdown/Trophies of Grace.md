# TROPHIES OF GRACE

2 Timothy 2:20-22 (NJKV)"But in a great house there are not only
vessels of gold and silver, but also of wood and clay, some for honor
and some for dishonor. 21 Therefore [if anyone cleanses himself from the
latter, he will be a vessel for honor]{.underline}, sanctified and
useful for the Master, prepared for every good work. 22 Flee also
youthful lusts; but pursue righteousness, faith, love, peace with those
who call on the Lord out of a pure heart."

The "Great House" in our text is the church -- the household of
faith, the family of God.

Even in God's house there are those vessels that are fit for noble
purposes and those that are not.

In our modern homes there are vessels for different purposes.

Vessels of honor:

- China dishes...

- Family heirlooms...

- Antiques and keepsakes...

Vessels of dishonor:

- Cleaning rags...

- Trash cans...

- Floor mats...

In the great house there were vessels of honor and dishonor. All of us
can be vessels of honor also known as "Trophies of Grace." The
difference between vessels of dishonor and "Trophies of grace" is
very simple -- "If anyone cleanses himself, he will be a vessel for
honor, sanctified and useful for the Master, prepared for every good
work."

Each person we celebrate today was once a vessel of dishonor but today
they are being celebrated as a "Trophy of Grace." How is this
possible? It is possible by doing three things:

- Cleanse yourself... (Nothing but the blood -- I'm not perfect, just
 forgiven)

- Flee youthful lusts... (Most addictions and self-defeating life
 patterns begin in our youth)

- Pursue righteousness... (The proof of desire is pursuit)

If you do these three things you will miraculously be transformed from a
vessel of dishonor into a "Trophy of Grace!"
