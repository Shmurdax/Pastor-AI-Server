# IT IS TIME FOR JESUS

TEXT: Ephesians 1:7-12 (NKJV) "In Him we have redemption through
His blood, the forgiveness of sins, according to the riches of His
grace ^8 ^which He made to abound toward us in all wisdom and
prudence, ^9 ^having made known to us the mystery of His will, according
to His good pleasure which He purposed in Himself, ^10 ^that in the
dispensation of the [fullness of the times]{.underline} He might gather
together in one all things in Christ, both which are in heaven and which
are on earth---in Him. ^11 ^In Him also we have obtained an inheritance,
being predestined according to the purpose of Him who works all things
according to the counsel of His will, ^12 ^that we who first trusted in
Christ should be to the praise of His glory."

Galatians 4:4-5 (NKJV) ^"^But when the [fullness of the
time]{.underline} had come, God sent forth His Son, born of a
woman, born under the law, ^5 ^to redeem those who were under the
law, that we might receive the adoption as sons."

INTRO: In the opening remarks of his letter to the Ephesians, Paul
feels it important to let them know right up front what it means to be
"in Christ". In doing so, he not only addressed what they could
expect if they continued "in Christ", but what those of us who
would come along at a later time could expect, if we continue, "in
Christ".

Then he say's something very curious in Vs. 10. "In the dispensation
of the [fullness of the times]{.underline} He might gather together in
one all things in Christ, both which are in heaven and which are on
earth---in Him." The word "fullness" is the word "Plhrwma"
(Pla-ro-mah) which means to complete or to fill up. In other words,
there will be a point, when time has filled up, that certain things will
happen on earth as well as in heaven. (That is a completely different
sermon)

In his letter to the Galatians, with regard to the first coming of
Christ, Paul writes ^"^But [when the fullness of the time had
come]{.underline}, God sent forth His Son, born of a woman, born under
the law, ^5 ^to redeem those who were under the law, that we might
receive the adoption as sons." The word "fullness" as it is
used here is the same word, "Plhrwma" (Pla-ro-mah) meaning to complete
or to fill up. In other words, "When the appointed time had fully
filled up..."

- When the time came...

- When the time was right...

- At the appointed time...

- At precisely the right time...

Listen to what Paul said would occur when time had filled completely up.
"God sent forth His Son, born of a woman, born under the law, to
redeem THOSE WHO WERE UNDER THE LAW, that WE might receive the adoption
as sons." When the time for Jesus' first coming had fully arrived,
His coming affected everyone who was alive then and there, those who
have lived since, and those of us who are alive now!

It is time for Jesus!

- Born -- Die...

- Plant -- Harvest...

- Kill -- Heal...

- Break down -- Build up...

- Weep -- Laugh...

- Mourn -- Dance...

- Lose -- Gain...

- Love -- Hate...

- Etc...

While there is a time for all things, there are certain things that
cannot happen until "Time has filled up."

It is time for Jesus!

THE FIRST COMING OF JESUS DID NOT OCCUR UNTIL THE TIME WAS FULLY COME
-- THE TIME WAS EXACTLY RIGHT

Galatians made this abundantly clear: ^"^[When the fullness of the
time had come]{.underline}, God sent forth His Son, born of a
woman, born under the law, ^5 ^to redeem those who were under the
law, that we might receive the adoption as sons."

Listen to what was said of Simeon, the uncle of Jesus: Luke 2:25-32
(KJV) "There was a man in Jerusalem whose name was Simeon, and this
man was just and devout, waiting for the Consolation of Israel, and the
Holy Spirit was upon him. ^26 ^And it had been revealed to him by the
Holy Spirit that [he would not see death before he had seen the Lord's
Christ]{.underline}. ^27 ^So he came by the Spirit into the temple. And
when the parents brought in the Child Jesus, to do for Him according to
the custom of the law, ^28 ^he took Him up in his arms and blessed God
and said: ^29 ^"Lord, now You are letting Your servant depart in peace,
According to Your word; ^30 ^For [my eyes have seen Your
salvation]{.underline} ^31 ^Which You have prepared before the face of
all peoples, ^32 ^A light to bring revelation to the Gentiles, and the
glory of Your people Israel."

Look at what was said of Joseph of Arimathea. Luke 23:50-51 (NKJV)
"There was a man named Joseph, a council member, a good and just
man. ^51 ^He had not consented to their decision and deed. He was from
Arimathea, a city of the Jews, who himself was also [waiting for the
kingdom of God."]{.underline}

Simeon and Joseph were waiting for Jesus, and when time filled up, they
saw Him with their very own eyes! I am looking for Jesus; you are
looking for Jesus; and when time fills up, we are going to see him!

It is time for Jesus!

THE SECOND COMING OF JESUS WILL NOT OCCUR UNTIL THE TIME HAS FULLY
COME

Because of the world's situation, the question on the lips of everyone
is: "When will Jesus return?" "When will the end of the world come?"
Listen to the words of Jesus - Matthew 24:32-36 (NKJV) "Learn this
parable from the fig tree: When its branch has already become tender and
puts forth leaves, you know that summer is near. ^33 ^So you also, when
you see all these things, know that it is near---at the
doors! ^34 ^Assuredly, I say to you, [this generation will by no means
pass away]{.underline} till all these things take place. ^35 ^Heaven and
earth will pass away, but My words will by no means pass away. ^36 ^"But
of that day and hour [no one knows]{.underline}, not even the angels of
heaven, but My Father only." 

We cannot predict the day nor hour of the second coming of Jesus. We
cannot predict the year. But we can know the season based on what the
Scripture say's will be happening just prior to His coming!
