INTRO: The setting for the first eleven verses of Acts chapter one
is this:

- God had become a man through Jesus...

- Jesus was born of a virgin birth...

- He lived 33 ½ sinless years...

- He had 3 ½ years of prolific ministry accentuated by signs, miracles,
 and wonders...

- He had been falsely accused, sentenced, and executed...

- He had been dead for three days conquering the keys of hell and of
 death...

- He arose from the dead leaving his grave clothes intact and the napkin
 neatly folded...

- Following His resurrection He spent forty day's proving His
 resurrection... "He also presented Himself alive after His
 suffering by many infallible proofs, being seen by them during forty
 days and speaking of the things pertaining to the kingdom of God."

In this passage Jesus and His disciples found themselves at Bethany's
launching pad, and suddenly Jesus was taken up into Heaven! This would
be the end right? Wrong, this was just the beginning!

I want us to place ourselves in the disciples shoes on that day:

- Jesus had promised to build a Kingdom...

- Rather than building a Kingdom he died...

- Just about the time they accepted His death and decided to move on
 with their lives, He was alive again...

- After forty day's they were beginning to believe this Kingdom talk
 again...

- At Bethany's launching pad they asked, "Lord, will You at this time
 restore the kingdom to Israel?"

Then, all of a sudden He is gone! Gone!!!

What were they to make of this? They have been on an emotional roller
coaster for the past six weeks! In Jesus fashion, He had no more than
disappeared when the disciples found themselves staring at some angels
giving them a promise for their future! The promise the angels made to
the disciples is also a promise for our future! Acts 1:10-11 (NKJV)
"And while they looked steadfastly toward heaven as He went up,

This is a PROMISE made by angels to men, the disciples, and you and I!
Let me paraphrase it for you - "This same Jesus that has gone into
Heaven is coming again in the clouds of Heaven."

The promise made to men by angels reminds me of a promise Douglas
McArthur made to the citizens of the Philippines during World War II.
During the later days of the war, General McArthur's men had taken a
beating so he withdrew them and moved them to Australia to recover. As
he boarded the DC 9 in the Philippines it was obvious to General
McArthur that the Philippine population was anxious about their future.

Standing there noticing the fear and anxiousness on the faces of the
Philippine people he spoke three words that have been immortalized in
American history, "I shall return!"

Several months of war continued but one day the Philippine skies were
filled with the hum of military planes as General McArthur made good on
his promise to return to the Philippines!
