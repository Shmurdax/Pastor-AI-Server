In this passage of Scripture, the Children of Israel followed God into a
cul-de-sac. They found themselves between hostile hills and the edge of
a sea, too deep to be forded, and too wide to be crossed. The
unmistakable indication of Exodus 14:1-4 is that the Lord took
responsibility for leading them into this very dangerous place -- AND He
did it on purpose!

He gave them specific, step-by-step instructions, leading them down a
route to apparent ruin: "Turn and camp; camp there; there, before the
entrapping sea; yes, right there in that impossible place."

How could Moses and Aaron keep from worrying when:

- The Red Sea was facing them?

- The mountains were beside them?

- The soldiers of Egypt were bearing down on them with swords drawn?

How can we keep from worrying when hell appears to have emptied out into
our living room, and our mother in law is threatening to move in on us?
We must keep our focus on Jesus!

The Egyptians were not one bit concerned about a confrontation with the
Israelites because these people had spent the last 400 years living as
slaves. Not one of them had been trained in the intricacies of warfare,
nor did one of them own a weapon!

What the Egyptians did not know is that the Israelites did not need a
weapon; they did not need to know how to fight; they had a secret
weapon - and His name is Jehovah!

Please understand that God was not being disingenuous to Israel by
having them in such a vulnerable spot:

- He was about to deal with Israel's enemies once and for all!

- He was about to allow them to see Him in a way they had never seen Him
 before! (Lazarus is dead and I am glad...)

- He was about to provide them with weapons to protect themselves,
 during the forty plus years of wilderness wandering!

Life can be very, very difficult. The reason life can be so difficult is
because God has appointed a destiny for us and there is a real, live
enemy, who will do everything in his power to sabotage and keep us back
from reaching our ultimate potential.

(TAKE ARIEL VIEW OF THE ISRAELITES AT THE RED SEA DOWN)

The Israelites could have denied the existence of an enemy, but that
would not have changed the fact that a hostile king was in hot pursuit
of this group of emancipated slaves.

Denial of the enemy never calms his hostilities. Acknowledgement of his
existence demands readiness. Charles Spurgeon once said, "The great
tyrant has not forgotten you, and he designs your capture and
re-enslavement."

When dealing with difficulty, it is imperative that we keep our focus on
the Lord.

There is little question with regard to the intentions of our enemy. He
is malicious, malignant, and venomous. I assure us, the enemy is not
tempting us to allow us to have a good time; he is attempting to drag us
down to Hell.

How do we overcome our opponent?

- The blood of Jesus Christ forgives our sins and resolves our guilt!

- The resurrection frees us from the fear of death, and satisfies our
 need for eternal significance and happi­ness!

- The presence of the Lord surrounds us!

- The promises of the Bible sustain us!

- The grace of God heals the wounds this world inflicts upon us!

- Our testimony becomes our triumphant shout, and the final nail in the
 coffin of the enemy!

We can be assured; our enemy will attempt to trap us in our troubles;
entangle us in our dilemmas; corner us in an impossible situation; or
lure us into temptation.

CONCLUSION:

When the Israelites were trapped at the Red Sea, it would have been very
easy for them to get their focus off of what God was about to do and
onto what the Egyptians were attempting to do.

When threatened by the devil's schemes, we should focus on the Word.
Daniel 11:32 (CEV) "The king will use deceit to win followers from
those who are unfaithful to God, but [those who remain faithful will do
everything possible to oppose him]{.underline}." James 4:7-8 (CEV)
"Surrender to God! [Resist the devil, and he will run from
you]{.underline}. 8 Come near to God, and he will come near to you.
Clean up your lives, you sinners. Purify your hearts, you people who
can't make up your mind." 1 Peter 5:9 (CEV) "But you must [resist
the devil and stay strong in your faith.]{.underline} You know that all
over the world the Lord's followers are suffering just as you are."

We should:

Resist the enemy in the name of Jesus...

Stand our ground against the trespasser...

shake off discourage­ment, and make the devil take it back...

Every time we resist the slightest temptation, we honor God. Every time
we overcome even the smallest problem by trusting and obeying the Lord
Jesus, God is glorified in our lives.

The devil came to church one Sunday and started shouting insults at the
crowd. Everyone vacated the building except one little old man. The
devil shouted to the old man, "Don't you know who I am?" To which the
man calmly replied, "Yes." The devil said, "Are you not afraid of
me?" The man said "no." The devil could not believe his ears and
said, "Why are you not afraid of me?" The man replied, "I was married
to your sister for fifty-five years!"

The devil orchestrates all manner of attacks against us, and one of his
most effective weapons is distraction. At the Red Sea, the Israelites
were distracted by the thunderous noise of Pharaoh's chariot wheels, but
Moses calmly declared to the Israelites... " [Don\'t be afraid! Be
brave, and you will see the LORD save you today]{.underline}. These
Egyptians will never bother you again. The LORD will fight for you,
and you won\'t have to do a thing."

Pharaoh could:

- Threaten with brute force...

- Protest their emancipation...

- Stir up frightening clouds of chariot dust...

- Terrify with a thousand clanging swords...

Pharaoh was powerless to actually harm the Israelites as long as they
remained focused on the Lord. All the Israelites had to do was:

- Remain calm, cool and collected...

- Follow the instructions of Moses...

- Shut out the noise of Pharaoh and his army...

- Focus on a God who can part a Red Sea...

When we become focused on the Lord, the very thing that defeats the
enemy brings our victory into view:

- The Rod of Moses pointed the Israelites toward their victory, while
 pointing the Egyptians to their watery grave...

- The pillar of cloud became darkness to the Egyptians, and a guiding
 light to the Israelites...

- The wind became a path of deliverance to Israel, and a death trap to
 the Egyptians...

- The Passover would later become a celebration of emancipation for the
 Israelites, and the remembrance of a mass funeral to the Egyptians...

As long as we stay focused on the Lord, there is nothing that hell can
devise which will be successful against us! Isaiah 54:17 (NKJV) "[No
weapon formed against you shall prosper]{.underline}, And [every tongue
which rises against you in judgment You shall condemn]{.underline}. This
is the [heritage of the servants of the
[Lord]{.smallcaps}]{.underline}[,]{.smallcaps} And their righteousness
is from Me," Says the [Lord]{.smallcaps}."

It is time to focus on the Lord.

The reason the Children of Israel were successful against their enemy at
the Red Sea is because Moses and Aaron refused to divert their attention
from the Lord to Pharaoh. They acknowledged the pursuit of the
Egyptians, but decided to focus on what Jehovah was about to do!

Look at the Miracle of the Red Sea crossing:

- The winds blew the sea split!

- The waters congealed into towering walls!

- The Israelites passed through on dry ground!

- Jehovah dried out the Red Sea but did not leave one footprint!

This happened not for the entertainment value of the experience, but to
prove to the Israelites and to us, in earth shaking, history-making
fashion that; even when we are most anxious and distressed; God will
make a way when there seems to be no way!

Focus does not mean that we will not experience difficulty:

- The Red Sea may roll before us...

- The desert may entrap us...

- The enemy may press in on our heels...

- The past may seem implausible...

- The future may seem impossible...

...but I assure us, God is working in ways we cannot see! This one thing
I am absolutely sure of - God will make a way of escape for those who
keep their focus on Him! Isaiah 43:19 (CEV) "I am creating something
new. There it is! Do you see it? [I have put roads in deserts, streams
in thirsty lands]{.underline}."

CONCLUSION:

Focus on this:

- There is no sea deeper than the ocean of His love!

- There is no army stronger than His hosts!

- There is no force greater than His throne of grace!

- There is no enemy that exists who can overcome His direct and indirect
 work in our lives!
