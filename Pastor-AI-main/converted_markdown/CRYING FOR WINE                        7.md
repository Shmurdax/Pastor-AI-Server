# CRYING FOR WINE 7-27-03

Isaiah 24:11 There is a crying for wine in the streets; all joy is
darkened, the mirth of the land is gone.

In their book, [Fearfully and Wonderfully Made]{.underline}, Dr. Paul
Brand and Philip Yancey, make an attempt to do what they call, "A
Surgeon's look at the Human and Spiritual Body". Their book explores the
complex piece of creation called the human body. With knowledge gained
from the world of science and medicine, they probe deep into the world
of cells, bones, skin, muscle, and nerves to show how the human body so
authentically expresses spiritual reality.

And they take from David's writing a phrase suggesting that this
creature from God is truly fearfully and wonderfully made.

Saint Augustine said, "Men go abroad to wonder at the height of
mountains, at the huge waves of the sea, at the long courses of the
rivers, at the vast compass of the ocean, at the circular motion of the
stars; and they pass by themselves without wondering.

For centuries the body has been the mystical creation that eludes the
reality of mankind. When you stop and think of the creation, and the
makeup of Adam and eventually us, it blows the mind. How can something
so complex and so detailed, come from something so common. We are from
dirt, my friends.

Yet somehow when creating this complex body, God so designed it with
something inside that has never been found by the microscope or other
probing instruments.

Inside that body lies the part that was left void and vacant. Empty was
how Job called it. Solomon said to fill it with anything but God was
simply vanity.

There lies inside each man, woman, boy, and girl that place...a place so
put there by God. That place is void of satisfaction, empty of comfort,
and without real peace until the creator come and fills it with a divine
presence.

That place cannot be filled by games or garments; it cannot be satisfied
with pleasures or personal gain; it will never be complacent with the
world's allurements or wonders of the flesh.

Whether you reside in America or Africa; Europe or England; the North or
South Pole, you cannot find anything that will fill this position
outside the realm of the Almighty God, Jehovah, King of Kings, Lord of
Lord, the Great I AM, Jesus Christ.

Inside that intricate body, lies the personal position that God put
there which is designed so as to only accept His will and plan.

You can fill it with things but like the Teflon of a pan, they won't
stick. They come and go and go and go until they no longer satisfy. The
old song so capably describes this dilemma of mankind.

"Only Jesus Can Satisfy Your Soul.

Only He can change your life

And make your whole.

He'll give you peace, you never knew,

Sweet Love and joy, and heaven too,

For Only Jesus Can Satisfy Your Soul."

All of creation groans and utters pains of hopelessness without this
presence in their lives. A person or group of people can attempt to find
and gain and hold to things and pleasures and gain, only to find it
filled with nothing.

There is a divine presence that is needed to satisfy and fill the
longing of the creation of God. He breathed into Adam the breath of
live. He still needs to breathe into humanity a breath that provides not
just the natural life but the eternal life that is so necessary for
future survival.

We need Him. Oh, how we need Him. This need is as much reality as it is
for a baby to need its mother at birth. WE need Him. How do I know? As
the kids sing the song, I know, because the Bible tells me so.

I cannot take the time to review the scene the prophet Isaiah saw. His
vision of things happening in that day was one of horror. And even
though I refuse to take the time to elaborate on the details, please
notice with me the words and phrases that this old prophet uses to
describe the scene.

Empty, waste, emptied and utterly spoiled, the earth fadeth; the
covenants defiled, transgression of the law, ordinances changed, the
everlasting covenant broken,.

Notice the response when discussing the joy of the land. The
merry-hearted do sigh; the mirth of the tambourine ceases, the noise of
the jubilant ends, the joy of the harp ceases.

Then he describes the city. The city of confusion is broken down, every
house is shut up, the gate is stricken with destruction.

How horrible. What a terrible scene.

But in the midst of all the descriptions and illustrations, the prophet
makes mention of one thing of importance. He said that there was a
crying for wine in the streets!

Sure, that was then and this is now. No one is crying like this today.

No one is crying for wine, preacher. Where is the relevance?

It's simple. In the scriptures there are many metaphors and types that
provide description as to why a writer was speaking. Wine is one of
them. Wine in the New Testament is symbolic of the presence of God or
the Holy Ghost.

Peter stood with the eleven at Pentecost and after being accused of
drinking strong drink, said, "these are not drunk as ye suppose..." He
did not deny they were drunk, just not drunk with strong drink. These
were filled with the Holy Ghost and the presence of God in ones life
makes a total difference.

There is a crying going on in the streets and whether people accept it
or not, it is a cry for the wine of God's presence.

The world needs more than religion...it needs the presence of the
Almighty.

This world needs more than just a vision for the future...it needs a
hope for the future that can only be found in the presence of God.

This world needs more than a lofty idea that will help it become
better...it is in desperate need of the Holy Ghost flowing in their
inner most being.

This world is crying. Crying for help and solutions

and answers. This world is crying. They need to have the Holy Ghost wine
flowing through their lives.

This church needs a brand new touch of the Holy Ghost so that it can
flow through the streets of the city.

Wine was a medicine in the Biblical days and still is in some areas. God
knows that we need spiritual medicine today. This world needs something
that will cleanse it from the filth, the sin, the evil, the degradation
that is so obvious today. There is a cry for wine.

We need the wine of the spirit moving in our lives so that we stay clean
and pure with all righteousness.

something to calm it down; to provide it with rest; to grant comfort and

Not only does the world need rest, but the church needs rest. We need
the comfort of the Holy Ghost in our lives.

Wine was used as a means of celebration especially that of joyful

The church needs relief also. Tension, stress, worry, fear...all are no

Give us joy, peace, righteousness, relief, comfort, guidance, direction,
power, strength...all available through the Holy Ghost wine!
