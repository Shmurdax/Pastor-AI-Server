# THE PROOF OF REPENTANCE

TEXT: 2 Cor. 7:8-11 "For though I made you sorry with a letter, I
do not [repent]{.underline}, though I did [repent]{.underline}: for I
perceive that the same epistle hath made you sorry, though it were but

sorrowed to [repentance]{.underline}: for ye were made sorry after a
godly manner, that ye might receive damage by us in nothing. For
godly sorrow worketh [repentance]{.underline} to salvation not to be
[repented]{.underline} of: but the sorrow of the world worketh death.
For behold this selfsame thing, that ye sorrowed after a godly sort,
what [carefulness]{.underline} it wrought in you, yea, what [clearing of
yourselves]{.underline}, yea, what [indignation]{.underline}, yea, what
[fear]{.underline}, yea, what [vehement desire]{.underline}, yea,

what [zeal]{.underline}, yea, what [revenge]{.underline}! In all things
ye have approved yourselves to be clear in this matter."

INTRO: The Apostle Paul is writing his second letter to the
Corinthian Church here. This man has some very obvious feelings of love
and concern for this Church. (He cares about what they think!) 2 Cor.
7:8-9 "For though I made you sorry with a letter, I do not repent,
[though I did repent]{.underline}: for I perceive that the same epistle
hath made you sorry, [though it were but for a season]{.underline}.
Now I rejoice, [not that ye were made sorry, but that ye sorrowed to
repentance]{.underline}: for ye were made sorry after a godly manner,
that ye might receive damage by us in nothing."

The reason Paul has a special place in his heart for this particular
Church is that on his second missionary journey, which was somewhere
around A.D. 50, he became the first man ever to share the good news of
the Gospel in the city of Corinth!

The City of Corinth was a very wealthy commercial center. Because of
it's commerce, and geographical location it became a hub of activity not
only for those who lived there but also for those in surrounding areas.

The moral climate of the city of Corinth was such, that by the most
conservative of estimates, it was a City of great wickedness. The
wickedness of the City caused it to be very fertile soil for the seed of
the Gospel! Romans 5:20 "... Where sin abounded, grace did much
more abound:"

Out of that missionary journey came the Corinthian Church!

Paul not only spent a lot of time in this City declaring the good news
of Jesus Christ, but he expended much effort giving written instructions
to these people on how to order their personal lives, as well as their
religious activities!

Paul begins verse eight of our text by saying, "for though I made you
sorry with a letter..."

Paul refers to this letter in 2 Cor. 2:4 "For out of much
affliction and anguish of heart I wrote unto you with many tears; [not
that ye should be grieved]{.underline}, but that ye might know the love
which I have more abundantly unto you."

There are two schools of thought with regard to this seemingly
mysterious "letter".

One School of thought is that there was a very severe "letter" from
Paul to the Corinthian Church that was somehow lost and never recorded.

The second school of thought is that he is referring to the book of
First Corinthians. After much study I am inclined to agree with this
second scenario.

The reason I am inclined to agree with the latter of these two opinions
is because of what Paul said in his first letter to the Corinthian
Christians! (Paul makes my preaching sound easy!)

1) He addresses the divisions in the Church!

2) He addresses the source of the divisions!

3) He addresses the absence of discipline in the Church!

4) He addresses civil litigation between members of the body of
Christ!

5) He addresses the moral decline of members of the Church!

6) He addresses marriage and divorce!

7) He addresses the eating of food sacrificed to idols! etc...

Now if you are a guilty party, this kind of preaching will make you do
one of two things, get mad, or "REPENT"!!! (If you throw a rock into
a pack of dogs the only one who barks is the one who got hit!)

This brings us to our subject matter for this message, "REPENTANCE"!

The doctrine of "REPENTANCE" is a very important doctrine in your
Bible. 110 times the word "REPENTANCE" or some form of the word is
used in Holy Writ!

Notice this, in Four verses the Apostle Paul uses the word "REPENT"
twice, the word "REPENTED" once, the word "REPENTANCE" twice,
the word "SORRY" Four times, the word "SORROW" twice, and the
word "SORROWED" twice! Now what message do you think the Apostle was
trying to drive across here?

I want us to look at the word "REPENTANCE", in hopes that we can
gain a proper understanding of it's meaning in our day to day lives.

"REPENTANCE"! As it is used here, comes from the Greek word
"METANOIA" (met-an-oy-ah),meaning compunction for guilt including
reformation!

The word "COMPUNCTION" means anxiety arising from the awareness of
guilt. To "REFORM" means to amend or improve by change of form, or
removal of faults or abuses!

So I think we could safely say that "REPENTANCE" means that we see
where we have been wrong, acknowledge it, and set out with renewed
passion never to repeat it!

This world has no problem with the words "I'M SORRY" but I wonder
how much real "REPENTANCE" there is in this world, or better yet in
the Church?

Paul said, Godly sorrow brings about "REPENTANCE"!

"REPENTANCE" is not a popular topic, but I can assure you that it is
an important one!

You can't get saved without "REPENTING"! You certainly can't live
for God long term without "REPENTING"!

"REPENTANCE" has become a lost sermon of the Church of this modern
hour, but it was on the top of Paul's list of candy sticks to preach!

There is much debate on the topic of what true "REPENTANCE" is. Some
believe you MUST shed tears! I've seen people shed enough tears to
generate enough electricity for this town for several days, but they
still don't live right!

Some people believe you must shake a preachers hand, or join the Church!
I've seen people do both, but it did not change their lives!

So what is "THE PROOF OF REPENTANCE"?

Paul say's in verse Eleven there are Seven attributes that will
assimilate themselves in the lives of those who learn the art of
"REPENTING", and I want us to look very closely at them because I
believe they "PROVE REPENTANCE";

## I. WHAT CAREFULNESS!

A. 2 Cor. 7:11 "For behold this selfsame thing, that ye
sorrowed after a godly sort, what [carefulness]{.underline} it wrought
in you,..."

The word "CAREFULNESS", as it is used here, comes from the Greek
word "SPOUDE" (spoo-day), meaning eagerness or earnestness!

Think about it. When you've been wrong, or sinned, that sin has been
discovered, you have owned up to it, and you have made the proper
apologies because you are truly sorry! Does it not make you far more
"CAREFUL" or cautious when you approach the tempting point of that
particular failure or sin the next time?

A portion of the "PROOF OF REPENTANCE" is that we will be
"CAREFUL" to make things right with God, ourselves, and others!

Paul knew what it meant to sin against the Lord! 1 Tim. 1:15 "This
is a faithful saying, and worthy of all acceptation, that Christ Jesus
came into the world to save sinners; of whom I am chief."

Paul knew that he had spent a great deal of his life disappointing the
Lord with his lifestyle. However, when he was convicted by the Holy
Spirit, he "REPENTED", and became extremely "CAREFUL" never to
repeat his past failures!

B. This world's system say's, if you've done something wrong and
were unfortunate to get caught, just say you are sorry, pay the minimum
penalty, and move on! It's no big deal!

I have found that the sorrow of the world is for having been caught, not
for what they have done!

Our justice system is a prime example of this short sighted philosophy.
Violent criminals are being allowed early release not because they have
been rehabilitated, but because of good behavior!

I want to tell you that anyone can be on good behavior for short periods
of time. However, real "REPENTANCE" will make you "CAREFUL" or
"EAGER" to do what ever is necessary to make sure you never repeat
that same mistake again!

"CAREFULNESS" is "PROOF OF REPENTANCE"!

## IV. WHAT FEAR!

"PHOBOS" (Fob'-os), which is derived from the word "PHEBOMAI"
(Feb-om-i) which means alarm or fright, or to be terrified!

In other words Paul is saying here that when your sin has been exposed,
and you have truly "REPENTED" the "PROOF OF THAT REPENTANCE"
will be "TERROR" with regard to that failure!

This goes farther than just a healthy respect!

Please understand that I know that we are not to be living in
"FEAR"! 2 Tim. 1:7 "For God hath not given us [the spirit of
fear]{.underline}; but of power, and of love, and of a sound mind."

However, the fact that we realize we are human beings who experience
moments of weakness, should strike a cord of "FEAR" in our hearts
with regard to our ability to fail!

When you are "AFRAID" of something, you stay away from it! I am
"AFRAID" of snakes! I would never go to a Church that handles
snakes! I am so "AFRAID" of snakes that I kill ALL snakes!

I am "AFRAID" of heights! I will not climb to the top of a
skyscraper to look off! I stay as far from the edge as I possibly can!!!
Why? Because heights "TERRIFY" me!

## V. WHAT VEHEMENT DESIRE!

A. 2 Cor. 7:11 "For behold this selfsame thing, that ye
sorrowed after a godly sort, what carefulness it wrought in you, yea,
what clearing of yourselves, yea, what indignation, yea, what fear, yea,
what [vehement desire]{.underline}..."

The word "VEHEMENT" as it is used here comes from the Greek word
"EPIPOTHESIS" (ep-ee-poth'-ay-sis), meaning "a longing for".

It also means "yearning", or "intense craving"!

The word "DESIRE" comes from the same Greek word meaning exactly the
same thing.

It is believed that the writer here uses the same word twice, for
emphasis sake.

Think about it. Paul's sinful condition has been exposed through the
conviction of the Holy Spirit! He has "REPENTED" of his sin, and
say's that the "PROOF OF THAT REPENTANCE" will be evidenced by what
he "LONGS FOR"!!!

Paul say's to the Colossian Christians, Col. 3:1-3 "If ye then be
risen with Christ, [seek]{.underline} those things which are above,
where Christ sitteth on the right hand of God. [Set your
affection]{.underline} on things above, not on things on the earth.
For ye are dead, and your life is hid with Christ in God."
