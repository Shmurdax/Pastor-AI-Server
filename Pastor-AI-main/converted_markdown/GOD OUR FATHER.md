# GOD OUR FATHER

INTRO: One of the many important decisions confronting new parents
is, "What will we name our baby?" (I am a Jr. -- We have four children
-- Jason & Kim -- Jeremy & Larissa - We have six grandchildren --
Braden, Rylee, Abrianna, Gabriel, Mason & Mila.)

The decision to name a child is one of the most important decisions a
parent will make because it is one that will likely follow the child the
rest of their lives! Unless the child decides to go through the legal
process of changing their name, they are stuck with the name they have
been given, for a lifetime. They are saddled with the negative or
positive consequences of a decision they had no influence over!

I could spend the rest of this service calling the names of people who
have wonderful names, and the very mention of their name brings positive
thoughts. On the other hand, some names leave you wondering, "What was
the parent thinking?"

Quite honestly, I have met a handful of people along the way, that if my
parents had given me such a name, I would have gone to court and changed
my name when I was old enough to do so! My thinking on this is, if they
were thoughtless enough to do this to a child, they should be numb
witted enough not to be offended when their child changes their name!

Because of the importance of a name, most new parents spend hours, days,
weeks, even months debating this question. Most parents arrive at a good
enough decision.

Susan and I have been through this process two times. Jason means
"Healer", Don means "Ruler." The name Larissa means "Cheerful or
lighthearted" and Suzanne means "Lily."

Names are important because one tends to become what the name
represents. Once the parents have decided on a name, the real challenge
begin: helping the child live up to the name they have been assigned.

Every so often we run across a person with multiple names, such as
Charles Phillip Arthur George Windsor. (I just want to say -- Why did
you do that to that child?) That sounds odd to us, until we discover its
Prince Charles! Such a name is quite long for a child to bear, but
Prince Charles is royalty, and royalty calls for such a name.

We are currently doing a series on The Father -- So, over the next two
Sunday's I would like to focus on the name "Everlasting Father".

EVERLASTING FATHER

In the Hebrew, the phrase "Everlasting Father" literally means
"the Father of Eternity."

This is a brain teaser, but God existed before time began. He has been
declared to have an "Everlasting priesthood." If one is
everlasting, they are without beginning and without ending! He is
timeless:

- He is before time...

- He is above time... "One day is as a thousand years and a thousand
 years as one day."

- He is beyond time... He is the only one that can stand in the present
 and speak of the future in past tense!

Oh - by the way - I am speaking of our Father!

In a very literal sense, He is the possessor of eternity! Eternity
belongs to our Father! As a result, He gives us eternal life, because it
is His to give! (Larissa telling a Board Member "My Daddy owns this
whole place!")

He is:

- The alpha and the omega...

- The first and the last...

- The beginning and the end...

- The keeper of creation...

- The creator of all...

- The architect of the universe...

- The manager of everything...

He:

- Always was...

- Always is...

- Always will be...

He is:

- Undeniable...

- Unmistakable...

- Unmoved...

- Unchanged...

- Undefeated...

- ... and never Undone!

GOD OUR FATHER

EVERLASTING FATHER

Speaking of eternity draws our minds into a dimension that almost
depletes our brain power. Eternity has no beginning and no ending. When
I say that we have an Everlasting Father, I am calling you to remember
that our Father has a starting point that transcends time, and continues
into infinity.

For us, the Eternal part of His existence is absolutely essential
because no one lives forever! But our Father does! That is wonderful for
us because we run out of time, but He has more time than He has anything
else!

Sooner or later, we will find our own place in the cemetery. We are
temporal beings rather than immortal beings. The human body has a shelf
life! The state of man is one of transition! We are always in a state of
transition! We're here today and gone tomorrow! The Word compares the
life of a man to:

- The wind...

- A vapor...

- A shadow...

- A flower...

- The grass...

A dead God will do us no good, dying men need an undying God! (My God
is not dead, sorry about yours!)

In the earthly realm, the promise to be a good father is only good for a
few decades at the very best. (Story of giving Larissa away)

Because he is like a father, the message being sent in being an
"Everlasting Father" is one of security. As our father He cares
for his people and they in turn feel secure. Please know He is not a
father that will be there until something better comes along, His
promise is to be a Father Forever!

Some of you will have a difficult time with this title because you had
an earthly father who abandoned you; that person was not a Father
Forever or maybe not at all! Others had a wonderful father, but their
father died, their father was not a Father Forever!

I am father to Jason and Larissa, but I cannot commit to being there for
them forever! All human fathers will sooner or later pass from the stage
of action, but the Heavenly Father is a Father Forever! (Mason -- "I
stay with you forever")

As an earthly father, when my children have a need, I attempted to meet
it! They belong to me, and I love them very much. I would do anything
within my power for them! I would take a bullet for them!

Someone said, "A father is a man who has pictures where his money used
to be!"

Charles Wadsworth said, "By the time a man is old enough to realize his
father was right, he usually has a son who thinks he is wrong!"

What I am to my children, in an inferior manner, the Father is to all of
us, in a completely perfect way. Matthew 7:11 (NKJV) "If you then,
being evil, know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more
will [your Father]{.underline} who is in heaven [give good things to
those who ask Him]{.underline}!"

One of the greatest opportunities of fatherhood is that of setting an
example for our children to follow. Clarence Kelland said, "My father
didn\'t tell me how to live; he lived; and let me watch him do it."

Another terrific opportunity of fatherhood is the level of care and
concern we can show to our families. The problem for us earthlings is
that in some way, we are all flawed. Because He is our perfect
Everlasting Father:

- When we fail, He forgives us!

- When we fall, He lifts us up!

- When we are weak, He is strong!

- When we are lost, He is the Way!

- When we are afraid, He is our courage!

- When we stumble, He steadies us!

- When we are broken, He mends us!

- When we are blind, He gives us sight!

- When we are hungry, He feeds us!

- When we face trials, He is with us!

- When we face persecution, He shields us!

- When we face problems, He comforts us!

- When we encounter loss, He provides for us!

Because He is our Everlasting Father we can rest assured:

- He will never leave us...

- He will never forsake us...

- He will never mislead us...

- He will never forget us...

- He will never overlook us...

- He will never mistreat us...

- ...and He will never cancel our appointment in HIS appointment book!
 (Larissa calling the office and asking me to come home)

The ultimate goal, of the Everlasting Father is a relationship with us!
Someone said, "A truly rich man is one whose children run into his arms
when his hands are empty." I would follow this Everlasting Father if He
had nothing to give!
