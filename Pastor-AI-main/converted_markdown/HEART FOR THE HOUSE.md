A HEART FOR GOD'S HOUSE

(Leave on screen until next slide)

Isaiah 53 is the lament by the prophet Isaiah concerning the captivity
of Israel and what is best known as "The suffering servant".

In this passage of scripture Israel had been carried into Babylonian
captivity. Have you experienced captivity? In what areas have you
experienced captivity?

- Marriage?

- Family?

- Career?

- Spiritual Life?

The captivity of Israel was not just a bump in the road, it lasted
seventy years and involved slavery.

The reason they were in captivity to begin with was because they allowed
their priorities to become scrambled. They fell into sin, and out of the
favor of God.

The same thing happens to us when we allow the world to distort our
priorities. When this occurs it is not long until our lives are
completely out of kilter and large amounts of our time, talent, and
treasure are being invested in directions we never intended to go.
Stephen R. Covey said, "Most of us spend too much time on what is
urgent and not enough time on what is important." (Leave on screen
until next slide)

Isaiah 54 begins by utilizing the example of a childless woman. In that
culture it was believed that if a woman had no children it was because
God was chastising her for sinful behavior. We know better, but this is
what they believed. In this passage Israel is the childless woman and
they had been banished from their land for seventy years because of
their sin.

In Isaiah 54 Israel was about to be reunited with her homeland because
she had recognized the error of her ways, and had turned back to God!
Good things happen when we recalibrate our priorities. The recalibration
of priorities can be difficult to say the least, because it first
requires us to admit we are off track. It also necessitates a plan to
set things back in order. Setting things back in order can be painful to
say the least. Dallin H. Oaks said, "Desires dictate our priorities,
priorities shape our choices, and choices determine our actions."
(Leave on screen until next slide)

A HEART FOR OTHERS HOUSE

It is interesting to me that Isaiah does not just prophesy freedom and
blessing over his house, his prophecy is over the entire nation of
Israel.

Isaiah and his family would most certainly become beneficiaries of the
fulfilled prophecy, but the scope of this word was much bigger than one
family or tribe. Sometimes it is difficult for us to get focused on what
someone else needs because we have issues that are looming bigger than
life.

The Dead Sea is called the Dead Sea because it has many contributories
but no outflow. The reason it is called the Dead Sea is because it is
incapable of sustaining marine life. Someone said, "God will give it to
you, if He can get it through you." A "me" mentality is the archenemy
of a "we" mentality. The only way to accomplish a God sized vision is
through a "we" mentality. God never intended for any of us to hole up in
a self-imposed cocoon, and live our lives selfishly. God designed us to
live our lives with other people. Genesis 2:18 (NKJV) "God said, It
is not good that man should be alone; I will make him a helper
comparable to him."

Our lives will never become what God intended as long as we insist on a
"me" mentality. Our vision will never become a reality until we embrace
a "we" mentality.

Elizabeth Elliott said, "The will of God is not something you add to
your life. It's a course you choose. You either line yourself up with
the Son of God, or you capitulate to the principle which governs the
rest of the world." (Leave on screen until next slide)

In order to do this we must put God first, others second, and ourselves
third. By properly prioritizing our lives in this way, our priorities
become a surge protector for our pride and power gifts.

The number one hurdle to getting blessed is to have the faith to sow
seed outside of our four walls. Listen to the words of Jesus. Luke 6:38
"Give, and it shall be given unto you; good measure, pressed down,
and shaken together, and running over, shall men give into your bosom.
For with the same measure that ye mete withal it shall be measured to
you again." (Leave on screen until finished with bullet points) Look
at this:

- You cannot get until you give... (That doesn't make sense, yet it is a
 spiritual and Biblical principle that works -- If you are in need,
 GIVE!)

- If you give, God will cause MEN to give to you... (When we give we
 expect God to bless us, but here He say's He will cause MEN to give to
 us!)

- Our blessing is commensurate to the size of our gift... (Use a
 teaspoon, a ladle, and a scoop shovel to illustrate this -- If you
 need a little blessing, give a little gift -- If you need a BIG
 blessing, give a BIG gift)

The correct prioritization of our heart is:

- God...

- Others...

- Us...

The correct prioritization of our resources is:

- God...

- Others...

- Us...

Whether it is a church or an individual, how we spend our resources is a
reflection of our heart. (Leave on screen until next slide)

something for someone who can never repay you."

A HEART FOR OUR HOUSE

In Isaiah 54 the seventy years of captivity was coming to an end for the
Israelites. They had realigned their priorities and as a result they
were about to experience the exponential blessings of the Lord. The
message of this passage is primarily to a nation but look how personal
it becomes: (Place on screen one at a time)

- The mourning woman gets her song back... Vs. 1, "Break out into
 loud and joyful song" The Word declares, "Weeping may endure for
 a night, but joy comes in the morning." When we get our priorities
 right we can say GOOD MORNING! The LONG night has ended and morning
 has dawned.

- Full redemption is made to her... Vs. 1, "She who was abandoned has
 more blessings now than she whose husband stayed!" When our
 priorities are in order those who have been living on one income can
 experience more blessings than those who have two incomes.

- Promises of exponential blessing... Vs. 2 & 3, "Enlarge your house;
 build on additions; spread out your home! 3 For you will soon be
 bursting at the seams!" When we get our house in order and our
 priorities proper aligned we can get ready, get ready, get ready
 because the blessings and expansion are going to be off the chain.

- The blessing to future generations... Vs. 3, "And your descendants
 will possess the cities left behind during the exile and rule the
 nations that took their lands." What we do here and now will affect
 our descendants and future generations.

- Fear and shame will be annihilated... Vs. 4, "Fear not; you will no
 longer live in shame. The shame of your youth and the sorrows of
 widowhood will be remembered no more." When we get our priorities
 right the shame of past failures fade into the distance.

- The Lord will be our partner... Vs. 5-7, "Your Creator will be your
 "husband." The Lord Almighty is his name; he is your Redeemer, the
 Holy One of Israel, the God of all the earth. 6 For the Lord has
 called you back from your grief---a young wife abandoned by her
 husband. 7 For a brief moment I abandoned you. But with great
 compassion I will take you back. 8 In a moment of anger I turned my
 face a little while; but with everlasting love I will have pity on
 you, says the Lord, your Redeemer." D.L. Moody said, "If God be
 your partner, make your plans big." It is time for us to go big or go
 home!

Are we ready for God to show up at our house and bring about some
much-needed miracles? If so, we must be willing to sow seed outside our
four walls. We must release the my-four-and-no-more mentality and firmly
grip the hand of God by sowing what you cannot afford to sow. Someone
said, "If it wont meet your need, make it your seed!"
