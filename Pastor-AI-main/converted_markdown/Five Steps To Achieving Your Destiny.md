## I. SENSITIVITY

The path to fulfilling our destiny begins with sensitivity. We must
learn to be:

- Sensitive to God

- Sensitive to others

- Sensitive to our surroundings

In Genesis chapter twelve we are introduced to a man by the name of
Abram. Abram was a man who feared the Lord and had a heart to follow
God. Abrams destiny is spelled out in Genesis 12:1-3 (NKJV) "Now the
[Lord]{.smallcaps} had said to Abram: 'Get out of your country, from
your family and from your father's house, to a land that [I
will]{.underline} show you. 2 [I will]{.underline} make you a great
nation; [I will]{.underline} bless you and make your name great; and you
shall be a blessing. 3 [I will]{.underline} bless those who bless you,
and [I will]{.underline} curse him who curses you; and in you all the
families of the earth shall be blessed.'"

Abrams destiny is admittedly somewhat vague, but certainly powerful and
far-reaching. Because of the realization of his destiny Abram became the
father of the Jewish race, and an ancestor of Jesus Christ. Because of
this, Abrams destiny is alive and being fulfilled even in our time.

God told Abram to go out and look for a city; He didn\'t tell him which
one but He told him when he found it he would know it. The Bible say\'s
"He looked for a city which had foundations whose builder and maker
is God"!

God was not being disingenuous to Abram, by not telling him where He was
taking him: God had Abraham enrolled in sensitivity training! Because of
what God told Abram regarding the city he was taking him to, he had to
treat each city as if this were it!

Much later (At 99 years old), Abram receives a divine revelation; a
confirmation of the promise; a name change and for the first time
specific instructions concerning the land that he was to possess.
Genesis 17:4-8 (NKJV) "... Behold, My covenant is with you, and [you
shall be a father of many nations]{.underline}.5 No longer shall your
name be called Abram, but [your name shall be Abraham]{.underline}; for
I have made you a father of many nations.6 I will make you exceedingly
fruitful; and I will make nations of you, and kings shall come from
you.7 And I will establish My covenant between Me and you and your
descendants after you in their generations, for an everlasting covenant,
to be God to you and your descendants after you.8 Also I give to you and
your descendants after you the land in which you are a stranger, [all
the land of Canaan]{.underline}, as an everlasting possession; and I
will be their God."

In order to reach his destiny Abraham had to learn to be sensitive!
Sensitivity is not imparted it is learned through experience. (Have
someone to come up and make only two or three chords -- Then have
someone an experienced musician come up and play the song)

It was important that Abraham learn to be sensitive because later he
would have to find the right mountain on which to offer his son, and
even more important, he would need to be sensitive enough to find a ram
that would die in place of his son!

Abraham\'s life was marked by questions like; which direction? Which
country? Which city? Which mountain?

The greatest proof of Abrahams sensitivity is revealed in Genesis 22:2-3
(NKJV) when God said to him, "'[Take now your son]{.underline}, your
only son Isaac, whom you love, and go to the land of Moriah, and [offer
him there as a burnt offering]{.underline} on one of the mountains of
which I shall tell you.' 3 So Abraham rose early in the morning and
saddled his donkey, and took two of his young men with him, and Isaac
his son; and he split the wood for the burnt offering, and arose and
went to the place of which God had told him."

If you and I are going to achieve our destiny we must learn to be
sensitive to God, sensitive to others, and sensitive to our
surroundings.

We must realize that God has a master plan for our life. This master
plan is replete with details, from our mom and dad's bedroom to the
funeral parlor; God carefully planned every day of our life before we
were a twinkle in our fathers' eye!

God's master plan for our life is not a fast track or overnight success
story; but a process that is intended to end with success. Hebrews 10:36
(NKJV) "For you have need of endurance, so that [after you have done
the will of God, you may receive the promise]{.underline}:"

From the time I began Pastoring I began praying "Lord, help me to be
sensitive to the moving of your Holy Spirit." Being sensitive to the
Holy Spirit has provided direction; prevented potential problems;
averted traps; and avoided pitfalls in my life!

Do you see how important it is to be sensitive? The realization of our
destiny depends on it, both physically, and spiritually!

The fulfillment of Abraham's destiny was dependent on the level of his
sensitivity.

We cannot fulfill our destiny without being sensitive. Anger,
frustration, bitterness, strife, etc., are enemies of sensitivity.
(Story about frustrated preacher)

When these things are permitted entry into our life all progress toward
our destiny ceases.

Are we going to be satisfied with living in a stunted growth process, or
are we going to commit ourselves to being sensitive to God, sensitive to
others, and sensitive to our surroundings, thus allowing for the
fulfillment of our destiny?

## II. SUBMISSION TO AUTHORITY

The life of Abraham is a perfect picture of submission to the authority
of God and to the authority of man. God called Abraham to go but failed
to specify where.

Abraham held a garage sale, told his family good-bye, pulled up the
stakes of his tent and went on a expedition of following God. Abraham
seems to have done all of this almost without a second thought. How
could he go without being furnished more detail? He was able to do such
a thing because he was living his life in total submission to God.

Abraham also proved his submission to man in the fact that he was almost
too timid in dealing with his family and the Kings who hosted him during
his expedition of finding the will of God. (His demeanor in dealing with
Lot -- His handling of he and Sarah's relationship with Abimelech -- She
was the daughter of his father, but not of his mother)

Of all the times Abraham was called upon to submit either to man or God,
the greatest test of his submission must have been the call of God to
sacrifice his son Isaac. (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME) Genesis
22:1-2 (NKJV) "Now it came to pass after these things that God tested
Abraham, and said to him, 'Abraham!' And he said, 'Here I am.'2 Then He
said, 'Take now your son, your only son Isaac, whom you love, and go to
the land of Moriah, and offer him there as a burnt offering on one of
the mountains of which I shall tell you.'" The first question that
comes to mind every time I read this passage of Scripture is; how could
God ask a man who has exercised such faith and submission to do such a
thing?

All the other times Abraham was called upon to exercise faith and
submission must have paled in the face of this test. All the other tests
were the proving ground for what would occur on the top of Mt. Moriah.
This was to be the greatest test of Abraham's submission and confidence
in God. This incident would no doubt put to the test the belief that God
had a destiny that he was taking Abraham too.

This test would no doubt define Abraham's life; when you hear Abraham
spoken of it is rare that this test is not mentioned; When writing to
the Hebrews, Paul could not resist the temptation to mention this test;
Hebrews 11:17-19 (NKJV) "By faith Abraham, when he was tested,
offered up Isaac, and he who had received the promises offered up his
only begotten son,18 of whom it was said, 'In Isaac your seed shall be
called,'19 concluding that God was able to raise him up, even from the
dead, from which he also received him in a figurative sense."

If Abraham could pass this test everything else would be academic.
(Small stuff -- don't sweat the small stuff -- remember, everything is
small stuff when God is involved)

Notice Genesis 22:3 "So Abraham rose early in the morning and saddled
his donkey, and took two of his young men with him, and Isaac his son;
and he split the wood for the burnt offering, and arose and went to the
place of which God had told him." Look at this; no argument; no
hedging; no fleeces; he just arose the next morning and started
splitting the wood; greasing the wagon wheels; choosing which servants
he would take with him and informed his son they were going to a father
and son conference on Mt. Moriah! Wow! What confidence and submission,
most of us would have needed to be dragged up Moriah by the hair of the
head! We would have gone kicking and screaming; raising multiple
objections; stating all kinds of theological questions and telling God
how wrong child abuse would be! We would have raised more sand than the
alligator did the day the pond went dry!

Abraham did nothing of the sort; he had perfected the art of submission
before he ever left Egypt. While at Moriah his submission would move him
one giant step closer to his ultimate destiny.

## C. To This point Abraham fully intended to sacrifice his son!
(ABRAHAM PUTS HIS ARM AROUND ISAAC AS IF EXPLAINING WHAT IS ABOUT TO
HAPPEN -- HE THEN PLACES HIM ON THE WOOD AND TIES HIM DOWN)

Abraham planned to sacrifice his son, but God had another plan. When he
raised the knife to take the life of his son, God spoke to him about a
ram caught in a thicket behind him! (THE ANGEL COMES OUT OF THE ALTAR
AND CATCHES ABRAHAM BY THE ARM --TAKES THE KNIFE AWAY AND POINTS TO THE
STAGE DOOR WHERE AN ANGEL COMES OUT LEADING A RAM)

A ram is uniquely designed to negotiate briar patches without being
trapped by them! What in the world is wrong with this ram? It wasn\'t
the briar patch, his GPS (Global Positioning System) was working
wonderfully. This ram had not experienced a lack of stamina it was a
holy stakeout! God tied the ram by his horns in the thicket so Abraham
would have a substitute!

Think about it, Abraham has stomped all over this mountain getting just
the right rocks. He and Isaac have no doubt had a conversation (Maybe
even a heated exchange) about what was about to transpire and this ram
never made a sound? This was a holy stakeout!

I believe Abraham was so glad to see this ram that he felt like kissing
him right on the lips in spite of the fact that the ram had chronic
halitosis! Have you ever been there? God provided a substitute for you!
A ram for a son, a son for a sin! There is no better substitute!

[[Abraham was sensitive enough to hear at a moments
notice!]{.underline}]{.mark} How far in advance does God have to start

Is God free to pull off a setup in your life at a moments notice? In
