## I. NEHEMIAH TOOK TIME FOR REFLECTION...

Nehemiah 2:11 (NKJV) "So I came to Jerusalem, and was there [three
days]{.underline}..." Even though Nehemiah had previously visited the
city to survey its need, upon arrival, he takes an additional three days
for reflection before proceeding.

I think it is also significant that Ezra waited three days after
arriving in Jerusalem, before beginning the work that God had called him
to do, Ezra 8:32. We do not have to look far into the Word of God to see
that the third day was very significant: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A
TIME)

- On the third day of creation, God made trees, grass, fruit and
 vegetation...

- On the third day if the sacrifice had not been completely consumed,
 what was left was considered to be contaminated and must be
 discarded... Leviticus 7:18

- On the third day the sacrifice was to be returned to the person who
 made the sacrifice... Abraham and Isaac Genesis 22 - Matthew 19:29 -
 Matthew 16:25

- On the third day the Lord promised to clear the name of His people...
 Joseph's prediction of the restoration of the Kings butler to his
 position - Genesis 40:13

- On the third day the Lord promised to speak to the people... Exodus
 19:9-11 (NKJV) "Then the LORD said to Moses, "I am going to come
 to you in a thick cloud so the people themselves can hear me as I
 speak to you. Then they will always have confidence in you. "Moses
 told the LORD what the people had said. 10 Then the LORD told Moses,
 "Go down and prepare the people for my visit. Purify them today and
 tomorrow, and have them wash their clothing. 11 Be sure they are ready
 [on the third day]{.underline}, for I will come down upon Mount Sinai
 as all the people watch."

- On the third day God provided water for the people... Exodus 15 - When
 the people traveled away from the Red Sea they were traveling without
 water and God provided water on the third day - Moses cast a tree into
 the bitter water of Marah and it became sweet - This happened on the
 third day

- On the third day the vision of the Children of Israel to inherit the
 Promised Land became reality... Joshua 1:11 (NKJV) "Go through the
 camp and tell the people to get their provisions ready. [In three days
 you will cross the Jordan River and take possession of the land the
 LORD your God has given you]{.underline}."

- On the third day God promises to give victory over our enemies... When
 the two spies were hiding in the house of Rahab, God promised to give
 them safe passage on the third day - Listen to the words of Rahab -
 Joshua 2:16 & 22 (NKJV) "Escape to the hill country," she told
 them. "Hide there for [three days]{.underline} until the men who are
 searching for you have returned; then go on your way." The spies went
 up into the hill country and stayed there [three days]{.underline}.
 The men who were chasing them had searched everywhere along the road,
 but they finally returned to the city without success."

- On the third day God has promised to heal our bodies... Hezekiah - 2
 Kings 20:5 (NKJV) "Go back to Hezekiah, the leader of my people.
 Tell him, 'This is what the LORD, the God of your ancestor David,
 says: I have heard your prayer and seen your tears. I will heal you,
 and [three days]{.underline} from now you will get out of bed and go
 to the Temple of the LORD."

- On the third day Jesus arose and all the third day promises find their
 fulfillment in him...

Even though, throughout the Word of God the third day is of great
significance, possibly the greatest significance here is time for
reflection, planning, and listening to the voice of God for
determination of how Jerusalem could be properly reconstructed. The Word
of God said, Isaiah 28:16 (KJV) "Therefore thus saith the Lord God,
behold, I lay in Zion for a foundation a stone, a tried stone, a sure
foundation: He that believeth [shall not make haste]{.underline}."

What significance does this have for us? Some people despise the past,
and say that we should not waste our time reflecting upon it. To prove
their point, they often use the Apostle Paul's words in Philippians
3:13-14 (NKJV) "Brethren, I do not count myself to have apprehended;
but one thing I do, [forgetting those things which are
behind]{.underline} and reaching forward to those things which are
ahead, (14) I press toward the goal for the prize of the upward call of
God in Christ Jesus." If we place Paul's words in proper context we
soon discover he is saying that he is going to forget some things from
the past because they were not conducive to pressing forward into the
future. All of his failures needed to be forgotten, and some of his
successes needed to be wiped from his memory as well, so that he could
forge into the future successfully.

While reflecting on the past should never become our dominant activity,
there are some legitimate reasons to take the time to do so. I believe
the time Ezra and Nehemiah set aside for reflection was a measured
intentional game plan for the future of Jerusalem. They were taking time
for reflection for a number of reasons: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A
TIME)

- They were remembering what Jerusalem had been, so that past mistakes
 were not repeated...

- They were remembering what Jerusalem had been, so that the good things
 could be repeated...

- They were remembering what Jerusalem had been, so that the renovation
 would transform the city into a better place than before...

I believe Ezra and Nehemiah emerged from their days of reflection with
excitement and resolve for the success of the future of Jerusalem.

We have set aside the month of January to pray and reflect on how God
has used the past to position Christian Temple to be a powerful force in
this city for the future. We will emerge from these days excited and
resolved to take the future on with new vitality and tenacity! Though
the past be glorious, I believe the future is even brighter! (It's here
for the taking!)

Please allow me to explain that there is not a person in this house
today who appreciates the history of this great Church, and its leaders
more than I do! I would be the first to admit that I am standing on the
shoulders of all previous leaders to point to the future!

As we wade through the process of preparing for the work of the future
we will need to reflect on the past from time to time because: (PLACE
ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- We do not want to repeat the mistakes of the past...

- We want to repeat the successes of the past, with greater intensity...

- We want to make sure the future is more effective than the past...

One of the most difficult lessons we learn on this spiritual journey is
to wait on God. Both Ezra and Nehemiah knew the value of this. They
spent their time reflecting and praying to determine how they fit into
Gods plan for Jerusalem, rather than how God fit into their plan for the
city.

The plans we are making for the future have been years in the making and
have been bathed in prayer, and we are excited that the Lord is allowing
Christian Temple to be a part of building His Kingdom by: (PLACE ON
THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Planting a satellite church in Pearland...

- Planting a Spanish speaking congregation in our present location...

- Building a Missions House for itinerating Missionaries on our present
 property...

- Expand our facilities through several expansion and improvement
 projects...

Some people would probably say that our goals are too industrious, but
we make our plans in light of the fact that we serve a God who is
capable of doing all things through those who walk in obedience to Him.
Goals are to a church what oxygen is to a person. Benjamin Mays said,
"The tragedy in life doesn\'t lie in not reaching your goal.  The
tragedy lies in having no goal to reach."

Reaching goals take time, not everything can be accomplished in a short
period of time. The accomplishment of certain goals often involve, a
lifetime of work. All of the things we are dreaming of doing can
certainly not be completed today or tomorrow, so we will be patient to
work on Gods schedule. If we cannot wait on God we will get our cart
before our horse and run the risk of sabotaging the will of God for this
church. This will all be accomplished on His timetable not ours!

As we have discovered, three days are significant throughout the Bible
but the most significant event to happen on the third day was the
resurrection of Jesus. In Nehemiah's case, the three days were used to
see their plans and purposes pass from death unto life, while they
calmly waited before God!

Bro. Grant said, "I'm convinced some people will make heaven, if they
don't run past the gate."

## II. NEHEMIAH SURVEYS THE DIFFICULTIES...

Nehemiah 2:12-13 (NKJV) "I arose in the night... neither told I any
man what my God had put in my heart to do... and viewed the walls which
were broken down" Nehemiah, like a wise physician, begins by making a
thorough diagnosis of the case.

Under the light of the moon and the stars, Nehemiah began to view the
devastation of the city. What he discovers is the difficulty of
dilapidated walls and heaps of rubbish. There was so much devastation
and waste that he could not even ride his horse through the streets.

Many Christians live in a fool's paradise because they refuse to believe
there can be any difficulty, after all they are doing the will of God.
After all, if we are doing the will of God shouldn't everything be easy?
Difficulties or the lack of them should never serve as an indicator of
whether or not one is doing the will of God. Throughout the Word of God
we encounter heroes of the faith doing the will of God in the face of
the greatest of adversity: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Noah...

- The Three Hebrew Children...

- Daniel...

- Paul...

Jesus never promised an easy road to success. Samuel Johnson said,
"Nothing will ever be attempted if all possible objections must first
be overcome."

Though Jesus never promised a journey devoid of difficulty He did say
the person who counts the cost before building a house, will have what
it takes to finish it! (Luke 14) Have you ever driven past a partially
completed house or building which has obviously been abandoned? Several
questions immediately come to mind: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A
TIME)

- Who started this building?

- Who does this property belong to?

- What is wrong with this building?

- Why did construction come to a halt?

- Will construction be resumed someday?

- What will this building look like when it is completed?

We can ill afford to chart the course for the future without weighing
the cost of the journey. The cost of the destiny must be viewed in
conjunction with the resources of the one who has ordained the dream.
Someone said "If God be our leader we should make our plans big."

Assessing the potential difficulty that lies between where we presently
are and where we are going can certainly be a challenging task, unless,
like Nehemiah, we know that God has placed everything necessary to
succeed within our own hearts and hands. Isaiah 54:17 (NKJV) "No
weapon [formed against you]{.underline} shall prosper, And every [tongue
which rises against you in judgment]{.underline} You shall condemn. This
is the heritage of the servants of the Lord, And their righteousness is
from Me," Says the Lord." The word "Formed" in this verse
literally means a plan crafted and created for your overthrow. The word
"Prosper" means to be successful. In view of these facts, this
Scripture can be paraphrased in this way: "No weapon crafted for your
overthrow shall be successful."

The promise of God to Joshua can also be claimed by all Christ
followers. Joshua 1:5 (NKJV) "[No man shall be able to stand before
you]{.underline} all the days of your life; as I was with Moses, so [I
will be with you]{.underline}. [I will not leave you nor forsake
you]{.underline}." The assignment Joshua was attempting to carry out
was to conquer and possess the Promised Land, which was occupied by
giants! Many difficulties were strewn in the path ahead of Joshua but
the promise of God was powerful on his behalf. Look at the fulfillment
of this promise. Joshua 21:44 (NKJV) "[The Lord gave them rest all
around]{.underline}, according to all that He had sworn to their
fathers. And [not a man of all their enemies stood against
them]{.underline}; the Lord delivered [all their enemies into their
hand]{.underline}." Joshua 23:9 (NKJV) "For [the Lord has driven
out from before you great and strong nations]{.underline}; but as for
you, [no one has been able to stand against you to this
day]{.underline}." Even though we face difficulties, Joshua's promise
is ours, just as the fulfillment of that promise belongs to us.

Someone said, "The road to success is not straight. There is a curve
called failure, a loop called confusion, speed bumps called friends, red
lights called enemies, caution lights called family and flats called
jobs. But, if you have a spare called determination, an engine called
perseverance, insurance called faith, and a driver called Jesus, you
will make it to a place called success."

Generations past have done their part to provide a full Gospel witness
in this city, now it's our turn to do what must be done, my question is,
are we up to the challenge?

I can assure you, it will not be easy street, there will be major
difficulties, but in spite of it all God will be with us.

## III. NEHEMIAH MAKES AN APPEAL FOR WORKERS... Nehemiah 2:17 (NKJV)
"Then I said to them, "You see [the distress that we are
in]{.underline},...Come and [let us build the wall of
Jerusalem]{.underline}, that we may no longer be a reproach." Some
people seem to believe that the best way to accomplish a thing is to get
someone else to do the work. They live by the philosophy, "Here am I,
Lord send Barney!" Sam Ewing said, "Hard work spotlights the character
of people:  some turn up their sleeves, some turn up their noses, and
some don\'t turn up at all."

Please notice, Nehemiah did not say, "Go and build," but "Come,
let [us]{.underline} build."

Nehemiah was aware that the gargantuan work of restoring Jerusalem to
its pristine glory and power could never be accomplished as long as:
(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The leader single-handedly attempts to do all the work... The leader
 who feels the need to do everything himself will never be able to lead
 an organization very far.

- The leader believes he is too good to get his hands dirty... The
 leader who refuses to work alongside the laborers will never develop
 enough influence at the grass roots to be trusted or followed.

- The people refuse to be involved... The organization which embraces
 the philosophy of "We hired a pastor and staff to do the work" will
 never succeed because there is a limit to what a small group of
 hireling leaders can accomplish. In this scenario there is too many
 designated leaders and no followers.

- The people want to lead instead of follow... The organization that
 views their leaders, as hired guns will eventually implode. Power
 struggles will eat up enormous amounts of time and resources. Time
 that should be spent building will be invested in jockeying for
 position.

When I was a young child we used to sing a little song which simply
said, "If we all pull together, together, together, if we all pull
together, how happy we'll be. For your work is my work and our work is
God's work, if we all pull together how happy we'll be." Psalms 133:1
(NIV) "How good and pleasant it is when brothers live together in
[unity]{.underline}!"

If we are going to accomplish the work God has committed to us, it
cannot be viewed as my work or your work; it must be viewed as the work
of the Lord which we are all privileged to participate in. It remains to
be seen what God can do through a church which remains together in total
unity over the long haul. This is certainly one of the reasons Jesus
prayed for us to become one in unity. John 17:23 (NIV) "May they be
brought to [complete unity]{.underline} to let the world know that you
sent me and have loved them even as you have loved me."

If you could grant the answer to the most important prayer request of
your best friend, would you do it? Jesus is supposed to be the most
important person in our life, and we have the power to answer His
request; we should do it without regard to personal cost.

The work which had been committed to Nehemiah was more than just
painting a Sunday school room or two; he was about to assume a major
enterprise which would necessitate the commitment of leaders and the
recruitment of an enormous number of workers and resources.

In order to do this successfully, he had to make sure every person was
committed to the work before its inception! Thomas Edison said,
"Opportunity is missed by most people because it is dressed in overalls
and looks like work."

The devastation of the city was so severe that one could not ride a
horse through the city without dismounting and continuing on foot.
Nehemiah 2:13-14 (NLT) "After dark I went out through the Valley
Gate, past the Jackal's Well, and over to the Dung Gate to inspect the
broken walls and burned gates. 14 Then I went to the Fountain Gate and
to the King's Pool, but [my donkey couldn't get through the
rubble]{.underline}." If a horse could not be ridden through the city
can you imagine what kind of devastation was apparent for as far as the
eye could see? It must have looked like New Orleans following the
Katrina disaster. (PLAY A THREE MINUTE VIDEO OF THE KATRINA DISASTER)

Can you imagine attempting to clear this kind of rubble and committing
to the work of rebuilding an entire city? Think about this, they did not
have one piece of automated or heavy equipment available to them. The
task which Nehemiah was facing was no small task. This would be
rigorous, pain-staking, back-breaking work which would require an
enormous amount of manpower and womanpower to see it through to its
successful conclusion.

The work which God has committed to us will require each of us to become
absolutely committed with our: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Time...

- Talent...

- Effort...

- Prayers...

- Resources...

In order for Nehemiah and his followers to accomplish their assignment
the philosophy which drove their organization must have been "All at
it, and always at it." This is a philosophy of everyone "pulling
together." Everyone would be called upon to contribute! Colossians 3:
11-14 (Message) " Words like Jewish and non-Jewish, religious and
irreligious, insider and outsider, uncivilized and uncouth, slave and
free, mean nothing. From now on [everyone is defined by Christ, everyone
is included in Christ]{.underline}. So, chosen by God for this new life
of love, dress in the wardrobe God picked out for you: compassion,
kindness, humility, quiet strength, discipline. Be even-tempered,
content with second place, quick to forgive an offense. Forgive as
quickly and completely as the Master forgave you. And regardless of what
else you put on, [wear love]{.underline}. [It\'s your basic, all-purpose
garment]{.underline}. Never be without it. Let the peace of Christ keep
you [in tune with each other, in step with each other. None of this
going off and doing your own thing]{.underline}."

A common need should lead to unity of faith, which should lead to unity
of effort. As the city of Jerusalem lying in ruin was a dishonor to
every Jew, so a Church that does not have vision for the future is a
dishonor to each individual member. Proverbs 29:18 (KJV) "Where there
is no vision, [the people perish]{.underline}."

The way to roll away the reproach is to arise and build, laying the
displaced stones into their proper place until the work is finished.
Everyone stays on the wall until the assignment is completed.

The task we have been assigned is no easy task, but if everyone will do
their part each day, the work will soon become a success. What
constitutes success? Success is your dreams with work clothes on.

Robert Byrne said, "The purpose of life is a life of purpose."

seek the well-being of the children of Israel. (19) But when Sanballat
the Horonite, Tobiah the Ammonite official, and Geshem the Arab heard of
it, [they laughed at us and despised us]{.underline}, and said, "What
is this thing that you are doing? Will you rebel against the king?"

Nehemiah 4:1-3 (NKJV) "But it so happened, when Sanballat heard that
we were rebuilding the wall, that [he was furious and very indignant,
and mocked the Jews]{.underline}. (2) And he spoke before his brethren
and the army of Samaria, and said, "[What are these feeble Jews doing?
Will they fortify themselves? Will they offer sacrifices? Will they
complete it in a day? Will they revive the stones from the heaps of
rubbish\--stones that are burned]{.underline}?" (3) Now Tobiah the

It is interesting to me that those who stood in opposition to Nehemiah
were not even on the radar screen as long as Jerusalem was laying in
ruin.

Notice several facts about opposition: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A
TIME)

- The initiation of opposition usually begins with people setting around
 talking about things they do not have a clue about... It is obvious
 that Sanballat, Tobiah and Geshem had been caucusing with regard to
 what the Jews were doing, all this without even consulting with
 Nehemiah who was the architect of the plan.

- The motivation for opposition rarely makes good sense... Why oppose a
 man who is attempting to restore a city? Of course they use their
 defense of the King as their motivation. They couldn't have cared less
 about the King, they were afraid their positions might be threatened.

- Opposition always asks rhetorical questions which are accompanied by
 answers that are skewed with half-truths... The questions they were
 asking, were never intended to be answered they were intended to
 create doubt in the minds of Nehemiah's helpers.

- The nature of opposition is always rebellion; therefore those who are
 involved usually turn bitter... Sanballat, Tobiah and Geshem began
 this tirade by talking among themselves; their actions turned to
 rhetorical questions; which led to false assumptions couched in
 bitterness. Look at it, "If a fox runs across their stone wall, he
 will break it down." Hogwash!

The more closely you try to follow the will of God, the bitterer the
opposition will become. This should not come as such a surprise to us,
the world and the Church hated Jesus without a cause, and the disciple
is not greater than his Lord. Matthew 10:24 (NKJV) "A disciple is not
above his teacher, nor a servant above his master."

Everyone stands to benefit from a city being restored. The benefits
include: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Improved housing...

- Improved living conditions...

- Improved educational system...

- Improved infrastructure, streets etc...

- Improved opportunities for making a living...

When you think about it they have everything to gain and nothing to
lose, yet they become opposition to the will of God. What is not to like
here? The opposition can rarely give you a good reason why they oppose a
thing. They are usually in opposition because it just doesn't fit their
style. This is never a good enough reason to stick our feet in the sand
in an effort to slow progress.

There are actually two unpardonable sins with human beings: (PLACE ON
THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Failure...

- Success...

Sadly the staunchest opposition to the plans of the church almost never
come from outside sources, but from within.

Nehemiah's opposition made a fatal mistake which is often made in a
spiritual venue, they failed to recognize that this was not Nehemiah's
work, it was Gods work! The opposition oftentimes underestimates the
level at which the directive originated.

Israel, [take care what you are planning to do to these
men]{.underline}! (36) Some time ago there was that fellow Theudas, who

he was killed, and his followers went their various ways. The whole
movement came to nothing. (37) After him, at the time of the census,
there was Judas of Galilee. He got some people to follow him, but he was

## V. NEHEMIAH ACHIEVS HIS DREAM...

Nehemiah faced the scoffers' opposition with a simple, brief, fearless
statement of faith in God. Nehemiah 2:20 (NKJV) "The
[God]{.underline} of heaven Himself [will prosper us]{.underline}."
Notice, Nehemiah never placed his trust in man; he placed his trust in
Gods ability to empower them to carry the plan of God to a successful
conclusion, using them to accomplish His will.

No God given destiny can be attained without exercising complete faith
in God. Hebrews 11:6 (KJV) "But without faith [it is impossible to
please Him]{.underline}; for he that cometh to God must [believe that he
is, and that He is a rewarder of them that diligently seek
Him]{.underline}."

Nehemiah knew better than to try to face the opposition on his own! We
cannot expect to emerge from this expedition with victory, if we lean on
the arm of flesh. Proverbs 3:5-6: (NKJV) "[Trust]{.underline} in the
Lord with all your heart, And [lean not on your own
understanding]{.underline}; (6) In all your [ways]{.underline}
[acknowledge Him]{.underline}, And [He shall direct your
paths]{.underline}."

- Trust - Be bold and secure, have confidence.

- Heart - Pouring everything we have into the work of God.

- Lean - To lean on, to trust in. (Use illustration of having someone
 stand behind me, and lean on them in such a way that if they move I
 fall...)

- Understanding - Discernment or intuition.

- Ways - Road or journey, course of life.

- Acknowledge - To know or to recognize.

- Direct - Upright or pleasing.

- Path - Way of living.

The Don Nordin Paraphrase on this Scripture looks like this: "If you
place your confidence in the Lord, your security will come from Him as
well. Place your heart, mind and soul into doing His will, pouring
yourself into His purposes. Do not trust in your own discernment or
intuition. In the course of life know and recognize God and he will
cause you to be upright and pleased with your way of living."

In saying "The God of heaven will prosper us," Nehemiah is saying
this is the work of the living, faithful, almighty God of Heaven:
(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- He is for it...

- He is with it...

- He is in it...

- He will make it successful...

A radio preacher by the name of Brother Ike, who was from a by-gone
generation used to say, "You can't lose with the stuff I use."

Doing the work of God is really not at all complicated or difficult; all
we must do is to establish whether this is our work or God's work. Once
we determine what we are attempting to do is the work of God, all we
have to do, is to do His work according to His instructions.

ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- We are His servants...

- We are doing His business...

- We will go on...

- We are prepared for His work...

- We will succeed...

Romans 8:31 (KJV) "If God be for us, who can be against us?"

(NKJV) "You have no heritage or right or memorial in Jerusalem."

- You are strangers to God...
