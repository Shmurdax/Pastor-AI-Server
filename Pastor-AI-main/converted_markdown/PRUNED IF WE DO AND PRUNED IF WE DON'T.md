# PRUNED IF WE DON'T

&

PRUNED IF WE DO

TEXT: John 15:1-8 (NKJV) "I am the true vine, and My Father is
the vinedresser. 2 Every branch in Me that does not bear fruit He takes
away; and every branch that bears fruit He prunes, that it may bear more
fruit. 3 You are already clean because of the word which I have spoken
to you. 4 Abide in Me, and I in you. As the branch cannot bear fruit of
itself, unless it abides in the vine, neither can you, unless you abide
in Me. 5 "I am the vine, you are the branches. He who abides in Me, and
I in him, bears much fruit; for without Me you can do nothing. 6 If
anyone does not abide in Me, he is cast out as a branch and is withered;
and they gather them and throw them into the fire, and they are burned.
7 If you abide in Me, and My words abide in you, you will ask what you
desire, and it shall be done for you. 8 By this My Father is glorified,
that you bear much fruit; so you will be My disciples."

INTRO: In this passage we are introduced to:

- The vineyard -- The Kingdom of God

- The Vinedresser -- Father God

- The vine -- Jesus

- The branches -- All of us

My first public job was working in a peach orchard when I was about 13
years old. It is shocking how many lessons I learned in a peach orchard,
that I can practically apply to ministry, even to this very day -- 52
years later.

I eventually worked every aspect of the orchard over about a four-year
period. I started as a general laborer, picking peaches. By the end of
my first day, I knew that I didn't want to make a career out of picking
peaches.

I graduated through a series of jobs in my upward trajectory of climbing
the ladder of success in the peach orchard:

- I moved to keeping the conveyor belts at the peach shed supplied with
 peaches that had come from the orchard...

- I worked in the peach shed, grading peaches...

- I worked with a crew picking up peach crates and staking them on the
 peach trailer, pulled by a guy driving the tractor...

- I became the tractor driver... (At 13-14 years old this was the most
 coveted job in the orchard)

- I eventually got to drive the Bob truck from the orchard to the
 market, which was about a 70 - mile round trip... (This was my
 favorite assignment and at 16 years old I can't believe anyone let me
 drive a Bob truck across the parking lot, let alone on a highway)

The job I liked the least was pruning! Honestly, I never really
understood it, I just tried as best as I could to make it look good
under the tree when I was finished!

Pruning sounds painful because it indicates something must be cut away.
Pruning is actually more painful than it sounds. However, pruning is
absolutely essential to the harvest.

Most of us don't like the thought of pruning. If you can't wait until
the next pruning season in your life or ministry that you lead -- You
may be dismissed! This is not the right conference for you! You are a
sick person and need a mental health conference!

Pruning is necessary for two reasons:

- Unfruitfulness...

- Fruitfulness...

UNFRUITFUL BRANCHES

Rest assured, we will be pruned if we do bear fruit and pruned if we
don't bear fruit! We might as well throw caution to the wind and do the
things necessary to bear fruit!

Listen to the words of Jesus in John 15:2 "Every branch in Me that
does not bear fruit He takes away..." What about pruning the
unfruitful branches rather than taking them away? The Greek word for
"takes away" is "Airo" (I'd -- row) and can be translated as
either ["]{dir="rtl"}lifts up" or ["]{dir="rtl"}takes up",
"raises."

Grapevines, if left unattended, will grow along the ground. And if left
lying on the ground, will send little roots down into the soil. In so
doing, if left unattended, they will begin to rely on their own root
system for life and sustainability, rather that drawing life from the
vine! (Place a pic of a grape branch growing along the ground and the
root system of such a branch)

If allowed to remain unattended, such a branch can produce some fruit,
but the fruit will eventually become bitter grapes, at best!

So, when a vinedresser comes along, and sees branches lying on the
ground, he knows what they need is not to draw their own nutrients from
the soil, but to get their nutrients from the vine, because the
vine[']{dir="rtl"}s root system goes deep.

In such a case, what does the vinedresser do? He lifts the branch up off
the ground and supports it in the air with a Trestle or lattice work.
(Place a silent video of this process happening on the screen) The
pruning process here, literally calls for the vinedresser to uproot the
branch! What is the Vinedresser attempting to uproot in our lives and
ministries?

This sounds painful, correct? You are right, it is painful, but only for
a short time, because now the source of the branch will come from a new
place, and so will the fruitfulness!

Pruning has been ordered in the Kingdom for all unfruitful branches.
Among ministry leaders the necessity of uprooting some systems stem
from:

- Putting our trust in people...

- Putting our trust in systems...

- Putting our trust in organizations...

- Putting our trust in the deep pockets of the donors...

At times the Vinedresser will come along and intentionally uproot these
things in our lives, for the purpose of making us fruitful! The trestle
or lattice that the Vinedresser utilizes, trains the branch to go in an
upward direction. (That's the work of the Holy Spirit in our lives -- He
is leading onward and upward -- moveon.org)

When the Vinedresser reroutes the branch, the branch has no choice but
to draw all its sustenance from the vine! What are we trusting in? When
we begin to draw our sustenance from the vine instead of from our own
deep- rooted systems, the result will be many large clusters of sweet
grapes, which to the Vinedresser equals fruitfulness.

Lifting up (Pruning) the grapevines canopy will influence not only the
potential yield of the crop, but also the quality of the grapes. This is
due to the access of air and sunlight needed for the grapes to ripen
fully and for preventing various diseases.

I am believing the Father is going to show up at Empowerment 2024 to see
what we have attached ourselves to, that is causing fruitlessness or
bitter fruit to be birthed in us!

I am also believing He is going to uproot us and fasten us to something
that will lift us in an upward direction. The question then: Are we
going to allow him to hang us on the trestle or lattice of His choosing!

Rest assured, rather than chopping us off and throwing us away -- He is
in the house to lift us up! He is here to attach us to the lattice or
trestle of the Holy Spirit! He is here to start a work in our lives of
training us to grow in the right direction.[\]{.underline}

FRUITFUL BRANCHES

Some of us are sitting here tonight saying, "Whew, I am getting off
Scott free -- I am producing Kingdom fruit!" Not so quick! Matthew
15:1-2 (NKJV) "I am the true vine, and My Father is the vinedresser.
2 Every branch in Me that does not bear fruit He takes away; and [every
branch that bears fruit He prunes, that it may bear more
fruit]{.underline}."

(Pick up pruning shears)

Who is subject to pruning?

- Unfruitful branches...

- Fruitful branches...

Who is authorized to do the pruning? The Husbandman and/or those He has
specifically trained and authorized to do so! Do you know, that with all
the modern agricultural machinery that has been developed -- No one has
been able to automate the pruning of a vineyard! To this very day, it is
all done by hand!

Grapevines grow through pruning. If all the energy goes into the leaves
and unproductive runners, then it will take away from the size and
quality of the fruit.

Some folks are at this conference who are at the top of their game. They
are walking in the most fruitful season of their lives. Yet, they are
experiencing the pain of pruning, and this makes no sense to us.

Even though the vines are yielding their strength, all vines must be
periodically pruned so that the size and quality of fruit can be
increased exponentially.

When I worked in the peach orchard, the grower knew that everything was
reliant on the trees being pruned at exactly the right time. The grower
knew the harvest was going to be affected proportionately with the
pruning, or lack of it.

When I look back on my experience in the peach orchard, the grower
wasn't trying to punish the trees with pruning. If the trees could talk,
they would tell you of the pain of that momentary experience, but when
harvesttime rolled around, they would thank the grower and his staff for
the pruning. (Play silent video of our Arizona Ash trees being pruned)

Are you ready for this?

Pruning ordered by the Husbandman is never punitive in nature -- It is
always future focused!

Ladies and gentlemen, the Father's pruning tool is His Word! He doesn't
show up with something that looks like this pruning tool that I hold in
my hand He shows up with His Word! (Trade pruning shears for a paper
Bible)

When the Father shows up with pruning shears in hand -- He doesn't have
punishment or discipline in mind, He shows up focused on the future! All
pruning by the Vinedresser is future focused! "Who for the joy set
before Him..."

Some of us in this room today:

- Are in survival mode...

- Are under stress...

- Have been damaged in some way...

Some of you made up your mind before you came to this conference, to go
a direction that you felt would help you recover. But the Vinedresser is
in the room today with his pruning shears to let you know; that's not
the direction you need to be going in; that's not the right decision.

He is here to lift you up and prune away the areas that are hindering
your destiny and your purpose. When He prunes us He is going to attach
us to that which will get us headed in the direction of the harvest!

Everything the Vinedresser does, including the pruning process, is done
with our future in mind.
