# LEFTOVERS

(October 17 & October 24, 2021 Series)

TEXT: Joel 2:15-27 (NKJV) "Blow the trumpet in Zion, Consecrate a
fast, Call a sacred assembly; Gather the people, Sanctify the
congregation, Assemble the elders, Gather the children and nursing
babes; Let the bridegroom go out from his chamber, And the bride from
her dressing room. Let the priests, who minister to the LORD, Weep
between the porch and the altar; Let them say, "Spare Your people, O
LORD, And do not give Your heritage to reproach, That the nations should
rule over them. Why should they say among the peoples, Where is their
God?'" Then the LORD will be zealous for His land, And pity His
people. The LORD will answer and say to His people, " Behold, I
will send you grain and new wine and oil, And you will be satisfied by
them; I will no longer make you a reproach among the nations. " But
I will remove far from you the northern army, And will drive him away
into a barren and desolate land, With his face toward the eastern sea
And his back toward the western sea; His stench will come up, And his
foul odor will rise, Because he has done monstrous things." Fear
not, O land; Be glad and rejoice, For the LORD has done marvelous
things! Do not be afraid, you beasts of the field; For the open
pastures are springing up, And the tree bears its fruit; The fig tree
and the vine yield their strength. Be glad then, you children of
Zion, And rejoice in the LORD your God; For He has given you the former
rain faithfully, And He will cause the rain to come down for you --- The
former rain, And the latter rain in the first month. The threshing
floors shall be full of wheat, And the vats shall overflow with new wine
and oil. " So [I will restore to you the years that the swarming
locust has eaten, The crawling locust, The consuming locust, And the
chewing locust,\
My great army which I sent among you]{.underline}. You shall eat in
plenty and be satisfied, And praise the name of the LORD your God, Who
has dealt wondrously with you; And My people shall never be put to
shame. Then you shall know that I am in the midst of Israel: I am
the LORD your God And there is no other. My people shall never be put to
shame."

INTRO: Please know that I am very much aware of the context of this
passage of Scripture. The Prophet Joel speaks this prophecy to Judah in
a time of national, moral, and economic crisis. The prophet admonishes
his nation and the church to turn to God in repentance. He declares that
if they are willing to do so, Jehovah will restore to them everything
the enemy has stolen, with interest! Interestingly enough, this prophecy
found its final fulfillment on the Day of Pentecost.

The point I want to make here is this: The people of Judah were supposed
to be the worshippers, and worshippers have the ability to get closer to
God than do others. Worship is the love language of the Kingdom.

Why is it that true worshippers seem to get closer to God than anyone
else? They know how to go beyond seeking the hand of God to knowing the
heart of God. "God made known His acts to the Children of Israel, He
made known His ways to Moses."

When one is permitted to know the heart of God, he/she automatically
receives everything they need from the hand of God! Matthew 6:33 (NKJV)
"But seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all
these things shall be added to you."

It should go without saying worshippers have a better chance than anyone
of receiving God's best!

Even though the children of Judah were natural worshippers, they had
allowed the enemy to come in and wreak havoc in their lives and leave
them in a position of poverty and spiritual decay. The picture being
painted here of the condition of the people of Judah is alarming to say
the least.

The amazing thing is, though the people had walked away from the Lord,
the Lord had not forsaken the people. He will never forsake His people!
Hebrews 13:5 (NKJV) "For He Himself has said, "I will
[never]{.underline} leave you nor forsake you." Pay attention to the
quotation marks in this verse. The writer of Hebrews is not speaking for
himself; he is quoting God and God said "I will NEVER leave you, nor
will I ever forsake you!" "God has never abandoned anyone."

Make no mistake about it -- God desires the first-fruit -- Our very
best, but if all you have left to give are the leftovers, He will work
with that as well.

## I. THE AGENDA OF THE ENEMY IS DESTRUCTION...

In case there is any doubt in anyone's mind today let me set the record
straight: God wants you blessed and the enemy wants you cursed:

- There are two opposing views...

- There are two opposite agendas...

- There are two very different results...

- The consequences are very different...

With the people of Judah, the enemy had totally decimated them. He had
taken them to new lows and accosted them at every level. These were the
people of God! They were the worshippers! God's promise to Judah was
that He would bless them from what was left -- The left-overs!

Just because we were raised in church and have a lengthy spiritual
genealogy does not mean we are exempt to falling into the traps of the
enemy!

The lives of the worshippers of Judah were in a sad state of disrepair.
The enemy appeared to be winning the war! But there was something left
and God was going to use it to get Judah blessed beyond belief!

The agenda of the enemy is very clear. John 10:10 (AMP) "The thief
comes only in order to steal and kill and destroy. [I came that they may
have and enjoy life, and have it in abundance]{.underline} (to the full,
till it overflows)."

Even though the enemy may have brought all kinds of hell into our lives,
the constant pattern of God's activity in the life of the believer is
rescue! Make no mistake about it; He can take the leftovers and work
wonders with them!

Trust me, we may feel that we have squandered every opportunity that has
been afforded, but He is the God of the leftovers! All we have to do is
give Him what's left and He will shock us in turning our situation
around.

## II. THE AGENDA OF GOD IS REDEMPTION...

The entire agenda of God is a process of redemption!

- The moment Adam sinned God rolled out a plan of redemption...

- Adams fall may have caught Adam by surprise but it did not catch God
 by surprise... When Adam sinned, God did not roll out "Plan B" He
 rolled out "Plan A!" God was going to take what was left and kick the
 daylights out of Lucifer, then rub his nose in it by populating Heaven
 with the leftovers!

- Throughout the Old and New Testaments, God was offering restoration to
 sinful people.... (Noah got drunk -- Abraham was a liar as was Isaac,
 and they named Jacob liar -- David was a murderer -- Solomon was a
 womanizer - Eli was a bad dad)

- The New Testament is initiated with the announcement of a savior...
 "For unto you is born this day in the city of David a Savior, which
 is Christ the Lord." (Paul was a terrorist -- Peter was a fickle
 follower -- The disciples were weak and untrustworthy)

CONCLUSION:

If you are in any doubt about the motives of God, let me settle it for
you once and for all! John 3:16 (NKJV) "For God so loved the world
that He gave His only begotten Son, [that whoever believes in Him should
not perish]{.underline} but have everlasting life." 2 Peter 3:9
(NKJV) "The Lord is not slack concerning His promise, as some count
slackness, but is longsuffering toward us, [not willing that any should
perish]{.underline} but that all should come to repentance."

The intentions of God are unmistakable with regard to Judah and with
regard to us: He wants to make something of the leftovers of our lives!

END OF SERMON #1 OCTOBER 17, 2021

## III. GOD CAN WORK WITH LEFT-OVERS!

Though our lives have been almost completely destroyed by the battering
ram of life, there is no doubt about it; God can work with the
leftovers!

When an automobile has been in an accident, the place for the car is a
body shop. One body shop had as its slogan "We fix wrecks!" That
should be the heart of every church. Most people who come to Christ have
been:

- Wrecked like a wretch...

- Beat up from the feet up...

- Tore up from the floor up...

- Messed up from the neck up...

- Broken from the dopin'...

- Reeling from a relationship...

I do not know many people who came to Christ until they had been
wrecked!

Some ministries and churches only want the up-and-outers; they do not
want to mess with the down-and-outers! Have you ever followed a car that
was running down the road cockeyed? Someone took the car to the wrong
body shop! When we see a person, whose life is cockeyed we can be pretty
confident, they have been fixed by the wrong source! They stopped at the
wrong church! (I mean body shop!)

I have been known to drive across the city to get to a great restaurant
and I would drive across this city to be in a powerful Pentecostal
church that believes in and demonstrates the power of God!

When Lot chose the well-watered plain of Jordan, Abraham was consigned
to the left-overs. What was left was a land that was not very
productive! (Some of you have been assigned the left-overs!)

Lot took the best land for himself in spite of the fact the wicked
cities of Sodom and Gomorrah were located there. If you think Lot did
not know what the risks were, you are wrong! The Word said, "He
pitched his tent toward Sodom." The end result of Lot's decision was
a total train wreck! He found himself tethered to:

- A dead wife...

- A drinking problem...

- An incestuous relationship with his daughter... (Because of the
 alcohol... I do not know of one redeeming quality about alcohol that
 cannot be accomplished another way!)

- He became the father of two inbred, evil nations -- Moab and Ammon...
 They caused church problems (I mean they caused Israel problems) for
 their entire existence.

Parents, grandparents, church leaders, saints, listen to the heart of
this preacher today; it does not pay to pitch your tent toward Sodom!
What does pitching your tent toward Sodom look like in our lives?

- Spending inferior amounts of time in the Word and prayer...

- Pursuing riches at the expense of our spiritual walk...

- Allowing things in our homes that are not pleasing to the Lord...
 (What I allow in His house -- He will allow in my house) (What we do
 in moderation -- Our children will do to excess)

- Allowing our families to prioritize sports and education above the
 need to be connected at the church...

- Placing ministry on the shelf as we pursue our careers and temporal
 success...

There are some folks in this building today that are going to pay for
pitching their tent toward Sodom -- and they will pay with their
children and grandchildren. What I want to know is, are we prepared to
pay the heavy price for loose living! (Couple I begged not to leave the
church and each other -- Paid with their kids)

In case my warning against pitching your tent toward Sodom has come a
little late, I want you to know, God is an artist at working with
leftovers!

Abraham was relegated to what was left from Lot's original decision. God
turned an inferior, lack-luster land into a Land of Promise, and caused
it to "Flow with milk and honey" with what was left! The desolate
piece of land that Abraham got left with is responsible for most of the
political turmoil in the world today. Every nation in the Middle East
wants to own the Promised Land! Nobody wants to own Sodom!

If all you have left are the leftovers, I dare you to trust Gpd with
them! He is the God of the left-overs!

In pursuit of a new and anointed King for Israel, the prophet Samuel
inspected the seven sons of Jesse. When the Lord rejected all of Jesse's
seven sons, the prophet Samuel said "Is this all of your sons?"
Jesse said, "I have one left but he is a teenager and a shepherd!"
God took what was left and:

- Turned a nation around spiritually...

- Turned a nation around financially...

- Transitioned the land from war to peace...

- Prepared and resourced the people to build Him a house of worship...

He is the God of the leftovers!

God gave Gideon 32,000 fighting men and told him to annihilate the
Midianites and the Amalekites. Before they could go to war God told
Gideon "Let everyone who is afraid, go home." Gideon said goodbye
to 22,000 men (Using the word men loosely). God said, "You still have
too many men. Take them to the brook and those who drink like dogs, send
them home." Gideon lost 9,700 more soldiers! Gideon is left with:

- 300 fighting men...

- Each man had a trumpet...

- Each man had a pitcher with a lantern inside...

- Each man was to shout "The sword of the Lord and of Gideon" and
 break the pitcher and lantern...

The Lord took Gideon's little army and completely destroyed the
innumerable host of the Midianites and Amalekites! He can work wonders
with the leftovers!

CONCLUSION:

From the leftovers God can:

- Make a saint from the sinner...

- Turn a broken heart into a hopeful heart...

- Turn financial disaster into a financial success...

- Put a family back together...

- He can turn a house into a home...

- Turn an unhealthy body into the picture of health... (If a doctor can
 practice medicine on what's left, don't you think God can do a healing
 on what's left?)

- Build an unbelievable church... (The slogan of the church should be
 "We fix wrecks!")

He is truly the God of the leftovers!

(End of October 24, 2021 Sermon)
