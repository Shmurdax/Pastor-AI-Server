# A GOOD MAN
Funeral for Joe Lara 
6/3/19 
 
We are here to celebrate the memory of Joe 
Lara. I didn’t know Joe that well, I visited him 
in the hospital a couple of times and had the 
opportunity of praying for him. 
 
I have known Miguel and Patricia for almost 
twenty years. This is an amazing family who 
has a deep-rooted faith in God. 
 
Joe was born 1/22/1940 and departed this life on 
5/28/2019 at the age of 79 years, 4 months, and 
6 days. 
 
In a world where good men are growing scarcer 
by the day, Joe was a good man. One does not 
become a good man by winning a popularity 
contest or an election; one becomes known as a 
good man by the sum total of the choices he 
makes in life. 

## I. JOE WAS A GOOD CITIZEN…
Joe faithfully served his country in the United 
States Marines. I stand here today to salute a 
man who was willing to give of himself for the 
greater good of this country. While others were 
burning flags and speaking evil of the United 
States of America, Joe was serving as a proud 
Marine and always will be a proud Marine. 
 
Joe was a good man! 
 

## II. JOE WAS A GOOD HUSBAND…
Joe was married to Shirley for more than 61 
years! They were committed to each other in a 
way this generation can hardly understand: 
•  Cultural problems could not separate 
them… 
•  Financial problems could not separate 
them… 
•  Health problems could not separate them… 
•  Nothing but death could separate Joe and 
Shirley… 
 

Joe and Shirley are the kind of people who 
make choices and then stand by them, no matter 
what! 
 
Joe was a good man! 
 

## III. JOE WAS A GOOD FATHER…
Joe and Shirley had three daughters: Joann, 
Patricia and Kathy. 
 
Joe taught his family the value of making a 
living. He was a good provider and a man who 
worked very hard. He worked for the railroad 
for more than 30 years. After his retirement he 
enjoyed landscaping and staying busy around 
the house. 
 
Between his three daughters they gave him: 
•  12 Grandchildren… 
•  32 Great Grandchildren… 
•   Great Great Grandchildren… 
 
Joe was a good man. 

CONCLUSION: 
The one thing Joe would want me to do is to 
give each of you an opportunity to accept 
Christ. If you do not know Jesus today, this 
entire congregation is going to repeat a prayer 
after me in just a moment. If you will repeat the 
prayer and mean it in your heart, you can be 
saved. Please bow your heads and everyone 
repeat this prayer after me. 
 
Dear Lord Jesus. I admit I am a sinner and 
cannot save myself. I am sorry for every sin I 
have ever committed. Please forgive me of my 
sins. I accept your blood sacrifice as more than 
enough to cover all of my sins. I accept you and 
declare that Jesus is now Lord of my life. I will 
serve you all the days of my life. I ask all these 
things in Jesus name, Amen.
