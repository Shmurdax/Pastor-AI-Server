As we begin this series of sermons I want to make sure everyone is on
the same page when we speak of giants in our lives. Giants in our lives
are things which seem almost impossible to beat or conquer. We all have
giants that we must face in our personal and professional lives, and
these giants will either give us a tremendous opportunity to step up to
the plate and allow God to be God or they will keep us from being less
that God planned for us to be, and in the worst case scenario they will
keep us defeated in our walk.

2 Corinthians 10:3-5 (LT) "We are human, but we don't wage war with

During the course of this study we are going to be using the life of
David as a template to discover what kind of giants we may face in our
lives. David faced five giants in the course of his life. (READ THE
SCRIPTURE TEXT HERE)

We can be sure we will face some of the same giants David faced. As a
Christian, the key to overcoming these giants is to identify and defeat
them one by one through the power of the Holy Spirit and the Word of
God. We may not have all of these evident in our lives, but chances are
at least one has been identified and needs to be dealt with.

## I. GOLIATH...

The name Goliath means "To reveal or advertise in a disgraceful sense;
reviler; verbal abuser; or gossiper." Think about this, what weapon did
Goliath use against the men of Israel? Words! The tongue! For forty days
he dared them to "send a man that we may fight together." (This is
the old maids prayer!!!)

We could say that Goliath is the giant of the untamed tongue. This is
probably a giant which all of us face from time to time in varying
degrees. It is certainly one of the biggest giants that we will face in
our lifetimes.

The tongue is a powerful weapon and can be used for good or evil.

What makes the destruction which the tongue can inflict so alarming is

fact that a destructive tongue is usually the result of several things:

- Unresolved wounds in ones life...

- Un-restrained bitterness...

- Un-confessed and un-conquered sin...

The person who is always striking out at the weaknesses in others is
usually attempting to cover up major failures and judgment lapses in
their own life.

Someone said "Hurting people, hurt people." The un-confessed sin of
bitterness is a major source of strength for the giant of the untamed
tongue.

## II. ISHBI-BENOB...

2 Samuel 21:15-17 (NKJV) "When the Philistines were at war again with
Israel, David and his servants with him went down and fought against the
Philistines; and David grew faint. Then [Ishbi-Benob]{.underline},
who was one of the sons of the giant, the weight of whose bronze spear
was three hundred shekels, who was bearing a new sword, thought he could

struck the Philistine and killed him. Then the men of David swore to
him, saying, "You shall go out no more with us to battle, lest you
quench the lamp of Israel."

Please note that just because you have dealt with one giant, does not
mean they are all dead! In a perfect world, when you have killed one
giant the rest of them leave you alone but this is not a perfect world
and the giants just keep coming.

In this scenario, David killed Goliath but once he laid that victory to
rest, Ishbi-Benob had to be dealt with. The name Ishbi-Benob means "My
dwelling is on the heights" or "I belong above." Without doing any
injustice to Scripture, I believe we could say Ishbi-Benob represents
pride. As soon as Ishbi-Benob's name is mentioned the Scripture gives
this description of him, "...The weight of whose bronze spear was
three hundred shekels, and he was bearing a new sword." Everything
about this guy reeks of pride and "He thought he could kill
David."

The giant of pride manifests itself in many ways throughout our
lifetime:

- Pride say's "You deserve better..."

- Pride causes one to stretch the truth...

- Pride causes us to cover our weaknesses and parade our strengths...

- Pride causes us to value and devalue certain relationships based on
 whether or not they are good for our public persona...

- Pride causes us to run into debt in an attempt to prove we are just as
 financially successful as the next person...

- Pride say's "I had this outfit imported; it is of the finest quality
 available anywhere in the world..."

- Pride will cause us to buy a Rolex knockoff in an attempt to make
 people think we are wearing the real thing... The only person who gets
 deceived in this scenario is the person wearing the watch! We can see
 the green on your wrist!!!

assumption of victory. Look at what the Scripture has to say about the
giant of pride.

Pride is a giant which uses the element of surprise as a masquerade to
gain entrance into our lives. Oftentimes this giant has set up a
stronghold within us without our even being aware of what has been going
on. This is one of the reasons each person needs an accountability
partner who has permission to speak into each of our lives. Others know
pride is present before the individual is aware of what is going on. In
the case of an accountability partner they can tell us what they see
taking place within us and rather than getting upset with our
accountability partner, we deal with the giant that is creating a place
of dominance within.

Pride shows up with a new spear and weapon. It is impressive and
glitters brighter than a diamond. It often veils itself within a spirit
of excellence. A spirit of Excellence is a good thing but if we are not
careful there is but one step between it and a spirit of pride.

Pride has a way of sneaking up on us in new ways throughout our
Christian walk. New Christians are not as susceptible to the spirit of
pride as seasoned believers are. Pride often follows a great victory or
it can surface after a string of victories. When pride is at work we
begin to feel superior or invincible.

The battle against pride becomes greater as we get more victories
notched on our spiritual belt. It looms greater as we become more
skilled at firing our rapid firing gospel gun!

From the time David killed Goliath, he was in a constant battle against
the giant of pride:

- Pride caused David to try to kill Nabal... ( Sam 25) Pride is what
 drives us to attempt vengeance, rather than allowing the Lord to do
 His job... Deuteronomy 32:35-36 (NKJV) "Vengeance is Mine, and
 recompense; Their foot shall slip in due time; For the day of their
 calamity is at hand, And the things to come hasten upon them.' 
 "For the [Lord]{.smallcaps} will judge His people And have compassion
 on His servants, When He sees that their power is gone, And there is
 no one remaining, bond or free."

- Pride caused David to commit adultery... (2 Samuel 11) David placed
 himself above the Law. He knew the woman Bathsheba was married and
 even knew her husband was one of his most loyal soldiers. Pride caused
 David to feel it was his right to have anyone he wanted.

- Pride caused David to number the fighting men... (1 Chronicles 21)
 70,000 men died by the plague.

Though most of us will battle with the giant of pride throughout our
Christian walk, this battle seems to be most severe at certain intervals
and these are the times we are most vulnerable:

- Following a great victory...

- Following a series of minor triumphs...

- Following times of success in business...

- Following a time when we have been recognized for certain
 achievements...

- Following times when our peers have recognized certain strengths in
 our lives...

- When we are weary from the labor of life and we have let down our
 guard on the spiritual front...

David could not kill this giant on his own! 2 Samuel 21:15-17 (NKJV)
"When the Philistines were at war again with Israel, David and his
servants with him went down and fought against the Philistines; and
David grew faint. Then [Ishbi-Benob]{.underline}, who was one of the

Who helped David in his fight against the giant of pride? "Abishai

means "leprous one." The person who stepped forward was the son of a

If we are to conquer this giant in our life we will likely need the
dynamic of some outside influences at work within us. (Accountability
Partner) James 5:16 (KJV) "[Confess your faults one to
another]{.underline}, and pray one for another, that ye may be healed.
The effectual fervent prayer of a righteous man availeth much." The
confession here is about accountability, not forgiveness. God always
forgives when we confess to him, but when we confess to an
accountability partner we are tied to a new level of responsibility
because that person now has permission to ask questions and speak into
our lives.

Every person will sooner or later struggle with the giant of pride and
it can manifest itself as a superior attitude or on the low side of the
scale it can manifest itself as low self-esteem. Romans 12:3 (KJV)
"For I say, through the grace given to me, to everyone who is among
you, [not to think of himself more highly than he ought to
think]{.underline}, but to think soberly, as God has dealt to each one a
measure of faith." 1 Corinthians 10:12 (KJV) "Therefore let him
who thinks he stands take heed lest he fall."

Keys to Victory

Who won the victory over Ishbi-Benob? This time it was not David but
Abishai, the warrior who became a "gift of grace" in David's life.
This is what we must do:

- Realize we are unable to conquer this giant solo... Zechariah 4:6
 (NKJV) "This is the word of the [Lord]{.smallcaps} to Zerubbabel:
 'Not by might nor by power, but by My Spirit,' Says the
 [Lord]{.smallcaps} of hosts. 'Who are you, O great mountain?
 Before Zerubbabel you shall become a plain! And he shall bring forth
 the capstone With shouts of "Grace, grace to it!"

- Realize God has provided sources of strength in the day of our
 battle... If we are to be successful against the giant of pride we can
 ill afford to go it alone, God and others are essential to defeating
 this enemy.

- Relinquish our rights to privacy... Privacy is the hotbed of Satan! He
 uses secrecy in his battle against the child of God. If we learn to
 bury our faults and failures rather than confessing them he will have
 us believing we are the only ones in the world who are struggling with
 these issues. Getting our faults and failures out in the open destroys
 the giant of pride and accelerates the healing and deliverance of the
 believer.

deception and is therefore a form of lying. The worlds system lends
itself to each person developing a public persona and a private persona.

Integrity occurs when an individual's life is in harmony with God and
their public persona and private persona are one and the same.

## V. The Giant of Greed

2 Samuel 21:20-21 (NKJV) "Yet again there was war at Gath, where

imposing, has six fingers on each hand and six toes on each foot. This
descriptive language points to a powerful grasp or maybe greed or
covetousness. This giant represents greed and this is the giant I would
like us to address here.

One of the great problems with greed is that it never allows for a place
of contentment in life. Philippians 4:11-13 (NKJV) "Not that I speak
in regard to need, for I have learned in [whatever state I am, to be
content]{.underline}: I know how to be abased, and I know how to
abound. Everywhere and in all things I have learned both to be full and
to be hungry, both to abound and to suffer need. I can do all things
through Christ who strengthens me."

One of the great things in life is being able to find a comfort zone, so
to speak, in which one can live and be able to enjoy the trip through
life. Greed interrupts such a life and never allows any semblance of
normalcy to return.

Greed is closely associated with Idolatry. When greed begins its
conquest of a person's life, it shoves to the side all sense of reason
and sets wealth and power up as a thing to be worshipped instead of God!
Greed is an idolatrous practice! This association of possessions and
power develops a culture of enslavement that causes the affected person
to live in a fog of deceit that has a tendency to skew reality, not
allowing the affected person to conduct an honest assessment of where
they are really at.
