# THE MANDATE OF THE CHURCH

INTRO:

I am a champion of the Church. Some have a pessimistic view of the
Church, but I am not one of them. Some believe the church is going to
cease to exist, I couldn't disagree more. (One person told me, "The
Church is going away!" I said, "How right you are! LOL)

I have been in Church my entire life. There has never been a season of
my life that doesn't involve the Church. It is a complete accident that
I wasn't born in a Church.

- I was dedicated to the Lord in a church...

- I attended Sunday School in a church...

- I was born again in a church...

- I was baptized in water through a church...

- I was baptized in Holy Spirit in a church... (You can call me, The
 Don! LOL)

- I was called to ministry in a church...

- I was married in a church...

- I raised my family in a church

- I dedicated my children to the Lord in a church...

- I have pastored a church for forty-seven years...

- My funeral will be held in a church...

- I am a product of the Church therefore I am a champion of the
 Church...

Trust me, the church is not going to cease to exist! "Upon this
rock..." He is coming back for "A glorious church that does not
have spot, wrinkle, or any such thing!"

If the Church is going to endure, what is the mandate of the Church? To
answer this question, I want to use the dedication of Solomons Temple.

The setting of the sixth chapter of 2 Chronicles is as follows:

- Solomon had built a house for God...

- The construction of the Temple had been completed...

- The Temple had been furnished according to the instructions of the
 Lord...

- The people had gathered for the dedication of this historic
 structure...

- Solomon spoke to the people outlining how the building had come
 about... ("You have spoken with your mouth and performed it with
 your hand.") If you are engaged in building His Church, look
 closely! His hand is on top of your hands!

- Solomon reminded God of His promises to His people and staked a claim
 for those promises... I am here to stake a claim tonight for the
 fulfillment of the mandate of the 21^st^ Century Church.

It is my belief that the prayer of Solomon reflects the mandate of the
Church.

THE MANDATE OF THE CHURCH IS THAT IT WILL BE A PLACE OF
FORGIVENESS

2 Chronicles 6:21 (NKJV) "May You hear the supplications of Your
servant and of Your people Israel, when they pray toward this place.
Hear from heaven Your dwelling place, and when You hear, forgive."

Solomon's request was that God, and the people, would be quick to
forgive sinners and one another. He prayed that the Temple would always
be a place of forgiveness.

Certainly, God is a God of forgiveness. Therefore, the Church should be
a place of forgiveness. When people come to Christ, we must forgive them
and assist them in forgiving themselves. Psalm 103:12 (NKJV) "As far
as the east is from the west, So far has He removed our transgressions
from us."

In order to fulfill the mandate of the Lord, the Church must become
known for it's willingness to forgive!

THE MANDATE OF THE CHURCH IS TO BE A PLACE OF MERCY

2 Chronicles 6:22-23 (NKJV) "If anyone sins against his neighbor, and
is forced to take an oath, and comes and takes an oath before Your altar
in this temple, 23 then hear from heaven, and act, and judge Your
servants, bringing retribution on the wicked by bringing his way on his
own head, and justifying the righteous by giving him according to his
righteousness."

Solomon was praying that the Temple would be a place of mercy! His
prayer was that...

- If someone has taken an oath, they cannot live up to... Let this be a
 house of mercy!

- If anyone sins against his neighbor... Let this be a house of mercy!

- If anyone misses the mark... Let this be a house of mercy!

- In retribution... Let this be a house of mercy!

When are we going to stop killing our wounded? When are we going to stop
beating the brakes off of those who show mercy? The military will risk
the lives of unwounded soldiers to retrieve a dead body from the
battlefield!

We are okay with having God's mercy meter reset in our lives every day,
but I am wondering how willing we are to push the reset button for
others.

If we are going to fulfill our mandate, the Church must be a place
marked by mercy!

THE MANDATE OF THE CHURCH IS TO BE A PLACE OF RESTORATION

2 Chronicles 6:24-25 (NKJV) "If Your people Israel are defeated
before an enemy because they have sinned against You, and return and
confess Your name, and pray and make supplication before You in this
temple, 25 then hear from heaven and forgive the sin of Your people
Israel, and bring them back to the land which You gave to them and their
fathers."

Solomon was asking God to cause the Temple to always be a place of
restoration. He wanted the Temple default to be restoration.

It is one thing to have mercy and forgive; it is another matter to dirty
our hands in the process of restoration! Restoration is both an attitude
and an activity.

Solomon said:

- When they sin... Restore them!

- When they are defeated... Restore them!

- When they are taken captive... Restore them!

- When they cry out to you... Restore them!

The constant state of God's activity is rescue!

There are those out there who have sinned against God, you, and your
church. If we are going to reach them, we must start here and now to be
people of restoration who build a house of restoration!

Don't forget... "All have sinned and come short of the glory of
God."

(We fix wrecks -- Car going down the road sideways -- Went to the wrong
body shop!)

THE MANDATE OF THE CHURCH IS TO BE A PLACE OF HARVEST AND PROSPERITY

2 Chronicles 6:26-27 (NKJV)  "When the heavens are shut up and there
is no rain because they have sinned against You, when they pray toward
this place and confess Your name, and turn from their sin because You
afflict them, 27 then hear in heaven, and forgive the sin of Your
servants, Your people Israel, that You may teach them the good way in
which they should walk; and send rain on Your land which You have given
to Your people as an inheritance."

 

Solomon recognized that the result of the sins of the people manifests
itself by dragging them down into an impoverished condition. Despite
this, he asked God, "When the people pray in or toward the Temple
that the house of the Lord would produce harvest and eventually
prosperity!"

Listen to his prayer...

- When they sin against you... Turn them toward the house of harvest and
 prosperity!

- When the Heavens are shut up... Turn them toward the house of harvest
 and prosperity!

- When they are impoverished... Turn them toward the house of harvest
 and prosperity!

- When they have no food... Turn them toward the house of harvest and
 prosperity!

Do you know anyone who is broke, busted, and disgusted? The preaching,
teaching and discipleship of the Church should lead them out of poverty
and into prosperity!

I was raised in poverty! Do you know where I learned how to get out of a
lifestyle of poverty? The church! I knew I couldn't work my way out -- I
had to give my way out!

Is there a parent in here that wants any of their children to struggle
one day in the pit of poverty? We serve a "How much more God!"

The Mandate of the Church is to be a place of harvest and prosperity!
