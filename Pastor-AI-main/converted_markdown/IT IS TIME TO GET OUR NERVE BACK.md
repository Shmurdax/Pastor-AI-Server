# IT IS TIME

TO GET OUR NERVE BACK

Proverbs 30:15-16 (NIV) ""The leech has two daughters. 'Give! Give!'
They cry. "There are three things that are never satisfied, four that
never say, 'Enough!': ^16 ^the grave, the barren womb, land, which is
never satisfied with water, and fire, which never says, 'Enough!'"

Notice how Solomon couches the following phrase: "The leech has two
daughters. 'Give! Give!' They cry." The "Give! Give!" here are
what is known as reflexive verbs. A reflexive verb is spoken by the
agent - and performs an action that is directed at the agent.

What I want to focus on today is the reflexes. Reflexes are routed from
the nerve directly through the spinal cord, and then to the muscle -
without ever going to the brain:

- When we get surprised our reflex is to scream like a girl...

- When something hurts us, our reflex is to recoil...

- My mom used to let us sit or stand in the front seat, without a
 seatbelt -- When she hit her brake her reflex was to throw her right
 arm up in front of us...

These things happen automatically, and do not employ our normal
checklist of rational reasoning that we usually go through before
deciding how to respond.

When we go to the doctor, he/she will usually check our reflexes. Have
you ever wondered why they do such a thing? When you think about it,
this seems to be a little peculiar. The doctor checking our reflexes is
much more than an icebreaker between doctor and patient.

The doctor checks the reflexes:

- To determine if there is something wrong with the nerves...

- To check to see if there is a back injury...

- To make sure everything is normal with the spinal cord...

A bad or slow reflex can be an indicator of certain diseases and/or
nerve damage.

Proverbs declares the reflex of the leech is to cry "give, give."
A leech is a blood sucking insect. A leech will attach itself to a human
or animal and suck blood until it pops or becomes so large that it can
no longer hold on and continue to drink blood. In so doing, the leech
say's "give, give" without thinking about it, because that is what
a leech does!

Could it be that our reflexes are off because we have some nerve damage?
Have we lost our nerve? Do we have the nerve to believe God for things
that are bigger than we can do on our own?

As the Pastor of this church, I must bring myself to respond to the
vision of the house, just like you have to respond to it. It is not my
vision; it is God's vision.

When the Board interviewed me to be the Pastor of CT Church, they were
very emphatic that they were looking for someone who had the nerve to
cast and implement vision. (Since that is what they were looking for, I
believe they chose the right person)

How long do we have to think about doing the will of God before actually
doing it? Some of us have great reflexes of faith and others of us need
to get our nerve back today!

I want to take the next few minutes and look at some folks in the
Scripture that did not have to think about their response to the will of
God:

- Noah -- Hebrews 11:7 (NKJV) "By faith Noah, being divinely warned
 of things not yet seen, moved with godly fear, prepared an ark for the
 saving of his household, by which he condemned the world and became
 heir of the righteousness which is according to faith."

- Three Hebrew Children -- Daniel 3:8-16 (KJV) "At that time certain
 Chaldeans came near, and accused the Jews. 9 They said to king
 Nebuchadnezzar, 10 King, you have made a decree, that every man that
 shall hear the sound of the music, shall fall down and worship the
 golden image: 11 And whoever does not fall down and worship, shall be
 cast into the midst of a burning fiery furnace. 12 There are CERTAIN
 JEWS whom thou hast set over the affairs of the province of Babylon,
 Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego; these men, O king, have not regarded
 you: they do not serve your gods, nor worship the golden image which
 you have set up. 13 Then Nebuchadnezzar in his rage and fury commanded
 to bring Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego. Then they brought these men
 before the king. 14  Nebuchadnezzar said unto them, Is it true, O
 Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego, that you do not serve my gods, nor
 worship the golden image which I have set up? 15 Now if you are ready,
 when you hear the sound of the music, you fall down and worship the
 image which I have made; WELL: but if you do not worship, you shall be
 cast into the burning fiery furnace; and [who is that God that shall
 deliver you out of my hands]{.underline}? 16  Shadrach, Meshach, and
 Abednego, answered and said to the king, O Nebuchadnezzar, we are not
 careful to answer you in this matter. (One version say's "We
 don't need to think about this." They are about to take a reflexive
 action proving they have not lost their nerve) Notice verse 17 "If
 it be so, (this refers to the king saying, "If you do not bow
 down, I am going to throw you into the fire") our God whom we
 serve is able to deliver us from the burning fiery furnace, and he
 WILL deliver us out of your hand, O king. 18 But if not, (This does
 not refer to whether or not God will deliver, it refers to whether the
 king is going to throw them into the fire) be it known unto thee, O
 king, that we will not serve thy gods, nor worship the golden image
 which thou hast set up." The action of the three Hebrew Children
 was a reflexive action that proved they had not lost their nerve!

- John on Patmos -- Revelation 1:10 (KJV) "I was in the Spirit on the
 Lord\'s day..." John was banished to the worst possible place and his
 reflex was to be in the Spirit of God. He did not have time to feel
 sorry for himself; his reflex was to be in the Spirit! John did not
 have any nerve damage!

If we were to check our reflexes today what would we find? Would we
discover that we do not even have to think about what to do, that we
automatically gravitate toward faith?

Would it be revealed that we trust in the flesh rather than trust in
God? Psalm 20:7 (NIV) "Some trust in chariots and some in horses, but
we trust in the name of the Lord our God"

When we face hard times, do we have the nerve to trust God?

- Family problems -- Psalm 68:6 (NKJV) "God sets the solitary in
 families; He brings out those who are bound into prosperity; But the
 rebellious dwell in a dry land." Do we have the nerve to believe
 God for household salvation?

- Financial problems -- Luke 6:38 (NKJV) "Give, and it will be given
 to you: good measure, pressed down, shaken together, and running over
 will be put into your bosom. For with the same measure that you use,
 it will be measured back to you." Do we have the nerve to give in
 the face of financial risk? (Use corn flakes here)

- Health problems -- 1 Peter 2:24 (NKJV) "Who Himself bore our sins
 in His own body on the tree, that we, having died to sins, might live
 for righteousness---by whose stripes you were healed." Do we have the
 nerve to believe the report of the Lord rather than believing the
 report of the world?

CONCLUSION:

Do we have the nerve to do the will of God? Have we lost our nerve? Are
we risk takers? Or do we constantly play it down the middle in the
safety zone?

Not one person mentioned in Hebrews 11 played it safe! They were all
risk takers!

- Able offered the first-fruits...

- Enoch walked with God and got closer to God's house than his house...

- Noah built and boarded the Ark...

- Abraham went out not knowing where he was going...

- Sarah birthed a child at 90 years of age...

- Moses became a first-time pastor at 80 years of age...

- Rahab kept the secret of the spies in the face of treason charges...

These people have one thing in common, NERVE, also known as faith! Faith
causes us to take risks... Risk-taking is where the reward is at... "No
pain, no gain..." "If we want a God-sized future, we must get off the
porch of the past!"
