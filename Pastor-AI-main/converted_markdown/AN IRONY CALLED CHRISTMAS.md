INTRO: Christians are people of faith. 
There is much about our belief system that 
cannot be proven by normal accepted 
practices of rationality. The irony of the 
whole thing is that no one can explain: 
• How God spoke the world into 
existence… 
• How God created man from the dust of 
the ground… 
• How a virgin could give birth to a 
baby… 
• How a God, without a body could 
become a man with a body… 
• How a Holy God could tolerate thirty-
three and one-half years of rubbing 
shoulders with a sinful society… 

• How an all-powerful God could allow 
His sin-less Son to die for the sins of 
humankind… 
• How a God-man, dead for three days, 
could rise from the dead… 
• How Jesus could defy the laws of 
nature… To walk through walls and 
make appearances to His disciples out of 
thin air. 
• How Jesus could disregard the law of 
gravity at Bethany’s launching pad to 
ascend to His father… 
 
Many things about Christianity must be 
accepted by faith; therefore, we are people 
of faith.

The Word of God has many ironies, yet 
nowhere in Scripture is the contrast between 
heaven and earth more evident than in the 
Christmas story. Luke takes us from the 
power and fame of Caesar to the weakness 
and obscurity of a baby in a stable, and then 
declares the baby in a manger to be a 
superior to Caesar. 
 
I would like us to look at the ironies in the 
Christmas story as told by Dr. Luke. 

IRONY #1 - JESUS EXPERIENCED A 
HUMAN BIRTH SO THAT WE COULD 
EXPERIENCE A SPIRITUAL BIRTH… 
Luke 2:4- 7 (NKJV) “Joseph also went up 
from Galilee, out of the city of Nazareth, 
into Judea, to the city of David, which is 
called Bethlehem, because he was of the 
house and lineage of David, 5to be 
registered with Mary, his betrothed wife, 
who was with child. So it was, that while 
they were there, the days were completed 
for her to be delivered. And she brought 
forth her firstborn Son, and wrapped Him 
in swaddling cloths, and laid Him in a 
manger, because there was no room for 
them in the inn.” Think of it, God 
becoming a human being. This is a plan so 
complex that it boggles the human mind, yet 
it is a plan so simple that the person with the 
lowest IQ can understand it. 
 
The thought of God becoming one of us is 
absolutely stunning to the human mind. If 

God becomes a human being He voluntarily 
accepts all the weaknesses, restrictions, and 
limitations experienced by mere mortals. 
It is ironic to think that a God so powerful 
that He constructed the world, created 
human life, and spoke the planets into place 
could condescend to become one of His own 
creatures. 
 
QUOTE: 
The late radio host Paul Harvey, once shared 
the following description of the Incarnation: 
“One raw winter night a man heard an 
irregular thumping sound against the 
kitchen storm door. He went to a window 
and watched as tiny, shivering sparrows, 
attracted to the evident warmth inside, beat 
in vain against the glass. Touched, the 
farmer bundled up and trudged through 
fresh snow to open the barn for the 
struggling birds. He turned on the lights, 
tossed some hay in a corner, and sprinkled a 
trail of saltine crackers to direct them to the 

barn. But the sparrows, which had scattered 
in all directions when he emerged from the 
house, still hid in the darkness, afraid of 
him. He tried various tactics: Circling 
behind the birds to drive them toward the 
barn; tossing cracker crumbs in the air 
toward them; retreating to his house to see if 
they’d flutter into the barn on their own. 
Nothing worked. He, a huge alien creature, 
had terrified them; the birds could not 
understand that he actually desired to help. 
He withdrew to his house and watched the 
doomed sparrows through a window. As he 
stared, a thought hit him like lightening from 
a clear blue sky: If only I could become a 
bird – one of them – just for a moment. Then 
I wouldn’t frighten them so. I could show 
them the way to warmth and safety. At the 
same moment, another thought dawned on 
him - He had grasped the whole principle of 
the Incarnation.” 

Doctor Luke describe the incarnation with 
great detail, right down to the: 
• Date and time… Tax time! 
• City… Bethlehem! 
• Place… A manger! 
• Clothing… Swaddling clothes! 
 
Man was created perfect and placed in a 
perfect environment called the Garden of 
Eden. As perfect as his environment was, 
mankind found a way to mess it up. Adam 
and Eve sinned, thus offending the holiness 
of God. The irony is that as soon as Adam 
and Eve sinned, God unveiled a plan of 
reconciliation for a fallen race. Genesis 3:15 
(NKJV) “And I will put enmity Between 
you and the woman, And between your 
seed and her Seed; He shall bruise your 
head, And you shall bruise His heel.” 
 
The only way sin could be atoned for was 
the sacrifice of a perfect human being. Wait 
a minute – a perfect human being? How 

could such a thing ever come to pass? From 
the time Adam and Eve sinned, decades and 
centuries came and went, yet not one human 
being could rise to the point of perfection in 
order to qualify as the sacrifice for sin. 
Romans 3:23 (NKJV) “For all have sinned 
and fall short of the glory of God.” 
 
In the Old Testament, the only way a 
sacrifice could satisfy a Holy God who had 
been offended by sin, was for that sacrifice 
to be spotless, blameless and perfect. 
 
In order to be the supreme sacrifice, this 
God-man would need to take upon Himself 
the form of a human being and the only way 
to do such a thing was to be born into the 
world through the strength of human flesh. 
 
Though Mary was a virgin, she brought 
Jesus into the world just like all other human 
beings are born. (Story of little boy’s who 
stole a statue of Mary from the Catholic 

Church and prayed “God if you ever want to 
see your mother again…”) 
 
The irony is… Jesus experienced a human 
birth so that we could receive a spiritual 
birth. Ephesians 2:1-6 (NKJV) “And you 
He made alive, who were dead in trespasses 
and sins, 2in which you once walked

IRONY #2 - JESUS OCCUPIED A 
STABLE SO THAT WE COULD 
OCCUPY A MANSION… 
Luke 2:4-7 (NKJV) “Joseph also went up 
from Galilee, out of the city of Nazareth, 
into Judea, to the city of David, which is 
called Bethlehem, because he was of the 
house and lineage of David, 5to be 
registered with Mary, his betrothed wife, 
who was with child. So it was, that while 
they were there, the days were completed 
for her to be delivered. And she brought 
forth her firstborn Son, and wrapped Him 
in swaddling cloths, and laid Him in a 
manger, because there was no room for 
them in the inn.” If we were to sit down to 
write a script for God to become a man we 
would probably begin with where such a 
happening should take place. We would 
probably have Him to be born in the most 
up-to-date hospital in the world, live in the 

finest mansion on the earth, be educated in 
the most excellent schools available and 
have the best of everything. 
 
According to Dr. Luke’s account of the birth 
of Jesus, God does not think on the same 
wavelength as we do. Isaiah 55:9 (NKJV) 
“For as the heavens are higher than the 
earth, So are My ways higher than your 
ways, And My thoughts than your 
thoughts.” 
 
Can you believe it? The savior of the world 
born to a peasant woman and a carpenter… 
and in a stable of all places! this must be the 
irony of all ironies! 
 
I believe Jesus was born to peasants in a 
stable so that He could take the most 
difficult path to the throne. Thus, He proves 
to us that the most disadvantaged person can 
follow His steps to prominence. 

ILLUSTRATION: 
An unusual evergreen is the lodgepole pine 
that is seen in great numbers in Yellowstone 
Park. The cones of this pine may hang on 
the tree for years and years, and even when 
they fall they do not open. These cones can 
only be opened when they come in contact 
with intense heat. When a forest fire rages 
throughout parks and forests all the trees are 
destroyed. At the same time, however, the 
heat of the fire opens the cones of the 
lodgepole pine; and these pines are often the 
first tree to grow in an area that has been 
burned by fire. The savior of the world was 
tested through the fire of adversity. His 
victory over adversity proves to all of us that 
we can succeed as well. 
 
Jesus, who was born in a stable, lived thirty-
three and a half sinless years, and ascended 
to the throne of Heaven, beckons to each of 
us to follow the path He has provided. John 

14:1-6 (NKJV) “Let not your heart be 
troubled; you believe in God, believe also in

4“And where I go you know, and the way

Though most of us are rich by this world’s 
overall standards, very few of us live in 
what the rich and famous would call a 
mansion. 
 
Take a look around the room today. The 
irony is, each person who has decided to 

follow Christ will one day live in a mansion 
that would blow the minds of the rich and 
famous. Heaven will be a place where: 
• The walls will be jasper… 
• Each gate will be one pearl… (I want to

How is this possible? Jesus was born in a 
stable so that we could live in a mansion.

relationship between Jehovah God and the 
human race. Throughout the Old Testament 
the relationship between God and man was 
strained, restricted, and limited at best.

IRONY #4 - JESUS BECAME SUBJECT 
TO HUMAN LIMITATIONS SO THAT 
WE COULD BE SET FREE FROM 
SIN’S RESTRICTIONS… 
Luke 2:4-7 (NKJV) “Joseph also went up 
from Galilee, out of the city of Nazareth, 
into Judea, to the city of David, which is 
called Bethlehem, because he was of the 
house and lineage of David, 5to be 
registered with Mary, his betrothed wife, 
who was with child. So it was, that while 
they were there, the days were completed 
for her to be delivered. And she brought 
forth her firstborn Son, and wrapped Him 

in swaddling cloths, and laid Him in a 
manger, because there was no room for 
them in the inn.” Ironically, when Jesus put 
on the robe of sinful flesh He willingly 
accepted the restrictions and limitations that 
such a thing carried with it. Jesus was God 
before coming to this earth and as such He 
was not limited to time or space. 
 
Though Jesus was a God-man, the man side 
of the scenario brought with it some 
limitations and restrictions. God becoming 
flesh meant that Jesus was limited by time 
and space. 
 
Christmas is a good time to talk about time 
and space because we all know that Santa is 
not limited by time or space! 
 
ILLUSTRATION: 
A preacher preached against Santa at 
Christmas and someone gave a message in 
tongues at the end of the sermon and the 

lady who interpreted it said, “Give Santa a 
break, he is a good man.” 
 
Because Jesus was a human being He was 
limited by time and space. As such, Jesus 
was a passionate person who had varied 
experiences: 
• He laughed… 
• He cried… 
• He loved… 
• He slept… 
• He became fatigued… 
• He became disappointed… 
• He developed relationships… 
• He experienced the pain of rejection… 
• He experienced the pain of a human 
body… 
• He experienced the mortality of human 
flesh… 
 
Why does man suffer all these limitations 
and restrictions? Because of the curse of 
sin! In the Garden of Eden man was neither 

limited by time nor space! The entire 
garden was at their disposal, and when they 
sinned God drove them out of the Garden to 
prevent them from eating of the Tree of Life 
and living forever in their sinful condition. 
 
The irony is that Jesus took upon himself the 
form of sinful flesh. 2 Corinthians 5:21 
(NKJV) “He made Him who knew no sin to 
be sin for us, that we might become the 
righteousness of God in Him.” 
 
What benefit is it for us that Jesus would 
come to the earth and do such a thing? 
• He became flesh so that we could 
become eternally saved… 
• He suffered so that we could live a 
rejoicing life… 
• He denied the enticement to sin so that 
we could deny it as well… 
• He took stripes on His back so that we 
could be healed… 

• He died on that horrible day so that we 
could live for an eternal day… 
• He rose from the dead so that death 
would no longer have dominion over 
us… 
• He ascended to OUR Heavenly Father so 
that He could make intercession for us…

ILLUSTRATION:

place where she lived was a pigpen! The 
biggest mess you can imagine! One 
seasoned inspector declared he’d never seen 
a residence in grater disarray. The woman 
had begged food at her neighbors’ back 
doors and gotten what clothes she had from 
the Salvation Army. From all outward 
appearances she was a penniless recluse, a 
pitiful and forgotten widow. But such was 
not the case. Amid the jumble of her 
unclean, disheveled belongings, two keys 
were found which led the officials to safe-
deposit boxes at two different local banks.

contained over 700 AT&T stock certificates, 
plus hundreds of other valuable certificates, 
bonds, and solid financial securities, not to 
mention a stack of cash amounting to more

certificates, only more currency, lots of it, 
more than $600,000 to be exact. Adding the 
net worth of both boxes, they found that the
