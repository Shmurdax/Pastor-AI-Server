# THE VALUE OF AN INDIVIDUAL

TEXT: Luke 15:1-7 "Now the tax collectors and "sinners" were all
gathering around to hear him. But the Pharisees and the teachers of the
law muttered, "This man welcomes sinners and eats with them." Then Jesus
told them this parable: "Suppose one of you has a hundred sheep and
[loses one of them]{.underline}. Does he not [leave the ninety-nine in
the open country and go after the lost sheep until he finds
it]{.underline}? And when he finds it, he [joyfully puts it on his
shoulders]{.underline} and goes home. Then he [calls his friends and
neighbors together and says, 'Rejoice with me; I have found my lost
sheep]{.underline}.' I tell you that in the same way [there will be more
rejoicing in heaven over one sinner who repents]{.underline} than over
ninety-nine righteous persons who do not need to repent."

INTRO: In our society everything is based on number; how much money
a person has, how high or low the stock market is the gross sales of a
company, the population of a city, and on and on I could go!

This group mentality has even found its way into the Church. We have
judged ourselves on:

- How big or small the crowd is...

- How many our group has in it...

- How large our staff is...

- How many people came to the evangelistic crusade...

- How many joined the Church...

- How many were baptized...

- How big the budget is...

- How many total dollars were given to missions...

The question must be asked, is God at all interested in our facts and
figures? Is He interested in our numbers? Yes! Several times in the
Bible people were commanded by God to number various things!

- The woman counted her coins...

- The fisherman counted his fish...

- The king counted his troops...

- The farmer counted his crop...

- The builder counted the cost...

While all of these things are important, our scripture text makes us
aware that nothing is more important than an individual.

The United States and Japan conducted a study to determine the value of
an individual. This study was based on the composition of the human
body. The value of the human body:

- 65% oxygen...

- 18% carbon

- 10% hydrogen...

- 3% nitrogen...

- 1.5% calcium...

- 1% phosphorus...

- .05% magnesium...

- .035% potassium...

- .025% sulfur...

- .015% sodium

- .015% chlorine...

- .0004% iron...

- .00004% iodine...

- Fourteen to eighteen square feet of skin...

Total value? \$4.50 - \$5.00! Maybe this explains why abortion is so
accepted. According to this worlds system an individual is not worth
much but according to God there is nothing more valuable than a person!

As a Church we must reach a point where the importance of an individual
is non-negotiable! We must guard against an "easy come, easy go,"
attitude with regard to individuals. As a Pastor, I have seen a time
when we had to subtract before we could multiply. In such cases I always
find myself wondering if something else could have been done. (Back door
revival)

Nowhere is the priority of the individual more vivid than in the life
and teachings of Christ Himself!

SALVATION IS AN INDIVIDUAL EXPERIENCE...

2 Peter 3:9 "The Lord is not slow in keeping his promise, as some
understand slowness. He is patient with you, not wanting
[anyone]{.underline} to perish, but everyone to come to repentance."
Here Peter reveals that Jesus and the Father are motivated to see the
salvation of 20 billion people who have called planet Earth their home!
That huge crowd does not even include those who are not yet born!

God deems individuals so important that He has set in motion a Divine
plan which provides for every person who would ever enter the world both
past, present, and future to be saved. Rev. 3:20 "Here I am! I stand
at the door and knock. [If anyone]{.underline} hears my voice and opens
the door, I will come in and [eat with him]{.underline}, [and he with
me]{.underline}."

The salvation of the individual drove the entire redemptive plan;
nothing else has ever taken priority! "Go into all the world and
preach the Gospel to every creature." (Changing of the Guard --
"Orders Remain Unchanged!")

If the whole world gets saved today, they will get saved one at a time!

THE INDIVIDUAL NATURE OF GOD'S PLAN...

The individual nature of God's plan overwhelmed the psalmist David.
Psalm 8:3-4 "When I consider your heavens, the work of your fingers,
the moon and the stars, which you have set in place, [what is man that
you are mindful of him, the son of man that you care for
him]{.underline}? With His fingers He traced out courses for the
Rivers, yet he is focused on some of the most pragmatic issues of our
day.

The love of Jesus demonstrates the individual is more important than
rivers, deserts, mountains, valleys etc....

Jesus' teaching communicates the centrality of the individual but his
life and ministry made this truth even clearer:

- Zacchaeus...

- The Samaritan woman...

- Legion... (We are so group oriented that we focus more on how many
 devils were cast out then the fact that this man sat at the feet of
 Jesus clothed and in his right mind.)

- The thief on the cross...

CONCLUSION:

Notice our text, Luke 15:1-7 "Now the tax collectors and "sinners"
were all gathering around to hear him. But the Pharisees and the
teachers of the law muttered, "This man welcomes sinners and eats with
them." Then Jesus told them this parable: "Suppose one of you has a
hundred sheep and [loses one of them.]{.underline} Does he not [leave
the ninety-nine in the open country and go after the lost sheep until he
finds it?]{.underline} And when he finds it, he [joyfully puts it on his
shoulders and goes home. Then he says, 'Rejoice with me; I have found my
lost sheep.']{.underline} I tell you that in the same way there will be
more rejoicing in heaven over one sinner who repents than over
ninety-nine righteous persons who do not need to repent."

Notice four very important things here:

- The shepherd who owns 100 sheep has lost one! (Probably not the first
 offense)

- The drastic measures to find one lone sheep! "He leaves 99 in the
 open country and searches until he finds the one lost sheep!" Two
 legs & a piece of ear... Take it to the sale barn... Shear it whether
 needed or not... No sale barn or lamb chops...

- The celebration! He puts it on his shoulders; he brings it home; he
 calls his friends and neighbors together and invites them to
 participate in the homecoming celebration!

<!-- -->

- The most astounding thing about all of this is the words of Jesus in
 verse 7, "I tell you that in the same way there will be more
 rejoicing in heaven over [one sinner who repents]{.underline} than
 over ninety-nine righteous persons who do not need to repent." (How
 many sinners? One sinner)
