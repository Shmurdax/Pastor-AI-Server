# BREAKING THE POWER OF LACK

TEXT: 3 John 1:2 (NKJV) "Beloved, I pray that you may prosper in
all things and be in health, just as your soul prospers."

INTRO: Over the next few weeks we are going to look into the word of
God and determine how to break the power of lack.

Anyone ever experienced lack?

For those of you that haven't please allow me to define it for you, so
you will know what we are talking about. According to Dictionary.com,
lack is, "The state of being without or not having enough of
something."

Lack is something that the devil introduced into the world, it did not
come from the Lord. When God placed Adam in the garden, he gave him
everything he needed and even gave him dominion. Anytime we experience
lack or see it in the world, we can rest assured, the devil is doing
that work. The devil is a lie!

I want to set the record straight today -- The poorest person in this
room is wealthy according to world standards.

According to Globalissues.org "Almost half the world --- over three
billion people --- live on less than \$2.50 a day. At least 80% of
humanity lives on less than \$10 a day. More than 80 percent of the
world\'s population lives in countries where income differentials are
widening."

According to Pew Research "By 2050 four out of ten Christians will be
living in poverty."

Everywhere poverty is found, one or more of the following factors can be
found:

- Foolish leadership...

- Corrupt leadership...

- Lack of education...

- Tragic events as an initiator...

(Give my testimony as a child and teenager -- Give mine and Susan's
testimony as adults)

Trust me when I say, I know what it is to be in lack -- I also have
enjoyed having a little more than enough to get by. If I have to choose
between poverty and prosperity -- I choose prosperity! I know it doesn't
make me sound very spiritual but I can tell you that prosperity is far
more comfortable.

My upbringing caused me to be very determined to not live my entire life
in the vicious cycle of poverty. And I really do mean vicious.
Therefore, I have done everything I can to keep my family from
continuing on this path.

The word of God is very clear. 3 John 1:2 (NKJV) "Beloved, I pray
that you may prosper in all things and be in health, just as your soul
prospers."

Everything I know about God and His Word points me in the direction of
believing He wants us to live beyond the power of Lack.

In spite of this being true, the fact remains that many around this
world, and many in this very church are living in lack. Many who are
listening to me right now:

- Have more month than money...

- Have more debt than income...

- Have more commitments that ability to meet them...

I believe much of the problem is that many Christians operate under the
influence of poverty. How can we know whether or not we are being
influenced by a poverty mindset? Here are eight indicators of a mindset
of poverty:

- Mal-contentment... When a person who is operating in a poverty mindset
 gets a job, immediately they start looking for another job. When they
 get a house, immediately they start looking for another house. When
 they get a car, immediately they want a better car. When they buy a
 phone, immediately they start looking for another phone.

- Hording... Those who have a poverty mindset, can't turn loose of
 anything because they are afraid they will never get anything to
 replace it. (Depression generation)

- Stinginess... A person who is struggling with a mindset of poverty, is
 stingy. They do not want to share -- They don't want to share
 anything. Why don't they want to share? Because there is an
 underground fear that they will not have enough for themselves.

- Worry... Those who battle a poverty mindset, worry about everything.
 They worry that they do not have anything to worry about. When they
 have more than enough, they worry that it will not last.

- Materialism... Those who battle a poverty mindset, can never get
 enough of anything to make them happy. It is not "money" that is
 the root of all evil, it is "the love of money" that is the root
 of all evil. You can have money without loving it, and you can love it
 without having it. I personally know more people who love it without
 having it, than I do those who have it and love it. (This may be due
 to the circles I run in)

- Bragging and Showing off... Those who struggle with a mindset of
 poverty tend to embellish everything. They brag on and strut
 everything that comes their way. Their identity is tied up in the
 things they have. "Our lives do not consist in the things that we
 have."

- Begging and borrowing... Those who have a poverty mentality know how
 to game the system and would rather have an entitlement than a job.
 Let me remind us, the first step to slavery is a handout. When a
 poverty mindset is in motion those who are affected by it fail to pay
 their rent, their car payment, electric bill, etc... And when
 something is taken away they become the victim. (People who strip
 houses when they fail to pay the rent or mortgage, and they are
 evicted or foreclosed on -- Not making our payments is not the fault
 of the landlord or mortgage company -- When folks do this kind of
 thing, they further complicate their lives -- They have to go to court
 etc...)

- Envy and covetousness... When a poverty mentality is being employed
 the individual is incessantly envious of the success of others. The
 Bible tells us to "mourn with those who mourn" but it also tells
 us to "rejoice with those who rejoice." If we are going to break
 the power of lack, we must learn to celebrate with the have's - and
 reach to the have not's. Rather than becoming envious of the blessings
 of others we should get close enough to them to see how they
 positioned themselves to be in the pathway of the blessing. Envy and
 covetousness blinds the less fortunate to the principles of success of
 the more fortunate.

I wanted to share these eight indicators so that we could self-assess
whether or not one or more of them apply to our lives. If one or more of
these indicators is roaming around in our lives, we must repent of it,
and set into motion some things that will counteract them. That's what
this series of messages is about! Over the next couple of weeks, I am
going to reveal four principles, that if followed, will most certainly
break the back of the power of lack!

TRUST

Tithing does not require trust and therefore does not necessarily bring
with it the blessing of multiplication. Tithing places the believer in a
position of releasing the Lord to rebuke the devourer for our sakes.
Tithing does not break the spirit of mammon on the money; the believer
should bind the spirit of mammon over their income and pronounce the
blessing of the Lord upon it.

First-fruit requires trust and enacts the blessing of multiplication
upon our resources. If I give the first of what I have believing for at
least nine more that requires trust -- Trust is a big deal to God)

A thing must be blessed before it can multiply! Jesus blessed the fish

KEY #2:

Only what is given away can multiply! The disciples were handling

Even though the food was blessed it had to be given away in order for it
to multiply. I am here to remind us today that only what we give away
can be multiplied! If we need a financial blessing we must find a way to

Some people say, "We use our tithe to give to needy people." "We
support other ministries with our tithe." "We give our tithe to
missions." The same people would never buy their groceries at Kroger
and go to price saver to pay for them. They wouldn't think of filling up
at Exxon and driving down to Valero to pay for their fuel. The Bible is
clear about this -- "Bring ALL the tithe into the STOREHOUSE." The
storehouse is the local church you attend and NOTHING else.
