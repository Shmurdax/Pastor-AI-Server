# FASTING & PRAYER

Today we are embarking upon a spiritual adventure together, a twenty-one
day fast! I hope you will follow along each day with Susan and I as we
share information on fasting and prayer. As we embark upon this journey
I want you to bear in mind that our theme for the year is freedom. We
are expecting God to bring freedom into every part of our family,
career, finances, and ministries. I do not believe we can expect to live
overcoming lives and expect freedom apart from the disciplines of
fasting and prayer.

Fasting and prayer are Scriptural principles. One hundred sixty times
the Bible references the word fasting or some variation of it. The word
fast means "one who has not eaten, one who is empty." Our word
"breakfast" simply means to break our time of fasting between the
evening meal and morning meal. Sad to say, but that is about as much
fasting as most Christians ever do.

People point to all kinds of excuses for not practicing the principle of
fasting:

- Fasting makes me sick...

- I am not physically strong enough to fast...

- My schedule will not allow for fasting...

- Fasting is the responsibility of church leaders...

None of these excuses are a good reason for neglecting the discipline of
fasting.

Prayer is communication between heaven and earth. The theme of our
journey together will be "Touching Heaven - Changing Earth" --
saints, if we actually touch Heaven with our prayers we will no doubt
become anointed to change earth!

I want to challenge us to view our time of prayer and fasting like an
airplane. An airplane has three components, two wings and a fuselage. I
want us to view the two wings as petition and praise, and the fuselage
as waiting in His presence to hear from God.

We are really good at the petition part but we often struggle with the
praise and waiting. Lets make the first wing praise and the second wing
petition. The reason I want us to put praise first is because the Bible
says "God lives in the praises of His people." God honors
gratitude. The leper that was made whole was the one who returned to
give Him praise. The other nine lepers were just healed of their
leprosy. If you are a leper healing is wonderful but being made whole is
AWESOSME! Wholesomeness flows out of gratitude. Please do not hesitate
to take your petitions to the Lord during this time but preface your
petition with praise.

After we have praised and made our petitions known to the Lord let us
not fail to reserve a period of waiting quietly in His presence to hear
from Him.

My prayer is that before we are finished with this journey together, you
will see such benefit in the spiritual disciplines of fasting and prayer
that you will be motivated to not only participate but have regular
times of fasting and prayer on your own throughout the year. Remember,
fasting and prayer is the most fruitful and productive things you can
do.

So on your mark? Get ready, Go!

PERIMETERS OF FASTING

Today I want to point out some perimeters for fasting. One should not go
on a radical or absolute fast if one has health problems that would be
worsened by abstaining from food or water.

If you are on medications, do not discontinue a medication without the
supervision of a doctor. Be aware that some medications must be taken
after consuming food and a beverage.

Once you have settled the issue of medication with your doctor you
should decide what kind of fast you are going to do and for how long.
This is probably a personal preference but I do not like to engage in a
fast until I know how long it is going to last. There are several
reasons for this:

- As soon as you initiate the fast the flesh and the enemy will be
 screaming at you, telling you that you have accomplished your goals
 and that you should begin eating again.

- Knowing how long you are going to fast from the inception will help
 you prepare yourself mentally for the journey.

- Having a set time of fasting will empower you to remain engaged
 spiritually throughout the appointed time.

- As you near the end of the fast, knowing the goal will give you faith
 that you can accomplish it.

Before beginning a fast of more than three days:

- Receive divine direction - Don\'t copy others; be sure you are led of
 the Lord as to what type of fast you are to do and how long it should
 last.

- No caffeine -- Discontinue the use of caffeine a few days before the
 fast.

- Restrict your food intake several days before the fast - Eat fruit as
 a last meal before the fast.

During the fast:

- Dress warmly - You can expect to feel colder during a fast, especially
 in your hands and feet.

- Use warm, not hot, water for showers and baths.

- Avoid ice cold beverages.

- Give extra time to prayer and Bible reading.

I challenge you to read your Bible aloud during this time. The Bible
say's "Faith comes by HEARING..." As you read your Bible and wait
in the presence of the Lord to hear from Him, bear in mind He wants to
free you and your family from every bondage.

FASTING IN THE OLD TESTAMENT

&

THE NEW TESTAMENT

In Judaism the Day of Atonement was a time of complete fasting, this
meant no food or water for twenty-four hours. This was the only
prescribed time of fasting and it was intended to be a day of national
repentance. During the Day of Atonement the participants were to refrain
from all labor. If one decided not to fast on this particular day the
punishment was death.

The Old Testament employs the word "[afflict]{.underline}" when
describing the discipline of fasting. When we read that a person
"afflicted, sanctified or consecrated themselves," the Scripture is
simply saying that they fasted.

Fasting in the Old Testament:

- Pastor Moses fasted -- (Fasted 40 days twice -- The result; the ten
 commandments)

- The Children of Israel fasted -- Judges 20, 1 Samuel 7, 1 Samuel 31, 2
 Samuel 1

- King David fasted -- 2 Samuel 12, Psalms 69, Psalms 109

- King Ahab fasted -- 1 Kings 12

- The servants of Saul fasted -- 1 Chronicles 10

- The prophet Ezra fasted -- Ezra 8&9

- Nehemiah fasted -- Nehemiah 1 (He fasted until God sent him to
 Jerusalem to rebuild the broken city)

- Mordecai fasted -- Esther 4 (Fasted for an intercessor)

- Zechariah and the priests fasted -- Zechariah 7

- The prophet Daniel fasted -- (Fasted 21 days before receiving an
 answer and visions) Daniel 9&10

- The prophet Joel fasted -- Joel 2

History tells us that during the time between the Old Testament and the
New Testament fasting became a Jewish characteristic or mark.

Fasting in the New Testament:

- The Pharisees fasted

- Jesus fasted -- Matthew 4, Mark 2, Mark 9 (This time of fasting
 prepared Him for public ministry)

- Anna fasted -- Luke 2

- Cornelius fasted -- Acts 10 (The Lord sent Peter to present the gift
 of the Holy Spirit to the gentile world)

- The Apostles fasted -- Acts 13 (Paul and Barnabas are selected as the
 first missionaries of the New Testament church)

- Paul fasted -- 2 Corinthians 6, 2 Corinthians 11

Fasting is both an Old Testament and New Testament principle and
activity. If fasting is a New Testament principle it is certainly worthy
of the consideration of every Christian. It is my prayer that during
this time of fasting and prayer God will reveal Himself to you in a new
and fresh way. If this occurs, I believe we will experience freedom as
never before!

TYPES OF FASTING

Let\'s take a look at the different types of fasting in the Bible. I
don\'t want you to feel intimidated or overwhelmed by the thought of
going without food for days on end. There are types of fasting that
don\'t involve such a radical commitment. The Bible gives examples of
many different kinds of fasting. The terms I will use to describe the
different types of fasts are not biblical terms they are terms I have
chosen. Let's look at them:

- Radical fast -- This is a fast that includes neither food nor water.
 This is the type of fast that Jesus and Moses went on. This type of
 fast can be risky to ones health. One should make sure God is telling
 one to embark upon such an odyssey. I would not recommend this fast
 for more than a maximum of three days and then only with a Doctors
 approval if you are under a doctors care.

- Absolute fast -- This is when we consume no food at all and drink only
 water. Jonathan did this in 1 Samuel 20:34. When embarking upon an
 absolute fast one should make sure God is calling one to do so and one
 should know how long the fast is to last before it begins.

- Partial fast -- In this fast one is to refrain from consuming certain
 foods. Maybe cut out meet, maybe desert. This is a fast that can go on
 indefinitely. Daniel, Shadrach, Meshach, Abednego fasted in this way;
 they refused to eat the Kings meat, bread, or deserts. Listen to the
 results; Daniel 1:15 (NKJV) "And at the end of ten days their
 features appeared [better and fatter in flesh than all the young men
 who ate the portion of the king's delicacies]{.underline}." Those
 who have health problems that would restrict them from a radical fast
 or an absolute fast can often do this type of fast.

- Intimate fast -- This type of fast can be entered into by couples who
 decide to set aside the pleasures of the flesh to seek the Lord for a
 specific time period. This should only be done with the consent of
 both marriage partners. 1 Corinthians 7:5

- Media fast -- I can find no scripture for this one but many people go
 on a media fast. In this culture we are inundated with downloading
 information from every direction imaginable. This type of fast
 refrains from any type of media exposure for a certain time period. In
 fact, if the purpose of fasting is to place ourselves in a position of
 hearing form God, I would suggest that if we are going to engage in
 any kind of fast, it should also include a media fast.

No matter what kind of fast you have decided to engage in, you should
set aside a time to listen quietly for the voice of God. In hearing from
God we will no doubt begin to see things that have had us bound, that we
never suspected were a problem before. I challenge us to deal with each
restriction as He identifies it. In so doing, we will continue on a path
toward complete freedom in Christ.

HOW TO HEAR FROM GOD

If you have been following the daily devotionals during this time of
fasting and prayer you will remember me saying that fasting does not
convince God to do anything that He is not already willing to do, it
places us in a position of being able to hear from God.

In the Old Testament, Daniel fasted and prayed for twenty-one days. Here
is an interesting thing, at the end of twenty-one days the Lord spoke to
Daniel and said, "I heard you the first time you prayed!"
Seriously? Twenty-one days of fasting and prayer and Daniel discovered
that God had heard his prayer the first time? How frustrating would that
be? I would suggest, this is true every time! The Bible say's "The
ear of the Lord is attuned to the cry of the righteous." It also says
"The eyes of the Lord are searching the world for someone to whom He
might show Himself strong." That doesn't sound like God is playing
hide-and-seek with us, it sounds like He is attempting to get His
answers to us.

The problem that Daniel experienced is the same problem that we all
face. God revealed to Daniel that the issue was not getting his prayers
heard; the problem was receiving the answer. The Word revealed that
"The prince of the power of Persia" had hindered the answer for
twenty-one days! In other words it took Daniel twenty-one day's of
fasting and prayer to position himself to receive the answer.

I do not want to come across as sacrilegious here but it appears to me
that the prince of the power of Persia was closer to God than Daniel
was. If you do not agree with me you will have to explain how the prince
was running interference between Daniel and the answer, if he was not
somewhere between God and Daniel. The cares of this world often drown
out the voice of God. We have so many voices screaming into our ears
that though we are praying and reading our Bibles, everything else seems
to be much more vivid than what we are hearing from God.

This is where setting aside time for fasting and prayer comes into play.
It is during this time that we push back our plate and choose by an act
of our own will to silence all other voices in lieu of hearing from God.

Remember, fasting does not convince God to act in our behalf; it places
us in a position of being better able to hear His voice and receive His
answers.

I am anxious to see what God say's to us during this journey of fasting
and prayer. His words will no doubt be words of freedom. In fact, the
Word said, "You shall know the truth and the truth shall make you
free."

ENDING A FAST

Ending a fast in a proper manner, especially if you have been on a
radical or absolute fast is very, very important. This is true from a
physical as well as a spiritual perspective and I want to address both.

After the fast:

- Avoid starches for a few days.

- Chew your food well.

- Stop eating as soon as you feel full.

- Don\'t become physically active too soon.

- Eat healthy food.

Here is a suggested schedule for reintroducing food after a radical or
absolute fast:

- DAY 1: Drink liquids every couple of hours in small amounts. Mix water
 with fruit juices.

- DAY 2: Eat chicken noodle soup or something similar. Yogurt in small
 quantities will be fine.

- DAY 3 & 4: Salads, soups without grease and cooked vegetables.

After a few days you can begin to resume a normal diet of food and
exercise. The last thing you should begin consuming is fish and meats.

From a spiritual perspective the best time to hear from the Lord is the
time following a radical or absolute fast. Be must be careful not to
reengage in behaviors that distract us from our quiet time with the
Lord; praying, reading His Word, and taking time to listen intently for
His voice. If we handle this time appropriately, we will hear from Him
as we never have before.

I think most of you will agree that ninety percent of the battle is
getting ourselves in a place to hear from God. If we know that we know
that we know that we have heard His voice, following His directives
become much easier for us to do.

Through fasting and prayer we have changed our position from being
hearing challenged, to being able to hear from God. Let us be very
careful about reintroducing disciplines that caused our hearing problems
to begin with. Let us walk in the freedom that He is offering today.
"He that the Son set's free is free indeed."

CAN YOU HEAR WHAT GOD IS SAYING?

Now that we are concluding our time of fasting we should be in a better
position to hear from the Lord. Surprisingly enough, I struggle during a
fast to hear anything from the Lord. It often seems that Heaven is
silent as I fast (Die) and pray. I know we call it fasting but it feels
an awful lot like dying!

I have heard people tell awesome stories of what God told them during a
fast and I am not taking away from that, but my experience has been that
God is strangely silent as I grovel with the discipline of fasting. Here
is the exciting part! Once I conclude a fast it seems that God is
speaking form every direction!

For a number of years I thought myself to be somewhat of an odd ball in
this area, until I read the account of Jesus' testing in the wilderness.
John the Baptist, baptized Jesus in the Jordan River, the Holy Spirit
descended in the form of a dove, God spoke from Heaven, and John the
Baptist declared Jesus to be the Messiah. You would think Jesus would
ride the momentum of the moment into a very public ministry and strike
while the iron was hot, but look at what really happened. The Bible said
"He was led by the Spirit into the wilderness to be tested by the
devil for forty days." Seriously? Really? Wow! What a turn of events!

If you study the account of Jesus' forty days of fasting you will notice
that the Bible does not record that God said one word during this time.
Conversely it reveals that during this time Jesus had at least three
face-to-face confrontations with Lucifer himself! Each time the enemy
attempted to convince Jesus to lay down His divinity and pick up His
humanity. During this ordeal Jesus never once relied on His flesh to
overcome the enemy. He took three scriptures from the Old Testament and
whipped the devil all over the desert!

Following the forty day fast, the Word declares, "Afterward, the
devil left Him alone for a season." Here is what we are being told
through the experience of Jesus:

- The forty days of fasting and solitude was spent crucifying the flesh.

- The time in the desert was a wrestling match between hell, earth, and
 heaven.

- The victory was not won in heaven or hell; it was won in the desert.

- After the testing Jesus was in such alignment with the Father that the
 devil was afraid to challenge Him.

- It was following this tremendous challenge that miracles, signs, and
 wonders began to be revealed through the earthly ministry of Jesus.

What lessons should we take from this experience of Jesus?

- We should not think it a strange thing if we find our time of solitude
 to be an uphill battle.

- During the battle we cannot rely on the flesh to win the battle, we
 must rely on His word to win the day.

- We should expect to hear vividly from God following our time of
 fasting and prayer.

- We should expect to see God act on our behalf going forward.

Listen! Can you hear what God is saying? He is saying, get ready! Get
ready! Get ready! Freedom is on its way into our lives and ministries.

PRAYER THAT PRODUCES FREEDOM

Take a look around you; people are grappling with addictions and phobias
of all kinds. Sadly, the bondage of believers often mirrors that of the
world. This should not be the case in the life of Christians. We are
Christ followers and He paid the price for our freedom at Calvary, all
we have to do is walk in the freedom He offers.

The bondages that we experience and allow can and will be broken through
with the power of prayer. Psalms 34 (NIT) lays out the freedom God
affords through prayer. In this passage we are told that the Lord will
do the following things in response to the prayer of the believer:

- Deliver us from fear (Vs.4)

- Save us from our trouble (Vs. 6)

- Guard and deliver us (Vs. 7)

- Be good to those who trust Him (Vs. 8)

- Supply all needs (Vs. 9)

- Hear us when we pray (Vs. 15)

- Redeem us (Vs 22)

Wow! All of the aforementioned things belong to every Christ follower. I
hope we will take advantage of what Christ purchased and step into the
freedom He purchased for us.

We are to receive these things through:

- Crying out to Him (Vs. 6, 17)

- Trusting Him (Vs. 8)

- Fearing Him (Vs. 7, 9)

- Refraining from lying (Vs. 13)

- Humbling ourselves (Vs. 18)

- Serving Him (Vs. 18)

If we walk according to His will and Word we will find ourselves in a
place of freedom that we have never experienced before. "You shall
know the truth and the truth shall make you free."

God promises great blessings to His people, but many of these blessings
require our active participation. In this instance our participation is
a consistent prayer life. Prayer is a requirement of living an
overcoming Christian life. If we hope to live free, we must be people of
prayer. My prayer for you and for CT is that we will come into a place
of freedom this year that we can see Him for who He is, and we can
receive from Him all that He has promised.
