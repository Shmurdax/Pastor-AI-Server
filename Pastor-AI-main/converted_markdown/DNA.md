<https://hbr.org/2008/07/unmasking-manly-men>

A 2008 article in Harvard Business review titled, "Unmasking Manly
Men," chronicled the results of an 18-month study of culture and
productivity on oil rigs and took a look at a decade-and-a-half of one
company's production.

Offshore oil rigs are a macho environment.

In summary, the findings were that things dramatically improved when the
men on those rigs were trained and began to focus on the mission and
placing team safety first, rather than proving themselves to be macho
(self) ...

"Over the 15-year period these changes in work practices, norms,
perceptions, and behaviors were implemented company-wide. The company's
accident rate declined by 84%, while productivity (number of barrels
produced), efficiency (cost per barrel), and reliability (production
"up" time) increased beyond the industry's previous benchmark."

When a group of people, especially the church, become united in vision

THE MINISTRY OF THE CHURCH

In order for ministry to be carried out in an effective and Christ
honoring fashion, God places leaders over us so that He can give vision
to them, and they can give us our marching orders.

Chain of command in the ministry:

- Jesus is the head of the church...

- The pastor is the human authority in the church...

- The pastor doesn't account he deacons the deacons account to the
 pastor... (The deacons are to implement the vision of the pastor)

- The people submit themselves to the pastor for the purpose of being
 trained...

- The people carry out the work of the ministry... (Sheep beget sheep)

These leaders are not responsible to do the ministry, they are assigned
the responsibility of training God's people to do the ministry.
