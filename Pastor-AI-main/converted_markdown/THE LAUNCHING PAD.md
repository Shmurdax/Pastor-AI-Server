## II. LAUNCH PAD...

A launch pad is the area and facilities where rockets or spacecraft lift
off. A [spaceport](http://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spaceport) (or rocket
launch site) can contain one or many launch pads.

## III. PURPOSE OF A LAUNCH PAD...

A typical launch pad consists of the service and umbilical structures.
The service structure provides an access platform to inspect the launch
vehicle prior to launch.

What are we to be launching? We are here to launch people and
ministries.

Most service structures can be moved or rotated to a safe distance. We
are to embrace people rather than hold them at a distance. Some will no
doubt blow up on us but that should not keep us from continuing to
embrace people and ministries.

## IV. FUEL AND COMMUNICATION...

The umbilical structure has propellant loading, gas, power, and
communication links to the launch vehicle.

We have been called to train and fuel people and ministries. this can be
grueling, time consuming, and expensive but when launch takes place it
will be worth it.

If NASA had never sent a vehicle into space we would not have:

- GPS...

- Computers...

- The internet...

- Smart phones...

- Satellite television...

- Knowledge that makes our life better...

## V. PROTECTION...

The launch vehicle sits atop the launch platform, which contains a flame
deflection structure to withstand the intense heat and forces generated
by rocket engines during liftoff.

Launching people and ministries is expensive and dangerous but I believe
God is committed to shielding and protecting people who dare to work
toward liftoff.
