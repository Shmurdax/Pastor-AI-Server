# IT IS TIME FOR RENEWAL

TEXT: 2 Chronicles 33:9-13 (NKJV) "Manasseh seduced Judah and the
inhabitants of Jerusalem to do more evil than the nations whom
the Lord had destroyed before the children of Israel. ^10 ^And
the Lord spoke to Manasseh and his people, but they would
not listen. ^11 ^Therefore the Lord brought upon them the captains of
the army of the king of Assyria, who took Manasseh with hooks, bound him
with bronze fetters, and carried him off to Babylon. ^12 ^Now when he
was in affliction, he implored the Lord his God, and humbled himself
greatly before the God of his fathers, ^13 ^and prayed to Him; and
He received his entreaty, heard his supplication, and brought him back
to Jerusalem into his kingdom. Then Manasseh knew that the Lord
was God."

INTRO: America needs renewal... CT needs renewal... ALL of us need
renewal! In just a few weeks we will host the Empowerment Conference,
which is our ministers conference and annual church revival. In an
effort to get us ready to be renewed and revived during that time, over
the next few weeks we are going to talk about five types of renewal:

- Personal renewal...

- Relational renewal...

- Missional renewal...

- Cultural renewal...

- Structural renewal...

As a basis for our study concerning renewal, I would like to use the Old
Testament character of Manasseh, which can be found in 2 Chronicles.

Manasseh, the son of Hezekiah, was the thirteenth king of the Southern
Kingdom.

Not only was his 55-year-reign, the longest of any king in the history
of the Israelites, but he also had the distinction of being the worst
king that Judah ever had. During his reign he reversed every good thing
his father Hezekiah had done. He not only took the nation in a bad
direction; he took the nation much further into idolatry than it was
when his father began to reign decades earlier.

God became so incensed with Manasseh and his godless reign and
lifestyle, that he allowed Manasseh to be captured and carried off to
Babylon where he was persecuted. While in Babylon, Manasseh repented
before the Lord and the Lord brought him out of captivity.

Manasseh experienced personal renewal and as a result, Israel
experienced renewal as well. I want to point out that repentance is the
common denominator of all renewal. America needs renewal -- Renewal will
only come if there is true repentance. 2 Chronicles 7:14 (NKJV) "If
My people who are called by My name will humble themselves, and pray and
seek My face, and turn from their wicked ways, then I will hear from
heaven, and will forgive their sin and heal their land."

Though few, if any, would have expected it, Manasseh changed in a good
way. (Susan has a saying -- "People can change -- But not much." 2
Chronicles 33:12 "In his distress he sought the favor of the Lord his
God and humbled himself greatly before the God of his ancestors. And
when he prayed to him. The Lord was moved by his entreaty and listened
to his plea; so he brought him back to Jerusalem and to his kingdom.
Then Manasseh knew that the Lord is God."

Manasseh then proceeded to remove idolatry and lead a spiritual renewal
by making positive changes:

- He repented... (This is always the first step to renewal -- Repentance
 is not just saying, "I am sorry", it is being sorry with the
 corresponding action of real-life change)

- He humbled himself... (Humility is an essential part of renewal --
 "God resists the proud but gives grace to the humble.")

- He prayed... (When we stop praying - We start sinning -- When we start
 praying - We stop sinning)

- He tore down pagan altars... (Renewal causes us to devalue what we
 once valued -And value what we once devalued)

- He repaired the altar of the Lord... (The altar is always important to
 those who are seeking renewal \Elijah did this on Mt. Carmel)

- He offered peace offerings to the Lord... (We can't offer a peace
 offering to the Lord if we continue doing the things that offend Him)

- He offered thank offerings to the Lord... (Worship becomes extremely
 important to those who are seeking renewal)

- He commanded the people to serve Jehovah exclusively... (Those who are
 serious about renewal will not only put God in first place - They will
 not permit any other God's in their orbit)

Manasseh's renewal began as does all renewal... With...

PERSONAL RENEWAL

While in Babylon, in the hands of his persecutors Manasseh turned his
heart to the Lord. All personal renewal starts with the heart.

If we need a personal renewal, that means we need to fall in love with
Jesus again. When we do that --- all of a sudden, it's not about
religion and rituals; it's about a relationship with Jesus.

Those who fall in love with Jesus and experience renewal will be
characterized by:

- Powerful living... (Spirit-filled living -- "If we walk in the
 Spirit we will not fulfill the lusts of the flesh." -- If our
 personal life is suspect that is a pretty good sign that we need
 renewal)

- Private prayer... (Praying people are Godly people -- Do you have a
 prayer life? What time do you pray? If you can't tell me what time you
 pray I'm going to say you don't have a prayer life -- It is absolutely
 certain that if we don't have a prayer life, renewal is on the menu
 for us)

- Personal Bible study... (Do you have a systematic pattern of Bible
 study? Do you have family devotions? If we are not in the Word every
 day, we are certainly weak in our walk, which means we need renewal --
 Walk Through The Word -- Patreon)

- Public worship... (Does every little thing keep us from public
 worship? If so, we need renewal -- "Forsake not the assembling of
 yourselves together, as the manner of some is, and so much more as you
 see the day approaching.")

- Plentiful giving... (Are we stingy or generous? This is a sure test
 for the need for renewal -- Generosity vs. Compliance)

- Purposeful witnessing... (Are we telling anyone about Jesus? If not,
 we need renewal -- Renewal will set us to talking about Jesus again)

- Praise-filled joy... (Renewed people are joyful people -- Those who
 need renewal are sad sacks)

Personal renewal changes everything! Just as a baby changes everything,
renewal changes everything. When Manasseh experienced personal renewal,
it affected every part of his life, both personal and professional.

Manasseh received from His father a nation that served the Lord and
worked together. Over his 55-year-reign he managed to destroy everything
his father had built, and surrendered his kingdom to the Assyrians.

In Babylon God turned his heart and he had relational renewal so that
God was able to place him back on the throne of Judah.

We need relational renewal -- relational renewal brings us together. If
we would get together, we could become the answer to Jesus' one and only
prayer request -- "Father, I pray that You would make them one."
The reason Jesus was so intent on us becoming one is that more can be
done in a group, than everyone can do singlehandedly. Leviticus 26:8
"Five of you shall chase a hundred, and a hundred of you shall chase
ten thousand." If one extrapolates the effect created by "Five
chasing a hundred" it stands to reason that one hundred can chase two
thousand. Yet the Bible says "A hundred of you can put ten thousand
to flight!"

Because of the synergy created by fighting together, the ability of a
person to produce, rises from a ratio of one-to-twenty, to one-to-one
hundred! We are better together than we are apart! We need relational
renewal!

Missional renewal is when we rediscover what God wants us to do. When
missional renewal occurs, we give up on our ideas and embrace His ideas.
It is at this point that we realize, we are a part of something bigger
than any one of us could accomplish solo.

While Manasseh was in Babylon, he experienced missional renewal.
Pre-Babylon Manasseh had a very secular worldview. Post-Babylon he had a
God worldview. When he had a missional renewal he made some major
reversals:

- He followed a different God...

- He governed differently...

- He exercised justice differently...

- The results of his reign were different...

When we experience missional renewal, we begin to realize we have a
kingdom assignment. John Wesley said, "Give me 100 men who fear nothing
but sin and desire nothing but God, and I will shake the world."

I think there is a mammoth knowledge gap in the church. We seem to think
we are here to serve our families and ourselves, but God has placed us
here to accomplish a Kingdom purpose. In accomplishing the Kingdom
purpose, we take care of ourselves and our families, but the scope of
what we do becomes broadened into a Kingdom centered effort.

When we embrace missional renewal everything changes:

- We go from living self-centered lives; to having a biblical
 worldview...

- We go from thinking first of self; to thinking of how we can have an
 impact on culture...

- We go from prioritizing everything on how our futures will be
 affected; to considering the eternal impact...

- We go from laying up an overabundance of treasure on temporal things;
 to making Kingdom investments...

What is your missional purpose?

Do you have one?

If not, are you willing to consider a missional renewal?
