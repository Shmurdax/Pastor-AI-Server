# THE PURPOSE OF THE CHURCH

TEXT: Luke 19:45-48 (NKJV) "Then He went into the temple and
began to drive out those who bought and sold in it,46 saying to them,
"It is written, 'My house is a house of prayer,' but you have made it a
'den of thieves.' "47 And He was teaching daily in the temple. But the
chief priests, the scribes, and the leaders of the people sought to
destroy Him,48 and were unable to do anything; for all the people were
very attentive to hear Him."

In this passage of Scripture we are introduced to a personality trait of
Jesus which is rarely seen in Scripture. As He enters the synagogue
Jesus is confronted with a group of people who are making their living
selling wares of worship. The Scripture makes us aware that Jesus was a
regular at the Synagogue. Luke 4:14-16 (NKJV) "Then Jesus returned in
the power of the Spirit to Galilee, and news of Him went out through all
the surrounding region.15 And [He taught in their
synagogues]{.underline}, being glorified by all. 16 So He came to
Nazareth, where He had been brought up. And [as His custom was, He went
into the synagogue on the Sabbath day]{.underline}, and stood up to
read." Jesus had a habit of church attendance and if we desire to be
like Him we should develop a similar habit.

The point here is that Jesus has no doubt been to this same synagogue on
many occasions and witnessed the abuse of worshippers, but this time is
different. We are allowed to peer just momentarily into the side of the
personality of Jesus which allows Him to become angry enough to drive
the money changers from the place of worship. (If we do not accept
mercy, we will eventually get judgment)

When He drives the money changers from the place of worship He makes a
statement which we seldom hear but would do well to study if we hope to
be the kind of church Jesus would be impressed with. At CT we are not
seeking the accolades of men but the approval of the heavenly Father.

I want us to look at the statements of Jesus in this passage of
Scripture and make an attempt to discover how it might look in this
modern time.

The word translated "Prayer" here comes from the Greek word
"Proseuche" (pros-yoo-khay') and means "A place set apart or
suited for the offering of prayer and worship." If a synagogue was not
accessible and the Jews prayed and worshipped in the countryside the
chosen place for prayer and worship would always be selected near a
creek or stream because they would wash themselves prior to prayer and
worship. This act was an act of purifying themselves to come into the
presence of Jehovah.

The word translated "Thieves" here comes from the Greek word
"Lestes" (lace-tace') and means "A place which has been plundered
or taken advantage of."

Jesus is saying the church will either be a "Den of thieves" or a
"House of prayer." I want us to take a look at what a "Den of
thieves" or a "House of prayer" might look like in 2007...
