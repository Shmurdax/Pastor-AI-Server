INTRO: Over the next 5 weeks we will attempt to cover the entire book of
James. This New Testament book is a powerful testimony and challenge to
live the Faith life. James is the author of this book and he refers to
himself as a "servant of God and of the Lord Jesus Christ."

We know him as the half brother of Jesus. Mark 6:3 (NKJV) "Is this not
the carpenter, the Son of Mary, and [brother of James]{.underline},
Joses, Judas, and Simon? And are not His sisters here with us? And
they were offended at Him." It is interesting to me that he chooses the
identity of "servant" rather than brother. James is certainly not a
person who drops names to impress his friends.

We know that James was a leader in the early church of Jerusalem. James
was an eyewitness of the resurrection. 1 Corinthians 15:7 (NKJV) "After
that [He was seen by James]{.underline}, then by all the apostles."

The book of James is written to the twelve tribes of Israel. James 1:1
(NKJV) "James, a bondservant of God and of the Lord Jesus Christ, [To
the twelve tribes which are scattered abroad]{.underline}:" This
writing is to churches which consisted mostly of Jewish people who had
been scattered among Gentile nations. So the book of James was a letter
to these people and takes on the qualities of Old Testament wisdom
literature. It deals with a variety of subjects which describe how to
live an upright life. It has much in common with the Sermon on the
Mount, taking on subjects like oaths, the tongue, and a challenge to be
peacemakers.

"wait (weight) is what broke the bridge down."

the malignant forces which would come against our faith.

> PRAYER MIXED WITH FAITH ALWAYS RESULTS IN MIRACLES...

James 5:17-18 (NKJV) "Elijah was a man with a nature like ours, and he
prayed earnestly that it would not rain; and it did not rain on the land
for three years and six months.18 And he prayed again, and the heaven
gave rain, and the earth produced its fruit." I think it very
interesting the way James chooses to tackle the issue of prayer.

He chooses to cite the example of the Old Testament prophet Elijah. He
states very simply that "Elijah prayed that it would not rain, and [it
rained not for the space of three years and six months]{.underline}." I
am sure Elijah's prayers were mixed with faith to get such extreme
results.

A lesson we learn in Faith 101 is that prayer mixed with faith moves the
hand of the Lord.

He simply says "And [he prayed again]{.underline} and the heavens gave
forth the rain, and the earth produced its fruit." I am quite sure that
Elijah did not wait three and one half years to pray again, but once God
shut the water faucet off, Elijah did not pray for rain for three and
one half years. The very next time he prayed he got results!

> James says that Elijah was not a special case, he is just like us. If
> God will respond to Elijah's prayers He will surely respond to ours!

> FAITH, THE ANSWER TO SIN AND FAILURE...

James 5:19-20 (NKJV) "Brethren, if anyone among you [wanders from the
truth]{.underline}, and someone turns him back,20 let him know that [he
who turns a sinner from the error of his way will save a soul from death
and cover a multitude of sins]{.underline}."

Everyone is susceptible to sin and failure. James lets us know that when
this occurs, faith in God and His Word will result in the guilty being
forgiven.

The greatest miracle is when a sin is forgiven. The greatest lesson of
Faith 101 is that faith is the answer to all sin and failure.
