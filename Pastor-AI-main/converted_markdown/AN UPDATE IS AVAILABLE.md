INTRO: (Smart phone vs. dumb phone)

I have an I-Phone 5 and I regularly receive notices of updates being
available. An update is a new version of software that you already have
available on your phone or computer.

Apple releases a stream of free software updates to enrich your
computing experience. Mac automatically checks weekly for software
updates provided you have an internet connection. If you do not have a
continuous internet connection, you can manually check for updates when
you are able to connect to the internet.

If Software Update finds an item to download, it appears in a list. So
before choosing to download, you can click on an item to get a
description.

If you see updates that you will never use, such as a language you don't
speak or drivers for a printer you don't own, you can simply choose
"Make Inactive" from the update menu and no one will force you to update
any system.

Most people, who call themselves Christian, believe in the person of the
Holy Spirit, at least in some limited dimension. The extent of these
beliefs has made for many interesting conversations as well as quite a
large number of heated religious debates.

Those who claim to have received the infilling of the Holy Spirit
following conversion, are widely known as Pentecostal or Charismatic.

To begin with, I want to point out what I believe to be several general
Scriptural facts concerning the person of the Holy Spirit: (PLACE ON
THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The Holy Spirit is the third person of the Holy trinity

- The Holy Spirit works in unity with the Father and the Son

- The Holy Spirit is the power behind the implementation of God's
 purpose in the earth

In order to fully understand the subject matter I want to attempt to
answer the following questions:

- Does a believer receive the Holy Spirit at conversion? Acts 19:1-7

- To receive the Holy Spirit, is it necessary to speak in tongues? To
 adequately answer this question, I want us to look at each time groups
 of people or individuals received the Holy Spirit in the book of Acts:

1) Upper Room - Acts 2:1-4 (KJV) "And when the day of Pentecost was
 fully come, they were all with one accord in one place. 2 And
 suddenly there came a sound from heaven as of a rushing mighty wind,
 and it filled all the house where they were sitting. 3 And there
 appeared unto them cloven tongues like as of fire, and it sat upon
 each of them. 4 And they were all filled with the Holy Ghost, and
 began to [speak with other tongues, as the Spirit gave them
 utterance]{.underline}." We must conclude that in this initial
 outpouring, each of the 120 did in fact speak in tongues.

2) Peter and John at Samaria - Acts 8:14-18 (KJV) "Now when the
 apostles which were at Jerusalem heard that Samaria had received the
 word of God, they sent unto them Peter and John: 15 Who, when they
 were come down, prayed for them, that they might receive the Holy
 Ghost: 16 (For as yet he was fallen upon none of them: only they

 This Scripture does not say that they spoke in tongues, but there
 was some kind of visible sign of the outpouring of the Spirit,
 because even Simon recognized what had happened.

3) Saul/Paul receives the Holy Spirit - Acts 9:17-18 (KJV) "And
 Ananias went his way, and entered into the house; and putting his

Why was tongues chosen as the initial physical evidence of Holy Spirit
Baptism? The tongue is the most unruly member of the body. James 3:6-8
(NKJV) "And [the tongue is a fire, a world of iniquity]{.underline}.
The tongue is so set among our members that [it defiles the whole
body]{.underline}, and [sets on fire the course of nature]{.underline};
and [it is set on fire by hell]{.underline}.7 For every kind of beast
and bird, of reptile and creature of the sea, is tamed and has been
tamed by mankind.8 But [no man can tame the tongue]{.underline}. [It is
an unruly evil, full of deadly poison]{.underline}."

Before we decide to write the Holy Spirit off as unimportant or
insignificant we should consider the words of Jesus as it relates to the
importance of the Holy Spirit. John 16:7 (KJV) "Nevertheless I tell
you the truth; [It is expedient for you]{.underline} that I go away: for
[if I go not away, the Comforter will not come]{.underline} unto you;
but [if I depart, I will send him unto you]{.underline}." In this
Scripture, Jesus is stressing to His disciples the necessity of His
departure. As long as He lived in a flesh and blood body He could only
be in one place at a time. He is attempting to explain to them that the
reason it was so necessary for Him to go to the Father was so that the
Holy Spirit would come and live in each believer, thus empowering each
believer to carry on the work of Christ in the world.

If Jesus thought it necessary to depart so that the Holy Spirit could
indwell and empower believers, where do we get off promoting the Holy
Spirit as optional equipment?

## VI. THIS UPDATE PROVIDES ROADSIDE ASSISTANCE! John 15:26 (KJV)
"But when the [Comforter]{.underline} is come, whom I will send unto

The Greek word for Comforter is Parakletos which means:

- One called alongside for the purpose of assistance (Like a big
 brother)

- One who pleads another\'s cause before a judge (He is our legal
 representation)

- A counselor for the purpose of a defense (He is our defense lawyer
 when we stand accused) (Talk about pre-paid legal fees)

- A legal assistant (He researches all similar cases and furnishes the
 information to the attorney)

- An intercessor (He is our most able advocate at Heaven's court)

- A helper (Someone to assist in all situations)

Do you get the picture? The comforter is a constant abiding presence and
is capable of assisting the believer in all situations!
