# FIVE-FOLD CHURCH

TEXT: Ephesians 4:11-16 (NKJV) "And He Himself gave some to
be apostles, some prophets, some evangelists, and some pastors and
teachers, ^12 ^for the equipping of the saints for the work of
ministry, for the edifying of the body of Christ, ^13 ^till we all come
to the unity of the faith and of the knowledge of the Son of God, to a
perfect man, to the measure of the stature of the fullness of
Christ; ^14 ^that we should no longer be children, tossed to and fro and
carried about with every wind of doctrine, by the trickery of men, in
the cunning craftiness of deceitful plotting, ^15 ^but, speaking the
truth in love, may grow up in all things into Him who is
the head---Christ--- ^16 ^from whom the whole body, joined and knit
together by what every joint supplies, according to the effective
working by which every part does its share, causes growth of the body
for the edifying of itself in love."

INTRO: I think we can all agree that the First Century Church was a
healthy, effective and powerful church. I hope we can all agree that we
want to attend a healthy, effective and powerful church.

Though times and culture have changed over the past twenty-one century's
it is my opinion that the words of the Apostle Paul to the Ephesians is
still relevant in the twenty first century church. What it took to build
a healthy, effective and powerful church in the first century, the same
will be required in the twenty first century.

The Apostle Paul makes his case that the only way for the body of Christ
to be healthy, effective and powerful is to operate in the five-fold
ministry model. Listen to what he says will be the outgrowth of such a
church:

- Unity in the faith... ("It is a good and pleasant thing for
 brethren to dwell together in unity")

- A more complete knowledge of Jesus... (The reason this occurs? In a
 five-fold ministry model we do life together and you can know what I
 know, and I can know what you know)

- Steady movement toward perfection... (We do not know what He is like,
 but when we see Him, we shall be like Him)

- We will no longer be driven about by fads and heresy... ("Believe
 His Word and you will be established -- Believe His prophets and you
 will prosper." -- In the five-fold model we are submitted to our
 head -- Jesus! Ecclesia -- A community of called believers who are
 submitted to no King but Jesus - We are submitted to those whom He has
 placed over us -- The apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, and
 teachers)

- Learn to speak the truth in love... (Jesus came "Full of grace and
 truth.")

When we get up under the authority of Jesus and His delegated
authorities, the apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, and teachers,
we become equipped to do the work of the ministry! Sounds like a
healthy, effective and powerful church to me!

I am afraid the refusal among church leaders to embrace a five-fold
ministry model, has all but plunged the twenty-first century church into
a very dark age. The model of the modern-day church is a Pastor (s) who
leads the church and does all the work while the people assume the
position of spectators and cry FEED ME! We have truly become a sick,
anemic, weak church that cannot save ourselves, let alone anyone else.

In 2001 Peter Wagner predicted "The five-fold ministry gifts will be
restored to the twenty-first century church." (Sister Eubanks)

APOSTLE

The thumb represents the apostle -- The thumb is the only finger that
can comfortably touch all the other fingers. The thumb enables the hand
to grip, and when the hand is closed in the formation of a fist
(Defensive or offensive posture), the thumb covers all the fingers.

APOSTOLIC MINISTRY

The (Thumb) apostle has a role in literally everything the (Hand) church
does. Apostles set up government structures -- Apostles form kingdom
culture. Apostles have authority over churches, cities, regions and
countries -- Apostles attract resources -- Doors automatically open to
apostles -- Apostles can make decisions with limited information and
they are quick to make such decisions. Apostles bring revelation.
Apostles in the generic sense are those sent to pioneer something new.
For example, they are often called in to turn failing ministries around.
They are missional from the word go. They plant churches and ministries
almost effortlessly (Apostles -- Not Pastor's planted churches in the
New Testament). Apostles are either launching something new or tinkering
with old systems to give new life to it. If they can't find a way to
give new life to an old system, they boldly take it out behind the barn
and shoot it! (SHARK TANK -- MR. WONDERFUL) Apostles start movements.
They become the architects of systems. If your church doesn't have a
relationship with an Apostle, I can recommend one to you.
