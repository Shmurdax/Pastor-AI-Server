# SEVEN CHURCHES

THE CHURCH OF EPHESUS

TEXT: Revelation 2:1-7 (NKJV) "To the angel of the church of
Ephesus write, These things says He who holds the seven stars in His
right hand, who walks in the midst of the seven golden lampstands:2 "I
know your works, your labor, your patience, and that you cannot bear
those who are evil. And you have tested those who say they are apostles
and are not, and have found them liars;3 "and you have persevered and
have patience, and have labored for My name's sake and have not become
weary.4 "Nevertheless I have this against you, that you have left your
first love.5 "Remember therefore from where you have fallen; repent and
do the first works, or else I will come to you quickly and remove your
lampstand from its place---unless you repent.6 "But this you have, that
you hate the deeds of the Nicolaitans, which I also hate.7 "He who has
an ear, let him hear what the Spirit says to the churches. To him who
overcomes I will give to eat from the tree of life, which is in the
midst of the Paradise of God."

INTRO: If you had been living at the time of the writing about the
seven churches of Revelation and you wanted to live in the most exciting
city in the world, you probably would have selected Ephesus. Two hundred
thirty cities dotted the coastline of Asia Minor, and many of them were
great cities, but Ephesus was the crown jewel of them all.

Ephesus was not only known for its commercial trade, but for the great
Temple of Diana, which was one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Diana
was the goddess with the grotesque head and many breasts which focused
her worshiper's attention on the sensual pleasure of prostitution.

Paul went to Ephesus on his [third]{.underline} missionary journey. He
stayed there two years. He wrote the following concerning this city. 1
Corinthians 16:9 (NKJV) "For a great and effective door has opened to
me, and there are many adversaries."

The church of Ephesus is the first church we read about in the book of
Revelation. The seven churches are: Ephesus, Smyrna, Pergamos, Thyatira,
Sardis, Philadelphia, and Laodicea. In addition to these there were
other churches in [Asia]{.underline}. Acts 20:5, Acts 20:17, 2
Corinthians 2:12, Colossians 1:2, Colossians 4:12.

The Bible does not tell us why John did not write to them, but we can
assume two things:

- God chose the seven churches that were symbolic of seven kinds of
 churches that were to exist in the [future]{.underline}.

- Even though each of the seven churches had [similarities]{.underline},
 each one was [different]{.underline}. This is, and always has been,
 true.

There are many churches around us today but they all fall into a handful
of categories.

Only as we do a sincere, honest, inventory of ourselves can we find
which church we are and where we stand with the Lord Jesus Christ. Let
us look at the seven verses of scripture that deal with the church at
Ephesus to see how we, as a church, compare to that church. The first
thing the Word of God reveals to us about the people in the church at
Ephesus deals with.....

## I. THE LEADER...

In verse 1 we read, Revelation 2:1 (NKJV) "To the angel of the church
of Ephesus write, These things says He who holds the seven stars in His
right hand, who walks in the midst of the seven golden lampstands."
Notice this letter is addressed to the leader of the church the word
"angel" is singular, not plural. He is not addressing all seven
church leaders; He is addressing the one leader of the church of
Ephesus.

This letter is written to the "[angel]{.underline}" or the pastor.
The reason for this is the pastor is the [messenger]{.underline} of God
to the church.

God does not tell the deacons and have them to tell the pastor what He
wants. God holds the pastor personally responsible. God is a God of
organization, not a God of confusion. The pastor must never forget that
God has placed him over the flock and with that special
[responsibility]{.underline} come special [accountability]{.underline}
for the task assigned. 1 Peter 5:2 (NKJV) "Shepherd the flock of God
which is among you, serving as overseers, not by compulsion but
willingly, not for dishonest gain but eagerly; (3) nor as being lords
over those entrusted to you, but being examples to the flock." The
pastor is to:

- [Shepherd]{.underline} the flock... Feed the flock!

- Take the [oversight]{.underline}... Set the direction!

- Live an exemplary [lifestyle]{.underline}... Set the values!

Notice some things that verse one tells us about the minister God. He is
referred to as an "angel." Then notice, John switches to the
plural -- "stars." That is, God holds all of His ministers in His
hands.

The minister of God is:

- Held in the hands of Christ...

- Hand-picked by Christ and placed in His hand...

- Nourished and nurtured by the hand of Christ...

- Protected by the hand of Christ...

- Positioned for a close relationship, being in the hand of Christ...

Why? The position of pastor is the leading position in the church. Every
entity on earth must have a leader:

- A [family]{.underline} needs a leader...

- A [nation]{.underline} needs a leader...

- A [business]{.underline} needs a leader...

- A [team]{.underline} needs a leader...

- A [church]{.underline} needs a leader...

When God was ready to address the church at Ephesus, He recognized the
pastor as the leader of His church as a result it was to the pastor this
letter was written.

## II. THE LAMPSTAND...

Revelation 2:1 (NKJV) "To the angel of the church of Ephesus write,
"These things says He who holds the seven stars in His right hand, who
walks in the midst of the [seven golden lampstands]{.underline}:" The
seven golden lamp stands represent the seven [churches]{.underline} of
Revelation.

As a pastor, I cannot express how honored I feel to have you as a part
of this church. I recognize there are good churches all over this city
that you could attend, but you are here because God has led us together
to build a lighthouse for Him in this community. As much as words can
convey, I deeply appreciate every one of you! But not one of us would be
here if the Lord Jesus was not here.

Sometimes we hear people say, "I do not like to go to church."
Generally they are not trying to be ugly when they say that; they
sincerely mean it. They are not to be blamed. There are some churches
Christians are not comfortable in. Why? If God is not there, they do not
want to be there either.

What is a church? Simply defined, it is: "A group of people who have
been called out and redeemed for the purposes of God."

Look at verse 1 again, ".....who walketh in the [midst]{.underline}
of the seven golden candlesticks." Look at that word "midst."
It is the same word we see in the promise in Matthew 18:20. (KJV)
"For where two or three are gathered together in my name, there am I
in the [midst]{.underline} of them." The thing that separates the
church from a social club is the presence of the Lord.

The streets of gold are not what will make heaven a great place. What is
going to make heaven so great is the Lord Jesus. He is going to be the
center of attraction! The thing that makes a church a great place is not
the building the people worship in, but the Lord who meets them there!
There would be no reason to assemble if He were not here. If He were not
here we might as well attend the Rotary Club!

If a church has lost the presence of the Lord, it has lost everything.
The people may as well shut the door and write Icabod (The glory of the
Lord has departed) across the door. We should never take the presence of
the Lord for granted but cherish it as a thing of value!

Similarly, what will make Hell so terrible are not the eternal flames,
nor the evil people who will inhabit that place! The thing that makes
hell so terrible is the fact that there will be eternal separation from
the presence of the Lord!

John tells us about the leader and the lamp stand of the church of
Ephesus. He also tells us about.......

## III. THE LABOR...

Revelation 2:2-3 (NKJV) "I know [your works, your labor, your
patience]{.underline}, and that you cannot bear those who are evil. And
you have tested those who say they are apostles and are not, and have
found them liars;3 "and you have persevered and have patience, and have
labored for My name's sake and have not become weary."

Look at the word "know" in verse 2; it means the Lord has a clear
vision of what was being done in the church of Ephesus! He was keeping
record of their deeds! If He was doing this with them He is keeping
record of our labor as well.

Nothing can obstruct His view of the church. He not only sees who is in
attendance He also is able to look into the hearts of those who attend
and knows why they attend His church. God always looks past the
[action]{.underline} to the [motivation]{.underline}. He will reward
[motivation]{.underline} before He will reward
[participation]{.underline}! It is at this point that many people will
lose their reward. (We need to get our want to fixed...)

The church of Ephesus was a busy church. The church calendar was filled
with many things.

ILLUSTRATION: They were not like the church that sent their annual
report into headquarters which stated, "No converts, no baptisms, no
new members by letter, no deaths, no births, no gifts to missions."
Then, at the bottom of the report, a note was added which said,
"Brethren, pray for us that we will hold our own."

That was not the case with the church of Ephesus. Their people could
write a wonderful report telling headquarters what they were doing. Look
at what the Lord Jesus said about them "I know your works and your
labor..."

We need to understand here the Lord is commending believers for their
works. He does not ask lost people to work for Him. Why? Because if He
did they would think they were earning their salvation by those good
works. However, we who are saved by His marvelous grace are expected to
work. If we are truly saved we will want to work!

If we are not willing to work then God's work will not be done. It is
not a situation where God cannot do His own work; He has chosen us to do
His work so that we can learn responsibility and receive reward.

The church of Ephesus was not like many churches today. Often it is hard
to find Christians willing to work for the Lord, but the Word of God
tells us the Christians of Ephesus were willing and they labored for the
Lord. (20% of the people do 80% of the work... 20% of the people finance
80% of the work)

"I know your patience..." In that pagan land, where the goddess
Diana was supreme, the Christians encountered many problems, but God
commended them because they were patient and did not quit on the work he
had called them to do.

"I know how thou can not bear them which are evil..." Look at the
phrase, ".....can not bear." The Ephesians would not tolerate for
a moment a church leader who did not live a holy life. In far too many
cases this is a break from the norm in today's church culture. The
church of Ephesus would not tolerate immorality within the
[leadership]{.underline} structure of the church.

"I know you have tried them which say they are apostles, and are not,
and have found them to be liars." Notice the words "tried
them." This church was careful not to embrace every fad and new
doctrine that made its way around the religious establishment. They
literally checked people out with the Word of God and if they did not
line up with God's Word they dismissed them with their teachings. Is
this a scriptural practice? Yes, it was -- and is! I John 4:1(KJV)
"Beloved, believe not every spirit, but [try the spirits]{.underline}
whether they are of God....." God has warned us that "wolves"
would come to us in "sheep's" clothing, yet many today fall
headlong into every new fad and doctrine that comes along!

"I know how you have borne, and have had patience, and for my name's
sake have labored, and have not fainted." The church of Ephesus
suffered many difficulties, but they stood for the name of
[Christ]{.underline}. Notice, under such a stressful load they did not
faint. 2 Thessalonians 3:13-14 (NKJV) "But as for you, brethren, [do
not grow weary in doing good]{.underline}.14 And if anyone does not obey
our word in this epistle, [note that person and do not keep company with
him]{.underline}, that he may be ashamed."

ILLUSTRATION: D.L. Moody came home from holding a meeting completely
exhausted. His family begged him to stay home and rest before he went on
to his next meeting. He told them "I grow weary in the work but not of
the work."

You see, everyone who has ever faithfully served the Lord knows what he
means. Sometimes our bodies are exhausted because of the load we are
carrying, but we never think of giving up because of the load. We may
grow weary in the work of God, but God helps us to never grow weary of
His work.

## IV. THE LEAVING...

So far, everything we read about this church seems to describe a
[good]{.underline} to [great]{.underline} church. In fact, I do not know
many pastors who would not be honored to pastor such a church. This
church had a God called Pastor and leadership team; the Spirit of the
lord is obviously present; each person is finding their place and
working as hard as they can; this church should be a pleasure to pastor.

But remember, God, who said that He knew their works, labor, patience,
etc. is the God who can look into the hearts of men and see what others
cannot see! There was a problem with this church which was not apparent
to the [casual]{.underline} observer. God looked into their hearts,
after saying all those nice things about them, and exposed a major
fault. Vs. 4 "Nevertheless I have somewhat against thee, because thou
have left your first love."

Our love for the Lord is what makes His church an exciting place to be.
It is possible to serve God every day of the week because it is routine
instead of serving Him out of love for Him. There is a distinct
difference between [loving]{.underline} God and [working]{.underline}
for Him. You can almost hear the hurt in the voice of the Father as he
makes this indictment against this church. He is saying you are working
hard enough but you have neglected the one who has redeemed and called
you! This can be a problem for all of us, we mistake busyness for
relationship and they are birds of a different feather.

## V. THE LESSON...

The lesson to be learned from the church of Ephesus is that we can go
through all the ritualism of worship and service and still find
ourselves coming up short on our [love]{.underline}
[relationship]{.underline} with the Father. He is a jealous God!

Instead of church being a place where Christians meet because they love
the Lord, it can become a place where we report for a work detail.

It is possible to:

Place God's tithes and our offerings in the offering receptacle and
never consider God in our thoughts...

Sing beautiful songs and never think about the words we are singing...

Hear wonderful sermons from the Word of God and never allow them to
penetrate our hearts...

Jesus said to the church of Ephesus, and other churches that are like
them, "Remember therefore from whence thou are fallen, and
[repent]{.underline} and [do the first works]{.underline}; or else I
will come unto thee quickly, and [I will remove thy candlestick out of
his place, except thou repent]{.underline}."

We are being commanded to work on our relationship with the Father and
[restore]{.underline} what we had when we first came to know Him. We are
to allow clean hearts to return; the joyous service; the determination
to tell everyone the good news; the outright devotion to His cause; and
our desire to please Him above all else.

If this does not happen the presence of the Lord will be lost from our
life and from the church. If this occurs our relationship and work will
become routine and we will be stressed out about the things of God. God
wants a relationship with us that which is built on [love]{.underline}
rather than [duty]{.underline}.

SEVEN CHURCHES

THE CHURCH OF SMYRNA

TEXT: Revelation 2:8-11 (NKJV) "And to the angel of the church in
Smyrna write, 'These things says the First and the Last, who was dead,
and came to life:9 "I know your works, tribulation, and poverty (but you
are rich); and I know the blasphemy of those who say they are Jews and
are not, but are a synagogue of Satan.10 "Do not fear any of those
things which you are about to suffer. Indeed, the devil is about to
throw some of you into prison, that you may be tested, and you will have
tribulation ten days. Be faithful until death, and I will give you the
crown of life.11 "He who has an ear, let him hear what the Spirit says
to the churches. He who overcomes shall not be hurt by the second
death."

INTRO: You will notice that of the seven churches that John
addressed, he gave the least space to the church of Smyrna. He was able
to cover everything he had to say about this church in just four verses.
However, we must not think less of this church, or feel they were
insignificant just because there are less verses about them. Why? There
were only two of the seven churches that the Lord did not have words of
condemnation for -- the church of Philadelphia and, you guessed it, the
Church of Smyrna. This church was a tremendous church, and the Lord did
not have one bad thing to say about it.

Even though the Lord had no words of condemnation for the people of
Smyrna the scripture tells us they were going through some very
difficult times. The church of Smyrna is known as the
[suffering]{.underline} church. When people are suffering, they do not
need words of condemnation, they need words of comfort. (My experience
on TBN following the Katrina disaster) The Lord, knowing this, had
nothing harsh to say about them.

All of those who have had to counsel people during difficult times in
their lives know how difficult it is to say the right thing. The Lord
Jesus is writing this personal letter Himself, and using John as the
postman to deliver it to them to encourage them to continue serving Him.

Everything about the life of Christ is a lesson to us. From this letter
that Christ sent to that suffering church we can learn a great deal
about how to help people when they are suffering. As we study these four
verses that deal with the church at Smyrna, we can see why they needed
comfort...

## I. THE CONTENTS...

In our study of the church at Ephesus we established that Ephesus was
one of the most exciting places on earth to live at that time. But,
Smyrna was one of the loveliest places to live. If you lived in Smyrna,
you lived at the top of social totem pole.

In our study of the church of Ephesus we discovered that Ephesus was the
Queen city of Asia. But, there are those who feel that Smyrna was the
Queen city, and the people of Smyrna felt that way.

Archeologists have found coins from that area upon which the words,
"[The first city of Asia]{.underline}," were inscribed.

We can best understand the picture of the competition between the people
of these two cities when we compare them to two football teams that
consider each other to be rivals. The people of Smyrna claimed their
town was number one and in many ways Smyrna was number one. Let me tell
you why:

- Smyrna was located only 35 miles north of Ephesus which means it had a
 good location...

- No one knows exactly when the church of Smyrna was started, but it was
 probably started when Paul was in Ephesus. He was there three years,
 and Acts 19:10 says, "....so that all they which dwelt in Asia
 heard the word of the Lord Jesus, both Jews and Greeks." Since
 Smyrna was only 35 miles away, we can safely assume that included
 them...

- Smyrna was originally founded as a Greek colony in 1000 B.C....

- It was later destroyed in 600 B.C....

- It was rebuilt by one of the great generals of Alexander the Great
 around 301-281 B.C....

- It was with great pride that the citizens of Smyrna would declare how
 their lovely city had once been destroyed, yet it was rebuilt even
 greater than it had been before...

- It was known for its large amphitheater that seated as many as twenty
 thousand at one time, which was extremely large for that day and
 time...

- Historians state it is the most beautiful city that the Greeks have
 ever built...

- It still exists... If you were to visit Turkey you would find it, now
 called Izmar. It has a population of 275,000 people.

Smyrna was referred to by many as the crown city, because it sat on the
top of a beautiful hill and as ships approached from the sea the
beautiful buildings looked like a crown on the top of that hill. It was
probably because of this that the Lord Jesus reminded those Christians
that one day they would not just live in a city which looked like a
crown but He would give them a real crown!

Smyrna got its name from its chief export which was a
[gum-like]{.underline} resin taken from a shrub that was very bitter.
This myrrh was used for four things:

(1) It was used for making [perfume]{.underline} -- Psalms 45:8.

<!-- -->

(1) It was used for [embalming]{.underline} -- John 19:39.

(2) It was used for the [purification]{.underline} of women -- Esther
 2:12.

(3) It was used for an [anesthetic]{.underline} -- Mark 15:23.

It was used three times in connection with the life of Christ:

(1) His [birth]{.underline} -- Matt. 2:11.

<!-- -->

(1) His [crucifixion]{.underline} -- Mark 15:23

(2) His [burial]{.underline} -- John 19:39.

As we look at the three times myrrh was connected to the life of Jesus
we discover that each time it was used as a gift, anesthetic and for His
burial.

It is also interesting to note that it is not mentioned in connection
with the future coming of Christ. Isaiah 60:6 (NKJV) "The multitude
of camels shall cover your land, The dromedaries of Midian and Ephah;
All those from Sheba shall come; They shall bring [gold and
incense]{.underline}, And they shall proclaim the praises of the
[Lord]{.smallcaps}." When Christ was born He was presented with gold,
frankincense and Myrrh. But, when Isaiah spoke of Christ's returning,
myrrh is not mentioned. Why? It is not mentioned because myrrh was used
as an anesthetic. When Christ died on the cross it was administered
because of His suffering. When He returns He will not be the suffering
Christ (Savior), He is coming back as King of Kings and Lord of Lords
and myrrh is not mentioned.

Smyrna comes from the word myrrh. Myrrh was the city's chief export and
many of the people became very wealthy as they would crush the plant and
extract its gum-like resin and ship it around the world to be used in
the four ways we have mentioned.

Now, with these facts in mind, let us look at the church of Smyrna and
see how the Smyrna Christians fit into that ritzy society.

## II. THE CHURCH...

We must remember this letter was not written to the city of Smyrna, but
to the church of Smyrna. Why was it written to the church? Because the
people of the church could relate to the city that they lived in, a city
that was proud of its culture, its beauty, its commercial wealth, and
its social life. It was a city where everyone was trying to climb the
social ladder. The Lord had this to say to the Christians in that
culture:

- Verse 8, "These things saith the first and the last..." In other
 words, everyone in Smyrna was trying so hard to be first on the social
 ladder. But Jesus said that He was "......the first and the
 last....." That is, when that crowd has come and gone, He is still
 going to be here.

- In verse 10, Christ said, "I will give thee a crown of life." In
 other words, "Everyone in Smyrna thinks they are so rich and have so
 much to offer you but do not pay any attention to that crowd, I will
 give you a crown of life for your faithfulness." (Paraphrase)

Who would ever have thought that some of the cruelest things ever done
to men were done to Christians in that lovely place?

We can drive by a lovely house and think it looks like a touch of heaven
on earth. The house is beautiful. It is well maintained. The yard is
immaculate. The trees sway in the breeze, and we say, "My, what a
lovely home. I would give anything to get to live there." Did you know
that in that house, where you or I would love to live, someone might
live who would give anything to be able to get out of there? That place
may look good from the outside, but inside it could be filled with
misery! Smyrna was like that.

The word, Smyrna, describes how the church was being crushed, like the
herb was crushed to extract myrrh. The Christians faithfully served the
Lord and would not denounce their faith and they were being tremendously
persecuted. They, like the herb, were being crushed, and from their
persecution there rose up a sweet, sweet fragrance to enter the nostrils
of God.

ILLUSTATION: There is a company that is well known for the perfume it
produces. Often the employees go to a nearby town for lunch. When the
workers from the plant go into town for lunch, those who wait on them
can tell that they work at the perfume plant because the sweet smell of
the perfume lingers on them.

People could identify the members of the Smyrna church by the sweet
[attitude]{.underline} they exuded as they were being crushed through
persecution. Why were they being persecuted? They were being persecuted
because of the simplicity of their worship.

Again, be reminded, Smyrna was a city for the affluent. The most
beautiful, ornate architecture of the world at that time was there. Of
all the seven cities that John addressed in Revelation, Smyrna was by
far the most beautiful. In the city there were beautiful temples for
worship, the Christians met in homes, or quietly worshiped under trees,
even in catacombs. Their simplicity of worship was more than the
sophisticated Jews could handle and this brought with it bitter
persecution.

In the very affluent society where the Jews saw the simplicity of the
Christians, their disgust for their own form of worship played a big
part in their persecuting those Christians.

But, there is another, stronger, reason they persecuted them. It was
because of emperor worship! Christians refused to participate in the
emperor worship. What was emperor worship? In order to fully understand
this concept you must understand a little about Roman government. Today
we look back at history and we see the Roman government's cruelty. The
Romans:

- Fed Christians to the [lions]{.underline} in the coliseum...

- Caused Christians to be [stoned, beaten, and jailed]{.underline}...

- Caused Christians to literally be [burned]{.underline} at the stake...

- Were so corrupt they could be persuaded to [execute]{.underline}
 innocent men...

- The Lord Jesus was [crucified]{.underline} at the hands of the Roman
 government...

Often the heads of the Roman government were tyrants. The Roman
government was one of the vilest governments that ever existed. That is
how we see the Roman government.

But, if we are going to get what we are supposed to get from this
letter, we're going to have to see the Roman government the way the
majority of the citizens of that day saw it. What was their view of it?
They considered the Roman government to be their great protector! How?
The Roman government became so strong that no one dared to attack any of
Rome's provinces.

ILLUSTRATION: When Ronald Reagan was commander-in-chief of this nation
he believed in a strong military. His theory was that the best way to
protect America is to become so strong that anyone would have to be a
fool to try to attack us.

ILLUSTRATION: We know that hundreds of people across America are mugged
each week. But, did you know that professional boxers, wrestlers, and
professional linebackers never get mugged? Why? Even a mugger knows
better than to pick on someone when he knows he can't win.

The reason I mention that is to show you the attitude of the people
under Rome's rule. The roman citizens saw the Roman government as a big
brother that protected them from all of their enemies. If you were a
citizen during that time you would not have had anything negative to say
about the Roman government. You would have thought, "I am thankful for
the Roman government because it protects me from our enemies."

The people began to think, "We love this government so much, and we
will worship the spirit of the Roman Empire." Then they said, "It is
difficult to worship a spirit, so let us worship the personification of
the spirit, who is the emperor."

Out of patriotic pride they began to worship the emperor. It was more
political than religious, but later the emperors felt that they had
taken on divine attributes and Emperor Worship became mandatory. The
Christian's refused to worship the emperor. They were simple minded in
that they stated, "We are Christians and we will bow only to the God of
Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob."

## III. THE CONFLICT...

When the Christians refused to bow, they became the targets for
persecution because, the people of Smyrna could not imagine anyone being
as unthankful and unpatriotic as to live under such a marvelous
government and not want to bow to the emperors. These Christians were
persecuted and tortured in just about any way you could imagine. Despite
the persecution, these Christians took their stand and paid the price
and the Lord had nothing but nice things to say to them.

The citizens of Smyrna lived [affluent]{.underline} lifestyles but
because of the intense persecution most of the Christians lived beneath
the [poverty]{.underline} [line]{.underline}. These Christians thought
themselves poor but look at what Jesus say's to them. Revelation 2:9
(NKJV) "I know your works, tribulation, and poverty ([but you are
rich]{.underline}); and I know the blasphemy of those who say they are
Jews and are not, but are a synagogue of Satan."

Christians were torn asunder, boiled, beaten with whips, arms and legs
amputated, and thrown alive into lion's dens while onlookers cheered.
These Christians were accused of being children of the devil while the
government and religious establishment claimed to be the true children
of God! In the midst of this kind of [persecution]{.underline} the
Smyrna Christians clung to the words of Jesus for dear life. Matthew
5:11-12 (NKJV) "Blessed are you when they revile and persecute you,
and say all kinds of evil against you falsely for My sake.12 "[Rejoice
and be exceedingly glad, for great is your reward in
heaven]{.underline}, for so they persecuted the prophets who were before
you."

In many of our churches we used to sing the song, "It Will Be Worth It
