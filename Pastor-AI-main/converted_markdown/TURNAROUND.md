# TURNAROUND

TEXT: 2 Samuel 9:3-7 (NKJV) "Then the king said, "Is there not
still someone of the house of Saul, to whom I may show the kindness of
God?" And Ziba (Zee-baw) said to the king, "There is still a son of
Jonathan who is lame in his feet." 4 So the king said to him, "Where
is he?" And Ziba (Zee-baw) said to the king, "Indeed he is in the
house of Machir (Maw-keer) the son of Ammiel, in Lo Debar." 5 Then King
David sent and brought him out of the house of Machir (Maw-keer) the son
of Ammiel, from Lo Debar. 6 Now when Mephibosheth the son of Jonathan,
the son of Saul, had come to David, he fell on his face and prostrated
himself. Then David said, "Mephibosheth?" And he answered, "Here is
your servant!" 7 So David said to him, "Do not fear, for I will surely
show you kindness for Jonathan your father's sake, and will restore to
you all the land of Saul your grandfather; and you shall eat bread at my
table continually."

INTRO: In this passage of Scripture David is King over Israel. The
backdrop for this story involves David making a covenant, many years
prior, with Jonathan, who was King Saul's son.

This covenant came out of a friendship between David and Jonathan, which
was solidified with Jonathans loyalty to David when his King father was
seeking the life of his friend. Saul wanted to kill David but Jonathan
protected him.

In this passage of Scripture Saul and Jonathan are dead and David is
King. Even though Jonathan, David's friend is dead, David remembers and
honors the covenant between him and his friend. Vs. 3 "Is there not
still someone of the house of Saul, to whom I may show the kindness of
God?"

Notice here, all David asked was whether or not there was a surviving
descendent of Saul with whom he could keep his covenant with Jonathan.
Listen to the response of Ziba (Zee-baw), Vs 3 "There is still a son
of Jonathan who is lame in his feet." Notice Ziba (Zee-baw)
remembers his shame before he remembers his name.

The same is true of everyone in this audience. People will remember our
failures...

- He cheated on his wife...

- She was arrested for shoplifting...

- He used to be an alcoholic...

- She had an abortion...

Mephibosheth's problem was that he had been dropped. 2 Samuel 4:4 (NKJV)
"Jonathan, Saul's son, had a son who was lame in his feet. He was
five years old when the news about Saul and Jonathan came from Jezreel;
and his nurse took him up and fled. And it happened, as she made haste
to flee, that [he fell and became lame]{.underline}. His name was
Mephibosheth."

The world is full of people who have been dropped. The church is full of
people who have been dropped. There are people in this room that have
been dropped. Let's face it, the church has been guilty of dropping a
lot of people! (We have the tendency of killing our wounded)

From the time his nurse dropped him Mephibosheth could never shake his
history. No matter where he went or what he did his history ruled the
day. He couldn't get away from his past. His legs were a constant
reminder to him.

There are people in this audience today that would love to escape their
past but everywhere they go it seems to arrive before they do.

Look at this, in one day Mephibosheth went from Prince to Pauper; from
fortune to unfortunate; from destined to destitute; from complete to
cripple; from capable to incapable!

Have you ever had a thing like this happen to you? One day you feel as
if you have the world by the tail on a down hill pull, and the next day
all opportunity has evaporated.

Mephibosheth believed his handicap had to do with his legs but in
reality, his legs were only a symptom of what was really wrong. His real
handicap was that:

HE WAS IN THE WRONG PLACE AT THE WRONG TIME...

The problem here was not as much with the condition of Mephibosheth as
it was with his position.

The real problem that Mephibosheth faced was that he was out of place.
He was bred and born for a palace, not the ghetto! That is precisely the
problem with many of us. We are not in the right place.

We are: (PLACE ON SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Heirs of God...

- Joint Heirs with Jesus...

- A Holy Nation...

- A Royal Priesthood...

- A Chosen Generation...

- A Peculiar People...

- Set Apart for the Work of God...

God commanded Elijah to go to the Brook Cherith and He would sustain him
THERE. If Elijah had gone anywhere else, he would have starved to death.
He had to be in the right place at the right time! (Israel: Under the
Cloud and Fire... Because of this, A Rock followed them)

If we are expecting a turnaround we must position ourselves for a
miracle!

All the enemy has to do is keep us out of place! When we are not where
we need to be, when we are supposed to be there, we miss out on the
provision of God! (Alignment -- Shimmy in my car -- Bye bye Shemmei)

Mephibosheth was in the house of Machir (Maw-keer). By all accounts,
Machir (Maw-keer) treats Mephibosheth very well, but his resources and
ability are very limited.

If you are not in the right place at the right time the thing that
blessed you yesterday can keep you from a blessing today. Eventually,
Elijah's brook dried up!

It is not difficult to leave those who are your enemies or who mistreat
you, but sometimes you have to leave your friends. Some friends have

(Abraham & the servants -- Stay here with the asses - the lad and I are

If Mephibosheth could see ahead to the King's palace, he would have no

see everything God has in store for us until we move out by faith.

## II. HE WAS BEING NEGATIVELY IMPACTED BY A LACK OF INFORMATION...

David found Mephibosheth living in the city of Lo Debar.

(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

The word Lo Debar comes from two Hebrew words and is defined in the
following ways:

- Lo - meaning "No"

- Debar - meaning "Word"

When you combine those two words it simply means "No Word."
Mephibosheth was living in the land of "No Word."

Mephibosheth knew nothing of: (PLACE ON SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The Covenant...

- The promise...

- The revelation...

- The blessing...

Just because Mephibosheth didn't know about the covenant did not void
it. The covenant was good, valid, and current!

Mephibosheth just needed to get himself into a place of being made aware
of, and accepting it by faith. (Example of people having money being
held for them but not being aware of it)

There are people in this place today who are living a sub-par existence
but God has greater things in mind. The reason we are not there is
because we have been living in the land of "No Word!" I have a
word for you, "GET UP CRIPPLE PRINCE AND MAKE YOUR WAY TOWARD THE
PALACE, THERE'S A TURNAROUND COMING!"

Notice the response of Mephibosheth to all that David told him... 2
Samuel 9:8 (NKJV) "Then he bowed himself, and said, "What is your
servant, that you should look upon such a [dead dog as
I]{.underline}?"

Mephibosheth was not just putting himself down he was speaking against
the covenant! When the Syrophenician woman came to Jesus, He told her it
was not right to "give the children's bread to dogs." What Jesus
was saying was, you have no place or covenant. This is what Mephibosheth
is saying... "I have no place, or covenant."

We must be able to see ourselves within the boundaries of the covenant
before we can have a turnaround.
