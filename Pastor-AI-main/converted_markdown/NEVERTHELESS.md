 factual, it does not lessen the greater truth of the second)

A fourth grader told his civics teacher that his dad told him that
Houston is the capital of Texas because it is the state's largest city.
The teacher could not dispute the fact that the dad made the statement,
or that Houston is the state's largest city, nevertheless Austin is
still the capital.

By understanding this one word, we build a bridge between:

- Fact and truth...

- Opinion and truth...

- A lesser truth and greater truth...

The possibility exists that a proper understanding of this one word can:

- Kick-start our faith...

- Restore confidence...

- Usher in a resurgence of joy...

- Re-establish victorious living...

When we come to a proper understanding of this one word, we will never
again be defenseless against the overpowering onslaught of hell. This
word allows us to acknowledge the challenges but focus on the Lord and
His word.

In this passage of Scripture, Jesus and others were confronted with
decisions. Jesus had to choose between two boats.

The decisions we make in our lives and ministries determine who we
become and what we are capable of doing. There are times when we are
called upon to make decisions that will prove to be genius or
detrimental.

Why did Jesus choose Peter's boat?

- He knew Peter would allow him to teach from his boat -- All ministry
 decisions should be made based on whether or not it enhances the
 opportunity for teaching/preaching the Word of God.

<!-- -->

- He knew Peter would give up control of the boat -- The boat belonged
 to Peter, but Jesus used it as His own. This is not our church or
 ministry. It belongs to Jesus and we are charged with the
 responsibility of making sure we use it as He would please. We will
 account for what we did with what we had.

- He knew Peter would make Him the Captain -- As owner of the boat and
 the fishing business, Peter was the Captain, but He quickly and
 willingly released that responsibility to Jesus. He called Jesus
 "Master."

- He knew Peter would be obedient -- After teaching, Jesus tells Peter
 to "Launch out into the deep and let down your nets for the
 draught." Peter basically says "We are the professional
 fishermen and you are the teacher -- NEVERTHELESS -- at your word we
 will do so."

- He knew the miracle was only going to happen from one boat -- He chose
 the boat whose captain and crew would take on a task in blind faith.
 Because of the obedience and faith of the Captain and Crew of one
 boat, the miracle affected the other boats. (When the water level goes
 up, all the boats in the harbor are raised.)

Think of this: Three of the twelve disciples (25%) were chosen to follow
Jesus that day!

Can you imagine reading this passage if Peter had refused to allow Jesus
to use his boat or take another stab at fishing? Can you imagine the New
Testament without Peter, James and John?

When it comes to our lives, ministries and church, Satan:

- Learns his lines...

- Practices his arguments...

- Misses no opportunities...

We must stand up to his stupidity and say, nevertheless! Are we to deny
facts? Never!

So, what are we to do? Once we have acknowledged the presentation of the
distorted facts, we are to turn to the enemy and say, nevertheless!

The enemy will tell us God will: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Permit prayer without answers... NEVERTHELESS the ear of God is tuned

- Work us without rewarding us... NEVERTHELESS He is coming and our

- Send us down an unrewarding career path... NEVERTHELESS I have never
 seen the righteous forsaken, nor his seed begging for bread!

- Use us for His purposes and kick us to the curb... NEVERTHELESS Gods

- Allow us to pour our passions into a fruitless effort... NEVERTHELESS

- Let us take a step of faith and then pull the rug out from under us...
 NEVERTHELESS weeping may endure for a night but joy is coming in the
 morning!

- What about all the...? NEVERTHELESS!

- This is never going to...? NEVERTHELESS!

- What if they don't...? NEVERTHELESS!

- They forgot your...? NEVERTHELESS!

This must frustrate the beejeebees out of the devil!
