into the wilderness to be tempted by the devil. And when He had
fasted forty days and forty nights, afterward He was hungry. Now
when the tempter came to Him, he said, "If You are the Son of God,
command that these stones become bread." But He answered and said,
"It is written, 'Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word
that proceeds from the mouth of God.'" Then the devil took Him up
into the holy city, set Him on the pinnacle of the temple, and said
to Him, "If You are the Son of God, throw Yourself down. For it is
written: 'He shall give His angels charge over you,' and, 'In their
hands they shall bear you up, Lest you dash your foot against a stone.'"
 Jesus said to him, "It is written again, 'You shall not tempt the
LORD your God.'" Again, the devil took Him up on an exceedingly high
mountain, and showed Him all the kingdoms of the world and their glory.
 And he said to Him, "All these things I will give You if You will
fall down and worship me." Then Jesus said to him, "Away with you,
Satan! For it is written, 'You shall worship the LORD your God, and Him
only you shall serve.'" Then the devil left Him, and behold, angels
came and ministered to Him."

INTRO: The story of the temptation of Jesus seems to have a strange
setting indeed. A baptismal service was being conducted in the Jordan
River and John the Baptist was the name of the minister performing the
ordinance of water baptism. John was not cut from the cloth of normal
priestly protocol quite the contrary is true: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE
AT A TIME)

- His message was a departure from the norm - His was a message of
 repentance.

- His attire was anything but fashionable - A camels skin suit held in
 place by a leather girdle.

- His diet was shocking - His favorite meal was grasshoppers and wild
 honey.

John was the Grizzly Adams of the religious right. He was a terrible
fashion disaster whose diet was never copied.

A phenomenon unlike anything the current religious leaders had ever saw
was beginning to occur, though his habits were terribly unorthodox and
his message anything but consoling, great multitudes followed the
ministry of John The Baptist. The impact of his ministry would be such,
that the effects of it would be felt for more than two thousand years.

Immediately following the baptism of one of the candidates John looked
up to see a young man approaching from the distant horizon. As the man
drew nearer John recognized the familiar stride of his cousin, Jesus
Christ. In his attempt to exchange pleasantries with Jesus, John utters
words that would become an historic anomaly and a prophetic benchmark;
"Behold the lamb of God which taketh away the sins of the
world." Everyone in the crowd caught and held their breath;
exchanged glances of wonder; and were apparently shocked at the bold
statement of this wilderness preacher. John was certainly not one who
made a habit of mincing words or beating around the bush but this was an
uncharacteristically bold statement, even for John.

Every Jewish person gathered in this small group of converts was very
well rehearsed with regard to the work of the sacrificial lamb on the
Day of Atonement. In his declaration of his Cousin Jesus\' arrival, John
not only declared Jesus to be a lamb but "THE Lamb of God" that
would "TAKE AWAY the sins of the world." John was declaring that
Jesus would attain a threshold of achievement that no other lamb for
centuries past had been able to attain, though they had given their very
lives. The work of the lamb that John was speaking of would be to
forgive sin - not just roll them ahead a year at a time. For all
practical purposes John declared Jesus to be the Messiah!

Following such an announcement, you would expect Jesus to:

- Pull together a ministry team...

- Write a book...

- Develop a magazine...

- Establish a television ministry

- Build a base of support...

In short, do the things that are generally expected and necessary to
begin a ministry that is capable of touching the entire earth. True to
His unconventional model of ministry Jesus does nothing of the sort; In
fact, He seems to downplay the announcement by requesting to be baptized
by this rugged wilderness preacher.

Upon His baptism, a wonderful phenomenon occurred involving a dove,
defying its nature to make physical contact with a human being, and a
voice booming from the sky saying "This is my beloved son in whom I
am well pleased."

This is the stuff wildly popular movies and superstars are made of. The
really shocking feature of this entire story is what happens next: Jesus
is "Led of the Spirit into the wilderness to be tempted by the
devil."

The expectation of the casual reader is for Jesus to: (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Maximize the moment...

- Strike while the iron was hot...

- Capitalize on the occasion...

But He does nothing of the sort; He goes into seclusion! What a waste of
momentum! Can you imagine such a thing? And He is doing all of this at
the instruction of the Spirit.

The picture becomes extremely vague over the next forty days but we do
know Jesus passed the time fasting and praying and in fellowship with
the father. Following this forty-day rendezvous with God Jesus emerges
from His sabbatical only to be met by a direct assault from Satan
himself. The events that follow are commonly referred to as the
"Temptation of Jesus".

THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN

TEMPTATION AND TESTING

The "Temptation of Jesus" is not what most people have believed it
to be. There is certainly more to it than would be immediately apparent
to the casual reader or somewhat distracted observer. This temptation
could probably be better described as the "Testing of Jesus".

A more in-depth look at these two words reveals a Scriptural truth that
has been almost totally ignored. Temptation is always self-inflicted
just as testing is allowed by our Heavenly Father for the purpose of:
(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Strengthening us...

- Building our faith...

- Preparing us for greatness...

- Developing character that can stand the onslaught of success and
 failure...

On the other hand, temptation as we know it, the enticement to do evil:
(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Does not build our faith...

- Does not better prepare us for success or failure...

- Is destructive to our character...

The motive of this kind of temptation is: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT
A TIME)

- To entice us to do evil...

- Terminate our influence...

- Separate us from close fellowship with God...

- Destroy the destiny God has called us to...

The testing of Jesus did not have anything to do with a moral lapse but
had everything to do with the lust of the flesh, the lust of the eyes,
and the pride of life. 1 John 2:15-17 (NKJV) "Do not love the world
or the things in the world. If anyone loves the world, the love of the
Father is not in him.16 For all that is in the world---the lust of the
flesh, the lust of the eyes, and the pride of life---is not of the
Father but is of the world.17 And the world is passing away, and the
lust of it; but he who does the will of God abides forever." The
lust of the flesh, the lust of the eyes, and the pride of life, has to
do with the three-part composition of man: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT
A TIME)

- Body

- Soul

- Spirit

The testing of Jesus consisted of the following: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN
ONE AT A TIME

- The lust of the flesh - He had not received nourishment for his body
 for forty days, and the first thing the enemy attempted to do was
 entice Him to use His supernatural ability to satisfy the flesh side
 of His nature. "If You are the Son of God, command that these
 stones become bread." Was He hungry? He was starving! I have heard
 individuals who were attempting to sound super spiritual say they
 fasted for several days and never experienced hunger! I have fasted
 extensively for many years, fasting at one point thirty days, drinking
 only water (losing 47 pounds in 30 days), and I can tell you that I
 craved food I otherwise didn\'t even like. People who fast and do not
 get hungry are either far more spiritual than Jesus, they are lying,
 or they are sneaking around and eating in private. Jesus was hungry
 and this was a test of the flesh. After forty days of fasting Jesus\'
 flesh was no doubt screaming for nourishment. The enemy tested Jesus\'
 commitment by attempting to get Him to use his supernatural abilities
 to satisfy the flesh.

- The Lust of the eyes - Satan tried to entice Jesus to cast himself
 down from the pinnacle of the temple, thus falling into a trap of
 using His divine connections and heightened spiritual awareness to do
 something that would defy natural and divine law. "If You are the
 Son of God, throw Yourself down. For it is written: 'He shall give His
 angels charge over you,' and, 'In their hands they shall bear you up,
 Lest you dash your foot against a stone.'" In so doing He would
 have been guilty of using His powers to appeal to the senses. Our
 Souls consist of our mind, will, and emotions. Unless we properly
 control our mind, will, and emotions through the power of the Holy
 Spirit we tend to become more sensual than spiritual. When we allow
 the sensual to prevail over the spiritual, we are by an act of our own
 will stepping outside the protection and provision of God. When we
 step outside the protection and provision of God we become drawn away
 of our own lust, caving in to temptation. This is a test of the
 sensual side of Jesus. The enemy was attempting to entice Him into the
 spectacular at a time of super spirituality. Jesus would have no part
 of it because He knew that a propensity to the sensual never satisfies
 the appetite of the eye. Jesus understood that if He jumped from the
 pinnacle of the Temple and Angels carried Him safely to the earth the
 sensual rush would never satisfy, but leave Him lusting for more. All
 attempts to satisfy the soul leave the individual lusting for more.

- The pride of life - Satan continues the test by attempting to appeal
 to the spiritual side of Jesus by leading Him to a high mountain and
 telling Him if He would bow down and worship, he would give Him all He
 could see. "Then Jesus said to him, "Away with you, Satan! For it
 is written, 'You shall worship the LORD your God, and Him only you
 shall serve.'" The spiritual side of every person is destined to
 rule as a king and priest. Because we are destined to rule as kings
 and priests every believer has a natural bent to worship and reign.
 This can get us into trouble if we worship the wrong things and try to
 reign over territory we are not called to. Because we have a
 predisposition for worshipping and reining many of the tests that come
 our way will be spiritual in nature. The enemy consistently attempts
 to keep us from becoming all we can be in the spiritual realm and then
 uses our lack of perceived progress as a catalyst to convince us to
 give up on our destiny. Jesus had a leg up on the tempter because he
 was fully aware that what he was offering was not his to give. This
 was an illusion as are all the enemy\'s claims of entitlement or
 bestowment.

THE VULNERABILITY OF TEMPTATION

James 1:12-15 (NKJV) "Blessed is the man who endures temptation; for
when he has been approved, he will receive the crown of life which the
Lord has promised to those who love Him.13 Let no one say when he is
tempted, "I am tempted by God"; for God cannot be tempted by evil, nor
does He Himself tempt anyone.14 But each one is tempted when he is drawn
away by his own desires and enticed.15 Then, when desire has conceived,
it gives birth to sin; and sin, when it is full-grown, brings forth
death."

James 1:12-15 (LB) "Happy is the man who doesn't give in and do
wrong when he is tempted, for afterwards he will get as his reward the
crown of life that God has promised those who love him. 13 And remember,
when someone wants to do wrong it is never God who is tempting him, for
God never wants to do wrong and never tempts anyone else to do it. 14
Temptation is the pull of man's own evil thoughts and wishes. 15 These
evil thoughts lead to evil actions and afterwards to the death penalty
from God."

There are three basic temptations or tests that we all face: (PLACE ON
THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Things God allows - This testing has its limits and our spiritual
 growth and character have been pre-tested and deemed sufficient for
 the test. I see no record in Scripture of anyone failing a test that
 was pre-approved by God. (Job) 1 Corinthians 10:13 (NKJV) "No
 temptation has overtaken you except such as is common to man; but God
 is faithful, who will not allow you to be tempted beyond what you are
 able, but with the temptation will also make the way of escape, that
 you may be able to bear it."

- Things Satan designs - God will never sanction a test that allows for
 a moral failure. The enemy cannot force you to sin; the devil did not
 make you do it! He can do no more than make a brief presentation of
 the temptation. (King David)

- Self-inflicted temptation - By our own volition and imagination we
 take the bait of Satan and imagine our way into a situation that we
 are powerless to win (Samson). Son of Manoah - Angel announced his
 birth - He was to be a Nazarite - He was to be a deliverer for
 Israel - The angel of the Lord ascended in the flame from a meat
 offering - The Spirit of the Lord moved upon him from time to time -
 Judges 14:1(NKJV) "Now Samson went down to Timnath, and saw a
 woman in Timnath of the daughters of the Philistines." He killed
 30 men to get garments to give to the men of Timnath -- He caught 300
 foxes, set their tails on fire, and burned the Philistines corn crop
 up -- He slew a host of Philistines hip and thigh - With the jaw bone
 of an ass he slew 1,000 Philistines - After this slaughter Samson was
 famished from thirst and God hollowed out a place in the jawbone from
 which water gushed forth, quenching his thirst - He frequented a
 prostitute in Gaza - He carried away the gates of the city of Gaza --
 In Judges :4 he meets Delilah - Breaks green vines that had never
 been dried - Breaks new ropes - Carries away a beam fastened to his
 head with a pin - He tells all - Delilah sold him out - He lost his
 power with God -- He was blinded and made a circus attraction - He was
 made to grind at the enemies wheel -- Upon his request God restored
 his unusual strength.

Temptation, as we know it, has to do with an enticement to do that which
is in direct moral conflict with Biblical standards and practices.
(Lying - stealing - cheating - etc.)

According to the Word, God cannot be tempted by these things and will
not authorize such an attack against us! James 1:13-15 (LB) "And
remember, when someone wants to do wrong it is never God who is tempting
him, for God never wants to do wrong and never tempts anyone else to do
it. 14 Temptation is the pull of man's own evil thoughts and wishes. 15
These evil thoughts lead to evil actions and afterwards to the death
penalty from God." (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE)

I think we could safely say that any attack against us that has attached
to it an enticement to do that which is not morally convenient, is from
Satan himself. If the attack carries these illegal components, it is a
covert attack and must be dealt with as such. In fighting against such
an attack we should use all the weapons at our disposal. Throw the book
(the Bible) at the devil! This kind of attack is normally won or lost at
the point of impact.

As we look into this verse the one thing that becomes very apparent;
temptation is often self-inflicted testing is not! "Every man is
tempted WHEN he is drawn away of his own lust and enticed."

The fact that many of us struggle so unsuccessfully is proof that we are
either acting on the thoughts the enemy has placed in our mind or we are
suffering a self-inflicted temptation.

Almost unknowingly many Christians willingly place themselves in
vulnerable positions. Others fail to implement a "Search and destroy"
mentality during initial impact.

Either way we become vulnerable to the enemy who is absolutely merciless
when he discovers the helplessness of his victim.

THE PROTECTION OF TESTING

You are probably thinking that Jesus faced the same temptations we do
yet He did not sin. This is true; the Bible states it very vigorously.
Hebrews 4:15 (NKJV) "For we do not have a High Priest who cannot
sympathize with our weaknesses, but was in all points tempted as we are,
yet without sin." Hebrews 4:15 (LB) "This High Priest of ours
understands our weaknesses since he had the same temptations we do,
though he never once gave way to them and sinned."

So what kind of temptation did Jesus face? (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT
A TIME)

- He was restricted by the limitations of the flesh - He became hungry -
 Matthew 4 (Turn these stones into bread), this was the first way the
 enemy tested Him.

- He became physically exhausted - Several times in Scripture He removed
 Himself from the crowd for some rest and relaxation.

- He became lonely - "Foxes have holes, birds have nests, but the
 son of man does not have a place to lay His head."

- He suffered rejection - The betrayal of Judas. The night prior to the
 crucifixion Jesus was agonizing in prayer and the disciples could not
 stay awake with Him for one hour - We can almost hear the rejection in
 Jesus\' voice as He say\'s "Sleep on now."

- He endured conflict within His family -- Mary and Joseph looking for
 Him after He had been gone three days.

- He was tempted to get angry and lose His temper - Dealing with the
 mercenaries in the Temple.

- He suffered physical pain - His crucifixion.

Though Jesus was tested in many ways, there is not one shred of evidence
in the Bible that He was tempted to do anything in conflict with
Biblical standards or practices!

- He was never tempted to relegate God to anything but first place in
 His life!

- He was never tempted to commit idolatry!

- He was never tempted to serve any God but Jehovah!

- He was never tempted to curse!

- He was never tempted to dishonor the Lord\'s Day!

- He was never tempted to dishonor His parents!

- He was never tempted to take an innocent life!

- He never lusted after another man\'s wife!

- He was never tempted to take anything that did not belong to Him!

- He was never tempted to covet another man\'s possessions!

Did the idea of doing any of these things ever enter into the mind of
Jesus? I\'m sure it did, but He knew the power of casting imaginations
down upon impact.

This is where our battle with the devil is won or lost. If we allow the
mental process to go beyond an initial thought we have stepped outside
the protection God has so graciously provided. When we allow ourselves
to step outside His protection and provision we are no match for the
devil. Just as Jesus overcame the temptation to do wrong, if we will
cast every thought of immorality and unscriptural practice down upon
impact we will never be tempted to do these things either!

In the Old Testament Job experienced a test - not a temptation - a test!
The test Job faced was actually brought on by the fact God was bragging
about Jobs integrity and spiritual maturity. God said to the devil
"Have you noticed my servant Job? He is the finest man in all the
earth --- a good man who fears God and will have nothing to do with
evil." Job 1:8 (LB) Considering the overall story of the testing of
Job it is obvious; all that came against him had to first of all meet
the approval of God. That sounds strange indeed but it is nonetheless
true! Job 23:10 (NKJV) "But He knows the way that I take; When He
has tested me, I shall come forth as gold."

The tests we face come as no surprise to God. They sometimes hit us
broadside but they never catch God off guard.

Every test we face must meet the approval of God in advance. (LEAVE ON
THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) God looks at the test and then looks
at where we are and what we are capable of enduring in the physical,
emotional, and spiritual realm. In this process He considers our faith
and endurance and would never allow the enemy to bring anything against
us that we are incapable of handling. "No testing has overtaken you
that is not common to everyone. God is faithful, and he will not let you
be tested beyond your strength, but with the testing he will also
provide the way out so that you may be able to endure it."

There is no way of getting around it or avoiding it, the fact is God
allowed this test to come into Jobs life - He could have prevented it
but He did not choose to do so. Why do you suppose God chose such a
course of action?

He was strengthening Job; building his faith; preparing him for a time
of success and greatness that was beyond his wildest imagination. Job
was about to be given twice as much as he had before the test; twice as
many children; twice as many cattle and oxen; twice as many resources;
twice as much life! Job was seventy years old at the onset of the test,
and he lived to be two hundred and ten years old!

When we stop to think about it, Job was enduring a test that he could
not fail because God had already examined the test and evaluated Job\'s
integrity and spiritual maturity. The enemy initiated a battle that he
was predisposed to lose from the beginning.

Testing has to do with living in right relationship with God and others
regardless of the pressure to do otherwise. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL
THE NEXT SLIDE) Testing also has to do with standing on the promises
of God despite the temptation to do otherwise. To the person who is
tested by God the highest form of reality is what the Word of God has to
say on the subject.

In the testing of Jesus He took three scriptures out of the Old
Testament book of Deuteronomy and whipped the devil all over the desert.
I am convinced that God looked at the test and then at His son Jesus and
said something like this to an angel; "This should be good; Satan
wants to test Jesus by offering Him food, sensationalism, and power!
What a dumb devil, doesn\'t he know who Jesus is and where He has
been?" By the way, we are also sons of God and have been given the
same rights, privileges and power as Jesus had when He was upon the
earth.

The bottom line here is temptation has everything to do with our own
lusts and appetites, testing has to do with our personal development and
spiritual maturity. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) When we
are working our way through a test rather than a temptation the sounds,
sights, and smell of victory are never beyond our reach!

PRACTICAL APPLICATION

The key to our success in any test we face is as follows: (PLACE ON
THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Live within the hedge of protection God has cast for us. The hedge
 consists of the Blood of Jesus and the Word of God. As long as we live
 our lives within those perimeters we have nothing to fear from the
 enemy. He can\'t win and we can\'t lose!

- Never allow the devil to entice us to step outside the Bloodline. We
 must never allow him to convince us that even though the Word of God
 speaks out against a certain behavior God will make an exception for
 us.

## I. FORGIVENESS IS ESSENTIAL TO OVERCOMING REJECTION...

In Bible days, people named their children for something of significance
in their life. When Joseph's first child was born he named him Manasseh.
Manasseh means "causing to forget or forgetfulness." Notice vs. 51
"And Joseph called the name of the firstborn Manasseh: For God, said
he hath made me [forget all my toil, and all my father's
house]{.underline}."

What is Joseph saying here?

- Is he saying God has removed his mother, father and eleven brothers
 from his memory? No!

- Is he saying God has erased the maltreatment by his siblings from his
 memory? No!

- Is he saying God has stricken the rejection by his brothers from his
 memory? No!

He is simply saying that God has taken the pain out of a past experience
for Him. If anyone has ever had a reason to be bitter and have a
critical attitude, surely Joseph did.

How could a guy who had been through what Joseph had endured ever be
able to get past the pain? The path to peace in Joseph's life is really
pretty simple. When his first son was born, he named him Manasseh
meaning, "The Lord hath made me to forget." While Joseph could not
forget his father, mother and eleven brothers, what he could do was to
forgive them, and that he did!

Have you ever been wronged? Been rejected? Treated badly? Been double
crossed? Cheated out of something? These things can bring with them a
boat load of pain; how we react to these things will define who we are!
If we forgive, we can move on with our life and become who God intended
us to be. If we withhold forgiveness, we immediately become stunted in
our growth pattern and slowly but surely become critical if not
downright bitter!

Many have critical spirits, who call themselves Christians. Why are they
critical? Because they have never come to a place of letting God take
the pain out of a past experience. Past experiences are why some people
don't serve God today. If we allow past experiences to stand between us
and serving God, we will lose out with God and our lives will be less
than they were intended to be.

Most people fail to forgive, hoping to inflict pain on the un-forgiven
individual when in reality the person who is withholding forgiveness
places themselves in a prison for a life term or until forgiveness can
be dispensed.

I don't know what you have been going through but if Joseph could endure
all this, and forget the past, surely you and I can forget the past and
serve God victoriously!

Keys to victory: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Allow God to take the pain out of a past experience... Paul, who had
 everything happen to him except his life being taken from him says in
 Philippians 3:13-14 (NKJV) "Brethren, I do not count myself to have
 apprehended; but one thing I do, [forgetting those things which are
 behind]{.underline} and [reaching forward to those things which are
 ahead]{.underline}, I press toward the goal for the prize of the
 upward call of God in Christ Jesus." How in the world is it
 possible to allow God to take the pain out of a past experience for
 us? First of all we must forgive! While teaching His disciples how to
 pray, Jesus makes this statement, Matthew 6:12 (NKJV) "And [forgive
 us our debts, As we forgive our debtors]{.underline}." If that is
 not enough, following "The Lord's Prayer" He says to His disciples,
 almost as an afterthought, Matthew 6:14-15 (NKJV) "For [if you
 forgive men their trespasses, your heavenly Father will also forgive
 you]{.underline}. "But [if you do not forgive men their
 trespasses, neither will your Father forgive your
 trespasses]{.underline}." How is this kind of forgiveness possible?
 The Bible tells us that God has "Taken our sins and cast them as
 far as the East is from the West [never]{.underline} to be remembered
 against us again." Think of what Christ did for us upon the cross
 of Calvary. Now think of all the times we have sinned against Him. As
 soon as we asked to be forgiven, He forgave us and forgot it, pure and
 simple! If He has done all of this for us, then we cannot withhold
 forgiveness from our brethren. (Ex. Parable of man who owed his master
 much, the debt was forgiven, and then he turned around and did not
 forgive a little debt owed to him.) Many people are just that way. Not
 only must we forgive, but if we think your brother or sister has a
 problem with us, we are responsible to go to that person and
 straighten things out. Matthew 5:23-24 (NKJV) "Therefore if you
 bring your gift to the altar, and there remember that your brother has
 something against you, "[leave your gift there before the altar,
 and go your way. First be reconciled to your brother, and then come
 and offer your gift]{.underline}." A lack of forgiveness is the
 very reason many Christians never mature, they have never learned to
 forgive. A good exercise for forgiveness is to write a letter of
 forgiveness... Make a phone call of forgiveness...

- Live in the present and the future and minimize the failures of the
 past... Philippians 3:13-14 (NKJV) "Brethren, I do not count myself
 to have apprehended; but one thing I do, [forgetting those things
 which are behind]{.underline} and [reaching forward to those things
 which are ahead]{.underline}, I [press toward the goal for the
 prize]{.underline} of the upward call of God in Christ Jesus."
 Un-forgiveness requires a lot of energy and attention. If we channel
 this energy and attention toward what is happening today and tomorrow
 we will not have what it takes to keep holding to the past!

- Do good deeds to those who have wronged us... Matthew 5:43-45 (NKJV)
 "You have heard that it was said, 'You shall love your neighbor and
 hate your enemy.' "But I say to you, [love your
 enemies]{.underline}, [bless those who curse you]{.underline}, [do
 good to those who hate you]{.underline}, and [pray for those who
 spitefully use you and persecute you]{.underline}, "that [you may
 be sons of your Father in heaven]{.underline}; for He makes His sun

 the Philippian Christians, Philippians 4:8 (NKJV) "Finally,

 work in us we do not have to do God's work for Him, He will manifest
 His miracles through us! We must allow our minds to be ridded of past
 failures, defeats, and wrongs, by dwelling on good things, forgetting
 the past, and dwelling on the future!

We see a perfect example of these things being pressed into service in
the life of Joseph. Joseph's 10 brothers came to buy corn in Egypt at
the command of their father Jacob. Joseph had the authority and the
motive to kill every one of them but notice what he did. He gives them
their corn, and puts their money back in their sacks. He keeps Simeon so
that they will bring Benjamin back with them.

As a last resort, Jacob consents to let Benjamin come with them. Joseph
makes a feast for them, gives them corn and supplies, and once again
puts their money back in their sacks, only this time he puts his silver
cup in Benjamin's sack and lets them go. When they are gone, he sends
his servants after them and his servants find the cup in Benjamin's
sack. Joseph's brothers plead with him, not knowing with whom they were
pleading then he reveals who he is. His brothers are afraid for their
lives.

Listen to the words of Joseph when his brothers realize who they are
dealing with. Genesis 45:4-8 (NKJV) "And Joseph said to his brothers,

The reason Joseph could deal with his brothers so gently when they came
to buy corn in Egypt is because he forgave them long before they
arrived! Why? Because "Manasseh, the Lord hath made me to forget!"
He named his son Manasseh so that each time he spoke his son's name, he
was reminded to keep forgiving his brothers. As Joseph forgave his
brothers he was releasing his own family from the shackles of the curse
of un-forgiveness!

This very same thing can happen to you! I don't know about you, but I
believe there were probably days when the past would begin to bother him
and all he had to do was to look at Manasseh and that was enough to
cause him to forget the past.

## II. FRUITFULNESS; THE RESULT OF FORGIVENESS...

Joseph named his second son Ephraim. Genesis 41:52 "And the name of
the second he called [Ephraim]{.underline}: 'For [God has caused me to
be fruitful in the land of my affliction]{.underline}.'" The name
Ephraim means "Double Fruit." Remember, Joseph named his first son
Manasseh because God had taken the pain out of a past experience for
him. Joseph's ability to forgive is absolutely unquestioned. Now his
second son comes along and he chooses an equally important and
significant name by naming him Ephraim.

There are three things which I want us to focus on: (PLACE ON THE
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- God caused Joseph to be fruitful...

- God caused Joseph to be fruitful in the land of his affliction...

- Joseph never lost sight of the fact that Egypt was not his home...

1\) Why did God cause Joseph to be fruitful? Because he was a man of
integrity and he demonstrated an ability not to allow his past to
dictate the present and the future.

Far too many times we allow our past to determine where we are today and
where we are going in all of our tomorrows. Be assured, if we cannot get
past a prior failure or injury we cannot succeed today or tomorrow.

Joseph makes a declaration which flies in the face of everything we
know; "[God has caused me to be fruitful]{.underline}....'" Joseph
should have been a bitter failure rotting in a jail cell but he informs
us that God has allowed fruitfulness in his life despite past failures
and setbacks.

This is great for Joseph but how can I bring this about in my life? Live
a life of integrity and forgive everyone who commits an offense against
us! The level of our integrity and ability to forgive is directly tied
to the level of the success we can achieve in our lives! Philippians
3:13-14 (NKJV) "Brethren, I do not count myself to have apprehended;
but one thing I do, [forgetting those things which are
behind]{.underline} and [reaching forward to those things which are
ahead]{.underline}, I [press toward the goal for the
prize]{.underline} of the upward call of God in Christ Jesus."

fruitful..." If he had stopped there this would still be an amazing
story on how to succeed when we have been rejected. Listen to the entire
statement, "God has caused me to be fruitful in the land of my
affliction."

Anyone can be successful when everything is going right but it takes
something special to succeed when all things are against you.

Everything was against Joseph: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- He was hated by his brothers...

- He was sold as an Egyptian slave...

- He was falsely accused of sexual assault...

- He was falsely imprisoned in a foreign land...

- He was estranged from everyone who could help him...

first opportunity possible. We would have chastised or killed the people
who sent us to Egypt, but Joseph never even considers such a thing,
which is precisely why God could place him in such a position of

God will only give us what he can trust us with!

- He was blessed with Divine direction...

- He was blessed with positions of influence...

- He was blessed with a family who honored God...

Though Joseph experienced the zenith of his success in Egypt, it was
never home to Joseph. According to the Scripture, we are also
"pilgrims and strangers" here. We are living in the land of
affliction, so to speak.

Joseph lived in the land of affliction, but he walked uprightly before
God. Because he walked uprightly before God, God blessed him.

No matter how good or difficult the journey becomes, we must keep in
mind that this world is not our end game. Joseph had a good run in
Egypt, but listen to what he says just before his death. Genesis
50:24-25 (KJV) And Joseph said unto his brethren, "I die: and God will
surely visit you, and bring you out of this land unto the land which he
sware to Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob." 25 And Joseph took an oath of
the children of Israel, saying, "God will surely visit you, and ye shall
carry up my bones from hence."
