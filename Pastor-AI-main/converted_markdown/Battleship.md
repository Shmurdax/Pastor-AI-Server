the [dynamics]{.underline} of the story; during ministry at the

 [ships]{.underline} in our story and the
 [church]{.underline}.

- Fishermen... I would like to draw a parallel between the
 [fishermen]{.underline} in this story and those of us who have
 decided to become a part of Gods [program]{.underline} on earth.

in their efforts until Jesus became involved. Notice the frustration in

mention being flat broke! Most church problems could be solved with a

churches smell sterile because they are! Mediocrity has all but neutered
the church!

When Jesus became involved he revealed both the
[problem]{.underline} and the [solution]{.underline}. Luke
5:4 "He said unto Simon, Launch out into the deep, and let down your
nets for a draught." Notice Jesus speaks to a question that has not

professionals. I'm not sure these fishermen were all that eager to get

out into the deep and fish on the other side of the boat. What does he
know about fishing? He made the sea and all the fish; he knows what
school they are in and what it takes to catch them.

Empty fishing boats spell tragedy for both [fishermen]{.underline}
and the [hungry]{.underline}. Never before in the history of the

[boats]{.underline} mean something is [wrong]{.underline}!

[ability]{.underline}, he is looking for

[when]{.underline} and [where]{.underline} he tells you to

[fills]{.underline} up. Some of the best church growth material on
