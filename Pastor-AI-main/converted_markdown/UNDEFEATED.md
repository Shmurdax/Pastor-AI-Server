# UNDEFEATED

TURNING MISTAKES INTO MINISTRY

Simon! Indeed, Satan has asked for you, that he may sift you as
wheat. ^32 ^But I have prayed for you, that your faith should not fail;
and when you have returned to Me, strengthen your brethren." ^33 ^But he
said to Him, "Lord, I am ready to go with You, both to prison and to
death. ^34 ^Then He said, "I tell you, Peter, the rooster shall not crow
this day before you will deny three times that you know Me..."
...^54 ^Having arrested Him, they led Him and brought Him into the high
priest's house. But Peter followed at a distance. ^55 ^Now when they had
kindled a fire in the midst of the courtyard and sat down together,
Peter sat among them. ^56 ^And a certain servant girl, seeing him as he
sat by the fire, looked intently at him and said, "This man was also
with Him." ^57 ^But he denied Him, saying, "Woman, I do not know

^58 ^And after a little while another saw him and said, "You also are

said, "Man, I do not know what you are saying!" Immediately, while he

INTRO: This failure by Peter is probably one of the most well-known
failures in the history of the world. How embarrassing to have your
failure plastered on the annals of time for everyone to see and discuss!
All of us have failures and we want our failures to be a little more
private, do we not?

- Some failures are private...

- Some failures are known by those closest to us...

- Some failures are very public...

Before we judge others for their failures we should consider two
scriptures:

- Romans 3:23 (NKJV) "For all have sinned and come short of the glory
 of God."

- Matthew 5:7 (NKJV) "Blessed are the merciful for they shall obtain
 mercy."

Most of us like to keep our failures as private as possible but the
failure of Simon Peter was as public as it gets.

Peter like ALL of us never expected to fail. We would all like to think
we have lived an undefeated life. Many live in a fantasy world of one
hundred percent success. Peter lived in the same world, "Lord, I am
ready to go with You, both to prison and to death." When Peter
realized he had experienced such a failure (The rooster was an envoy of
revival to Peter), he was devastated by what he had done. (Especially
since it involved Jesus who he felt very close to)

What is the difference between defeated and undefeated people?
Undefeated people turn their mistakes into opportunities! Robert
Kiyosaki said, "Losers quit when they fail. Winners fail until they
succeed." 

## I. UNDEFEATED PEOLE VIEW FAILURES AS TRANSITION, NOT TERMINATION...

There is a back story to Peter's failure... Judas had double-crossed
Jesus and sold Him for 30 pieces of silver... Judas could not handle his
failure properly:

- He did not spend the 30 pieces of silver on himself...

- He threw the 30 pieces of silver away...

- He could not reconcile his mistake...

- He committed suicide...

Judas' failure terminated him! Our failures either transition us or
terminate us!

Peter on the other hand realized he had failed and allowed his failure
to transition him to something better. Luke 22:60-62 (NKJV)
"Immediately, while he was still speaking, the
rooster crowed. ^61 ^And the Lord turned and looked at Peter. Then Peter
remembered the word of the Lord, how He had said to him, "Before the
rooster crows, you will deny Me three times." ^62 ^So Peter went out and
wept bitterly."  

Within 53 days Peter went from being the biggest dufus of all to being
the first, and one of the most powerful voices in Pentecost!

Where is your failure going to lead? To defeat? Or will it transition
you to triumph? Will our failures bring about transition or termination?

## C.S. Lewis said, "Failures are finger posts on the road to
achievement." 

## II. UNDEFEATED PEOPLE VIEW FAILURE AS TEMPRORAY NOT PERMANENT...

1 Peter 1:3-7 (NKJV) "Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus
Christ, who according to His abundant mercy has begotten us again to a
living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the
dead, ^4 ^to an inheritance incorruptible and undefiled and that does
not fade away, reserved in heaven for you, ^5 ^who are kept by the power
of God through faith for salvation ready to be revealed in the last
time. ^6 ^In this you greatly rejoice, though now for a little while, if
need be, you have been grieved by various trials, ^7 ^that the
genuineness of your faith, being much more precious than gold that
perishes, though it is tested by fire, may be found to praise, honor,
and glory at the revelation of Jesus Christ."

- He has begotten us in living hope - through mercy...

- He has decided to reward us with an inheritance that does not fade
 away... (This is not a merit based salvation but we will be rewarded
 for our works)

- Our trials and failures are "for a little while..."

- The things we go through bring out the true colors of our faith...

So we have failed?

We have come short of the glory of God?

We have missed the mark?

We must STOP looking at our failures as permanent -- It is a momentary
setback! We will get up again because we are undefeated! Napoleon Hill
said, "Most great people have attained their greatest success just one
step beyond their greatest failure."

We were never promised an easy journey, we were promised a safe landing!

John 16:33 (NKJV) "These things I have spoken to you, that in Me you
may have peace. In the world you will have tribulation;
but be of good cheer, I have overcome the world."

Simon Peter recovered from his failure to become an Apostle in the New
Testament Church and I declare that we will recover from our mistakes as
well! We will not be defeated! We will be undefeated!

## III. UNDEFEATED PEOPLE REFUSE TO HANG OUT IN A PLACE CALLED
FAILURE...

- Judas could not move past his failure but Peter could...

- Abraham refused to settle for a mistake -- He received a promised
 son...

- Moses decided Jethro's farm wasn't big enough for his vision -- He
 found a deliverer within...

- Isaac decided a name and a past would not define him, he wrestled with
 God until God changed his name and blessed him...

- David did not allow adultery and murder to define him -- Jesus the son
 of David... (Man after Gods own heart)

- Saul (Paul) did not allow his past to decided his future... (He was
 suspicioned by the church -- Annanias -- Straight Street)

CONCLUSION:

Are we going to hang out in a place called failure or are we going to
pick up and move to a place called victory? "Weeping may endure for a
night but joy comes in the morning." GOOD MORNING!!!

George Bernard Shaw said, "A life spent making mistakes is not only
more honorable but more useful than a life spent in doing nothing." 

The reason I can say we are undefeated is because the contest is not yet
over! "Well done you good and faithful servant! You have been
faithful over A FEW THINGS -- I will make you RULER OVER MANY
THINGS!"
