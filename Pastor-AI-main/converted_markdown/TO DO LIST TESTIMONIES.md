# DOUG & AUDREY TURNBULL

April 8th 2005, Andrew was full term but was born with 1 and 1/4 lungs,
severe pulmonary hypertension and a heart on the opposite side that also
had veins mixing good and bad blood. 

After a few days in the NICU his progression worsened and his parents
were told that all medications and machines had been exhausted and they
needed to discuss the use of life saving measures.  The doctors clearly
said there was "Nothing else they could do, as they really did not know
the source of the problem."  Doug and Audrey reluctantly signed the
"do not resuscitate" paperwork and even discussed doing an autopsy
when he passed.  The family and ministers were called in and one last
prayer time was convened for little Andrew.  Every hour after that final
prayer, Andrew\'s health continued to get better. 

Within hours, the doctors asked Doug and Audrey to give them permission
to shred the "do not resuscitate" paperwork.  Andrew's parents told
the medical team they would like it to be kept on file but they
wouldn\'t hear of it.  They got rid of the paperwork and Andrew
continued to heal during his time in the NICU. What was to be months of
recovery and healing became weeks. 

The final test would be an MRI to determine not if he had brain damage
but how much brain damage he would have, of course the MRI was clear
too!  The final challenge from the doctors was to keep him well until
three years of age.  Andrew turned 7 on April 8 2012, Easter Sunday!

 

Doug and Audrey write, "Pastor asked how this health challenge changed
us as parents. It changed us as it did Abraham and Isaac. We took Andrew
up that mountain as "One" and our communication with God were also in
one voice. Our prayer was as follows: 'Father, we accept Your Will but
if you allow Andrew to remain in our care, we ask that he be whole and
without extreme challenges. If you take him to be with you, then we
accept Your will to have another angel in Heaven.'" 

During the time Andrew was clinging to life, prayers were gong up across
the US and Andrew\'s story tugged at the hearts of even strangers. 

His parents said, "We will never know extent of the impact of Andrew\'s
Miracle but we pray that his testimony will continue to reach others
throughout his life."

DARRELL & TONYA MAYFIELD 

In February of 2005, Darrell and Tonya Mayfield received news that
Tonya\'s brother and his wife, who lived in Alaska, had been killed in a
vehicle crash.  Jay and Terry had three teenagers, ages 16, 15 and 13,
Danielle, Jaclyn and Travis.  These children were told that according to
their parents\' wishes as stated in their wills, they would be moving to
Houston to live with family whom they had seen two or maybe three times
in their life. Darrell and Tonya had two children of their own, Clint
and Derek.  Clint was married and Derek, who was 11, was the only child
still living at home. 

 

For these three-orphaned children there were more people in their new
school in Deer Park, than the population of the entire town where they
lived in Alaska.  For Derek, his life changed from an only child at home
to the youngest of four children. For Danielle, Jaclyn, and Travis,
everything changed!  The Mayfield family was living in a small three
bedroom house at the time.  Within a year, God provided them with a
brand new home that would comfortably take care of a family of six. 

 

According to Darrell and Tonya, there were some pretty rough days during
this journey.  There were days when they feared the children would run
away and then there were days when they would have gladly helped them
pack to go.  There were days that they wondered if God knew what He was
doing sending these children to them?  But God had a plan bigger than
what Darrell and Tonya could see or even imagine at the time.  God sent
people along the way to encourage them in this journey and to hold up
their hands when they couldn\'t hold them up on their own.  Darrell and
Tonya said, "Our family helped us in so many ways and our church family
was with us every step of the way.  Our pastor\'s wife even came to
Alaska for the funeral and braved the ice and snow in shoes that were
inadequate in such weather.  We won\'t ever forget the love that was
shown to us by everyone." 

 

The kids have become healthy and well adjusted and are living out the
plans that God has for them.  Danielle is married.  She and Ben live in
the Woodlands and own their own business.  Jaclyn is still at home with
the Mayfield's and is a full time student at University of Houston
working on her degree.  Travis is in the Navy and is out to sea even as
we speak.  Derek is in the Air Force and is being sent of all places to
Alaska for his permanent duty assignment.  Clint and Andrea have given
Darrell and Tonya their first granddaughter, Gabby, and live in Deer
Park. 

 

Darrell and Tonya said, "What the enemy meant for evil, God turned it
all around for good.  God had a plan! He is still providing!  What God
promises, He fulfils!"

Darrell and Tonya discovered that God could enlarge their hearts to
include three extra teenagers!

 

DOCTOR MICHAEL HOLMES

Diagnosed with a rare form of cancer, Dr. Michael Holmes felt from the
beginning that he was going to have to go through the whole process with
some kind of treatment, but that God would be with him. The first or
second Sunday after diagnosis Frankie Fridge gave him a word, "Not to
worry, that God would be with him."

At the very beginning, Dr. Mike told his wife Becky "I can do this if
you do not blame God and you are beside me." Many times when he was
weak, she was strong. Dr. Mike learned things about Becky during this
time that he did not know, like how really strong and full of faith she
was.

We know that God could have healed him in one big miraculous event, but
His way was to do many miracles along the journey of the treatment
process. The doctor told this couple that once chemo started Dr. Mike
would need to take off work because "He would most likely be too weak
and sick to work." Other than two or three days in the hospital for a
blood clot in his leg, he only missed one day because he was too sick to
work. When Dr. Mike went for his follow-up after treatment was
completed, the doctor asked if he had gone back to work? When Dr. Mike
told her that "He had only missed one day" she could not believe it.

In the beginning, the lymph node on his neck was about the size of a
ping-pong ball but after the first three days of chemo it was nowhere to
be found! As a precaution he still had to endure eleven weeks of chemo
and radiation.

Dr. Mike developed a blood clot in his leg due to the chemo, and on
Christmas Eve the doctor came in and said that he would need to be on
blood thinners for a period of time and that he could go home that day
if they could give him a certain drug, but it was \$4,000 a month and
insurance would not pay for it. If they put him on an alternate drug he
would have to stay in the hospital another week. Dr. Mike said, "Becky
and I wanted to be home for Christmas so we prayed about it. The doctor
said I could call my insurance company to see if they would agree to pay
for the drug. It was Christmas Eve and I was sure no one would answer.
But I called the Insurance Company and someone answered but they said
that my company HR department would have to agree to that." Dr. Mike
got the number to the HR Department and again thought no one would
answer on Christmas Eve. Dr. Mike said, "I called my company's HR
Department and a man answered, so I explained that the drug they

wanted to give me was not covered by the insurance and I asked him who
did I need to talk to about getting it on the approved list?" He
stated, "He was the person that approved that." He said, "No one else
was at his office and he had only come in to the office to pick
something up that he had left at work, when the phone rang." He said he
would "Be happy to approve the drug and would stay until he put all the
information in the computer and the

hospital received it." So Dr. Mike and Becky went home on Christmas
Eve to spend Christmas with their family!

After the chemo was finished Dr. Mike had to endure six weeks of
radiation on a daily basis. He was given an hour and half class on how
bad it was going to be. It was an hour and a half of scary, very
negative talk of what to expect. A few days after the radiation started
it became very painful to swallow. The Doctor prescribed some potent
pain medication that is still in the medicine cabinet, unopened!

During the third week the pain disappeared! On the next Doctor's visit,
Dr. Mike asked him "If they had changed the radiation in anyway because
all the pain had gone?" He said "No they had not changed anything and
the pain only gets worse through the rest of the treatment and for a few
weeks thereafter." Dr Mike told him "I am no longer in any pain, can
you explain that." He just shrugged his shoulders in response. Dr. Mike
said, "I told him that I had a lot of people praying for me." He had
no comment! The radiation continued for the remainder of the six weeks
with no pain and no pain medication.

A few weeks after all treatment was completed Dr. Mike was put through a
battery of tests including a CT scan. When they met with the doctor she
told them what they already knew, he was CANCER FREE! The radiology
Doctor said, "I am going to make you my radiation poster child because
of how well you did with the radiation." Dr Mike told her that he "Had
a lot of people praying for him!" This was a different doctor, but no
comment.

Dr. Mike said, "Its hard to put into words all the changes in my life
and my faith, that the adversity from this has brought about. It has
made me realize that you need to enjoy the time you have because you
have no assurance what tomorrow will bring. This was a very rare type

cancer; of more than 300 million people

in the United States, there are less than 1,000 cases of this
particular cancer per year. So I'm not sure how confident the doctors
were in the outcome of their treatment but I was very confident! I am
less worried about my life here and more confident of the life to come.
My timetable is timed by a stop-watch but God uses a sun dial for his
timing, so I am more patient when it comes to waiting for an answer from
God. I more fully understand the scripture, Romans 8:28 "And we know
that all things work together for good to them that love God, to them
who are called according to his purpose."

Dr. Mike concluded his testimony by saying, "I made it through all the
treatment because I could trust God to be faithful and with me the whole
way. Also a big part was my wife and family being there to help lift me
up."

ANDREW & TINA LAX 

For four years, Andrew and Tina owned a small business in Pearland.  At
the beginning, they were open seven days a week, and the store struggled
financially.  After about a year and a half, they closed the store
Sunday's, started attending church and started tithing.  Around this
time, their business really started to take off and do well.  While it
had its up's and down's, overall the business was doing well.  They were
asked to speak at a conference in Florida on how they had grown their
business so significantly. At about this time they were given the
business next door for free. 

Andrew said, "When we became successful and took over the business next
door, we started doing things on our own.  For a while, we stopped going
to church, opened on Sunday's and borrowed money to pay our tithe."

 

As one might imagine during the next year things started to go down hill
financially very quickly for them.  Sales dropped and expenses increased
dramatically.  This whole time, they were wondering why?  They said,
"God, why are you doing this?  Why are you letting this happen to us? 
We love you and are saved so why is this happening?  It literally
brought us to our knees, we were praying nightly for God to save our
store and start blessing us again.  Looking back on this, we were
praying, but did not open our ears to listen to what He was saying. 
Towards the end of that year, Pastor Nordin came by the store to talk to
Tina, and asked us to go back to church.  We started going Sunday
mornings, but did not go Sunday nights or Wednesday's, and still were
borrowing money to pay our tithe.  We were behind on our rent, and
shortly before Christmas, 2009, we decided enough is enough and closed
the store. We then started to attend church regularly and pay our tithe
from our money, not the banks.  We were also receiving multiple
collection calls and letters from the various banks who loaned us
money.  Considering we were in debt for more than \$100,000 we decided
to ignore the calls.  We continued to pray, asking God to deliver us
from this financial mess we got ourselves into.  While we had the stress
of the debt hanging over us, we were unbelievably a lot happier.  We
were able to spend time together as a family, spend time with God and go
to church.  These were things we were not really able to do while we had
the store.  Later that year, we were forced to file bankruptcy.  While I
understand this is hard on anyone, being a CPA, I took it harder than
most, God showed me who was in charge!  Surprisingly, the filing was
painless and went very smoothly. Through this adversity, we are so much
better off. We rediscovered that God is our true source, He has since
blessed us spiritually, blessed us with two wonderful teenage daughters,
improved our relationship with each other, increased our finances,
provided us with an all expense paid trip to the Bahamas and provided my
daughter with a brand new car that was paid for by someone else."  

Andrew and Tina now spend time with God daily; attend church on Sunday
mornings and evenings; attend church on Wednesday evenings; are involved
in ministry here at CT Church and spend quality time together as a
family, which they did not do when they had the store. 

They said, "We became extremely happy when we realized God is our
source, and not us.  We could not do this alone, and do not want to do
this alone."

RICKY & VICKI LEE

In February 2011 Ricky and Vicki Lee's daughter and son-in-law, Tiffany
and Johnnie bought their first home in Trussville, AL where they lived
with their son two year old son, Brodie.  They were blessed to get a HUD
foreclosure that was built in the late 80\'s but had been newly
remodeled. The home was situated on about an acre of land.

The home featured three bedrooms, two baths, a large playroom and a
basement. They were able to purchase the home at a ridiculously low
price.

On January 23rd 2012 around 3:00 a.m. tornado warnings were rampant and
the tornadoes were heading straight toward this young family. Tiffany
called her mom and dad to begin praying, as the storms were due there in
about an hour.  Ricky and Vicki began praying fervently.  

Tiffany, Johnnie and Brodie went downstairs and got under the stairwell
closet. Vicki said, "We stayed on the phone, checking the tracking of
the storm and realizing it was headed directly at our family, we were
steadily praying."  

Just as predicted, about one hour later the storm hit and the phone went
dead. Vicki said, "It was then that I received a text from our younger
daughter who was tracking the storm and she said the tornado is hitting
them NOW." Vicki said, "I can\'t describe the feeling that came over
me when that phone went dead."  

In desperation, Ricky and Vicki prayed fervently in the spirit and with
their understanding that God would spare their children.  They said they
remember praying specifically asking God to "Put angels over that roof
so the roof would not blow away."  

After five to seven minutes the phone rang and it was Tiffany, they were
alive but she was screaming! Johnnie got on the phone and let Ricky and
Vicki know "Everyone had survived and they were alright."

Tiffany said, "We couldn\'t all fit under the stairwell and close the
door. I put my body over the baby and Johnnie put his body over both of
us with half of his body outside the closet." Johnnie braced himself as
best as he could in an attempt to hold the mattress over his wife and
son.  

Tiffany said, "The wind was so strong

It was very difficult to hold on to Brodie, even using both arms. The
wind was pulling and

Pulling but I prayed in the spirit the entire time and only once spoke
in English, and that to say, 'wind be still.'" Brodie who was two later
recounted this to Vicki saying, "Mommy said, 'wind be still' and the
wind stopped."

Because it was dark outside it was not possible to see how much damage
had been done until the sun came up. The next morning when the damage
could be assessed they discovered: (PUT UP PICTURES OF THE HOME)

- The entire left side of the basement was destroyed...

- One column of brick were left holding up one entire side of the
 house...

- The roof had been lifted off and sat back down... (Remember the
 earnest prayer about the roof not coming off)

- Every tree was gone...

- Every shrub had disappeared...

- Brodie's wooden play-set that had been concreted into the ground was
 nowhere to be found...

Ricky and Vicki said, "It looked like a bomb had gone off. There were
holes in the roof, water everywhere, the gas main was leaking, and
debris was everywhere."

The home was barely standing, so contractors were called in to stabilize
the home so that certain valuables could be salvaged. The home was
bulldozed to the ground the next day.

Ricky and Vicki said, "Here\'s what is so amazing. Where the tornado
hit, it stopped as if a line had been drawn on the ground. The basement
was completely destroyed but the wall for the closet where Tiffany,
Johnnie and Brodie were was left standing, untouched!"

Tiffany later noticed that "Even the shelf on the

top of the closet didn\'t fall.  It was as if an angel of the Lord
stood there and the storm could not go any farther."

Miraculously, this family made it out alive and not a scratch on them!
To God be the Glory!  

Because they were well covered with insurance, one month later, they
moved into their new home that is larger, with four bedrooms, three full
baths, an extra large basement with poured

cement walls and only 1 year old.

After insurance claims were paid, they were left with a mortgage of
around \$13,000 and still have their lot to sell.  When the lot sells
they will quite possibly have a one-year-old home, debt free! Not bad
for 24 year old kids!

When the family was moving into their new home Brodie told Ricky and
Vicki "Thank you Jesus for my new home."  Vicki told him that he was
right for thanking Jesus and he said, "Do you know what Jesus said Nan

Nan?" She asked him what Jesus said and he replied, "You are
Welcome."
