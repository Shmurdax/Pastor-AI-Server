communicating the CT vision with everyone connected to CT Church.

## I. BIBLICAL INTEGRITY -- "Do it with the right heart."

2 Timothy 4:1-5 (NKJV) "I charge you therefore before God and the
Lord Jesus Christ, who will judge the living and the dead at His
appearing and His kingdom: 2 Preach the word! Be ready in season and out
of season. Convince, rebuke, exhort, with all longsuffering and
teaching. 3 For the time will come when they will not endure sound
doctrine, but according to their own desires, because they have itching
ears, they will heap up for themselves teachers; 4 and they will turn
their ears away from the truth, and be turned aside to fables. 5 But you
be watchful in all things, endure afflictions, do the work of an
evangelist, fulfill your ministry."

Paul knew that after his death, his understudy, Timothy, was going to
face the temptation to go off the tracks theologically.

Paul's concern was that the culture would slowly, but surely; cause
Timothy to depart from sound Biblical teaching. It is an absolute fact
that the culture is a constant pull on church leaders. The church was
intended to create the culture rather than being affected by the
culture. Rather than allowing the culture to dictate what the church can
and cannot preach, the church must embrace a philosophy of relevance
without compromise:

- The church cannot ignore [shifts]{.underline} in the culture...

- The church must [adapt]{.underline} its [methods]{.underline} to
 attract the culture...

- The church must NEVER allow the culture to change the
 [message]{.underline} we [preach]{.underline}...

Some of the way's we have accomplished this at CT Church:

- We have gone outward rather than inward... (The culture does not feel
 the need to go to church as other cultures have -- So we must go to
 them)

- We moved discipleship from Sunday morning to Wednesday night... (The
 culture stopped attending Sunday School at 9:45 on Sunday morning but
 would attend on Wednesday night)

- We stopped having Sunday night services and started having life
 groups... (We were having 100-150 attendees on Sunday night -- We now
 have more than 400 attending life groups)

We have done all this, and it has been VERY effective, yet, we have not
compromised the message we preach or the doctrine we believe.

How do we assure Biblical Integrity at CT church?

- We are not open to changing our [doctrine]{.underline} for anyone...

- We [demand]{.underline} to know what each teacher and life group
 leader is teaching...

- Everything preached and taught in this church must line up with the
 [doctrine]{.underline} we espouse...

Biblical Integrity has much to do with preaching and teaching sound
doctrine but it is also much more. Biblical integrity has much to do
with making sure that with all our doing, we ["Do it with the right
heart."]{.underline} Ecclesiastes 9:10 (NKJV) "Whatever your hand
finds to do, do it with your might; for there is no work or device or
knowledge or wisdom in the grave where you are going."

To be completely true to our core value of Biblical Integrity we must
make sure that we do everything:

- For the right [reason]{.underline}... Motives must constantly be
 examined. This Scripture is all about motive

- With the right [passion]{.underline}... We must be passionate about
 everything we do. Every person must work with passion every time --
 "Do it with your might."

- With a sense of [urgency]{.underline}... Every chance we have for
 ministry should be approached with a sense of urgency -- "Ministry
 cannot be accomplished in the grave that you are going to."

In an effort to fulfill this CT Core Value each of us must stay in the
[lane]{.underline} of being true to our doctrine and working with a
sense of [urgency]{.underline}! Say it with me... Biblical Integrity
-- "Do it for the right reason."

## II. RIDICULOUS STEWARDSHIP -- "Do it in the right way."

Steward -- "A person who [manages]{.underline} another\'s property or
financial affairs; one who [administers]{.underline} anything as the
agent of another or others." (dictionary.com)

Stewardship -- "The responsible [overseeing]{.underline} and
[protection]{.underline} of something considered worth caring for and
preserving." (dictionary.com)

When the leadership of CT Church was walking through the process of
developing our Core Values we decided that stewardship should be at or
near the top of our list. Not only did we believe that we should espouse
stewardship but we should practice and demand Ridiculous Stewardship. It
is one thing to be a steward but it is a totally different thing to
practice it in a [ridiculous manner]{.underline}.

We are told in the Word that our Christian duty is to be faithful as a
steward. 1 Corinthians 4:1-5 (NKJV) "Let a man so consider us, as
servants of Christ and stewards of the mysteries of God. 2 Moreover it
is required in stewards that one be found faithful. 3 But with me it is
a very small thing that I should be judged by you or by a human
court.\[a\] In fact, I do not even judge myself. 4 For I know of nothing
against myself, yet I am not justified by this; but He who judges me is
the Lord. 5 Therefore judge nothing before the time, until the Lord
comes, who will both bring to light the hidden things of darkness and
reveal the counsels of the hearts. Then each one's praise will come from
God." Paul lays out the case for stewardship to the Corinthian
Church:

As followers of Christ we are "Stewards of the mysteries of God."
-- We are not just another social organization, we are the
[church]{.underline} and as such we are the ones who possess the answers
to [all]{.underline} the world's problems! Therefore, we should not just
practice stewardship we should practice Ridiculous Stewardship!

Stewards are "REQUIRED to be found FAITHFUL."

- Required = Greek -- Zhtew (Zay-tay-O) -- An [affinity]{.underline} to
 seek to plot about a desire or endeavor. This means that we are going
 to be held accountable for the assignment we have been given in the
 Kingdom. We have already established that we are all stewards -- this
 is not just another assignment -- this is a Kingdom assignment and we
 are "REQUIRED" to do it right!

- Faithful = Greek -- Pistoj (Pis-tos') -- To be objectively and
 subjectively [trustworthy]{.underline}. In other words we are to be
 the kinds of stewards that do not need much oversight to perform our
 responsibilities. But when oversight comes we continue performing at
 an extremely high level rather than resisting the oversight!

In the context of CT Church what areas are we required to exercise
Ridiculous Stewardship?

- [Prayer]{.underline}... Don't just pray -- Live in an attitude of
 prayer! "Praying always, with all prayer and supplication..."

- The [Word]{.underline}... Don't just read your Bible study it!
 "Study to show yourself approved..."

- [Relationships]{.underline}... Don't just show yourself friendly -- Be
 extremely friendly! "He that has friends must show himself
 friendly..." Host or attend a life group.

- [Serving]{.underline}... Each CT'er should have a ministry to the Lord
 (worship), a ministry within the church (Where are you serving?), a
 ministry to the community (What outreach are you involved in?), and a
 ministry to the world! (Be a missions supporter -- Give & Go!)
 "Whatever your hand finds to do..."

- Caring for [assets]{.underline}... Part of stewardship is taking great
 care of what you already have. If we cannot take care of what we
 already have, why would God want to give us more? Treat all CT assets
 better than you would treat your own -- Take ownership not
 proprietorship -- Pick up trash etc -- Never say, "It's not my job!"
 Practice the golden rule - "Do unto others (Stuff) as you
 would have them to do unto your (Stuff)..." If you want to
 borrow something, notify the proper people... Bring it back... Bring
 it back in at least as good condition as you received it...

- [Giving]{.underline}... If you are going to work in the union, you
 have to pay union dues -- If you are going to be involved at CT Church
 we EXPECT your minimum financial commitment to be 10% of your gross
 family income! When you tithe the devourer is rebuked! When you give
 above your tithe you are enacting the spirit of multiplication! Do you
 want addition or multiplication in your finances? Are you going to be
 a steward or practice Ridiculous Stewardship? Four things we EXPECT
 you to do financially -- Tithe (10% of your gross family income) --
 Give to missions monthly -- Participate in H H -- Participate in
 Harvest Sunday) "The generous person shall be made fat..." "The
 person who hoards tends to poverty... The person who scatters
 (Gives) will be given a harvest."

You may say, "This is a ridiculous expectation" and you would be
right! Our Core Value is [Ridiculous Stewardship]{.underline}!

Here at CT we are going to practice Ridiculous Stewardship -- We are
committed to "Do it the right way!"

We are not talking here just about dependability -- we are talking about
Absolute Dependability! We are not talking about a 50/50 chance that we

110% chance we can be depended on to do what we said we would do!

## V. EXTREME EXCELLENCE -- "Do it well"

Ecclesiastes 9:10 (NKJV) "Whatever your hand finds to do, do it with
your might; for there is no work or device or knowledge or wisdom in the
grave where you are going."

This passage tells us several things:

- Make sure your [hand]{.underline} finds something to do... We should
 all find something we can do and do it well. No free loaders or
 deadbeats allowed!

- Do it with your [might]{.underline}... We are looking for passionate
 people who are tired of passivity. Passivity is the neutering of the
 church. For those committed to Extreme Excellence and doing it well,
 there is no place for passivity.

- Whatever we do, we should do it [well]{.underline}... We must feed the
 passion of ministry every day if we expect to maintain enough momentum
 to do things well.

- The day will come when we will not be able to do what we
 [want]{.underline}... "Work while it is day for the night is coming
 when no one can work."

In light of this, think through this core value:

- Extreme = Going to the [utmost]{.underline} or very great lengths in
 action, habit, opinion, etc...

- Excellence = The fact or state of [superiority]{.underline} or
 [eminence]{.underline}...

(Dictionary.com)

Excuses are the enemy of Extreme Excellence and doing things well.

To be Extremely Excellent we must stop with the excuses for not "Doing
it (Whatever we have committed to do) well!"

Excuses are the [archenemy]{.underline} of Extreme Excellence! Excuses
render the people with the best motives far less than Excellent! Lack of
excellence is a momentum killer. Momentum is very difficult to create

organization or group.

Momentum is very difficult to create and even more difficult to
[recreate]{.underline}. Making allowances for people who kill momentum

possess the Promised Land we soon discover the fatal sin of
half-heartedness. The very reason for forty years spent wandering around

This Scripture deals exclusively with the sin of half-heartedness.
Half-heartedness is simply a failure to pour ones best effort into a
thing. In spiritual terms, half-heartedness is a stubborn refusal to
wholly follow God!

In this Scripture Moses is telling the tribes of Reuben and Gad that
their sin was one of half-heartedness. They were requesting to stay on

SERVANTHOOD:

Matthew 8:5-10 (NKJV) "Now when Jesus had entered Capernaum, a
centurion came to Him, pleading with Him, ^6 ^saying, "Lord, my servant
is lying at home paralyzed, dreadfully tormented." ^7 ^And Jesus

In this passage of scripture, we have one of the greatest lessons of

The centurion was a man of great influence and when he approached Jesus,
his request was not for himself but for his servant. "Now when Jesus
had entered Capernaum, a centurion came to Him, pleading with Him,
^6 ^saying, "Lord, my servant is lying at home paralyzed, dreadfully
tormented." Servant leadership is always about others; it is never
about us.

We should ask ourselves the following questions to determine whether our
service is really producing Radical Respect:

- Are we serving to make ourselves feel good or to [benefit]{.underline}
 someone else?

- Are we serving because we [love]{.underline} these people or because
 God [loves]{.underline} them? If we serve honorable with Radical
 Respect we will find ourselves needing to serve people that we have a
 hard time loving.

- Are we serving to be [seen]{.underline} of man or of
 [God]{.underline}?

- Are we serving for a [temporal]{.underline} or [eternal]{.underline}
 reward?

Because of Radical Respect, the centurion placed his life on hold and
laid aside his very important responsibilities to pursue Jesus on behalf

Radical Respect demands that when we are going to serve in an area, we
should do so as a 100%'er! What does 100% look like?

- Serve when we [feel]{.underline} like it...

- Serve when we [don't feel]{.underline} like it...

- Be where we are [supposed]{.underline} to be when we are
 [supposed]{.underline} to be there...

- Serve with a [good attitu]{.underline}de... (If you serve and then
 complain, you might as well not have served at all)

A person committed to serving with Radical Respect always adds value to

ALIGNMENT:

The centurion understood the value of alignment. "Lord, I am not

servant will be healed. For I also am a man under authority..." The
centurion seemed to understand three things here that I find to be
profound:

- He understood that it was not the touch of Jesus that was bringing the
 miracles about; it was the [word]{.underline} of Jesus...

- He realized that Jesus was not doing these things on His own, but was
 doing them as the result of a much higher [authority]{.underline}...
 "I also am a man [under]{.underline} authority."

- He understood that when a person comes [under authority]{.underline},
 they have the support of those above them... If we refuse to come
 under authority we will NEVER have the full support of those above us
 and therefore cannot be in authority!

The centurion valued the power of being in alignment. Think with me
about alignment:

- Your car needs alignment periodically...

- If the spine is out of alignment, nothing else works right...

- To have a line means someone is in the front and someone is in the
 back - alignment must occur between those two points...

Those who serve with Radical Respect and Honor always:

- Stay in [alignment]{.underline}...

- Follow [protocols]{.underline}...

- Do not [over-step]{.underline}...

- Do not [under-step]{.underline}...

- Do not [side-step]{.underline}...
