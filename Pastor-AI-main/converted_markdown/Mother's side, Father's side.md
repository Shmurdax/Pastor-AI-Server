Galatians 4:4

But when the fullness of time was come, God sent forth his Son, made of
a woman, made under the law. He was God\'s Son, but he was Mary\'s baby.

A child was born, but a Son was given. Isaiah 9:6

The Lion became a Lamb.

The Strong became weak.

The Ancient of Days became an infant.

The Omnipotent became a dependant life to be fed and clothed and
nourished by human hands.

He placed himself in the hands of man, that man might place himself in
the hands of God.

The Heavenly Jewel - the holy, harmless, undefiled, sinless, immaculate
Son of God suffered the cruelty, abuse, and misunderstanding of those He
came to help.

Here is the governor carrying an armload of pardons to the prison house,
but is waylaid and assaulted by the ones he came to set free. Oh, the
insanity of sin!

Let us question Jesus about His life:

Q. Do you ever meet strangers?

A. Now on which side are you referring to?

On my mother\'s side, I meet many strangers, while on my Father\'s side
I met several people in the Old Testament record.

I met Hagar and her son in the wilderness.

I met Abraham sitting in his tent door.

I met Jacob in the field one night.

I spoke to Moses out of the burning bush.

I stroked the vicious lions and calmed their resistive nature when
Daniel was in their den.

I robbed the fiery furnace of its heat and held a fellowship meeting
with the Hebrew children.

I met Joshua as the Captain of the Lord of Hosts.

In fact, on my Father\'s side there is nothing hidden from my eyes.

Q. How old are you?

A. Do you mean on my mother\'s side or my Father\'s side?

On my mother\'s side I am twelve, but on my Father's side I am older
than Abraham. In fact, he rejoiced to see my day and saw it and was
glad. I am Alpha and Omega on my Father side.

Q. Do you ever get hungry?

A. On my mother\'s side or my Father\'s side? Now on my mother's side
you would never realize how hungry I get at times. That's on my
mother\'s side. But on my Father's side, I have meat to eat that ye know
not of. On my Father\'s side if I were hungry, I would not tell you. On
my Father\'s side, I multiply bread in abundance.

Q. Do you get thirsty?

A. On my mother\'s side, I get very thirsty. I even begged a drink of
water at Jacob's well. But on my Father's side, I have that living water
which I drink from and never thirst. In fact, on my Father\'s side, I
created the ocean and the seas.

Q. Do you obey your parents?

A. Well, on my mother's side I am subject to my parents. But on my
Father\'s side, I am above every name that is named.

Q. What kind of transportation do you have?

A. Well, on my mother's side I ride a donkey, but on my Father\'s side I
ride on the wings of the wind (Psalm 18:10) and I have 20,000 chariots.

Q. Do you need a light at night?

A. Now on my mother\'s side I use a lamp or candle. But on my Father\'s
side I put darkness under my feet. (Psalm 18:9) In fact, the darkness
and the light are both alike unto me.

Q. Are you limited by time and space?

A. Yes, on my mother\'s side only. I can only be in one place at a time,
but on my Father\'s side I am everywhere present at the same

Q. How old were you when you entered the ministry?

A. On my mother\'s side I was 30. On my Father's side, I was here when
the morning stars sang together and the sons of God shouted for joy.

Q. How old were you when you healed the sick and saved the sinners?

On my mother\'s side, I began when I was 30. On my Father\'s side, I was
the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world. I was the antidote for
the poison, safety net for tempted men.

Q. Are you always gentle?

A. Well, on my mother\'s side I am like a lamb being led to the
slaughter. But on my Father\'s side I am the Lion of the tribe of Judah.
On my mother's side I tolerate injustice and aggression. But on my
Father\'s side I tread the wine press of the wrath of God. I sink
navies, cause nations to stack their arms, and break mighty armies
across my knees.

Q. Are you weak?

A. On my mother\'s side I do not raise a hand in my defense, while on my
Father\'s side I can call down 12 legions of angels to my rescue.

Q. Are you affected by human tragedies?

A. Yes. On my mother\'s side, I wept at Lazarus' tomb. But on my
Father\'s side I called him back to life again. I wept over Jerusalem. I
am sorrowful when I see the injustice of the Pharisees, etc. But on my
Father\'s side I realize the triumph of the wicked is short; while they
spread themselves like a green bay tree, I know that the wicked shall be
turned into hell and all nations that forget God.

Q. Do you ever have a need?

A. Now on my mother\'s side I had need of a donkey to ride into
Jerusalem, but on my Father\'s side I own the cattle on a thousand
hills. The earth is mine and the fullness thereof.

Q. Have you made any funeral arrangements?

A. Yes, on my mother\'s side I\'ll be buried in a borrowed tomb. Now on
my Father's side it is quite a different story. You see, on His side,
death is a thing of the past. I am the resurrection. I am alive
forevermore.
