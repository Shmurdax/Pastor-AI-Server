> FASTING

In a culture which focuses upon the sensual, fasting is a subject which
is not very high on the list of talking points among Christians. Even
though this is an almost forgotten discipline fasting is a Scriptural
principle. One hundred sixty times the Bible references the word fasting
or some variation of it.

The word fast means "one who has not eaten, one who is empty." Our
word "breakfast" simply means to break our time of fasting between the
evening meal and morning meal. Sad to say, but that is about as much
fasting as most Christians ever do.

People point to all kinds of excuses for not practicing the principle of
fasting: Fasting makes me sick; I am not physically strong enough to
fast; my schedule will not allow for fasting; fasting is the
responsibility of church leaders. None of these excuses are a good
reason for neglecting the discipline of fasting. My prayer is that
before you finish this article you will see such benefit in this
spiritual discipline that you will be motivated to participate at some
level.

Fasting is one of the most fruitful and productive things you can do.

FASTING IN THE OLD TESTAMENT AND THE NEW TESTAMENT...

In Judaism the Day of Atonement was a time of complete fasting, this
meant no food or water for twenty four hours. This was the only
prescribed time of fasting and it was intended to be a day of national
repentance. During the Day of Atonement the participants were to refrain
from all labor. If one decided not to fast on this particular day the
punishment was death. I suppose this would motivate most of us to make
an effort to comply!

The Old Testament employs the words "afflict, sanctify, and
consecrate" when describing the discipline of fasting. When you read
that a person "afflicted, sanctified or consecrated themselves," the
Scripture can certainly be implying that they fasted.

Moses fasted for a period of forty day's on two separate occasions. The
result: With His very own finger God carved out ten moral laws for the
Children of Israel and the human family to live by. He did this in stone
and within full view of Moses! The Children of Israel were instructed
repeatedly to exercise the discipline of fasting. King David fasted for
the healing of his son. Ahab fasted; the servants of Saul fasted; the
prophet Ezra fasted; Nehemiah fasted until he received approval to
rebuild the walls of Jerusalem; Mordecai fasted for the preservation of
the Jewish race; Zechariah and the priests fasted; The prophet Daniel
fasted for 21 days before receiving his answer from the Lord; the
prophet Joel fasted. All these individuals benefited greatly from this
very important discipline.

History tells us; during the time between the Old Testament and the New
Testament fasting became a Jewish characteristic or mark.

Fasting in the New Testament is extremely obvious as well. The Pharisees
fasted; Jesus fasted in preparation for public ministry; Anna fasted;
Cornelius fasted resulting in the Apostle Peters visit to his home and
of course the outpouring of the holy Spirit upon the Gentile race; the
Apostles fasted and the first missionaries were selected and sent by the
church; The Apostle Paul practiced the discipline of fasting as well.

Fasting is both an Old Testament and New Testament principle and
activity. If fasting is a New Testament principle it is certainly worthy
of the consideration of every Christian.

WHY FAST?

I am going to make a statement which may shock the casual reader but if
you will hear me out I think you will agree that it is a Biblically
based statement which flies in the face of all we have ever imagined
fasting to be. FASTING WILL NOT PERSUADE GOD TO DO ANYTHING HE WOULD NOT
ORDINARILY DO. It is my opinion that if a person fasts to convince God
to do a miracle they are wasting their time. God already desires to give
good things to His children. (Matthew 7:11, 2 Chronicles 16:9

If fasting does not convince God to do anything He would not ordinarily
do; why fast? We should fast because the words of Jesus implore us to do
so. When the Pharisees asked why Jesus' disciples were not in the habit
of fasting Jesus simply told them that when He was taken from their
presence they would then need to fast. (Matthew 9:14-15)

Fasting is the duty of every Christian. In Matthew 6:16 Jesus gives
special instructions regarding this spiritual discipline and he say's
"When you fast," notice this not "If you fast" but "When you fast." It
is the duty of each of Gods children to do so.

Fasting crucifies the flesh. The flesh is the enemy of faith and
therefore it is the enemy of God. We cannot allow the flesh to become
dominant over the spirit within us and expect to maintain our
relationship with the Lord or our own integrity. The way we keep this
from happening is crucifying the flesh and feeding the spiritual man.

Fasting humbles the believer before God. Fast a few days and you will
find yourself more humble than ever before. We live in such an arrogant,
self-reliant culture that there is a constant battle going on with each
of us to exercise Godly humility. Fasting is the one discipline which
can accomplish this.

Fasting teaches the believer dependence upon God as his source. If we do
not keep our guard up we can fall into the trap of self-sufficiency but
God desires for us to look to Him as our source. As a minister it is a
short step to be conditioned to believe our church is our source but
this is not true. I began preaching at the age of fourteen and accepted
my first pastorate when I was nineteen. One day when I was in my
mid-twenties God spoke to me and let me know He wanted me to look to Him
as my source and that if I would take this posture I would never have a
need He would not supply. I believed what He said and He has been
providing for me for the past twenty-seven years! He is a tremendous
provider! He spoke this to me when I was pastoring a church of less than
twenty persons and I now pastor a church of more than 1200.

Fasting expresses submission to God. Submission to God helps us
appreciate the need for self-discipline. In teaching His disciples to
pray Jesus commanded them and us to pray "Your kingdom come. Your
will be done On earth as it is in heaven." Matthew 6:10 (NKJV)

Fasting prepares us to receive revelation from God. This is one of the
most important purposes of fasting. Moses, David, Nehemiah and Daniel
all received revelation from God in response to a fast.

Fasting brings victory! To be Christ like is a life of victory. Doubt is
of the flesh, refusal to crucify the flesh brings defeat. When we
crucify the flesh we have victory.

WHEN TO FAST

The timing of a fast is probably as crucial as the decision to fast. We
are not to fast just for the exercise of it, we are to fast in an
attempt to crucify the flesh and move closer to God.

There are times when a believer feels a special need to draw closer to
the Lord. This is the Spirit moving within our hearts letting us know
that we need to do something out of the ordinary to meet God on His
turf. James 4:5-10 (KJV) The best way to get on God's turf is to crucify
the flesh; the most effective method of crucifying the flesh is through
fasting. Galatians 5:16-26 (CEV)

We should fast:

When God lays a fast on our hearts... We should be sensitive to what the
Spirit is saying. If we are constantly feeding the flesh and starving
the spirit we will not feel the need to fast. Recently I was watching
the Animal Channel and the animal control officers were arresting a man
who was walking a bull dog. The man was as healthy looking as could be
but you could count all of the dog's ribs from fifty yards away. When I
witnessed this the Holy Spirit said to me, this is the problem with many
Christians they have starved the Spirit and over-nourished the flesh
then they're disillusioned when they lose a battle.

When our prayer life needs to be strengthened... Fasting will enhance
your prayer life like you wouldn't believe. It is in this setting you
can sense the nearness of His presence. (Over the years some church
members have done wonders for my prayer life but a fast will do what a
church member can never do!

When we need to prepare ourselves to receive God\'s message... This is
probably one of the greatest benefits of fasting. Oftentimes it is
difficult to hear from God during the fast. Fasting is a crucifixion of
the flesh and when you are crucifying something all you can hear is its
screams and protests; this racket will drown out everything else for a
few days. In the latter part of an extended fast and following the fast
hearing from God is not difficult at all. When Jesus ended His fast the
enemy tempted Him and Jesus took three Scriptures from the book of
Deuteronomy and whipped the devil all over the desert. Listen to how the
Bible records the response of the enemy. Luke 4:13 (KJV) "And when
the devil had ended all the temptation, he departed from him for a
season."

When we become cold in our spirit... Fasting brings a heightened
intimacy. Intimacy requires us to get to know the person we are intimate
with on a more personal level. When you fast you can begin to see God in
a different way.

When we are experiencing crisis in our life... I have made it a practice
not to make major decisions without a minimum of three days fasting.
Several times in twenty- five years of pastoring I have had my mind made
up to do a certain thing a certain way and because of my commitment to
fast before making major decision God was able to help me see things in
a different light and my decisions were changed.

When spiritual leaders call a fast... This is probably the most
difficult fast to embark upon because our response seems more of a
response to man than to God. Throughout the Old Testament spiritual
leaders used their authority to call the people to a fast from time to
time. In the New Testament we are told to submit to authority. (Hebrews
13:17). It is the responsibility of spiritual leaders to know when a
fast should be called. It is the responsibility of the people to submit
to the leader.

Fasting is a very difficult decision to reach but it is probably one of
the most important disciplines in the Christian life.

TYPES OF FASTING

Let\'s take a look at the different types of fasting in the Bible. Many
people feel intimidated or overwhelmed by the thought of going without
food for days on end. There are types of fasting that don\'t involve
such a radical commitment. The Bible gives examples of many different
kinds of fasting. The terms I will use to describe the different types
of fasts are not biblical terms they are terms I have chosen to describe
Biblical methods of fasting. Let's look at them:

Radical fast -- This is a fast that includes neither food nor water.
This is the type of fast which Jesus and Moses went on. This type of
fast can be risky to your health. Make sure God is telling you to embark
upon such an odyssey. I would not recommend this fast for more than a
maximum of three days and then only with a Doctors approval.

Absolute fast -- This is when you consume no food at all and drink only
water. When embarking upon an absolute fast you should make sure God is
calling you to do so and you should know how long the fast is to last.

Partial fast - This fast you deny yourself of only certain food. Maybe
cut out meat, bread and maybe desert. This is a fast that can go on
indefinitely. Daniel, Shadrach, Meshach, Abednego fasted in this way;
They refused to eat the Kings meat, bread, or deserts. The result; they
were healthier than all the Kings men. (Daniel 1:15) This type of fast
can be done by those who have health problems which would restrict them
from a radical fast or an absolute fast.

Intimate fast -- This type of fast can be entered into by couples who
decide to set aside the pleasures of the flesh to seek the Lord for a
specific time period. This should only be done with the consent of both
marriage partners. (1 Corinthians 7:5)

Media fast -- I can find no scripture for this one but many people will
go on a media fast. In a culture which is inundated with media this fast
makes sense.

This type of fast refrains from any type of media exposure for a certain
time period.

In conclusion, you should not go on a radical fast or absolute fast if
you have health problems which would be worsened by abstaining from food
or water.
