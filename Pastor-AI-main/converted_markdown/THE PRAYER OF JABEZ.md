INTRO: The setting of our Scripture is a listing (or chronicle) of
the genealogies of Israel approximately 3000 years ago. If you read the
books of Chronicles it will not take you long to discover why it is
called Chronicles. This book of 1 Chronicles traces the genealogies of
the tribes of Israel from [Adam]{.underline} to the death of
[David]{.underline}.

In this book there are literally hundreds of names most of which seem
pretty insignificant, and most of which the majority of us cannot
accurately pronounce. As a result, most of us who systematically read
the Bible through dread the task of reading this portion of the
Scripture. In fact, most of us only read it so that we can say that we
read the Bible completely through. When I read the Bible through I feel
somewhat of a relief when I have completed this portion of Scripture.

Of all the names mentioned in the first [four]{.underline}
chapters of this book, the writer stops and gives a little explanation
of the life of one man, Jabez, and then in the very next verse the
writer continues to ramble on with names we don't recognize or know how
to pronounce.

You can take this Bible and study it from the Index to the maps and you
will find absolutely zero information on this man named Jabez. In fact
the only other place the name appears in the Word of God is two chapters
earlier (2:55) and then it refers to the name of a village in which some
scribes dwelt. It also let's us know that the Kenites dwelt in Jabez.
The Kenites were a tribe or nation of Canaan.

Jabez comes out of nowhere to appear in Holy Writ, prays one prayer of
33 words, receives an [answer]{.underline} and then fades into
[oblivion]{.underline}.

Jabez, saying, because I bore him in pain." The name Jabez comes from
the Hebrew word yabets (yah-bates'), and literally means to grieve or be
sorrowful. Johnny Cash used to sing about a boy named Sue, but here we
have a boy named grief or sorrow. It would be like naming your child
tragedy or funeral.

Many people have difficult beginnings; poverty; abuse; birth defects
because of alcohol or drug abuse during pregnancy; physical handicaps;
mental handicaps; dysfunctional families; etc...

This story of the life of Jabez starts out [extremely]{.underline}
[bad]{.underline}; In Old Testament time's mothers named their
children for something of significance in their lives. Look at this;
this mother names her son Jabez: He will always be known as grief and
sorrow. Every where he goes, everything he does, when his name is
mentioned people will think of grief or sorrow.

We're not sure why this man's mother dealt him such a name; possibly she
had an extremely difficult pregnancy; it could be that during her
pregnancy some tragic event occurred within her family that would make
both of their lives difficult; I could think of a thousand scenarios.
The fact is we are not sure what happened, but we do know that she set
her son up for [failure]{.underline}. Many young lives have been
set up for [failure]{.underline} they have been told they are
never going to [amount]{.underline} to
[anything]{.underline}. When you do this you are cursing your
children; "The power of life and death is in your tongue." The
power of life and death can also be in your actions and reactions. (My

It doesn't take a person with an I.Q. of 1246 to see that this child has
been set up for failure. One thing in Jabez' favor is that anything he
succeeds at will be a surprise to his mother and everyone else.

Somewhere, somehow Jabez discovered the power of

 would say it rocked his world! I say it rearranged his world! 1
 Chronicles 4:10 (NKJV) "So God granted him what he requested."

## II. TWO KINDS OF BLESSINGS!

1 Chronicles 4:10 (NKJV) "And Jabez called on the God of Israel
saying, 'Oh, that You would bless me indeed, and enlarge my territory,
that Your hand would be with me, and that You would keep me from evil,
that I may not cause pain!' So God granted him what he requested." I
think it almost goes without saying that all of us desire to have the
blessing of the lord upon everything we do. The problem is that most of
us do not understand what the blessing of the Lord really looks like, or
how to receive it. In order to understand what it looks like and how to
receive it we must first understand what it is and is not.

A. BLESSING DEFINED - The word "Bless" means to favor, honor,
or bestow special [gifts]{.underline} or
[status]{.underline}. The Old Testament presses this word into
service almost four hundred times.

In the Bible fathers pronounced blessings on their sons passing along
the family birthright or blessing.

- [Abraham]{.underline} blessed [Isaac]{.underline}

- [Isaac]{.underline} blessed [Jacob]{.underline} &
 [Esau]{.underline}

- [David]{.underline} blessed [Solomon]{.underline}

An earthly father pronouncing a blessing on his children is good and
needful but what we are going to focus on tonight is our Heavenly
Father's favor or blessing upon us! The gifts we receive from him cannot
be earned, and can be received from no other source.

The Bible clearly teaches God is concerned about all that we have need
of; spiritually, physically; emotionally; relationally; and financially.
John 10:10 (NKJV) "The thief does not come except to steal, and to
kill, and to destroy. I have come that they may have life, and that they
may have it more abundantly." The word "Abundantly" as it is
used here comes from the Greek word "Perissos" (per-is-sos') and
means superior. Philippians 4:19 (NKJV) "My God shall supply all your
need according to His riches in glory by Christ Jesus."

I think we could safely conclude that it is the will of our Father to
bestow favor, honor, special gifts and status upon us.

There are two kinds of blessings that I want us to explore tonight:

B. UNCONDITIONAL BLESSING -- Unconditional blessings could also be
called compassionate blessings. An unconditional blessing is a blessing
based on the [nature]{.underline} and
[character]{.underline} of God, He has simply decided to give the
gift with no strings attached.

James 1:17-18 (NKJV) "Every good gift and every perfect gift is from
above, and comes down from the Father of lights, with whom there is no
variation or shadow of turning.18 Of His own will He brought us forth by
the word of truth, that we might be a kind of firstfruits of His
creatures."

I want us to look at some examples of the unconditional blessing of the
Lord:

- [Abraham]{.underline} -- Genesis 12:1-3 (NKJV) "Now the
 [Lord]{.smallcaps} had said to Abram: Get out of your country, from
 your family and from your father's house, to a land that I will show
 you. 2 I will make you a great nation; I will bless you and make your
 name great; and you shall be a blessing. 3 I will bless those who
 bless you, and I will curse him who curses you; and in you all the
 families of the earth shall be blessed."

- [Obed]{.underline}-[Edom]{.underline} - 2 Samuel 6:11
 (NKJV "The ark of the [Lord]{.smallcaps} remained in the house of
 Obed-Edom the Gittite three months. And the [Lord]{.smallcaps} blessed
 Obed-Edom and all his household."

- [Salvation]{.underline} - John 3:16 (NKJV) "For God so loved
 the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in
 Him should not perish but have everlasting life."

An unconditional blessing of the Lord is given with no additional
requirements; for example;

GRACE + NOTHING = [SALVATION]{.underline}

## C. CONDITIONAL BLESSING - On God's part a conditional blessing is
given from the same [free]{.underline} [hearted]{.underline}

8:28-31 (NKJV) "And we know that all things work together for good to

purpose.29 For whom He foreknew, He also predestined to be conformed to

brethren.30 Moreover whom He predestined, these He also called; whom He
called, these He also justified; and whom He justified, these He also
glorified. 31 What then shall we say to these things? If God is for us,
who can be against us?" Ephesians 1:3-7 (NKJV) "Blessed be the God
and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us with every
spiritual blessing in the heavenly places in Christ,4 just as He chose
us in Him before the foundation of the world, that we should be holy and
without blame before Him in love,5 having predestined us to adoption as
sons by Jesus Christ to Himself, according to the good pleasure of His

the forgiveness of sins, according to the riches of His grace."
(Explain predestination; free moral agency; plan of salvation; gifts and
calling without repentance; Romans 11:29)

In the matter of a conditional blessing there are certain things you
must do in order to activate the gift or blessing in your life. It's

Some examples of conditional blessings include:

- Baptism in the [Holy]{.underline} [Spirit]{.underline};
 CONDITIONS -- saved - yielded

transmitted by the nervous system. We are going to look at this in a
very literal sense.

If I hit my finger with a hammer every available warning system in my
body springs into action; my body notifies my nervous system something
has gone terribly wrong; my nervous system notifies my brain that an
injury has occurred and pain has invaded my body; my brain notifies my
defense system and my opposite hand grabs the finger; my whole body
springs into action and prepares to do whatever is necessary to initiate
measures of damage control.

At this point I have a decision to make:

- Employ all available damage control measures

- Take a pain killer and go to bed

If I employ all measures of damage control all my resources become
available to eliminate the pain and begin the healing process. (Family
members & friends; bank accounts, savings accounts & holdings; medical

suffered injury; they have buried, ignored, and denied their pain hoping

disease from spreading. If the disease is not properly dealt with it has
the potential of negatively affecting the entire body and ultimately
causing death.

Miracles occur when the source of pain has been
[identified]{.underline} and [dealt]{.underline} with. A
surgery can be painful but the potential of good health is much greater
after the surgery than before.

Once the source has been dealt with and healing occurs the pain will
disappear and there will be no more need for
[medication]{.underline} or [intoxication]{.underline}.
