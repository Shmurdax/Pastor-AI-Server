# THE VALUE OF AN INDIVIDUAL

TEXT: Luke 15:1-7 "Now the tax collectors and "sinners" were
all gathering around to hear him. But the Pharisees and the teachers
of the law muttered, "This man welcomes sinners and eats with them."
Then Jesus told them this parable: "Suppose one of you has a
hundred sheep and [loses one of them]{.underline}. Does he not [leave
the ninety-nine in the open country and go after the lost sheep until he
finds it?]{.underline} And when he finds it, he [joyfully puts it on
his shoulders]{.underline} and goes home. Then he [calls his friends
and neighbors together and says, \'Rejoice with me; I have found my lost
sheep]{.underline}.\' I tell you that in the same way [there will be
more rejoicing in heaven over one sinner who repents]{.underline} than
over ninety-nine righteous persons who do not need to repent."

INTRO: In our society everything is based on numbers; How much money
a person has, how high or low the stock market is, the gross sales of a
company, the population of a city, and on and on I could go!

This group mentality has even found it's way into the Church. We have
judged ourselves on how big or small the crowd is! How many our group
has in it! How large our staff is! How many people came to the
evangelistic crusade! How many joined the Church! How many were
baptised! How big the budget is! How many total dollars were given to
missions! etc...

The question must be asked, is God at all interested in our facts and
figures? Is he interested in our numbers? Yes! Several times in the
Bible people were commanded by God to number various things! At other
times they were instructed not to count! The woman counted her coins.
the fisherman counted his fish. The king counted his troops. The farmer
counted his crop. The builder counted the cost. God deemed numbers so
important that he named a book in his Bible "NUMBERS"!

While all of these things are important, our scripture text makes us
aware that nothing is more important than an "INDIVIDUAL"!!!

As a Church we must reach a point where the "IMPORTANCE OF AN
INDIVIDUAL" is non-negotiable! Do we have an easy come, easy go,
attitude with regard to "INDIVIDUALS"?

Nowhere is the priority of the "INDIVIDUAL" more vivid than in the
life and teachings of Christ himself!

## I. SALVATION IS AN INDIVIDUAL EXPERIENCE!

A. 2 Peter 3:9 "The Lord is not slow in keeping his promise,
as some understand slowness. He is patient with you, not wanting anyone
to perish, but everyone to come to repentance."

Here Peter reveals that Jesus and the Father are motivated to see the
salvation of 20 billion people who have called planet Earth their home!
That huge crowd does not even include those who are not yet born!

B. Rev. 3:20 ["Here I am! I stand at the door and knock. If
anyone hears my voice and opens the door, I will come in and eat with
him, and he with me."]{.underline}

Here we are made aware that the incredible of salvation for every person
will never exceed the rate of one "INDIVIDUAL" at a time!

## C. The salvation of the "INDIVIDUAL" drove the entire redemptive
plan, nothing else has ever taken priority!

Salvation is never a group experience! Salvation is for the
"INDIVIDUAL"! If the whole world gets saved today, they will get
saved "ONE AT A TIME"!!!

## II. LOOK AT THE INDIVIDUAL NATURE OF GOD'S PLAN!

A. The "INDIVIDUAL" nature of God's plan overwhelmed the
psalmist David. Psalm 8:3-4 "When I consider your heavens, the
work of your fingers, the moon and the stars, which you have set in
place, [what is man that you are mindful of him, the son of man that
you care for him?"]{.underline}

A survey of the entire Bible reveals that God always prioritizes the
"INDIVIDUAL"!

B. From Melchisedec to Malachi, from Zidon to Zechariah, from
Hilkiah to Haggai, from Zipporah to Zephaniah, from Hagar to Habakkuk,
from Noah to Nahum, from Miriam to Micah, from Jehosophat to Jonah, from
Obed to Obadiah, from Abraham to Amos, from Jochebed to Joel, from
Hezekiah to Hosea, from Deborah to Daniel, from Enoch to Ezekiel, from
Jacob to Jeremiah, from Isaac to Isaiah, God's love singled out each
"INDIVIDUAL"!

## C. Jesus' teaching communicates the centrality of the
"INDIVIDUAL", but his life and ministry made this truth even
clearer;

1) Zacchaeus

2) The Samaritan woman

3) Legion

4) The thief on the cross

It is true that there is strength in numbers, "ONE" can put a
hundred to flight but "TWO" can put ten thousand to flight!

It is also true that "ONE" is enough for every challenge, if
empowered by God!

1) David and Goliath

2) Samson pulled down the house of the Philistines!

3) Shammah defended a pea patch

4) A little boys lunch

CONCLUSION: Notice our text, Luke 15:1-7 "Now the tax
collectors and "sinners" were all gathering around to hear him. But
the Pharisees and the teachers of the law muttered, "This man welcomes
sinners and eats with them." Then Jesus told them this parable:
"Suppose one of you has a hundred sheep and [loses one of
them]{.underline}. Does he not [leave the ninety-nine in the open
country and go after the lost sheep until he finds it?]{.underline}
And when he finds it, he [joyfully puts it on his shoulders and
goes home. Then he calls his friends and neighbors together and says,
\'Rejoice with me; I have found my lost sheep.]{.underline}\' I tell
you that in the same way there will be more rejoicing in heaven over one
sinner who repents than over ninety-nine righteous persons who do not
need to repent."

There are three things here;

1) The shepherd who owns 100 sheep has lost one!

2) Notice the drastic measures to find one lone sheep! "He leaves
99 in the open country and searches until he finds the one lost
sheep!"

3) Notice the celebration! He puts it on his shoulders- He brings it
home- He calls his friends and neighbors together and invites them to
participate in the party!

The most astounding thing about all of this is the words of Jesus in
verse 7, ["I tell you that in the same way there will be more
rejoicing in heaven over one sinner who repents than over ninety-nine
righteous persons who do not need to repent."]{.underline}

Can anyone determine the "VALUE OF AN INDIVIDUAL"?

As a Church we need to focus on the "VALUE OF AN INDIVIDUAL!"
