God placed the Israelites in this particular spot for three very
distinct reasons:

- To give the Egyptians the illusion, the Israelites were trapped...

- To show Israel nothing is too hard for the Lord...

- To give the Israelites the best view of a sea being dried out by the
 nostrils of God... (Sometimes we are inclined to go beyond what God
 has said and get too close to the forest to see the trees!)

Who besides God would think to dry out a sea using a man with an
outstretched hand and an east wind? I would have used an earth quake,
drought, volcano or a tsunami; but God uses the outstretched hand of a
man and an east wind! B. H. Carroll said "There came a mighty wind like
a wedge and split that sea open clear to its center."

The Israelites, held stationary and silent between the sea and the
sword! They watched in unbear­able suspense as an old man by the name of
Moses raised his rod over the imposing sea. An eastern wind suddenly
began to howl from heaven, dividing a sea, the bottom of which had never
before seen the light of day.

This wind began to batter tents and tenants as God set in place one of
the greatest miracles of the Bible. The crowd who was privileged to
witness this act of God was mostly skeptical people. I am quite sure
they watched in disbelief as the downdraft intensified, hit the water,
and slowly plowed through the sea like a mighty turbo charged bulldozer.

The waves rose to unbelievable heights on the right hand and the left
hand, as God provided an avenue of escape for His people, while setting
a trap for the Egyptians. The sea would become a gateway to the one and
a graveyard to the other.

This occurrence became the Old Testament\'s most dramatic object lesson.
The lesson; trust God to deliver in His way, not ours!

When we think of miracles we think of instances like the splitting of
the Red Sea. The Greek word for miracle is "dunamis" and means
"miraculous power or might." Harold Horton defines miracles in the
following ways: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- "A miracle is a supernatural act on the natural plane."

- "Works contrary to nature, accomplishments, so to speak, of the
 impossible, sudden and unaccountable reversals of the order to which
 we are accustomed."

<!-- -->

- "A miracle is a supernatural intervention in the ordinary course of
 nature; a temporary suspension of the accustomed order; an
 interruption of the system of nature as we know it."

- "A miracle is a sovereign act of the Spirit of God irrespective of
 laws or systems."

Based on Harold Horton's definition, we encounter miracles with more
regularity than we might have envisioned. Throughout the Bible, God
takes ordinary circumstances and turns them into miracles. The same is
true today when His children encounter difficult circumstances, He
oftentimes shows up in a subtle but big way! For the Christian, there is
no such thing as luck or coincidence only the divine ordering of our
steps!
