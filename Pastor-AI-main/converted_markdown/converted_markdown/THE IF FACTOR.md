INTRO: I want to talk to you today about:

- One of the greatest enemies of faith...

- One of the greatest deterrents to a miracle...

- One of the most difficult hurdles to overcome...

- One of the most destructive things we deal with in terms of receiving from God...

The IF FACTOR! At its worst the word IF carries the idea of whether or not something could happen; at its best it means supposing that it should happen. Neither option is good in terms of faith in God! Hebrews 11:6 (NKJV) "But without faith it is impossible to please Him, for he who comes to God must believe that He is, and that He is a rewarder of those who diligently seek Him."

I suppose there are several reasons one would ask the question IF? (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Being totally ignorant of God and His unlimited ability in all situations...

- Being overwhelmed by the present circumstances of ones life... (This is where the Dad in this situation seemed to be)

- Being so devastated by feelings of unbelief that one cannot seem to ascertain what the will of the Lord is regarding the situation...

It is the will of God for you to be well and whole. All of His promises are "Yes and amen!"

For starters, I believe it is ALWAYS God's will to heal and perform miracles! Why would Jesus take stripes on his back and then let us keep on being sick or living in lack? If we don't believe that it is the will of God for us to be well, then why do we keep going to the doctor or seeking human help for our problems?

If we do not believe it is God's will for us to be well we should lead a campaign against hospitals and health clinics, counselors and attorneys! We are going to do nothing of the sort because we believe it is Gods will to heal and deliver and He often uses many avenues to do so!

As best as I can count there are one hundred sixty eight separate and specific healings in the Bible not counting the times the Scripture mentions that "He healed all that were oppressed." (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE)

After all the Biblical examples and personal examples we have of a healing, delivering Jesus, we often allow the IF FACTOR to rule the day wondering whether our healing and deliverance is really the will of the Lord!

The way we allow the IF FACTOR to rule the day is by giving more credence to a few examples of people who did not receive their healing or deliverance, than we do to the examples of the Bible, and what the Word has to say on the subject!

If one is to receive a miracle there are some steps of preparation that must be followed.

## I. HOPE IS REQUIRED WHEN DEALING WITH THE IF FACTOR...

I think we could safely say that the father in our text came to Jesus as a last resort. This father had a big problem; a deaf and mute spirit troubled his son. This spirit would manifest itself from time to time by causing the child to go into convulsions; fall on the ground; become stiff as a board; unable to speak or hear! Often this spirit would cast the boy into the fire or the water in an effort to kill him while he was still in this condition!

This child had to have around-the-clock supervision. This father must have been emotionally, physically, and spiritually exhausted! Not to mention the toll this situation had taken on the rest of his family life!

This man had probably tried anything and everything to no avail! He came to Jesus, but even at that, the IF FACTOR was dominant when the conversation began! "If You can do anything, have compassion on us and help..."

Part of the problem here is the lack of hope! A deficiency of hope is the enemy of your miracle! The resurgence of hope is the foundational stone of getting what you need from God! ## II. FAITH MUST TRUMP THE IF FACTOR...

Last week we talked about hope, this week we are going to talk about faith. In order to receive a miracle from the Lord we need both hope and faith working in tandem in our lives. This is not an either/or proposition it is a both/and proposal.

Hope is a dream of something that is either wanted or needed, but faith is what turns the dream into reality. Without hope there is nothing for faith to create substance from and without faith, hope is nothing more than just a powerless dream.

Hebrews 11:3 (NKJV) "By faith we understand that the worlds were framed by the word of God, so that the things which are seen were not made of things which are visible."

Genesis 1:1-3 (NKJV) "In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth. 2 The earth was without form, and void; and darkness was on the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God was hovering over the face of the waters.

3 Then God said, "Let there be light"; and there was light."

These scriptures do not say there was nothing there; they basically say that He made the world out of things where were not apparent! In other words, when He formed the entire universe, He placed all the raw material in place and during the six day's of creation He called that which was invisible, into visible form!

During the six day's of creation, by His Word, God transitioned what we couldn't see into what we could see.

When God formed man He formed him in His image. A person that has put on their hope, and is operating in the will of God, and according to their faith in God, has God's creative power available to him/her! Faith makes visible what hope believed possible!

While hope and faith are inextricably connected to our pursuit of the miraculous, there is a difference between the two:

- Hope is the anticipation of a favorable outcome -- Faith sees the outcome before it is visible!

- Hope lays hold of what one believes God for - Faith morphs it into a thing that can be touched, seen, and held!

- Hope wants the unknown - Faith believes one knows, when there is no way of knowing!

- Hope is wishing something would come to pass -- Faith knows our something will become a reality!

Smith Wigglesworth said, "I am not moved by what I see. I am not moved by what I feel. I am moved only by what I believe!"

If we are going to get our miracle today, we must decide right here and now whether we are going to believe God or continue living according to the IF FACTOR! Mark 9:22 (NKJV) "But if You can do anything, have compassion on us and help..."

The father in our text showed up at the healing crusade Jesus was conducting, operating according to the IF FACTOR!

This kid was not going to get healed or delivered as long as the IF FACTOR was ruling the day! For our miracle to come to pass, faith must rule the day! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE)

Jesus knew He was going to have to raise the faith level for this Dad before the miracle could occur. The New Living Translation frames the words of Jesus in this way. Mark 9:23 (NLT) "What do you mean, 'If I can'?" Jesus asked. "Anything is possible if a person believes."

This Dad enters desperate but doubting.

Desperate doubting people never receive the promises! It is desperate people of faith that receive miracles! Gordon Lindsey said, "Never dig up in unbelief what you have sown in faith!"

Jesus is saying to this dad, "If you will believe me for what you cannot see, I will do what you are hoping for!"

An unknown person said, "Faith is: Dead to doubts, dumb to discouragement, and blind to impossibilities!" (Elaine & Hugh Looney)

The difference between hope and faith is that hope believes God can; faith believes God will! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE)

- Noah took God at His word and saved his family as well as the human race...

- Abraham believed God for a son and became a father of many nations...

- Daniel believed God could take away the appetite of hungry lions, and He did...

- The three Hebrew children believed God could quench the violence of the fire and not a hair was singed...

- Paul believed God could neutralize the venom of a poisonous snake, and faith counteracted the toxin...

- John the Revelator believed God could deactivate the heat of boiling oil, and he did not so much as receive a scar...

Faith is an indispensable element of receiving from the hand of God:

- Hearing the Word initiates faith... "Faith comes by hearing, hearing by the Word of God."

- Speaking the Word activates faith... "Calling those things that are not as though they were."

- Doing the Word demonstrates faith... "Be doers of the Word."

Kenneth Copeland said, "Fear tolerated is faith contaminated."

If our faith can trump the IF FACTOR we can receive our miracle before we exit the building!

Corrie Ten Boon said, "Never be afraid an unknown future to a known God!"

## III. THE MIRACLE MANIFESTS WHEN THE POWER OF HOPE AND FAITH BECOMES GREATER THAN THE POWER OF THE IF FACTOR...

Mark 9:22-24 (NKJV) "If You can do anything, have compassion on us and help us." 23 Jesus said to him, "If you can believe, all things \are\ possible to him who believes." 24 Immediately the father of the child cried out and said with tears, "Lord, I believe; help my unbelief!"

When this dad arrived he was in no condition to receive a miracle! "If you can do anything, have compassion and help us." I am not convinced that this dad's problem resulted from a lack of faith in Jesus' ability, as much as I am persuaded that his doubts stemmed form a deficiency of faith with regard to Jesus willingness to do this for him!

If this man did not believe Jesus could, he would not have attended the meeting! He knew He could, this dad was having a difficult time believing He would!

(Talk about the difference of believing God can and God will...)

Ephesians 3:20 (KJV) "Now unto him that is able to do exceeding abundantly above all that we ask or think, according to the power that worketh in us." (NIV) "Now to him who is able to do immeasurably more than all we ask or imagine -- God is able to do so much more you can't even measure what God is Able to do."

This phrase "Exceeding abundantly" is a fun phrase in the original language. Paul uses a double compound word; in fact, he makes up his own word phrase. He is so caught up in the enormity of God that he creates a new word phrase to make his point. He stacks these words one upon another in an attempt to say,

"God is not just BIG, but He is Big-uhmundo-huge-a-riffic - He is Fan-awe-stical-some -- He is Magni-great-more-big-fully -- He is Super-beyond-erful -- He os Incredi-outrag-i-dible!"

In Ephesians 3:20 Paul is morphing words together to make his point! He is saying that God is:

- Ably able...

<!-- -->

- Amazingly Amazing...

- Awesomely awesome...

- Extravagantly extravagant...

- Fantastically fantastical...

- Indescribably indescribable.

- Infinitely infinite...

- Incredibly incredible...

- Outrageously outrageous...

God always exceeds expectations! With God, we always get more than we expect:

- He didn't just make a single drop of water He created oceans of water...

- He didn't create one lone star (No matter what Texan's think) God masterminded and created galaxies...

- He didn't just design a hill, God made mountain ranges...

- He didn't just make the sun, God designed sunsets and sunrises....

In God's world there are no limits or boundaries:

- There are no boundaries to His blessings...

- There are no containers that can hold His compassion...

- There are no fences withholding His faithfulness...

- There are no guardrails that stand between His grace and us...

- There are no lines drawn separating us from His presence...

- There are no restrictions to experiencing His life...

1 Corinthinas 2:9-10 (NKJV) ""Eye has not seen, nor ear heard, Nor have entered into the heart of man The things which God has prepared for those who love Him." ^10 ^But God has revealed them to us through His Spirit. For the Spirit searches all things, yes, the deep things of God." God can do ANYTHING!

Our lack of hope and faith for miracles stems from:

- Knowing that we are imperfect people... God does not give miracles based on merit!

- Knowing that others may have a greater need than we do... God is not running short on the stuff He makes miracles out of!

- Not believing that God would actually be mindful of such insignificant person's, as we seem to be... We are heirs of God and joint-heirs with Jesus Christ!

Some of us have come here today out of shear desperation. Our questions are not so much whether He can, as they are whether He will do anything on our behalf? God does not respond to fear and desperation, he responds to hope and faith!

We will get nowhere begging, we must come boldly confessing His unequalled power in and over our lives! Hebrews 4:14-16 (NKJV) "Seeing then that we have a great High Priest who has passed through the heavens, Jesus the Son of God, let us hold fast our confession. ^15 ^For we do not have a High Priest who cannot sympathize with our weaknesses, but was in all points tempted as we are, yet without sin. ^16 ^Let us therefore come boldly to the throne of grace, that we may obtain mercy and find grace to help in time of need." (CEV) "We have a great high priest, who has gone into heaven, and he is Jesus the Son of God. That is why we must hold on to what we have said about him. Jesus understands every weakness of ours, because he was tempted in every way that we are. But he did not sin! So whenever we are in need, we should come bravely before the throne of our merciful God. There we will be treated with undeserved kindness, and we will find help."

Jesus say's to the man in our text, "The question is not IF I can, the question is whether you can believe." Really He is saying if you can allow your hope and faith to rise above, what you are knowing and feeling, the miracle can then come to pass!

The Man made a declaration of faith by saying, "Lord, I believe!"

Hope and faith were on the rise but you can bet the flesh will almost always attempt to block the onslaught of hope and faith. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) The man follows this declaration of faith with, "Lord, help my unbelief."

As soon as our hope and faith begins to arise, the IF FACTOR wants to jump back into the drivers seat! Please do not allow that to happen!

God wants you healthy, happy and whole! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE)

I challenge us today to allow hope and faith to become greater than the IF FACTOR!

In March 2008, I received the most devastating news a divorced mother could possibly receive. After being with NASA for over 10 years, on March 10 I received a layoff notice. On that very same day I received a phone call that my baby brother (parents only son) had been murdered. The only way I knew to deal with these emotions was to withdraw from the world, especially the church. I didn't want to be bothered with anybody, even my own children. All I wanted to do was sleep and never wake up.

After a very long absence, I forced myself to start going to church, occasionally. So at least I was getting some Word to strengthen me. Pastor Nordin mentioned on one of those Sundays when I was there, maybe during the offering, the importance of tithing out of our own need. Pastor Nordin's words stayed with me. Although I was only receiving unemployment, I would tithe faithfully, out of any income I received. This is when my obedience kicked in. The only thing is, I wasn't attending church often enough to return my tithes regularly. As a result, I was stashing my tithe in my purse until the next time I went to church. When the kids and I finally decided to attend church again, we went to get in the truck and THERE WAS NO TRUCK. The truck had been repossessed! I went to my room and prayed. I asked God why He allowed them to take my truck as that was the only transportation I had. I reminded the Lord that His Word said that if I returned my tithes He would "rebuke the devourer for my sake and open up the windows of Heaven and pour me out a blessing that I won't have room enough to receive." God said to me, "But you DIDN'T RETURN YOUR TITHES! You hid them in your purse!"

When I brought my tithes to Christian Temple that Sunday, I received a check the next day which was more than enough to cover the delinquency of my truck, and have enough left over to pay some other bills and get things we needed.

After becoming a faithful tither, all sorts of unexpected financial blessings came my way. My friends would randomly drop off \$100 here and there; refund checks from school suddenly appeared without my knowing I was supposed to receive them. I'm still in my house by the grace of God. Now that I have a steady income to a certain extent, I'm able to afford the payments. These blessings are all a direct result of my being a faithful tither and I tithe on EVERYTHING, not just income...birthday gifts and anything monetary that I receive.

My faith is at an unbelievable level right now because of how God has carried me for 4 years!

Right before my surgery a couple of months ago, the enemy tried to throw me off my game. The only income I had at the time was unemployment and they ceased that due to some technical issue.

Pastor Nordin preached on first fruit tithing. On-line tithing helps me to be a first fruit tither. I almost had a heart-attack when I thought one of my other bills had cleared my checking account before my tithes ACH transaction.

I went to the post office the other day and opened up a letter from NASA/USA stating as a result of my being separated from their company, they would be sending me \$33,000 from pension that I didn't even know was there. Because I am not old enough to retire they are releasing the funds to me allowing me to make financial decision with the money for my future. I know that may not sound like a lot of money to most, but I felt like I had won the lottery. I couldn't even finish reading the letter. I had praise and worship right there in the middle of the post office. My sister asked me if I was going to tithe from the \$33,000? I told her "YES!" She asked, "Do you know how much that would be?" I told her "YES!" God has been too good to me for me not to give Him only 10% of what He has blessed me with. He could have asked for 90%; but He only requests 10%.

I claim 2012 as my year of total prosperity. I love you Pastors Don and Susan Nordin and the Christian Temple Family!

Sincerely,

Rosalyn Blakely
