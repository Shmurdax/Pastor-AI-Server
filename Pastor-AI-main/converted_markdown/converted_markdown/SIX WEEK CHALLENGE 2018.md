# SIX-WEEK CHALLENGE

INTRO: We are embarking on a season of new beginnings. It is a new year and we are all trying to get started off on the right foot. Many are no doubt making New Years resolutions.

As we begin this year, I want to offer a challenge to us as a corporate body of believers. I am challenging you to do the following things for the next six weeks: (January 14 -- February 18)

- Fast... Fast one meal a day for six consecutive weeks - or one day a week for six consecutive weeks.

- Read the Word each day for six consecutive weeks... Read at least 3 chapters a day for six consecutive weeks -- Join me for my daily Facebook devotional at 7:15 AM Monday through Friday for six consecutive weeks.

- Tithe 10 % of our total family income for six consecutive weeks... Faithfully tithe on your gross family income for six consecutive weeks.

- Attend church and a life group for six consecutive weeks... Attend the 10 AM Sunday morning service and a life group for six consecutive weeks.

- Serve in a ministry or outreach for six consecutive weeks... Do something to serve your church for six consecutive weeks.

- Reach out to un-churched people for six consecutive weeks... Make a hit list of six un-churched families and invite one of them each week to church and life group with you over a six consecutive week period.

CHALLENGE \# 1 -- FAST A MEAL A DAY FOR SIX CONSECUTIVE WEEKS OR FAST A DAY A WEEK FOR SIX CONSECUTIVE WEEKS...

I am challenging you today to begin a six- week journey with me. I am asking you to do the following from today through February 18, 2018:

- Fast one meal a day every day for six consecutive weeks...

- Or, fast one day a week for six consecutive weeks...

I want to take a few moments and establish a Scriptural basis for why we should practice the discipline of fasting. Fasting is a Scriptural principle. One hundred sixty times the Bible references the word fasting or some variation of it.

The word fast means "one who has not eaten, one who is empty." I call fasting dying! Our word "breakfast" simply means to break our time of fasting between the evening meal and morning meal. Sad to say, but that is about as much fasting as most Christians ever do. (I almost fasted last week... I almost prayed today...)

Fasting in the Old Testament:

- Pastor Moses fasted... (Fasted 40 days twice -- The result; the Ten Commandments)

- The Children of Israel fasted... Judges 20, 1 Samuel 7, 1 Samuel 31, 2 Samuel 1

- King David fasted... 2 Samuel 12, Psalms 69, Psalms 109

- King Ahab fasted... 1 Kings 12

- The servants of Saul fasted... 1 Chronicles 10

- The prophet Ezra fasted... Ezra 8&9

- Nehemiah fasted... Nehemiah 1 (He fasted until God sent him to Jerusalem to rebuild the broken city)

- Mordecai fasted... Esther 4 (Fasted for an intercessor)

- Zechariah and the priests fasted... Zechariah 7

- The prophet Daniel fasted... (Fasted 21 days before receiving an answer and visions) Daniel 9&10

- The prophet Joel fasted... Joel 2

History tells us; during the time between the Old Testament and the New Testament fasting became a Jewish characteristic.

Fasting in the New Testament:

- The Pharisees fasted...

- Jesus fasted... Matthew 4, Mark 2, Mark 9 (This time of fasting prepared Him for public ministry)

- Anna fasted... Luke 2

- Cornelius fasted... Acts 10 (The Lord sent Peter to present the gift of the Holy Spirit to the gentile world)

- The Apostles fasted... Acts 13 (Paul and Barnabas were selected as the first missionaries of the New Testament church)

- Paul fasted... 2 Corinthians 6, 2 Corinthians 11

Fasting is both an Old Testament and New Testament principle and activity. If fasting is a New Testament principle it is certainly worthy of the consideration of every Christian.

Ok, so we have established that fasting is an Old Testament and a New Testament principle but why should we fast?

Let me begin this segment of this study by saying that fasting will not persuade God to do anything that He would not ordinarily do. If you are fasting to convince God to do a miracle you are wasting your time. God already desires to give good things to His children. Matthew 7:11 (KJV) "If you then, being evil, know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more will your Father who is in heaven give good things to those who ask Him!" 2 Chronicles 16:9 (NKJV) "For the eyes of the Lord run to and fro throughout the whole earth, to show Himself strong on behalf of those whose heart is loyal to Him..."

If fasting does not convince God to do anything He would not ordinarily do; why fast?

- The words of Jesus implore us to do so... Matthew 9:14-15 (NLT) "One day the disciples of John the Baptist came to Jesus and asked him, "Why do we and the Pharisees fast, but your disciples don't fast?" 15 Jesus responded, "Should the wedding guests mourn while celebrating with the groom? Someday he will be taken from them, and then they will fast."

- Fasting is the duty of every Christian... Matthew 6:16 (NLT) "And when you fast, don't make it obvious, as the hypocrites do, who try to look pale and disheveled so people will admire them for their fasting. I assure you, that is the only reward they will ever get."

- Fasting crucifies the flesh... The flesh is the enemy of faith and therefore it is the enemy of God. Galatians 5:16-26 (NLT) "So I advise you to live according to your new life in the Holy Spirit. Then you won't be doing what your sinful nature craves. 17 The old sinful nature loves to do evil, which is just opposite from what the Holy Spirit wants. And the Spirit gives us desires that are opposite from what the sinful nature desires. These two forces are constantly fighting each other, and your choices are never free from this conflict. 18 But when you are directed by the Holy Spirit, you are no longer subject to the law. 19 When you follow the desires of your sinful nature, your lives will produce these evil results: sexual immorality, impure thoughts, eagerness for lustful pleasure, 20 idolatry, participation in demonic activities, hostility, quarreling, jealousy, outbursts of anger, selfish ambition, divisions, the feeling that everyone is wrong except those in your own little group, 21 envy, drunkenness, wild parties, and other kinds of sin. Here there is no conflict with the law. 24 Those who belong to Christ Jesus have nailed the passions and desires of their sinful nature to his cross and crucified them there. 25 If we are living now by the Holy Spirit, let us follow the Holy Spirit's leading in every part of our lives. 26 Let us not become conceited, or irritate one another, or be jealous of one another." Fasting kills all of the above -- Fasting crucifies the old man -- If you get crucified you are dead - And dead men do not lust!

- Fasting humbles the believer before God... Fast a few days and you will find yourself more humble than ever before.

- Fasting teaches the believer dependence upon God as his source...

- Fasting expresses submission to God... Submission to God helps us appreciate the need of self-discipline. Matthew 6:10 NKJV) "Your kingdom come. Your will be done On earth as it is in heaven."

- Fasting prepares us to receive revelation from God... (Moses, David, Nehemiah and Daniel)

- Fasting brings victory... To be Christ like is a life of victory. Doubt is of the flesh, refusal to crucify the flesh brings defeat. When we crucify the flesh we have victory.

- Fasting places the believer in a position of hearing from God... (Daniel -- 21 days)

CONCLUSION:

The Bible has much to say about giving; therefore a pastor is indebted to his parishioners to teach good, sound, Biblical, principles for giving. Just because there are people in the church who are uncomfortable with the pastor talking openly about money does not release him from the responsibility of teaching on the subject!

Whether you like it or not, whether you are comfortable or uncomfortable with your pastor talking about money, the fact is that money is something we all deal with every day and we desperately need to use proven Biblical principles to guide our business practices!

Those of us who are operating in a true spirit of stewardship will continually ask ourselves the following question. "What would God have us to do with what He has entrusted to us?" If this question rules our life then we get it! We have a real shot at becoming the stewards God would have us to be! Once we become the stewards God would have us to be, the tithing debate will be settled in our life. We will tithe because we realize everything we have belongs to the Lord, the tithe never belonged to us to begin with therefore we cannot GIVE IT, all we can do is RETURN IT!

The Lord has required of us as His business managers to invest a minimum of ten percent back into His Kingdom!

CHALLENGE \# 5 -- SERVE IN A MINISTRY FOR SIX CONSECUTIVE WEEKS...

Romans 12:1 (NKJV) "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that you present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable to God, which is your reasonable service."

Matthew 23:10-12 (KJV) "Neither be ye called masters: for one is your Master, even Christ. 11 But he that is greatest among you shall be your servant. 12 And whosoever shall exalt himself shall be abased; and he that shall humble himself shall be exalted."

Our society is geared toward leadership rather than serving. Everywhere you turn you see a new best-selling book or a conference being advertised, all having to do with leadership. John Maxwell said "Everything rises and falls on leadership." I am certainly not in a position to dispute Mr. Maxwell but I believe the bedrock of our effectiveness is tied to what Jesus said in Matthew 23:10-12 (KJV) "Neither be ye called masters: for one is your Master, even Christ. 11 But he that is greatest among you shall be your servant. 12 And whosoever shall exalt himself shall be abased; and he that shall humble himself shall be exalted."

Someone said "He who bows lowest, stands the tallest." Jesus not only preached and taught servant-hood but He modeled it as well. Everywhere Jesus went He found a way to serve the people:

- He preached in their Synagogues...

- He taught them valuable life lessons...

- He healed the sick...

Most of us have become saturated with lessons on how to be a good leader, what we need is to figure out how to incorporate a little servant-hood into our leadership. Servant leadership is the most effective kind of leadership.

I want us to look at some Biblical reasons for serving:

- Serving is worship... (Explain how mature Christians worship -- They worship not to music but with a towel on their arm serving others) Jesus said to Peter... John 21:15 (NKJV) "So when they had eaten breakfast, Jesus said to Simon Peter, "Simon, son of Jonah, do you love Me more than these?" He said to Him, "Yes, Lord; You know that I love You." He said to him, "Feed My lambs." The writer of Romans calls our service to God\'s people "Our reasonable service."

- We are to serve in an effort to demonstrate God's love for others... The Bible makes very clear the love of God for all the people of this world. John 3:16 (KJV) "For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life." There is no greater way to manifest the love of our Heavenly Father than to love someone who does not deserve it! Matthew 5:46 (NKJV) "For if you love those who love you, what reward have you?" I am challenging each of us to find a place to serve others for six consecutive weeks for the purpose of demonstrating His love for others! (I pray every day that as I reach out and touch the people of this church that when I pull my hands back, you will have the fingerprints of God on your life because He has touched you through me!)

- We are to serve others as a demonstration of our faithfulness to God... None of us would question God\'s faithfulness to us. The \$64,000.00 question is our faithfulness to Him. God is faithful even when we are not, but life is made richer and fuller when we reciprocate His faithfulness. When we serve others our service is a demonstration of our faithfulness to Him. Jesus placed a premium on faithfulness and servant-hood. There is a reward to be gained from serving faithfully. There is a consequence for lack of serving. Unfaithful people lose out because of neglect. Hebrews 2:3 (NKJV) "How shall we escape if we neglect so great a salvation?" We must re-prioritize our entire life if necessary.
