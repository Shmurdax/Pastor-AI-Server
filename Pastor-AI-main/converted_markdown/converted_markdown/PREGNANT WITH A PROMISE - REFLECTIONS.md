WHAT IS A PROMISE?

For some time now the Lord has been dealing with me concerning the subject we are going to delve into over the next several weeks; "PREGNANT WITH A PROMISE."

Because of the ever increasing information on the subject of leadership we hear more and more about dreams, visions, and goals. All these are necessary and good but if they are ever to become anything more than a good idea they must begin and end with a deposit of God in our spirits. These things should be a natural outflow of a God given promise.

A promise is a deposit of God in our spirit.

Dreams, visions, and goals are man\'s way of attempting to strategically place him-self in a position to allow the promise to come to pass.

If the dreams, visions, and goals we are striving toward have not been conceived by God, we will wear our selves out reaching for what seems to be illusive and oftentimes unattainable.

No matter how much we study, plan, or work; if our dreams, visions, and goals are not a result of a deposit of God in our life in the form of a promise, the achievement of those things will leave us lusting for more.

Webster defines a promise in the following ways:

- A declaration that one will do or refrain from doing something specified...

- A legally binding declaration that gives the person to whom it is made a right to expect or to claim the performance of a specified act...

- Grounds for expectation of success, improvement, or excellence...

Once we have received His promise we can then set direction that is governed by dreams, visions, and goals. These are vehicles that drive us toward the place where we can receive and accomplish the "promise of God!"

The promise then, is the initiation of a process. Most of us do not like anything that requires a process; we want instant gratification, results and success.

## IV. THE WAITING PERIOD!

Every woman knows that when she discovers she is pregnant she is facing a nine month waiting period! Nine months! Why nine months? Because in the Divine order of God, that\'s how long it takes to develop a healthy baby!

Giving birth to a baby is a process; a process requires patience because it demands a waiting period. The process of pregnancy is described by Charles Betticelli in the following way: "During the first two months of pregnancy the developing organism is called an embryo. Its development is programmed by the chemical information in the chromosomes from both the egg and the sperm. Within the first day after fertilization, the egg begins to divide. By the end of the first week, it has developed into a hollow ball of more than one hundred cells. During the second week, part of this ball becomes implanted (set) in the lining of the uterus... The embryo begins to look like a human being after about five weeks. After about two months nearly all the internal organs have formed, and the growing organism is called a fetus. After about three months the fetus looks very much like a human baby... The mother can feel the fetus moving after about four months. Growth and development continue until about nine months after fertilization. The baby is then ready to be born."

According to Charles Betticelli the pregnancy and its progress can be tracked from twenty four hours to nine months after fertilization and "the baby is then ready to be born." Not one moment before and not one moment later!

Everyone knows that a premature baby presents a whole list of complications for the baby, the parents, and the doctor!

We also know that if a woman is allowed to continue with a pregnancy beyond her due date, the overdue pregnancy will present equally difficult complications.

When some Christians become pregnant with a promise, they begin to focus all their energies on convincing the doctor to induce labor early or perform an early cesarean section. Over-anxiousness and lack of patience often cause complications that the pregnant person is not ready for.

Contractions announce the approaching birth...

The water breaks...

The baby begins to travel down the birth canal...

The mother must aid in the process by pushing...

The pain is so intense it seems to be unbearable...
