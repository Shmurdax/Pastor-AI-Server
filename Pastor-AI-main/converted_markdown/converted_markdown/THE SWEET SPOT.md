INTRO: For the next couple of weeks we 
are going to be talking about the sweet spot. 
The sweet spot is defined as “The point or 
area on a bat, club, or racket at which it 
makes most effective contact with the ball.” 
 
I am not much of a golfer, but I have had a 
few instances with driving the ball where I 
hit the sweet spot on my club. When this 
occurs, the swing feels effortless, the impact 
gives a certain sound, and you can hardly 
tell from the vibration in the club that you 
have hit the ball at all. When a connection is 
made with the sweet spot the ball goes much 

farther than normal. Hitting the sweet spot 
keeps one coming back. 
 
Recently I was in Foley Alabama with my 
family. We went into a Krispy Kreme donut 
shop while the hot sign was on. When the 
hot sign is on, you can watch the process of 
the dough becoming some of the sweetest 
and best donuts to ever make contact with 
taste buds! Before the donuts become a 
finished product: 
• The ingredients must be mixed 
together… 
• The dough must be given time to rise… 
• The dough must be rolled into one solid 
sheet… 
• The round template must cut through the 
dough… 
• Holes must be punched in the dough… 
• The donuts must be fried to a golden 
brown… 

This whole process must be arduously 
followed before the donuts are ready to have 
the icing applied. The icing is what we are 
really after, but the Krispy Kreme donut 
would not be up to Krispy Kreme standards 
without following the entire process. 
 
If the donut is not positioned to receive a 
baptism of icing, an employee takes an 
instrument and strategically places it in the 
path of an avalanche of icing. 
 
All of us would like to live life in the sweet 
spot, but a process is required to get us 
there. Circumventing any part of the process 
is a guarantee that the purpose for our lives 
will be aborted and the sweet spot will 
probably never occur. 
 
For the believer, the sweet spot is the place 
of greatest effectiveness. 

What kind of process are we willing to 
endure to get ourselves into the sweet spot 
where we can be all God has intended us to 
be? 
 

## I. PATMOS IS A PREREQUISITE TO
THE SWEET SPOT… 
Revelation 1:1, 10-18 (NKJV) “The 
Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave 
Him to show His servants—things which 
must shortly take place. And He sent and 
signified it by His angel to His servant 
John, 10 I was in the Spirit on the Lord’s 
Day, and I heard behind me a loud voice, 
as of a trumpet, 11 saying, “I am the Alpha 
and the Omega, the First and the Last,” 
and, “What you see, write in a book and 
send it to the seven churches which are in 
Asia: to Ephesus, to Smyrna, to Pergamos, 
to Thyatira, to Sardis, to Philadelphia, and 
to Laodicea.” 12 Then I turned to see the 
voice that spoke with me. And having 
turned I saw seven golden lampstands, 

13 and in the midst of the seven lampstands 
One like the Son of Man, clothed with a 
garment down to the feet and girded about 
the chest with a golden band. 14 His head 
and hair were white like wool, as white as 
snow, and His eyes like a flame of fire; 
15 His feet were like fine brass, as if refined 
in a furnace, and His voice as the sound of 
many waters; 16 He had in His right hand 
seven stars, out of His mouth went a sharp 
two-edged sword, and His countenance was 
like the sun shining in its strength. 17 And 
when I saw Him, I fell at His feet as dead. 
But He laid His right hand on me, saying 
to me, “Do not be afraid; I am the First 
and the Last. 18 I am He who lives, and was 
dead, and behold, I am alive forevermore. 
Amen. And I have the keys of Hades and of 
Death.” 
 
In our text we are introduced to John, often 
referred to as the “Revelator.” 

John the apostle was the author of the book 
of Saint John. He was the son of Zebedee 
and the brother of James the elder. He is that 
disciple who is spoken of as especially dear 
to Jesus. He is also the author of the book of 
Revelation. 
 
The Book of Revelation was revealed to the 
Apostle John on an island called Patmos. He 
was banished there for his testimony of 
Jesus. Patmos is mentioned only once in 
Scripture. For most folks, a little dab of 
Patmos will do you! 
 
Patmos is: 
• Off the South West coast of Turkey… 
• About thirty miles in circumference… 
• A sterile Island, unable to produce 
offspring… 
• Free from living things that reproduce 
themselves… 
• A place without inspiration or vitality… 

The very definition of Patmos is “My 
killing.” 
 
Because of its rocky, barren and desolate 
nature, the Roman government used the 
island as a place of banishment for 
criminals. The prisoners were compelled to 
work the mines of the island. The Emperor 
Domitian banished John to this island, about 
A.D. 95. 
 
While the topography of the island exposes 
its magnificent scenery, we must not forget 
it was a place of banishment for the purpose 
of punishment. John was not there because 
of any desire of his own; he was there 
because he was being persecuted for 
proclaiming the powerful message of Jesus 
Christ! 
 
How interesting that God would use such a 
place to bring to John the Revelation of 

Jesus Christ! John already knew Jesus, but 
his experience on Patmos was about to take 
their relationship to a new level. 
 
God chooses some of the most peculiar 
places and circumstances to reveal the sweet 
spots in our lives. Are we willing to go 
anywhere or do anything to get in the sweet 
spot? How about Patmos? Are we willing to 
live on Patmos for God to reveal His 
purpose through us? Are we willing to give 
up our dream to pursue His? 
 
Admiral Jim Stockdale who spent eight 
years in the Hanoi Hilton (POW Camp) said, 
“You must never confuse faith that you will 
prevail in the end, which you can never 
afford to lose, with the discipline of the most 
brutal facts of your current reality. 
Whatever they might be.” (LEAVE ON 
THE SCREEN UNTIL THE END OF 
THE SERMON) 

Patmos was no picnic! Patmos is never a 
picnic! It means “My Killing!” 
 
On Patmos, John was subject to the wicked 
taunts of evil and Christ-rejecting men! Men 
in authority had banished him to that island 
to make him suffer for his pricking their 
consciences through the preaching of the 
mighty Word of God! 
On Patmos John was suffering. He was 
living in the patience of Jesus Christ, not his 
own! 
 
As you know, one of the biggest differences 
between a pro athlete and an average player 
is practice, practice, practice! Practice can 
be brutal, but practice makes perfect! 
 
One does not consistently hit the sweet spot 
on a bat, club or racket without endless 
hours of grueling practice! This can be 
painful, but if you want to be the best you 
have to pay the price! Drug use among 

athletes is an attempt at a shortcut around 
the price that must be paid! 
 
What are we willing to endure to allow the 
instrument in the hands of God to push us 
under the avalanche of icing? 
 
(END OF SERMON #1)

Think of the difficulty John had endured not 
just at Patmos, but leading up to his 
banishment. I am sure the devil was playing 
tricks in his mind with such things as: 
• Where is your God now? 
• Are you sure your message is right? 
• If you were on target, wouldn’t your 
God rescue you? 
• Don’t you think it is time to admit your 
mistake? 

John was not only in difficulty; he was 
surrounded by difficult circumstances. One 
of the most amazing things about this 
passage is verse 10. “I was in the Spirit on 
the Lord’s Day…” 
 
Despite the difficulty, John had created an 
atmosphere for God to reveal Himself. 
 
Let me remind us, John did not discover 
Jesus on Patmos; he was intimately 
acquainted with Him long before his ordeal 
on the barren island began! If we wait until 
Patmos to get acquainted with Jesus we may 
have a difficult time with the things we will 
face there. 
 
John was the disciple who: 
• Jesus loved… 
• Worshipped with sweet abandon… 
• Operated in humility in Jesus’ 
presence… 
• Laid his head on the chest of Jesus… 

• Was appointed to be Mary’s son after 
Jesus’ death… 
 
Can you imagine how devastating the cross 
must have been for a person like John who 
dared to love his Master so deeply? 
 
Because of his intimate relationship with 
Jesus, the Gospel according to St. John’s is 
considered by many to be the deepest and 
most spiritual book in the entire Bible. 
John’s writings are the ones that list the “I 
Am’s of Christ.”

• I Am the Bread of Life (6:35) 
• I Am from above (8:23)

• I Am the Resurrection and the Life 
(11:25) 
• I Am the Lord and Master (John 13:13) 

• I Am the Way, the Truth, and the Life 
(14:6) 
• I Am the True Vine (15:1) 
• I Am the Alpha and Omega (1:8) 
• I Am the First and the Last (1:17) 
 
It would seem that John truly knew Jesus 
beyond just a head-knowledge! Because of 
the level at which John knew Jesus, he wrote 
about things that others did not mention!

I am convinced the sweet spot in life can 
only be revealed through difficulty. A pearl 
is not developed in a mall! (HOLD A 
STRAND OF PEARLS) It is developed in 
the life of an oyster and is the result of 
months, if not years, of suffering and 
irritation. 
 
Most of us expend a lot of time, energy and 
resources attempting to avoid difficult spots 
altogether. For every difficult spot we are 
able to avoid, we run the risk of missing the 
icing of life and losing the opportunities that 
can only be discovered in the sweet spot.

• The dough must be kneaded…

Nothing could have been more difficult for 
John than Patmos, but listen to his words: “I 
was in the Spirit on the Lord’s Day.” Not 
only was John in the Spirit on the Lord’s 
Day, He received revelation that no one 
before or after him ever received. In fact the 
revelation was so strong that he described 
portions of it as “not lawful to utter.” 
 
When we can bring ourselves to trust God 
enough to bravely walk into a personal 
Patmos, we will likely see a greater 
revelation of Jesus through our lives than we 
could have anticipated.

we create an atmosphere for God to move, 
we will see miraculous things take place! 
We will see Him in His glory!

## III. THE SWEET SPOT IS WHERE
GOD’S POWER IS REVEALED… 
Notice in our text, John is now exiled to the 
isle of Patmos. Again, the Greek word 

“Patmos” is translated to mean “my 
killing.” 
 
Patmos was a rugged, barren, deserted 
island, littered with the skeletal remains of 
those who had preceded John to this eerie 
place. Yet it was here that John entered the 
presence of Jesus. Revelation 1:13-16 
(NKJV) “In the midst of the seven 
lampstands One like the Son of Man, 
clothed with a garment down to the feet 
and girded about the chest with a golden 
band. 14 His head and hair were white like 
wool, as white as snow, and His eyes like a 
flame of fire; 15 His feet were like fine 
brass, as if refined in a furnace, and His 
voice as the sound of many waters; 16 He 
had in His right hand seven stars, out of 
His mouth went a sharp two-edged sword, 
and His countenance was like the sun 
shining in its strength.”

At Patmos, John saw Jesus! Patmos sure 
didn’t look like a sweet spot. But for John, 
Jesus took the stick of life and pushed him 
under the avalanche of icing, right into the 
sweet spot!

Admiral Jim Stockdale who spent eight 
years in the Hanoi Hilton (POW Camp) said, 
“What separates people is not the presence 
or absence of difficulty but how they deal 
with the inevitable difficulties of life.” 
 
It was on Patmos that John penned the book 
of Revelation, telling of things that were and 

are, and things that would eventually come 
to pass. 
 
Most of us never get to a place where God 
can get through to us without a personal 
Patmos! Oftentimes the only way we can 
receive a true, first-hand revelation from 
God is to be pushed out of our comfort zone. 
We need to quit struggling against the stick 
of life and accept what comes our way with 
contentment. Philippians 4:11 (NKJV) “I 
have learned in whatever state I am, to be 
content.” 
 
The difficulty we face could very easily be 
the stick of life being held by a nail-scarred 
hand attempting to push us under the 
avalanche of icing! 
 
The uncomfortable spots of life are: 
• Lonely… 
• Remote… 
• Isolated… 

• Deserted… 
• Secluded… 
• Gut-wrenching… 
 
As close as John was to Jesus, it was only at 
Patmos that John saw Jesus on a completely 
different level. Revelation 1:17 (NKJV) 
“When I saw Him, I fell at His feet as 
dead.” John had never responded to Jesus in 
this way before! The stick of life pushed 
John under the avalanche of icing on 
Patmos. 
 

CONCLUSION: 
In the sweet spot called Patmos, John wrote 
the last few chapters of the Bible and they 
stand in stark contrast to the first few books 
of the Bible: 
• Genesis speaks of the creation of the sun 
- Revelation tells of a place that needs no 
sun 

• Genesis speaks of the entrance of sin 
into this world - Revelation tells of the 
banishment of all sin 
• Genesis speaks of the curse of sin that is 
pronounced upon mankind - Revelation 
declares the curse of sin to be broken 
• Genesis speaks of Satan’s first 
triumphant victory over mankind - 
Revelation speaks of Satan’s demise 
• Genesis speaks of the exclusion from the 
Tree of Life - Revelation speaks of the 
admission to the Tree of Life 
 
If we want to live in the sweet spot we must 
be willing to: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN 
ONE AT A TIME – THEN LEAVE ALL 
ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE END OF 
THE SERVICE) 
• Patiently endure hardships… 
• Be content in all circumstances… 
• Be willing to get shoved around a 
little… 

• Walk in the Spirit in spite of all 
circumstances…
