INTRO: Most people, who call themselves 
Christian, believe in the person of the Holy 
Spirit, at least in some limited dimension. 

The extent of these beliefs has made for 
many interesting conversations as well as 
quite a large number of heated religious 
debates. 
 
Following conversion, those who have 
received the infilling of the Holy Spirit as a 
second definite work of grace are widely 
known as Pentecostal or Charismatic. 
 
It is estimated that 
Pentecostals/Charasmatics world-wide 
number more that 500,000,000! 
 
To begin with, I want to point out what I 
believe to be several general Scriptural facts 
concerning the person of the Holy Spirit: 
• The Holy Spirit is the third person of the 
Holy trinity 
• The Holy Spirit works in unity with the 
Father and the Son (The best example is 
that of husband and wife, they are two 
people yet they work together as one) 

• The Holy Spirit is the power behind the 
implementation of Gods purpose in the 
earth

• Why was tongues chosen as the initial 
physical evidence of Holy Spirit 
Baptism? The tongue is the most unruly 
member of the body. James 3:6-8 
(NKJV) "And the tongue is a fire, a 
world of iniquity. The tongue is so set 
among our members that it defiles the 
whole body, and sets on fire the course 
of nature; and it is set on fire by hell.7 
For every kind of beast and bird, of 
reptile and creature of the sea, is tamed 
and has been tamed by mankind.8 But 

no man can tame the tongue. It is an 
unruly evil, full of deadly poison." 
 
Before we decide to write the Holy Spirit off 
as unimportant or insignificant we should 
consider the words of Jesus as it relates to 
the importance of the Holy Spirit. John 16:7 
(KJV) "Nevertheless I tell you the truth; It 
is expedient for you that I go away: for if I 
go not away, the Comforter will not come 
unto you; but if I depart, I will send him 
unto you." 
In this Scripture, Jesus is stressing to His 
disciples the necessity of His departure. As 
long as He lived in a flesh and blood body 
He could only be in one place at a time. He 
is attempting to explain to them that the 
reason it was so necessary for Him to go to 
the Father was so that the Holy Spirit would 
come and live in each believer, thus 
empowering each believer to carry on the 
work of Christ in the world. If Jesus thought 
it necessary to depart so that the Holy Spirit 

could indwell and empower believers, where 
do we get off promoting the Holy Spirit as 
optional equipment?
