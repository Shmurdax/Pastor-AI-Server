INTRO: I thank God for the opportunity to have been raised in a truly Pentecostal atmosphere. Someone asked the question, "What would you be if you were not Pentecostal?" The answer, "I would be disappointed."

Pentecost or being Pentecostal has somehow received a bad name in this culture. Pentecost is not some mystical thing. Pentecost simply means fiftieth day. Jesus spent forty days on the earth after His resurrection and ten days following His discussion with His disciples at Bethany's launching pad, the Holy Spirit filled the believers who were assembled in the Upper Room. Since that time, all those who have received the infilling of the Holy Spirit have become known as Pentecostals.

Pentecostals have received a lot of bad publicity because of:

- The excesses which have been allowed

- The failure to demonstrate the power of Pentecost

- The legalism that has been championed

- The quacks which have been allowed to continue

In spite of all of this, I will tell you that there is an authentic Pentecostalism which brings with it the ability for the church to rise to a level never before achieved. During the course of this sermon, I am going to attempt to show you why being Pentecostal is so vital to the believer and ultimately to the body of Christ.

Being an authentic Pentecostal is more than speaking in tongues, it is about four distinct things:

- Prayer

- Power

- Perseverance

- Perpetuation

## I. AUTHENTIC PENTECOSTALISM RAISES THE LEVEL OF A BELIEVER'S PRAYER LIFE!

Prayer is probably one of the most discussed and least practiced theological topics of the church. Pentecostals have historically been known for their praying. Someone said, "Bent knees make straight men." "He stands longest, who kneels lowest."

Jesus our great example laid out a pattern for us to follow:

- He often got up a great while before dawn and went to prayer.

- He was known to pray all night.

- He always prayed before making major decisions.

- He prayed before performing the miracle of the loaves and the fishes.

- He agonized in prayer in the garden of Gethsemane.

Let us look to a couple of Scriptures which allow us to look at what being filled with the Spirit does for our prayer life. Romans 8:26-27 (KJV) "Likewise the Spirit also helpeth our infirmities: for we know not what we should pray for as we ought: but the Spirit itself maketh intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered.

^27 ^And he that searcheth the hearts knoweth what is the mind of the Spirit, because he maketh intercession for the saints according to the will of God."

According to these passages of Scripture when we are filled with the Holy Spirit we receive a prayer language that:

- Engages heaven

- Allows us to pray at a level which exceeds our vocabulary

- Builds our faith to otherwise impossible levels

The church I was raised in was such a powerful Pentecostal church that the prayer meetings and altar services were the main event. If you went to the altar in that church, you did not stay at the altar until you were satisfied, you stayed until they were satisfied!

## II. AUTHENTIC PENTECOSTALISM CAUSES THE BELIEVER TO WALK IN THE SUPERNATURAL POWER OF GOD!

I am afraid that we are embarking upon a powerless Pentecost in this modern day church. The New Testament church has always been and should always be a church of power.

Listen to the words of Jesus with regard to His intentions for this Pentecostal Church. Mark 16:14-19 (KJV) "^ ^And he said unto them, Go ye into all the world, and preach the gospel to every creature. ^16 ^He that believeth and is baptized shall be saved; but he that believeth not shall be damned.

^17 ^And these signs shall follow them that believe; In my name shall they cast out devils; they shall speak with new tongues;

^18 ^They shall take up serpents; and if they drink any deadly thing, it shall not hurt them; they shall lay hands on the sick, and they shall recover. ^19 ^So then after the Lord had spoken unto them, he was received up into heaven, and sat on the right hand of God. ^20 ^And they went forth, and preached every where, the Lord working with them, and confirming the word with signs following. Amen."

If we are authentic Pentecostals where are the miracles?

Pentecostal power is unlike the power of the world. It far exceeds other powers. Other powers have limitations, but Pentecostal power in unlimited. The reason it is unlimited is because it is God-breathed. This power causes humankind to be clothed, empowered, and impelled by Omnipotence!

## III. AUTHENTIC PENTECOSTALISM PRODUCES PEOPLE WHO ARE DETERMINED TO PERSEVERE!

The early church was full of folks who had made up their mind to serve Jesus. I have trouble with anemic, weak-willed, whiny Pentecostal people. When those who are authentic Pentecostals are persecuted they rebound, go to Lowe's and get a ladder and get-over-it; they shake it off and make the devil take it back!

The first century Church was full of people who went the distance in spite of the odds. Look at the perseverance of the twelve disciples:

- Andrew died on a cross

- Simon was crucified

- Bartholomew was flayed alive

- James the son of Zebedee was beheaded

- James the son of Alphaeus was beaten to death

- Thomas was run through with a lance

- Mathias was stoned and then beheaded

- Matthew was slain by the sword

- Peter was crucified upside down

- Thaddeus was shot to death with arrows

- Philip was hanged

- Only John made it through alive but he was boiled in oil and exiled to a small island in the middle of the Mediterranean Sea

I thank God for authentic Pentecostals who are willing to persist despite the pressure to quit.

I publicly renew my commitment today to be an authentic Pentecostal come hell or high water!

## IV. AUTHENTIC PENTECOSTALISM WILL PERPETUATE ANOTHER GENERATION OF PENTECOSTALS!

The early church was a generation of Pentecostals that knew how to perpetuate another generation of Pentecostals. Look at the results in the First Century Church:

- 3,000 saved the first day (Acts 2:37-38)

- God added to the church daily such as should be saved (Acts 2:47)

- 2,000 are saved at Solomon's Porch (Acts 3:1-4)

- The Samaritans become Christians (Acts 8:1-12)

- Men and women were baptized following their conversion (Acts 8:12-13)

- The Ethiopian eunuch is converted (Acts 8:36-39)

- Saul (Paul) receives Christ (Acts 9:18)

- The household of Cornelius becomes Christians (Acts 10:43)

- The people of Antioch are converted (Acts 11:19-21)

- Sergius Paulus becomes a Christian (Acts 13:4-12)

- Many Jews and religious proselytes follow Paul and Barnabas at Antioch (Acts 13:13-48)

- A great multitude of individuals follow Christ at Iconium (Acts 14:1)

- An undetermined number of people make decisions for Christ at Lystra and Derbe (Acts 14)

- The Philippian jailer and his family become followers of Christ (Acts 16)

- Lydia comes to faith in Christ (Acts 16)

- A great multitude of the Greeks and many of the leading women join themselves to the faith at Thessalonica (Acts 17:1-12)

- The conversion of the Athenians at Mars Hill (Acts 17:16-34)

- Many Corinthian people are converted (Acts 18:8)

- At Corinth Aquila and Pricilla follow Jesus (Acts 18:24-28)

- The twelve disciples of John the Baptist are converted (Acts 19:1-10)

You cannot become Pentecostal through osmosis anymore than a waitress will absorb food through her skin. There must be a rebirth of Pentecost in every generation! Just because you've attended a Pentecostal church does not mean you are an authentic Pentecostal. A fish can stay in salt water his entire life without the salt water getting in him! There are those who go through the spiritual motions of being Pentecostal, but they are not really Pentecostal!

Authentic Pentecostalism is always in the process of perpetuating another generation of Pentecostals.
