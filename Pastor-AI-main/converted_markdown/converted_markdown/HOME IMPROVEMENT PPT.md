BUILDING BLOCKS OF A HEALTHY RELATIONSHIP

Building a healthy relationship is just what it sounds like -- Work!

How committed are you to your marriage?

Marriage is all about two people finding their place, doing their part and running in their lane.

A culture of openness can only be created if the following three rules are meticulously applied:

- Each person should be able to share his or her likes and dislikes...

- When ones spouse shares a concern one should be careful of ones attitude, body language and facial expressions...

- Each person must be willing to accommodate the other as long as it does not violate a scriptural principle...

Marriage is a developmental process, not an event that became complete when you fell in love.

True intimacy grows only as a couple gets to know each other better. Marriage is a living developmental process that needs nurturing and care in order for it to grow and thrive.

SEXUALITY

Sex between marriage partners should occupy a high priority but it cannot be the thing that everything else is judged by.

Sex is more than a physical act it is an emotional and spiritual interaction between two people God intentionally put together.

Remember the following things taken from 1 Corinthians 13:

- Love waits...

<!-- -->

- Love shares...

- Love rejoices...

- Love is constantly honoring...

- Love comes from a heart overflowing with affection for God...

A rule that must be honored in all marriages is that only what is enjoyable to both partners should be permitted.

Four central principles taught in 1 Corinthians 7:2-5 concerning sex:

- Both the husband and the wife have sexual needs and drives that should be fulfilled inside their marriage...

- When one marries they forfeit control of their body to their partner...

- Both partners are forbidden to refuse to meet the sexual needs of their mate...

- The act of sex within a marriage is approved by God and must be treated as such by all who commit to the marriage covenant...
