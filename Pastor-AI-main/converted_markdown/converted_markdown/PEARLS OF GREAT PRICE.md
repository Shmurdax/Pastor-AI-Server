TEXT: Matthew 13:45-46 (NKJV) "Again, the kingdom of heaven is like a merchant seeking beautiful pearls, 46 who, when he had found one pearl of great price, went and sold all that he had and bought it."

INTRO: Jesus used a variety of visual effects to get His point across. He was a master communicator and He used a technique of teaching called parables. The word parable is derived from the Greek verb paraballo, and means "to lay by the side of, or to compare something with a likeness, or similitude." (New Unger's Bible Dictionary) In common terms a parable is a common story which points to common things to teach a Kingdom Principle.

Matthew 13 is almost entirely devoted to a collection of parables. In this collection of parables there is a common thread which runs through each of them, the subject matter is the affect of the Word of God on the life of believers. More specifically it comes down to the rise and fall of Christian faith in the life of a believer.

Of all the parables listed in Matthew 13, this one is probably the most obvious when it comes to interpretation, but there are a few things I want to highlight.

When we hear the word pearl it doesn't really register with us what is going on because in our culture a pearl is a beautiful, expensive, coveted piece of jewelry.

On the other hand, to this Jewish audience a pearl had very little value; it was actually shocking from a Hebrew standpoint that someone would sell all he had to buy a pearl because a Hebrew person wouldn't walk across the street to pick up pearls if they were being given away.

On the other hand pearls were an expensive commodity in places such as Egypt as well as the ancient city of Nineveh. The Hebrews looked upon those outside their race as dogs, so the fact that the Egyptians and the Ninevites valued the pearl mattered not to the Hebrews.

Also, consider this, Jesus is speaking to fishermen (disciples) and they hated oysters because the oysters were responsible for tearing up their nets, therefore they avoided oysters at all costs.

With this in mind it must have been a tremendous shock to the Disciples as well as this Jewish audience to hear Jesus speaking of a person selling all he has to purchase a pearl.

## II. THE OYSTER IS THE WORLD... The pearl is the only stone that is a by-product of a living organism. I do not know of one other kind of stone that evolves from another living organism. Look at how a pearl is produced. A pearl is born out of suffering. In this world there is suffering for as far as the eye can see. This world can be brutal.

A spec of sand or parasite makes its way into the oyster. The oyster is a living organism and this intruder brings great pain to the oyster. To protect itself, the oyster secretes a substance known as mother-of-pearl, or aragonite (nacre). This secretion surrounds the sand or parasite and layer by SLOW layer the wound of intrusion becomes a valuable jewel, better known as a pearl.

The pain involved in the development of a pearl is excruciating so the pearl is quite literally the product of an injured life. Every person born into this world is born in pain and sin. The pain of childbirth is almost unbearable but the pain inflicted by sin is completely unbearable by mere mortals.

The processes of life can be extremely painful and usually when a person comes to Christ they come as a result of some type of crisis.

Contrary to public opinion natural pearls aren't smooth, they are actually rough in texture and misshaped. They are never smooth and perfectly round unless created in a lab.
