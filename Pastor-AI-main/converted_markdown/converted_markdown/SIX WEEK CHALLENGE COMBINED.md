# CHALLENGE # 4 – ATTEND CHURCH
AND A LIFE GROUP FOR SIX 
CONSECUTIVE WEEKS… 
1) Fasting… One meal a day or one day a 
week for six weeks… 
2) Read the word each day for six 
weeks… Sign up to receive the reading 
plan text… 
3) Tithing… Tithe 10% of your total 
family income for the next six 
consecutive weeks… 
 
(My history) In forty-nine years of living on 
this earth there has never been a time when I 
was not "In Church": 
• I was raised in a church! 
• I was dedicated to the Lord in a church! 
• I was saved in a church! 
• I was baptized in water in a church! 
• I was received into membership in a 
church! 
• Susan and I were married in a church! 
• We dedicated our children in a church! 

• We have raised our family in a church! 
• My funeral will be held in a church! 
 
I am literally a product of the church. 
Because I am a product of the church, I am a 
champion of the church. I would fight hell 
with a water pistol in defense of the church! 
 
• Jesus went to church and met in 
homes… 
• The apostles went to church and 
attended life groups… 
• We are commanded to go to church and 
to have fellowship with one another… 
Hebrews 10:25 (NKJV) "Not forsaking 
the assembling of ourselves together, as 
is the manner of some, but exhorting 
one another, and so much the more as 
you see the Day approaching." 
 
When I talk about church attendance I am 
talking about not only attending the 10 AM 

Sunday service but also attending a life 
group. 
• We should attend church and a life 
group to be fed spiritually… 
• We should attend church and a life 
group to function as a body… 1 
Corinthians 12:18-20, 27 (NKJV) "But 
now God has set the members, each one 
of them, in the body just as He 
pleased.19 And if they were all one 
member, where would the body be?20 
But now indeed there are many 
members, yet one body. - 27 Now you 
are the body of Christ, and members 
individually." Every member of the 
body is vitally important but we must be 
TOGETHER to function with maximum 
impact. 
• We should attend church and a life 
group for the opportunity to exercise our 
gifts… 
 

CONCLUSION: 

My challenge to you is to attend church and 
a life group for at least six consecutive 
weeks. 
 
God ordained the church for the purpose of 
ministering to the saints and reaching the 
world. We cannot very well reach the world 
if we are not receiving ministry from others 
and offering ministry to others. 
 
What better place to receive and give 
ministry than the local church? 
 
Take a moment to think about what kind of 
Christians we would be if the following 
were not available to us: 
• No other Christians to fellowship with… 
• No teachers to teach us… 
• No discipleship to train us… 
• No Pastor to shepherd us; counsel us; 
marry us; or bury us… 
• No small groups that allow us to do life 
together… 

 
“What kind of church would this church be, 
if every member were just like me?” 
 
Can we commit ourselves today to making 
this a much better church by attending 
church and a life group for six consecutive 
weeks? The first step of living a successful 
Christian life is to connect oneself to a 
church and to attend faithfully. 
 
CHALLENGE # 5 – SERVE IN A 
MINISTRY FOR SIX CONSECUTIVE 
WEEKS… 
Romans 12:1 (NKJV) "I beseech you 
therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, 
that you present your bodies a living 
sacrifice, holy, acceptable to God, which is 
your reasonable service." 
 
Matthew 23:10-12 (KJV) "Neither be ye 
called masters: for one is your Master, even 
Christ. 11 But he that is greatest among 

you shall be your servant. 12 And 
whosoever shall exalt himself shall be 
abased; and he that shall humble himself 
shall be exalted." 
 
Our society is geared toward leadership 
rather than serving. Everywhere you turn 
you see a new best-selling book or a 
conference being advertised, all having to do 
with leadership. John Maxwell said 
"Everything rises and falls on leadership." I 
am certainly not in a position to dispute Mr. 
Maxwell but I believe the bedrock of our 
effectiveness is tied to what Jesus said in 
Matthew 23:10-12 (KJV) "Neither be ye 
called masters: for one is your Master, even 
Christ. 11 But he that is greatest among 
you shall be your servant. 12 And 
whosoever shall exalt himself shall be 
abased; and he that shall humble himself 
shall be exalted." 
 

Someone said "He who bows lowest, stands 
the tallest." Jesus not only preached and 
taught servant-hood but He modeled it as 
well. Everywhere Jesus went He found a 
way to serve the people: 
• He preached in their Synagogues… 
• He taught them valuable life lessons… 
• He healed the sick… 
 
Most of us have become saturated with 
lessons on how to be a good leader, what we 
need is to figure out how to incorporate a 
little servant-hood into our leadership. 
Servant leadership is the most effective kind 
of leadership. 
 
I want us to look at some Biblical reasons 
for serving: 
• Serving is worship… (Explain how 
mature Christians worship – They 
worship not to music but with a towel on 
their arm serving others) Jesus said to 
Peter… John 21:15 (NKJV) "So when 

they had eaten breakfast, Jesus said to 
Simon Peter, “Simon, son of Jonah, do 
you love Me more than these?” He said 
to Him, “Yes, Lord; You know that I 
love You.” He said to him, “Feed My 
lambs.” The writer of Romans calls our 
service to God's people "Our 
reasonable service." 
• We are to serve in an effort to 
demonstrate God’s love for others… I 
am challenging each of us to find a place 
to serve others for six consecutive weeks 
for the purpose of demonstrating His 
love for others! (I pray every day that as 
I reach out and touch the people of this 
church that when I pull my hands back, 
you will have the fingerprints of God on 
your life because He has touched you 
through me!) 
• We are to serve others as a 
demonstration of our faithfulness to 
God… None of us would question God's 
faithfulness to us. The $64,000.00 

question is our faithfulness to Him. 
Speaking of the parable of the talents, 
Jesus says the following of the faithful 
servants. Matthew 25:21 (NKJV) “His 
lord said to him, ‘Well done, good and 
faithful servant; you were faithful over 
a few things, I will make you ruler over 
many things. Enter into the joy of your 
lord." 
 
I am challenging each one of us to serve for 
six consecutive weeks for the purpose of 
demonstrating our faithfulness to Him! 
 

CONCLUSION: 
Serving God and serving His Church 
introduces great satisfaction in the life of 
His servants! I want to conclude today by 
asking a few soul-searching questions: 
• Who is called to serve? Everyone! 
• Why do we need to serve? To 
demonstrate our love for God and 
others! 

• Where do we need to serve? Find a 
ministry – Volunteer in an outreach! 
• Who did Jesus say would be greatest in 
the Kingdom of God? Whoever is 
servant to all! 
 
I challenge you to serve in a ministry for six 
consecutive weeks!
