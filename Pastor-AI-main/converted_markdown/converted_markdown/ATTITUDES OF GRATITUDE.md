Attitude is more of a decision than an activity. We cannot control what happens to us but we can control the attitude with which we DECIDE to confront such things.

According to Dictionary.com attitude = Manner, disposition, feeling - Tendency or orientation, especially of the mind.

Orientation = Position in relation to some benchmark.

In aeronautics the attitude of an aircraft is determined by its relationship to the ground.

When flying, the pilot has in front of him in the cockpit an "AI" or attitude indicator. The attitude indicator is an instrument used to inform the pilot of the orientation of the aircraft relative to Earth\'s horizon.

An aircraft can have three basic attitudes: (PLACE ON SCREEN ONE AT A TIME WITH THE APPROPRIATE ATTITUDE OF AN AIRCRAFT)

- When the centerline of the plane is parallel to the ground the plane is in a Level Attitude... A level attitude covers more ground over the long haul...

- When the nose of the aircraft is above the horizon, it is called a Nose High Attitude... We have to get our nose up to get off the ground... If we keep our nose up long enough we will reach an altitude the aircraft cannot sustain...

- If the nose of the aircraft is below the horizon the aircraft is in a Nose Low Attitude... At times we must get our nose down to land at a destination... (humility -- "God blesses the humble and resists the proud") If we keep our nose down too long we will crash...

While in flight, the pilot must monitor not just the plane's altitude but also the plane's attitude. If we are going to have an attitude of gratitude we must never be concerned with altitude only, but the attitude with which we are flying at that altitude!

Whether the nose is level, pointed up, or pointed down is going to make a world of difference to the pilot and any passengers he may have.

It is accurate to say, that the pilot must maintain vigilance concerning a plane's attitude, it's orientation to the horizon and ground, in order to stay on course and avoid catastrophe.
