INTRO: The book of Esther was written by Ezra. The book of Esther is a relatively short book, ten chapters and one hundred sixty five verses, but what a story it tells!

If we had time it would be very interesting to do an in depth study of each of the characters in this book, as each of them are very colorful individuals. In the interest of time we are going to focus our time on five main characters: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Ahasuerus - He is the king who ruled during the time of the Babylonian captivity.

- Vashti -- She was the queen of Ahasuerus. The book of Esther depicts Vashti as a very vain, self-willed person. Vashti means beauty. Esther 1:11 (NKJV) The King commands his servants "to bring Queen Vashti before the king, wearing her royal crown, in order to show her beauty to the people and the officials, for she was beautiful to behold."

- Mordecai -- Mordecai is from the tribe of Benjamin, however he is called a Jew, proving that the term "Jew" is used of any Israelite and not just of the tribe of Judah only. In the book of Esther the term "Jew" is even applied to gentiles who became proselytes.

- Esther -- Esther was the niece of Mordecai who was brought to the king's house to enter the contest to determine who was the most beautiful, and talented virgin in all the empire. To make a long story short, Esther won the favor of the king, as well as the contest, and became the queen of Ahasuerus!

- Haman - Haman had a very successful career as a prince under Ahasuerus, and was eventually made second in command.
