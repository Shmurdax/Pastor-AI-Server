INTRO: The dwelling place of God among 
the Israeli people was within the Holy of 
Holies. Inside the Holy of Holies was an 
interesting piece of furniture called the Ark 
of the Covenant of the Lord. (Place a 
picture of the Ark on the screen until next 
slide) 
 
The Ark was three feet, nine inches long, 
two feet three inches wide, and two feet 
three inches deep. The Ark of the Covenant 
did not represent His presence; it housed His 
presence. Because it housed His presence 
the Ark was the most hallowed and revered 
piece of furniture in the world. 
 
In the same way that the Ark housed the 
presence of God, we have become hosts of 
the Spirit of God. I Corinthians 3:16 (NKJV) 
“Do you not know that you are the temple 
of God and that the Spirit of God dwells in 
you?” 

Inside the Ark of the Covenant were: (Place 
these slides on the screen one at a time, 
with a pic of the Ark in the background) 
• The tables of the law… (Representing 
the holiness of God) 
• A golden pot of manna… (Manna was 
placed in the Ark as a reminder of all 
God had done and would do for Israel) 
• Aarons rod that budded… (Aaron’s rod 
was a constant reminder that God had 
brought them out of slavery by a strong 
hand and a mighty arm) 
 
Everywhere the Israelites went the Ark went 
first. Moses said, “If Your presence goes 
not with us, carry us not up hence.” 
 
The Ark was to be carried by the Priests and 
no one was to touch it. It was to be carried 
on the shoulders of the Priests utilizing 
staves that were fitted into the rings on each 
end of the Ark. 

Everywhere the Ark traveled there was great 
victory. So much so that the Philistines 
recognized the supernatural power that 
surrounded the Ark and decided that if they 
could capture it, they might become the 
beneficiaries of its blessings. We should 
never assume that just because a friend or 
family member is anointed or blessed we 
will be as well. The key? The Philistines 
were not of the house of Israel. You cant 
join the kingdom, you have to be born into 
it. 
 
The presence of God in the right vessel is a 
great thing; His presence in the wrong hands 
can be very destructive. 
 
In the process of time the Philistines 
captured the Ark. The capture of the Ark of 
the Covenant by the Philistines was the 
fulfillment of prophecies spoken against the 
house of Eli. This occurred because Eli's 
sons were unfaithful in executing their 

priestly responsibilities. 
 
Although Eli was apparently a good man, he 
never enforced proper correction upon his 
son’s who were performing priestly duties 
while engaging in immoral acts and 
lifestyles. Failure to execute proper 
correction at God’s house rendered Eli as an 
unfaithful priest and unfit to fill his office. 
 
What you allow at God’s house, He will 
allow at your house! 
 
When Israel engaged in battle with the 
Philistines at Ebenezer and Aphek, about 
twenty miles due west of Shiloh, where the 
tabernacle, the ark, and Eli and his sons 
were, they found themselves on the losing 
end of the conflict, and as messed up as they 
were, they called for the Ark. I Samuel 4:3-
4 (NKJV) “And when the people had come 
into the camp, the elders of Israel said, 
“Why has the LORD defeated us today 

before the Philistines? Let us bring the ark 
of the covenant of the LORD from Shiloh to 
us, that when it comes among us it may 
save us from the hand of our enemies.” 
4 So the people sent to Shiloh, that they 
might bring from there the ark of the 
covenant of the LORD of hosts, who dwells 
between the cherubim. And the two sons of 
Eli, Hophni and Phinehas, were there with 
the ark of the covenant of God.” 
 
They likely called for it because it had 
brought great victory in the past. Please 
allow me to point out an often overlooked 
fact. 1 Samuel 4:5 (NKJV) “When the ark 
of the covenant of the LORD came into the 
camp, all Israel shouted so loudly that the 
earth shook.” Just because we are excited 
about the presence of God is no indication 
that we are ready for it. No amount of 
shouting can compensate for sin in the 
camp. (Talk about the dangers of attempting 
to handle holy things with immoral actions 

and motives) 
 
This time the presence of the Ark did not 
convey victory, and Israel lost thirty 
thousand more men in the process. When the 
dust settled, the dead included Hophni, and 
Phinehas the evil sons of Eli who were 
playing a priestly role. 
 
When Eli heard the news of his sons’ death, 
his only concern was for the status of the 
Ark. When he heard of its loss, he passed 
out, fell backwards, and broke his neck in 
the fall. 
 
The final victim on that infamous day was 
Phinehas’ unnamed wife, who used her final 
breath to name her child Ichabod, meaning, 
“The glory of the Lord has departed.” 
 
The Philistines assumed the capture of the 
Ark would acquire the favor they had 
witnessed happening to the Israelites. Rather 

than favor, it brought curses on them 
 
From the time the Ark was captured until it 
arrived in Jerusalem was 92 years! Can you 
imagine having the presence of God 
displaced in your family for 92 years? 
 
The Ark was taken to Ashdod and was put 
in the temple of the Philistine god Dagon. 
However, this synthetic god could not stand 
in the presence of the Ark. 
 
In the seven months that it resided in the 
land of Philistia, the cities Ashdod, Gath, 
and Ekron experienced what was likely the 
bubonic plague, and was overrun with rats. 
 
After seven months of calamity the 
Philistines decided to see if the presence of 
the Ark was the culprit to their calamity. 
They had a brand new cart made, and 
decided to have it pulled by never-before-
harnessed cows that had young calves that 

they placed in a stall. They placed the Ark 
on it. Disgustingly, they also included 
replicas of the rats, and tumors, made of 
gold. 
 
The Philistine experts in the supernatural 
made this a litmus test to determine whether 
the Ark was the problem or their problems 
were just coincidental. 
 
The test would be that if the cows head 
straight back to where the Ark came from, 
toward Beth-she-mesh, rather than going to 
where their calves were locked up they 
would conclude this catastrophe was indeed 
a divine judgment, but if not, they would 
know that God had nothing to do with it—it 
was just a coincidence. 
 
God, in His wonderful response to queries, 
caused two un-driven cows that had never 
pulled a cart, who were mooing with hunger, 
and leaving their calves behind, to walk 

straight from Ek-ron to Beth-she-mesh, a 
border town of Israel. Amazingly, the cows 
never left the road. 
 
It is interesting to note that Ekron means to 
uproot or to immigrate! Ekron is the place 
the cows and the Ark were leaving from! 
Bethshemesh means the house of the sun or 
the sun temple. The cows were literally 
taking the presence of God in the direction 
of the sun (Son)! 
 
I want to ask us today, what direction are we 
taking our lives and our families? What 
direction are we going? 
• In the direction of materialism? 
• In the direction of secularism? 
• In the direction of the Son? 
 
The Philistines were satisfied that what they 
had experienced was in fact a judgment of 
God upon them for commandeering the Ark. 

Could it be that we are experiencing 
difficulty because we are pursuing the things 
of this world rather than the things of the 
Spirit? 
 
In these last day’s God is making an attempt 
to turn us back toward His Son even if He 
has to use calamity to get our attention.

All the while the Ark had been relegated to

It was not until David finally conquered 
Jerusalem in the seventh year of his reign 
that he called for them to bring the ark to

In preparation for the trip from the house of 
Abinadab to Jerusalem, a new cart was

Philistines getting by with carrying the Ark

Just because the world is doing it does not 
mean we should! 
 
They built a new cart and loaded the Ark

were assigned to attend it. 
 
At the threshing floor of Nachon, the oxen

Abinadab’s son Uzzah, who had been

hand to steady it. To the shock and dismay 
of everyone involved Uzzah was 
immediately struck dead. 
 
Does this seem a little severe to you? I have 
a little problem with this. In the historical 
sense, this happened because David ignored 

the instructions on how the Ark was to be 
transported and Uzzah violated the 
commandment regarding touching the Ark. 
Numbers 4:15 (NKJV) “When Aaron and 
his sons have finished covering the 
sanctuary and all the furnishings of the 
sanctuary, when the camp is set to go, then 
the sons of Kohath shall come to carry 
them; but they shall not touch any holy 
thing, lest they die. ‘These are the things in

The transport of the Ark on this journey was 
severely flawed from the beginning. They 
were carrying the Ark of God in an improper 
manner. They had very specific instructions 
from God as to how His presence was to be 
conveyed and they failed to pay attention.

The Ark was to rest upon the shoulders of 
men set apart and sanctified for the duty of 
carrying it. Uzzah was not such a man. 
 
Again, not even those whose duty it was to 
bear the ark were allowed to touch it. The 
history of improper conveyance of the Ark 
should have been enough to warn them of 
the danger that lurked nearby. On one 
occasion fifty thousand men had perished 
because they looked into the Ark. 
 
Uzzah in his zeal and hot impatient 
eagerness, tried to steady the ark and as a 
result, he was struck dead. 
 
I believe this is a warning for all those in 
this day who are making attempts to steady 
the Ark. Here is what an attempt to steady 
the Ark looks like today: (Place on the 
screen one at a time) 
• A failure to preach the whole counsel of 
God… 

• A determination to engage people with 
no plan for real life change… 
• A failure to introduce people to the 
Holiness of God… 
• Believing that methods don’t matter 
motives are what is really important… 
• Relegating the work of the Holy Spirit to 
a back room or beyond the property 
boundary… (This is done so that givers 
won’t be offended) 
 
Think about Uzzah’s life. He was the son of 
Abinadab, in whose house the Ark had been 
stored for so many years. He had grown up 
with the Ark in his house. He and his brother 
were charged with taking care of it as it 
made its way from their home to its new 
resting place in Jerusalem. 
 
Uzzah probably felt a special sense of 
closeness with the Ark. He makes one fatal 
mistake – he touched the Ark and tried to 
steady it. 

If we put ourselves in the place of Uzzah, 
it’s easy to understand why he would do 
so. He was used to having it around – 
although he knew it was holy, it was also a 
familiar thing. It seemed perfectly natural to 
try to steady it, to keep it from falling. 
 
Could it be that those of us in this room who 
have contended for Pentecost are just as 
guilty of a lack of respect for the presence of 
God as those who have relegated His 
presence to a back room or abandoned it 
altogether? 
 
Being a champion of Pentecost is not 
enough! Believing in the supernatural is not 
adequate! We cannot allow for anything to 
become a substitute for the miracle working 
power of God to be ushered back into His 
church! 

Like Uzzah, we are often guilty of reaching 
out with our hands instead of reaching up 
with our hearts. We are guilty of trying to do 
spiritual work in the power of the flesh. 
 
Could it be that we have become so 
enamored with defending His presence that 
in reality we are not experiencing His 
presence? What does His presence look like 
in a ministry? 
• Souls saved… 
• Believers filled… 
• The bound delivered… 
• The broken hearted healed… 
• The poor being touched with acts of 
kindness… 
 
Quite frankly, David was terrified by the 
power of the Ark after it killed Uzzah, and 
he suspended its journey for three months. 
 
When we realize we have strayed form His 
presence, and His power is no longer 

present, we should suspend everything and 
call a fast; declare a solemn assembly; and 
pray heaven down in our town.

When Uzzah died David asked Obed-Edom 
if he could leave the Ark at his house. David 

wasn’t just asking for a place to park the 
Ark, he was asking if Obed would do what 
needed to be done to attend the Ark. In 
effect he was saying “Can we trust you with 
this Ark?” 
 
Against the backdrop of the death of Uzzah, 
wouldn’t we be a little more than reluctant 
to have the Ark parked at our house? There 
is no sign of hesitation on the part of Obed, 
he was not afraid to receive the Ark. 
 
Obed knew that a man with clean hands and 
a pure heart had nothing to fear from the 
Lord. 
 
I believe the lack of hesitation on the part of 
Obed is because he already had his house in 
order. Before the Ark ever arrived Obed 
feared and served the God of the ark, and so 
he knew that the presence of the Ark, which 
had been a source of disaster to the 

unworthy, would be a source of unbroken 
blessing to him and his household. 
 
I ask every preacher and church member in 
this house, can we trust this Ark to you? Can 
we leave this Ark with you and trust you to 
treat it in a proper manner? Jude 1:3 (NKJV) 
“Beloved, while I was very diligent to write 
to you concerning our common salvation, I 
found it necessary to write to you exhorting 
you to contend earnestly for the faith which 
was once for all delivered to the saints.” 
 
Remember, while the Ark was in the 
possession of the Philistines it brought 
curses; when it was in the custody of 
Abinadab it never seemed to bring a 
blessing or a curse; but when Obed-Edom 
hosted the Ark, it blessed him and his entire 
house! 

The ark of God, that overthrew Dagon and 
smote Uzzah, was nothing but a fountain of 
blessing in the household of Obed-Edom. 
 
What was the difference? The attitude and 
priority with which Obed-Edom cared for 
the Ark, released the blessing from the 
storehouse of God. Can we leave this Ark 
with you? Will you give it priority? 
 
In a three-month period the fortunes of 
Obed’s house took a drastic turn: 
• His house turned from poverty to 
prominence… (God is El Shaddai not El 
Get-by!) 
• He became a man of honor rather than 
being despised... (Obed means slave – 
Every time someone called him by his 
name it was a painful reminder of who 
he was – The name Edom was assigned 
to him because he was an Edomite and a 
descendent of Esau - He was a “Gittite” 
this means that he was from the city of 

Gath, the place of David’s greatest 
enemy, Goliath.) 
• Obed and his descendants became 
addicted to the presence of God… 
 
Because he gave the Ark proper respect, 
Obed reversed the curse and found his 
destiny. 
 
The day came that David returned to retrieve 
the Ark. Obed had a choice to make: 
• Go back to his old way of life… 
• Grieve the absence of the Ark… 
• Pack up and follow the Ark… 
 
Apparently, during the three months that 
passed between the death of Uzzah and 
David’s decision to go get the Ark, he had 
been doing his homework. Either someone 
told David how the Ark was supposed to be 
moved, or he read the Law for himself. 
Either way, he went and got it and brought it 
home the right way. This time there would 

be no shortcuts and no disobedience; David 
would do it the right way. He would do it 
the Lord’s way! Just to be sure, the Levites 
carried the Ark in the proper manner and 
stopped after they had gone six steps and 
sacrificed to the Lord. 
 
When David came to take the Ark to 
Jerusalem Obed and his house left all and 
followed the Ark! 
 
When David came to take the Ark to 
Jerusalem, Obed and his house left all and 
followed the Ark! 
 
This was an easy choice for Obed; he would 
give up his life and livelihood and follow the 
presence of God. In so doing his attitude, 
actions, and aspirations created a legacy of 
faith, and relationship with the Lord. The 
Lord was faithful back to Obed-Edom 
establishing a legacy of generational 
blessings. 

Obed Edom: 
• Became the keeper of the gate at the 
Temple… 
• Became a singer and musician in the 
house of the Lord… 
• Became the keeper of the treasury… 
What does each of these responsibilities 
have in common? Each job change moved 
him closer to the Ark! 
 
His addiction to the presence of God caused 
Obed Edom to pass down a generational 
blessing to his offspring. He had 62 
descendents that are all called blessed of 
God and served in the temple! 
 
It doesn’t matter what sort of background, 
education, or family history you have. When 
you have the good sense to give His 
presence the honor it deserves you are going 
to be blessed and so will your offspring.

If we can be trusted with His presence we

accompany the Ark. If we treat His presence

struggling in every area of our lives. I want

You can leave this Ark with me! Can we
