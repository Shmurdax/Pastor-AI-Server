INTRO: 1 Chronicles 16 is one of the first times the expression
"Give Thanks" is used in the Bible. I stand to echo the words of
the Psalmist who said, "Give thanks." Thanksgiving should not be a
one-time-a- year event but the daily practice of every Christian.

In this passage David is welcoming the Ark of the Covenant back to its
rightful place in the tabernacle among the appliances of worship.

I want to give you three reasons from 1 Chronicles 16 that we should
live a thankful life by giving thanks to the Lord.

## I. WE SHOULD BE THANKFUL THAT HE IS A GOD OF SECOND CHANCES...

David had just attempted to relocate the Ark of God from the house of
Abinadab to Jerusalem. Remember, in the Old Testament, the Ark was where
God lived among His people.

David was bringing the Ark back to Jerusalem, however, it wasn\'t
handled correctly and David's good intentions ended in disaster with the
death of Uzza. David attempted to do a good thing the wrong way.

Even though his first attempt ended in a catastrophe, God allowed him
another chance and this time he succeeded. 

David then authors a psalm of thanksgiving in 1 Chronicles 16. You can
tell he is deeply grateful to the Lord, as we should all be, when we
have another shot to get something right. 

One thing David admonished us to do in verse 10 is to give "glory in
His Holy name," which means we should bask in who He is in order to
keep our hearts filled with gratitude.  

One verse about God's name that comes to mind is something else David
said in Psalm 90:2. He says of the Lord, "from everlasting to
everlasting you are God." David is saying from beginning to end, from
eternity past to eternity present, He is God!

And because of this, He sees our lives from a far bigger picture than we
do. We are ready to give up after one attempt but God encourages us to
keep moving forward: "If at first we don't succeed, try try, again!"
(Thomas H. Parker)

God remains steadfastly at work in us long after we have given up on
ourselves.

I'm thankful that God doesn't dwell on our short-term failures but
rather, works in us for our long-term potential.

When we mess up and fess up, God moves on, and so should we! The problem
is that we get high-centered in our mess when God is attempting to coax
us into a new opportunity.

He will extend a second chance, or even a third, fourth, and fifth if
necessary in order to see his will prevail in our lives. That is a very
good reason to give thanks!

## II. WE SHOULD BE THANKFUL THAT GOD CREATES BIG THINGS FROM SMALL
BEGINNINGS...

In this Psalm of thanks, David recalls the history of God's people and
God's faithfulness to them up to the present point. 

Let me pause and throw in a principle of gratitude worth remembering:
Remembering God's faithfulness in our PAST should help us REJOICE in
our PRESENT because His PAST RECORD is a good indicator of His FUTURE
ACTIVITY!

In this passage, David recalls just how fragile and small God's people
started out. They went to Egypt a family (66 people) and left a nation
(Of 2 ½ - 3 million people).

In Canaan the story is even greater, they multiplied exponentially and
became a nation of multiplied millions.  

David says in verses 19-20, "When they were but few in number, few
indeed, and strangers in it, they wandered from nation to nation, from
one kingdom to another." David says, "This whole thing started
small, but God did big things to get us to this point!" 

And God still does today:

- He is still able to calm a storm...

- He can still multiply the loaves and the fish...

- He can still stop the mouths of hungry lions...

- He can still rob a furnace of its fire...

- He can still order the steps of good people... 

- He is still a rewarder to those who diligently seek Him... 

Here is a truth we must never forget: "In seasons of WANDERING we lose
our WONDER!"

In those seasons when we feel small and WANDER aimlessly because we fail
to understand what in the world is going on, we cannot allow ourselves
to lose our WONDER of God.

Here is a good definition of the word "wander" and "wonder" that is
worth remembering:

- To wander-means to ponder the affairs of your life without God in
 mind... 

- To wonder-means to ponder the affairs of your life with God and
 His character in mind... 

Here is the difference between WANDERING and WONDER:

- One leads to despair, the other devotion...

- One leads to fear, and the other faith...

- One leads to doubt, the other to destiny...

- One leads to mistakes, the other to miracles...

- One leads to denial, the other to determination...

Let\'s keep our WONDER even in seasons of WANDERING, and do as God said
in Zechariah 4:10 "Do not despise the days of small beginnings."
God made a nation of a family! Give thanks for small beginnings.

We see the present circumstances for what they are; God sees them for
what they are going to be! If we could see what God sees we would quit
WANDERING and engage in WONDERMENT!  

## III. WE SHOULD GIVE THANKS FOR HIS NAME...

David say's in verse 29, "ascribe to the Lord the glory that is due
his name." 

In the beginning of this chapter, after the Ark of the Lord had been
returned, David actually appointed people whose sole job was to
continually offer thanksgiving to the Lord. He then encourages EVERYONE
to do the same! 

In other words, David was very intentional in offering gratitude to God,
and so should we. We should ascribe to the Lord the praise that is due
Him.  

To ascribe means to assign or give credit, and that is exactly what we
are doing when we express praise and gratitude to God.

However, there is a powerful principle when it comes to being
intentional in our praise and here it is:  

- Our praise ATTACHES us to the presence of God and DETACHES us from the
 presence of anything else that would drag us down! 

- Essentially, when we ascribe praise to God, we attach ourselves to
 God's presence and we can say what David says in Psalm 16:11, "You
 make known to me the path of life; you will fill me with joy in your
 presence, with eternal pleasures at your right hand."

If we don't have anything else to praise Him for we should praise Him
for His name:

- His name is a saving name...

- His name is a delivering name...

- His name is a healing name...

- His name is a redeeming name...

- His name is above every name...

CONCLUSION:

Here is a bottom line. We must not forget that:

- Our gratitude is a gateway to Gods blessing, and a giant leap forward
 to His glory...

<!-- -->

- Our thanks is a trap to the enemy and a cry of triumph to our
 troubles...

- Our praise is poison to our perplexities and a passing lane to
 prosperity...

- Our worship is a weapon in the hands of God and a deathblow to the
 worries that crease our brows...

This truth alone makes me grateful for the privilege of offering
thanksgiving itself to God!
