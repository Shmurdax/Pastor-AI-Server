TEXT: Genesis 6:5-8 (NKJV) "Then the Lord saw that the wickedness of man was great in the earth, and that every intent of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually. ^6 ^And the Lord was sorry that He had made man on the earth, and He was grieved in His heart. ^7 ^So the Lord said, "I will destroy man whom I have created from the face of the earth, both man and beast, creeping thing and birds of the air, for I am sorry that I have made them." ^8 ^But Noah found grace in the eyes of the Lord."

INTRO: God created Adam and Eve and placed them in the garden. In short order they sinned against God and were driven out of the garden. When they were expelled from the paradise of God, the curse of sin began to take affect.

The effects of sin are so brutal that only ten generations passed from Adam to Noah and in Genesis chapter six we find God repenting that he had even made man! Some of the saddest words in Scripture can be found in Genesis 6:6-7 (NKJV) "And the Lord was sorry that He had made man on the earth, and He was grieved in His heart. ^7 ^So the Lord said, "I will destroy man whom I have created from the face of the earth, both man and beast, creeping thing and birds of the air, for I am sorry that I have made them."

Let me give you some information about Noah:

- He is listed as the ninth descendent of Adam...

- He was 480 years old when he received the call to build the Ark... Genesis 6:8 (NKJV) "But Noah found grace in the eyes of the Lord." One transalation say's "But Noah was different. God liked what he saw in Noah."

- When Noah was 480 years old, God not only called him to build an ark, He also let him know that He was going to destroy every wicked person from the face of the earth. Yet, Noah chose to have children at 500 years old (20 years after God called him), 100 years before the flood came! If you knew the world was about to be destroyed, why would you have children? Noah was confident that he could raise godly seed in an ungodly world! Wow! Do we have the same confidence with regard to our children?

We are living in times that mirror the wickedness of Noah's day! Luke 17:26-27 (NKJV) "And as it was in the days of Noah, so it will be also in the days of the Son of Man: ^27 ^They ate, they drank, they married wives, they were given in marriage, until the day that Noah entered the ark, and the flood came and destroyed them all."

The story of Noah and his actions should preach to every leader of every family today. Noah needed to build an ark to save his family and sustain the human race. Look at this again... Genesis 6:8 "But Noah was different. God liked what he saw in Noah."

Noah made a break with his culture and dared to be different. Anyone who hopes to save his or her family MUST make a break with this culture!

What was so different about Noah? Genesis 6:9 (NKJV) "Noah was a Just man, perfect in his generations, and Noah walked with God." The scripture points out three things that Noah did that every head of every family MUST DO if we hope to save our families:

- He was a JUST man... The word just means righteous. He was focused on making the right decision every time! Not what was convenient or popular -- The right decision!

- He was PERFECT in his generations... This is not insinuating that Noah never made a mistake. The Word is pointing to the fact that Noah lived an exemplary life before his decedents. He knew that the time would come that he would need to instruct his family to board the ark and when he did, he needed to be believable to them.

- Noah WALKED with God... He was faithful! He followed the commands of God no matter what! One command from God lasted Noah 120 years!

Noah's sons grew up watching their father work on the project that would save them! As unpopular as it was at the time, Noah's most public accomplishment was the building of an ark to save his house! Hebrews 11:7 (NIV) "By faith Noah, when warned about the things not yet seen, in holy fear built an ark to save his family." (Notice not himself -- His family!) By his faith he condemned the world and became the heir of the righteousness that is in keeping the faith." We do not know what Noah did for a living -- We do know what he did to save his family!

I want to ask every head of every family, what will our families remember us for?

- The things we provide?

- That we made every sporting event?

- That we took them on world-class vacations?

- That we were a part of the in crowd, and kept company with the upper class? (The upper crust is a bunch of crumbs held together by some dough)

We need to be remembered for preparing an ark to save our families. (Joshua -- As for me and my house...)

A lot of things happened during the 120 years of building the ark:

- Noah had to make a living...

- The pressures of raising a family persisted...

- The ridicule his family endured had to be addressed...

- Noah had to stay focused despite the pressure to become distracted...

One word from God lasted Noah 120 years! Can we be consistently righteous even when God is silent?

CONCLUSION:

Noah did not have the opportunity to test the ark; he had to make sure it was waterproof. I ask us, is our family waterproof in these last days?

Genesis 7:1 (NKJV) "Then the Lord said to Noah, "Come into the ark, you and all your household, because I have seen that you are righteous before Me in this generation."

Notice the results -- Genesis 7:13 Noah -- Mrs. Noah -- Their three sons -- Their three daughter-in-laws boarded the ark.

Noah saved his entire family! Save yourself and save your family.

Jesus is coming is your family ready? Save your family!

I want to say to every parent in this place today; the only way to save your family is by putting your trust in Jesus.
