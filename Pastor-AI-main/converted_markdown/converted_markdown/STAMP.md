INTRODUCTION: You and I know that a little postage stamp can't really speak or preach a sermon, but if it could, I believe the stamp would give us these "Seven Sentence Sermons."

# I. I REPRESENT MY COUNTRY

The stamp shows what country it represents. As a Christian, you should represent your country (Heaven).

We are called to be "ambassadors of Christ" (II Corinthians 5:20). Heaven is our home, we should be a

good representative for it.

## II. I AM ALWAYS READY FOR SERVICE

A stamp is always ready to be of service. We should be also. Peter was ready for service (Luke 22:33).

Paul was ready for service (Romans 1:15). God tells us, "be ready always" (I Peter 3:15).

## III. I GO WHEREVER I AM SENT

A stamp never argues about where it is to go, Jesus says to us, "As my Father hath sent me, even so

send I you" (John 20:21) and "..Go..preach the gospel..." (Mark 16:15). "Here am I; send me" (Isaiah 6:8).

## IV. I STICK TO MY TASK UNTIL IT IS DONE

Perseverance is a wonderful trait. Many people do not stick to their task until finished. God tells us:

"Therefore, my beloved brethren, be ye steadfast, unmoveable.." (I Corinthians 15:58).

He promises, (I Chronicles 28:20).

## V. I DON'T STRIKE BACK WHEN I AM STRUCK

Jesus instructed us: Resist not evil: but whosoever shall smite thee on the right cheek, turn to him the

other also." (Matthew 5:39)

## VI. I DON'T GIVE UP WHEN I AM LICKED

Paul said, "...we are struck down to the ground, but never struck out and destroyed" (II Cor. 4:9)

## VII. I AM SMALL, BUT I CARRY GREAT MESSAGES

Very important messages are sent in letters, but the greatest message is God's message to a lost and

dying world. Here is a great message from God: "For God so loved the world, that he gave his only

begotten So, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life." (John 3:16)
