# BROKEN THINGS

TEXT: 1 Corinthians 11:23-30 (NKJV) "For I received from the Lord
that which I also delivered to you: that the Lord Jesus on the same
night in which He was betrayed took bread; 24 and when He had given
thanks, He [broke]{.underline} it and said, "Take, eat; this is My body
which is [broken]{.underline} for you; do this in remembrance of Me."
25 In the same manner He also took the cup after supper, saying, "This
cup is the new covenant in My blood. This do, as often as you drink it,
in remembrance of Me." 26 For as often as you eat this bread and drink
this cup, you proclaim the Lord's death till He comes. 27 Therefore
whoever eats this bread or drinks this cup of the Lord in an unworthy
manner will be guilty of the body and blood of the Lord. 28 But let a
man examine himself, and so let him eat of the bread and drink of the
cup. 29 For he who eats and drinks in an unworthy manner eats and drinks
judgment to himself, not discerning the Lord's body. 30 For this reason
many are weak and sick among you, and many sleep."

INTRO: My mom used to say "I can't have anything nice with you
around."

Our world discounts the value of broken things therefore, rather than
fixing the things that are broken we tend to discard them:

- Resale and thrift stores... It is here that you can purchase things
 for pennies on the dollar (Susan say's "I am saving you money)

- Landfills... If you see a hill in Houston it is a landfill or an
 overpass -- It is here that valuable things have been discarded,
 because they have lost their value

- Wrecking yards... Women's parking lot - Vehicles that have been
 wrecked are torn apart, crushed and recycled

The Bible is a book about broken things:

- Broken world... The sin of Adam and Eve introduced brokenness to an
 unbroken world -- Adam broke paradise

- Broken law... Aaron said, "We threw the gold in and out walked a
 calf!" -- If we break one part of the law, it is utterly broken

- Broken people... Noah got drunk -- Abraham lied -- Isaac lied -- They
 just named Jacob liar - David cheated -- Peter cussed -- Paul said
 "I am the chief of sinners..."

- Broken promises... Promises are made to be broken

Twice in our text Paul uses the word broken to describe the very
important ordinance of communion, "When He had given thanks, He
[broke]{.underline} it and said, "Take, eat; this is My body which is
[broken]{.underline} for you; do this in remembrance of Me."

Today, I want to look at three broken things as we partake of the Lords
Supper.

## I. A BROKEN LIFE...

The only reason we can take communion today is because Jesus allowed His
life to be broken:

- He was only 33 years old when He was crucified... That is broken!

- He lived a sinless life, yet He was treated as guilty of the worst of
 society... That is broken!

- His human relationships would completely change because of his beating
 and crucifixion... Peter was not the only one to deny Jesus on the day
 of His crucifixion -- Simon carried His cross -- The disciples forsook
 Him and fled -- That is broken!

As Jesus broke the bread and distributed it to His disciples, He had in
the mind the broken lives of every man, woman, and child that would ever
live.

He could look at all of us, from Adam down through all of His
descendants, and His heart was stirred with a desire for reconciliation
and restoration.

God's deepest desire is that the broken lives of today become the
restored lives of tomorrow. When Jesus sees the brokenness of our souls,
He is moved to compassion.

The work of Christ was directed toward the brokenness of the human race.
Luke 4:18-19 (NIV) "The Spirit of the Lord is on me, because he has
anointed me to preach [good news to the poor]{.underline}. He has sent
me to proclaim [freedom for the prisoners]{.underline} and [recovery of
sight for the blind]{.underline}, to [release the
oppressed]{.underline}, to proclaim the year of the Lord's favor"

## II. A BROKEN BODY...

Those attending the kangaroo court and crucifixion of Jesus had a front
row seat to the most horrific breaking of a human body ever to be
witnessed by humankind.

- They heard the power of the whip that beat his back to a bloody
 pulp...

- They heard the "Thud" of the cat of nine tails as it found its
 target in the flesh and muscle in the back of Jesus...

- They could see the bloody sweat as it covered the whipping post...

- They heard the dripping of His blood and see it form a dirty pool on
 the ground...

- They saw His sinless blood trickle down His naked side and drip off
 His toes...

- They saw His blood oozing from the spikes in His wrists and ankles...

- They saw blood and water gush into a sacrificial fountain as the spear
 was thrust into His side...

- They heard the sighs, the groans of the Savior as He submitted to the
 agony thrust upon Him by the sins of the world...

- They could see the fierce pain and rejection crease His brow as God
 the Father turned His back because He could not bear to look upon Him.
 this occurred because of the enormity of the sin laid upon Him...

- They could clearly see the parched lips of a sinless savior, as he
 begged for a drink of water but was offered vinegar on a sponge...
 (Talk about this)

- They heard the passion in His voice as He prayed, "Forgive them,
 for they do not know what they are doing."

Paul's Corinthian audience were very much aware to the gravity of His
words as he mentioned the breaking of the bread that points to the
breaking of His body!

His body was broken FOR US!

Fanny Crosby wrote these timeless words:

"Jesus, keep me near the cross, There a precious fountain - Free to
all, a healing stream - Flows from Calv'ry's mountain.

Refrain:

In the cross, in the cross, Be my glory ever -

Till my raptured soul shall find - Rest beyond the river.

Near the cross, a trembling soul, Love and Mercy found me - There the
bright and morning star - Sheds its beams around me.

Near the cross! O Lamb of God, Bring its scenes before me - Help me
walk from day to day, With its shadows o'er me.

Near the cross I'll watch and wait, Hoping, trusting ever - Till I
reach the golden strand,

Just beyond the river."

The Lord's Supper represents the broken body of Jesus Christ on the
Cross. But more than that, it represents the length to which God is
willing to go to restore a broken world, and the broken lives of all who
dwell there.

## III. A BROKEN HEART...

The heart of Jesus was broken for the sins of the world. Two times we
are told that Jesus wept:

- Over the city of Jerusalem... Luke 19 "Jerusalem, Jerusalem, how
 often would I have gathered you under my wings as a hen does her
 chickens..."

- At the tomb of Lazarus... John 11 (Shortest verse in the Bible -- The
 people said, "See how He loved him...)

The heart of Jesus was no doubt broken as He celebrated the Passover
with His disciples knowing that Judas had already betrayed Him!

We look at how Judas betrayed Jesus and say "How could he have done
such a thing?"

Allow me to put it into perspective by saying:

- We are all guilty of the sin of Adam... Intentional sin - breaks the
 heart of Jesus!

- We are all guilty of the sin of Peter... We have rejected Him by
 distancing ourselves from Him! This breaks His heart!

- We are all guilty of the sin of Judas... We have sold Him for far less
 than thirty pieces of silver! This breaks His heart!

CONCLUSION:

As I take communion today I do so realizing:

- My foolish actions drove the spikes into His wrists and ankles...

- My bad attitude thrust the spear into His side...

- My complacency moved Him to the action of the cross...

- My cruelty fueled His compassion...

- My sin brought His grace...
