Day 39 -- February 8

(Reading Assignment Pages 170-174 of LaGard Smith's Daily Chronological
Bible)

Numbers 12 & 13

📖

Ever wished you could have a sneak peak into the future? The story of
"spying out the land" was exactly that.

•

But do you realize that to "spy out the land" was not God's idea?
He already knew what was in the land, and it was the land He had
promised to them.

•

A key word used in Numbers 13:2 reveals that it was not God's idea to do
this. It's the Hebrew word for "send". In Hebrew the word is
Shelah-lekha; which means, "Send for yourself." In other words,
God is telling Moses, "If you want to send some spies, you have my
permission."

•

Sending spies was not the impulse of God, rather, it was that God was
RESPONDING to a REQUEST from MOSES, and Moses was replying to the
REQUEST of the PEOPLE, by taking the matter to the Lord. It was the
people of Israel who were requesting this because they were unsure of
the promise.... They were still doubting... They were still fearful,
and lacking faith

•

The details of why the spies were sent is further revealed in
Deuteronomy 1:22-23. "And every one of you came near to me and said,
'Let us send men before us, and let them search out the land for us, and
bring back word to us of the way by which we should go up, and of the
cities into which we shall come.' "The plan pleased me well; so I took
twelve of your men, one man from each tribe." The "sneak peek" into
the Promised Land backfired on the Children of Israel. They were minutes
away from seeing their promise fulfilled, but the request to "see for
ourselves" sent them backwards and wandering for 40 years. Except for
Caleb and Joshua, the people never got to see the land of promise that
God had led them all this way for. "Faith is the substance of things
hoped for and evidence of things NOT SEEN!" To request a sneak peek
into the future reveals a lack of faith and suggests that we doubt God's
plan for us. All we need to do is just trust Him. If there are giants
when we arrive, He will already have a plan for their defeat and our
victory.

Day 40 February 9

(Reading Assignment Pages 174-179 of LaGard Smith's Daily Chronological
Bible)

Numbers 16-17-18

📖

Korah, the Levite, began a rebellion against the leadership of Moses
with Dathan and Abiram co-operating. Interesting little fact about
Dathan and Abiram... Dathan and Abiram's tribal founder, Reuben, was the
rightful firstborn of Jacob and by custom should have been handed the
leadership of Israel upon Jacob's death. Jacob rejected Reuben and
bypassed him and gave the firstborn rights to Judah and Joseph. This was
done because Reuben had slept with Jacob's concubine. These descendants
of Reuben, after all this time, still had not accepted God's will given
through Jacob. They had never come to grips with the fact that THEY
would not lead Israel.

•

A little leftover bitterness in the family line? Not just a little
bitterness... A lot of bitterness! By this time, Reuben has been dead
for at least 300 years. And yet this bitterness has been festering all
this time in the descendants of Rueben.

•

The path to open rebellion against God and his established leadership
structure began with dissatisfaction and skepticism, then led to
grumbling, then onto bitterness and resentment, followed by rebellion
and open hostility. If we find ourselves often dissatisfied, skeptical,
complaining or bitter--- We should beware of the seeds the enemy is
trying to grow within us.

•

Alignment to spiritual authority is a big deal, and to get out of
alignment doesn't just affect us, it can cause collateral damage for
generations to come. The collateral damage in the story of Korah is
tragic, in that it was not just the men who were killed, women and
children also died.

All of their belongings, their tents and all their physical possessions
were also swallowed up. And one man - The rebel - Levite Korah,
instigated this whole mess.

•

May we never be guilty of rebellion against authority but continually be
in perfect alignment. If we rebel we become a rebel.
