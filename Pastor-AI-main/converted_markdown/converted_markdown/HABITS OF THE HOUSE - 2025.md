# HABITS OF THE HOUSE

TEXT: 2 Chronicles 6 (Solomons Dedicatory prayer for the newly constructed Temple)

INTRO: The setting of the sixth chapter of 2 Chronicles is as follows:

- Solomon had built a house for God...

- The construction of the Temple had been completed...

- The Temple had been furnished according to the instructions of the Lord through Moses...

- The people had gathered for the dedication of this historic structure...

- Solomon spoke to the people outlining how the building had come about... (You have spoken with your mouth and performed it with your hand)

- Solomon reminded God of His promises to His people and staked a claim for those promises...

Solomon then laid out what He wanted Gods attitude, but more importantly the attitude of the people to be, concerning the prayers of the people regarding the human condition.

It is my belief that the prayer of Solomon should be the habits of the church. We must remember, the attitude with which we approach life and approach people, will make an impact upon the lives of those who have never been born again, and those who have broken fellowship with God and/or the church.

THIS CHURCH SHOULD HAVE A HABIT OF FORGIVENESS

2 Chronicles 6:21 (NKJV) "May You hear the supplications of Your servant and of Your people Israel, when they pray toward this place. Hear from heaven Your dwelling place, and when You hear, forgive."

Solomon's request was that God, and the people, would be quick to forgive sinners, and one another, and, that the Temple would always be a place of forgiveness.

Certainly, God is a God of forgiveness. Therefore, the church should be a place of forgiveness. Sadly, the Church is often a place of penance rather than a place of forgiveness. The horrible habit of the organized church is to kill its wounded. It is sometimes a place where peoples history is meticulously maintained:

- That is Bob the boozer...

- That is Dan the drunk...

- That is Andy the addict...

- That is Sally, the home wrecker...

- That is Frances the fornicator...

- That is Chris and Christy, the people who had their children out of wedlock...

That cannot, will not be the habit of Community Transformation Church! When people come to Christ, we must forgive them and assist them in forgiving themselves. The church has almost put the devil out of his own business -- His business is that he is an accuser of everyone:

- He accuses us to God -- Job

- He accuses us to others -- Gossip

- He accuses us to ourselves -- Self condemnation -- low self-esteem -- Under performance -- Oblivious to purpose and destiny

Psalm 103:12 (NKJV) "As far as the east is from the west, So far has He removed our transgressions from us."

THIS CHURCH SHOULD HAVE A HABIT OF EXTENDING MERCY

2 Chronicles 6:22-23 (NKJV) "If anyone sins against his neighbor, and is forced to take an oath, and comes and takes an oath before Your altar in this temple, 23 then hear from heaven, and act, and judge Your servants, bringing retribution on the wicked by bringing his way on his own head, and justifying the righteous by giving him according to his righteousness."

Solomon was saying he wanted the Temple to be a place that extended mercy! His prayer was that...

- If someone had taken an oath, they could not live up to... The Temple would be a house of mercy!

- If anyone sinned against his neighbor... The Temple would be a house of mercy!

- If anyone missed the mark... The Temple would be a house of mercy!

- In retribution... The Temple would be a house of mercy! Retribution = "A form of punishment or payback that is considered morally right and deserved in response to wrongdoing or harm." (Chat GPT 08/01/25) King David committed adultery, Fathered an illegitimate child, and murdered a man, yet God never sat him down from the throne! HOWEVER, he reaped the negative consequences of his actions for a lifetime!

We are ok with having God's mercy meter reset in our lives every day, but how willing are we to push the reset button for others?

If we are going to reach people, this must be a house of mercy!

May this church be a place that is marked by mercy!

Conclusion:

Community Transformation is and will always be, a place of forgiveness and mercy:

- We will accept you where you are...

- We will accept you as you are...

- We will forgive you...

- We will help you know how to forgive yourself...

- We will help you redeem your past...

- We will help you find your purpose...

- We will help you step into your destiny...

We will:

- Crawl with you if you need to crawl...

- We will walk with you if you need to walk...

- We will run with you if you can run...

Warning: If you stop, we are moving on!

(End of sermon one)

THE CHURCH SHOULD BE A PLACE THAT HAS THE HABIT OF PROTECTION AND VICTORY

TEXT: 2 Chronicles 6:28-30 (NKJV) "When there is famine in the land, pestilence or blight or mildew, locusts or grasshoppers; when their enemies besiege them in the land of their cities; whatever plague or whatever sickness there is; 29 whatever prayer, whatever supplication is made by anyone, or by all Your people Israel, when each one knows his own burden and his own grief, and spreads out his hands to this temple: 30 then hear from heaven Your dwelling place, and forgive, and give to everyone according to all his ways, whose heart You know (for You alone know the hearts of the sons of men)."

INTRO: In the last few weeks, we have talked about the need for the church to have a habit of extending:

- Forgiveness...

- Mercy...

- Restoration...

- Harvest and Prosperity...

Solomon prays that the Temple will be a place of protection! If the church is a place of protection, it will become a big deal to those who feel unprotected.

The world that we exist in is a dangerous place to say the least. Literally everywhere we turn we find a predatory environment. This predatory environment includes our:

- Spirituality... We face principalities, powers, the rulers of the darkness of this world, and wickedness in high places. Despite this, it is our responsibility to focus on bringing Heaven to Houston!

- Finances... Literally everywhere we turn, the world is nickel and diming us to death. The cost of living is out of control. Taxes are obscene. Yet, the people of God strive to live by a Kingdom economy.

- Physical well-being... We live in unredeemed flesh. When we get saved our spirit was made right with God. Following conversion, it is a constant fight to bring the soul and body under subjection to the Lordship of Christ.

- Relationships... Relationships have had issues since the fall of man. All of us have issues with maintaining our relationships in Godliness and the bond of peace. As Christ followers we are to reflect Christlikeness in every relationship.

Solomon prayed that the Temple would be a big deal because it was a place of protection. Listen to how he prays...

- When there is famine in the land... May the Temple be a safe zone!

- When the people are overwhelmed by sickness... May the Temple be a safe zone!

- When plagues overrun the land... May the Temple be a safe zone!

- When there is terror on every side... May the Temple be a safe zone!

My prayer is that Community Transformation Church will have the habit of being a place of protection from a hostile, hateful world!

This should be a place where people know they have entered a safe zone! A place where we have each other's backs! If you take one of us on, you will have to deal with all of us!

Leviticus 26:8 (NKJV) "Five of you shall chase a hundred, and a hundred of you shall put ten thousand to flight; your enemies shall fall by the sword before you." If one extrapolates the effect created by "Five chasing a hundred" it stands to reason that one hundred can chase two thousand. Yet the Bible says, "A hundred of you can put ten thousand to flight!"

Because of the synergy of people fighting together, the ability of a person to produce rises from a ratio of one-to-twenty to one-to-one hundred!

What would happen if the several hundred people in this room fought back-to-back through every issue?

I tell you what would happen -- This would be a safe zone and would be a big deal to every person who feels threatened by this hostile world we live in.
