INTRO: The book of Second Corinthians is Paul the Apostles second letter to the Corinthian church. The Corinthian church was located in the ancient city of Corinth and the moral climate of the city of Corinth was such, that by the most conservative of estimates, it was a city of great wickedness. The wickedness of the city caused it to be very fertile soil for the seed of the Gospel! Romans 5:20 (KJV) "... Where sin abounded, grace did much more abound."

Paul was the founder of the Corinthian church, having established it on one of his missionary journeys. The Apostle spent a great deal of time in the city of Corinth declaring the good news of Jesus Christ, but he also expended much effort and energy giving written instructions to these people on how to order their personal lives, as well as their religious activities.

Paul begins verse eight of our text by saying, "for though I made you sorry with a letter..." This letter was also referred to in 2 Corinthians 2:4 (KJV) "For out of much affliction and anguish of heart I wrote unto you with many tears; not that ye should be grieved, but that ye might know the love which I have more abundantly unto you."

There are two schools of thought with regard to this seemingly mysterious "letter." One school of thought is that there was a very severe "letter" from Paul to the Corinthian Church that was somehow lost and never recorded. The second school of thought is that he is referring to the book of First Corinthians. After much study I am inclined to agree with this second scenario. The reason I am inclined to agree with the latter of these two opinions is because of what Paul said in his first letter to the Corinthian Christians! He addresses:

1) Divisiveness in the Church...

2) The source of the divisiveness...

3) The absence of discipline in the church...

4) Civil litigation between members of the body of Christ...

5) The moral decline of members of the church...

6) Marriage and divorce...

7) The eating of food sacrificed to idols. Etc...

This kind of a letter is no big deal unless you are a guilty party. If you are a guilty party, this kind of preaching will make you do one of two things, get mad, or repent. An elderly preacher once told me, "If you throw a rock into a pack of dogs the only one who barks is the one who got hit!"

This brings us to our subject matter for this message, which is the importance of repentance. The doctrine of repentance is a very important doctrine in your Bible. One hundred and ten times the word repentance or some form of the word is used in the Bible.

Now what message do you think the Apostle Paul was trying to drive across here?

I want us to look at the word repentance in hopes that we can gain a proper understanding of its meaning in our day to day lives. Repentance, as it is used here comes from the Greek word "metanoia" (met-an-oy-ah), meaning compunction for guilt including reformation. The word compunction means anxiety arising from the awareness of guilt. To reform means to amend or improve by change or form, or removal of faults or abuses.

I think we could safely say that repentance means that we see where we have been wrong, acknowledge it, and set out with renewed passion never to repeat the failure again.

The words "I'm sorry" seems to fall effortlessly from the lips of this culture but I wonder how much real repentance there is in this world, or better yet in the church?

Paul said "Godly sorrow brings about repentance." Repentance is not a popular topic, but I can assure you that it is an important one; in fact, one cannot get saved without repenting. If we cannot get saved without repentance, we certainly cannot live an overcoming life without walking in repentance and forgiveness.

Repentance has become a lost sermon of the Church of this modern hour, but it was on the top of Paul's list of candy sticks to preach.

There is much debate on the topic of what true repentance is. Some do not believe in dry eyed confessions, they believe you must shed tears to repent and be saved. I've seen people shed enough tears to generate electricity for this town for several days, but they still don't live right. Some people believe that you must shake a preacher's hand, or join the Church to be saved. I've seen people do both but it didn't change their lives. So what is the proof of repentance?

Paul says in verse eleven that there are seven attributes that will assimilate themselves in the lives of those who truly repent. I want us to look very closely at them because I believe they prove that one has repented.

## IV. WHAT FEAR...

The heart is the seat of our emotions! So what he is saying here is that we will be passionate in the area that we have chosen for our heart to dwell. The key to setting our affections and proving our repentance is getting our heart in the right place!

## VI. WHAT ZEAL...

The word zeal comes from the Greek word zelos (zay\'-los), and means ardor. The word ardor means, (1) Emotional -warmth; passion. (2) Enthusiasm. (3) Intense heat; fire.

The term zeal is different than the term, vehement desire. Vehement desire allows for us to get our focus back to where it should have been, and enables us to set our affections in the proper place, and on the proper things. The zeal spoken of here causes us to create a plan of action that will lead to the revenge that we will talk about later.

While Paul, who was then Saul, was on Straight Street he began to realize the awfulness of what he had been involved in. Sometime during this three day rendezvous with God, it began to dawn on Paul that if he was going to leave there a different kind of person he had to form a plan of attack against the flesh, and against Satan!

Some people seem to believe that God brain washed Paul with His search light from heaven, and he just woke up in another world, as a different kind of person! Sure there were some things in Paul's life that God did change, but there were many more that Paul himself was going to need to change!

How is it possible to change ones life? Someone said "People can change, but not much." This appears to be a pretty pessimistic point of view, but without the help of the Holy Spirit it is a pretty realistic point of view.

The only way any of us can hope to make major life changes is through repentance. Contrary to the opinions of the majority of folks who actually believe God can make such changes in us, God is not going to come down and hold a gun to our heads and make sure we change some things in our lives. Some people say, "When God gets ready for me to quit this habit he will take away the desire."

The truly repentant will discover they have adequate zeal to choose a plan of action that will guarantee success! Zeal is the emotional motivation that drives the process of change. Zeal is the power source of our desire to be different! The proof of repentance is having adequate zeal to make the changes!

Pentecostals are really good at being stirred:

- We cry...

- We clap...

- We jump...

- We shout...

- We laugh...

- We dance...

There is nothing inherently wrong with any of these things, in fact I encourage them, I think we should be emotional concerning the most epic event in our lives but what I want to know is, when the emotions have dried up, in what way have we been changed?

God is not nearly as interested in how emotional you have become, what He wants to know is that after the emotion has drained out of the service and sermon, are we still motivated enough to change? Anybody can cry! If you don\'t believe it come up here and let me see if I can unstop your tear ducts by hitting your thumb with a hammer!

The answer to the whole thing is being sorry enough for our sin that we have engaged in true repentance which will produce enough zeal to develop a successful plan of attack against our old sinful nature!

Let us take a moment to discover Paul's plan of attack against the old nature following the three day rendezvous with God on Straight Street: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

## VII. WHAT REVENGE...

The word revenge as it is used here, comes from the Greek word "ekdikesis" (ek-dik\'-ay-sis) and means vindication, or retribution. To vindicate means: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- To clear from criticism, blame, or suspicion...

- To defend against opposition...

- To justify...

The word retribution means, punishment for evil done, or reward for good done!

I realize there is great danger in what I am about to share with you today, some people would like to take a sermon on revenge, and use it against someone in their family, a coworker, an employee, or an employer. The Word of God speaks out very strongly about taking revenge against our fellow man. Leviticus 19:18 (KJV) "Thou shalt not avenge, nor bear any grudge against the children of thy people, but thou shalt love thy neighbor as thyself: I am the Lord." Matthew 5:39 (KJV) "But I say unto you, That ye resist not evil: but whosoever shall smite thee on thy right cheek, turn to him the other also." 1 Peter 3:8-9 (KJV) "Finally, be ye all of one mind, having compassion one of another, love as brethren, be pitiful, be courteous: Not rendering evil for evil, or railing for railing: but contrariwise blessing; knowing that ye are thereunto called, that ye should inherit a blessing."

Even though we are not to ever take revenge on another human being, we have been given permission by our Heavenly Father to take revenge on the enemy! Following Paul\'s three day rendezvous with God on Straight Street he began immediately taking revenge on the enemy, and never let up until the final breath had been drawn. Before conversion, Paul by his own admission was the chief of sinners; he was a very valuable tool in the hands of the enemy. When he was converted, he made up his mind to be at least as good a Christian as he was a sinner. Let us take some time and look at the revenge Paul took against the enemy!

The Church at Antioch, inspired by the Holy Spirit ordained Paul and Barnabas for missionary service, and sent John Mark with them. From this launching pad Paul was instrumental in preaching the Gospel all over the world, and establishing Churches in places that had never heard the Gospel of Christ before. As you are well aware, Paul wrote much of the New Testament, thus, more than 2,000 years later he is still getting his revenge on the enemy through this sermon.

The problem with many modern day Christians is that we have become so lethargic that we don\'t have enough spiritual adrenaline to cause us to go to Church three times a week, let alone take revenge on the enemy! ## VIII. IN ALL THINGS YOU HAVE APPROVED YOURSELVES TO BE CLEAR IN THIS MATTER...

We have thoroughly addressed all seven aspects of Paul's discourse on repentance. He quickly moves from speaking on the proof of repentance to making a statement which does not seem to fit. 2 Corinthians 7:11 (KJV) "... In all things ye have approved yourselves to be clear in this matter." There are two very important words in verse eleven: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The word approved comes from the Greek word, "sunistao" (soon-is-tah\'-o) and figuratively means to exhibit.

- The word clear comes from the Greek word "hagnos" (hag-nos\') and means to be clean, perfect, or pure. In a figurative sense it means innocent.

In other words Paul is saying that through the seven proofs of repentance you have exhibited that you are innocent in this matter! We are innocent of what? You will recall that we began this series of sermons by talking about the letter Paul had written to this Church that might have initially stirred up some emotions of anger, but eventually led to Godly sorrow which produced proof of repentance.

In order that we might understand what the Corinthian Church is being deemed innocent of, we must first look at what Paul had charged them with: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Divisions in the Church...

- The absence of discipline in the Church...

- Civil litigation between members of the body of Christ...

- The moral decline of members of the Church...

- Willingness to treat marriage as something less than a covenant...

- The eating of food sacrificed to idols...

Paul laid out some pretty stiff and serious charges against the Corinthian Church! I wonder what the charges would be against some of us?

Paul used 15 chapters, 447 verses to lay out the charges, and then he say\'s "In all things you have exhibited that you are innocent in this matter." Think of it, all the trouble to bring all these charges, then the prosecution asks for an innocent verdict! Is that not a little frustrating and disappointing? How could a thing like that happen? These are serious charges!

Here is how it happened: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- They were guilty as charged to begin with...

- Paul\'s charges against them caused them to sorrow to repentance...

- Their repentance was proven by their attitudes, actions, and conduct...

- Because of their repentance and the proof of their repentance they were found innocent of all charges...

Now this thing would be treated as if it never happened!

CONCLUSION: The same things happen with us! We have been formally charged by the Word of God! We are guilty! The charges have us dead to rights!

Jesus has paid the price for our pardon and redemption! Isaiah 55:7 (KJV) "Let the wicked forsake his way, and the unrighteous man his thoughts: And let him return unto the Lord, and he will have mercy upon him; and to our God, for he will abundantly pardon." 1 John 1:9 (KJV) "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness."

The only thing remaining is for us to plead guilty to all charges, sorrow to repentance, and prove our repentance through our lifestyles! When this has been done, notice the results!
