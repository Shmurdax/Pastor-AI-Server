John 10:30 I and \<my\> Father are one.

John 17:22 And the glory which thou gavest me I have given them; that they may be one, even as we are one:

Romans 12:5 So we, \<being\> many, are one body in Christ, and every one members one of another.

1 Corinthians 3:8 Now he that planteth and he that watereth are one: and every man shall receive his own reward according to his own labour.

1 Corinthians 10:17 For we \<being\> many are one bread, and one body: for we are all partakers of that one bread.

1 Corinthians 12:12 For as the body is one, and hath many members, and all the members of that one body, being many, are one body: so also is Christ.

1 John 5:7 For there are three that bear record in heaven, the Father, the Word, and the Holy Ghost: and these three are one.

The thing that all these scriptures have in common of course are the use of the word ONE. Each instance of the usage of the word ONE in all of these scriptures comes from the same root Greek word- HEIS- (pronounced --HISE). The meaning of HEIS is quite literally to be one. This has everything to do with purpose, unity, and harmony but has absolutely nothing to do with physical merge of two or more personalities occupying the same body. When you get into two or more personalities occupying the same body you then have what is known as a split personality which borders on demon possession.
