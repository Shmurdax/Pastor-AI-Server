INTRO: As a boy growing up in northwestern Arkansas there were a number of old sayings that I became accustomed to hearing. I grew up hearing the old adage, "Charge it to the dust, and let the rain settle it."

When someone was approached about a debt that they knew they couldn't possibly pay, they would say, "Charge it to the dust, and let the rain settle it." While sleeping, the Lord gave me a sermon based on this old adage and I want to entitle it, Charge it to the cross, and let the blood settle it. (Some of the best sermons I ever preached I preached in my sleet -- Some of the best sermons I ever preached I preached in some other peoples sleep between 11 & 12 on a Sunday morning!)

First of all let me say the death, burial, and resurrection of Jesus is the central theme of the Word of God. The death of Christ is directly mentioned 175 times in the New Testament.

The Cross and the Christ of the cross are represented as one, because they are eternally connected. The Apostle Paul writing in his first letter to the Corinthian Church sets forward the preaching of the cross as being of paramount importance in the carrying out of the Great Commission! 1 Corinthians 1:18 "For the preaching of the cross is to them that perish foolishness; but unto us which are saved it is the power of God."

To the Jews, the Cross was a tree of shame and horror. According to the Jews, a person who was wicked enough or unfortunate enough to be crucified was "accursed of God." This belief was based on a scripture in Deuteronomy 21:23 "... he that is hanged is accursed of God..." Galatians 3:13 "Christ hath redeemed us from the curse of the law, being made a curse for us; for it is written, Cursed is every one that hangeth on a tree."

To the Greeks, the cross was the gallows-style execution of a slave's infamy and a murderer's punishment. Historically, there was not a single association connected with the cross except those of shame and agony! Where our culture glamorizes the cross, displaying it décor in churches, homes and places of business as well as wearing it as jewelry, the very thought of a crucified messiah seemed to the Jews a revolting if not blasphemous idea.

They expected their messiah to mount a charger and enter Jerusalem as a King. This is precisely why an entire nation and system of religion missed their moment! Our world has glamorized the cross to the point that it is nothing more than a religious symbol or another piece of décor or jewelry, while missing the whole point and person of the cross.

The worship of a crucified malefactor seemed to the Greeks a detestable superstition.

The crucifixion of Christ was carried out in an era when public opinion of people who died the death of the cross was at such an all-time low. It was against this backdrop that the Apostle Paul came preaching to the Corinthian Church -- "Jesus Christ (The Jewish Messiah), and him crucified."

This is partially why Paul attracted the bitter persecution from the religious establishment. It is rather obvious that Paul was not seeking popularity or even immediate success. If he had been, he would have preached something to this Church other than the cross. The results of this message on the subject of the cross, proved his doing and undoing!

As unpopular as this message was, the very cross that Paul preached became the recognized badge of Christianity. The symbol of the cross is universal. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE.) Three centuries later the cross was woven in gold upon the banners and set in jewels on the diadems of the Roman Empire.

I want to tell you today, after so many centuries have come and gone, the preaching of the cross has not lost one ounce of its potency, influence, or ability to turn one around and send him/her in a new direction. "It reaches to the highest mountain and it flows to the lowest valley!" To preach the cross is not enough, we must also preach Christ crucified. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) The wood in the cross could not save a mosquito but the blood that ran down it's frame can save the world!

To glory in the cross is to glory in that grace which, through, death, has put away sin and slain the enmity of the human heart. Dr. Luke records the prophecy of Zacharias, which put it like this, Luke 1:78-79 (KJV) "Through the tender mercy of our God; whereby the dayspring from on high hath visited us, (79) To give light to them that sit in darkness and in the shadow of death, to guide our feet into the way (Lifestyle) of peace."

Let us charge our panic to the cross and allow the finished work of the savior to provide peace in turbulent situations!
