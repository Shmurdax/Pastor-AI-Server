INTRO: In this passage we have a chapter 
out of the Life of David and History of 
Israel. 

In his lifetime David experienced three 
separate times where the Scripture points to 
the fact that he received an anointing: 

1. 
The first anointing occurred at the 
age of seventeen – When Samuel 
anointed him to be King 

2. 
The second anointing occurred at the 
age of thirty – When he was anointed to 
reign over Judah 

3. 
The third anointing occurred at the 
age of thirty-seven – When he was 
anointed to be king over all Israel 
 
Though David experienced three separate 
times where he was anointed He had to: 
• Kill a giant… 
• Sing to a mad king… 
• Endure humiliation… 
• Face the furnace of affliction… 
• Encounter the heaviness of despair… 
• Experience fantastic deliverances… 

Remember: Real anointing never comes 
without sacrifice: 
• The waiting… 
• The preparation… Preparation is a 
prerequisite for promotion 
• Plodding a less than illustrious path… 
• Waiting for advancement… 
• Hoping for deliverance… 
 
All breakthrough is prefaced by sacrifice. 
The pattern of sacrifice begins with Abel’s 
sacrifice and ends with the cries of the saints 
from beneath the altar in Revelation. It 
should not come as much of a surprise to us 
that we are often called upon to sacrifice. 
STOP being a victim and START being a 
victor! (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL

IN order for David to solidify his hold on 
Israel he would have to conquer Jerusalem. 

Jerusalem was not only filled with Jebusites, 
the Jebusites were full of insults! 2 Samuel 
5:6 (LB) “David now led his troops to 
Jerusalem to fight against the Jebusites 
who lived there. “You’ll never come in 
here,” they told him. “Even the blind and 
lame could keep you out!” For they 
thought they were safe.” Have you ever 
met a Jebusite? 
• In your home… 
• On your job… 
• In your finances… 
• With your health… 
• Among your children… 
 
I want to use this incident in the life of 
David to talk about “BREAKTHROUGH” 
and the Biblical pattern for us to parlay his 
experience into our current situation. 
 
Breakthrough is more than incremental 
advancement. It is that moment in time 

where everything changes in one critical 
moment! Breakthrough means: 
• Anointed increase… 
• Radical expansion… 
• Exponential enlargement… 
• Turning point… 
• Turnaround… 
• Breakthrough… 
 
The Psalmist describes breakthrough like 
this; “Run through a troop - Leap over a 
wall.” (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL 
NEXT SLIDE) 
 
Notice that David did not allow the 
conditions or the insults of the Jebusites to 
dissuade him! What are we allowing to 
dissuade us? 

The Bible say’s the Philistines assembled in 
the valley of Rephaim. Rephaim means the 
valley of the giants! Ever had one of those? 
If you need a breakthrough follow David’s 
pattern for victory! 
 

## I. PRAY…
2 Samuel 5:19 (NKJV) “David inquired of 
the Lord, saying, “Shall I go up against the 
Philistines? Will You deliver them into my 
hand?” And the Lord said to David, “Go 
up, for I will doubtless deliver the 
Philistines into your hand.” 
 
No breakthrough occurs without prayer! 
When we are in need of a breakthrough we 
must never assume we know what is in the 
mind of God. Prayer is necessary to know 
the mind of God and to chart a course of 
victory! 

Missionary Jim Elliot, who in the 1950’s 
bravely pioneered outreach to the Indians of 
Ecuador, said, “The saint who advances on 
his knees never retreats!” 
 
Francios (Fran-swah) Fenelon (Fee-Knee-
Lone), a 17th century French Catholic 
Archbishop advised: “Tell God all that is in 
your heart, as one unloads one’s heart to a 
dear friend: 
• Tell Him your troubles, that He may 
comfort you… 
• Tell Him your joys, that He may sober 
you… 
• Tell Him your longings, that He may 
purify them… 
• Tell Him your dislikes, that He may help 
you to conquer them… 

• Talk to Him of your temptations, that He 
may shield you from them… 
• Show Him the wounds of your heart, that 
He may heal them… 
 
Before the first battle David prayed and in 
the second battle he prayed. Never make a 
major decision without committing at least 
three day’s to fasting and prayer. 
 
David needed a breakthrough therefore he 
could not afford to get it wrong! The only 
way to make sure we don’t get it wrong is to 
bathe our decisions in prayer. 
 

## II. OBEY…
David was obedient in the first battle and 
won a great victory. 2 Samuel 5:20-21 
(NKJV) “So David went to Baal Perazim, 
and David defeated them there; and he 
said, “The Lord has broken through my 

enemies before me, like a breakthrough of 
water.” Therefore he called the name of 
that place Baal Perazim. 21 And they left 
their images there, and David and his men 
carried them away.” 
 
Because of David’s prayer and obedience 
the valley of the giants had become the place 
of breakthrough! Prayer and obedience will 
cause your Rephaim to become Baal 
Perazim! 
 
Notice, the victory was so great, the 
Philistines left their god’s behind! That is 
what I call breakthrough! 
 
Though the victory was great, the Philistines 
were not going to roll over and play dead! 
Your adversaries are not going to give up 
easy! David’s enemies said, “Even the blind 
and lame could keep you out.” 

Be assured, your enemy will: 
• Mock you… 
• Stand in defiance against you… 
• Scream into your face that you should 
give up… 
 
British Prime Minister Winston Churchill, 
whose words of courage strengthened his 
countrymen and led to victory in the face of 
ferocious bombing by the Nazis, said, “You 
have enemies? Good! That means you’ve 
stood up for something at sometime in your 
life!” 
 

## III. HAVE FAITH…
It is one thing to obey but it is another thing 
to have faith enough to transition when the 
battle plan changes! 2 Samuel 5:22-24 
(NKJV) “Then the Philistines went up once 
again and deployed themselves in the 

Valley of Rephaim. 23 Therefore David 
inquired of the Lord, and He said, “You 
shall not go up; circle around behind them, 
and come upon them in front of the 
mulberry trees. 24 And it shall be, when 
you hear the sound of marching in the tops 
of the mulberry trees, then you shall 
advance quickly. For then the Lord will go 
out before you to strike the camp of the 
Philistines.” 
 
In the first battle he was told to "Attack" 
but in the second battle he was told to, 
"wait". I like "Attack" better than "Wait"! 
The writer of Hebrews said, "You have 
need of patience..." Patience in the battle 
proves one has faith! Are you walking or 
running through “The valley of the shadow 
of death”? 
 
Remember, the enemy is not fazed by our 
previous victories! The enemy will 

persevere in the face of continuous defeats! 
 
If David had followed the strategy of 
yesterday in the battle of today, he would 
have missed his divine resources and would 
not have received his breakthrough. If we 
fail to implement a GOD strategy we will 
miss the breakthrough! If we are too 
traditional to transition we very well may 
miss a NOW word from God, and for our 
breakthrough! 
 
If you will have enough faith to implement 
the strategy of God, He will turn: 
 
• Impediments into impending victories! 
• Setbacks into breakthroughs! 
• Breaches into bastions of victory! 
• Stumbling blocks into stepping-stones! 
• Confusion into citadels! 
 
Winning the victory requires trusting God. 
Trusting God requires putting ourselves 

squarely at His disposal. It will no doubt 
take faith to win the battle we face. R.W. 
Shambach used to say, “You don’t have any 
trouble, all you need is faith in God!” 
 

## IV. WAGE WAR…
Corrrie Ten Boom said, "The first step on 
the way to victory is to recognize the 
enemy." George Washington said, “There is 
nothing so likely to produce peace as to be 
well prepared to meet the enemy.” 
 
Here is the strategy for the breakthrough 
against the Philistines: 
• Sneak around behind them… 
• Position themselves in the mulberry 
trees… 
• When marching occurs in the tops of the 
trees – wage war… 
 
Have you ever executed a ridiculous strategy 
to receive a major breakthrough? Sometimes 

we must do the ridiculous while expecting 
the miraculous. 
 
If we want an extraordinary breakthrough 
we must be willing to implement a less than 
orthodox strategy. 
 
I ask us: 
• Do we have a strategy? 
• What is our strategy? 
• Is our strategy from God? 
• Is our strategy based on His Word? 
 
I am calling on every minister in this house 
to cast down the spirit of timidity and wage 
war on the enemy! 
 

## V. EXPECT THE MIRACULOUS…
David’s breakthrough was totally dependent 
on a miraculous action. The word 
“marching” or “going” in verse 24 is very 
interesting indeed. The Aramic version say’s 

“The sound of horses hooves.” The Lord 
said to David, “When you hear the sound 
of horses running through the tops of the 
trees, attack!” the last time I checked, 
horses can’t run through the tops of the 
trees! 
 
I can almost see the young king telling his 
men there will be the sound of horse and 
horsemen in the tops of the mulberry trees!!! 
Breakthrough is reserved for folks who go 
out on a limb expecting the miraculous! 
 
Billy Graham once asked - “Jonah 
swallowed by a fish?” He said, “I’d believe 
it if Scripture said Jonah swallowed the 
fish!” He continued by saying, “It’s not 
difficult to believe, if you believe in a God of 
miracles.” 

The Lord was saying to David, “You will 
know by the sound that the God of the 
Battle, is present to do battle." 
 
I hear the sound of breakthrough and 
miracles in this house!
