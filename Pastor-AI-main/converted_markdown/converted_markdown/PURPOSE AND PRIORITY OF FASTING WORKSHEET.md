## II. WHY FAST?

God already desires to give good things to His children. Matthew 7:11, 2 Chronicles 16:9

Why fast?

- The words of Jesus implore us to do so. Matthew 9:14-15

- Fasting is the duty of every Christian. Matthew 6:16

- Fasting crucifies the flesh. Galatians 5:16-26

- Fasting humbles the believer before God.

- Fasting teaches the believer dependence upon God as his source.

- Fasting expresses submission to God.

<!-- -->

- Fasting prepares us to receive revelation from God.

- Fasting brings victory!

- Fasting places the believer in a position of hearing from God.

## III. WHEN TO FAST...

The timing of a fast is probably as crucial as the decision to fast. James 4:5-10

The most effective method of crucifying the flesh is through fasting. Galatians 5:16-26

We should fast:

- When God lays a fast on our hearts...

- When our prayer life needs to be strengthened...

- When we need to prepare ourselves to receive God\'s message... Luke 4:13

- When we become cold in our spirit...

- When we are experiencing crisis in our life...

- When spiritual leaders call a fast... Joel 1:14, Hebrews 13:17

Fasting is a very difficult decision to reach but it is probably one of the most important disciplines in the Christian life.

CONCLUSION:

You should not go on a radical fast or absolute fast if you have health problems which would be worsened by abstaining from food or water.

GUIDELINES FOR AN EXTENDED FAST

Before beginning a fast of more than three days:

- Receive divine direction - Don\'t copy others; be sure you are led of the Lord as to what type of fast you are to do and how long it should last.

- No caffeine -- Discontinue the use of caffeine a few days before the fast.

- Restrict your food intake several days before the fast - Eat fruit as a last meal before the fast.

During the fast:

- Dress warmly - You can expect to feel colder during a fast, especially in your hands and feet.

- Use warm, not hot, water for showers and baths.

- Avoid ice cold beverages.

- Give extra time to prayer and Bible reading.

After the fast:

- Avoid starches for a few days.

- Chew your food well.

- Stop eating when you feel full.

- Don\'t become physically active too soon.

- Eat healthy food.

Here is a suggested schedule for reintroducing food after a complete fast:

- DAY 1: Drink liquids every couple of hours in small amounts. Mix water with fruit juices.

- DAY 2: Eat chicken noodle soup or something similar. Yogurt in small quantities will be fine.

- DAY 3 & 4: Salads, soups without grease and cooked vegetables.

- After a few days you can begin to resume a normal diet.

- The last thing you should begin consuming is fish and meats.
