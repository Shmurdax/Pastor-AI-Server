## II. STRATEGY #2: BE MORE CONCERNED FOR GODS GLORY THAN FOR OUR RELIEF!

As we begin to deal with the temptation of seeking our relief above everything else, let me just say that this is a very natural reaction to difficult circumstances. When we hit our thumb with a hammer or stump our toe, every part of our body is focused on the care of the member which has been injured!

At the same time we must be aware that the natural mind is the enemy of faith and of Gods will. When we encounter difficult times we must always remember that God is using this to set us up for victory, while setting the enemy up for defeat.

In Scripture, God often allowed His people to endure hardship while he was collecting their enemies for a major disaster! Such is the case here at the Red Sea! Notice Exodus 14:3-4, (KJV) "For Pharaoh will say of the children of Israel, They are entangled in the land, the wilderness hath shut them in. 4 And I will harden Pharaoh's heart, that he shall follow after them; and I will be honored upon Pharaoh, and upon all his host; that the Egyptians may know that I am the LORD. And they did so."

When we encounter impossible odds we should always ask ourselves, "What is God up to in this situation?" When we come upon a difficult crisis we must always remember that if we operate in faith rather than fear, God will ultimately reveal His: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

Divine grace! (Do not frustrate the grace of God...)

Divine sufficiency! (Paul -- My grace is sufficient for you...)

Divine will! (You are either in it or out of it...)

Every trial is meant to enable the believer to preserve the balance of the Christian life and to bring glory to God in the most difficult of times.

In difficult times we are often perplexed because the trial seems to be without rhyme or reason and we cannot find the answers to our questions. This is mostly because we are asking the wrong questions. We ask questions like; (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

How did I get into this mess?

Why did this happen to me?

How quickly can I solve this problem?

These are logical questions, but they may be the wrong ones to ask. The children of Israel made this mistake, Exodus 14:10-12 (CEV) "When the Israelites saw the king coming with his army, they were frightened and begged the Lord for help. 11 They also complained to Moses, "Wasn't there enough room in Egypt to bury us? Is that why you brought us out here to die in the desert? Why did you bring us out of Egypt anyway? 12 While we were there, didn't we tell you to leave us alone? We had rather be slaves in Egypt than die in this desert!" (Moses went from hero to heretic at the first sign of Pharaohs army)

There is a better approach, one that results in an entirely new way of looking at difficulties; one that puts our problems into a different context and creates a new paradigm for dealing with tough situations.

(PLACE THE MAP ON THE SCREEN)

Israel was befuddled by the sight of the Red Sea on two sides, the mountains on the other, and Pharaoh's army closing in from behind! Moses and Aaron could have joined in the negativity of the crowd but they decided to trust God and find out what His plan was for their situation. Exodus 14:13-14 (CEV) "But Moses answered, "Don't be afraid! Be brave, and you will see the Lord save you today. These Egyptians will never bother you again. 14 The Lord will fight for you, and you won't have to do a thing."

The next time we are overwhelmed, instead of asking, "How can I get out of this mess?" We should try asking, "How can God be glorified in this situation?"

Our perspective is entirely changed by the spiritual realities behind this approach. Notice the way this develops in Exodus 14:3-4 (CEV). With regard to where they were to camp, God said to Moses, (CEV) "The king will think they were afraid to cross the desert and that they are wandering around, trying to find another way to leave the country. 4 I will make the king stubborn again, and he will try to catch you. God deliberately orchestrated the impending attack of Egypt's army upon the Israelites here at the Red Sea! What is God thinking? He is about to demonstrate to Israel and the rest of the world the power He wields over His enemies and the elements. The New International version puts it like this, "I will gain glory for myself through Pharaoh and his army."

## III. STRATEGY #3: ACKNOWLEDGE THE ENEMY, BUT KEEP OUR EYES ON THE LORD!

There is no question that Israel had an enemy and that enemy was bent on destruction and demise. Look at what Israel was facing. Exodus 14:5-9 (CEV) "When the king of Egypt heard that the Israelites had finally left, he and his officials changed their minds and said, "Look what we have done! We let them get away, and they will no longer be our slaves. (You never know what a person is really like until you deal with their pocket book)"6 The king got his war chariot and army ready. 7 He commanded his officers in charge of his six hundred best chariots and all his other chariots to start after the Israelites. 8 The Lord made the king so stubborn that he went after them, even though the Israelites proudly went on their way."

The Israelites could have denied the existence of an enemy but that would not have changed the fact that a hostile king was in hot pursuit of this group of emancipated slaves. (PLACE PICTURES 1 & 2 ON SCREEN)

Denial of the enemy never calms his hostilities but the acknowledgement of his existence demands readiness. Charles Spurgeon paraphrases this Scripture in this way, "The great tyrant has not forgotten you, and he designs your capture and re-enslavement."

The devil sometimes launches direct, frontal attacks against us but he is usually more conniving and insidious. In Ephesians 6:12-13 (NKJV) " For we do not wrestle against flesh and blood, but against principalities, against powers, against the rulers of the darkness of this age, against spiritual hosts of wickedness in the heavenly places.13 Therefore take up the whole armor of God, that you may be able to withstand in the evil day."

Whenever the enemy attacks, whether covertly or overtly, he is normally closer and cruder than we first imagined, much like the Pharaoh of the Exodus.

Have you ever felt pursued? Oppressed? Sensed the devil nipping at your heels? Ever wondered if your simultaneous troubles were orchestrated by a brutal, invisible hand? Suspected that your depression or anger stemmed from a malicious source?

When dealing with difficulty we should always acknowledge that the enemy is present b ut we should do so without taking our eyes off the Lord.

I want us to consider the parallels between Pharaoh and Satan: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

Both are unyielding enemies!

Both covet power over their subjects! (Give the devil an inch and he will take a mile)

## IV. STRATEGY #4: PRAY!

Exodus 14:10 (NKJV) "And when Pharaoh drew near, the children of Israel lifted their eyes, and behold, the Egyptians marched after them. So they were very afraid, and the children of Israel cried out to the Lord."

The children of Israel were in a hopeless situation as far as the arm of flesh is concerned. The mountains and the sea fenced them in with the only route for escape in the direction of the Egyptian troops. The chariot wheels of this formidable foe were rumbling through the mountain pass toward the sea and their horses hooves were churning up dust. Matthew Henry said, "Their fear set them a praying, and that was a good effect of it. God brings us into straits that He may bring us to our knees."

All of us are bound to encounter circumstances that tend to cause us to choose between panic and prayer. Most of us have a propensity to panic before we pray. I must admit that I have had my share of hyperventilating, heart-racing, panic attacks. Panic is never the answer! Prayer is always the answer!

The Lord desires us to know that prayer is the means by which we can stay even-tempered, self-possessed, cool-headed and strong-spirited even in the midst of a crisis!

When we can\'t press forward, move sideward, or step backward, it\'s time to look upward and ask God to make a way where there seems to be no way.

In a time of uncertainty, the patriarch Jacob said, Genesis 35:3 (NKJV) "Then let us arise and go up to Bethel; and I will make an altar there to God, who answered me in the day of my distress and has been with me in the way which I have gone."

Referring to his days as a fugitive, David wrote, 2 Samuel 22:7 (NKJV) "In my distress I called upon the Lord, And cried out to my God; He heard my voice from His temple, And my cry entered His ears."

Psalm 107:25-29 (NKJV) "For He commands and raises the stormy wind, which lifts up the waves of the sea. 26 They mount up to the heavens, they go down again to the depths; their soul melts because of trouble. 27 They reel to and fro, and stagger like a drunken man, And are at their wits' end. Then they cry out to the Lord in their trouble, And He brings them out of their distresses. 29 He calms the storm, So that its waves are still."

That\'s just what happened as the Israelites cried out to God at the Red Sea, except there the waves became trembling walls of water, held back by invisible hands. It takes great faith to go into a sea whose walls are apparently held back by an east wind! I am not so sure that the Israelites went into the sea because of their faith in God as much as their fear of Pharaoh. My fear of my dad rather than God prevented me from getting involved in a lot of things while I was growing up.

Daily devotions and crisis time prayer vigils are worlds apart! Crisis time prayers are prayers of importunity and intensity. Prayers during life-threatening or soul-shatter­ing events tend to be passionate appeals to a God who is bigger and stronger than our own abilities.

In a time of crisis we are brought to a place where we not only realize but actually acknowledge Gods ability and our inability! Matthew 17:21 (NKJV) "This kind does not go out except by prayer and fasting."

Ephesians 6:18 (NKJV) "Praying always with all prayer and supplication in the Spirit, being watchful to this end with all perseverance and supplication for all the saints." The Message Bible say's "Pray hard and long,"

So they were very afraid, and the children of Israel cried out to the Lord." Their seaside cry was sincere, genuine, heartfelt, urgent and united but unbelieving. The urgency of their prayer was obvious, evidenced by the verb "cried."

Cameron Thompson, in his booklet, "Master Secrets of Prayer" makes the following statement, "There comes a time, in spite of our soft, modern ways, when we must be desperate in prayer, when we must wrestle, when we must be outspoken, shameless and importunate."

This literally means that the producing, valid, intensely earnest prayer of a righteous person will get the job done.

When I have experienced a crisis in the church...

When I am tempted to lose my temper, "Make me sweet Lord" seems to work!

When I encounter a person I do not like very much, "Grant me your love Lord" seems to work!

When I am tempted to dress someone down with my words, "Let me speak your words Lord" seems to work!

Mackintosh wrote, "God never gives guidance for two steps at a time. I must take one step, and then I get light for the next. This keeps the heart in abiding dependence upon God."

You must stand trial before Caesar; and God has graciously given you the lives of all who sail with you.\' So keep up your courage, men, for I have faith in God that it will happen just as he told me" Paul's definition of faith, "believing that things will happen just as He has told me."

When we connect the dots we discover faith is; (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

Believing what the Lord has said to us will be accomplished...

Believing the maker of the promise to be faithful...

Being fully persuaded that God has the power to do what He has promised...

Believing that things will happen just as He has told us...

Every one of us must live by faith!

When we take our morning shower, we assume there\'s going to be water, preferably hot...

When we eat our cereal, we expect it to be healthy and wholesome...

When we drive to work, we proceed through green lights, assum­ing they are red for intersecting traffic...

When we step on the brake we expect the vehicle to stop...

When we press the accelerator we expect the vehicle to accelerate...

Even the most sincere atheist lives by faith, not only in his athe­istic philosophy, but in the very processes and procedures of everyday life. God created this universe so that the faith principle is always at work.

For Christians, faith is making certain assumptions about God\'s care and control over our lives, based on His scriptural promises and His historical performance. We may not understand every circum­stance or appreciate every event but each test is uniquely qualifying us for the ultimate purpose God has in mind for us. Sometimes we\'re backed up to the Red Sea with the Egyptians in hot pursuit, but God has given us promises which He is able and dependable to perform.

We disappoint Him when we question His ability to keep His Word. This is proven by the experience of the disciples on the Sea of Galilee. Mark 4:39-40 (NLT) "When he woke up, he rebuked the wind and said to the water, "Quiet down!" Suddenly the wind stopped, and there was a great calm. 40 And he asked them, "Why are you so afraid? Do you still not have faith in me?"

Warren Wiersbe said, "A faith that can\'t be tested can\'t be trusted." The level of faith differs from person to person! One person\'s faith is turbocharged while another\'s sputters on one cylinder. The reason for this discrepancy of faith is not that one person was given more faith than the other, in fact the Scripture tells us; everyone started out with the same amount of faith. Romans 12:3 (KJV) "For I say, through the grace given unto me, to every man that is among you, not to think of himself more highly than he ought to think; but to think soberly, according as God hath dealt to every man the measure of faith."

Several factors determine the level of our faith: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

The level of intimacy between the individual and the maker of the promise...

The level of the practical knowledge of the Word of God...

The amount of exercise our faith has received over the months and years...

Our willingness and ability to endure hardship without whimpering...

As Jesus wandered through Palestine, He had an x-ray vision which penetrated hearts and measured faith. He seemed intensely interested in the quantity as well as the quality of faith being exercised by those who crossed His path. He made various statements with regard to those whom he met:

The Centurion of Capernaum whose servant was at the point of death told Jesus He did not need to come to his house to heal his servant. He said "Just speak the word and he will be healed." Jesus said, Matthew 8:10 (NLT "I tell you the truth, I haven't seen faith like this in all the land of Israel!"

The Canaanite woman whose daughter was demon possessed would not take no for an answer said "Even the dogs get crumbs from the Masters table!" Jesus said, Matthew 15:28 "Woman, your faith is great. Your request is granted."

To the Disciples on the Sea of Galilee He said; "Why are you so afraid? Do you still not have faith in me?" Matthew 8:26 (NLT)

Several Friends brought a paralyzed man to Jesus in Matthew 9:2 (NLT) "Seeing their faith, Jesus said to the paralyzed man, "Take heart, son! Your sins are forgiven."

Without question, it takes faith to move the hand of God and when He sees our faith He rewards us for trusting in Him. Hebrews 11:6 (NKJV) "But without faith it is impossible to please Him, for he who comes to God must believe that He is, and that He is a rewarder of those who diligently seek Him."

Faith isn\'t something I can muster up at will; it must be given by God and developed according to the processes of life, which God has ordained and we have successfully negotiated.

He is, after all, a God who grows things, almost always using a process! Physically, each of us began life as a tiny speck. It\'s a marvelous cell, for all the characteristics of each person; the sex, eye color, shoe size, intelligence, and so forth are determined at fertilization by the baby\'s genetic code (DNA) that resides in the forty-six human chromosomes in that one cell. We begin life micro­scopically, and then grow into a mature human being. In the same way faith begins small and grows mighty! God intends to develop us spiritually. How? Like any good teacher, He bestows truth; then devises tests to review and reinforce that truth, to trans­fer and translate it into lasting, life changing experiences.

Like any good coach, He sits down with His players, using a game book of Scripture, gives us information, explains the rules, and reviews the plays. Then the time for a scrimmage arrives; the video tapes; the correction; more information and another scrimmage. It is virtually an endless cycle.

In the process: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

Good players become skilled professionals...

Good people become growing disciples...

Little faith grows into great faith...

Mountains become molehills...

Difficulties become opportunities...

This process is how God chose to deal with the Children of Israel and with the disciples. With the Israelites, He gave instructions through Moses, and then brought them to the edge of the Red Sea. He provided a miracle in the Red Sea passage then led them to Canaan. Because of their unbelief He led them from Canaan to the wilderness where He allowed them to be tested. The wilderness is the place where the Children of Israel were permitted to apply their promises to their problems.

Jesus taught the disciples on the mountainside, then loaded them into a boat and sent them into a terrific storm designed to help them apply the promise to the problem.

The same is true for us. At church we hear the Word of God, and in our devotions we feed on the Scriptures. The Lord then allows a trial into our lives to give us an oppor­tunity to put His teachings into practice. As we trust Him and pass the test, we\'re strengthened for the future.

This, then, is the principle: our faith grows when we choose to apply God\'s promises to today\'s problems and use the experiences to mature us for tomorrow\'s challenges. In a sense, we are storing up faith for times ahead.

This is precisely why the Bible is so full of promises. We never encounter any situation for which God has not provided a precious promise for our perplexing problem.

The Puritan Thomas Watson said, "Faith lives in a promise, as the fish lives in the water. The promises are both comforting and quickening. The promises of God are flotation devices to keep us from sinking when we come to the waters of affliction!"

J. I. Packer stated, "In the days when the Bible was uni­versally acknowledged in the churches as \'God\'s Word writ­ten', it was clearly understood that the promises recorded in Scripture were the proper, God-given basis for all our life of faith, and that the way to strengthen ones faith was to focus it upon particular promises that spoke to one\'s condition."

Faith, then, is simply finding and claiming the promises of God in every situation. Based on those promises, we should be making consistent decisions and becoming fully persuaded that God has the power to do what He has promised.

## X. STRATEGY #10: DON'T FORGET TO PRAISE HIM...

I want you to envision the Children of Israel at the Red Sea when the waters began to part!

At first there was dead silence. Picture this, a mute multitude gazing in stunned disbelief at a body of water whose powerful waves had only a few hours before been their worst nightmare. This same water now becomes a passageway to deliverance.

They traverse their difficulty on dry ground and look back on their greatest trial with disbelief. The terror was over; the enemy was gone; the night had ended successfully. Nothing was left but the stillness of a shocked people at daybreak. Finally someone breathed. Then came a whispering; then a buzzing; then the rumbling of three million emancipated slaves who are for the first time aware of the magnitude of the power of their God!

The volume must have sounded like a mighty tornado, as the ranges of emotions were finally released into the morning skies.

In a few minutes someone said, "I will sing to the LORD, for He has triumphed gloriously! The horse and its rider He has thrown into the sea!" Another rang out: "The LORD is my strength and song, and He has become my salvation." Another sang: "He is my God, and I will praise Him; My father\'s God, and I will exalt Him." The flood­gates of emotion broke, and the singing burst forth into the first recorded song in Scripture. Exodus 15:1-19 (NKJV) "Then Moses and the children of Israel sang this song to the Lord, and spoke, saying: 1 "I will sing to the Lord, For He has triumphed gloriously! The horse and its rider He has thrown into the sea! 2 The Lord is my strength and song, And He has become my salvation; He is my God, and I will praise Him; My father's God, and I will exalt Him. 3 The Lord is a man of war; The Lord is His name. 4 Pharaoh's chariots and his army He has cast into the sea; His chosen captains also are drowned in the Red Sea. 5 The depths have covered them; They sank to the bottom like a stone. 6 "Your right hand, O Lord, has become glorious in power; Your right hand, O Lord, has dashed the enemy in pieces.7 And in the greatness of Your excellence You have overthrown those who rose against You; You sent forth Your wrath; It consumed them like stubble.8 And with the blast of Your nostrils The waters were gathered together; The floods stood upright like a heap; The depths congealed in the heart of the sea. 9 The enemy said, 'I will pursue, I will overtake, I will divide the spoil; My desire shall be satisfied on them. But the children of Israel went on dry land in the midst of the sea."

I can assure you that this glorious celebration in Exodus chapter fifteen was about as animated a gathering as any of us would ever attend. They did not sit around on the sea shore and hold hands and sing cum-by-yah-my-Lord. There were no dim lights, thick curtains and soft music here! You find that kind of thing in a funeral home! This is a festival rather than a funeral!

A nation of liberated slaves sang and danced and clapped and played their tambourines until they collapsed in frenzied exhaus­tion. If we had been there we would have heard the roar of countless voices echoing across the desert and through the skies all the way to heaven. Listen to this, Exodus 15:20-21 (NLT) "Then Miriam the prophet, Aaron's sister, took a tambourine and led all the women in rhythm and dance. 21 And Miriam sang this song: "I will sing to the Lord, for he has triumphed gloriously; he has thrown both horse and rider into the sea."

We should be constantly prepared to praise God with reckless abandonment! Most of the time we are not ready or willing to do so! The reason; because we have not been in a difficult spot for so long that we have forgotten how thankful we are for His delivering power!

Maybe we need a crisis! One of the reasons God allows us to encounter tough situations or allows us to be where we are is to give us the opportunity to sound forth His praises when He delivers us from our difficulties!

God expects our gratitude for His deliverances! Don't forget to praise Him! Don't be one of the nine lepers who never returned to give thanks, be the one who is grateful! If we return thanks we will be made whole! Give Him the glory!

In the seventeenth century, John Trapp wrote, "He lets out His mercies to us for the rent of our praise, and is content that we may have the benefit of them so He may have the glory."

In the history of the Christian church, some of our great­est hymns were written in the darkest moments:

"Amazing Grace" was written by John Newton after a life of sin in which he considered murder and then suicide. At one point he was a deserter from the British Navy, and almost perished on the high seas. From that backdrop come the words "Through many dangers toils and snares I have already come. His grace has brought me safe this far and grace will lead me on."

Sarah Fowler Adams wrote "Nearer My God to Thee" for her Pastor William Johnson Fox for his Sunday Sermon at a time when her own health was failing.

After having lost his fiancé Joseph Scriven was notified of a crisis his own mother was going through, and he wrote "What a Friend We Have in Jesus" as a poem to her.

Scottish Doctor W.P. Mackay was raised by a godly mother who gave him a Bible when he left home. During a difficult time he sold the Bible for a very small price. Years later he was attending to a patient who had no family but great faith. When the patient's things were being sorted out following his death, Dr. Mackay came across a Bible and discovered it was the very Bible his mother had given to him. In the cover of the bible was a handwritten note from his mother! Dr. Mackay rededicated his life to the Lord and wrote the hymn "Revive us O Lord."

The blind songwriter, Fanny Crosby wrote "Blessed Assurance."

Louisa Stead who was a missionary in South Africa and Rhodesia lost her husband during a family picnic as he rescued their daughter Lily from drowning and in the process perished himself. In these conditions she penned the words to "Tis So Sweet To Trust In Jesus."

George Bennard's father died when George was only sixteen. George responded to his dad's death by taking on the responsibility of being the sole provider for his mother and sisters. Years later he trained for the ministry and became an evangelist in the Salvation Army and the Methodist Episcopal Church. During a difficult time of ministry God revealed some things to him concerning John 3:16 and from that revelation he penned the words of "The Old Rugged cross."

We should end our day with praise to Him...

During our daily prayer times, instead of rattling off all our problems to the Lord like a grocery list, we should spend some serious time thanking Him for all the things He has done!

We should work on accentuating the positive and deemphasizing the negative! Remember every dark cloud has a silver lining! Charles Spurgeon said we should "Wash our face every morning in a bath of praise."

Only being able to worship to a certain kind of music...
