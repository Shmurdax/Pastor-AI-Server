Servant Leadership:

Matthew 8:5-10 (NKJV) "Now when Jesus had entered Capernaum, a centurion came to Him, pleading with Him, ^6 ^saying, "Lord, my servant is lying at home paralyzed, dreadfully tormented." ^7 ^And Jesus said to him, "I will come and heal him." ^8 ^The centurion answered and said, "Lord, I am not worthy that You should come under my roof. But only speak a word, and my servant will be healed. ^9 ^For I also am a man under authority, having soldiers under me. And I say to this one, 'Go,' and he goes; and to another, 'Come,' and he comes; and to my servant, 'Do this,' and he does it." ^10 ^When Jesus heard it, He marveled, and said to those who followed, "Assuredly, I say to you, I have not found such great faith, not even in Israel!"

In this passage of scripture we have one of the greatest lessons on servant leadership that you will find in the Bible or anywhere else. The centurion is a man of great influence and when he approached Jesus his request was not for himself but for his servant. "Now when Jesus had entered Capernaum, a centurion came to Him, pleading with Him, ^6 ^saying, "Lord, my servant is lying at home paralyzed, dreadfully tormented." Servant leadership is always about others it is never about us.

We should ask ourselves the following questions to determine whether our service is really servant leadership: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Are we serving to make ourselves feel good or to benefit someone else?

- Are we serving because we love these people or because God loves them?

- Are we serving to be seen of man or of God?

- Are we serving for a temporal or eternal reward?

Servant leadership is about giving 100%. The centurion placed his life on hold and laid aside his very important responsibilities to pursue Jesus on behalf of his servant.

The thing that separates serving from servant leadership is whether one is serving with a whole heart or serving with a halfhearted attitude. Half hearted service cannot be defined as servant leadership and what a healthy church must have is a large number of servant leaders.

When I was growing up, my mother would use the following scripture on me to keep me on the straight and narrow path. Numbers 32:23 (NKJV) "But if you do not do so, then take note, you have sinned against the Lord; and be sure your sin will find you out." My mother made me believe every sin I committed would be brought out into the open for all to see. She made a miserable sinner out of me.

We all know that no one ever tells his or her entire life's story and that not all sin gets exposed. All of us have committed sins that we placed under the blood and they are going to stay right there. Praise God for that!

So what could this scripture possibly mean? When Moses was giving final instructions to the tribes of Israel, the tribes of Reuben and Gad requested special permission to stay on the opposite side of Jordan from the Promised Land. They were cattle farmers and the land was good for raising cattle.

Moses discouraged them from doing so but finally relents with a special provision. They were to cross the Jordan and help the Israelites conquer the land, only then could they return and set up camp. Moses is saying: "If you do what I have said in conquering the land then you can do as you have requested, but if you do not do as I have commanded, be sure your sin will find you out."

Moses is pointing to the sin of half- heartedness and say's "The sin of half heartedness will find you out." Did you get that? "The sin of half heartedness." God sees half-heartedness as a sin!

The sin of half-heartedness cannot be covered for very long on any level. Half-heartedness always reveals itself: (PLACE ON SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- In our relationship with God...

- In our educational process...

- In our marriages and families...

- In our work life and careers...

- Especially in our service to God and others...

If we are going to serve in an area we should do so as a 100%'er! What does 100% look like?

- Serve when you feel like it...

- Serve when you don't feel like it...

- Be where you are supposed to be when you are supposed to be there...

- Serve with a good attitude... (If you serve and then complain, you might as well not have served at all)
