# SECOND MILE LEADERSHIP

SESSION B

QUALIFICATIONS

In this final lesson we are going to talk about the qualifications and
expectations of a Second Mile Leader. We expect you to:

- [Host]{.underline} a life group...

- [Assist]{.underline} a life group leader...

- Be a [home opener]{.underline} for a life group...

- Be a [section]{.underline} pastor...

Tonight those of us who are going to be a Second Mile Leader will enter
into a covenant with CT Church and with one another. The Second Mile
Leadership Covenant will be one of [character]{.underline} and
[service]{.underline}: 1 Timothy 4:12 "Set an example for the
believers in speech, in conduct, in love, in faith and in purity."

SPEECH

Second Mile Leaders only speak words that [build]{.underline} others up.
As a leader we refrain from speaking negatively over others and their
lives. We also refuse to speak negative words about this church, its
leaders, or our fellow Second Mile Leaders. If we have issues with
things that happen in the course of relationships or with the operation
of ministries, we agree to bring those matters to the leaders privately,
requesting assistance in providing remedy for the situation.

As a Second Mile Leader I agree to conduct my life so as not to be a
stumbling block for anyone through the words I say or the actions I
take. I agree to:

- Bridle my [tongue]{.underline}...

- Refrain from the use of [alcohol]{.underline}...

- Refrain from the use of [illegal drugs]{.underline}...

- Refrain from the use of [tobacco]{.underline}...

CONDUCT

As a leader in this body:

- I commit to behave in such a manner as to [influence]{.underline}
 people toward Christ, not away from Him or His church...

- I will seek to live a life that is worthy of
 [imitation]{.underline}...

- I will conduct myself in such a way, that if people follow in my
 [footsteps]{.underline}, they will be led straight to Jesus...

A Second Mile Leader knows that no area of service is
[beneath]{.underline} them.

Second Mile Leaders serve with:

- Biblical [integrity]{.underline}... (A good name is to be desired
 above great riches)

- Ridiculous [stewardship]{.underline}... (Be a tither -- We don't walk
 with thieves)

- Absolute [dependability]{.underline}... (No excuses -- Be where you
 are supposed to be when you are supposed to be there -- Be prepared --
 Being on time is being late)

- Selfless [teamwork]{.underline}... (Be mindful of the rest of the team
 -- Take the highest road)

- Extreme [excellence]{.underline}... (Treat your ministry as if it is
 the most important thing you are ever going to do -- Because it is --
 Laying up treasure where moth and rust don't corrupt and where thieves
 do not break through and steal)

- Radical [respect]{.underline}... (Always prefer others ahead of
 yourself)

LOVE

Second Mile Leaders commit to exhibiting the characteristics of [1
Corinthians 13]{.underline} through our lives and ministries: How do we
to know whether we are walking in love?

- Love is [patient]{.underline} with people...

- Love is [kind]{.underline} and [good]{.underline}... (Showing and
 showering favors)

- Love does not allow for [envy]{.underline} or
 [jealousy]{.underline}...

- Love does not brag or seek [recognition]{.underline} from others...

- Love does not [feel]{.underline} or [act]{.underline} better than
 others...

- Love is not [rude]{.underline}...

- Love is not touchy or quick [tempered]{.underline}...

- Love does not keep record of [wrongs]{.underline} done...

- Love allows for no gloating over the [wrongdoing]{.underline} or
 [misfortune]{.underline} of others...

- Love is satisfied only with [truth]{.underline}...

- Love is sturdy under heavy responsibility and is willing to
 [cover]{.underline} for weaker Christians...

- Love chooses to [trust]{.underline}...

- Love believes the [best]{.underline} in all situations...

- Love never loses [hope]{.underline}...

- Love remains faithful in spite of repeated [attacks]{.underline}...

- Love never [quits]{.underline}...

- Love is [perfect]{.underline} and [complete]{.underline}...

- Love displays [maturity]{.underline} rather than
 [immaturity]{.underline}...

- Love [handles]{.underline} others as if it were [handling]{.underline}
 God...

Leaders believe the best about our fellow leaders. We choose to cover
them with support and prayer rather than exposing their weaknesses and
failures. Second Mile Leaders do not take the [high road]{.underline}
when offended: We take the [highest road]{.underline}.

FAITH

Second Mile Leaders commit to being a person who is in the
[word]{.underline} and [prayer]{.underline} daily. "Faith comes by
hearing... Hearing from the Word of God." If you do not have a set
time to pray and read your Word every day -- You don't have a prayer
life. If you don't have a prayer life you can't be a Second Mile Leader.
You can't [give]{.underline} what you don't [have]{.underline}.

Second Mile Leaders know that it is only out of an overflow of the
presence of God in our lives that we are able be of any
[assistance]{.underline} to those who need us.

PURITY

As people who cast a shadow of influence over those we lead, Second Mile
Leaders realize that our [personal conduct]{.underline} and [spiritual
condition]{.underline} bears influence over those we lead. Because of
that we must commit to keep our minds, eyes, and body pure from this
world. According to the book of James, we must walk in an accountable
relationship with God and others. All Second Mile Leaders must have an
[accountability]{.underline} partner. James 5:16 (LB) "Admit your
faults to one another and pray for each other so that you may be healed.
The earnest prayer of a righteous man has great power and wonderful
results."

Those who want to be effective leaders maintain an accountable
relationship with a peer. We also willingly allow our leaders to check
with our accountability partners to assure we have been open, honest and
transparent with them regarding issues in our lives.

people in your section and to assist in creating a positive experience

Second Mile Leaders do not become offended when:

- The [parking lot]{.underline} fills up we are asked to move our
 [cars]{.underline}...

 toilets]{.underline} and [stack chairs]{.underline}...

- The room [fills up]{.underline}, and we are asked to [give
 up]{.underline} our seats...

- We move into multiple services and are asked ask to attend a [new
 service]{.underline} at a time we don't prefer...

- We are asked to lead the way making a [financial
 sacrifice]{.underline}...

We may be asking you to serve:

- On the security team...

- On the parking lot...

- As an usher or greeter...

- As a nursery or children's worker...

- As a volunteer with the youth group...

As a Second Mile Leader we do not have the luxury of [saying
no]{.underline} to serve where we are needed, or to [cop a bad
attitude]{.underline} because we don't want to serve.

CONTRIBUTION

Give

You must tithe to be in leadership. We sincerely believe the tithe is
set apart unto God and is accursed if we spend it on ourselves. To do
so, is to bring a curse on the house just like Akin's family. This will
not be allowed for leaders. Your tithe must be clearly marked where it
can be [tracked]{.underline}. [Anonymous]{.underline} giving will not
work for leaders.

Attend

Each of you are expected to attend [faithfully]{.underline} not
[regularly]{.underline}. We should be here early and plan to stay late
following the services. Our duty is to serve and connect people and we
cannot do this without showing up.

Serve

Since you are now expected to be leaders, serving is
[mandatory]{.underline}.

As a Second Mile Leader you will now be expected to serve at:

- Church [services]{.underline}...

- Life [groups]{.underline}...

- Food [fairs]{.underline}...

- Health [fairs]{.underline}...

- Training [opportunities]{.underline}...

Second Mile Leaders must always be aware of the fact that a church will
never rise above its [leadership]{.underline}!

As a Second Mile Leader you can expect:

- [Difficulty]{.underline}...

- [Resistance]{.underline}...

- [Offenses]{.underline}...

When you step into leadership you are engaging in serious spiritual
warfare. Your position in the battle just changed from the mess hall to
the front line. You are moving from a soldier in the army of God to a
commander in the army of God.

This is way more serious than any of us ever think. You and your family
are about to become prime targets for the enemy. You will be blindsided
and you will be tempted to forget why you are where you are and who your
source is. This is a spiritual battle in which the leaders are the prime
target of the enemy.

Keep your guard up. Keep yourself in accountable in all of your
relationships. Keep yourself in a place of constant prayer and prayer
covering.

There will be times when it will be difficult to keep going. You simply
cannot quit. "There is no discharge in the army of God."

"A good soldier is willing to endure affliction."

"He who puts his hand to the plough and looks back is not fit for the
Kingdom of God."

"If any man will come after me let him deny himself, take up his
cross and follow me."

As Second Mile Leaders far too many people are counting on us for us to
have the luxury of failure. You will build deep relationships with
people - to remove yourself quickly from those relationships will be to
destroy their very souls.

God is not going to call you one day and then undo that decision the
next day. Don't take the easy way out. Take the difficult path through.

Today I am asking you to make a [one-year]{.underline} commitment to
your position and leadership as a Second Mile Leader.

How do we accomplish the passing of the baton of responsibility?

- I do the work and you [watch]{.underline} me...

- You help me do the [work]{.underline}...

- You do the work and I [watch]{.underline} you...

- You do the work and I hold you [accountable]{.underline}...

MY ND MILE LEADERSHIP COVENANT

I recognize the importance of having spiritual, moral, and ethical
accountability. I will seek and and receive coaching, correction and
spiritual discipline in my life.

Should I violate any of these covenants, I welcome loving correction in
my life. I will humbly and sincerely submit to correction and will seek
to remedy any situation or offense for which I am responsible.

If, in the future I fail to submit to direction of leadership or Godly,
loving, coaching and correction, I will submit a quiet resignation from
my post of duty. I commit to NEVER bring a reproach upon the leadership
of CT Church or my fellow Second Mile Leaders.
