Today we are going to talk about how the actions and expectations of others have a profound impact upon our lives. Words and actions have consequences for good and for bad. Words and actions have the power to build one up, and words and actions have the power to tear one down. "Life and death is in the power of the tongue."

I want to use a beautiful Old Testament story of privilege, tragedy, and grace to illustrate my point today. (2 Samuel 9)

 

The setting for this story is initiated in a friendship between Jonathan, the son of Saul, and David, a shepherd transitioning to King! 

 

When Saul the first King of Israel, sinned against the Lord, the prophet came to the house of Jesse and anointed David to be the successor of Saul. Saul eventually becomes jealous of David and tries to kill him. It was through these treacherous circumstances that David forged a strong relationship with Jonathan, Saul's son.

Through a violent battle with his archenemy, the Philistines, Saul was deposed as King and he and his sons were killed in that very battle. Remember, "Pride goes before destruction, and a haughty spirit before a fall."  

 

As a matter of course, when a King was deposed in the manner that Saul was, his entire family line was to either be killed or exiled.  In addition, all of their wealth was to become the property of the successor, or, of the nation itself. 

 

When Saul was defeated, David began to rule over Judah and reigned for seven years and six months, at which time he began presiding over the entire nation and moved his governmental headquarters to Jerusalem.  In the process of time David decided to find out whether there were any descendants of the house of Saul who remained. 

In his busyness of being King, David had lost touch with all who remained from the house of Saul, so he asked his servant Ziba to find out if anyone from Saul's family line had survived.

Ziba revealed to David that there was a lone descendent of the house of Saul. His name was Mephibosheth and he was the son of Jonathan and the grandson of Saul.

Mephibosheth was once a prince facing life in the privilege of the palace, and maybe even ascending to the throne himself one day! The expectations of others were running pretty high! Everyone expected Mephibosheth to live out his life in the lap of luxury.

At the age of five everything changed for Mephibosheth. His father, Jonathan, and his grandfather Saul were killed in a battle against the Philistines. When this occurred the expectations and actions of others turned on a dime, as they often do. One can go from a hero to a haint in a matter of minutes.

When the nanny who was caring for Mephibosheth heard of the tragic results of the battle between the Israelites and Philistines she picked up five-year-old Mephibosheth, and made a run-for-it.  In her haste to flee, she dropped the child and he suffered a spinal injury.  In one tragic day, at the age of five Mephibosheth:

- Lost his mobility... 

- Lost his position as a prince...

- Lost a very influential grandfather...

- Lost a father who no doubt adored him...

- Lost his ability to make a living because of his injury...

- Lost the security of being protected as a result of his family name...

 

Not only did Mephibosheth lose all of these things, Ziba revealed that he was living in the house of Machir, in Lo Debar.  Interestingly enough, Machir is the brother of Bathsheba! 

 

Machir, the son of Ammiel resided in Lo Debar:

- The name Ammiel comes from a Hebrew word meaning, "People of God."  

<!-- -->

- The word Machir comes from the Hebrew word, "makik" (maw-kak) meaning "To be brought low -- decaying."  The place Lo Debar was a pasture-less place in Palestine, probably desert and therefore more than likely it was a poverty-stricken place. 

 

Notice the sad trajectory of this once promising family with whom Mephibosheth was living:

- At one time they were the people of God...

- They had been brought low... 

- They were living in a poverty-stricken place...

 

If that is not bad enough, Mephibosheth was living with them, and had become completely reliant on their mercy and resources!  If they were in a dreadful spot in their lives, Mephibosheth was in an even worse position!   

 

The expectations for Mephibosheth's life had changed from power, prestige and prominence; to poverty, pity and even pathetic.

When we allow ourselves to be driven by the expectations and actions of others we tend to live up to, or down to, their expectations! As a result of the expectations of others:

- Some of you are underachieving because of the words that have been spoken over you...

- Some of you do not believe you can climb out of the vicious cycle that has plagued your family...

- Some of you believe you are going to have to live in poverty the rest of your life, because that is the way it has been for generations in your family...

- Some of you believe you can't start that business that you have in your heart, because of the fear imposed upon you by someone else...

- Some of you are in a career that you did not choose, it was chosen for you, and someone is living their life vicariously through you...

- Some of you feel trapped in a relationship that was not of your choosing...

- Some of you believe you are going to suffer with a certain disease because you were told that the disease "Runs in the family..."

- Some of you don't believe you can be involved in ministry because of a past failure...

I am here to tell you today that everything is not as it seems or as it has been stated!  God is able to turn the tables on the enemy!

This is where the story of Mephibosheth begins to turn!  

 

Others may not know we are alive and in the condition we are in, but God the Holy Spirit does! If we will allow the Holy Spirit to get involved in our devastating situation, He WILL unchain us from the expectations of others!  

Because of what others did, the pattern for Mephibosheth seemed to be:  

- One failure after another...

- Frustrations that built one upon another...

- Dashed dreams that appeared to be forever gone...  

- Disillusionments that appeared impossible to resolve...

- Disappointments that remained unsettled year after year...

 

God was about to unchain Mephibosheth from the actions and words of others and He is going to do the same for some others in this room today!  

  

David didn't owe Mephibosheth anything neither did God owe us anything.  If David did not owe Mephibosheth anything why would he give him a second thought?  2 Samuel 9:1 (NKJV) "David said, "Is there still anyone who is left of the house of Saul, that I may show him kindness for Jonathan's sake?"
