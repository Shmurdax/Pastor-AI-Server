CHURCH ATTENDANCE

(My history) In forty nine years of living on this earth there has never been a time when I was not "In Church":

- I was raised in a church!

- I was dedicated to the Lord in a church!

- I was saved in a church!

- I was baptized in water in a church!

- I was received into membership in a church!

- Susan and I were married in a church!

- We dedicated our children in a church!

- We have raised our family in a church!

- My funeral will be held in a church!

I am literally a product of the church. Because I am a product of the church, I am a champion of the church. I would fight hell with a water pistol in defense of the church!

I know there are some stinkers (Hypocrites) in the church but Noah had a couple of stinkers (Skunks) on his boat as well, but it was the best thing afloat!

Jesus went to church. Luke 4:16 (NKJV) "So He came to Nazareth, where He had been brought up. And as His custom was, He went into the synagogue on the Sabbath day, ..."

The apostles went to church. Acts 3:1 (NKJV) "Now Peter and John went up together to the temple at the hour of prayer, ..."

We are commanded to go to church. Hebrews 10:25 (NKJV) "Not forsaking the assembling of ourselves together, as is the manner of some, but exhorting one another, and so much the more as you see the Day approaching."

There are sins of omission and sins of commission, if I disobey a command I have sinned! We have been commanded to go to church, not to do so would be to commit sin.

The example of church attendance by Jesus and His disciples, as well as the scriptural commandment for us to attend church more regularly as we see the end time approaching is surely based on more than an effort to give us something to do to occupy our time! There are many reasons for a mandate concerning church attendance; I want us to focus on seven of them...

WE SHOULD ATTEND CHURCH TO BE FED SPIRITUALLY!

People have various opinions of what the responsibilities of the Pastor should be but the Word of God makes it clear that his primary responsibility is: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- To feed the flock! - John 21:15-17 (NKJV) "So when they had eaten breakfast, Jesus said to Simon Peter, "Simon, son of Jonah, do you love Me more than these?" If we are a faithful church attendee and the pastor discharges his responsibly he will not only preach to us but teach us line upon line, precept upon precept, all the while building us up in our most Holy faith. The first step to living a successful Christian life is to attend church faithfully.

If we attend church faithfully we will be surprised at how often the word of God will discern what we are dealing with or anticipate what we will be dealing with. Hebrews 4:12 (NKJV) "For the word of God is living and powerful, and sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing even to the division of soul and spirit, and of joints and marrow, and is a discerner of the thoughts and intents of the heart."

WE SHOULD ATTEND CHURCH TO FUNCTION AS A BODY!

When we connect ourselves as we should to a local church it helps us realize we are a part of something bigger than we are. Being connected to a local church aids us in our attempt to understand what it means to be a part of the corporate or triumphant church. Matthew 16:18 "And I also say to you that you are Peter, and on this rock I will build My church, and the gates of Hades shall not prevail against it."

Being connected to the local church points up the fact, we should make a contribution to other believers, by offering up our abilities in service to them.

When we are a faithful part of the local church we become aware of the importance of every other member of the local church. 1 Corinthians 12:18-20, 27 (NKJV) "But now God has set the members, each one of them, in the body just as He pleased.19 And if they were all one member, where would the body be?20 But now indeed there are many members, yet one body. - 27 Now you are the body of Christ, and members individually." Every member of the body is vitally important.

WE SHOULD ATTEND CHURCH TO BE UNDER SPIRITUAL AUTHORITY!

All authority comes from the Lord. When we connect to a proper local church we will be taught to submit to both civil and spiritual authority! All of us have a need to be under authority. Faithful church attendance teaches us the need to submit to spiritual authority. (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME) We come to realize God has placed a line of authority within the local church for its proper governance:

- Jesus is the good Shepherd!

- The Pastor is the under shepherd!

- Local leaders are called alongside the Pastor for the purpose of implementing the vision God has given to the Pastor!

Any kind of variance from this scriptural model leads to anarchy and division!

When we are closely enough connected to the local church to see its proper operation it gives us a model to govern our families.

Everyone needs accountability! Power corrupts, but absolute power, absolutely corrupts!

ILLUSTRATION:

A member of a certain church, who previously had been attending services regularly, suddenly stopped coming to church. After a few weeks, the Pastor decided to visit.\ \ The Pastor found the man at home alone, sitting before a blazing fire.  Guessing the reason for his Pastor\'s visit, the man welcomed him, led him to a comfortable chair near the fireplace and waited. The pastor made himself at home but said nothing. In the grave silence, he contemplated the dance of the flames around the burning logs.\ \ After some minutes, the Pastor took the fire tongs, carefully picked up a brightly burning ember and placed it to one side of the hearth all alone.  Then he sat back in his chair, still silent. The host watched all this in quiet contemplation.   As the one lone ember\'s flame flickered and diminished, there was a momentary glow and then its fire was no more. Soon it was cold and lifeless.  The Pastor glanced at his watch and realized it was time to leave; he slowly stood up, picked up the cold, dead ember and placed it back In the middle of the fire. Immediately it began to glow, once more with the light and warmth of the burning coals around it.  As the Pastor reached the door to leave, his host said with a tear running down his cheek, "Thank you so much for your visit and especially for the fiery sermon. I will be back in church next Sunday."

God ordained the church for the purpose of ministering to the saints and reaching the world. We cannot very well reach the world if we are not receiving ministry from others.

What better place to receive ministry than the local church?

INTRO: Prayer is a subject that transcends all religious barriers. Every religion has their own idea of the proper means, times and reasons for prayer.

Various religions pray to a diversity of gods: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Muslim and Islamic believers pray to Allah, five times a day

- Buddhist believers pray to Buddha

- Hindu believers pray to hundreds of gods, two times a day

- Jewish believers pray to Yahweh, three times a day

- Catholics pray to Mary

- All Christians pray to Jehovah, the one true God

The thing that sets the prayers of the Christian apart from all others is that other religions pray to dead deities, we pray to THE God who is very much alive. (My dad once had a bumper sticker on his vehicle that said: "My God\'s not dead, sorry about yours!")

Peter Kreeft, Author and professor of theology at Boston College made this observation concerning prayer: "I strongly suspect that if we saw all the difference even the tiniest of our prayers make, and all the people those little prayers were destined to affect, and all the consequences of those prayers down through the centuries, we would be so paralyzed with awe at the power of prayer that we would be unable to get up off of our knees for the rest of our lives." 

The Word of God makes it clear; prayer is the act of communicating with God. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) When teaching believers to pray Jesus said, "Our Father which art in Heaven..." If prayer is the means of communication between an earthly believer and his Heavenly Father the earthly believer should look forward to speaking with his Father/Mentor!

John 14:26 (NKJV) "But the Helper, the Holy Spirit, whom the Father will send in My name, He will teach you all things, and bring to your remembrance all things that I said to you."

The closer the relationship we develop with the teacher, the more we are capable of learning! It is not the responsibility of the mentor to make time for the mentee; it is the responsibility of the mentee to carve out time to spend at the feet of the mentor! The more shallow the relationships with the teacher, the less we are capable of learning! We are as close to Him as we want to be!

We should receive the Holy Spirit to receive Godly instruction!

- Every tenth animal belonged to the Lord. They were to number the animals and give the tenth one to the Lord. Vs. 32-33 "The Lord also owns every tenth animal counted off from your herds and flocks. Tithing does not require faith and does not necessarily bring with it the blessing of multiplication. Tithing places the believer in a position of releasing the Lord to rebuke the devourer for our sakes. Tithing does not break the spirit of mammon on the money; the believer should bind the spirit of mammon over their income and pronounce the blessing of the Lord upon it. First-fruit requires faith and enacts the blessing of multiplication upon our resources. One can tithe by giving one of ten, any one of ten, but giving the first-fruit requires giving the very first believing there will be nine more! First-fruit requires faith and carries with it the thrill of high adventure with regard to our giving!

- These were not suggestions for the Israelites; they were commandments that Moses received on Mt. Sinai! Vs. 34 "These are the commands that the Lord gave to the Israelites through Moses on Mount Sinai." The same people still believe we should live by the Ten Commandments! This Scripture lets us know that Moses received this law concerning giving in exactly the same place and from the same source that he received the Ten Commandments! Christ fulfilled the sacrificial part of the law but did not do away with Old Testament principles!

Based on this Scripture, I submit to you that anyone who is not a tither is stealing from God (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) and therefore is a thief! I seriously doubt if there is anyone in this place that would take money out of the offering receptacle, or be so bold as to hold a gun on the ushers! Estee Lauder said "When a person with experience meets a person with money, the person with experience will get the money and the person with money will get experience." (A deacon once told me that if I was looking for experience they could furnish it. He was right!)

When we have withheld our tithe we have robbed God; we have robbed our neighbor setting down the pew from us; and we have robbed the community in which the church is located; because of the thievery of some of God's people, the church will not have adequate resources to minister to its adherents or the world as it should.

We should return the tithe because a failure to do so makes us a thief!

WE SHOULD TITHE BECAUSE WE HAVE BEEN COMMANDED TO DO SO!

Leviticus 27:30-34 (NLT) "A tenth of the produce of the land, whether grain or fruit, belongs to the Lord and must be set apart to him as holy. 31 If you want to redeem the Lord's tenth of the fruit or grain, you must pay its value, plus 20 percent. 32 The Lord also owns every tenth animal counted off from your herds and flocks. Malachi 3:10 (KJV) "Bring all the tithes into the storehouse..."

It would be literally impossible for a rational person to read these two Scriptures and conclude it is anything other than a commandment! Tithing is not a suggestion for a Christian; it is a commandment to honor God with our finances. This commandment is not because He needs our money, it is because we need to give!

We need to give the tenth part of our income to God because when we do we are acknowledging that what we have does not belong to us, it belongs to God!

The reason these Scriptures sound so much alike is because they have the same author! This has little to do with Moses or Malachi; the words are being spoken by God through His servants!

The first rule of stewardship is to seek God first. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) The person who resists His commands is not seeking Him first, they have an ulterior motive. A true steward will always ask himself, "What would God want me to do with what He has entrusted to me?" The answer to this question; He would no doubt want us to invest a portion, at least ten percent, into His Kingdom. A good rule of thumb is 10% to God, 10% to savings, 33% to the government and 47% to live on. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE)

When a person is not a tither, his resources are invested in the following way; Luke 12:16-19 (NKJV) "Then He spoke a parable to them, saying: "The ground of a certain rich man yielded plentifully.17 "And he thought within himself, saying, 'What shall I do, since I have no room to store my crops?'18 "So he said, 'I will do this: I will pull down my barns and build greater, and there I will store all my crops and my goods.19 'And I will say to my soul, "Soul, you have many goods laid up for many years; take your ease; eat, drink, and be merry." Listen to how Jesus finishes this parable... Luke 12:20-21 (NKJV) "But God said to him, 'Fool! This night your soul will be required of you; then whose will those things be which you have provided?'21 "So is he who lays up treasure for himself, and is not rich toward God."

We have established that we are stewards, God\'s business managers; stewards are not required to be successful, they are required to be faithful! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) 1 Corinthians 4:2 (NKJV) "Moreover it is required in stewards that one be found faithful."

When we tithe we not only acknowledge that everything we have belongs to God, but we begin to walk in obedience to His command, thus placing ourselves in the pathway of a blessing. Listen to the words of Jesus with regard to tithing, Matthew 23:23 (CEV) "You Pharisees and teachers are show-offs, and you're in for trouble! If you are one of those people who are hung up on tithing being an Old Testament principle, Jesus himself says here in the New Testament that the Pharisees should be paying their tithes.

Tithing fulfills a direct commandment of the Lord. We should tithe because we have been commanded to do so!

The government of God and man is predicated upon the principle of each person adhering to the laws set forth by God and man. If the law is not followed judgment must be enacted upon the offender. If judgment is not enacted upon the offender eventually anarchy will set in and everyone will be a law unto themselves and will do that which is right in their own eyes!

Everyone in this place is aware that for a society to function properly there must be laws and penalties for breaking those laws. (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE) If this were not true, you would not be safe anywhere.

Those who intentionally break laws, whether they are of God or man, are rebellious, thus they become rebels!

From one end of the Bible to the other, obedience is a trait that is heralded as extremely important in the life of the believer. Adam and Eve got into trouble in Genesis for a lack of obedience. It is clear in Revelation 21:8 that the disobedient will be cast into the lake of fire.

We have established the tithing principle to be a commandment of God; therefore, we should do everything in our power to be obedient to His command. 2. Prosperity - Prosperity has been given a bad name by people, who want to abuse it, but the intention of our Heavenly Father is to prosper everyone who has enough integrity and character to do what is right with their finances. Prosperity is having enough, plus a little!

WE SHOULD TITHE BECAUSE WE WANT THE STOREHOUSE TO HAVE SUFFICIENT RESOURCES TO MINISTER AS IT SHOULD!

Malachi 3:10 (NKJV) "Bring all the tithes into the storehouse, That there may be food in My house..." I have intentionally left the issue of resources for the church until last because most people in the church assume that when the pastor speaks on tithing he is attempting to increase the revenues of the church. In this teaching on tithing I have only scratched the surface of the benefits of tithing.

It is true that when more of God's people tithe, the church will have more resources, but that is just one component of what is being said here. Even though I left this lesson until last it is no less important than any of the others.

The word "storehouse" in our Scripture comes from the Hebrew word Owtsar (ot-sow), and means that through tithing God intends for His house to have: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Treasures of gold and silver

- Supplies of food and drink

- Supplies of weapons

- Resources for times of difficulty

While God intends all these things He never intended for His house to be a bank but rather a resource center. I want to point out several things about the storehouse: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- The storehouse is the place we are instructed to bring our tithe

- The storehouse is the place where we receive Biblical instruction and fellowship (The local church)

- The storehouse should have responsible parties who have been assigned the responsibility of managing its resources according to Biblical guidelines

- Biblical guidelines for storehouse management are: (This is the CT philosophy as well)

 1. Take care of the widows and orphans

 2. Take care of the stranger in the gate

 3. Provide facilities for worshippers

 4. Provide for the discipleship of all believers connected to the storehouse

 5. Provide for the needs of the ministers who labor in the storehouse This has been the case from early Old Testament times 1 Timothy 5:17-18 (NKJV) "Let the elders who rule well be counted worthy of double honor, especially those who labor in the word and doctrine.18 For the Scripture says, "You shall not muzzle an ox while it treads out the grain," and, "The laborer is worthy of his wages."

 6. Keep two to three months operating capital

 7. Stay out of debt as much as possible

Throughout Scripture we are told repeatedly that all believers are connected through being a part of the body of Christ. Because we are all a part of one body, what I do has an effect on you, and what you do has an effect on me. This point is clearly illustrated in the storehouse principle of tithing; (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- If I do not return the tithe it affects everyone in my church, my church is not capable of doing what it could because I have restricted its resources

- If I do not return the tithe I bring the dynamic of a curse into the corporate church (Achan in the Old Testament)

- If I do not return the tithe my church will be somewhat limited in its role in the great commission

Again, I want us to ask ourselves the question, "If every member of this church were just like me, what kind of church, would this church be?" (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL THE NEXT SLIDE)

- Would we be able to provide the various ministries for the families of our church?

- Would we be able to hire staff to lead vital ministries?

- Would we be able to have a climate controlled atmosphere?

- Would we be a great Missions church?

If the answer to any of these questions is no, then you are not pulling your weight in the body of Christ! You should start now!
