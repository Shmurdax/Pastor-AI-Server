INTRO: Tomorrow we will be celebrating Memorial Day. Memorial Day is a federal holiday observed on the last Monday of May. This holiday was formerly known as Decoration Day and commemorates U.S. soldiers who died while in military service. This holiday was first enacted to honor Union soldiers of the American Civil War, and it was extended after World War I to honor all American soldiers who have died in all wars.

I would like us to have a moment of silence to honor these heroes who paid the ultimate price for our freedom.

On this occasion, I want to expand the scope of our thoughts to include all those who have gone before us.

ILLUSTRATION:

(As I refer to the pictures in the background on the stage, have the cameras focus in on them)

The common denominator of every war and all deaths is sin. All problems that we have suffered in our lives have been a direct result of imperfect people who are attempting to live in an imperfect world.

Sin was introduced in the Garden of Eden through the first Adam whose wife's name was Eve. Jesus is the last Adam. Look at the differences between the two:

- The first Adam was created -- The last Adam preexisted

- The first Adam was a man and nothing more -- The last Adam was God incarnate

- The first Adam sinned -- The last Adam atoned sin

- The first Adam bombed out in a paradise -- The last Adam didn't sin, even though He was in a difficult wilderness

- The first Adam made us losers -- The last Adam made us winners

- The first Adam introduced death into the culture -- The last Adam offers abundant and eternal life

The path to overcome sin in this present world is through the last Adam.

## I. SIN RUINED THE DAY...

Because of what the first Adam introduced into the world, all of us have experienced more than our fair share of difficulty. Romans 5:12,18,19 (NKJV) "Just as through one man sin entered the world, and death through sin, and thus death spread to all men, because all sinned. ^18 ^Therefore, as through one man's offense judgment came to all men, resulting in condemnation, even so through one Man's righteous act the free gift came to all men, resulting in justification of life. ^19 ^For as by one man's disobedience many were made sinners, so also by one Man's obedience many will be made righteous."

The first Adam introduced:

- Sin

- Sickness

- Disease

- Poverty

As a result, everyone who has followed the first Adam to planet Earth has experienced some, if not all of these maladies.

For those of us who are following the last Adam I predict: better days are ahead!

## II. JESUS GREATLY IMPROVED THE DAY...

Better days are ahead because God wants you to have abundant life and Jesus paid the price for it. John 10:10 (NKJV) "The thief does not come except to steal, and to kill, and to destroy. I have come that they may have life, and that they may have it more abundantly." The word "abundantly" as it is used here comes from the Greek word perissos (per-is-sos') meaning:

- Over and above...

- More than is necessary...

- Something supremely better...

- Extraordinary...

- Preeminent...

- Remarkable...

- Superadded...

- Uncommon...

- Excellent...

- Superior...

To summarize, the abundant life Jesus offers - means "to exceed some number or measure or rank or need." (Strongs)

If we have made Jesus the top priority in life, better days are ahead.

## III. THE BEST IS YET TO COME...

The sin of the first Adam ruined the day but the sacrifice of the last Adam greatly improved the course of history and all of our days. As good as life can be there is no way to compare the best day here to the worst day in heaven.

As we celebrate Memorial Day, we do so with the knowledge that our loved ones are enjoying a day like we have never known.

2 Corinthians 4:17-18 (KJV) "For our light affliction, which is but for a moment, worketh for us a far more exceeding and eternal weight of glory; ^18 ^While we look not at the things which are seen, but at the things which are not seen: for the things which are seen are temporal; but the things which are not seen are eternal."
