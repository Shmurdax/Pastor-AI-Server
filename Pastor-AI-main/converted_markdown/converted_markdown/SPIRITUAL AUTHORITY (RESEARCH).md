Understanding Spiritual Authority

# THE PRINCIPLE OF AUTHORITY

The centurion said that he was one under authority not one of
authority. When one is under authority, one can recognize authority and
knows the necessity of obedience to the word of authority or suffer the
consequences. As seen here, recognition of authority provides faith.

The Centurion recognized that Jesus walked in spiritual authority. That
was why Jesus had only to speak the word and He would be obeyed. The
Centurion understood this because, he too, walked in governmental
authority and could, by a word, direct those for whom he was
responsible.

When the Centurion said that he was "a man under authority", he meant
that he derived his authority from the government that he served. He
didn\'t have authority because he was under an army commander. He had
authority because he was part of the government\'s army. Had he left the
army, none of those of whom he spoke would obey him anymore because he
no longer had any governmental authority.

It is the same in the Kingdom of God. We exercise governmental
authority, which is "loaned" to us as long as we live and work under
His government. To exercise authority one must have the right to do
so. To attempt to exercise authority without having the right to do it
makes it illegitimate authority and puts it in the category of the
world and the principle of Satan.

Another example of having authority when under governmental authority is
Adam. God placed Adam under His authority and gave him a commandment
(law) that he might learn obedience: "Do not eat of the tree in the
midst of the Garden." Then, God gave Adam dominion (authority) over all
of the created things. As long as Adam remained obedient under
authority, he had governmental authority over everything else. When he
sinned (disobeyed), he came out from under God\'s authority and lost his
governmental authority over the created things.

God built into Adam the desire and ability to rule. However, He didn\'t
intend for Adam to rule without Him but would rule as His delegate in
the earth. Adam\'s rule over the living things on the earth would flow
from his fellowship with God and understanding of His purposes.

When Adam fell, the desire to rule was still inherent in him, however,
he lost the ability to rule in a godly manner. This is what he passed
to the generations that followed.

There was also another aspect of Adam\'s rulership. Because he ruled
before the fall as a delegate of God, there is also inherent in Adam\'s
descendants the need to be ruled as God ruled Adam.

The Principle of Satan

Because of the fall of Adam, natural authority is taken rather than
given. Because it is exercised without the fellowship of God it is of
the same nature as the authority exercised by Satan.

Before he became Satan, Lucifer, at some point, decided that he would
take authority over all, even God.

Pride caused Lucifer to desire to usurp God\'s authority---to ascend to

NATURAL AND SPIRITUAL AUTHORITY

There are two types of authority---natural and spiritual. All authority
whether natural or spiritual is derived from our relationships. The
natural authority in a family is derived from God\'s order and structure
and our relationship to it. Spiritual authority is derived from our
relationship with the Lord and the work that we are called to in Him.
All authority relates to responsibility. If we have responsibility, we
have authority and vice versa.

I have authority in my family because I am the husband of my wife and
the father of my children. Because they are related to me, they are
subject to my authority. They are not subject because I am good or wise
but because God established it that way. My family doesn\'t have to
decide to be subject or not. They are subject by being my family. (They
do have to decide about obedience\-- remember, submission is absolute,
but obedience is conditional.)

The same is true of our authority in the Family of God. The authority of
the believer is based on the fact that one has a relationship with Jesus
Christ and, therefore, with the Father. It is based on who Christ is and
on who we are in Christ.

God has chosen (by His own design) in the family to set the husband as
representative of Christ and the wife as representative of the Church.
It is difficult for the wife to submit to her husband until she can see
the delegated authority vested in him. The real issue is God\'s
authority not her husband.

CONFLICT OF AUTHORITY

All spiritual authority has its basis in God and is received from God.
It cannot be taken but only received as God gives it to us. Jesus did
not take authority but received it from the Father.

Matthew 28:18 NASB\
(18) And Jesus came up and spoke to them, saying, "All authority has
been given to Me in heaven and on earth.

When the Father made Jesus both Lord and King, He became the ruler of
the Kingdom of God and it is from His position as King that He exercises
authority.

1 Corinthians 15:24 NASB\
(24) then comes the end, when He hands over the kingdom to the God and
Father, when He has abolished all rule and all authority and power.

Since Jesus has all authority, what authority is He abolishing in His
Kingdom? He is abolishing all natural, positional, secular authority
that has been taken under the principle of Satan without regard to God.

Jesus clearly warned us of the difference between the authority of the
world and His authority.

Matthew 20:25-28 NASB\
(25) But Jesus called them to Himself and said, "You know that the
rulers of the Gentiles lord it over them, and their great men exercise
authority over them.

\(26\) "It is not this way among you, but whoever wishes to become
great among you shall be your servant,

\(27\) and whoever wishes to be first among you shall be your slave;\
(28) just as the Son of Man did not come to be served, but to serve, and
to give His life a

ransom for many."

Jesus clearly contrasts the way the world handles authority and the way
that we should as His followers. Spiritual authority has nothing to do
with being great or exercising authority over people but instead is
service to others. Natural or worldly authority seeks to be served and
exercise authority over as many people as possible, whereas spiritual
authority seeks to serve others and never seeks to exercise authority
over people.

Application of Spiritual Authority

The crucial aspect of spiritual authority is how it is applied within
the Church. The problem that has occurred previously is that our
application of authority has been based on our understanding of
authority as being over people (authority applied according to the
World\'s principles). Let\'s look at the scriptures with the
understanding of authority as service; that it relates to function; and
that leadership is by example.

Jesus repeatedly expressed authority in terms of example,
responsibility, and function by using the picture of shepherds and
sheep. Shepherds lead sheep by going in front, never by driving them
from behind.

We think of "being in charge" as having the authority to order people
around. This is not the way Jesus thought of "being in charge".

Luke 12:42 NASB\
(42) And the Lord said, "Who then is the faithful and sensible steward,
whom his master will put in charge of his servants, to give them their
rations at the proper time?

In this scripture being "in charge" dealt with function and the
responsibility of providing food, not with the authority to order people
around. We also see this concept applied by Paul.

1 Thessalonians 5:12 NASB\
(12) But we request of you, brethren, that you appreciate those who
diligently labor among you, and have charge over you in the Lord and
give you instruction,

In this scripture having "charge over you" relates to function and their
responsibility is to give instruction (food). Again, this has nothing to
do with ordering people around.

It is interesting that the New American Standard Bible translates "rule"
as manage when speaking of the requirements for Overseers and Deacons as
discussed in 1 Timothy chapter 3.

The idea of managing is probably closer to the intended meaning of the
use of the word "rule". We understand that we manage that for which we
are responsible. A department manager in a business only has authority
over the business functions and how people relate to the necessities of
operations. The manager has no authority over the employees for non-job
related conduct except as it affects job performance.

The Overseers or elders of the Church go before the people to lead by
example and conduct in order to provide instruction and manage the
flock. They are held accountable by the Lord for how they perform these
functions. We are to submit to the Lord in them and obey His
instructions through them as they walk in governmental authority.

How To Be Heard:

We live in a generation that is [enamored]{.underline} with having their
voice heard, and their ideas taken seriously. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL
NEXT SLIDE) Any leader who does not recognize this is doomed to fail.

The reason they are demanding to have their voice heard is because they
do not believe those in leadership are aware of the challenges of the
future. It appears to them that many leaders are asleep at the wheel or
at least intoxicated with the systems of the past.

There is a desperate need in the church for older leaders to understand
younger leaders and vice versa. This leadership lesson is intended to
accomplish two purposes:

1. Help established leaders to understand what the younger generation
 is clamoring for and where they are coming from.

2. Help younger leaders to realize the path to having one's voice heard
 is not through demanding to be heard.

I have spent more than thirty-five years as a lead pastor. During this
time the role of pastor has undergone such a metamorphosis, that if you
used the measuring stick of what an effective lead pastor looked like in
1978 to measure the twenty-first century lead pastor, you would discover
that the two have so few similarities that it would be impossible to
evaluate the two with the same measurement.

This generation has been raised with the following idiosyncrasies:
(PLACE ON SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- They are [latch]{.underline} [key]{.underline} children...

- Their early years have been spent in [day]{.underline}
 [care]{.underline} [centers]{.underline}...

- They have been forced to deal with the challenges of
 [single]{.underline} [parent]{.underline} households...

- They have watched the demise of the moral [veracity]{.underline} of
 this society...

- They have witnessed the emergence of [visual]{.underline} forms of
 communication...

- They have encountered the invention of the [internet]{.underline}...

- They are grappling with the challenge of how to establish a life with
 some semblance of normalcy in the face of [terrorism]{.underline}...

- They are currently watching helplessly as the [economy]{.underline} is
 in a state of disarray...

Because of this, the expectations of this generation are much more
demanding. They are accustomed to options and a lot of them. All these
things have converged to produce a generation of people who have a
greater desire to be heard than any other generation before them. The
need of this generation to be heard stems from several factors: (PLACE

- They feel that their parent's generation has lost its
 [courage]{.underline}...

- They believe their parent's generation has a [consumer]{.underline}
 mentality rather than a [pioneer]{.underline} mentality...

- They believe their parent's generation has charted a course that has a
 date with [disaster]{.underline}...

- They believe their parent's generation has placed its loyalty in
 [organizations]{.underline} that have lost their way...

In short, they believe being heard is a matter of life and death! For
them, being heard will have much to do with whether or not they have a
future to pass on to their children.

I suppose each generation has felt the frustration of suspecting the
leaders of the previous generation have turned a deaf ear to their ideas
and treated them as if their ideas were premature or completely
illegitimate.

The dilemma that leaders and followers face is simply that leaders feel
[threatened]{.underline} and followers feel [ignored]{.underline}.

The tragedy is that this huge chasm of communication breakdown exists in
the following places:

- The home...

- The culture...

- The government...

- The church...

This problem is as old as the world, but it seems to be an extremely
sensitive issue in this culture because of the other unique challenges
facing this civilization. I believe God and His Word have an answer for
every question and challenge facing us.

Leaders have a responsibility to listen to the people who are following
them, but they also have a responsibility to anticipate changes in the
culture and lead in a manner so that their leadership plan continues to
be relevant. Followers rebel against authority that appears to have lost
touch with [reality]{.underline}. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

There should not be a communication gap between generations. One should
work with the other, bearing in mind that leadership is not a sprint; it
is a relay race. Each leader is eternally connected to their predecessor
and successor. Everything we build will be built on the shoulders of our
[predecessor]{.underline} and the goal should be to hand it off
gracefully to our [successor]{.underline}. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT
SLIDE) Leadership that operates otherwise is misguided and selfish.

David received a war torn nation, reigned for forty years, and handed
his son Solomon a peaceful nation ready to construct the most important
building in the land - the temple. This kind of selfless leadership is
pleasing to God.

The ultimate goal of a lead pastor and his key leaders should be to take
what they were given, improve on it, and gracefully pass it to the next
generation. Two difficulties I see in the 21^st^ century church are:

1. By in large, lead pastors do not prepare a healthy transition path
 for succession. It is as important to know when to step aside as it
 is to know when to step up.

2. By in large, key leaders fail to anticipate when their time of
 service in a particular role is finished and step aside to allow for
 younger leaders to step up.

Leaders who lead for the right reason always place the success of the
organization ahead of their own personal ambitions.

Now that we have established a paradigm for rightly directed leadership,
how should followers go about having their voice heard by those who are
presently in authority? Key leaders who are following a visionary leader
should be actively attempting to get up under the anointing of the man

Key leaders can have their voices heard by following some very simple
discipleship rules.

We must be willing to be [humbled]{.underline}. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL

conversion was a radical conversion similar to the conversion of Saul on
the Damascus road.

As soon as he accepted Christ, I recognized the call of God on his life
and we started spending lots of time together. As we spent time together
and I began the process of discipleship with him, he began to grow
quickly. He was a great student of the Word of God and a great student
of discipleship.

From the first moment Britt Brooks recognized there was a call of God on
his life he desired to be where I was with regard to leadership. I was
not threatened by that, I was honored by his desire to follow in my
footsteps. For him though, this meant he would have to humble himself to
serve someone that was already where he eventually wanted to be.

A person who is placed in authority before traveling the road of
humility will be required to travel a tough road, which will eventually
lead to public humiliation.

Key leaders can receive the mantle of a visionary, but they must first
humble themselves to serve God and the vision God is directing through a
visionary leader. People who desire to be in a key leadership position
must be willing to do the menial things first.

Every effective leader must first be [humbled]{.underline}. (LEAVE ON

 follow another leader...

station. One day a lady in a Cadillac pulled up to the gas pump and
wanted \$10.00 worth of gasoline, a window wash, the oil checked, and

There is such an important truth here, if we cannot be faithful to
humble ourselves in the smallest tasks, how will we be able to
appropriately handle ourselves in the larger responsibilities?

Dave, the founder of Wendy's said he "Received his MBA - Masters in

All leaders, including the pastor, must be willing to get their hands
dirty in the work of God. No one on the leadership team should be exempt
from serving in menial tasks. The higher one goes in authority, the more
important it becomes to humble ourselves.

Britt Brooks humbled himself before God and me, and served faithfully
for thirteen years. Now he and his family are leading a great church of
their own.

Submit to [authority]{.underline}. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

When we are in the process of being mentored, when we are on the journey
of being raised up to our destiny, when we are waiting in the wings for
God's timing to place us in a role to impact direction, we should always
keep in mind, we are not the authority. It is not yet our turn to lead!
This is our turn to submit to the authority God has set over us.

Key leaders walk a delicate road. They must possess leadership skills to
be in a key role, yet they must fly under the radar of submitting to a
visionary leader.

I am quite sure there were many times that my Britt found it necessary
to do something that was not necessarily his cup of tea or in his
gifting. What are we to do when this occurs? We are to submit and do the
very best job for our leader that we can possibly do. I can tell you
that for thirteen years that is exactly what this young man did. We
should do everything "... as unto the Lord!"

Fortunately there are only two times that we will be expected to submit:

- When we agree...

- When we do not agree...

Never forget the law of sowing and reaping is always in effect. Key
leaders must remember this: whatever they sow at God's house will begin
to surface at their house. We may not always agree with our leaders, but
we should always submit to them!

Submission is not something you can pick up and lay down at will; it is
something you do when you feel like it and when you don't, when you
agree and disagree. A follower is to submit to the leader in all cases
except when doing so requires a [violation]{.underline} of the written
Word of God. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

If we desire to be heard, we will submit to the leadership God has
chosen to place over us.
