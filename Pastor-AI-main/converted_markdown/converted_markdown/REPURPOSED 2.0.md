It's amazing to see how creative minds work to take what's dilapidated and make it beautiful.

The Premise for today's message is this:

God delights in repurposing human lives to demonstrate our worth and His creativity.

God wants us out of the landfill and into the marketplace. 2 Peter 3:9 (NKJV) "The Lord is not slack concerning His promise, as some count slackness, but is longsuffering toward us, not willing that any should perish but that all should come to repentance."

As humans we have a free will, as such, it is our task to cooperate with God's repurposing process.

His repurposing process can be a lifelong journey of refinement, growth, and fruitfulness if we will walk in obedience to His will and surrender to His purposes. John 15:1-2, 4 (NJKV) "I am the true vine, and My Father is the vinedresser. Every branch in Me that does not bear fruit He takes away; and every branch that bears fruit He prunes, that it may bear more fruit... Abide in Me, and I in you. As the branch cannot bear fruit of itself, unless it abides in the vine, neither can you, unless you abide in me."

THE POTTER

Our text points to:

- The potters house... (The place to be repurposed -- Stay in the house)

- The potter... (The person to do the repurposing -- God and our leaders)

- The wheel... (The process of repurposing -- It is never easy and it is almost always painful)

- The clay... (The attitude with which we submit ourselves to being repurposed -- Are we pliable or unworkable?)

If that clay could speak and feel, it would probably scream "Ouch!" It would also probably offer a complaint about how it's being treated. But the process of being remade is per the will of the potter, not the will of the clay!

My goal for this sermon today is to inspire you that now is the time for spiritual transformation for you and your family. I am praying that what may look junky or useless, today, can be made by God into a fruitful and productive vessel for His glory before the end of the year.

The crossroads or intersections of our lives are the places where we decide we are going to do our own thing, or we are going to finally get on the wheel and stay still long enough for the potter to create the image that is in his mind.

Lots of good and bad things happen at intersections:

- Good decisions and bad decisions happen at intersections... (We decide to obey or not obey traffic signals)

- Purposeful direction and accidents happen at intersections... (We decide whether to believe the GPS or not)

- Acts of progress or distress happen at intersections... (We decide to move forward to suspend the journey)

CONCLUSION:

Mark 10:46-47 (NKJV) "Now they came to Jericho. As He went out of Jericho with His disciples and a great multitude, blind Bartimaeus, the son of Timaeus, sat by the road begging. And when he heard that it was Jesus of Nazareth, he began to cry out and say, "Jesus, Son of David, have mercy on me!"

Bartimaeus was at an intersection. The people around him told him to shut up, to be quiet and to calm down. But Bartimaeus knew this was his moment and he was not going to miss his opportunity.

We see three things happening here:

- The people were telling him to be quiet...

- His circumstances were telling him he would always be a blind beggar...

- Jesus was close enough to get involved...

Bartimaeus could:

- Listen to the people...

- Let his history determine his future...

- Present himself to Jesus to be repurposed...

As the people attempted to quiet him down "He cried out louder than before!" The more negative feedback he received, the greater became his cries to get Jesus' attention.

For his tenacity and faith he got a set of repurposed eyes!

The clay on the potters wheel did not decide what it was going to become, the potter did! Even when the clay was found to be broken the potter "Made it another vessel as seemed good to the potter."

God is in the repurposing business and I have a vision of each of us GOING BEYOND our availability to the potter, to make us what He envisions us to be as individuals.
