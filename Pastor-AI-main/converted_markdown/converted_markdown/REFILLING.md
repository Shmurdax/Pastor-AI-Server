INTRO: Today is Pentecost Sunday. Pentecost means fiftieth day:

- Pentecost is historically and symbolically related to the Jewish Harvest Festival of Shavuot, which commemorates God giving the Ten Commandments fifty day's after the exodus. The Harvest Festival of Shavuot is the equivalent of our Thanksgiving holiday.

- Pentecost is celebrated on the tenth day following ascension Thursday. After the Resurrection, Jesus walked the earth for forty days before ascending into Heaven from Bethany's launching pad. Ten day's later the Holy Spirit was poured out in the Upper Room, thus the writer of Acts declared, "When the Day of Pentecost (Fiftieth day) was fully come..."

- Pentecost commemorates the descent of the Holy Spirit upon the Apostles and other followers of Jesus. This outpouring happened in an Upper Room in Jerusalem on the fiftieth day after the resurrection.

- Pentecost is the birthday of the New Testament Church. The Church was not born until the outpouring of the Holy Spirit on the Day of Pentecost.

Throughout the entirely of God's Word we see the relationship between oil and the Holy Spirit. This truth is so plain that even non-Pentecostal theologians draw a parallel between the two.

Consider the importance of oil in Old Testament worship:

- Oil was used to fuel the light...

- Oil was used to anoint individuals...

- Oil was used in the bread and the wafers...

One of the most important uses for oil in Old Testament worship was to keep the lamp burning in the place of worship. Notice Leviticus 24:2 (NKJV) "Command the children of Israel that they bring to you pure oil of pressed olives for the light, to make the lamps burn continually." This scripture tells us that the lamp was to burn continually. The lamp was to be serviced in the mornings and in the evenings. Where there is no oil, there is no light.

The priest was responsible for making sure there was oil for the lamps in the Holy place.

Numbers 4:16 (NKJV) "The appointed duty of Eleazar the son of Aaron the priest is the oil for the light, the sweet incense, the daily grain offering, the anointing oil, the oversight of all the tabernacle, of all that is in it, with the sanctuary and its furnishings."

One of the priestly responsibilities was to insure the lamps in the holy place never went out. In order to do this the reservoir of oil had to remain full. No reservoir stays full on its own!

We are the priest of our own lives and the responsibility for refilling belongs to us. Being filled is important, but continuing to be full is equally important!

Things that need to be refilled:

- A drink...

- A vehicle...

- A baby's bottle...

- A swimming pool...

## I. NO (RE)FILLING - NO MINISTRY

Eli was a Judge and High Priest in Israel. Everything we know about Eli's personal life points to the fact that he was a godly man. However, there are three alarming facts about Eli that I want to point out:

- Eli lost his vision... Notice 1 Samuel 3:2 (NKJV) "It came to pass at that time, when Eli was laid down in his place, and his eyes began to wax dim, that he could not see." I am not as sure he couldn't see, as I am, he didn't want to see. "Where there is no vision the people perish."

- Eli failed to maintain godliness in his home as well as at the church...

- Eli allowed the oil to run dry. The most disturbing thing about Eli to me is pointed out in 1 Samuel 3:3 (NKJV) "And ere the lamp of God went out in the temple of the LORD, where the ark of God was."

Eli didn't do his job. He neglected the refilling. It was literally impossible for the lamp to be refilled on it's own. Someone had to take care of it every morning and every evening. The oil in our personal lives and places of worship is not going to be placed on automatic refill we must take personal responsibility for the maintenance of the oil reservoir.

Judgment came to the house of Eli and also to Israel because someone neglected the refilling. In one horrific day, Eli, his two sons, and his daughter-in-law died! Before his daughter-in-law died, she named her son Ichabod. The name Ichabod means "The Glory of the Lord has departed."

What a sad commentary on a family that could have been divinely blessed of the Lord, but someone failed to see the need for refilling the reservoir of oil.

Stephen R. Covey -- "Ineffective people live day after day with unused potential. They experience synergy only in small peripheral ways in their lives. But creative experiences can be produced regularly, consistently, almost daily in peoples lives."

No oil -- no light! No oil -- no ministry!

## II. NO (RE)FILLING - NO STAMINA

Matthew 25:1-13 (NKJV) "Then the kingdom of heaven shall be likened to ten virgins who took their lamps and went out to meet the bridegroom. Now five of them were wise, and five were foolish. Those who were foolish took their lamps and took no oil with them, but the wise took oil in their vessels with their lamps." To me, the most astonishing thing about this passage of Scripture is that the most significant day in the lives of these ten virgins is their wedding day, yet, five of them were ill prepared. They were not ready for the bridegroom for one reason; they had an insufficient amount of oil in their lamps to go the distance!

One of the most significant features of being continually full of the Holy Spirit is endurance in the face of adversity. The reason the five foolish virgins failed to go the distance is because the oil level in their lamps was not a priority.

The virgins who had an adequate supply of oil are referred to in Scripture as "wise virgins." The five virgins who failed to bring an adequate supply of oil are referred to as "foolish virgins" and I want to point out the fact that the foolish virgins did not get married. They did not go the distance!

# They were too focused on things other than the wedding. The failure to have enough oil is inexcusable. The failure of the foolish virgins was not immorality it was a failure to focus on the importance of the wedding. C.S.
