The setting for this wonderful story of honor, grace and reconciliation has its foundation in a friendship between Jonathan, the son of Saul, and David, a shepherd transitioning to King!

When Saul the first King of Israel, sinned against the Lord, the prophet came to the house of Jesse and anointed David to be the successor of Saul.

Through a violent battle with his archenemy, the Philistines, Saul was deposed as King and he and his sons were killed in that very battle. Remember, "Pride goes before destruction, and a haughty spirit before a fall."

As a matter of course, when a King was deposed in the manner that Saul was, his entire family line was to either be killed or exiled. In addition, all of their wealth was to become the property of their successor or of the nation itself.

When Saul was defeated, David began to rule over Judah and reigned for seven years and six months, at which time he began presiding over the entire nation and moved his governmental headquarters to Jerusalem. In the process of time David decided to find out whether there were any descendants of the house of Saul who remained.

## I. EVERYTHING IS NOT AS IT SEEMS... David asked one of Saul's unemployed servants of the whereabouts of the descendants of Saul.

Ziba revealed there was one lone descendent of Saul, who happened to be the son of Jonathan but had been handicapped by a fall. Ziba revealed this descendant was living in the House of Machir in Lo Debar.

Mephibosheth, once a prince was doing life as a pauper! When Mephibosheth was five years old, his grandfather Saul and his father Jonathan were killed in a battle against the Philistines.

When the nanny who was caring for Mephibosheth heard of the tragic results of the battle between the Israelites and Philistines she picked up five-year-old Mephibosheth, and made a run-for-it. In her haste to flee she dropped the child and he suffered a spinal injury. In one tragic day, at the age of five Mephibosheth: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Lost his mobility...

- Lost his position as a prince...

- Lost a very influential grandfather...

- Lost a father who no doubt adored him...

- Lost his ability to make a living because of his injury...

- Lost the security of being protected as a result of his family name...

Remember, everything is not as it seems!

Not only did Mephibosheth lose all of these things, Ziba revealed that he was living in the house of Machir, in Lo Debar. Interestingly enough, Machir is the brother of Bathsheba!

Machir, the son of Ammiel resided in Lo Debar. The name Ammiel comes from the Hebrew word "ammiyel," meaning, "People of God." The word Machir comes from the Hebrew word, "makik" (maw-kak) meaning "To be brought low -- decaying." The place Lo Debar was a pasture-less place in Palestine, probably desert and therefore more than likely it was a poverty stricken place.

Notice the sad trajectory of this once promising family with whom Mephibosheth was living: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- At one time they were the people of God...

- They had been brought low...

- They were living in a poverty stricken place...

If that is not bad enough, Mephibosheth was living with them, and had become completely reliant on their mercy and resources! If they were in a dreadful spot in their lives, Mephibosheth was in an even worse position!

Remember, everything is not as it seems! God is able to turn the tables on the enemy!

This is where the story begins to turn! In this passage of scripture, Ziba is a type of the Holy Spirit: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- David did not know if Saul had any surviving family members, but Ziba did...

- David did not know about Mephibosheth, but Ziba did...

- David did not know where to find Mephibosheth, but Ziba did...

- David did not know about the condition of Mephibosheth, but Ziba did...

Others may not know you are alive and in the condition you are in, but God the Holy Spirit does! If we will allow the Holy Spirit to get involved in our devastating situation, He WILL turn the tables on the devil! Everything is not what it seems!

## II. MOST THINGS ARE BETTER THAN THEY SEEM...

The pattern for Mephibosheth seemed to be: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- One failure after another...

- Frustrations that built one upon another...

- Dashed dreams that appeared to be forever gone...

- Disillusionments that appeared impossible to resolve...

- Disappointments that remained unsettled year after year...

Mephibosheth started life as a Prince who would very likely sit on the throne of his grandfather Saul. He found himself rendered immobile by a fall, unable to make a living, totally dependent on the generosity of financially destitute extended family members!

There were no indicators that the forecast for his life was about to take a dramatic turn for the better. Mephibosheth assumed his life would be permanently pitiful. Most things are better than they seem!

Our lives were a pathetic disgrace until the Father sent the Holy Spirit to knock on the door to our hearts. When we opened the door to the Holy Spirit, He revealed the fathers intentions.

David didn't owe Mephibosheth anything neither did God owe us anything. If David did not owe Mephibosheth anything why would he give him a second thought? 2 Samuel 9:1 (NKJV) "David said, "Is there still anyone who is left of the house of Saul, that I may show him kindness for Jonathan's sake?"

None of us deserve to be loved or blessed by God!

## IV. THOUGH DELIBERATE, THE PLAN OF GOD IS BRILLIANT...

God did not come up with a plan for Mephibosheth on the day David summoned him to the palace. The plan of God was at work in the life of Mephibosheth:

- Before he was born into the family line of Saul...

- Before his father and David became best friends...

- Before his father and grandfather were killed...

- Before he was orphaned and placed in the house of Machir...

- Before his fortunes turned from promising to pathetic...

- Before he knew who Ziba, the servant of David was...

- Before he was summoned to the palace...

- Before he met the King...

- Before his fortunes were restored...

- Before he was invited to eat at the Kings table...

The story of Mephibosheth reiterates to us the following principles: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- God is never without a plan for us...

- God does not rush to reveal His plan to us...

- God is capable of working His plan despite our present circumstances...

Think of this:

- God made Abraham the father to nations even though he was an impatient liar...

- God called Moses to be a deliverer even though he was a murderer...

- God anointed David to be King of Israel even though his resume' was a bit thin...

- God called Paul to be an Apostle even though his past actions had proven him to be an angry, self-absorbed, murdering maniac...

God has a brilliant, slow developing plan for each of us despite the baggage of the past.

The reason the plan of God is so slow to develop is:

- God is helping us unpack our past baggage so we do not use it to mess up His brilliant plan for our future...

- God is behind the scenes arranging hundreds of details so that they will all converge in the right place, at exactly the right time, for the right purpose...

- God is determined to not allow His plan to come to pass prematurely in order to keep us from messing up a set up...

While Mephibosheth was languishing in the house of Machir in Lo Debar:

- God was texting Ziba thoughts of the plight of the lame son of Jonathan...

- God was e-mailing Ziba the google earth position of the only known survivor of the house of Jonathan...

- God was positioning Ziba to be the trusted servant of David...

- God was influencing the heart of Machir to be willing to share his resources with Mephibosheth until God was ready to relocate him to the palace...

Though deliberate, the plan of God was brilliant in the life of Mephibosheth! This plan included: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Assigning Ziba, his 15 sons and twenty servants to become custodians of all the property that had been returned to Mephibosheth...
