Guys, 80% of the makeup of a child comes from its father. How a child sees its father is the exact prism through which they will view the Heavenly Father!

The most significant problem facing America is the absence of fathers from the home and family. According to the U.S. Census Bureau, America now leads the world in fatherless families.

Gentlemen, we are better than that!

Almost 75% of American children living in a fatherless household will experience poverty before the age of eleven, compared to only 20% of those raised by two parents.

Children living in homes where fathers are absent are far more likely to be expelled from, or drop out of school. Children from fatherless homes are far more likely to:

- Develop emotional problems...

- Have behavioral problems...

- Commit suicide...

- Be neglected...

- Be abused...

Gentlemen, we are better than that!

Male children from a fatherless home are also far more likely to become violent criminals. As a matter of fact, men who grew up without dads currently represent 70% of the prison population serving long-term sentences. Gentlemen, we are better than that!

The Wall Street Journal reported that, on the average, American parents spend less than 15 minutes a week in serious conversation with their children. Fathers spend 17 seconds a day in intimate contact with their children. The important role parents play in socializing their children and forming their character is a difficult task, but it is an impossible one if the parents fail to be present or bond with their kids.

Gentlemen, we are better than that!

Let me give you a Biblical example of the influence of a bad dad. Nebat is the father, and Jeroboam is the son:

- Nebat means "He who opposes the people."

- Twelve times the Bible says "Jeroboam (the son) made Israel to sin."

- The Bible tells us that four of Jeroboam's (the son's) successors "Followed after the sins of Jeroboam the son of Nebat."

- Twenty five times the Bible refers to Jerobaom as "The son of Nebat."

The Scripture is unquestionably tying the bad character of Jeroboam to his father Nebat. Nebat was either a horrible person or allowed his son to behave miserably. Either way, Nebat bore responsibility before God for what his son Jeroboam turned out to be.

Gentlemen, we are better than that!
