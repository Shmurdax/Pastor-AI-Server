# SPIRITUAL AUTHORITY
(Pastor Installation Service) 
 

TEXT: Romans 13:1-7 (NKJV) “Let every soul 
be subject to the governing authorities. For 
there is no authority except from God, and the 
authorities that exist are appointed by God. 
2 Therefore whoever resists the authority resists 
the ordinance of God, and those who resist 
will bring judgment on themselves. 3 For rulers 
are not a terror to good works, but to evil. Do 
you want to be unafraid of the authority? Do 
what is good, and you will have praise from the 
same. 4 For he is God’s minister to you for 
good. But if you do evil, be afraid; for he does 
not bear the sword in vain; for he is God’s 
minister, an avenger to execute wrath on him 
who practices evil. 5 Therefore you must be 
subject, not only because of wrath but also for 
conscience’ sake. 6 For because of this you also 
pay taxes, for they are God’s ministers 
attending continually to this very 

thing. 7 Render therefore to all their due: taxes 
to whom taxes are due, customs to whom 
customs, fear to whom fear, honor to whom 
honor.” 

INTRO: Today we are officially installing a 
Pastor and his wife as the pastors of this church. 
This means they will be the spiritual authorities 
of this house. If you choose to attend here they 
will be your spiritual authorities. 
There can be no authority, spiritual or 
otherwise, without submission on the part of 
those who are being led by an authority. For 
starters, all of us must be under the authority of 
God but we also must place ourselves under the 
authority to a human being. 
Submission to authority must be transacted 
EXCEPT when such submission would cause us 
to violate a biblical mandate. (Explain what that 
is and is not) 
Spiritual principles highlighted in our text 
include: 

• Every person must be subject to governing 
authorities… (This means to be in obedience 
to) 
• All authority is appointed by God… (There 
are times that God allows for ungodly 
rulership because of the sin of the people – 
Israel wanted a King) 
• Anyone who resist authority resist the 
government of God… 
• Those in authority are not a threat to those 
who do good but to those who do evil… 
• Anyone who stands against authority that 
has been appointed by God will reap the 
negative consequences of their actions… 
• If we do good, we need not fear those in 
authority… 
• If we do evil, we are right to fear those in 
authority because both they and God have a 
sword that can be turned against evildoers… 
• If we do good, we will receive praise from 
those who are in authority… 

• We are to be doers of good deeds, not 
simply because we are afraid of the wrath of 
God or our authorities, but because we want 
to live with a good conscience… 
• We are to pay honor and respect to those to 
whom honor is due… (We are to respect the 
office even when we do not respect the 
person who holds the office) 
The opposite of submission is rebellion. 
Rebellion brings about a spirit of lawlessness: 
• Lawlessness is the spirit of antichrist and 
is alive and well in the world and in the 
church… (Lawlessness is making major 
decisions in our lives without consulting 
those who are in authority over us – Pastor 
Vs. preacher) 
• Rebellion is usually an attitude before it is 
an activity… (One can be in rebellion 
without saying a word or taking an action) 
• Obedience to those in authority is an 
expectation that God has placed upon all 
Christ followers… (Many people claim to 

be submitted to authority but their actions 
say otherwise – We won’t know if we are 
submitted until we disagree – My service 
to Bro. Granberry at the camp) 
• We were not born with the ability to obey; 
obedience is a trait that must be developed 
over time and through circumstances… (A 
child doesn’t have to be taught to 
misbehave, they have to be taught to obey 
– As human beings, submission to 
authority is contrary to our nature – The 
lack of submission was the sin of Nadab 
and Abihu – The ground opened up and 
swallowed them and all those who sided 
with them – I seen the equivalent of that 
within the church) 
• Each time we come to a place of 
disagreement with authority, we must 
make a conscience decision of whether to 
submit or rebel… (We are either in 
submission to authority or we are in 
rebellion – Most people would say, “I am 
not rebelling, I am disagreeing.” Look at 

this - The word submit means to “Stand 
under” but the word resist means to “Stand 
against” – The decision to stand under or 
to stand against belongs to the individual 
believer – The sum total of those decisions 
will determine whether we are UNDER 
authority or we are in REBELLION 
against the authorities in our lives, and 
ultimately to God) 
Unless submission causes one to violate the 
Word of God, we are all called upon to submit 
to all authority, and especially to spiritual 
authority. 
Yet, I must hasten to add, those who misuse 
authority will most certainly place themselves in 
the path of the judgement of God. The hand of 
the Lord is often swift and stern but sometimes 
it is slow and sure. One thing is for certain, it 
grinds to powder. 
 

CONCLUSION: 

Hebrews 13:17 (KJV) “Obey them that have 
the rule over you, and submit yourselves: for 
they watch for your souls, as they that must 
give account, that they may do it with joy, and 
not with grief: for that is unprofitable for you.” 
Look at what is being said here: 
• Obey spiritual authority… (Believe them – 
Have confidence in them) 
• Spiritual authority is to rule over us… (the 
word “rule” here possesses the connotation 
of a governor or judge – In other words, 
spiritual authority is to have the same kind 
of weight a governor or judge would have 
over our lives – They have the final say!) 
• We are to submit ourselves to spiritual 
authority… (The word submit here literally 
means to surrender to them) 
• Our spiritual authorities will give an account 
for us… (On judgment day, a spiritual 
authority will not have weight in whether a 
person is saved or lost, but they will have 
influence in the reward a person will 
receive) 

• We must live a life of submission to spiritual 
authority so that when they are called upon 
to give an account for us, they may do it 
with joy and not with sorrow… 
• A spiritual authority giving a good report on 
our behalf delivers great reward for us… 
(“You have been faithful over a few things, 
I will make you ruler over many things.”) 
• A spiritual authority giving a bad report on 
our behalf will have devastating results… (It 
is after the Judgment Seat of Christ that all 
tears are wiped away from our eyes) 
When choosing a spiritual authority, we must be 
careful because everything flows down from 
headship. (Lady who wanted to see the Pastors 
new car because she wanted to know what her 
next new car was going to look like) 
I want to always make sure that those I place 
myself under are doing more than I am, because 
all things flow down in the spiritual realm.
