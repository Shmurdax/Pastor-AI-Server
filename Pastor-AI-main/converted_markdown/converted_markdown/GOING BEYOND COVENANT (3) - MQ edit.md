INTRO: Covenant (Holman Bible Dictionary): A pact, treaty, alliance or agreement between two parties.

- Pact = An agreement. Harmony of opinion.

- Treaty = A formal agreement for peace, military alliance or commerce.

(ILLUSTRATE HOW COVENANTS WERE CONSUMATED IN OLD TESTAMENT TIMES)

When we are in covenant with God and with one another in the body of Christ, it means that we have made a formal commitment to work in harmony to advance common interests. It means that we have given our personal guarantee to carry out explicit responsibilities.

Our God is a covenant-maker:

- He made a covenant with Noah to never again destroy the earth with a flood and set as His sign the rainbow... (Genesis 9:8)

- He made a covenant with Abraham to give the Promised Land to Abraham's descendants... (Genesis 15:18)

- He made a covenant with Israel's King David to establish His throne forever... (2 Samuel 7:8-17)

- He affirmed that covenant with David's son, King Solomon... (1 Kings 9:1-9)

- He declared He would keep His covenants for a thousand generations... (Deuteronomy 7:9)

These are only a few examples of the many covenants of God with individuals and nations, recorded in the pages of Holy Scripture. They had certain things in common:

- God initiated them... This is of great encouragement to me because it demonstrates His forethought, His love and His desire to bless His people and manifest His will for them.

<!-- -->

- In each, God committed Himself to take certain actions and called upon the other party to walk in humility, faith and obedience...

We have been called by our Lord Jesus Christ to come into covenant with Him and one another on many different levels.

Our spiritual life is like the shape of a cross. It extends vertically, up and down, between God and us, but it also extends horizontally, from left to right, with one another.

Over the next few weeks we are going to talk about coming into covenant with our brothers and sisters in four different areas:

- Covenant to love each other...

- Covenant to attend church and a life group faithfully...

- Covenant to create a culture of invitation... Become a body of bringers.

- Covenant to support the work of God through our financial resources...

COVENANT LOVE

John 15:9-17 (NKJV) "As the Father loved Me, I also have loved you; abide in My love. 10 If you keep My commandments, you will abide in My love, just as I have kept My Father's commandments and abide in His love. 11 "These things I have spoken to you, that My joy may remain in you, and that your joy may be full. 12 This is My commandment, that you love one another as I have loved you. 13 Greater love has no one than this, than to lay down one's life for his friends. 14 You are My friends if you do whatever I command you. 15 No longer do I call you servants, for a servant does not know what his master is doing; but I have called you friends, for all things that I heard from My Father I have made known to you. 16 You did not choose Me, but I chose you and appointed you that you should go and bear fruit, and that your fruit should remain, that whatever you ask the Father in My name He may give you. 17 These things I command you, that you love one another." This indicates that we are to come into a covenant relationship with each other and commit ourselves to loving one another despite the pressure to do otherwise.

If we truly love God, then we will love others. 1 John 4:20 "If someone says, "I love God," and hates his brother, he is a liar; for he who does not love his brother whom he has seen, how can he love God whom he has not seen?"

Jesus gives us in John 15 His commandment that we love one another. This is a direct order from Christ. It is not a suggestion.

There are two Greek words that alternate for our English word "love" in John 15:

- Agape or benevolent love...When Jesus says keeping His commandments results in abiding in His love it is "agape" -- that is, benevolent love. It is love that gives what the other needs. Jesus uses "agape" when He says "Greater love has no one than this, than to lay down one's life for His friends." Agape is the giving kind of love.

- Agapao love, or a choice of the will... Jesus uses the word "agapao" in describing the love of the Father towards Him, and the love that He calls us to show one another.

What we are saying is that the covenantal love that Jesus calls us to is not based on carnal feelings, desires, or selfish motives. It is a matter of the will born of benevolent hearts:

- We love because we are Christians... Period.

- We love because He first love us... Period.

- We love because the Spirit of love indwells us... Period.

If we choose to come into a covenant love relationship with our brothers and sisters, what does that look like? Jesus says we are to love "as He loved us." Wow! Behold what manner of love is this?

- It is a love that takes the first step...

- It is a love that initiates from the lover and is not based on the worth of the lovee...

- It is a love that is benevolent -- it looks for ways to add to and benefit the other...

- It is a love that seeks a way and place to serve...

- It is a love that is self-sacrificing...

- It is love that abides and is enduring...

Feelings come and go. Godly love remains! It remains because it is based on character and choice, and not feelings and self-centeredness.

IF WE OBEY HIS COMMAND TO LOVE, WE WILL ABIDE IN HIS LOVE

To abide means to remain, to have a home, to have permanence.

IF WE OBEY HIS COMMAND TO LOVE, HIS JOY REMAINS IN US

Our English word "happy" derives from the Old English word, "hap," which has to do with possessing pleasant circumstances.

The joy of the Lord is something completely different. It's not based on circumstances -- it is based on abiding in Him. It surpasses all human understanding because it is literally out of this world!

IF WE OBEY HIS COMMAND TO LOVE, HE COUNTS US AS HIS FRIENDS

To have a friend is to have a confidant. A friend is one that you:

- Share secrets with...

- Share feelings with...

- Share material possessions with...

Obedience to love one another results in a friendship with God! It results in a special communion in which revelation and thought sharing reach ever increasing heights.

IF WE OBEY HIS COMMAND TO LOVE, WE WILL BEAR EVERLASTING FRUIT

When we love as Jesus commands, it results in words and deeds that please Him. That fruit will remain. It will go before us to the Judgment Seat of Christ. It will ensure that we experience an abundant entrance into His glorious kingdom.

IF WE OBEY HIS COMMAND TO LOVE, WE WILL PREVAIL IN PRAYER

What a powerful and glorious promise of Jesus! Without Him we can do nothing:

- He is the breath in our lungs...

- He is the guardian of our mind...

- He is the rewarder of our labor...

Through prayer we:

To prevail in prayer is to prevail in every area of life, work and ministry. Our prayers will be much more effective if we walk in covenant love with God and our brothers and sisters in the Lord.

INTRO: Tithing is a subject that most ministers shy away from, simply because they know that the giving nerve is, with most people, a very sensitive nerve. Just because there are people in the church who are uncomfortable with the pastor talking openly about money does not release him from the responsibility of teaching on the subject! You can tell where a person\'s heart is by taking a quick look at their checkbook! Jesus reiterated this in Matthew 6:21 (NKJV) "For where your treasure is, there your heart will be also."

Someone asked Willie Sutton, the notorious bank robber, "Why he robbed so many banks?" He replied, "That\'s where the money is." Each of us will leave a clear money trail to the location of our most valuable treasure!

Stewardship and tithing is all about a proper understanding of ownership; to whom do all our possessions belong? Psalm 24:1 (NKJV) "The earth is the Lord's, and all its fullness, The world and those who dwell therein." Once we establish true ownership, stewardship becomes much easier. God is the owner of all our possessions! In the Bible, a steward was the business manager for a wealthy person. Remember Joseph, who was the steward (Business Manager) over the house of Potiphar?

The person who has developed a proper understanding of stewardship knows it is more of an attitude than an activity! 2 Corinthians 9:6-7 (NKJV) "But this I say: He who sows sparingly will also reap sparingly, and he who sows bountifully will also reap bountifully.7 So let each one give as he purposes in his heart, not grudgingly or of necessity; for God loves a cheerful giver."

Those of us who are operating in a true spirit of stewardship will continually ask ourselves the following question: "What would God have us to do with what He has entrusted to us?"

We can't give the tithe because it does not belong to us. If you loan me something, and I bring it back, I cannot take credit for giving that item to you because all I did was return it. The best we can do is return the tithe to the Lord because it never belonged to us to begin with.

THE COMMAND TO GIVE

Leviticus 27:30-34 (NLT) "A tenth of the produce of the land, whether grain or fruit, belongs to the Lord and must be set apart to Him as holy. 31 If you want to redeem the Lord's tenth of the fruit or grain, you must pay its value, plus 20 percent. 32 The Lord also owns every tenth animal counted off from your herds and flocks. They are set apart to him as holy. 33 The tenth animal must not be selected on the basis of whether it is good or bad, and no substitutions will be allowed. If any exchange is in fact made, then both the original animal and the substituted one will be considered holy and cannot be redeemed." 34 These are the commands that the Lord gave to the Israelites through Moses on Mount Sinai." Malachi 3:10 (KJV) "Bring all the tithes into the storehouse..."

It would be literally impossible for a rational person to read these two Scriptures and conclude that tithing is anything other than a commandment! Tithing is not a suggestion for a Christian; it is a commandment to honor God with our finances. This commandment is not because He needs our money, it is because we need to give! It is not because the church needs our money, it is because we need to give!

We need to give the tenth part of our income to God because when we do we are acknowledging that ALL that we have belongs to God!

When a person is not a tither they tend to view their resources as belonging to them rather than to God.

We are stewards, God\'s business managers. Business managers carry out the plans of the proprietor of the business. Stewards are not required to be successful, they are required to be faithful! 1 Corinthians 4:2 (NKJV) "Moreover it is required in stewards that one be found faithful."

Tithing fulfills a direct commandment of the Lord. We should tithe because we have been commanded to do so!

Can we agree to enter into covenant with God, this church and our fellow believers to be faithful in our giving in 2017?

GENEROSITY OR THEFT?

Malachi 3:8 (NKJV) "Will a man rob God? Yet you have robbed Me! But you say, 'In what way have we robbed You?' In tithes and offerings." I realize calling anyone who does not tithe a thief is pretty harsh and tends to upset some people. It only upsets people who are not tithers! (Story of Robert Hogan and the man that was upset about a special offering)

If you are not a tither I am not purposely attempting to call you names or upset you, I am not the author - I am the messenger! Because I am not the author I have no right to edit the contents of this book! (Sid Roth asked me about making some edits to my book)

God used His prophet to call these people thieves because they were. They were robbing Him of the first fruits and/or the tenth of all they acquired. These people were well versed in the law. They were very much aware of their financial responsibility toward God and the temple; yet, they chose to not obey a clear directive from the Lord.

Leviticus 27 makes very clear several things:

- A tenth of all produce belonged to the Lord and was to be considered Holy! Vs. 30 "A tenth of the produce of the land, whether grain or fruit, belongs to the Lord and must be set apart to him as holy."

- If the tithe were to be used for other things, when it was returned a twenty percent penalty was to be collected! Vs. 31 "If you want to redeem the Lord's tenth of the fruit or grain, you must pay its value, plus 20 percent."

- Every tenth animal belonged to the Lord. They were to number the animals and give the tenth to the Lord. If it were to be discovered that they had substituted an animal for the tenth one, they were to give the tenth animal as well as the substitute! Vs. 32-33 "The Lord also owns every tenth animal counted off from your herds and flocks. They are set apart to him as holy. 33 The tenth animal must not be selected on the basis of whether it is good or bad, and no substitutions will be allowed. If any exchange is in fact made, then both the original animal and the substituted one will be considered holy and cannot be redeemed."

- Tithing does not require faith and does not necessarily bring with it the blessing of multiplication. Tithing places the believer in a position of releasing the Lord to rebuke the devourer for the believer's sake.

- Tithing does not break the spirit of mammon on the money; the believer should bind the spirit of mammon over their income and pronounce the blessing of the Lord upon it.

- First fruit giving requires faith and enacts the blessing of multiplication upon our resources. One can tithe by giving one of ten, any one of ten, but giving the first-fruit requires giving the very first believing there will be nine more! First fruit giving requires faith and carries with it the thrill of high adventure with regard to our giving! (Bill Moore -- Cow had triplets)

I become extremely amused at people who attempt to put up an argument concerning tithing based on the fact that the Old Testament has much more to say about tithing than does the New Testament. The same people still believe we should live by the Ten Commandments! This Scripture lets us know that Moses received this law concerning giving in exactly the same place and from the same source that he received the Ten Commandments! The law of tithing predates the Ten Commandments. Christ fulfilled the sacrificial part of the law but did not do away with Old Testament principles!

Listen to the words of Jesus with regard to tithing, Matthew 23:23 (CEV) "You Pharisees and teachers are show-offs, and you're in for trouble! You give God a tenth of the spices from your garden, such as mint, dill, and cumin. Yet you neglect the more important matters of the Law, such as justice, mercy, and faithfulness. These are the important things you should have done, though you should not have left the others undone either." If you are one of those people who are hung up on tithing being an Old Testament principle, Jesus himself says here in the New Testament that the Pharisees should be paying their tithes.

Tithing was a righteous principle that pre-dated the Law:

- Abraham gave a tenth to the Lord after victory over three kings in order to honor and acknowledge the Lord as the source of that accomplishment. Genesis 18:20 (NKJV) "And blessed be God Most High, Who has delivered your enemies into your hand." And he (Abraham) gave him a tithe of all."

- Jacob, the grandson of Abraham, promised that if God would keep him on his journey and bring him home to his family, that of everything he gained he would give the Lord a tithe. Genesis 28:20 (NKJV) Then Jacob made a vow, saying, "If God will be with me, and keep me in this way that I am going, and give me bread to eat and clothing to put on, so that I come back to my father's house in peace, then the Lord shall be my God. And this stone which I have set as a pillar shall be God's house, and of all that You give me I will surely give a tenth to You."

So you see, tithing came before the Law! It was a righteous principle that was incorporated into the Law. Therefore, if the Law had never been given the principle of tithing would still have been in effect!

I seriously doubt if there is anyone in this place that would take money out of the offering receptacle or be so bold as to hold a gun on the ushers! However, professing Christians do something far worse every week. They hold a gun to God\'s ribs by not returning the tithe!

Estee Lauder said, "When a person with experience meets a person with money, the person with experience will get the money and the person with money will get experience." (A deacon once told me that if I was "Looking for experience they could furnish it." He was right!)

When we withhold our tithe:

- We rob God...

- We rob our families...

- We rob our neighbor sitting down the pew from us...

- We rob the community in which the church is located...

Because of the thievery of some of God's people, the church will not have adequate resources to minister to its adherents or the world as it should.

ACTIONS THAT LEAD TO BLESSING

Malachi 3:10 (KJV) "Bring all the tithes into the storehouse, That there may be food in My house, And try Me now in this," Says the Lord of hosts, "If I will not open for you the windows of heaven And pour out for you such blessing That there will not be room enough to receive it."

The word blessing comes from the Hebrew word baraka (bear-aw-kah), and means:

- Source of blessing...

- Prosperity...

- Praise of God...

- A gift or present...

- A treaty of peace...

A closer look at this verse would reveal our tithing produces:

- A constant source of blessing -- Who would refuse such a source of supply? Our faithfulness in tithing insures the source of divine blessing will be constantly flowing in our direction.

- Prosperity - Prosperity has been given a bad name by people who want to abuse it. Conversely, the intention of our Heavenly Father is to prosper everyone who has enough integrity and character to do what is right with their finances. Prosperity is having enough, plus a little!

- Praise of God - When we tithe faithfully, God is proud of us and even praises our works! He brags on us. The end result of God bragging on us is that we will eventually hear it from His own lips, "Well done good and faithful servant..."

- A gift or present - When we tithe we can get ready to receive gifts from the Lord! Not just at Christmas, on our birthday, or anniversary, but random acts of kindness when it is least expected! He will not be out-given!

- A treaty of peace - When we tithe, we enter into a treaty (Covenant) of peace! Peace in our life, home, work, church, and other relationships! Peace with God, His church and ourselves.

We should tithe because we want to enact these blessings on our lives and our families! In fact, His promise is, "I will open for you the windows of heaven And pour out for you such blessing That there will not be room enough to receive it."

Can we agree to enter into covenant with God, this church and our fellow believers to be faithful in our giving in 2017?
