INTRO: This past Tuesday, November 11th, 2003 our country celebrated
Veterans Day. Veterans Day is a very important holiday in our country
because it honors all of those, living and dead, who have served in the
U.S. armed forces.

Veterans Day was formerly known as Armistice Day, and was first
proclaimed by President Woodrow Wilson on November 11, 1919 and marked
the one year anniversary of the ending of World War I. In 1954,
following World War II and the Korean Conflict, Armistice Day became
known as Veterans Day. This commemorative day is observed each year on
November 11th. In reality, the day to honor our veterans should be every
day, for we owe so much to the brave men and women who have fought for
freedom around the world.

In 1921, an unknown World War I American soldier was buried in Arlington
National Cemetery. Later the bodies of unknown soldiers from World War
II, the Korean Conflict, and the Vietnam War were entombed at this
location as well. The tomb of these soldiers has become known as the
"Tomb of the Unknown Soldier" and has become a major tourist
attraction in our Nations Capital.

This past August my family and I had the privilege of going to
Washington D.C. and while there we visited Arlington National Cemetery
and the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier. Every hour on the hour, 365 days a
year, a new soldier reports for duty. After an hour a relief soldier
comes up to take over for the next hour. They face each other and the
soldier going off duty says something to the new guard who is coming on
duty. As I sat in awed silence and watched the ceremony that surrounds
the changing of the guard I was struck by the words that are exchanged
as the responsibility of guard changes from one Soldier to another. The
words that are spoken are "Orders Remain Unchanged". (VIDEO CLIP)

The Tomb of the Unknown Soldier was dedicated on Armistice Day, November
11, 1921, and has been guarded continually since 1937. This tomb is
guarded by the Honor Guard Company of the 1st Battle Group, 3rd Infantry
