INTRO: Over the past few weeks I have been preaching a series of
sermons entitled "Ten Strategies for Dealing with Difficulty." This
series deals with the challenges the Children of Israel faced at the Red
Sea. Today, I want to focus on one of those strategies, "Acknowledge
the Enemy, but Keep Your Eyes on the Lord."

Many of you are pursuing ministry as a profession; others of you will
follow alternate options in serving the Lord. One choice is not greater
than the other; the important thing is to go after the will of God for
your life.

I hope all of you will make an attempt to achieve your God given destiny
in life. As you are launched into the real world you will discover life
to be a bit more difficult than it appeared to be. (Buttermilk @ 19 --
Christine Blankenship 16 years) (Young Pastor I mentored said, "You
made this look so easy!")

The reason life can be so difficult is because God has appointed a
destiny for you and there is a real, live enemy who will do everything
in his power to sabotage you and keep you back from reaching your
ultimate potential. Your enemy is not your parents, professors, or the
school President; your enemy is Satan!

There is no question that Israel had an enemy. The design of the enemy
is consistently bent on destruction and demise.

Look at what Israel was facing. Exodus 14:5-9 (CEV) "When the king of
Egypt heard that the Israelites had finally left, he and his officials
[changed their minds]{.underline} and said, "Look what we have done! We
let them get away, and they will no longer be our slaves."6 The king got
his war chariot and army ready. 7 He commanded his officers in charge of
his six hundred best chariots and all his other chariots to [start after
the Israelites]{.underline}. 8 The [Lord]{.smallcaps} made the king so
stubborn that he [went after them]{.underline}, even though the
Israelites proudly went on their way."

The Israelites could have denied the existence of an enemy but that
would not have changed the fact that a hostile king was in hot pursuit
of this group of emancipated slaves.

Denial of the enemy never calms his hostilities but the acknowledgement
of his existence demands readiness. Charles Spurgeon once said, "The
great tyrant has not forgotten you, and he designs your capture and
re-enslavement."

The devil sometimes launches direct, frontal attacks against us but he
is usually more conniving and insidious. In Ephesians 6:12 (NKJV)
" For we do not wrestle against flesh and blood, but against
[principalities]{.underline}, against [powers]{.underline}, against the
[rulers of the darkness of this age]{.underline}, against [spiritual
hosts of wickedness in the heavenly places]{.underline}.."

Whenever the enemy attacks, whether covertly or overtly, he is normally
closer and cruder than we first imagined, much like the Pharaoh of the
Exodus.

When dealing with difficulty we should always acknowledge our enemy,
while keeping our eyes on the Lord. (Use two students to illustrate
this)

Look at the patriarch (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME) Job and how he
faced his difficulties courageously! In one terrible season:

- He lost his herds to barbarians!

- He lost his children to a tornado!

- He lost his health to a disease!

- He lost his wealth to misfortune!

- He lost his friends to a critical spirit!

- He lost his wife to doubt!

As he sat among the ashes scraping his boils with pottery shards and
bemoaning his fate, he didn\'t real­ize that his afflictions had been
orchestrated by Satan in an attempt to destroy his soul. This was
nonetheless a fact!

Job emerged on the other side of the trial with his faith in tact and
his attitude optimistic! As a result, God gave him twice as much as he
had before the trial. He was 70 when the trial began, and he lived to be
210!

The devil came to church one Sunday and started shouting insults at the
crowd. Everyone vacated the building except one little old man. The
devil shouted to the old man, "Don't you know who I am?" To which the
man calmly replied, "Yes." The devil said, "Are you not afraid of
me?" The man said "no." The devil could not believe his ears and
said, "Why are you not afraid of me?" The man replied, "I was married
to your sister for fifty five years!"

> The same devil orchestrates similar attacks against us today.

How do we respond? We draw near to Christ and keep ourselves under the
protective cloud of His grace.

In Exodus 14, Pharaoh could threaten with brute force and protest; he
could stir up frightening clouds of chariot dust; he could terrify with
a thousand swords. But he was powerless to actually harm the Israelites
as long as they remained under the protective cloud of God\'s glory and
grace. (That cloud became darkness to the Egyptians and light to the
Israelites)
