INTRO: In 1921, an unknown World War I American soldier was buried
in Arlington National Cemetery. Later the bodies of unknown soldiers
from World War II, the Korean Conflict, and the Vietnam War were
entombed at this location as well.

The burial site of these soldiers is known as the "Tomb of the Unknown
Soldier" and has become a major tourist attraction in our Nation's
Capital. I have always been intrigued by the pomp and circumstance that
surrounds this tourist attraction.

Several years ago, I had the opportunity to visit Arlington National
Cemetery and the "Tomb of the Unknown Soldier." (PLAY VIDEO CLIP
WITHOUT SOUND)

At the "Tomb of the Unknown Soldier," every hour on the hour, 365 days
a year, a new soldier reports for duty. After an hour, a relief soldier
comes up to take over for the next hour. They face each other and the
soldier going off duty says something to the new guard who is coming on
duty.

As I sat in awed silence and watched the ceremony that surrounds the
changing of the guard, I was struck by the fact that the soldier going
off duty whispers something to the soldier coming on duty. As a result
of my curiosity I did a little research and discovered that only three
words are spoken, and they are "Orders Remain Unchanged."

The Tomb of the Unknown Soldier was dedicated on Armistice Day, November
11, 1921, and has had a military guard continually since 1937. Think of
this for the past eighty-one years, five months, and twenty-six days,
this Tomb has been guarded:

- Day and night...

- Rain or shine...

- Hot or cold...

- Storm and calm...

The original order? (PLACE ON SCREEN - LEAVE THIS SLIDE UP FOR A FEW
MINUTES)

"Guard with your life the memory and honor of these particular
soldiers and generally all the soldiers in Arlington National
Cemetery!" This order does not need to be restated; it is simply to be
carried out as each soldier relays the message to the next, "Orders
Remain Unchanged".

We are all aware of what the great commission is but rather than
focusing on the great commission, I want to focus on the "How to" of
the great commission. Jesus said in Acts 1:8 (KJV) "But ye shall
receive power, after that the Holy Ghost is come upon you: and ye shall
be witnesses unto me both in Jerusalem, and in all Judaea, and in
Samaria, and unto the uttermost part of the earth."

In the military, when an order has been given, it is not a plea or
suggestion, it is a directive! A military order is not to be voted on by
the enlisted men or the officers, it is a command that if not carried
out, bears severe consequences for those who are derelict in their
duties!

The greatest institution on the face of planet earth is the church of
the Living God! The orders of the Commander in Chief are not a plea or
suggestion and they are not up for a vote! They are orders that MUST be
carried out by those of us who have enlisted in His service!

Of course, we are all interested in carrying out the wishes of Jesus but
the "How to" seems to be a little clouded for us from time to time.
Jesus outlines three things in this Scripture: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN
ONE AT A TIME)

- Power to go... (The Holy Spirit!)

- What to do... Become witnesses! (There is far more to this than just
 speaking in tongues!)

- Where to go... (Jerusalem -- Our homes! All of Judea -- Our workplace!
 Samaria -- Our neighborhoods! The uttermost parts of the earth --
 World Missions!)

I believe in missions, but giving to missions will never relieve us from
the personal responsibility of soul winning! If the church is to
accomplish the purpose of God in the earth, we must re-focus on the
value of personal evangelism.

The Church must return to the frame of mind that views the great
commission as more than a nice little idea. The philosophical stance of
the Church must be that the great commission is a personal, individual
command to every child of God to go locally, nationally, and globally!

The New Testament does not place the responsibility for winning the
world on a corporate, ethereal, group of people; the responsibility for
evangelism is upon every individual Christian.

"Orders Remain Unchanged!"

In the military, once an order has been given, it remains in effect
until another order is given that alters, rescinds or supersedes the
original order! One order can last minutes or decades depending on the
magnitude and scope of the order and whether it is followed by
additional orders.

Based on a single order, the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier has been
constantly guarded for the past:

- 977 months...

- 4,248 weeks...

- 29,737 days...

- 713,684 hours...

- 42,821,031 minutes... (M)

- 2,569,261,842 seconds... (B)

Our Commander-In-Chief gave the original orders to his disciples who
were, in turn, supposed to pass along the orders to succeeding
generations! If the orders of the Commander-In-Chief have remained the
same since they were given, every generation has been responsible for
carrying out the orders in their day. All that needs to be stated when
one generation of disciples pass the baton to the next generation is,
"Orders Remain Unchanged."

Never before in the history of the world has a single generation been
given a greater opportunity to touch its world than has this high-tech
generation. The world is literally at our fingertips. (I minister to
thousands each week with nothing more than a cell phone through
Facebook)

Because we have been given such an opportunity, at the end of the day
our accountability will be greater than the responsibility of any other
generation. "Orders Remain Unchanged!"

In the military, when an order is given no person of an inferior ranking
has the power to alter or change the original order! Once the order is
given, for it to be altered, rescinded, or superseded an officer of
higher rank must give the revised order. The Captain of the Lords Host
gave us our orders and it is not possible for an organization,
denomination, or person to change the order! Mark 16:15 (KJV) "And
he said unto them, Go ye into all the world, and preach the gospel to
every creature." Matthew 28:19 (KJV) "Go ye therefore, and teach
all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father, and of the Son,
and of the Holy Ghost:" No one has the rank or authority to change
the Orders; they are to "Remain Unchanged!" Whether we carry out His
orders or not, they "Remain Unchanged!"

In 2,019 years, no higher order has ever been given to the church than

- 2,019 years old

- 24,288 months old

- 104,988 weeks old

- 736,935 days old

- 17,686,440 hours old (M)

- 1,061,186,400 minutes old (B)

Though this order is ancient, it is as relevant, powerful, and effective
as it was on the day it was given!

I do not want my generation to drop the ball and fail to carry out the
orders of the Commander-In-Chief! I want us to "Be about the Fathers
business" of carrying out the orders of His Son!

Jesus' model for building the church; saving the lost; populating
heaven; and de-populating hell was to use the feet, the hands, the lives
and the lips of dedicated disciples to turn their world upside down.

It is interesting that Jesus seemed to believe that if He could get
twelve guys to buy into His philosophy, and they in turn would do the
same thing, that the world would be won in short order. The place where
this plan went wrong is when the discipleship process turned inward
rather than outward - toward fattening up the saints rather than
creating disciple-making disciples. (Explain what the Lord spoke to me
about discipleship)

Think of this, if twelve people would commit to winning one person per
year and making that convert into a disciple who brings a person a year
to Christ, over a twenty-year period, 12,582,900 people would be won to
the Lord! In the world of the disciples, if the command of Jesus had
been carried out by twelve men over a forty-year period the population
of the planet would have been converted. If every Christian would commit
to winning one person a year to Christ and turning that individual into
a person who wins a person a year to Christ, the world's population
would be saved in far less than forty years!

2) See and seize all opportunities to speak out for Christ!

4) Go to the mission field - beginning with our families!

5) Send a missionary where we cannot go! (GIVE & GO!)
