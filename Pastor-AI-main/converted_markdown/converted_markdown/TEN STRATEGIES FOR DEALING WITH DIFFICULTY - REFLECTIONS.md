# TEN STRATEGIES FOR DEALING
WITH DIFFICULTY 
 
TRAPPED 
This is life! Tough times will come, and 
they will come to all of us; rich or poor; 
healthy or unhealthy; famous or infamous; 
saved or unsaved! As Christians tough 
times can often be the enemy attempting to 
convince us that we cannot do what God has 
called us to do! 
 
In difficult times we must get our minds 
made up as to whether we are going to stay 
and fight, or allow our destiny to be reduced 
to a level our enemy can be comfortable 
with! Psalm 34:19 (NLT) “The righteous 
face many troubles, but the LORD rescues 
them from each and every one.” 
 
When we are walking through difficult times 
one of the most productive things we can 
possibly do is to praise God for what He has 

already brought us through! 
 
The Red Sea passage is one of the great 
miracles of the Bible. Someone said that it 
was not a miracle at all because the Red Sea 
was only a very small body of water that 
could have easily been waded through by a 
child. If that were so, this instance becomes 
an even greater miracle because God 
drowned the host of the Egyptians in a mud 
hole! 
 
This miracle is laid out before us in the 
fourteenth chapter of Exodus. The Israelites 
have been freed from 400 years of slavery in 
Egypt and they march triumphantly out of 
the land. The sounds and sights of 
celebration were everywhere! 
 
When reality begins to dawn on the 
Egyptians, concerning the burden the 
Israelites have been carrying for the 
Egyptian economy, the Egyptians change 

their minds about releasing the slaves, and 
they pursue Israel. 
 
The Israelites are now faced with the 
dilemma of the Red Sea in front of them and 
Pharaoh’s army behind them! They are 
trapped! There is no way out! Have you 
ever been there? Do you feel trapped? 
• In your marriage? 
• On your job? 
• With your children? 
• In your finances? 
• In your spiritual walk? 
 
The feeling of being trapped is the feeling of 
hopelessness; there just seems to be no way 
out! God still performs miracles! The day 
of miracles are not over because there never 
was a day of miracles, this is a God of 
miracles! It is God’s nature to do a miracle, 
He is more apt do perform a miracle than 
not to perform one! 

Charles Spurgeon said, “The Lord will make 
a way for you where no foot has been 
before. That which, like a sea, threatens to 
drown you, shall be a highway for your 
escape.” Being trapped and feeling 
hopeless calls for a miracle! 
 
IS IT OK TO QUESTION GOD? 
When we need a miracle we expect God to 
build it, decorate it and deliver it to us in a 
nice neat carefully wrapped package, but 
listen very carefully to the words of Asaph, 
as translated by King James in verses 19 - 

20. (KJV) “Thy way is in the sea, and thy 
path in the great waters, and thy footsteps 
are not known. 20 Thou leddest thy people 
like a flock by the hand of Moses and 
Aaron.:” 
 
When God leads us, whether through His 
own personal hand or the hand of His 
appointed leader, His leadership does not 
allow for failure. 

This is like a shepherd leading his flock 
from pasture to pasture. 
 
In this passage Asaph say’s that God 
wrought His miracle for Israel “By the hand 
of Moses and Aaron.” Very few times will 
we receive a miracle without putting forth 
some effort on our own! God is willing to 
make a miracle for our life but we must be 
obedient in order for the thing to be brought 
forth in us! 
 
In the story of the Red Sea, Asaph found 
strength to overcome his own dilemma! 
Asaph makes 5 statements and asks 7 
questions… 
• In the day of trouble I sought the lord! 
• I experienced trouble I could not control! 
• I complained to God! 
• My spirit was overwhelmed! 
• I am so troubled I cannot speak! 

When you are in this kind of dilemma, is it 
ok to question God? The only people who 
do not want to be questioned are people who 
do not have answers. God is never without 
answers. Asaph is so overwhelmed that he 
asks seven questions and his questions are 
not so dissimilar to ours: 
• Will God ever hear me again? 
• Will God ignore me forever? 
• Will He ever be good to me again? 
• Will He ever show me mercy again? 
• Will His promises be good? 
• Will He be gracious? 
• Is God so angry that He fails to show me 
mercy? 
 
Asaph continues in Psalm 77:11-20 (NKJV) 
“Our LORD, I will remember the things you 
have done, your miracles of long ago. 12 I 
will think about each one of your mighty 
deeds. 13 Everything you do is right, and 
no other god compares with you. 14 You 
alone work miracles, and you have let 

nations see your mighty power. 15 With 
your own arm you rescued your people, the 
descendants of Jacob and Joseph. 16 The 
ocean looked at you, God, and it trembled 
deep down with fear. 17 Water flowed from 
the clouds. Thunder was heard above as 
your arrows of lightning flashed about. 18 
Your thunder roared like chariot wheels. 
The world was made bright by lightning, 
and all the earth trembled. 19 You walked 
through the water of the mighty sea, but 
your footprints were never seen. 20 You 
guided your people like a flock of sheep, 
and you chose Moses and Aaron to be their 
leaders.” 
 
THE MIRACLE 
Look at the Miracle of the Red Sea crossing: 
• The winds blew the sea split! 
• The waters congealed into towering 
walls! 
• The Israelites passed through on dry 
ground! 

• Jehovah dried out the Red Sea but did 
not leave one footprint! 
 
This happened not for the entertainment 
value of the experience, but to prove to the 
Israelites and to us in earth shaking, history-
making fashion that; even when we are most 
anxious and distressed; God will make a 
way when there seems to be no way! 
 
Exodus 14 provides us a biblical model of 
how to properly process difficulties by faith, 
in the light of God's almighty: 
• Presence! He is omnipresent! 
• Providence! He is omniscient! 
• Promise! He is a God who cannot lie! 
• Power! He is omnipotent! 
 
The Red Sea may roll before us; the desert 
may entrap us; the enemy may press on our 
heels; the past may seem implausible; the 
future impossible; but God works in ways 
we cannot see! This one thing I am 

absolutely sure of, God will make a way of 
escape for His children! Isaiah 43:19 (CEV) 
“I am creating something new. There it is! 
Do you see it? I have put roads in deserts, 
streams in thirsty lands.” 
 
1 Corinthians 10:13 (CEV) “You are 
tempted in the same way that everyone else 
is tempted. But God can be trusted not to 
let you be tempted too much, and he will 
show you how to escape from your 
temptations.” 
 
Colossians 4:2-3 (CEV) “Never give up 
praying. And when you pray, keep alert 
and be thankful. 3 Be sure to pray that God 
will make a way for us to spread his 
message and explain the mystery about 
Christ, even though I am in jail for doing 
this.” 
 
Isaiah 11:15-16 (CEV) “The Lord will dry 
up the arm of the Red Sea near Egypt, and 

he will send a scorching wind to divide the 
Euphrates River into seven streams that 
anyone can step across. 16 Then for his 
people who survive, there will be a good 
road from Assyria, just as there was a good 
road for their ancestors when they left 
Egypt.” 
 
Remember: 
• No sea is deeper than the ocean of His 
love! 
• No army is stronger than His hosts! 
• No force is greater than His throne of 
grace! 
• No enemy exists who can overcome His 
direct and indirect work in our lives! 
 
The reality of the Red Sea, in a word, is this: 
God will always make a way for His tired, 
yet trusting, children, even if He must split 
the sea to do it! 

In Judges 8:4 there is a little known 
Scripture that comes about on the heels of 
Gideon defeating the host of the Midianites 
with 300 men. It seems that when he returns 
home after this great victory, rather than 
encountering a celebratory environment, he 
finds himself embroiled in a conflict with 
the men of Ephraim who accuse him of 
showboating and not inviting them to join 
him in the fight. Do you think in his 
gathering and narrowing down an army of 
32,000 to a company of 300 there is any way 
possible these guys did not know about the 
impending battle? They did not want to be 
part of the solution they just wanted to share 
the spoils of the victory! 
 
Listen to this verse, Judges 8:4 (KJV) “And 
Gideon came to Jordan, and passed over, 
he, and the three hundred men that were 
with him, faint, yet pursuing them.” This 
was a gutsy group of fighting men who were 

determined that they would win irrespective 
of the cost! 
 
Why did Gideon not invite the men of 
Ephraim? Psalm 78:9-12 “The warriors 
from Ephraim were armed with arrows, but 
they ran away when the battle began.10 
They broke their agreement with God, and 
they turned their backs on his teaching. 11 
They forgot all he had done, even the 
mighty miracles 12 he did for their 
ancestors near Zoan in Egypt.” These men 
had the DNA of a quitter! Who wants to go 
to battle with a quitter in tow? Are we going 
to be quitters or winners in the day of 
difficulty? 
 
WORRY – THE ENEMY OF FAITH 
AND DESTINY 
The Lord is so specific with Moses that He 
actually tells him to turn back and camp at a 
specific place. What I am about to say will 
sound somewhat sacrilegious but I do not 

mean it that way! God’s instructions very 
seldom make logical sense and for that 
reason, those who pursue His will 
understand that they must receive His 
instructions with a spiritual mind, rather 
than a logical mind. 
 
The place where they camped really 
hemmed them in! The sea was before them, 
Pharaoh's hosts behind them, and the 
mountains around them. All this, at Gods 
instruction! 
 
Three things we should remember when we 
face difficulty: 
• We are able to control the outcome of 
some difficulties! 
• Some circumstances are beyond our 
control! 
• Whether or not we have any control, 
God will guide every circumstance to a 
satisfactory conclusion, if we will trust 
and obey Him! 

When we face difficulty it is easy for us to 
allow ourselves to begin to explore the 
world of worry! Someone described worry 
like this: “Worry is the trickle of fear that 
meanders through the mind, cutting a 
channel into that which all other thoughts 
flow.” 
 
John R. Rice said, "Worry is putting 
question marks where God has put 
Periods.” Bishop Fulton J. Sheen called 
worry, "Is a form of atheism, for it portrays 
a lack of faith and trust in God." 
 
But for some of us, worry seems as inherent 
as breathing. Most of us do not feel that we 
have done our due diligence until we have 
worried sufficiently in difficult times. 
 
Worry is the enemy of faith and destiny! 
Look at what Jesus had to say about worry. 
Matthew 6:25-34 (CEV) “I tell you not to 

worry about your life. Don’t worry about 
having something to eat, drink, or wear. 
Isn’t life more than food or clothing? 26 
Look at the birds in the sky! They don’t 
plant or harvest. They don’t even store 
grain in barns. Yet your Father in heaven 
takes care of them. Aren’t you worth more 
than birds? 27 Can worry make you live 
longer? 28 Why worry about clothes? 
Look how the wild flowers grow. They 
don’t work hard to make their clothes. 29 
But I tell you that Solomon with all his 
wealth wasn’t as well clothed as one of 
them. 30 God gives such beauty to 
everything that grows in the fields, even 
though it is here today and thrown into a 
fire tomorrow. He will surely do even more 
for you! Why do you have such little faith? 
31 Don’t worry and ask yourselves, “Will 
we have anything to eat? Will we have 
anything to drink? Will we have any 
clothes to wear?” 32 Only people who don’t 
know God are always worrying about such 

things. Your Father in heaven knows that 
you need all of these. 33 But more than 
anything else, put God’s work first and do 
what he wants. Then the other things will 
be yours as well. 34 Don’t worry about 
tomorrow. It will take care of itself. You 
have enough to worry about today.” 
 
How can we keep from worrying when? 
• Our outgo exceeds our income and it 
appears our upkeep is going to be our 
downfall? 
• The creditors are calling? 
• Our financial portfolio is collapsing? 
• When we are terminated from a job we 
intended to retire from? 
• When a loved one is diagnosed with an 
incurable disease? 
• When a child is living in a rebellious 
state, doing who knows what, with who 
knows who? (Fox News: After the New 
Orleans flood a reporter interviewed a 
group of gothic teens who ran a tattoo 

parlor on Bourbon Street. They were 
walking out of the city; one girl asked to 
use the reporter’s cell phone and when 
the person on the other end answered she 
said, “Daddy, can you come get me?” 
As it turned out, she was a Pastor’s 
daughter from Ohio. Daddy had 
probably been praying, “Lord whatever 
it takes…”) 
 
How could Moses and Aaron keep from 
worrying when: 
• The Red Sea was facing them… 
• The mountains were beside them… 
• The soldiers of Egypt were bearing 
down on them with swords drawn… 
 
The Egyptians were not one bit concerned 
about a confrontation with the Israelites 
because these people had spent the last 400 
years living as slaves. Not one of them had 
been trained in the intricacies of warfare nor 
did one of them own a weapon! What the 

Egyptians did not know is that the Israelites 
did not need a weapon; they did not need to 
know how to fight; they had a secret weapon 
and His name was Jehovah! 
 
What helped create faith and kill worry in a 
moment of terror was the fact that they knew 
Jehovah delivered them from Egypt and that 
it was His will for them to go to Canaan! 
Moses determined that they were where they 
were not by their own will, but according to 
the will of God! 
 
When we determine we are where we are by 
the will of God, we should begin converting 
our worries into prayers and our fears into 
faith. God will never send us to a place 
without furnishing the grace to do His will 
while maintaining our sanity and integrity! 
 
WHEN DELAY DELIVERS DESTINY 
Shortly after their deliverance from Egypt, 
God deliberately led the Israelites into a cul-

de-sac between hostile hills and the edge of 
a sea too deep to be forded and too wide to 
he crossed. 
 
The unmistakable indication of Exodus 
14:1-4 is that the Lord took responsibility 
for leading them into peril. (The devil may 
not be behind your current delay of game) 
 
God gave them specific, step-by-step 
instructions, leading them down a route to 
apparent ruin: “Turn and camp; camp there; 
there, before the entrapping sea; yes, right 
there in that impossible place.” 
 
Please understand that God was not being 
disingenuous to Israel: 
• He was about to deal with Israel’s 
enemies once and for all! 
• He was about to allow them to see Him 
in a way they had never seen Him 
before! 

• He was about to provide them with 
weapons to protect themselves, during 
the forty plus years of wilderness 
wandering! 
 
The Lord occasionally does the same with 
us: 
• Testing our faith… 
• Leading us into hardship… 
• Teaching us wisdom… 
• Showing us His ways... 
 
When we hit a delay, our first reaction may 
be a surge of panic and a sense of alarm, but 
we must learn to: 
• Make sure we are in His will! 
• Make sure we have not sinned! 
• Express faith rather than fear! 
 
In difficult times we must be patient while 
He brings the components necessary for our 
deliverance together! We must speak the 
Word rather than focusing on the 

circumstances! Remember Abraham’s 
journey up the mountain with Isaac? Do 
you think Abraham understood everything 
about this trip? We don’t always understand 
all the details about the trip but we are to 
have faith that the Father is working on our 
behalf! The delays are difficult but many 
times they spare us great difficulty! 
 
On September 11, 2001: 
• One man survived because his son was 
beginning kindergarten and he went with 
him to school! 
• One man was running late to work 
because it was his day to bring donuts; 
as a result his life was spared! 
• One woman was late because her alarm 
failed to function properly; her life was 
spared! 
• One person was late to work because of 
a traffic tie up on the New Jersey 
turnpike; a life was spared! 

• One person missed the bus and was 
running late; a life was spared! 
• One lady spilled food on her clothing 
and had to take time to change; her life 
was spared! 
• One person’s car would not start; a life 
was spared! 
• One person could not get a taxi; a life 
was spared! 
• One man wore a new pair of shoes 
which wore a blister on his heel; he 
stopped at a drug store to purchase a 
band aid and it saved his life! 
 
Consider these men and women of the Bible 
who, through no fault of their own, found 
themselves inundated with delays and 
disabling difficulties while trying to follow 
God: 
• Hagar, a single mom, forced into the 
desert with her boy, to die of thirst! 
• Joseph, wanting to fulfill divine dreams, 
was seized, stripped, sold as a slave, and 

imprisoned in Egypt! The Bible say’s 
“God was making a man for Egypt!” 
• Moses was caught between the splendors 
of Egyptian royalty and thankless 
affliction with God's people! 
• David was anointed by Samuel and then 
pursued by Saul and Israelite troops! 
• Hezekiah, seeking revival, was trapped 
by the most powerful army on earth, and 
they were bent on annihilating him and 
his people! 
• The Lord's disciples sailed at His 
command on Galilee only to face a 
terror-filled night of storms and waves! 
• The Son of man Himself fulfilling the 
Father's will, was nailed fast to a 
wooden cross and left to hang by His 
hands and feet until dead! 
• The apostles, trying to preach this 
Crucified One and were horsewhipped! 
 
The leader of that apostolic band later wrote, 
1 Peter 4:12 (NKJV) “Beloved, do not think 

it strange concerning the fiery trial which 
is to try you, as though some strange thing 
happened to you.” Jesus said, in John 
16:33-34 (NKJV) “These things I have 
spoken to you, that in Me you may have 
peace. In the world you will have 
tribulation; but be of good cheer, I have 
overcome the world.” The Psalmist said, 
Psalms (NKJV) Psalm 37:23-24 “The steps 
of a good man are ordered by the LORD, 
And He delights in his way.24 Though he 
fall, he shall not be utterly cast down; For 
the LORD upholds him with His hand.” 
 
(This is like a father following closely 
behind his child who is learning to ride a 
bicycle; he allows the child to ride but is 
determined not to allow him to get hurt) 
 
When we hit a delay we must remember; the 
same God who led us in; will lead us out! 
Our difficulties may lock us in; but they 

cannot lock our Lord out! We would do 
well to remember: 
• Daniel… 
• The three Hebrew Children 
• Paul’s shipwreck 
• John’s plight on the Island of Patmos 
 
God will never allow us to be placed where 
His grace cannot go! God will never show 
up too late or with too little, but He is 
always on time with more than enough! 
 
 
NO ACCIDENTS – ONLY 
PROVIDENCE 
There are no mistakes in God's plan. Psalm 
77:13“Everything God does is right!” 
Notice He doesn’t say: 
• Everything God does seems right… 
• Everything God does appears right… 
• Everything god does feels right… 
 
He say’s “Everything God does IS right…” 

 A. W Tozer said, "To the child of God, 
there is no such thing as an accident. He 
travels an appointed way... Accidents may 
indeed appear to befall him and misfortune 
stalk his way; but these evils will be so in 
appearance only, and will seem to be evil 
only because we cannot read the secret 
Script of God's hidden providence.” 
• No such thing as an accident… 
• We are traveling an appointed path… 
• We APPEAR to encounter accidents and 
misfortunes… 
• These things are only in appearance – In 
time, they will turn around for good… 
• Everything that happens to us is in 
God’s plan… 
 
During a very difficult time South African 
Pastor Andrew Murray made the following 
statement: 
• He brought me here; it is by His will that 
I am in this strait place: in that fact I 
will rest! 

• He will keep me here in His love, and 
give me grace to behave as His child! 
• He will make the trial a blessing, 
teaching me the lessons He intends me to 
learn, and working in me the grace He 
means to bestow! 
• In His good time He can bring me out 
again, how and when only He knows! 
 
God means for us to be where we are! 
 
There is no such thing as an accident – Only 
providence! 
 
SONG: “I’ll go where you want me to go.” 
 
WHAT IS GOD UP TO WITH THIS? 
When we encounter difficulty or perplexity 
we often seek relief above all else. Let me 
just say that this is a very natural reaction to 
difficult circumstances. 

When we hit our thumb with a hammer or 
stump our toe, every part of our body is 
focused on the care of the member that has 
been injured! 
 
When we encounter difficulties or 
perplexities we should get focused on what 
is God up to with this, but let be honest, that 
is rarely our default. Our default is to take 
flight rather than fight. 
 
We must be aware that the natural mind is 
the enemy of faith and of Gods will. When 
we encounter difficult times we must always 
remember that God is using this to set us up 
for victory, while setting the enemy up for 
defeat. 
 
In Scripture, God often allowed His people 
to endure hardship while he was collecting 
their enemies for a major disaster! Such is 
the case here at the Red Sea! Notice Exodus 
14:3-4, (KJV) “For Pharaoh will say of the 

children of Israel, They are entangled in 
the land, the wilderness hath shut them in. 
4 And I will harden Pharaoh’s heart, that 
he shall follow after them; and I will be 
honored upon Pharaoh, and upon all his 
host; that the Egyptians may know that I 
am the LORD. And they did so.” 
 
When we encounter impossible odds we 
should always ask ourselves, “What is God 
up to in this situation?” When we come 
upon a difficult crisis we must always 
remember that if we operate in faith rather 
than fear, God will ultimately reveal His: 
• Divine grace! (Do not frustrate the grace 
of God…) 
• Divine sufficiency! (Paul – My grace is 
sufficient for you…) 
• Divine will! (You are either in it or out 
of it…) 
 
Every trial is meant to enable the believer to 
preserve the balance of the Christian life and 

to bring glory to God in the most difficult of 
times. 
 
In difficult times we are often perplexed 
because the trial seems to be without rhyme 
or reason and we cannot find the answers to 
our questions. This is mostly because we are 
asking the wrong questions. We ask 
questions like: 
• How did I get into this mess? 
• Why did this happen to me? 
• How quickly can I solve this problem? 
 
These are logical questions, but they may be 
the wrong ones to ask. The children of Israel 
made this mistake, Exodus 14:10-12 (CEV) 
“When the Israelites saw the king coming 
with his army, they were frightened and 
begged the LORD for help. 11 They also 
complained to Moses, “Wasn’t there 
enough room in Egypt to bury us? Is that 
why you brought us out here to die in the 
desert? Why did you bring us out of Egypt 

anyway? 12 While we were there, didn’t we 
tell you to leave us alone? We had rather 
be slaves in Egypt than die in this desert!” 
(Moses went from hero to heretic at the first 
sign of Pharaohs army) 
 
There is a better approach, one that results in 
an entirely new way of looking at 
difficulties. This new approach puts our 
problems into a different context and creates 
a new paradigm for dealing with tough 
situations. 
 
This new way of looking at difficulty and 
perplexities is to begin with questions like: 
• What is God is up to with this? 
• What should I learn from this? 
• How should I react to this? 
• How can God get glory from this? 
 
DID GOD START THIS? 

Have you ever been drawn into a situation 
where you were left wondering, “Who 
started this?” “How did this get started?” 
 
As a Pastor I have often been drawn into the 
middle of difficult situations that I had 
nothing to do with initiating the 
disagreement or difficulty. Consequently, 
one of my favorite sayings is, “I didn’t start 
it to raining!” I am saying that I am having 
to resolve an issue that was not of my 
making. 
 
If we create the mess ourselves we can at 
least accept the fact that the fallout of the 
situation impacts us. But it is a completely 
different thing altogether when the impact 
hits us and we had nothing to do with the 
initiation whatsoever. 
 
Think of this: 

• God started the fight with the devil over 
Job… “Have you considered my servant 
Job?” 
• God started the issue at the Red Sea! If 
He had parted the Sea as soon as they 
arrived the Egyptians would never have 
been an issue! 
 
In the story of the Red Sea crossing, the 
sight of the Red Sea in front of them, the 
mountains on two sides, and Pharaoh’s army 
closing in from behind befuddled Israel! 
Exodus 14:10-12 (CEV) “When the 
Israelites saw the king coming with his 
army, they were frightened and begged the 
LORD for help. 11 They also complained to 
Moses, “Wasn’t there enough room in 
Egypt to bury us? Is that why you brought 
us out here to die in the desert? Why did 
you bring us out of Egypt anyway? 12 
While we were there, didn’t we tell you to 
leave us alone? We had rather be slaves in 
Egypt than die in this desert!” 

Moses and Aaron could have joined in the 
negativity of the crowd but they decided to 
trust God and find out what His plan was for 
their situation. Exodus 14:13-14 (CEV) 
“But Moses answered, “Don’t be afraid! 
Be brave, and you will see the LORD save 
you today. These Egyptians will never 
bother you again. 14 The LORD will fight 
for you, and you won’t have to do a thing.” 
 
The next time we are overwhelmed, instead 
of asking, "How can I get out of this mess?" 
We should try asking, "How can God be 
glorified in this situation?” “Did God start 
this? Trust me, If God started it, He will 
finish it for our good and His glory! (Larissa 
– “That is what I hate about you – You 
always know the answers before you ask the 
questions!”) 
 
Our perspective is entirely changed by the 
spiritual realities behind this approach. 
Notice the way this develops in Exodus 

14:3-4 (CEV). With regard to where they 
were to camp, God said to Moses, (CEV) 
“The king will think they were afraid to 
cross the desert and that they are 
wandering around, trying to find another 
way to leave the country. 4 I will make the 
king stubborn again, and he will try to 
catch you. Then I will destroy him and his 
army. People everywhere will praise me for 
my victory, and the Egyptians will know 
that I really am the LORD. The Israelites 
obeyed the LORD and camped where he told 
them.” 
 
God deliberately orchestrated the impending 
attack of Egypt’s army upon the Israelites 
here at the Red Sea! What was God 
thinking? 
 
He was about to demonstrate to Israel and 
the rest of the world the power He wields 
over His enemies and the elements. The 
New International version puts it like this, “I 

will gain glory for myself through Pharaoh 
and his army.” 
 
On that dramatic evening: 
• God's glory was revealed while His 
people, who appeared to be hopelessly 
trapped, were feeling the spray of the 
Red Sea! 
• The water of the mighty sea was divided 
by a strong east wind! 
• The wind created liquid walls of water 
from that which only hours before 
appeared to be a formidable foe! 
• The ocean floor began to appear as dry 
land! 
 
All this was accomplished as the wide-eyed 
Israelites became aware of the power of the 
God they would follow, through forty plus 
years of wilderness wandering. 
 
DIFFICULTY TO DELIVERANCE 

The miracle of the Red Sea was revealed in 
the most difficult of circumstances. This 
miracle became the substance of their songs 
and stories for many generations to come. 
Psalm 136:10-16 (CEV) “God struck down 
the first-born in every Egyptian family. 
God’s love never fails. 11 He rescued Israel 
from Egypt. God’s love never fails. 12 God 
used his great strength and his powerful 
arm. God’s love never fails. 13 He split the 
Red Sea apart. God’s love never fails. 14 
The Lord brought Israel safely through the 
sea. God’s love never fails. 15 He destroyed 
the Egyptian king and his army there. 
God’s love never fails. 16 The Lord led his 
people through the desert. God’s love never 
fails.” 
 
When facing difficulty we should attempt to 
maintain the attitude of J. Hudson Taylor 
who said, “I know He tries me only to 
increase my faith, and that is all in love. If 
His will is glorified I am content.” 

• God allows trials to increase our faith… 
• Whatever God allows is done in love… 
• If God gets glory I should be content… 
 
Psalm 106:7-8 (CEV) “When they were in 
Egypt, they paid no attention to your 
marvelous deeds or your wonderful love. 
And they turned against you at the Red 
Sea. 8 But you were true to your name, and 
you rescued them to prove how mighty you 
are.” 
 
How then, did God take an impossible 
situation, with a group of people with a bad 
attitude, flip it around, and use it for His 
honor? The story of parted waters shows us 
that God gains glory when: 
• His enemies are defeated! 
• His children are delivered! 
• His name is exalted! 
• His exploits are remembered! 
• His praises are sounded! 

Matthew Henry said, "God sometimes raises 
difficulties in the way of His people, that He 
may have the glory of subduing them, and 
helping His people over them. The Lord 
devises ways of turning difficulties into 
deliverances and problems into praise. He 
gives beauty for ashes and an attitude of 
worship for the spirit of heaviness. He will 
glorify His name in the lives of His children, 
whatever their afflictions. He will gain 
honor for Himself over our adversarial 
situations. In the process, He will leave 
behind such blessings as make the burdens 
melt away like wax in the sunshine.” 
 
Someone once said “The sorest afflictions 
never appear intolerable, except when we 
see them in the wrong light.” 
 
With the same confidence we can say that in 
some way or other God will deliver His 
people from every trial and trouble they 
encounter. It might not be my way; it might 

not be your way; yet in Hs own way the 
Lord will deliver. He will do it for His own 
name's sake; He will do it for His own 
glory! Psalm 34:19 (CEV) “The LORD’s 
people may suffer a lot, but he will always 
bring them safely through.” 
 
In the events of the Red Sea the Lord 
intended from the beginning to gain glory 
for Himself by snatching His people from 
the jaws of annihilation at the last moment. 
 
God never worried about the outcome, 
knowing He could provide an escape route 
at any time! Why should we worry? 1 
Corinthians 10:13 (CEV) “You are tempted 
in the same way that everyone else is 
tempted. But God can be trusted not to let 
you be tempted too much, and he will show 
you how to escape from your temptations.” 
 
I will be the first to admit that the Lord 
doesn't always deliver us from our problems 

in the way we want Him to. He does it His 
way, but in the long run His way is always 
best, and it always leads to Gods glory! 
Psalm 50:15 (CEV) “Pray to me in time of 
trouble. I will rescue you, and you will 
honor me.”
