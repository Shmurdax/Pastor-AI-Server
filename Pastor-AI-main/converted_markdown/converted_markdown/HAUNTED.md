## I. HAUNTED BY THE SINS OF ANCESTORS

Many people do not believe in generational curses. It doesn't take a very smart person to see that there are certain medical issues, personal habits, sins and weakness that seem to follow family lines.

Go to a Doctor for the first time and see how long it takes you to fill out the paperwork! Almost every question either has to do with your ancestry or your previous medical issues.

Here is what our text say's on the subject... "I, the Lord your God, am a jealous God, punishing the children for the sin of the parents to the third and fourth generation of those who hate me." Notice here, the sins of the father are visited upon the children to the third and fourth generation! How is this possible? This word is explicitly to "...those who hate me." Most people would not own up to hating the Lord. So, who are those who hate the Lord?

- Those who do not follow the Lord...

- Those who ignore the commandments of the Lord...

- Those who choose to walk in their own way, rather than in the way of the Lord...

- Those who despise (Do not properly value) the Lord, His word and His Church...

The sins of these people continue their unbroken chain of devastation in the family line because there is no action taken to stop it. Sin is a learned behavior! When sinful behavior is learned and not changed the consequences also remain the same! The definition of insanity is to keep doing things the same way while expecting different results!

Think of the example of the Children of Israel:

- They lived in slavery for 400 years...

- The slave mentality passed to their children and because of that, the next generation could not go into Canaan...

- Moses and Joshua broke the vicious cycle of doubt and unbelief (It took them 40 years to do it), so that the grandchildren actually inherited the Promised Land...

- Once Joshua took them into Canaan it was 150 years before anyone actually saw a giant... The giants are what kept them out and they didn't even see one for 150 years!

If in our generation and on our watch, we want to break the grip Satan has had on our family line, the only way we can do it is through the power of God!

Look at this... The sins of the parents can pass to the third and fourth generation (My Great- Great-Grandchildren). Please know that none of us are being charged for the sins of anyone else, the issue is that because of the sins of those who came before us, we learned bad habits and behaviors that if not broken, will have a negative effect on our lives, and an even worse effect on our children... Grandchildren... And great-grandchildren...

I have a word, in fact, I have two words for someone this morning... STOP IT!!! STOP THE CURSE IN YOUR GENERATION!!!

CONCLUSION:

Renowned Pentecostal theologian, Stanley Horton said, "Does a Christian need to do something about breaking a generational curse? The word hate in the Hebrew is a participle indicating characteristic or continuous action. Thus, the children, grandchildren, and great-grandchildren who keep on hating God reap a cumulative effect of God's judgment. On the other hand, those who turn from hatred of God and choose to love Him reap something far greater. They become part of a line that reaps the benefits of God's love on a thousand generations of godly people who preceded them. In considering how poor decisions can haunt us I cannot help but think of one of the Bible's most prolific characters -- Moses!

The history (His-story) of Moses:

- God called Abraham to follow Him and He became the father of the Jewish race...

- The descendants of Abraham ended up in Egypt, and after a time became enslaved for four hundred years...

- In a state of paranoia, Pharaoh ordered all male, Hebrew babies, two years and under, to be killed...

- Moses was born during the time that Pharaoh was executing children...

<!-- -->

- Moses' parents accomplish a fete that is near unto impossible -- They hid their newborn baby three months...

- When she could no longer hide her child, the mother of Moses placed him in a basket on the Nile river where Pharaoh's daughter was known to bathe...

- Moses was adopted by Pharaoh's daughter and raised as Pharaoh's grandson (With all the privileges that go with it)...

- Moses birth mother (Jocabed) was hired to be Moses' nanny... (Not bad -- Get paid to raise your own children -- Isn't that just like the Lord)

- No doubt Jocabed (His real mother) instilled in his little heart that he was a Hebrew, not an Egyptian...

- As Moses grew into manhood, he became unsettled by the suffering of the Israelis...

- At the age of forty, in a moment of seething anger, Moses killed an Egyptian for mistreating an Israelite...

- Because of this uncharacteristically bad decision, Moses was forced to flee as a wanted man... (One decision defined the life of Moses for the next forty years -- How long have you been haunted by a poor decision?)

- Moses fled to the desert of Midian where he tended sheep for the next forty years... (From age 40 to age 80! How many bad days do you think Moses had over this 40-year stretch? He could have been living in a palace but he was camped out on a hill in Midian watching sheep! Talk about tough breaks...)

Just because Moses made a bad decision doesn't automatically follow that his entire life had to be defined by a poor decision! The same is true for all of us! How long are we going to wallow in the defeat of a poor decision? How long are we going to allow the haunting to continue?

At the age of eighty GOD made a trip to the desert of Midian, to visit a fugitive from justice, and look at what occurs there!
