place at the occasion of the first harvest after entering into the
Promised Land.

and in celebration of where has brought us too. Deuteronomy 26:5-10

## I. THE HISTORY OF THE HARVEST...

Their ancestors were Syrians = A group of nomads with no permanent home,
permanent land or stability.

Their temporary dwelling place was Egypt = A place of oppression,
bondage and frustration. They grew from 66 people to 2 ½ - 3 million in
such a place!

Egypt was never home! They were anticipating living in the "Promised
Land" = A place of inheritance, permanence and stability. "A land
flowing with milk and honey."

Canaan represented the "Harvest" = A place of experiencing the
blessing of God on a continual basis.

## II. HARVEST IS NOT ONLY A TIME OF REAPING IT IS A TIME OF GIVING...

Harvest is a time of gathering the anticipated fruits of our labor.
Everyone loves harvest time! It is a time of exponential growth!

Harvest is also a time of:

- Paying the creditors from the last crop...

- Enjoying some things we have not typically been accustomed to...

- Laying aside adequate seed for the future crop...

Scripturally, giving is responsive - to what God has done - It can also
be done in anticipation - of what God is going to do.

We return the tithe as an acknowledgement of God\'s provision. It
doesn't take any faith to tithe -- we are giving based on what we
already have in our hand! It does take trust to tithe -- trusting that
90% will go further than 100%!

Anticipatory giving (Harvest offering) takes faith and as such triggers
the hand of God in our lives.

We sow offerings, plant seed, as an act of faith, anticipating blessing
and increase so that we can do more sowing!

## III. HARVEST IS A TIME OF BLESSING...

We have scheduled an annual Harvest Sunday in obedience to what the Lord
spoke to me and our purpose is to see you blessed. We want you and your
family to walk in the favor of God (FOG Alert).

In order to walk in a Harvest blessing one must:

- Give in response to the harvest... 100% of the people giving 10% of
 what you have already received.

- Give in anticipation of the harvest... Each of us tithing on what we
 want to be making a year from now. This takes faith, and faith moves
 the hand of God on our behalf!

Nowhere is this spirit and virtue of giving more gloriously displayed
than in the New Testament example of the Macedonian Church. 2
Corinthians 8:1-4 "Moreover, brethren, we make known to you the grace
of God bestowed on the churches of Macedonia: 2 that in a great trial of
affliction the abundance of their joy and their deep poverty abounded in
the riches of their liberality. 3 For I bear witness that according to
their ability, yes, and beyond their ability, they were freely willing,
4 imploring us with much urgency that we would receive the gift and the
fellowship of the ministering to the saints."

We know from this passage and from history that the Macedonian Church
(in Greece) was experiencing:

- Great trials of affliction...

- Deep poverty...

- Limited ability...

They gave because they had been recipients of God's grace and they were
anticipating being blessed in spite of their current circumstances.

When they gave they received "Great grace from God." What is it
about giving out of need that moves the hand of God? FAITH!

CONCLUSION:

Just after writing this glowing report of the Macedonian church in
Greece, Paul pens these words... 2 Corinthians 9:6-11 "But this I
say: He who sows sparingly will also reap sparingly, and he who sows
bountifully will also reap bountifully. 7 So let each one give as he
purposes in his heart, not grudgingly or of necessity; for God loves a
cheerful giver. 8 And God is able to make all grace abound toward you,
that you, always having all sufficiency in all things, may have an
abundance for every good work."

In this passage God promises to:

- Bless us commensurate to our sowing... "Whatever measure you use to
 give..."

- Show His affection to those who give with a good attitude... "God
 loves a cheerful giver..." It is God that loves a cheerful giver --
 We will accept an offering from a grouch!

- Bless us to a level that we will have more than enough in every
 season... "Always having ALL sufficiency in ALL things..."

- Use our giving to catapult us into works of grace and service toward
 others... "Having abundance for all good works..."
