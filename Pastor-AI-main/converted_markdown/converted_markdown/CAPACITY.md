Capacity = "The maximum amount that something can contain." "The maximum amount that something can produce."

Either way there is a place where:

- A person taps out...

- An organization hits the glass ceiling... (Story of Moses & Jethro)

<!-- -->

- The apparatus fails to bear the load...

Nothing good happens when one tries to force something beyond its capacity.

In this Biblical account of Jethro and Moses, both Moses and Israel were at maximum capacity unless and until some changes were made. Are you there? Is your church you lead, there? Is your family there? Is your marriage there?

Exodus 18:17-18 ^"^So Moses' father-in-law said to him, "The thing that you do is not good. ^18 ^Both you and these people who are with you will surely wear yourselves out. For this thing is too much for you; you are not able to perform it by yourself."

As leaders, if we must have our fingerprints on everything, we greatly limit our own capacity and the capacity of the organizations we lead.

Moses sin was not a moral failing, it was a leadership failure. Both are costly. He failed to recognize when he and the people were reaching maximum capacity.

In committing this leadership infraction, he was becoming the savior of the people rather than the leader of the people. Saviors ALWAYS get crucified!

As leaders, we dream of having faithful people to whom we can delegate responsibility. I am going to give you an earth-shaking truth... The people are already in the house! Our job is to:

- Identify their gifts... (giftstest.com)

- Assess their capacity...

- Monitor their success...

- Offer course corrections...

Most leaders do not have issues with delegating, they have issues with releasing authority when they assign responsibility.

If we desire to grow a healthy organization, we must become comfortable in the skin of releasing authority as we assign responsibility.

If all we do is assign responsibility while hoarding authority, we create minions who are trying to impress us. This leadership style runs dry quickly and creates haters. (Over ripes)

There are currently 9 Community Transformation Campuses, and I would dare say, there are people in some of our campuses that wouldn\'t recognize me if I met them on isle 13 at Walmart - The ego of most pastors couldn\'t handle that. I am not trying to build an organization that reaches my capacity, I am trying to build one that can reach the capacity of all of CT Nation collectively.

Jethro was not just telling Moses to distribute his responsibility, he was telling him that he would have to allow these capable people to make decisions, AND that he would SUPPORT them in their decisions. (Support publicly -- correct privately)

If a leader cannot part with authority when they assign responsibility, they have discovered their lid.

The unpardonable sin of leadership is expecting people to take responsibility without giving them authority.

As leaders we must be able to discern who is capable of leading at the following levels:

- 10...

- 50...

- 100...

- 1,000...

Think of this:

If we place a leader of 10 over 50, they will fail!

When they fail, they will either disengage, become an ongoing problem or leave altogether. Or they will do all three in the order that I have mentioned.

(What I told Brother Granberry about promoting a guy one level above his capability)

If we place a leader of 1,000 over 100, they will either become bored or drive everyone crazy.

(A deacon asked me what I thought this church would be if I devoted all my attention to the church)

Either way, they eventually disengage, become a problem, or leave altogether. Oftentimes they will do all three in the order that I have mentioned.

When we do not get the assignment of those who can lead at the levels of 10, 50, 100 & 1,000 right, we sow frustration and chaos throughout the organization.

Leaders, "They might not be bad people, they might be in the wrong spot!" Whose fault is it when people are assigned in the wrong responsibility? The leader bears the blame - AND I mean it is the fault of every leader above them in the organization.

Leaders should not be afraid to move people around! Moving people around requires:

- Wisdom...

- Finesse...

- Salesmanship...

People must understand:

- Why they are being moved...

- How it makes them more effective...

- How it benefits the organization...

- What the Kingdom impact will be...
