INTRO: In Jesus’ day, Roman soldiers had 
the authority to force civilians to carry their 
loads for one mile. The Jews held to the 
letter of the law on this. They measured the 
mile in steps: one thousand exactly. 
 
Jesus’ was saying, “Give people more than 
they have the right to demand.” 
 
SESSION A – IT’S NOT ABOUT YOU 
As Americans, we find ourselves in the 
middle of a capitalist nation where people 
are free to pursue a life of their choosing, be 

rewarded for hard work, and have things 
they have earned. 
 
And there’s nothing wrong with any of 
that….. Except when …. Those ideas and 
attitudes spill over into the Kingdom of 
God… And we expect His Kingdom to have 
the same philosophies and attitudes as the 
USA. It doesn’t and it’s not supposed to. 
 
In America, the individual is king! His 
preferences and pursuits are the highest form 
of reality. In America the “I” is the idol. We 
have: 
• The iPad… 
• The iMac… 
• The iPod… 
• The iPhone… 
• Self Magazine… 

We are used to and conditioned to this. It is 
part of a free market and competition and 
it’s a good thing! However, when we bring 
this philosophy into the church and expect it 
to operate the same way, the consequences 
are devastating! 
 
We choose churches based on what they 
have to offer. We stay as long as they cater 
to our preferences. We stay as long as we 
like the music, temperature, messages, 
people, parking, presentation and 
expectations. When those are not met, or 
those get uncomfortable to us, we leave. 
 
Americans have made the church into a 
corporation and we have moved ourselves 
from members with obligations to 
shareholders with rights. We have 
exchanged contributing for consuming. We 

act like we are customers and we are always 
right. We have made the church about … 
ME. 
 
I have bad news tonight! My opening 
message for those who would be Second 
Mile Leaders is this: 
• It’s not about you! 
• Jesus never called the individual to 
elevate himself. Instead He called us to 
humble ourselves. 
 
He taught us to take the lowest seat at the 
table. He said, “The greatest would be 
servant of all.” He said, “The first shall be 
last and the last first.” He modeled this in 
the garden of Gethsemane when he begged 
God for another way and then chose death 
on the cross because there was no other way. 

In short, Jesus told and showed us there is 
something more important than the 
individual. 
 
It’s not about YOU! 
 
DEATH TO THE DISCIPLE 
Luke 9:23 Then he said to the crowd, “If 
any of you wants to be my follower, you 
must turn from your selfish ways, take up

try to hang on to your life, you will lose it. 
But if you give up your life for my sake,

I think that somewhere along the way, we 
missed what is radical about our faith and 
replaced it with what is comfortable. We are 

settling for a Christianity that revolves 
around catering to ourselves when the 
central message of Christianity is actually 
about abandoning ourselves. 
 
A true disciple is one who realizes that they 
are in this world for God’s purpose and not 
their own. 
 
What should a radical follower of Jesus 
expect? In Luke nine: 
• Jesus sent the twelve out… 
• He fed five thousand… 
• Peter declared Jesus to be the Son of 
God… 
• Jesus was transfigured on the Mt. of 
Olives… 
• A demon-possessed boy was delivered… 

The crowd had witnessed all of this and 
three separate people volunteer to follow 
Jesus. Look at His response to their offers: 
• I will follow you but I don’t want to 
leave my house… “Foxes have holes 
and birds have nests but the Son of God 
has no place to lay His head.” 
• I will follow you but I first need to bury 
my father… “Let the dead bury the 
dead.” 
• I will follow you but I must arrange my 
business affairs… “No one having put 
his hand to the plough and looks back 
if fit for the Kingdom” 
 
As far as we can tell from this scripture, 
Jesus talked these guys out of following 
him. This gives us a picture of ourselves: 
Many of us want the benefit of Christ 

without sacrifice, but often Jesus calls us to 
the sacrifice without the benefit. 
 
If we are to be Second Mile Leaders, we 
have to understand Jesus is calling us into a 
mission that is bigger than us. Jesus is 
saying: It’s not about you! 
 
Jesus called His disciples to abandon 
themselves. They were leaving: 
• Certainty for uncertainty… 
• Safety for danger… 
• Self-preservation for self-denunciation... 
 
In a world that prizes promoting oneself, 
they were following a teacher who told them 
to crucify themselves. And history tells us 
the result. Almost all of them would lose 
their lives because they responded to his 
invitation. 

Jesus calls us to his cause intending to have 
us to surrender our own!

THE CORRECT MEANING OF 
"EKKLESIA" 
If we misunderstand what Jesus said, then 
we will be working our entire lives to do the 
wrong thing! We will be dedicated to Jesus, 
but still be in error. 
 
The word, "ekklésia" is the greek word used 
when your bible reads “church.” This Greek 
word appears in this grammatical form 114 
times in the bible. 

Ekklesia = a body of Christians called out of 
the Roman and Judean system to come 
together into a separate civil community. 
 
Ekklesia meant a politically autonomous 
body of Christians under no king but Jesus. 
This was the powerful organism that Jesus 
left here on earth and empowered once the 
Holy Spirit was given. It was so pure, so 
radical, that it literally turned the world 
upside down! 
 
The ekklésias we read about in the New 
Testament were independent civil bodies of 
Christians. They were a body, not a 
building.

EVERY BELIEVER A MINISTER 
The First Century Church was a passionate 
group of disciples who turned their world 
upside down. After a few centuries Christ 
Follower quickly became passive about their 
faith and relinquished responsibility to those 
who were professionals. The word go was 
replaced with the word come. 
 
This represented an emerging shift from 
functional leadership to a distinct and 
separate clergy office entrusted with the 
knowledge required for the proper life of the 
church. 
 
Although one may fault these changes 
because of their long-term consequences, 
they were nevertheless quite understandable 
given the circumstances. After a long period 

of struggle and periodic brutal persecution, 
it would have been difficult not to believe 
that the purposes of God could be achieved 
through the model that Constantine was 
attempting to establish. 
 
As a consequence, however, the church 
moved into a more settled, established, and 
organized model. No longer a missional 
band of God's people, it became a religious 
organization. 
 
Believers stopped realizing the great 
commission was their mission, they went off 
active duty and forgot their duty altogether. 
What a brilliant move of Satan! 
 
In a move that must have seemed like an 
answer to prayer to the believers who had 

been persecuted, tortured and killed for their 
faith, now they have relief, rest and apathy. 
 
Think about it! The role of the clergy 
merged the gifts of the whole body into one 
person. Everyone else in the church would 
merely be an ordinary believer who held 
secular jobs where they specialize in non-
spiritual activities such as: 
• Bankers… 
• Lawyers… 
• Doctors… 
• Teachers… 
• Carpenters… 
• Factory workers… 
 
So, in effect, what ought to be accomplished 
in an ordinary, decentralized, non-
professional manner by all church members 
working together, would instead be 

accomplished by a single, full-time 
professional - The Pastor. 
 
Doesn’t it stand to reason that since the 
pastor is paid to be the specialist in church 
operations and management, that the laity 
assumes a passive role in the church? 
 
Rather than contributing their part to edify 
the church, the laity would go to church as 
passive receivers to be edified. Rather than 
actively spending the time and energy to 
exercise their gift for the good of the body, 
they sit back and let the pastor run the show. 
(As long as he runs the show the way they 
think he should. Because, even though they 
don’t know how to do his job, they know 
how he should do his job.) 

You and I have inherited that mindset 
because that’s the church we came from: 
First the Roman Catholic Church, then the 
Anglican Church, then the Methodist 
Church, then the Pentecostal Church. We’ve 
inherited our methods of leading and 
governing the church from those ideas. 
 
This is a crippled ekklesia! Our system has 
gutted the army of God and left it without 
anything other than captains and generals.

do His work in them. If we break apart, 
people will be lost. 
 
Many people have left the church because of 
all of this breaking apart - because of 

fractures in relationships, because of 
conflict, and because people have not 
worked to resolve their issues one with 
another. 
 
As Second Mile Leaders we are not in the 
business of being right, we are in the 
business making people right with God. 
 
Sometimes that means you and I are going 
to have a little friction on our lives. There 
will be a little tension at the joints from time 
to time. We're not always going to be able to 
get to be right. As leaders we will no doubt 
have to surrender our rights as we climb the 
leadership ladder. Second Mile Leaders 
know that the higher they go on the ladder 
of leadership the fewer rights we have. 

If the surrendering of rights isn’t alright with 
you, then maybe you’re not ready for 
leadership. As leaders, we can't afford to be 
offended or offensive. We have to be people 
that can connect one to another so that God 
can do what he wants to do through us. We 
have to connect with people we like and 
people we may struggle to like. So how do 
we make sure that we fit together and do our 
job well? 
 
There are 4 C’s that create great leaders and 
great teams. A deficiency or a lack of one of 
these will surface soon or later if you don't 
have it. And if you don't have it, it will 
cause a structural failure in your leadership 
and those you are connected to. Four C’s of 
leadership: 
• Character… Character is your internal 
quality. Without character you will 

break, and in that breaking you will 
break others. People with character are 
reliable - they can be depended on. You 
can’t be excited about a new initiative 
and then when it gets harder before it 
gets easier, you decide to bail on the 
process. If this is you, you have already 
capped your ability to lead. You are not 
a person of character. Why? No one can 
really rely on you. You are like a limb 
that looks trustworthy, but when you 
start to put some weight on it - crack! 
And that’s the sound of your bones 
hitting the ground. This is what happens 
to the people in your life if you can’t 
follow through. A person of character is 
trustworthy. Can people count on you 
too not only be there for them, but to 
cover them? Do you celebrate exposing 
the weaknesses of others? If someone 

tells you something in confidence, can 
they count on you to keep it to yourself 
or are you going to tell others? Can 
people share their burdens with you and 
know that you were not going to turn 
around and stab them in the back and 
betray their confidence to someone else? 
Character means you are a person of 
integrity. Are you who you say you are? 
Or are you a hypocrite? Is there a 
discrepancy between what you say you 
are and what you do? Hypocrisy will 
take you out of leadership. The 
difference in failure and hypocrisy - 
Failure is acknowledged - Hypocrisy 
ignores or covers failure and pretends it 
doesn’t exist. That’s called being phony. 
And people run from phony! There is no 
place at the Second Mile table for a 
phony! Character means you are a 

quality person. Do you have a deep 
reliance on your relationship with God 
that guides your decisions and actions? 
Can you be trusted to do what is in the 
best interest of God, others, and yourself 
in that order? Or are you in this just for 
you? Your gifting will only take you as 
far as your character can sustain you. 
Character is the first and foremost 
qualification of a Second Mile Leader. If 
you are not a person of character, you 
simply are not able to lead. As a leader, 
if we are not a person of character, 
people will figure that out and eventually 
disconnect from us. 
• Competence… Competence is your 
ability through your knowledge and 
skill. This is the question of ability, not 
of trustworthiness. Are you capable of 
doing what you’re supposed to do? If 

not, are you capable of learning? Do you 
have the drive to get better at what 
you’re doing? Are you studying what 
you want to be doing, finding ways to do 
it better? When you don’t know what to 
do are you willing to ask? Do you have a 
mentor? A person can be extremely 
competent to do one thing and 
completely incompetent in others. Some 
people couldn’t organize their way out 
of a paper sack, but they’re terrific at 
connecting with people. Are you 
working to get better? Leaders are 
lifetime learners. Are you studying what 
you need to know to lead more 
effectively? In the church, people want 
to follow leaders. They do this 
voluntarily. Many times incompetent 
leaders in the church do not get fired. 
The leader of the church may not fire 

you but the people fire incompetence by 
leaving. As a leader, if you’re not 
competent at what you’re doing, others 
will eventually disconnect from you. (A 
leader who had made a huge mess once 
asked me – “Are you going to fire me?” 
Even though I loved the person dearly 
my response was, “If you knew to do this 
and didn’t you are negligent! If you did 
not know to do this, you are 
incompetent. Either way I have to fire 
you.” Had I not fired this person I would 
have been just as negligent and 
incompetent as they were.) 
• Culture… The culture of an organization 
is its DNA and vision. When we first 
started this Second Mile Leadership 
class, some of you probably didn’t know 
some of the things I would present. I am 
here to attempt to raise the level of the 

leadership culture in this church. The 
culture cannot be adjusted unless and 
until it is fully implemented in the minds 
of the Second Mile Leaders. It may 
come as a surprise to you, but not 
everyone will be excited about the vision 
of the pastor. Some families will leave 
based on the culture the pastor seeks to 
establish. Second mile leaders don’t 
leave they learn and adjust. Do you have 
the DNA of the organization? We cannot 
have division and leadership. We cannot 
have two different directions for the 
church to go. You must serve under 
leadership just as the pastor serves under 
leadership. If you want to go another 
direction, then you cannot lead. If they 
have a different DNA or Culture, you 
have to submit or you cannot stick 
around. The DNA and culture are the 

systems, philosophies and beliefs of the 
organization. You can be a part of the 
weekend gathering without adhering to 
the Culture and DNA, but you cannot 
lead without adhering to them. 
• Chemistry… Chemistry is that intangible 
thing that helps you connect with others. 
Have you ever met people that just had a 
hard time connecting? Without 
chemistry, you can’t bond! You won’t 
be able to “gel.” You won’t be able to 
connect. You’ll be like a magnet turned 
the wrong way. If you don’t have 
chemistry, people just won’t like you. 
You may be a great person, but just 
missing that connection. Work on it. Ask 
others what they think, be humble, make 
corrections. 

As a Second Mile Leader it is essential that 
you have all four C’s. Having all four is 
important, because one without the other can 
prove to be a major lid on the life of a 
leader. 
 
Character + Competence + Culture without 
Chemistry = Lonely 
 
Character + Culture + Chemistry without 
Competence = Faulty 
 
Culture + Chemistry + Competence without 
Character = Deadly 
 
Chemistry + Competence + Character 
without Culture = Division

ATTITUDE 
 
One of the big goals of these sessions is to 
help us develop and step into our God given 
roles as leaders! I want to empower leaders 
to carry out the work of the ministry. 
 
Leading well begins with what we think and 
what we say. It starts with the “A” word – 
The attitude of the leaders. Ephesians 4:21-
23 (NLT) “Since you have heard about 
Jesus and have learned the truth that 
comes from him, 22 throw off your old 
sinful nature and your former way of life, 
which is corrupted by lust and deception. 
23 Instead, let the Spirit renew your 
thoughts and attitudes. 24 Put on your new 
nature, created to be like God—truly 
righteous and holy.” 

We often assume that leadership is all about 
skills and techniques. In reality, our 
leadership begins when we possess the right 
attitude about our circumstances and 
ourselves. 
 
William James, the father of modern 
psychology, wrote: “The greatest discovery 
of my generation is that humans can alter 
their lives by altering their attitude of 
mind.” 
 
Zig Ziglar said that “Your attitude, not your 
aptitude will determine your altitude!” In 
the last session, we learned that aptitude 
(Competence) is extremely important in the 
life of the leader but attitude is equally 
important. 

Here is why - Attitude (your thought-
feelings) determines action and actions 
determine your accomplishments. So, think 
about it! Whatever attitude you operate in 
pre-determines the outcome! 
 
Henry Ford said, “Whether a man thinks he 
can or he can’t, he’s right!” 
 
In so many situations, the battle is won or 
lost before the battle has begun. It all has to 
do with the frame of mind with which we 
enter the battle. Are we full of faith, hope 
and optimism? Or are we negative and 
doubtful of getting results? 
 
This principle was illustrated when Moses 
sent the twelve spies into the Promised Land 
in Numbers Chapter 13-14. Joshua and 
Caleb came back with a positive report. The 

other ten spies returned with a negative 
report. Their attitude prevailed, and that 
generation of people never got to enter the 
land: 
 
• Joshua and Caleb saw the possibilities 
the other ten spies saw the problems. 
• Joshua and Caleb saw themselves as 
strong through the Lord. The other ten 
spies saw themselves as small and weak. 
(Grasshoppers) 
• Joshua and Caleb were optimistic about 
the future. The other ten spies were 
pessimistic about the future. 
• Joshua and Caleb encouraged the people 
to step out in. The other ten spies caused 
the heart of the people to melt. 
 
In the days ahead, as you begin leading, 
you’re going to be faced with many days of 

feeling that you can’t or don’t want to. This 
attitude will sabotage your leadership before 
you ever get started. 
 
Conversely, a right attitude can make up for 
what you may lack in resources. Many 
leaders in the past have accomplished great 
things without any resources other than a 
positive attitude of faith. (The right attitude 
is your greatest resource) 
 
My attitude toward others is the greatest 
determining factor of their attitude toward 
the vision. 
 
Generally speaking, people are mirrors. 
They will reflect the attitude of their leader. 
We must initiate the attitude we want in 
return. Leaders must understand this to get 
results. 

• Belief begets belief… 
• Trust begets trust… 
• Hope begets hope… 
• Positivity begets positivity… 
 
If you’re negative, always saying how 
unfaithful people are, how bad people are, 
always finding fault with others, always 
speaking death over your ministry, I can 
guarantee people will not follow you as a 
leader. And the very attitudes you espouse 
will become truths. 
 
Try this: Walk into a room with joy and a 
smile even if you don’t feel it and watch 
people respond. Then, walk into a room 
grumpy and see what happens. 
 
Attitude can turn problems into blessings. 

We can begin dreaming or become 
discouraged. We can build or blame. We can 
take action or get angry. We can conquer or 
concede. Whether the problem becomes a 
blessing depends on us more than God. He 
desires to turn all things into blessings. 
 
Remember, prisoners, oppressed minorities, 
and those in captivity wrote much of the 
Bible. The writers rose above their 
circumstances. As Second Mile Leaders are 
we going to allow the circumstances to 
control our attitudes or are we going to rise 
above the circumstances to have a good 
positive attitude? Mark 9:23 (NIV) 
“Everything is possible to him that 
believes.”

Very few people are committed to a ministry

volunteers through announcements is 
fruitless – Recruitment is most effective 
through relationships. There are two reasons 
that people volunteer: 
• They believe in the leader… 
• They believe in the vision of the 
leader…

My attitude, not my achievements will

The thoughts in your mind are more

know what the problem with this is? When

SECOND MILE LEADERSHIP 

SESSION A 
MAKING DISCIPLES 
 
Matthew 28:18-20 “Jesus came and told his 
disciples, “I have been given all authority 
in heaven and on earth. 19 Therefore, go 
and make disciples of all the nations, 
baptizing them in the name of the Father 
and the Son and the Holy Spirit. 20 Teach 
these new disciples to obey all the 
commands I have given you. And be sure 
of this: I am with you always, even to the 
end of the age.” 
 
Jesus didn’t say: 
• Preach… 
• Witness… 
• Follow up… 
• Make converts... 
• Go to church… 

Jesus said make disciples. The definition of 
discipleship is as follows: 
• Helping someone to progressively learn 
the Word of God… 
• Helping someone to become spiritually 
mature… 
• Helping someone to grow from a learner 
to a true Christ follower… 
 
 God gave me a definition of discipleship 
that changed my life and the way I look at 
being a leader: “You haven’t truly made a 
disciple until you have reached someone, 
trained them and they have reached 
someone and are in the process of training 
them. Then and only then is the process of 
discipleship complete.” 
 
In order to make disciples we are going to 
have to get out of our rows and into circles. 

Disciple making is best done face-to-face 
and life-on-life over a long period of time. 
 
It’s not until we really get to know someone 
and experience their struggle that we can 
really speak to the real issues of their lives. 
People come forward for prayer on the 
weekend asking that we pray for their father, 
spouse, or whatever but what they don’t say 
is: 
• I’m bound by porn... 
• I’m cheating on my spouse… 
• I’m abusing my children… 
• I’m living in my car... 
 
We don’t find those things out until we get 
into relationship with them. These 
relationships have to be life-on-life and 
long-term, deep interpersonal relationships. 
They are built on trust and grow over time. 

Disciple making is all about people building. 
An old Chinese proverb says: “If you want 
one year of prosperity, grow grain. If you 
want ten years of prosperity, grow trees. If 
you want one hundred years of prosperity, 
grow people.” 
 
If you’re going to be a Second Mile Leader, 
it is going to require being in relationships 
with people for a long time. This is not a 
short-term assignment. Second Mile Leaders 
are not a flash in the pan they are in this for 
the long haul. 
 
Here’s the discipleship formula: 
• Connect… 
• Serve… 
• Influence... 

To disciple people, we must connect with 
people. We connect with people by 
demonstrating genuine concern and care - 
about them, not you “People don’t care how 
much you know until they know how much 
you care.” 
 
What is the best way to connect with 
people? 
• Communicate with openness and 
sincerity – BE REAL! People can smell 
a phony a mile away. 
• Know our audience – When we work 
with individuals, knowing our audience 
means learning people’s names, finding 
out their histories, asking about their 
dreams. 
• Speak about what they care about, not 
what we care about - Learn to be an 
active listener! God gave us two ears and 

one mouth, maybe we should listen 
twice as much as we talk! 
• Practice what we preach - Live our 
message. People can spot a phony a mile 
away. 
• Go where they are – Remove as many 
barriers to communication as possible. 
Try to be attuned to their culture, 
background, education, and so on. 
• Adapt to them - Don’t expect them to 
adapt to you. 
• Sit where they sit. Understand things 
from their perspective. 
• Focus on them – We must get the focus 
of ourselves and get it on them. 
• Make eye contact - Listen to what they 
are saying rather than focusing on what 
you are going to say next. 

• Learn their names and their story - Stop 
talking about us and let them tell us 
about themselves. 
• Be available to them – We must give 
them our phone numbers - Answer the 
phone - Call them and pray for them. We 
can’t be a discipler or a Second Mile 
Leader if we do not want to be 
inconvenienced. 
• Believe in Them – It’s one thing to 
communicate to people because you 
believe you have something of value to 
say. It’s another to communicate with 
people because you believe they have 
value. People’s opinions of us have less 
to do with what they see in us than with 
what we can help them see in 
themselves. 
• Give them hope – French general 
Napoleon Bonaparte said, “Leaders are 

dealers in hope.” When you give people 
hope, you give them a future. Jeremiah 
29:11 “I know the plans I have for 
you… They are good and not bad… 
Give you hope and a future.” 
 
In discipleship the discipler must always be 
the initiator. Second Mile Leaders don’t wait 
for people to connect with them, they make 
a hit list and connect with others. (Right 
now I want you to make a list of six people 
you have influence with that you are going 
to begin the process of discipleship with) 
 
Second Mile Leaders purposely connect 
with others with the intention of 
discipleship. We must take the first step with 
others and then make the effort to continue 
building relationships. 

Second Mile Leaders see themselves as 
servants more than they see themselves as 
leaders. When we serve others, we add value 
to them. 
 
Many people view leadership like success: 
• Go as far and as fast as you can… 
• Climb the ladder… (Step on the fingers 
of others if necessary) 
• Achieve the highest possible position 
regardless of the cost to yourself or 
others… 
 
Jesus said, “He who wants to be greatest 
must become servant to all.” Leadership 
isn’t how far we advance ourselves but how 
far we advance others. 
 
All relationships either add to or subtract 
from a person’s life. So, one critical 

question: Are you making things better for 
the people who follow you? 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Four guidelines for adding value to others: 
• Truly value others… Effective leaders 
go beyond not harming others they 
intentionally help others. The care for 
others must be tangible. (Taking time to 
talk, visit, build relationships…) When 
we walk past people we are walking past 
our work. 
• Make yourself more valuable to others… 
The more intentional we have been in 
growing personally, the more we have to 
offer our followers. If we have nothing 
of value to add, then we can’t add value. 
We can’t give what you don’t possess. 
Read a book, take a course, learn 
something to pass it on. Read a book on 
parenting or a struggle someone is facing 

so you can help them. For sure, stay in 
the word of God. 
• Know and relate to what others value… 
One can only do this by listening to 
others stories, hopes and dreams. 
Inexperienced leaders are quick to 
attempt to lead before knowing anything 
about the people they intend to lead. 
• Learn what is valuable to them and then 
lead based on what you’ve learned... 
This is manipulation if we don’t care, 
but serving if we do care. If you desire to 
add value by serving others, you will 
become a better leader. 
 
The true measure of leadership is influence. 
Do you have influence? Do people follow 
you and what you say and do? 

Several factors that drive a leader’s 
influence: 
• Character… True leadership always 
begins with the inner person. People can 
sense the depth of a person’s character. 
• Relationships… Leadership is relational 
leverage. If you don’t have relationships, 
you do not have the leverage to lead. 
• Knowledge… What They Know – 
information is vital. You need a grasp of 
the facts to develop an accurate vision 
for the future. 
• Experience… Do you know what you’re 
talking about? The greater challenges 
you have overcome, the more likely 
followers will be willing to let you lead. 
 
Here is a great leadership proverb: “He who 
thinks he leads, but has no followers, is only 
taking a walk.” If you can’t influence 

others, they won’t follow you. And if they 
won’t follow, you’re not a leader. That’s the 
Law of Influence. No matter what anybody 
else tells you, remember that leadership is 
influence — nothing more, nothing less. 
 
So discipleship is about influence. We get to 
influence by serving. We serve best after we 
connect and know what the needs are. 
 
To make a disciple, we must give our lives 
away. And in order for others to want what 
we are giving, we must have the right 
character, purpose and desire to influence 
others toward following Jesus.

THE SHEPHERDS HEART 
 
As Second Mile Leaders, God is calling us 
into a ministry that will require us to be 
motivated by a force that compels us out of 
our comfort zone. It will be necessary for us 
to have the energy and capacity to go 
beyond what we experienced in the past. 
 
Our natural tendency is to be selfish. That’s 
mine. That’s yours. (A baby thinks only of 
itself – It does not care when or who it 
inconveniences) 
 
I would be lying if I said to you that I 
always wanted to do what is in the best 
interest of others. More often than not, I 
want to do what is in the best interest of 
myself. 

If we are going to be Second Mile Leaders 
we must move beyond: 
• Self-interest… 
• Self-promotion… 
• Self-preservation… 
 
 As Second Mile Leaders we must move into 
a mode of operation that is so counter-
intuitive. We must learn to be motivated by 
something other than our own wisdom and 
greatness.

makes us do great things for God. It is what

Matthew 14:13-16 “But the crowds heard

THE LAW OF SOLID GROUND 
 
The BIG question here is, can you be 
trusted? More importantly, can the pastor 
trust you? 

There is actually a currency in relationships. 
It is the capital that keeps the relational 
economy flowing. Trust is the reliance on 
the integrity, strength, ability or surety of a 
person. 
 
I can say I trust this chair but I cannot 
demonstrate that trust without sitting in it. I 
can say I trust a person, but I cannot 
demonstrate that trust without putting 
myself in a place of reliance on that person. 
 
So, think of it this way, when you’ve had a 
chair break on you multiple times, you stop 
sitting in that kind of chair. When you and I 
hurt people enough, they stop relying on us. 
When we do not bear up under our 
responsibility our leaders stop calling on us 
because they cannot trust us. 

As a Second Mile Leader, you must be 
trustworthy. These are qualities that A 
Second Mile Leader must demonstrate to 
everyone they are serving: 
• Keep confidences… If people open up 
and share their hurts, their sins, their 
faults, will you push them away or will 
you embrace them while keeping their 
secret / confidence? When you commit 
to keeping a confidence but only tell one 
person, you have broken the trust. Once 
the trust is broken you are no longer a 
Second Mile Leader. When is it ok to 
share? When you are asking for help 
with a person in authority over you that 
you can trust to keep the same 
confidence. If you share with someone 
else in the church, you have broken trust. 
One must do this with relationships or 

we will find ourselves out of leadership 
(influence). 
• Demonstrate genuine care…We talked 
about this last week. Are you concerned 
enough about the person and their 
spiritual well being to stop talking and 
start listening? If you care, answer your 
phone. If you care pray for them like you 
said you would. If you care call them 
when they are missing in action? If you 
care go visit them when they are in the 
hospital. If you care make their needs a 
priority. If you care follow through. 
Second Mile Leaders ALWAYS do what 
they say they are going to do. Don’t 
create an expectation of a meal, an 
appointment, prayer, follow up, phone 
call, etc and then fail to do it. If we drop 
the ball it communicates that we don’t 

care. It may not be true, but that’s the 
feeling a missed opportunity creates. 
 
As a Second Mile Leader can I trust you as a 
partner in ministry? When the Apostles laid 
their hands on the first Deacons they were 
saying “These men are an extension of us.” 
And that is what I am saying to you. Can I 
trust you to be an extension of Susan and 
me? 
 
By making you a Second Mile Leader we 
are putting our credibility behind you, 
saying to people - these are trustworthy 
individuals that will pastor you like I pastor 
you. So my question to you is, can we trust 
you? 
• Can we trust you not to start your own 
ministry? (To be under authority – The 

Centurion “I am a man under authority” 
- Campus Pastors agreement) 
• Can we trust you with people who have 
a grievance with me or CT? ( My 
experience with a man who called me to 
complain about my successor in 
Arkansas - Silence is consent) 
• Can we trust you to help build the 
Kingdom and not to ever do harm to it? 
(Some people can build but they 
eventually do more harm than good) 
• Can we trust you to make decisions 
based on what you believe the pastor 
wants rather than how you think it 
should be? (My experience serving Bro. 
Granberry) 
 
I the person who is ultimately responsible, 
not you. If you follow my lead, I take the 
heat. If you don’t follow my lead, you are 

wrong even if you’re right and you take the 
hit. 
 
As a Second Mile Leader when dealing with 
grievances and frustrations, do not: 
• Share with a peer… 
• Share with someone you lead… 
• Share with congregation… 
 
This is poison and Jesus had strong words 
for such actions – “It would be better for 
you to have a millstone tied around your 
neck and cast into the sea than to offend 
one of these little ones.” Sometimes one 
comment is all it takes. 
 
As a Second Mile Leader when dealing with 
grievances and frustrations, do the 
following: 
• Ask questions for clarification… 

• Offer improvements in the decision… 
• Praise publicly / challenge privately… 
• Be kind but candid… (I welcome 
feedback - You must welcome it as well) 
 
Candid is uncomfortable but necessary 
Why? Candor is important because it takes 
all the “nice” out and cuts to the chase. It 
allows us the ability to improve. 
 
A lack of candor allows for distrust to grow. 
Why? When a decision doesn’t sit well with 
us, we question it. If we don’t ask for 
clarification, we start questioning motives. If 
you don’t come to request clarification from 
me, you start wondering about my motives. 
Can you trust me? 
 
If I don’t come to you, then I start 
wondering if I can trust you to lead. 

The reason we don’t have the conversation 
is because it’s uncomfortable. Sometimes 
we think it’s too small a matter to bring up. 
However, without the conversation the air 
isn’t clear and ultimately, offense and 
strongholds develop. If we give the devil a 
foothold he will climb into our minds. 
 
People leave the church because they are 
unwilling to address conflict. Conflict 
avoidance ultimately becomes person 
avoidance. 
 
This means, as a leader, I need you to be 
able to receive correction and constructive 
criticism. I open myself to you to explain 
myself whenever you need an explanation. 
In this relationship you must remember that 
I have information that you do not have and 

I may not be able to share it with you. In 
such a case you have to trust that I have the 
best interest of everyone at heart.

LEADING WITH AN OPEN HEART 
 
What you’re about to be asked to do will 
undoubtedly produce wounds in you. 
 
We want to reach everyone in our 
communities that no one else is reaching. 
I’m not opposed to people coming from 
other churches, but it is absolutely nowhere 
near my radar. I want the rejected, the 

unloved, the unwanted and the forgotten. 
That’s who I want us to go after. 
 
You know what all those people have in 
common? Hurt! Emotional pain. Do you 
know what those people are capable of 
causing in us? Hurt! Emotional pain. 
 
Hurt people hurt people. I’m asking you to 
get on the front lines with me. And you’re 
likely to suffer if you do. 
 
A Second Mile Leader is much like an 
Emergency Medical Technician (EMT): 
• An EMT is a person who runs toward 
situations that everyone else is running 
from… When everyone else leaves and 
abandons the injured, an EMT must find 
a way to get to them while doing their 
best not to become a patient themselves. 

This is exactly what a Second Mile 
Leader does. 
• An EMT wears personal protective 
equipment… PPE is first and foremost. 
What is the point in saving others while 
creating casualties? Second Mile 
Leaders protect themselves with prayer, 
the Word, the armor of God and close 
personal relationships with other Second 
Mile Leaders. (Iron sharpens iron) 
• An EMT always asks “Is the scene safe” 
before proceeding… Second Mile 
Leaders don’t become impatient but 
move forward deliberately. We don’t 
want to die in the process of saving 
others. So we must use precautions. “If 
you see your brother overtaken in a 
fault, ye which are spiritual restore him 
considering yourself, lest ye also be 
tempted.” 

The spiritual scene won’t ever be completely 
safe. But, we can protect ourselves by 
forgiving those who do us wrong. Matthew 
18:21-23 “Then Peter came to Jesus and 
asked, "Lord, how many times shall I 
forgive my brother when he sins against 
me? Up to seven times?" 22 Jesus 
answered, "I tell you, not seven times, but 
seventy-seven times.” 
 
Forgiveness plays one of the highest roles in 
the Kingdom. Jesus makes it clear that we 
are the ones forgiven much from the master, 
so we had better forgive the small debts 
against us. We have to be able to “let it go.” 
The only person that is being bothered, I 
assure you, is YOU. 

Do we want to win the argument or win the 
person? 
 
Don’t return evil for evil. Don’t out people 
on social media. Don’t run them down to 
others. (Friend of mine recently won a $2.1 
million dollar liable lawsuit – First ever tried 
based on social media) 
 
The number one reason people leave a 
church is unresolved conflict. Our tendency 
is to ignore (Sideswipe) conflict hoping it 
will just go away. We sweep it under the 
rug, but it grows inside us as a cancer and 
ultimately will take us out of the 
relationship. 
 
Second Mile Leaders confront 
disagreements and conflict with an open 
heart. We handle situations as if we are 

dealing with our own mother. (My mom’s 
comments about retiring) 
 
Allow people to have a path back while 
maintaining their dignity. If you don’t allow 
people to ask forgiveness and return to you 
in relationship, you will not be able to lead. 
(Pastor who wanted to dismiss a deacon 
despite the fact the man had already 
resigned) 
 
The goal of a Second Mile Leader is to 
restore people rather than have to be right. 
Sometimes we can’t be right and restore at 
the same time. , 
 
As a leader we must always give the benefit 
of the doubt. 

We tend to judge ourselves by our motives, 
but others by their actions. We don’t like to 
give people the benefit of the doubt. People 
say things that are mean. And, even though 
they are characterized by being kind, we 
throw them away over one act. We don’t 
chalk it up to a bad day or a 
misunderstanding we judge them to have an 
ulterior motive. 
 
In the American court system, we are 
innocent until proven guilty. In the church, 
we are guilty until proven innocent. Second 
Mile Leaders always give others the benefit 
of the doubt. 
 
Second Mile Leaders trade their rights for 
their responsibilities. Romans 14:20-21 
“Don’t tear apart the work of God over 
what you eat. Remember, all foods are 

acceptable, but it is wrong to eat something 
if it makes another person stumble. 21 It is 
better not to eat meat or drink wine or do 
anything else if it might cause another 
believer to stumble.” 1 John 2:9-10 
“Anyone who claims to be in the light but 
hates his brother is still in the darkness. 10 
Whoever loves his brother lives in the light, 
and there is nothing in him to make him 
stumble. 11 But whoever hates his brother 
is in the darkness and walks around in the 
darkness; he does not know where he is 
going, because the darkness has blinded 
him.” 
 
Second Mile Leaders: 
• Walk in forgiveness... 
• Live with an open heart... 
• Give the benefit of the doubt... 

• Trade their rights for their 
responsibilities...

about three critical elements that are 
absolutely necessary to be a Second Mile 
Leader: 
• Faithful… 
• Available… 
• Teachable… 
 
If we honestly evaluated ourselves could we 
truly conclude that we are faithful, available 
and teachable? If we have pause in any of 
these areas we must move quickly to fix 
what is lacking, if we intend to be a Second 
Mile Leader.

qualities and do not have enough integrity to 
step up, or step out of the position they have 
been asked to fulfill. When this happens, 
they place the supervisor in the dubious 
position of having to call the individual into 
question or set them down. Oftentimes the 
person who has not fulfilled their 
responsibilities becomes offended and plays 
the part of a martyr. When this occurs they 
are proving the leader was right to make the 
decision to pull the leadership plug on the

• When a Second Mile Leader comes into

While men use methods, God's methods are 
men and women. 
 
Lets look at these in depth. 
 
Are you faithful? 

In 2 Timothy 2:2, Paul admonished 
Timothy, "The things that you have heard 
from me among many witnesses, commit 
these to faithful men who will be able to 
teach others also." Notice, Paul doesn’t tell 
Timothy to commit his teachings to talented 
men, or to influential men, or to wealthy 
men. Paul doesn’t say – “Hey Timothy - find 
the man who can really wow people. 
Timothy - find the people who are connected 
in the city - The movers and shakers. Hey 
Timothy - find the people who give more 
than anyone else in your church - that’s who 
you need to entrust this to.” 
 
Nope! He said “Invest into faithful people. 
Invest in people that are reliable. Invest in 
people who commit and follow through. 
Invest in people of character and integrity”. 

The word faithful means: 
• Strict or thorough in the performance of 
duty, not halfway. 
• True to one's word, promises, vows, 
etc… 
• Steady in allegiance or affection; loyal; 
constant... 
• Reliable, trusted, or to be believed... 
 
1 Samuel 2:35 (NLT) "Then I will raise up 
a faithful priest who will serve me and do 
what I desire. I will establish his family, 
and they will be priests to my anointed 
kings forever.” 
 
God said the kind of priest he wanted would 
be: 
• Faithful… What was the key to this 
priest being anointed forever? 
Faithfulness! Will you be Faithful to the 

ministry with which you are about to be 
entrusted? Will you own it as though it is 
your own? Will you make sure it is taken 
care of if you’re out of town? Will you 
do it even when you don’t feel like it, or 
you want to move to another ministry? 
Will you stay at your post of duty until 
you have raised up your own 
replacement? Will you raise up a faithful 
replacement? 
• Established… Established means to be 
made permanent. Neither God nor I are 
looking for flashes in a pan! People who 
come to leadership and make a BIG 
splash and then quickly fade are worse 
for the organization than not having a 
leader to begin with! Second Mile 
Leaders are established leaders. 
• Generational… Notice, the Scripture 
mentions that faithful leaders establish a 

family line of anointed leaders! I believe 
in generational anointing. Susan and I 
have been blessed with faithful parents – 
We have had the opportunity to do what 
our parents could never have dreamed 
possible! Our children will no doubt go 
far past what we will be able to do! Our 
grandchildren will do more than our 
children! The faithfulness or 
unfaithfulness we demonstrate will 
establish a generational anointing of 
success or a generational pattern of 
failure! 
 
1 Corinthians 4:2 (NLT) “Now, a person 
who is put in charge as a manager must be 
faithful.” 
 
A Second Mile Leader MUST be faithful. 

Available… 
1 Samuel 3:10 (NLT) “And the LORD 
came and called as before, "Samuel!

your servant is listening." Isaiah 6:8 (NLT) 
“Then I heard the Lord asking, "Whom 
should I send as a messenger to this 
people? Who will go for us?" I said, "Here

No matter how gifted they are little can be 
done with someone who is already working 
eighteen hours a day and can't even find 
time for their family, or for the Lord. If a 
person is unwilling or unable to alter their 
priorities or adjust their schedule; they 
cannot be a Second Mile Leader. 
 
In order to lead in God’s Kingdom, you and 
I must make the things of God a priority in 

our lives. We have to adjust our schedule. 
We have to give up some leisure. (Maybe 
some trips - Maybe some hunting - Maybe 
some “me” time) 
 
It is very disheartening when the leader can 
clearly see great Kingdom potential in 
certain people, yet it becomes impossible to 
get them to commit. They say they’re 
good… They say they are going to be there 
to help you… They say they are going to 
follow through… But when push comes to 
shove, they just aren’t there! 
 
Second Mile Leaders put themselves out 
there for whatever, wherever, whenever. 
 
There are many people who have the talent 
and they have the character, but they simply 
won’t make themselves available. Gifted 

people who are not available are 
inconsequential to the Kingdom of God! (I 
have many examples of this over the years – 
A family member who had more gifts than 
almost anyone I know but their lack of 
availability caused him to waste his life - 
Abraham dragging donkeys up the hill) 
 
One of the most important qualities of a 
Second Mile Leader is to be available and 
show up. 
 
If you want to be a Second Mile Leader just 
show up, and keep showing up! Why? I’m 
not willing to invest training and time in a 
person who can’t prove they are faithful and 
available. All too often I have experienced 
the pain of training someone to do some 
complex task and then they don’t show up! I 
have learned to invest in people who are 

available and faithful to show up to perform 
menial tasks! 
 
Sometimes people wonder why it seems that 
individuals who are less qualified get to lead 
in a certain area. Why do they get the 
microphone? Why do they seem to be the 
right hand of the leader? Why do they 
always seem to land in the honey hole? Why 
do they have the ear of the leader? Probably 
because they are available and faithful! 
 
Leaders prefer availability and faithfulness 
over gifts and talent! 
 
Some people say, “Make a spot for me and 
I’ll show up!” I say, “Show up, and your 
availability and faithfulness will make a spot 
for you!” 

Leaders would rather train and correct a 
faithful and available person than to try to 
find a way to use a super-talented one! 
Why? Because leaders know that superstars 
are normally in it for themselves, not for the 
vision. 
 
In the beginning, high performers may have 
been the least qualified person to lead or 
talk. But they kept showing up and before 
you know it, they earned the trust and 
respect of the leader, and acquired the skills 
necessary to do the job effectively. 
 
If you are unavailable the organization will 
move past you and you will find yourself on 
the outside looking in. People move on 
without you. Your leadership fades and 
erodes and you find yourself without a place 
to lead. 

Make yourself available. 
 
Teachable… 
 
Leaders and followers must be teachable. It 
should be understood that we don’t know all 
we need to know. We are learning. We are 
lifetime learners. 
 
The truth is, we can learn something from 
anyone and everyone. Any man or woman 
can be your teacher so long as you have a 
humble heart. 
 
God used the disciples not only because they 
were faithful and available, but also because 
they were teachable. 

Mark Twain once said, "Never undertake to 
instruct a pig to sing. You'll only frustrate 
yourself and annoy the pig." 
 
Second Mile Leaders avoid the pitfall of 
becoming a know-it-all. 
 
Know-it-alls: 
• Do not see the need to be taught… 
• They always have an excuse for not 
showing up to be trained… 
• They believe they are always the 
smartest person in the room… 
• They are annoying to everyone else and 
are the only one who don’t know it… 
 
Paul told Timothy that know-it-alls are, 
“Ever learning, and never able to come to 
the knowledge of the truth.” 

If we think we are going to be able to lead 
others, we must be willing to receive 
instruction! Hebrews 5:12 (NKJV) “ For 
though by this time you ought to be 
teachers, you need someone to teach you 
again the first principles of the oracles of 
God; and you have come to need milk and 
not solid food.” 
 
The teachable test includes: 
• Accepting correction willingly… 
• Seeking feedback from various 
sources… 
• Seeing correction as an opportunity 
rather than a threat… 
• Constantly looking for better methods to 
do things… 
• Being a lifetime learner… 

If we are not teachable then we are doomed 
to stay stuck exactly where we are. God and 
I love you too much to leave you like you 
are. 
 
A Second Mile Leader knows what they 
don’t know. 
 
The more I learn, the more I realize I have to 
learn. I have a vision, but God has given me 
a lot of wisdom from people that are buying 
into the vision. Each person on the team has 
some insight that each of us can learn and 
benefit from. 
 
As a leader I must guard against being 
threatened by the wisdom of others, rather 
looking at it as an opportunity to grow and 
become more effective. 

Let me ask you, do you make it difficult for 
your leader to correct you? Hebrews 13:17 
(NLT) “Obey your spiritual leaders, and do 
what they say. Their work is to watch over 
your souls, and they are accountable to 
God. Give them reason to do this with joy 
and not with sorrow. That would certainly 
not be for your benefit.” 
 
Second Mile Leaders give permission to 
those who are leading them to say: 
• What they want to… 
• When they want to… 
• How they want to… 
 
… and we give up the right to be offended. 
 
Being teachable means we have a spirit of 
humility and a spirit that is eager to grow. 

Matthew 23:12 “For whoever exalts 
himself will be humbled, and whoever 
humbles himself will be exalted."

QUALIFICATIONS 
 
In this final lesson we are going to talk 
about the qualifications and expectations of 
a Second Mile Leader. 
 
Tonight those of us who are going to be a 
Second Mile Leader will enter into a 
covenant with this Church and with one 
another. The Second Mile Leadership 
Covenant will be one of character and 
service: 1 Timothy 4:12 “Set an example 
for the believers in speech, in conduct, in 
love, in faith and in purity.” 

SPEECH 
Second Mile Leaders only speak words that 
build others up. As a leader we refrain from 
speaking negatively over others and their 
lives. We also refuse to speak negative 
words about this church, its leaders, or our 
fellow Second Mile Leaders. If we have 
issues with things that happen in the course 
of relationships or with the operation of 
ministries, we agree to bring those matters to 
the leaders privately, requesting assistance 
in providing remedy for the situation. 
 
As a Second Mile Leader I agree to conduct 
my life so as not to be a stumbling block for 
anyone through the words I say or the 
actions I take. I agree to: 
• Bridle my tongue… 
• Refrain from the use of alcohol… 

• Refrain from the use of illegal drugs… 
• Refrain from the use of tobacco… 
 
CONDUCT 
As a leader in this body: 
• I commit to behave in such a manner as 
to influence people toward Christ, not 
away from Him or His church... 
• I will seek to live a life that is worthy of 
imitation... 
• I will conduct myself in such a way, that 
if people follow in my footsteps, they 
will be led straight to Jesus… 
 
Other than unplanned true emergencies, or 
when I am required to work on my first job, 
I will seek to actively be engaged in serving 
and leading the ministry activities of the 
church. 

A Second Mile Leader knows that no area of 
service is beneath them. We will serve 
wherever, whenever and do whatever is 
needed in order to provide an environment 
where others can experience the love and 
power of Jesus Christ. 
 
Second Mile Leaders serve with: 
• Biblical integrity… (A good name is to 
be desired above great riches) 
• Ridiculous stewardship… (Be a tither – 
We don’t walk with thieves) 
• Absolute dependability… (No excuses – 
Be where you are supposed to be when 
you are supposed to be there – Be 
prepared – Being on time is being late) 
• Selfless teamwork… (Be mindful of the 
rest of the team – Take the highest road) 
• Extreme excellence… (Treat your 
ministry as if it is the most important 

thing you are ever going to do – Because 
it is – Laying up treasure where moth 
and rust don’t corrupt and where thieves 
do not break through and steal) 
• Radical respect… (Always prefer others 
ahead of yourself) 
 
LOVE 
Second Mile Leaders commit to exhibiting 
the characteristics of 1 Corinthians 13 
through our lives and ministries: How do we 
to know whether we are walking in love? 
• Love is patient with people… 
• Love is kind and good… (Showing and 
showering favors) 
• Love does not allow for envy or 
jealousy… 
• Love does not brag or seek recognition 
from others… 
• Love does not feel or act better than 
others… 

• Love is not rude… 
• Love is not touchy or quick tempered… 
• Love does not keep record of wrongs 
done… 
• Love allows for no gloating over the 
wrongdoing or misfortune of others… 
• Love is satisfied only with truth… 
• Love is sturdy under heavy 
responsibility and is willing to cover for 
weaker Christians… 
• Love chooses to trust… 
• Love believes the best in all situations… 
• Love never loses hope… 
• Love remains faithful in spite of 
repeated attacks… 
• Love never quits… 
• Love is perfect and complete… 
• Love displays maturity rather than 
immaturity… 
• Love handles others as if it were 
handling God… 

Second Mile Leaders do not take the high 
road when offended: We take the highest 
road. 
 
There will be times when we must lovingly 
confront others about their attitudes and 
behaviors. When this is necessary we are to 
do so from a motivation that seeks their best 
interest and strengthening their relationship 
with God. When this becomes necessary, we 
must do this in a manner that deals with the 
situation with as much privacy and dignity 
as possible. This will have the propensity to 
draw them toward, not push them away from 
Christ. 
 
FAITH 
Second Mile Leaders commit to being a 
person who is in the word and prayer daily. 
“Faith comes by hearing… Hearing from 

the Word of God.” If you do not have a set 
time to pray and read your Word every day 
– You don’t have a prayer life. If you don’t 
have a prayer life you can’t be a Second 
Mile Leader. You can’t give what you don’t 
have. 
 
Second Mile Leaders know that it is only out 
of an overflow of the presence of God in our 
lives that we are able be of any assistance to 
those who need us. 
 
We realize that people will need to lean on 
us as a source of strength and hope and it is 
imperative that our faith is strong enough to 
support them and us. We must make sure 
our relationship with God is fresh so we can 
lift the faith of others. 
 
PURITY 

As people who cast a shadow of influence 
over those we lead, Second Mile Leaders 
realize that our personal conduct and 
spiritual condition bears influence over 
those we lead. Because of that we must 
commit to keep our minds, eyes, and body 
pure from this world. According to the book 
of James, we must walk in an accountable 
relationship with God and others. All 
Second Mile Leaders must have an 
accountability partner. James 5:16 (LB) 
“Admit your faults to one another and pray 
for each other so that you may be healed. 
The earnest prayer of a righteous man has 
great power and wonderful results.” 
Those who want to be effective leaders 
maintain an accountable relationship with a 
peer. We also willingly allow our leaders to 
check with our accountability partners to 
assure we have been open, honest and 

transparent with them regarding issues in 
our lives. 
 
SHEPHERDING DUTIES 
As a Second Mile Leader you are needed 
each week to connect with the people in 
your section and to assist in creating a 
positive experience for guests, from the 
parking lot, to connecting them to a small 
group, and then getting them engaged in 
serving. 
 
Second Mile Leaders do not become 
offended when: 
• The parking lot fills up we are asked to 
move our cars… 
• We are asked to show up early or stay 
late and clean toilets and stack chairs… 
• The room fills up, and we are asked to 
give up our seats... 

• We move into multiple services and are 
asked ask to attend a new service at a 
time we don’t prefer… 
• We are asked to lead the way making a 
financial sacrifice… 
 
We may be asking you to serve: 
• On the security team… 
• On the parking lot… 
• As an usher or greeter… 
• As a nursery or children’s worker… 
• As a volunteer with the youth group… 
 
As a Second Mile Leader we do not have the 
luxury of saying no to serve where we are 
needed, or to cop a bad attitude because we 
don’t want to serve. 
 
TEAM LEADERSHIP 

Each Second Mile Leader is expected to 
either take a lead role, or an apprentice role 
with a leader of an area of ministry. 
 
We should always be staffing for growth. In 
order to double in size, we need twice the 
leaders and volunteers that we currently 
have. You should always be looking for 
your replacement in your ministry position. 
Second Mile Leaders always lead and 
mentor continuously and simultaneously. 
 
CORE BELIEFS 
In order to be a Second Mile Leader, you 
must adhere to, practice and speak the same 
doctrinal beliefs of this Church. 
 
GIVE 
You must tithe to be in leadership. We 
sincerely believe the tithe is set apart unto 

God and is accursed if we spend it on 
ourselves. To do so, is to bring a curse on 
the house just like Akin’s family. This will 
not be allowed for leaders. Your tithe must 
be clearly marked where it can be tracked. 
Anonymous giving will not work for 
leaders. 
 
ATTEND 
Each of you are expected to attend faithfully 
not regularly. We should be here early and 
plan to stay late following the services. Our 
duty is to serve and connect people and we 
cannot do this without showing up. 
 
SERVE 
Since you are now expected to be leaders, 
serving is mandatory. 

As a Second Mile Leader you will now be 
expected to serve at: 
• Church services… 
• Outreaches… 
• Training opportunities… 
 
Second Mile Leaders must always be aware 
of the fact that a church will never rise 
above its leadership! 
 
As a Second Mile Leader you can expect: 
• Difficulty... 
• Resistance... 
• Offenses... 
 
When you step into leadership you are 
engaging in serious spiritual warfare. Your 
position in the battle just changed from the 
mess hall to the front line. You are moving 

from a soldier in the army of God to a 
commander in the army of God. 
 
This is way more serious than any of us ever 
think. You and your family are about to 
become prime targets for the enemy. You 
will be blindsided and you will be tempted 
to forget why you are where you are and 
who your source is. This is a spiritual battle 
in which the leaders are the prime target of 
the enemy. 
 
Keep your guard up. Keep yourself in 
accountable in all of your relationships. 
Keep yourself in a place of constant prayer 
and prayer covering. 
 
There will be times when it will be difficult 
to keep going. You simply cannot quit. 

“There is no discharge in the army of 
God.” 
 
“A good soldier is willing to endure 
affliction.” 
 
“He who puts his hand to the plough and 
looks back is not fit for the Kingdom of 
God.” 
 
“If any man will come after me let him 
deny himself, take up his cross and follow 
me.” 
 
As Second Mile Leaders far too many 
people are counting on us for us to have the 
luxury of failure. You will build deep 
relationships with people - to remove 
yourself quickly from those relationships 
will be to destroy their very souls. 

God is not going to call you one day and 
then undo that decision the next day. Don’t 
take the easy way out. Take the difficult 
path through. 
 
Today I am asking you to make a one-year 
commitment to your position and leadership 
as a Second Mile Leader. 
 
If and when you decide to move from your 
position of leadership, it is your 
responsibility to find and train your own 
replacement. You can’t just walk away and 
leave an area uncovered. Your replacement 
must be able to do what you do better than 
you can do it. How do we accomplish the 
passing of the baton of responsibility? 
• I do the work and you watch me… 
• You help me do the work… 

• You do the work and I watch you… 
• You do the work and I hold you 
accountable…
