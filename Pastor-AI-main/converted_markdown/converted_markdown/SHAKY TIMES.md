# SHAKY TIMES

TEXT: Hebrews 12:25-28 (NKJV) "See that you do not refuse Him who speaks. For if they did not escape who refused Him who spoke on earth, much more shall we not escape if we turn away from Him who speaks from heaven, whose voice then shook the earth; but now He has promised, saying, "Yet once more I shake not only the earth, but also heaven." Now this, "Yet once more," indicates the removal of those things that are being shaken, as of things that are made, that the things which cannot be shaken may remain.\ Therefore, since we are receiving a kingdom which cannot be shaken, let us have grace, by which we may serve God acceptably with reverence and godly fear."

INTRO: I think all of us could agree, we are living in shaky times:

- The moral fabric of our society lies in ruin...

- Terrorist threaten the safety of the world...

- The world economy teeters on the brink of collapse...

- The religious establishment cannot decide what is right and wrong...

- Abortionists kill babies by the millions while those who know it is murder, sit idly by...

- The gay agenda is marching forward, while traditional marriage is being maligned...

Our culture and world is being shaken!

## I. WE NEED THE GRIT TO FACE THE FACTS OF A SHAKING WORLD...

There are people in this room today, whose lives have been shaken by one or more of the things I have just mentioned.

Take it from someone who has first-hand experience with things being shaken, "Tough times don't last but tough people do."

What I am calling grit is simply faith in that which cannot be shaken. Faith in God will give you:

The guts of a skydiver...

A head like a piece of granite

A backbone like an oak log...

The tenacity of a hungry lion...

Things are being shaken in our culture the question for the Christian is, what are you putting your faith in? Has your confidence been placed in things and people or is your faith rooted in the Lord? If your faith is founded upon the God of Heaven you will make it! R.W. Shambach made famous the statement, "You don't have any trouble, all you need is faith in God."

Remember this, "When the going get's tough, the tough get going."

It is no small thing for a person to be able to stand amidst the ruins of a home, a business, a family, or a dream and proclaim, "Even so, my faith has not been shaken!"

Toward the beginning of the second World War, the mayor of the Norwegian seaport of Narvik surveyed the disastrous results of the city that had been seized by the Germans, bombarded by the British, and pounded again and again by relentless bombing raids. Responding to reporters, the mayor looked at the wreckage around him and then looked at the distant snow capped mountain peaks that looked peacefully down upon the city and he said, "The mountain's are still ours!"
