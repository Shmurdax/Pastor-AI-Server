# AS YOUR DAYS ARE SO SHALL YOUR STRENGTH BE

TEXT: Deut: 33:24-29

For all of us, the word tomorrow normally brings with it two simultaneous emotions; an excitement about what a new day will bring forth; and a fear of what it might hold. None of us can see or predict the events of a day not to mention the long term future. If we could roll back the spiritual curtain and take a look down the long road of the future of our lives we would either be filled with anticipation or dread.

One thing we can be absolutely certain of is that our future will present new challenges. During the course of this series of sermons I want us to take a long serious look at some of the challenges we could possibly face; what our response will be; and what the outcome will be. The outcome will be predicated upon how we handle each challenge. If we face difficulty with panic the outcome will be reactionary responses which will cause the outcome to be all over the chart. If we face the difficulty with calm faith our difficulty will lead us one step closer to our destiny.

Even though all of our futures are shrouded in the unknown we certainly know who holds tomorrow. Because we know who holds tomorrow we can face the uncertainty of the future with calm, cool, and collected faith! (FAITH IS THE SUBSTANCE OF THINGS HOPED FOR...)

I want to call your attention to Deuteronomy 33:25. In this passage of Scripture Moses is blessing the twelve tribes of Israel before he makes his final trip to Mt. Nebo where he will meet God face to face and then step into eternity. When he begins to bless the tribe of Asher a portion of that blessing simply say's "As thy days are, so shall thy strength be."

This may not seem like much of a blessing but when you begin to think about it, he is saying that their strength would be adequate for the challenge of all their tomorrows. Some of us need to hear that today because we know we are facing some challenges this year. We should not move into the future with a sense of dread but we should face it with renewed vitality! Spiritual vitality! Determine to walk in His strength rather than our own strength! We are not on our own Jehovah is walking beside us!

The Scripture say's "If God is for us, who can be against us?" That scripture gave me heart burn for a number of years because it seemed to leave the door open as to whether or not God is for us. When I did a little research I discovered the little word "IF" means "forasmuch as"...

If we are saved by the blood of Jesus, God is on our side, and no matter what we face in the future, everything will be alright because Jehovah God is with us. We are to place no confidence in the flesh but we are to fully invest our confidence in God! (IN MY WEAKNESS HE IS MADE STRONG...)

We will not face even one of our tomorrows alone! When everyone else has forsaken us and fled, God will not leave us alone! Hebrews 13:5-6 (CEV) "God has said, "I will never fail you. I will never forsake you." 6 That is why we can say with confidence, "The Lord is my helper, so I will not be afraid. " The Psalmist said, Psalm 27:10 (CEV) "Even if my father and mother abandon me, the Lord will hold me close."

The promise to the tribe of Asher was, "As thy days are, so shall thy strength be." God's promise to us is exactly the same! (HE WILL BE A FRIEND THAT WILL STICK CLOSER THAN A BROTHER...)

We could say:

- Each day our strength will be sufficient for the challenges we face...

- Whatever demands a day brings, our strength will be more than equal to the task...

Think of this, no matter what we face in the future, God will give enough grace to meet each difficulty with enough stamina to overcome it or outlast it!

When we begin to look at the bigger picture we know that God will give us strength to deal with every physical difficulty which comes against us:

## I. HE IS JEHOVAH TSIDKENU... Jehovah Tsidkenu means "The Lord our righteousness." (ALL OUR RIGHTEOUSNESS IS AS FILTHY RAGS...) As we look toward the future, one of our goals should be to walk in right standing with God and man. Try as we may, everything the flesh attempts to do to be in right standing with God or man is a futile attempt at the impossible. I can face my tomorrows in confidence concerning my ability to be right before God and man because I am not going to trust in the ability of the flesh to make me right, I am going to walk in the righteousness Jesus purchased with His sinless life and substitutional death.

He will forgive every failure and mistake... He is Jehovah Tsidkenu, the Lord our righteousness...

## I. He will give us physical stamina to withstand the onslaught of sickness... He is Jehovah Rapha, the Lord our healer!

## II. He will provide the resources necessary to meet all our financial needs... He is Jehovah Jireh, the Lord our provider...

## III. He will bring resolution to every relational difficulty... He is Jehovah Shalom the Lord our peace...

## IV. When we do not have direction for the next step and we are at wits end to know what direction to take next, He will give divine guidance... He is Jehovah Nissi the Lord our banner...

The 365 days of 2006 will no doubt present many challenges. If we will look to the Lord as our source he will turn each challenge into an opportunity for ministry, deliverance and victory!

My challenge to you today is to seize the New Year by faith. Face all of your challenges with confidence and trust God to create opportunities for your light to shine!

"As your days are, so shall your strength be."
