## I. DESTINY DISCOVERY

Joseph was the son of Jacob and Rachael. For his parents he was the child who was born late in life and as a result, he no doubt received some special treatment which caused his siblings to develop a strong dislike for him.

Joseph began to dream dreams of exaltation. According to the Scripture, when Joseph shared his dreams with his brothers, "They hated him even more." Joseph believed in his destiny and dreamed of how it would come to pass. Orison Swett Marden said, "The Creator has not given you a longing to do what you have no ability to do."

If we dream about our destiny and share our dreams with others, oftentimes it will not go over well. We must be careful who we share our dreams with. Not everyone will be thrilled with our dreams. Jack Welch said, "Control your destiny or somebody else will."

Joseph's dreams came as a result of being in the presence of God. Dreams born in the presence of God always come to pass, but they are often slow in the developmental stage. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) I have some good advice for Godly dreamers: patience, patience, patience!

The discovery of his destiny caused many problems to surface in Joseph's life. His brothers conspired to kill him! His brother Reuben intervened and convinced the remainder of the brotherly mob to throw Joseph in a pit, thinking he would come back at a later time and raise Joseph up out of the pit. Before Reuben could retrieve him, some Midianite merchantmen found Joseph and sold him to the Ishmeelites for twenty pieces of silver. Mark Twain said, "Keep away from people who try to belittle your ambitions.  Small people always do that, but the really great, make you feel that you, too, can become great."   (LEAVE UP UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

The discovery of a destiny can often bring with it exhilarating highs and depressing lows. Through it all, the roller coaster ride of destiny discovery settles the issue in the heart of the individual. No matter whether we are on a high or fighting our way through a low place, if God gave us the dream we will ascend to the summit of our destiny and lay claim to the prize at the end of the race. The winning prize is to those who are faithful and persistent. Ecclesiastes 9:11 (NKJV) "The race is not to the swift, Nor the battle to the strong, Nor bread to the wise, Nor riches to men of understanding, Nor favor to men of skill; But time and chance happen to them all." Matthew 24:13 (NKJV) "He that shall endure unto the end, the same shall be saved."

We cannot afford to get our eyes on the circumstances or on the amount of time it is taking for our destiny to come to pass. We must place our confidence in the God who created the destiny and believe He is faithful to perform His Word. Hebrews 10:35-36 (NKJV) "Do not cast away your confidence, which has great reward. For you have need of endurance, so that after you have done the will of God, you may receive the promise."

## II. INTEGRITY DEVELOPMENT

By the age of seventeen or so, Joseph Joseph is thrown away by his brothers, left for dead, and twice sold.

This thing is not turning out like Joseph had imagined. I don't think I have ever talked to a successful person who has not had their fair share of pain and rejection.

Joseph settles into life as a slave serving his master Potiphar. As he serves Potiphar, God begins to bless Potiphar in unusual ways. The blessing of God upon Joseph's work catches the attention of Potiphar. Being the good businessman he is, Potiphar slowly begins to give Joseph extra responsibility as well as authority. Joseph garners more responsibility and authority in Potiphar's house than one would think a slave would ordinarily be able to acquire.

What we do with authority and responsibility will be a factor in whether or not we will be able to achieve our destiny. A little authority goes a long way with some people; others view authority as an opportunity to faithfully discharge responsibility. Authority allows for some people to become dictators; others allow authority to hone their leadership skills and actually serve in a more beneficial and effective way.

In the process of time, Potiphar gives Joseph such responsibility and authority that Joseph is made the Business Manager for Potiphar.

Joseph scored pretty high when confronted with difficulty, but how would he fare in the face of success? As with Joseph, we all face two very vulnerable moments in one's life: (PLACE ON SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- When we face trials

- When we achieve success

In the midst of the greatest level of success Joseph had known in his short life, he was about to be confronted with the greatest test to date. This test would be a test of his integrity.

Integrity and character are essential cornerstones which one should build any success upon. One cannot succeed over the long haul if this bedrock ingredient is missing from one's life.

Joseph was a young, virile man who was already fighting against the demands of testosterone when the wife of Potiphar begins an onslaught of seduction in relation to Joseph. She subjects him to this overwhelming attempt to get him to commit an immoral act with her day after day.

Joseph allows his integrity to be developed by continuing to reject the untoward advances of Mrs. Potiphar. Aristotle said, "Men acquire a particular quality by constantly acting in a particular way." A person does not become known as a person of integrity by making one good decision. Integrity often involves a lifetime of good choices. On the other hand, one can work a lifetime to build a reputation of character and integrity only to throw it all away with one bad decision. Tyron Edwards said, "Thoughts lead on to purposes; purposes go forth in action; actions form habits; habits decide character; and character fixes our destiny." (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

Joseph rejects the sexual advances of Mrs. Potiphar and even goes so far as to completely avoid being in her presence. Genesis 39:6-9 (CEV) "Joseph was well-built and handsome, and Potiphar's wife soon noticed him. She asked him to make love to her, but he refused and said, "My master isn't worried about anything in his house, because he has placed me in charge of everything he owns. No one in my master's house is more important than I am. The only thing he hasn't given me is you, and that's because you are his wife. I won't sin against God by doing such a terrible thing as this." She kept begging Joseph day after day, but he refused to do what she wanted or even to go near her." Joseph was determined to live by a certain code of ethics even when no-one was looking! Harry Truman said, "A person who is fundamentally honest doesn\'t need a code of ethics. The Ten Commandments and the Sermon on the Mount are all the ethical code anybody needs." (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

One day while Potiphar was away, this immoral woman attempts to force Joseph to commit adultery with her. When he rejects her advances, she rips his coat from him, lays it beside her on the bed, and cries rape! Joseph is trapped in a hopeless situation. Though he is the Business Manager for Potiphar, he is also a slave from another country. There would be no hearing, no trial, no jury, and no mercy. He is sentenced to an Egyptian prison.

Joseph had resisted temptation and it seemed his reward would be a lifetime confined to a prison in Egypt! The feelings Joseph experienced are not too different from ours when we seem to be getting it altogether and all of a sudden we experience an onslaught of reversals.

Think of this, in one terrible night Joseph went from Business Manager for one of the richest, most popular men in all of Egypt to being a prisoner, but he never surrendered his integrity or his dream. (ADD NEXT SLIDE TO THIS SLIDE THEN LEAVE BOTH ON THE SCREEN UNTIL END OF THE SERVICE)

## III. DEDICATION OF A SERVANTS HEART

Some would say Joseph had developed a servant's heart during his tenure in the house of Potiphar and I would agree the process probably began there. However; in Potiphar's house, though Joseph was a slave, he did have some liberties and was somewhat compensated.

In prison, Joseph had no liberties and was not compensated at all. This is where the real Joseph would come to light and be discovered. (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

The real question here is whether he could dedicate his heart to servant-hood when all things were against him. Could he be confined to prison and preserve a good attitude?

Just when he is beginning to feel that life may turn out as he had dreamed -- wham! His legs have been cut out from under him, once again. It must have seemed like one step forward and two steps backward.

Joseph probably thought of quitting, but his dream kept him from doing something which would dumb-down his destiny. Joseph is in a crucial time, would he be able to maintain his dream from prison?

Will we be able to maintain our dream when it seems all things are against us? This is such a critical time. What happens during this time frame will become the measuring stick of later success.

Listen to what the Bible has to say about Joseph's incarceration. Genesis 39:20 (NKJV) "Then Joseph's master took him and put him into the prison, a place where the king's prisoners were confined. And he was there in the prison." That's it. A couple of sentences. He was in prison, end of the story, right? Wrong! I am not sure how long Joseph was in prison before the warden recognized Joseph's gifts, but Joseph's attitude, work ethic, and integrity eventually attracted the attention of the warden. When we are in a difficult place, we should continue allowing the Holy Spirit to empower us. If we do this, eventually things will begin to turn for us. ## IV. DOING THE DREAM

- Joseph is now 30 years old, thirteen years have transpired between the time he was sold into Egyptian slavery and the time he stood before the King to become the Prime Minister of Egypt. Think of it, thirteen years in Potiphar's house and the prison! This has not been an easy road; in fact it has been an unusually difficult path to travel.

Butler and baker...

Minutes turned into hours, hours turned into days, days turned into weeks, weeks turned into months, months turned into years -- two years to be exact. Joseph must have felt that his life would never be disconnected from the emotional roller coaster he seemed to be hopelessly trapped on.

After two years, Pharaoh dreams a dream which neither he nor his spiritual advisers could interpret. The butler remembers Joseph and the King calls for him. Joseph tells the king there will be seven years of bumper crops in Egypt followed by seven years of famine. Joseph advises the King to store grain and food in the seven years of plenty so that the Kingdom could be sustained during the seven years of famine.

Pharaoh agrees with the interpretation and makes Joseph the Prime Minister. Joseph is there. He has arrived. He finally made it. He is now doing the dream!

God almost never works within our time frame. We cannot afford to attempt to squeeze God into our time restraint but patiently await His timing!

Think of others who found themselves awaiting the promise of God: (PLACE ON SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Moses was eighty when he became the Senior Pastor of First Assembly of God in the Wilderness

- Caleb was eighty-five when he was given permission to conquer his inheritance within Canaan

- Abraham was one-hundred and Sara was ninety when she gave birth to the promised son

So we want to do the dream? How patient are we? Are we willing to wait through the development stage? If God allowed us to step into our destiny prematurely we would mess up the set-up! Winston Churchill said,\ "It is a mistake to look too far ahead. Only one link in

the chain of destiny can be handled at a time." (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

Joseph survived thirty years of dashed dreams to finally stand on the pinnacle of his destiny. If we had set out to write a novel that allowed for Joseph's success, none of us would have had the creativity to map out such a path. The path of Joseph is marked by high drama. The important thing is that he maintained his dream while not sacrificing his integrity to climb the ladder of success.

What good is success if we sacrifice our integrity and character to get there? (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) Some of God's people have grown a ministry that was bigger than their character and they have embarrassed themselves and reeked havoc on the Kingdom of God!

- Dream the dream

- Develop his integrity

- Develop a servant's heart

When Joseph became Prime Minister, he was mature enough to understand that his steps were being ordered of God rather than a result of his own ability.

Because he knew that being afforded the opportunity to do the dream was about what God wanted to use him to do for others, rather than a plan for his own comfort, Joseph: (PLACE ON SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)
