## I. THE SETTING OF THE SCRIPTURE

Notice the events here at the Jordan River. Moses has died and Joshua is the new leader of the Israelites. Though he has been the right hand man to Moses, he is an unproven leader, because he has spent more than forty years taking orders and doing what he is told.

The Israelites are at the end of their wilderness wandering and they are about to reach a place of being offered another opportunity to occupy Canaan. Before the Israelites can occupy the Promised Land they must: (PLACE ON SCREEN, ONE AT A TIME)

- Cross Jordan (While it is at flood stage)

- Defeat Jericho (A fortified city)

- Defeat Ai

- Conquer Canaan (Occupied by giants)

This does not sound too difficult as long as you are reading a story, but it is far more difficult to live out in real life. Listen to the instructions of Joshua to Israel. Joshua 3:1-3 (NKJV) "Then Joshua rose early in the morning; and they set out from Acacia Grove and came to the Jordan, he and all the children of Israel, and lodged there before they crossed over. So it was, after three days, that the officers went through the camp; and they commanded the people, saying, "When you see the ark of the covenant of the Lord your God, and the priests, the Levites, bearing it, then you shall set out from your place and go after it."

The role of the people would be to follow the leadership of the Priests who were in charge of the Ark of the Covenant.

The instructions to the Priests were a bit more complicated. Joshua 3:8 (NKJV) "The priests who bear the Ark of the Covenant, shall 'come to the edge of the water of the Jordan, and shall stand still in the Jordan."

The Priests were to actually step into the Jordan. Remember, at this point the Jordan is a river at flood-stage! Joshua 3:13 (NKJV) "And it shall come to pass, as soon as the soles of the feet of the priests who bear the ark of the Lord, the Lord of all the earth, shall rest in the waters of the Jordan, that the waters of the Jordan shall be cut off, the waters that come down from upstream, and they shall stand as a heap."

Jordan was overflowing its banks. This overflowing happened every spring due to the melting of the snow on the mountains of Lebanon to the North. To the Israelites the Jordan was an obstacle to a long awaited promise, to the inhabitants of Jericho it was a first line of defense against a dangerous enemy! (LEAVE ON SCREEN, UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) The inhabitants of Jericho hoped a flooding Jordan River would keep the Israelites away.

Did God not know that at this particular time of the year, Jordan would be at flood-stage? Of course God knew! He is all knowing! Plus the fact, this happened every year at about the same time.

## II. FACING THE DIFFICULTY

A river at flood-stage is not an easy thing to deal with. (My experience with the Red River -- Hurricane Ike)

(PICTURES OF HURRICANE IKE OVERFLOWING THE SEAWALL -- OTHER PICTURES OF OVERFLOWING RIVERS)

God does not place difficulties in our lives but when they come He provides the grace and power to deal with them! Jordan experiences are a normal part of life! It may seem to be the case, but difficult times are not unique to your situation, everyone has them!

There have been times:

- I visited the hospital and should have been a patient...

- I have counseled people when I needed counseling...

- I have given business advice when I could not get my hands around my own affairs...

Because we are reading an historical account we know the "Rest of the story," Jordan would divide; there would be a path three miles wide; the Children of Israel would walk across 5,000 abreast; (LEAVE ON SCREEN, UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) this sounds easy enough after the fact, but can you imagine how difficult a thing like that would be to believe for?

The significant fact here was the river would divide providing the Children of Israel with a path to victory, but this would not occur until the feet of the priests were in the overflowing river! Stepping in a river at flood stage is either the most idiotic thing one could do or it is an incredible act of faith!

As in this case, most of the time it seems that the Lord waits until the last possible minute to intervene in our situation! This is not unusual and it is not disingenuous on His part, it is His allowance for the development of faith in the person He is mentoring! Someone said, "God never comes too late with too little, He is always right on time with more than enough!" (LEAVE ON SCREEN, UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

Jordan overflowing its banks suggests everything being out of control; insurmountable difficulty; no help in sight, but we must remember the words of the psalmist, Psalm 121:1-8 (NKJV) "I will lift up my eyes to the hills---From whence comes my help? My help comes from the Lord, Who made heaven and earth. He will not allow your foot to be moved; He who keeps you will not slumber. Behold, He who keeps Israel Shall neither slumber nor sleep. The Lord is your keeper; The Lord is your shade at your right hand. The sun shall not strike you by day, Nor the moon by night. The Lord shall preserve you from all evil; He shall preserve your soul. The Lord shall preserve your going out and your coming in From this time forth, and even forevermore."

Notice: They were about to go in and possess the Promised Land when Jordan overflowed! There are those who have witnessed Jordan overflowing its banks in their lives; they have been there when the situation seemed to get down to the last minute; when they've gone their limit! Someone said, "The darkest hour is just before the dawn!"

## III. PROBLES ARE MORE PREVELANT DURING HARVEST TIME

Notice when Jordan was out of control, during harvest time! Joshua 3:15 (NKJV) "Jordan overflows all its banks during the whole time of harvest." (Gideon -- Judges 6:11-16 He was hiding to thrash wheat but the Angel called him "A mighty man of valor!")

When we are in the time of harvest, difficulty; problems; trials; and troubles seem more frequent and severe. (LEAVE ON SCREEN, UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) That's because they are, it is not an illusion! The enemy must make a last ditch effort to bring us down before we lay our hands on the promise! Isaiah 59:19 (NKJV) "When the enemy comes in like a flood, The Spirit of the Lord will lift up a standard against him."

We must always remember, the difficulties we face do not necessarily happen because one is out of the will of God---but rather, because we're human and this is the time of the harvest! (LEAVE ON SCREEN, UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) The question one must ask is the same question Jeremiah asked in his day, Jeremiah 12:5 (NKJV) "If thou hast run with the footmen, and they have wearied thee, then how canst thou contend with horses? and if in the land of peace, wherein thou trustedst, they wearied thee, then how wilt thou do in the swelling of Jordan?"
