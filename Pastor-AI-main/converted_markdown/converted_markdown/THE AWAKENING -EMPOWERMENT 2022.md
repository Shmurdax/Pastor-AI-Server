# THE AWAKENING

INTRO: I have literally been in and around the church my whole life
(Explain this).

I had the privilege of spending my teenage years in a church that was in
the midst of a red hot, Holy Ghost revival. (Welcome Hill -- It has
never been a big church but it has been a powerful church -- Chris
Bradley -- Myself) This experience impacted my life in a positive
direction, and gave me an ongoing insatiable desire to see a real move
of God in the earth, otherwise known in church circles as revival.

I have had the opportunity of being a part of some of the great revivals
of the past (Malvern - Brownsville). I could literally morph into a
revival junkie with little to no effort whatsoever. The revivals of the
past have been more or less soaking events. They featured:

- Protracted services... (Weeks or even months in duration)

- Passionate worship...

- Sermons filled with conviction...

- Strong altar calls... (Preaching a sermon and not giving an altar call
 makes about as much sense as a professional fisherman getting his nets
 full and cutting them lose rather than dealing with the catch)

- Extended altar services...

There is certainly nothing wrong with any of this, in fact, I could live
like this indefinitely.

One of the things I remember growing up, is the prediction, if not
prophecy, that there would be an end time revival. Based on this, we
(The church) have waited with baited breath, for this tsunami of the
presence of God to hit the church, so we can all sit in circles, hold
hands, and soak in the presence of the Lord.

But I must ask the question, is this what the world needs and is it in
reality what an end time revival will look like?

I hope it will have many of these characteristics, but I think the world
needs something far more consequential. (What the Lord said to me about
the Brownsville revival --

So, the question needs to be asked, "What will this next great
Awakening look like?"

The Biblical prediction is that there will be an end time move of God
and a great falling away simultaneously. How can such a thing be? Here
is what I am seeing happen:

- The love of many has waxed cold... We are seeing this now -- People
 can go everywhere but to church -- Covid

- Those who seek another soaking event have become discontented with
 organized religion, because they are not experiencing what they have
 witnessed in the past... Many of these people have intentionally
 absented themselves not only from church services, but from the body
 of Christ altogether -- A few years ago I was praying for God to show
 this next generation His power, and He said to me, "Can you embrace
 it if it looks different than what you have imagined?"

- The expectation of my generation for a move of God for the next
 generation has not come to fruition - I think this is because we are
 looking for something that God never intended to send - What if what
 the Lord is intending to send, and what we are expecting, is two
 different things?

- When our expectation and what the Lord is trying to do is different,
 is it possible for those who are hungry for the next great revival to
 miss it altogether? I believe the answer to this is YES!

I believe what God is going to do in this next season is going to be
marked by the following characteristics:

event!

Some nations of the earth are approaching 25% of their population being
born again believers. The world's largest church is located in Seol
South Korea, and is a Pentecostal church! The church has a membership of

If we want to be a part of the great awakening, we must make the lost
our priority. What is the value of an individual in our church or
ministry? What percentage of your budgets are we designating to go after
the lost?
