In an effort to learn how to overcome the things that hold us back from God's best, I want us to look at the story of a cripple Prince by the name of Mephibosheth.

In this passage of Scripture David is King over Israel. The backdrop for this story involves David making a covenant, many years prior, with Jonathan, who was King Saul's son.

Saul wanted to kill David but Jonathan protected him (The twisted culture we live in has made far more of this than was actually there). This covenant came out of a friendship between David and Jonathan, which was solidified with Jonathans loyalty to David when his King father was seeking the life of his friend.

In this passage of Scripture Saul and Jonathan are dead and David is King. Even though Jonathan, David's friend is dead, David remembers and honors the covenant between him and his friend. Vs. 3 "Is there not still someone of the house of Saul, to whom I may show the kindness of God?"

Notice here, all David asked was whether or not there was a surviving descendent of Saul with whom he could keep his covenant with Jonathan. Listen to the response of Ziba (Zee-baw), Vs 3 "There is still a son of Jonathan who is lame in his feet." Notice Ziba (Zee-baw) remembers his shame before he remembers his name. The same is true of everyone in this audience; people will always remember our failures...

- He cheated on his wife...

- She was arrested for shoplifting...

- He used to be an alcoholic...

- She had an abortion...

Mephibosheth's problem was that he had been dropped. 2 Samuel 4:4 (NKJV) "Jonathan, Saul's son, had a son who was lame in his feet. He was five years old when the news about Saul and Jonathan came from Jezreel; and his nurse took him up and fled. And it happened, as she made haste to flee, that he fell and became lame. His name was Mephibosheth."

The world is full of people who have been dropped. The church is full of people who have been dropped. The church has been guilty of dropping a lot of people!

From the time his nurse dropped him Mephibosheth could never shake his history. No matter where he went or what he did his history ruled the day. He couldn't get away from his past. His legs were a constant reminder to him.

There are people in this audience today that would love to escape their past but everywhere they go it seems to arrive before they do.

Look at this, in one day Mephibosheth went from Prince to Pauper; from fortune to unfortunate; from destined to destitute; from complete to cripple; from capable to incapable! Have you ever had a thing like this happen to you? One day you feel as if you have the world by the tail on a down hill pull and the next day all opportunity has evaporated.

Mephibosheth believed his handicap had to do with his legs but in reality, his legs were only a symptom of what was really wrong. His real handicap was that:

## I. HE WAS IN THE WRONG PLACE AT THE WRONG TIME...

A. The problem here was not as much with the condition of Mephibosheth as it was with his position.

The real problem that Mephibosheth faced was that he was out of place. He was bred and born for a palace, not the ghetto! That is precisely the problem with many of us. We are not in the right place.

God commanded Elijah to go to the Brook Cherith and He would sustain him THERE. If Elijah had gone anywhere else, he would have starved to death. He had to be in the right place at the right time! (Israel: Under the Cloud and Fire... Because of this, A Rock followed them)

B. All the enemy has to do is keep us out of place! When we are not where we need to be, when we are supposed to be there, we miss out on the provision of God!

Mephibosheth was in the house of Machir (Maw-keer). By all accounts, Machir (Maw-keer) treats Mephibosheth very well, but his resources and ability are very limited.

If you are not in the right place at the right time the thing that blessed you yesterday can keep you from a blessing today. Eventually, Elijah's brook dried up!

It is not difficult to leave those who are your enemies or who mistreat you, but sometimes you have to leave your friends. ## II. HE WAS BEING NEGATIVELY IMPACTED BY A LACK OF INFORMATION...

The word Lo Debar comes from two Hebrew words and is defined in the following ways:

- Lo - meaning "No"

- Debar - meaning "Word"

When you combine those two words it simply means "No Word." Mephibosheth was living in the land of "No Word."
