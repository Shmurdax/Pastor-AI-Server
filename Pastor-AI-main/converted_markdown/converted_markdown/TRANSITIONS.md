# TRANSITIONS

For I have appointed him to be ruler over Israel and Judah."

INTRO: I would like to use the transition between David and Solomon to discuss "How to prepare for successful transitions."

David had been king for 40 years and now it was time for his son, Solomon, to become the new leader of Israel. A huge transition was taking place.

Life is full of transitions:

- Some are welcomed transitions, and some are un-welcome...

- Some are instant and some are incremental...

- Some thrust us forward and some set us back...

- Some are in our best interest, and some are detrimental...

Let's think through of some of the transitions we have been through in the past:

- Marriage... (For better or worse)

- Buying a house... (Our first house -- 1990 - \$57,250 PLACE A PIC ON THE SCREEN)

- Changing jobs... (When I came to CT I had already been a lead Pastor for more than 20 years, and some folks acted like I had never pastored a day in my life)

- Having a baby... (A baby changes everything)

- Retirement... (Totally new norms financially and in every other way)

A life transition can be positive or negative, planned or unexpected. Some transitions happen without warning and can be quite dramatic or traumatic. Others are planned and very methodical.

Whether positive or negative, life transitions cause us to leave behind the familiar and force us to adjust to new ways of living, at least temporarily. They can leave us feeling completely unprepared.

In David's last words to his son Solomon, he gives his son some great advice for the transition he was about to experience:

- "Be strong - Act like a man." - He was saying, it is time to grow up. It was time for Solomon to step into another level of maturity for this new assignment. Successful Transitions ALWAYS require us to go to a new level of maturity!

- "Be Obedient To God's Word" -- This may have been the best advice in a transition. Solomon would need God's Word to guide him as he led in this new assignment. We are going to need God's eternal Word as the bedrock for everything we are going to experience through all our transitions!

Transitions are unavoidable. They will happen. I am going to give you a list of things that I have found that Successful Transitions have taught me in the past:

- Transitions bring about submission - Following God's divine will for our lives will require us to say YES to some scary assignments at times. (Daniel in the lion's den) We will never know whether we are submitted until we do not agree or do not understand the direction, we seem to be pushed in.

- Transitions bring us into a deeper relationship with God - They cause us to depend on Him in deeper ways than we ever have before. (John on the Island of Patmos)

- Transition brings about God's missional plan for us - Sometimes my transition is not God repositioning me for MY benefit, but for someone else's benefit. (Paul & Silas -- The Philippian jailer and his family -- Jophn & Sandra Tracy)

We should get to the place where we welcome transitions rather than resisting them because we know that nothing comes to us that has not been filtered through the clearing house of Father God.

Do we trust Him when we find ourselves in a transition that we do not understand? When we do, can we say...

\(1\) I am here by God\'s appointment

\(2\) I am here in God's keeping

\(3\) I am here under God's training

\(4\) I am here for God's time

CONCLUSION:

Why was God taking Solomon on this journey? Because He trusted him with the assignment. Why has he taken us through transition? Because He trusts us. He has a story for us to live out.
