## VIII. THE PARABLE OF THE HOUSEHOLDER...

In this parable Jesus is attempting to explain to His disciples that
they possess the same [abilities]{.underline} and
[privileges]{.underline} the scribes and householders possessed.

The Jewish Scribe:

- Possessed two unusual traits; they were extremely
 [devoted]{.underline} and were able to commit all their time to the
 [study]{.underline} of their religion.

The Householder:

- Was the head of a house and possessed all the [treasures]{.underline}
 of the estate including its food and the food supply. The householder
 had two primary duties with regard to food; he was to store the old
 food in a manner which kept it [fresh]{.underline} for as long as
 possible and he was to add the new food to the old, serving each at
 the [appropriate]{.underline} time.

With regard to the scribes:

- Christ wanted His disciples to develop the same [devotion]{.underline}
 as a scribe, but direct it toward the Kingdom of Heaven... Romans
 12:1-2, Galatians 2:20, Matthew 22:37

- The scribe spent the bulk of his time studying [religion]{.underline}.
 Jesus wanted to divert the disciple's attention from this present age
 to the Kingdom of Heaven... 2 Timothy 2:15

- The scribe spent his time searching the [truths]{.underline} of the
 Old Testament but Jesus wanted the disciples to focus on both the Old
 and the New Testaments... 2 Timothy 3:16

With regard to a householder:

- The householder was responsible to share his [resources]{.underline}
 with his family, employees and friends... Matthew 28:19-20, Luke
 19:10, John 20:21

- The householder was to share his [revelation]{.underline}, both old
 and new... John 1:17-18

- The householder was to share his [truth]{.underline}, both old and
 new... John 14:6

- The householder was to share [information]{.underline}, both old and
 new... Matthew 5:17, Romans 8:3, Hebrews 8:6, Hebrews 9:14-15

The godly [past]{.underline} is not to be discarded, it is to be
developed, built upon, fulfilled, and made better. Philippians 3:13-14
