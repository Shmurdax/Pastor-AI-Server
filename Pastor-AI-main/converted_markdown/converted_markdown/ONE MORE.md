THE LOST SON

Luke 15:11-32 "Then He said: "A certain man had two sons. 12 And the younger of them said to his father, 'Father, give me the portion of goods that falls to me.' So he divided to them his livelihood. 13 And not many days after, the younger son gathered all together, journeyed to a far country, and there wasted his possessions with prodigal living."

Today marks the third and final message in our ONE MORE series.

In this passage of Scripture, in order to make His point, Jesus puts forth three parables:

- The lost sheep...

- The lost coin...

- The lost son...

Wait a minute! The lost son? In this particular parable Jesus attempts to put real flesh and blood on what He was attempting to teach the His Jewish audience. (Mostly Pharisees and teachers of the Law)

These religious snobs, in their self-righteous arrogance, devalued and despised those they deemed lesser than themselves. (They hated the Samaritans -- They were bigots of the worst kind)

Jesus confronted them directly and forcefully. He revealed the heart of the Father with regard to ALL persons! This group of up and out lawmakers - and law-keepers - valued religious systems and protocols but devalued the importance of individuals. This is not too unlike the modern church world! Religious leaders are constantly looking to play to the up-and-outers and devalue the down-and-outers! (Pastor who told me he spends 50% of his time with the top 10% of his givers)

Through these parables Jesus was attempting to communicate, that this gospel is to:

- The haves and have nots...

- The anyone's and everyone's...

- The moral and the immoral...

- The righteous and the unrighteous...

- The down-and outers and the up-and-outers...

- The whosoever will's and the whosoever wont's...

Hear me today -- He didn't die for some! He died for all!

Jesus went from talking about an animal -- To talking about an inanimate object -- To talking about a human being! As misguided as the prodigal son may have been, he was still the son of a father, and as such, the object of a father's love!

The younger son of the father:

They are ONE MORE! Go after them!

- Wasted the inheritance of the father... The inheritance never belonged to him -- EVER! It was not the sons to demand, it was the fathers to give, at the appropriate time!
