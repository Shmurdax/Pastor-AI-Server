## I. TOTAL COMMITMENT

1 Chronicles 12:8 (KJV) "And of the Gadites there separated themselves unto David into the hold to the wilderness...." Please remember, when these people made the decision to support David and his effort, they could never retreat! Retreat would not be tolerated; in fact, to do so would almost certainly be a signature on their death warrant! This decision was a total commitment to David and his cause!

The same thing happens to you and I; Christ followers have no real place of retreat! Proverbs 13:15 (KJV) "Good understanding giveth favour: but the way of transgressors is hard." If one is going to live a victorious life the best thing that we can do is to burn the bridges of retreat behind us!

ILLUSTRATION: When Cortez landed at Vera Cruz in 1519 to begin his conquest of Mexico, he had a small band of soldiers, which consisted of about 700 men. Once he landed it is said that he purposely set fire to his small fleet of 11 ships. His small army watched as their only means of retreat sank to the bottom of the Gulf of Mexico. With the only means of retreat destroyed, the only way to move was forward, into the Mexican interior to meet whatever might come their way!

So it is with being a soldier in the army of God! If one is to be effective in the Christian walk a total commitment to Christ and His cause must be made in advance! 2 Timothy 2:4 (KJV) "No man that warreth entangleth himself with the affairs of this life; that he may please him who hath chosen him to be a soldier." (13) Let us go forth therefore unto him without the camp, bearing his reproach. (14) For here have we no continuing city, but we seek one to come."

We live in perilous times, and it is sad when those who are supposed to be Soldiers in the army of God side with those who have rejected Christ as king! Are we totally committed to Christ to the point that we have already determined that nothing can separate us from Him or His cause? ## II. THOROUGH TRAINING

Notice 1 Chronicles 12:2, 8, & 38 (KJV) "They were armed with bows and could use both the right hand and the left in hurling stones and shooting arrows out of a bow, even of Saul\'s brethren of Benjamin. (8) And of the Gadites there separated themselves unto David into the hold to the wilderness men of might, and men of war fit for the battle, that could handle shield and buckler, whose faces were like the faces of lions, and were as swift as roes upon the mountains; (38) All these men of war, that could keep rank, came with a perfect heart to Hebron, to make David king over all Israel: and all the rest also of Israel were of one heart to make David king."

The men who chose to support David were not sissies, they were lean, mean, fighting machines who had their mind made up that this was a cause worth dying for! As a result, they kept themselves in the best physical shape possible and subjected themselves to rigorous ordeal of training. The training they were subjected to was extremely demanding physically and mentally but they understood that if they desired to be the best warrior possible they would be required to go beyond the norm in the area of preparedness.

General Juan Lopez of the United States Marine Corp said, "Training gives you a skill set to perform a specific task."

The fighting men who served in David's military machine were expected to get up every morning prepared to fight the most intense battle of their lives. Some day's they were needed, some day's they weren't but the level of preparedness, which was required, remained the same each day.

How foolish to believe the Christian life will be easy street! If we are going to be effective soldiers we must make up our minds, this is a cause worth dying for. If this is such a worthy cause we should do everything in our power to become trained, disciplined, and properly equipped.

We have been given the whole armor of God for a reason, to fight the enemy! What kind of hardship are we willing to endure in order to win this good fight of faith? If we hope to be victorious, we must have our mind made up to persevere despite the odds.

## V. UNQUESTIONED LOYALTY

1 Chronicles 12:16-18 (KJV) "And there came of the children of Benjamin and Judah to the hold unto David. (17) And David went out to meet them, and answered and said unto them, if ye become peaceably unto me to help me, mine heart shall be knit unto you: but if ye be come to betray me to mine enemies, seeing there is no wrong in mine hands, the God of our fathers look thereon, and rebuke it. (18) Then the spirit came upon Amasai, who was chief of the captains, and he said, Thine are we, David, and on thy side, thou son of Jesse: peace, peace be unto thee, and peace be to thine helpers; for thy God helpeth thee. Then David received them, and made them captains of the band,"

The setting of 1 Chronicles chapter twelve is that David and those supporting him are fleeing from Saul, as a result their living conditions lacked any kind of convenience whatsoever. In fact, they were holed up in a makeshift fort in the middle of the wilderness. (Story of convincing Susan to go with me to preach a wilderness camp in Northern Manitoba Canada)

On the surface, this setup had a Robin Hood kind of feel to it but the reality of the matter was much different. The conditions awaiting anyone defecting to David and his cause were: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Low morale...

- Lack of trust...

- Primitive living conditions...

- Hiding from the government...

- Fleeing from a psychotic King...

- Being viewed as a rebel rather than a warrior...

Given the conditions, it is amazing to me that anyone would defect from the Army of Saul to join with David. Many did, and among them were the eleven men from the tribe of Gad. The choice the eleven men of Gad made was certainly not a flippant decision but a calculated risk whose upside potential proved to be astronomical.

The point is, David was right and he was God's chosen successor for Saul whom He had rejected because the spirit of rebellion had tarnished his reign.

The path of least resistance is rarely ever the will of God! This worlds system attempts to pour everyone into the same mold and have each of us to go with the flow. There comes a time for each of us to: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Swim upstream...

- Take a stand against evil decisions...

- Stand against the tide of public opinion...

- Be willing to risk life and limb for what is right...

While we must stand against evil there are two things we must always remember: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- We must make sure we are choosing to be on the side of what is right...

- We must always maintain a proper attitude while taking our stand...

You can bet these eleven men were convinced David was right and he was God's appointed and anointed leader before they came to join themselves to his cause. I think the bad attitude of Saul toward David and David's excellent spirit toward Saul may have been the thing that tipped the scales for this decision.

Before we attach our loyalty to a thing we must make sure we are siding with what is right, and then maintain a good attitude while defeating evil in this world!

Robert Benchley said, "A boy can learn a lot from a dog; obedience, loyalty, and the importance of turning around three times before lying down."

At the beginning, David questioned the loyalty of these warriors. In the end however, he could sense the loyalty of these brave, courageous, determined, fearless, gutsy followers. He could sense that these were people who would have his back on the day of battle. They were people who would not cause worry lines in his forehead! Joyce Maynard said, "A person who deserves my loyalty receives it." These men asked for, earned, and received, David's trust and respect. I am a Pastor who expects the loyalty of church members and with staff it is non-negotiable. Just as I expect loyalty, I give loyalty in return. Loyalty is built on trust, when trust is eroded there is nothing to support the weight of loyalty and it collapses. When one expects and gives loyalty the result is a high-risk relationship. The deeper one loves the more disappointment hurts. However, when everyone in the relationship walks within the perimeters of loyalty, the sky is the k

Limit for the upside of the relationship.

The ancestry of the tribe of Gad can be traced to the seventh son of Jacob. The tribe of Gad was by-in-large sheep tenders and livestock growers. For them to morph into a rag tag group of fierce warriors was a gargantuan leap of faith and effort.

The people who had joined David previously were mostly a group of weary, distressed, discontented, broke, busted, soldiers of fortune who were in it more for what they could get than what they could add to the cause.

The eleven sons of Gad joining the cause of David was an epic event and marked a change in the kind of people who would eventually carry David to the throne and fight his enemies over the forty-year period of his reign. It is interesting to note that after receiving a war torn nation, the forty-year reign of David produced a nation in peace when Solomon came to power. The eleven sons of Gad were an important part of David's Kingdom and served with the utmost loyalty and dignity throughout David's reign.

If we are going to live a victorious life we must be people of unquestioned loyalty! The harder the battle, the more necessary the commitment to loyalty becomes! Mark 8:34 (KJV) "And when he had called the people unto him with his disciples also, he said unto them, Whosoever will come after me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross, and follow me." According to Jesus, three things are required to become one of His disciples:

- Self-denial...

- Cross-bearing...

- Follow-ship...

Edwin Louis Cole said, "Loyalty is the virtue of faithfulness." (LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL END OF SERVICE) The crown of faithfulness will be given to those who finish well. We must not lose patience in the fight and make a break with our loyalty because "Those who endure to the end shall be saved."
