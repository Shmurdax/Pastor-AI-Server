INTRO: Angels are introduced to the human race through the venue of Scripture. As far as we know, most of us have never entertained an angel face to face. (Guy who said his wife was an angel, because she was "Always up in the air harping about something.") The only time I know of in my life that I may have seen an angel is when I was a little child and a person brought food and Christmas gifts to our home. The Bible says it is possible that we could entertain angels and not even be aware of it, and most of us would like to keep it that way!

If we are going to entertain an angel we probably do not want to start with the model which: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Guards the Garden, having a flaming sword in his hand and turns 360degrees at all times -- Adam encountered this model...

If we are going to entertain an angel we would most likely want to start out with the basic two winged model that happens to be in a good mood! The very notion of angels set some people on edge but the Scripture teaches that angels are everywhere and they are sent to be ministering spirits! I believe we entertain angels every day but they are mostly the invisible model! Someone once told me that the way I drive I must wear out a guardian angel a year, which was a hateful but true thing to say!

In our Scripture text, a peasant girl from Nazareth is approached by, and carries on a conversation with an angel! Mary is somewhat alarmed by the presence of Gabriel and several things happen in just a few verses: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- A decision was reached; "Then Mary said, 'Behold the maidservant of the Lord! Let it be to me according to your word.'"

Most people believe that when we are walking in the perfect will of God, we are supposed to be constantly hearing the voice of God. I mean after all, we used to sing about it in church, "And He walks with me and He talks with me and He tells me I am His own. And the joy we share as we tarry there none other has ever known."

I wish I could tell you that this is the way it will always be, but the truth is most of the time we have to walk by faith. We cannot just set around on Gods lap and aggravate Him all the time!

The angel Gabriel appeared to Mary and: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Announced the intentions of the Almighty by telling her she would birth the Son of God...

- Confirmed His will by telling her she would be overshadowed by the Almighty...

- Reassured her by telling her not to be afraid because the Lord would be with her...

Then the Bible says, "The angel departed from her." As far as I know this was Mary's last communication with Gabriel! Surely if Gabriel and the Father expected this peasant girl to participate in a plan that was so far over the top, they would agree for Gabriel to camp out at Mary's house for at least nine months and smite anyone who spoke out against the plan! Not so, Gabriel left, never to return!

This brings us to the question at hand, what are we to do when the angel leaves the house? (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Follow through on the commitment which has been made...

- Encourage yourself in the Lord...

- Worship your way through the trial...

- Keep on keeping on...

## I. FOLLOW THROUGH ON THE COMMITMENT WHICH HAS BEEN MADE... (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE) When Mary told Gabriel, "Let it be to me according to your word" she was saying; "I commit to you that I will follow through on what the Almighty has asked of me!" This would not be easy! She was a peasant girl with little to no resources and she was being asked to raise a child as a single parent if necessary. At this point she did not know what Joseph might do, she just said, "I will place my life on hold, let it be to me according to your word." She had made a commitment that she would follow through on, no matter what! Martin Luther King said, "If you are not willing to die for something, you are not fit to live."

Being single and pregnant carried with it consequences which were far more detrimental than in this present culture. If she were pregnant and unmarried, she would likely be unmarried for the rest of her life, and she would probably be choosing a life of poverty for her and her child! Yet, she said, "Let it be to me according to your word!"

The promise the Angel made to Mary would: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Take nine months to come to pass...

- Place her engagement to the man of her dreams on hold...

- Exact a high personal price with regard to her public persona...

Thomas Jefferson said, "When you do a thing, act as if all the world were watching."

She would be the earthly mother of Jesus for more than thirty years, and this came with the good, the bad and the ugly! The emotional roller coaster Mary would be asked to ride would experience exhilarating highs and dangerous lows. The emotional highs and lows which Mary experienced vicariously through her son, included: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Turning water into wine, then crying out for a drink on the cross...

- Forgiving an adulterous woman condemned to death, then being rejected by the religious establishment...

- Healing ten lepers, then being betrayed by a disciple...

- Feeding five thousand with a little boy's lunch, then not having a place to lay His head...

- Healing of Peter's mother in law, then having to appoint a guardian for His own mother...

- Raising the dead to life, then the death of the cross...

How in the world could Mary have endured such a thing? She made a commitment to Gabriel which she would make good on, "Let it be to me according to your word!" The commitment Mary made was a commitment to endure whatever the Lord decided to send her way, coupled with a steadfast pledge to do it with a good attitude!

What did our commitment to that promise include? I have some great counsel for those of us who have received a promise from the Lord and are currently involved in the waiting stage, keep the promise in sight and know he will perform His word!

## II. ENCOURAGE YOURSELF IN THE LORD... In the case of Mary, she has just received a personal message from Jehovah through the Angel Gabriel saying that she will be a central figure in the incarnation of God. Think about this, this peasant girl will be a primary figure in a story which will be told worldwide, for centuries to come, and the effects of this happening will change the lives of untold millions. Who would ever have imagined that a girl from Nazareth could ever be a part of something so far reaching? Yet, here it is, Gabriel announces that Mary will be the mother of Jesus! Luke 2:31-33 (NKJV) "You will conceive in your womb and bring forth a Son, and shall call His name Jesus. "He will be great, and will be called the Son of the Highest; and the Lord God will give Him the throne of His father David. "And He will reign over the house of Jacob forever, and of His kingdom there will be no end." (LEAVE THE SCRIPTURE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

Mary must have left this meeting on an emotional high. She was so high that she kept these things and pondered them in her heart. I am not sure when all of this began to settle in on her but eventually she began to realize that though she was "Blessed and highly favored of the Lord," the road she had been called to travel would not be easy street, in fact it would be a very, very, difficult journey.

I want you to imagine with me Mary: (PLACE THE SLIDES ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Telling her parents she was expecting...

- Telling Joseph she was pregnant...

- Beginning to show and dealing with the criticism of her relatives, friends and community...

- Dealing with morning sickness and the discomforts of pregnancy while Gabriel was hanging out around the throne and dangling his feet in the River of Life...

I do not think it was too long before Mary started to wonder when Gabriel would be back to bring a little encouragement, but the angel had left the house and would not be back! It was now up to Mary to endure a pregnancy and raise a child without the encouragement of an angel!

Mary must have experienced some very difficult, gloomy, discouraging, dark days! Did the fact that her path was difficult mean that she was not doing the will of God? No! A thousand times no! She was in the perfect center of the will of God but she would be called upon to walk by faith and not by sight! She would need to encourage herself in the Lord!

William Mather Lewis said, "The abundant life does not come to those who have had a lot of obstacles removed from their path by others. It develops from within and is rooted in strong mental and moral fiber."

- Properly raising Jesus, the Son of God...

- Dealing with the problem of a child who is experiencing fame...

- Watching the rejection of her son as the Son of God...

- Knowing He would eventually be crucified by those He was attempting to help...

How in the world was a peasant girl from Nazareth going to equip herself to deal with the onslaught of such dire difficulty? Mary learned to deal with the difficulty early on! She made a trip to the home of Zachariah and Elizabeth and it was upon her arrival there that something extraordinary occurred. Luke 1:41 (NKJV) "When Elizabeth heard the greeting of Mary, that the babe leaped in her womb; and Elizabeth was filled with the Holy Spirit." ## IV. KEEP ON KEEPING ON... When the Angel is still present in the house, keeping sight of the promise and maintaining excitement is no problem. When the Angel leaves the house, never to return, it is not long until one must step up to the plate and walk by faith as all the adrenalin has drained from the encounter with the Angel! This no doubt happened to Mary and it will inevitably happen to us! Franklin D. Roosevelt said, "When you come to the end of your rope, tie a knot and hang on."

On the strength of one appearance from an Angel Mary: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A TIME)

- Received an unprecedented assignment from Gabriel...

- As an unwed mother carried a baby to full term...

- Accepted the pressure of negative publicity which would change her life forever...

- Assumed the responsibility of raising Gods own Son...

- Faithfully discharged her duties for thirty three and one half years...

Oftentimes the memory of the Angel and what he has said grows very faint in our minds. Think of this: Gabriel not only made himself scarce, visiting only once, he only spoke one hundred fifty seven words during his conversation with Mary! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN UNTIL NEXT SLIDE)

To place this in proper perspective, Gabriel and Mary did not sit around all afternoon and carry on a conversation. The Angel showed up, made an announcement and answered one question and was then off to whatever his next assignment might have been! The average sheet of 8 ½ X 11 paper contains approximately 450 words. The angel words filled up less than a third of a sheet of typing paper, and Mary was going to be expected to take on an unprecedented task which would span three and one half decades, on the strength of this lone conversation! How did she do it?

I believe she wrote His words down. She certainly meditated upon his words! Luke 2:19 (NKJV) "But Mary kept all these things and pondered them in her heart." The word "pondered" comes from the Greek word "Sumballo" and means "To confer with oneself." I think Mary rehearsed the meeting with Gabriel until she could quote the conversation word for word. Habakkuk 2:2-4 (NKJV) "And the LORD answered me, and said, Write the vision, and make it plain upon tables, that he may run that readeth it. In thirty three and one half years Mary probably wore the tablet the encounter with the angel was written on, completely out. Mary knew her path would not be easy but she was determined to keep on keeping on!

John Quincy Adams said, "Patience and perseverance have a magical effect before which difficulties disappear and obstacles vanish."

If we will keep on keeping on, the Lord will surely perform His promise!
