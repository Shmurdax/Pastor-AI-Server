# REDEMPTION

(Study Guide Answer Key)

Exodus 3:1-22

Salvation and redemption are not [Interchangeable.]{.underline}

According to dictionary.com "redemption" is:

An act of [redeeming](http://www.dictionary.com/browse/redeem) or
atoning for a [fault]{.underline} or [mistake]{.underline}.

One can be [delivered]{.underline} without being
[redeemed]{.underline} - But one cannot be [redeemed]{.underline}
without being [delivered]{.underline}.

When Joseph and Moses said "God will surely visit you and deliver
you" they were pointing to redemption to let them know:

- You will not be [staying]{.underline} in Egypt

- God is going to [deliver]{.underline} you

- God is going to set the [stage]{.underline} for redemption

- Take my [bones]{.underline} with you

When God spoke the words "God will surely visit you, and deliver
you" to Moses, He was intentionally letting Moses know the day of the
[Redemption]{.underline} of the Israelites was at hand!

The difference between deliverance and redemption:

- Deliverance is being set free from a [circumstance]{.underline}

- Redemption involves a [payback]{.underline}

Redemption involves

- Past [mistakes]{.underline} and [sins]{.underline} being resolved

- [Deliverance]{.underline} and [rescue]{.underline} for His people

- Bringing our families from [sin]{.underline} to
 [salvation]{.underline}

- Atonement for [guilty]{.underline} verdicts and conscious's

- Bring people and things back into our lives that we thought were a
 lost [cause]{.underline} and missed [opportunities]{.underline}

The roadmap to redemption Psalm 34:14

Three things are called for here:

- Depart from [evil]{.underline}

- Do [good]{.underline}

- Seek [peace]{.underline}

By acting in this way from [below]{.underline}, we will awaken the
[Almighty]{.underline} from Above to bring the redemption. 2 Chronicles
7:14
