Introduction;

God commanded his people after they came our of Egypt to hold meetings with him three times per year. They were called feasts. Yet they were mostly convocations or meeting times.

 

In our modern terminology, when Americans hear the word feast, we think of a buffet line. And I have told you what buffet means. Instead of an elaborate meal or banquet, these were special holy convocations or assemblies established by God when the Jews would come together to meet God in a special way. (God wants to meet man in a special way) They were religious gatherings. In a sense, Sunday services are a type of religious feasts of the New Testament church.

 

God established 7 feasts and scheduled them on the Hebrew calendar in such a way the Jews would have to travel to Jerusalem three times per year to keep them.

 

There were three feasts seasons.

 

PASSOVER.

PENTECOST.

TABERNACLES.

 

These Three feasts seasons represented three major encounters with God in the lives of His covenant people. These three encounters were for the purpose of God providing them with His divine PEACE, POWER AND REST for their lives.

 

The first feast was PASSOVER. It included, FEASTS OF PASSOVER, UNLEAVEN BREAD, and FIRST FRUITS.

 

The next feast was PENTECOST. It was a single feast which taught the Jews how to receive the POWER of GOD!

 

The Thirst Feast was TABERNACLES. The purpose of this feast was to show God's people how to enter God's rest. It included the FEAST of TRUMPETS, ATONEMENT, and TABERNACLES.

 

But these feasts or meetings were for the sole design of teaching HIS people how to enter his PEACE, POWER, and REST.

 

And my aim is to show you that God has not done away with these measures. He still wants you to enter HIS PEACE, POWER, and REST!

 

A child is taught in early grammar school the visual aids of blocks of their ABC's. They are taught to associate the blocks with letters, and then they begin to put them together and learn their ABC's.

 

God was using these meetings, feasts, as VISUAL AIDS to show HIS people how to enter the PEACE, POWER, and REST he had for them.

 

Taken as a whole, these feasts of Jehovah form a complete visual aid of the person and work of JESUS CHRIST, and the steps one must take to walk in the peace, power and rest of God.

Much of what I have said and especially the scripture texts seems as mundane. One just reading through chapter 23 of Leviticus may overlook the dates and times and seasons, but hey are very important to note and consider.

 

Jewish calendar- It is very important to see that these feasts were on the Jewish calendar, not the Gregorian calendar. Our calendar is based on the earth rotating around the sun. The days begin at midnight, and last 24 hours. It takes approx. 365 and one queater days for the earth to make a complete circle around the sun. This determines the length of the year. Some adjustments must be made to make up for the one quarter of a day. This is why an extra day is added every four years making a leap year of 366 days.

 

THE IMPORTANCE OF THE UNDERSTANDING OF THE JEWISH calendar is because these feasts were based on their calendar not ours.

 

The Jewish calendar is based on the movement of the moon around the earth. The day begins at sundown (APPROX. 6 PM) lasts for 24 hours. It takes 29 and a half days for the moon to circle the earth. Twelve of these LUNAR months add up to about 354 days for the year. Our calendar is about 11 and one quarter days longer than theirs. There are many other variables to long to mention. But it is important to get these straight in our minds to see how God wanted things to be.

 

 

> I.                 Passover!

Passover was celebrated in the month Nisan (March- April) and was the first feast. During this feast, we will discover 7 steps to a Christian's maturity and God's rest.

 

> A)                Passover was a memorial of the Hebrews > deliverance from Egypt.

It represented God's first encounter with his people.

Egypt had enslaved the Hebrews for about 400 years. God sent Moses as His instrument to lead the Hebrews out of Egypt. God sent 10 horrible plagues on Egypt to convince Pharaoh to let the Hebrews go. Yet each time God sent a plague, Pharaoh Heart was hardened.

 

On the tenth and final plague, was to be the death of every firstborn of Egypt. But with this special decree of death came special instructions for the Lord about how to be saved from the death angel that was going to go through the land and destroy all the first born. This is how the Passover got its name.
