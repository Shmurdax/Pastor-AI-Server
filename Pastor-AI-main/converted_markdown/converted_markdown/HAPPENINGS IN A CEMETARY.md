INTRO: The Scripture setting this morning is at the end of a horrific weekend that culminated in the death of the Son of God. Jesus was killed in the most despicable way a person could be murdered.

ILLUSTRATION: A woman wrote J. Vernon McGee and said: "Our preacher said that on Easter, Jesus just swooned on the cross and that the disciples nursed him back to health. What do you think?" McGee replied, "Dear Sister, beat your preacher with a leather whip for thirty-nine heavy strokes. Nail him to a cross. Hang him in the sun for six hours. Run a spear through his heart. Embalm him. Though we may not visit the empty tomb in this life, we can take a trip there through God's Word.

The tomb was literally located at the foot of the hill where Jesus was crucified.

Following the crucifixion, the body of Jesus was placed in a borrowed tomb belonging to a rich man by the name of Joseph of Arimithea.

The body of Jesus was buried late on Friday afternoon. Very early on Sunday morning, while it was still dark, a group of women left Bethany, which was about 2 miles from Jerusalem, and arrived at the tomb of the stricken savior just after sunrise. The sun had already risen before they reached the tomb.

When they arrived at the tomb, they discovered some very unusual things were happening. A cemetery is not a place where one would expect much excitement, but this was no ordinary day, and what happened in that cemetery was off the chain.

AN EARTHQUAKE HAPPENED...

Matthew 28:2 (KJV) "And behold, there was a great earthquake."

Earthquakes are mentioned several times in the Bible in both the Old and New Testaments. There was an earthquake when Jesus was crucified and another one occurred in the cemetery when Jesus was raised from the dead. Earthquakes reveal several things:

- They reveal God's power... (It takes some power to shake the earth)

- They reveal God's presence... (The veil was ripped)

- They reveal God... (Mt. Sinai - 10 commandments)

- They reveal God's Judgment... Hebrews 12:25-28 (KJV) "For if they escaped not who refused him that spake on earth, much more shall not we escape, if we turn away from him that speaketh from heaven: 26 Whose voice then shook the earth: but now he hath promised, saying, Yet once more I shake not the earth only, but also heaven. And this word, Yet once more, signifieth the removing of those things that are shaken, as of things that are made, that those things which cannot be shaken may remain."

 

DIVINE INTERVENTION HAPPENED...

Matthew 28:2 (KJV) "Behold, there was a great earthquake: for the angel of the Lord descended from heaven, and came and rolled back the stone from the door, and sat upon it."

Angels visited the cemetery and rolled the stone away from the tomb of Jesus. The Angel did not roll the stone away to let Jesus out, but to let the people on the outside witness the fact that he had risen. (Jesus walked through walls after His resurrection)

It is interesting to note that the angel was still sitting on the stone when the women arrived. Why would the angel linger in the cemetery, just sitting there on the stone?

- It was a symbol of comfort... (The women missed the resurrection but not the messenger)

- It was a symbol of defiance... (Death had been defeated and the Angel sat in defiance of the greatest enemy of humankind - Jesus abolished death)

- It was a symbol of victory... (The political system convicted Him -- The crowd sentenced Him -- The soldiers killed Him -- The presence of an Angel sitting on a stone in front of an empty tomb said everything)

THE WORLD'S GREATEST DECLARATION HAPPENED...

Matthew 28:5-6 (KJV) "The angel answered and said unto the women, Fear not ye: for I know that ye seek Jesus, which was crucified. 6 He is not here: for he is risen, as he said. Come, see the place where the Lord lay."

It is very humorous to me that the Angel said to the women who had just witnessed a resurrection, "Fear not..." Seriously? You think?

As we take a closer look, we see that there were four declarations in the cemetery:

- The angel declared the whereabouts of Jesus... Matthew 28:6 (KJV) "He is not here...:"

- The Angel declared His resurrection... Matthew 28:6 (KJV) "He is risen, as he said. Come, see the place where the Lord lay."

- The Angel declared His delegation... Matthew 28:7 (KJV) "Go quickly, and tell his disciples that he is risen from the dead..."

- The Angel declared His visitation... Matthew 28:7 (KJV) "Behold, he goeth before you into Galilee; there shall ye see him..."

 

CONCLUSION:

So what does all this mean to us?

A true story is told about a distinguished man, the only white person buried in a Georgia cemetery reserved exclusively for blacks. He had lost his mother when he was just a baby. His father, who never married again, hired a black woman named Mandy to help raise his son. She was a Christian and she took her task seriously. Seldom has a motherless boy received such warmhearted attention. One of his earliest memories was of Mandy bending tenderly over him in his upstairs bedroom each day and softly saying, "Wake up my boy, God\'s mornin\' is come."

As the years passed, this devoted woman continued to serve as his surrogate mother. The young man went away to college, but when he would come home on holidays and in the summer she would still climb the stairs and call him saying, Wake up my boy, God's mornin' has come."

One day after he had become a successful statesman, the sad message came: "Mandy is dead. Can you attend her funeral?"

As he stood by her grave in the cemetery, he turned to his friends and said, "If I die before Jesus comes, I want to be buried here beside Mandy. I like to think that on Resurrection Day she\'ll speak to me again and say, \'Wake up, my boy, God\'s mornin\' is come!"\' \-- James S. Hewett, Illustrations Unlimited (Wheaton: Tyndale House Publishers, Inc, 1988) pp. 163-164.
