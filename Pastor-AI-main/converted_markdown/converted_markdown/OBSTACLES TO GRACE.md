Titus 3:3-7 (NKJV)  "For we ourselves also were sometimes foolish, disobedient, deceived, serving divers lusts and pleasures, living in malice and envy, hateful, and hating one another. 4 But after that the kindness and love of God our Saviour toward man appeared, 5 Not by works of righteousness which we have done, but according to his mercy he saved us, by the washing of regeneration, and renewing of the Holy Ghost; 6 Which he shed on us abundantly through Jesus Christ our Saviour; 7 That being justified by his grace, we should be made heirs according to the hope of eternal life."

INTRO: Mercy is not getting what I do deserve:

- I didn't get punished for my sin, which I did deserve

- I didn't go to hell for the payment of my sin, which I did deserve

- I didn't get cut off from God for my sin, which I did deserve

- I didn't get ratted out for every mistake I have made, which I did deserve

(Talk about the mercy meter -- God's mercies are new every morning)

Grace is getting what I do not deserve:

- I don't deserve a good life, but I am living a good life

- I don't deserve the family I have, but I have them

- I don't deserve the house I live in, or the car I drive, but I have them

- I don't deserve to pastor this church, but I am here

1) Sin -- Of course, sin prevents a person from being able to receive the grace of God. (Self loathing) Sin in the life of the believer prevents the believer from being able to accept grace. Sin in the life of the believer is a confidence killer when we pray and approach God. (Cast not away your confidence...)

2) The World -- 1 John 2:16 (KJV) "For all that is in the world, the lust of the flesh, and the lust of the eyes, and the pride of life, is not of the Father, but is of the world."

A) Lust of the flesh = Sex, lust & perversion

B) Lust of the eye = Possessions, position & power

C) Pride of life = Always needing to be number one, willing to tear others down to build oneself up & bragging rights

## II. HOW DO WE OVERCOME THE OBSTACLES TO GRACE?

1) Become crucified with Christ

2) Die to self

3) Obedience to God

4) Put on Christ

5) Fellowship with other believers
