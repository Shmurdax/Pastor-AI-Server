INTRO: Today is Pentecost Sunday. Pentecost comes from the Greek word Pentekoste (Pent-tay-kos-tay) meaning the fiftieth day, the fiftieth part of a thing, or the fiftieth in order.

The Jews used the word Pentecost to name the second of their three most important feasts. This day was celebrated on the fiftieth day after Passover Sabbath. It was also a harvest festival and was accordingly called the "Feast of Harvest" because it marks the completion of the barley harvest. The celebration of Pentecost is also called the "Feast of Weeks" since it is situated on the calendar seven weeks and one day after Passover. Pentecost is called the "Day of the Giving of the Law" because it is believed that Moses received the Ten Commandments fifty days after Passover.

The phenomenon in the Upper Room occurred exactly fifty days after the resurrection, and is still celebrated and known as the Day of Pentecost. Those who have received the Upper Room experience and speak in other tongues are called Pentecostals. There are in excess of 500,000,000 Spirit filled believers on this globe right now!

The Feast of Pentecost is highly esteemed in Jewish circles to this very day.

It is against this backdrop that I want to talk about the initial outpouring of the Holy Spirit and what happened when the wind of the Holy Spirit blew into the Upper Room.

THE WIND BLEW GOD OUT AS A PLACE AND BLEW HIM IN AS A PRESENCE...

Throughout the Old Testament, one had to go to a certain place to worship God. The woman at the well made this very clear. John 4:20 (NKJV) "Our fathers worshiped on this mountain, and you Jews say that in Jerusalem is the place where one ought to worship." Jesus makes it clear that until this time, salvation was pretty much a national thing, and geographically God was believed to live in one place, behind the curtain, in the Temple. He hastens on to reveal that things were about to change! John 4:22-23 (NKJV) "Salvation is of the Jews. ^23 ^But the hour is coming, and now is, when the true worshipers will worship the Father in spirit and truth; for the Father is seeking such to worship Him."

Under the dispensation of law, one was considered very fortunate to live in close proximity to the Temple. If the worship place was within traveling distance one could go often, but the bulk of the people could only make the journey once a year. For many people, a trip to the Temple to meet with God was a pilgrimage of several days journey.

The reason the people were willing to make such a trip is because the Temple was where God lived and if one desired to commune with Him or have their sins rolled ahead another year the Temple was the only place that could accommodate such an occurrence!

In the Old Testament economy, God lived behind the curtain that separated the Holy Place form the Holy of Holies. If one wanted to worship or confess they had to buy or bring a sacrifice to the Temple and have the Priest go behind the curtain, into the Holy of Holies. There the Priest would petition the Lord to move the sin of the people forward, toward the day when Messiah would come.

God knew the world with its burgeoning tribes, advancing civilizations, and population explosions would eventually reach a point where it would be utterly impossible for everyone to descend on a temple in the tiny country of Israel for the purpose of worship and confession.
