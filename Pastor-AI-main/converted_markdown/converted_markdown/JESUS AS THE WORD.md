# JESUS AS THE WORD

Jesus is The Word -- John 1 "The Word was made flesh and dwelt among us..."

The miracle of the incarnation of Christ is that He was 100% God and 100% man simultaneously. Galatians 4:4 (KJV) "But when the fullness of time was come, God sent forth his Son, made of a woman, made under the law."

- God\'s Son became Mary's Baby...

- A child was born, but a son was given....

- The Lion of the Tribe of Judah became a lamb slain from the foundation of the world... (From everlasting to everlasting -- Eternal Priesthood after the order -- Before Abraham was, I am)

- An equal to God became a lowly servant of all mankind...

- The Ancient of Days became an infant born in the stable of Bethlehem...

- The Omnipotent became a dependent life to be fed, clothed and nourished by human hands...

- He placed himself in the hands of man, that man might place himself in the hands of God... (We are commanded to cast ourselves upon the mercies of God -- His mercies are new every morning)

- He often experienced hunger even though He is the Bread of Life...

- He used candles as a light in the darkness, but He gave light to the world three days before there was a son, moon, or stars...

- He walked, rode a donkey or sailed in a ship everywhere He went, yet He rides on the wings of the wind...

- In the Word He asked a woman of ill repute for a drink of water, even though He created the oceans and the fountains of the deep...

- In His earthly sojourn, He could only be in one place at a time, but He is God omnipresent...

- In His earthly visit He had to rely on the facts that He could see, but we know He is God omniscient...

- He wept at the tomb of His friend Lazarus, but then called Lazarus back to life...

- While He lived here, He tolerated injustice and aggression, but we know full well that He is going to tread out the wine press of the wrath of God and break mighty armies across His knees...

- He only lived thirty-three- and one-half years, yet He is the Father of Eternity...

- While He broke under the weight of a cross, we know He is God omnipotent...

- While He was led as a lamb to the slaughter, we know He was really the Lion of the Tribe of Judah...

- He died a cruel death on the cross, while simultaneously abolishing death all together...

- His mother called Him Jesus, but His father gave Him a name above all other names...

- 

- On His Mothers side He tolerated injustice and aggression, on His Fathers side He treads out the wine press of the wrath of God and breaks mighty armies across His knees...

- On His Mothers side He was Jesus, Mary's boy, on His Fathers side He is King of Kings and Lord of Lords...

- On His Mothers side He never had his name in lights, on His Fathers side He will reign forever...

- On His Mothers side He was never elected to high office, on His Fathers side He will rule over Heaven and Earth...

- On His Mothers side He was of no reputation, on His Fathers side every knee shall bow and every tongue shall confess He is Lord to the glory of God the Father...

As God, He knew the cross was the only way; as man He wondered if there might be a better way. Matthew 26:39-44 (NKJV) "He went a little farther and fell on His face, and prayed, saying, "O My Father, if it is possible, let this cup pass from Me; nevertheless, not as I will, but as You will." 40 Then He came to the disciples and found them asleep, and said to Peter, "What? Could you not watch with Me one hour? 41 Watch and pray, lest you enter into temptation. The spirit indeed is willing, but the flesh is weak." 42 Again, a second time, He went away and prayed, saying, "O My Father, if this cup cannot pass away from Me unless I drink it, Your will be done." 43 And He came and found them asleep again, for their eyes were heavy. 44 So He left them, went away again, and prayed the third time, saying the same words."

It is here in the Garden that we get a momentary glimpse into the humanity of Jesus, He was struggling with what He knew lay in His pathway:

- Betrayal...

- False accusations...

- Wrongful imprisonment...

- A beating that would have killed a weaker man... (Young American who vandalized some cars in Singapore received a caning)

- A crown of thorns that was intended to bring humiliation...

- Nails in His hands and feet that were intended to hold Him to the cross until dead...

- A spear in the side just to make sure He did not survive the ordeal of the cross...

- The weight of a sinful world resting squarely on His shoulders...

We must NEVER forget, Jesus was crucified as the sinless son of man rather than the sinless Son of God.

Look at this, God's Son prayed three times and God was silent each time! It is astounding to me that Father God does not utter one word in the Garden! In this passage of Scripture there is not even a hint of direction from Heaven as to whether there would be a last minute substitute! Think of this, many times the silence of God is the answer of God; we must go through the difficulty rather than be delivered from it!

The mistake we often make is that we assume the silence of God is a deafening statement of His inattention to the activities of our lives or worse, His disapproval of us on a personal level!

We should never mistake the silence of God for:

- Deafness...

- Unconcern...

- Inattention...

- Disapproval...

One of the most significant aspects of walking with God is to have a feel for His timing. Sometimes the silence of God is a matter of timing. Psalms 90:4 (NKJV) "For a thousand years in Your sight Are like yesterday when it is past, And like a watch in the night." 2 Peter 3:8 (NKJV) "But, beloved, do not forget this one thing, that with the Lord one day is as a thousand years, and a thousand years as one day."

My question is, "How could Jesus leave Heaven to come to earth and live as a mere mortal for thirty three and one half sinless years and then tolerate the silence of God on the eve of His crucifixion?" What brought Jesus through the most difficult night of His life; a night when God was silent? Hebrews 12:2 (NKJV) "Looking unto Jesus, the author and finisher of our faith, who for the joy that was set before Him endured the cross, despising the shame, and has sat down at the right hand of the throne of God."
