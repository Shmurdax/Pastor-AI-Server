Saul’s jealousy caused him to hate David, thus 
he pursued David to kill him. It did not help 
that the women were in the streets singing 
“Saul hath killed his thousands, David his ten 
thousands.” 

After a while David became weary with all the 
pressures of being pursued and he began to take 
matters into his own hands and actually hatched 
a plan to move his family and his followers to 
the land of the Philistines. 
 
During this time David had opportunity to kill 
Saul on two different occasions, but refused to 
do so. (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT 
A TIME) 
 

1. The first time David and his men were 
hiding in a cave and Saul came into the 
cave to use the restroom and when he 
removed his robe David cut a portion of 
the Kings garment off so that when Saul 
exited the cave he would realize that 
David had spared his life. 

2. The second time David and Abishai 
entered a cave where Saul and his men 
were sleeping and they took Saul’s spear 
and his canteen. Again David refused to 
kill him though Abishai begged for the 
privilege. I want to point out that though 
David did not kill Saul while he had the 
opportunity, he did feed the flesh by 
letting Saul know he could have killed 
him. On this occasion when David and 
Abishai left the cave and were a safe 

distance away they called to Saul and his 
army and taunted them with the spear and 
canteen. 
 
In the process of time David approached Achish 
King of Gath about asylum in the capital city.

After a proving period David asks Achish, King 
of Gath to allow he and his men to move to a 
small town called Ziklag. 1 Samuel 27:5-7 
(KJV) “And David said unto Achish, If I have 
now found grace in thine eyes, let them give 
me a place in some town in the country, that I 
may dwell there; for why should thy servant 

dwell in the royal city with thee? 6 Then 
Achish gave him Ziklag that day: wherefore 
Ziklag pertaineth unto the kings of Judah 
unto this day. 7 And the time that David dwelt 
in the country of the Philistines was a full year 
and four months.” 
 
Ziklag represents a hiding place or refuge 
outside the known will of God! Most of the 
time it represents the path of least resistance. 
 
Notice if you will, that David didn’t stay in 
Ziklag all the time, from time to time he would 
go onto Saul’s turf and when the pressure was 
on, he would return to Ziklag! 
 
After an undetermined amount of time, David 
was even willing to help Achish fight Saul! 
Remember, before Ziklag, David spared Saul’s 
life on two different occasions! 
 
When one chooses to live outside the will of 
God, everything changes. 
 
When people spend some time outside God’s 
will they become way more tolerant of things 
they once disapproved of doing. (The danger of 
the gradual – The little foxes spoil the vines) 

Look closely at the results of sixteen months of 
hiding, retreating, self-pity and living in fear.
