TEXT: John 14:1-3 (KJV) "Let not your heart be troubled: ye believe in God, believe also in me. In my Father's house are many mansions: if it were not so, I would have told you. I go to prepare a place for you. And if I go and prepare a place for you, I will come again, and receive you unto myself; that where I am, there ye may be also."

INTRO: Here in John chapter fourteen Jesus is conditioning His Disciples to the fact that He will be leaving them very soon. One of the things He say's to them which was specifically designed to bring cheer to their hearts and smiles to their faces is the promise, "I will come again." More than twenty centuries have come and gone and when I read those words it develops hope in my heart and causes a smile to break out on my face!

This world is in big trouble but let me explain to you that everything is working according to Gods calendar! A lot of people want to know what is about to happen and tonight I want to take a few minutes to tell you exactly what is about to transpire! ILLUSTRATION:

One of the great heroes of American history is a man who was born and raised in Little Rock, Arkansas; his name is General Douglas McArthur. During World War II, his leadership ability came to the forefront as he was placed in charge of the South Pacific Theatre and was stationed in the Philippines.

During the later days of the war, General McArthur's men had taken a beating so he withdrew them and moved them to Australia to recover. As he boarded the DC 9 in the Philippine it was obvious to General McArthur that the Philippine population was anxious about their future.

Standing there noticing the fear and anxiousness on the faces of the Philippine people he spoke three words that have been immortalized in American history, "I shall return!"

Several months of war continued but one day the Philippine skies were filled with the hum of military planes as General McArthur made good on his promise to return to the Philippines!

A little over 2,000 years ago, Jesus stood on a mountain over-looking that wonderful city of Jerusalem and said to his beloved disciples, John 14:1-3 (NKJV) "Let not your heart be troubled; you believe in God, believe also in Me. "In My Father's house are many mansions; if it were not so, I would have told you. I go to prepare a place for you. "And if I go and prepare a place for you, I will come again and receive you to Myself; that where I am, there you may be also."

ILLUSTRATION:

"The Wonderful Window."

A London clerk worked in a run-down, drab, depressing, part of the city. This clerk refused to allow his outlook to determine his happiness so one day he bought a picture with an inspiring scene and installed it in the window of his office that overlooked the worst part of the city. The picture was of beautiful castles and towers, green rolling hills, beautiful parks and lovely homes. In the picture a Knight rode a stallion through the streets and watched over the people with pride.

As the clerk worked long hours at the tedious task of making everything balance he felt as though he was working for the Knight in the picture. This produced a sense of honor, dignity, and hope. As he looked at the picture of what could be rather than looking at what is, the picture changed his life!"

There is no need for us to allow our circumstances or surroundings discourage us! God has placed us where we are but He assured us He would "Come again!" (PLAY RAPTURE VIDEO)

We need to quit looking at the drab scenes, and look at the promises He left us!
