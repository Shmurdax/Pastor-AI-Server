## I. THE CONSEQUENCES OF A
FAMINE IN THE CHURCH 
Bethlehem Judah had probably been home 
to Elimelech, Naomi and their son’s for 
most, if not all of their lives. The reason I 
believe Bethlehem Judah represents the 
church is that its very name means “The 
house of bread”! 
 
Historically bread has always been the one 
thing that was an indicator of the presence of 
God. In Old Testament worship bread 
occupied a very important place in the form 
of the showbread. This showbread was 

called "The bread of The Presence". 
(LEAVE ON SCREEN UNTIL NEXT 
SLIDE) 
 
Elimelech, Naomi and their family had no 
doubt developed lifelong relationships, and 
probably never had a thought of living 
anywhere other than Bethlehem. Yet, the 
Word of God tells us that this family of four 
pulled up stakes and moved to Moab. This 
is no small decision; it is a life shift. This 
decision necessitated: 
• Leaving their family 
• Leaving their property 
• Leaving their neighborhood 
• Leaving their school 
• Leaving their doctor 
• Leaving their synagogue 
• Leaving their Wal-Mart 
 
The reason given for this radical life shift 
was because “there was a famine in the 

land.” A famine will cause one to make 
unthinkable, irrational, unbelievable choices. 
 
The very land that had been known for 
raising the necessary ingredients for making 
and manufacturing bread could no longer 
sustain its residents, because it had become 
barren. 
 
Moab is a type of the world, in essence 
Elimelech, Naomi and their two sons 
backslid and moved to Moab. Friends that 
is absolutely tragic. What’s more tragic than 
the move they made is why they made the 
move, there was “no bread in the house of 
bread.” 
 
The Pentecostal Church in America has been 
in the midst of a famine; there is “not 
enough bread in the house of bread.” 
Think of the churches who at one time 
feasted at the table of God, ate bread at the 
Lord’s table, and for whatever reason they 

have decided to move to Moab. (You don’t 
have to believe to belong – We don’t give 
altar calls because we don’t want to offend 
our clients – My experience in looking for a 
church for Tim & Constance Carnell to 
attend) 
 
I want to ask Pastors and church leaders, if 
the world came to your door tonight, what 
would they discover? Would they find fresh 
baked bread from the ovens of Heaven, or 
would they discover offices full of recipes 
for bread, cold ovens, and dusty barren 
shelves? 
 
The very best some churches have to offer to 
those who wander through their doors in 
search of bread is to give them permission to 
search through the carpet for a few crumbs 
of yester-year's revivals. The crumbs will 
NEVER satisfy the hungry soul! 

Just as the light burning continually in the 
Temple was the responsibility of the Old 
Testament Priest, pastor, church leader, your 
ultimate responsibility is to keep the bread 
baking! 
 
Is it possible that we have become so 
preoccupied with sweeping out imaginary 
crumbs from bakeries with no bread that we 
are not even aware that we are in the midst 
of a famine? 
 
God wants to restore the sense of His 
awesome manifest presence in our lives and 
places of worship. Over and over we talk 
about the glory of God covering the earth, 
but how is His Glory going to flow through 
the streets of our cities if it can't even flow 
down the aisles of our churches? 
 
If we are going to have bread in the house of 
bread we must start somewhere, and it's not 
going to start out there, it must start in here! 

For the presence of God to be rediscovered 
in the Church four things must happen: 
(PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A 
TIME) (START THROWING BREAD) 
• Someone must pay the price to have 
bread in the house of bread 
• We must be willing to lay aside our 
agenda, and become willing to abide by 
His agenda, and His alone 
• We must be willing to be a consumer 
and distributor of the bread of His 
presence 
• We must be hungry “He hath filled the 
hungry with good things, the rich He 
hath sent empty away.” 
 
Ron Phillips stated just recently that there 
are more people being filled with the Holy 
Spirit in Baptist churches in America than 
there are in the Pentecostal Church! Ezekiel 
wrote: "...I saw water coming out from 
under the threshold of the door of the 

Temple..." (Ezek. 47:1) Susan recently 
released a word over our Church, “The 
drought is over”. 
 
Are we going to allow a famine to continue 
to rob us of the ability to raise the necessary 
ingredients to manufacture bread? Are we 
going to be satisfied to talk about bread, and 
distribute our products as if they have been 
rationed? 
 
Or are we going to rise up by faith with 
bulldog determination and build a bakery 
that manufactures fresh bread? 
 

## II. THE HIGH COST OF
BACKSLIDING 
The priority of God's presence has been lost 
in the majority of the modern church. We're 
like bakeries that are open, but have no 
bread. 

When there is no bread in the house of bread 
backsliding will accelerate. People will 
walk away disillusioned, many of them will 
never return. Are we willing to take that 
risk? 
 
Elimelech, Naomi and their boy’s didn’t just 
wake up one morning and decide to throw 
all their belongings in the back of a wagon 
and move from Bethlehem to Moab! They 
probably sat around and discussed their 
options. (Life and death is in the power of 
the tongue!) 
 
Elimelech, Naomi and their son’s lived in a 
backslidden condition for ten years. A 
family of four persons left Bethlehem and 
only one of them ever returned. Elimelech, 
Mahlon and Chilion never came home they 
died in Moab! 
 
Pastors and church leaders, if people 
backslide because of a famine in our church, 

Church leaders will be held personally 
responsible for the lack of bread in the 
house! (LEAVE ON THE SCREEN

## III. RUMORS OF BREAD REACH
MOAB 
Naomi discovered: (PLACE ON THE 
SCREEN ONE AT A TIME) 
• Moab is a cruel place 
• Moab will steal your children from you 
and bury them before their time 
• Moab will cause you to embrace things 
you know are wrong 
• Moab will rob you of the vitality of life 
 
In the final analysis, all that Naomi had left 
were two daughters-in-law whom she had 
known only ten years or less. With nothing 
but a gloomy and disastrous future staring 
her in the face, she told them, "You might 

as well not hang around me either. I don't 
have any more sons to give you." 
 
Some of the most heartening words in this 
Book are found in Ruth 1:6 “...she had 
heard in the country of Moab how that the 
Lord had visited his people in giving them 
bread.” 
 
There is an information grapevine that winds 
its way through every community, hamlet, 
and city of the world. It winds its way down 
all our coasts, over every mountain range, 
and into every place where men and women 
dwell. It is the "grapevine of the hungry." 
 
The news of fresh bread in the house of 
bread will leapfrog from one household to 
the other, from one place to another almost 
instantaneously. The news will break, and 
people will start coming out of the 
woodwork. The backsliders will come back 
to the church en masse! (My prediction of 

the future of the Pentecostal church – It is 
going to be difficult for preachers who have 
made a production of leaving the house of 
bread to walk it back) 
 
The first thing one must do to build a 
Pentecostal church is to make sure there is 
bread in the house of bread. 
 
When Naomi returned to Bethlehem she 
brought Ruth with her. Ruth was a 
Moabitess. When the rumors of bread start 
circulating, the backsliders will return and 
they will bring the world with them.
