# FIVE-FOLD CHURCH
 

TEXT: Ephesians 4:11-16 (NKJV) “And He 
Himself gave some to be apostles, some 
prophets, some evangelists, and some pastors 
and teachers, 12 for the equipping of the saints 
for the work of ministry, for the edifying of the 
body of Christ, 13 till we all come to the unity of 
the faith and of the knowledge of the Son of 
God, to a perfect man, to the measure of the 
stature of the fullness of Christ; 14 that we 
should no longer be children, tossed to and fro 
and carried about with every wind of doctrine, 
by the trickery of men, in the cunning 
craftiness of deceitful plotting, 15 but, speaking 
the truth in love, may grow up in all things into 
Him who is the head—Christ— 16 from whom 
the whole body, joined and knit together by 
what every joint supplies, according to the 
effective working by which every part does its 
share, causes growth of the body for the 
edifying of itself in love.” 
 

INTRO: I think we can all agree that the First 
Century Church was a healthy, effective and 
powerful church. I hope we can all agree that we 
want to attend a healthy, effective and powerful 
church. 
 
Though times and culture have changed over the 
past twenty-one century’s it is my opinion that 
the words of the Apostle Paul to the Ephesians 
is still relevant in the twenty first century 
church. What it took to build a healthy, effective 
and powerful church in the first century, the 
same will be required in the twenty first century. 
 
The Apostle Paul makes his case that the only 
way for the body of Christ to be healthy, 
effective and powerful is to operate in the five-
fold ministry model. Listen to what he says will 
be the outgrowth of such a church: 
•  Unity in the faith… (“It is a good and 
pleasant thing for brethren to dwell 
together in unity”) 

•  A more complete knowledge of Jesus… 
(The reason this occurs? In a five-fold 
ministry model we do life together and you 
can know what I know, and I can know what 
you know) 
•  Steady movement toward perfection… (We 
do not know what He is like, but when we 
see Him, we shall be like Him) 
•  We will no longer be driven about by fads 
and heresy… (“Believe His Word and you 
will be established – Believe His prophets 
and you will prosper.” – In the five-fold 
model we are submitted to our head – Jesus! 
Ecclesia – A community of called believers 
who are submitted to no King but Jesus - 
We are submitted to those whom He has 
placed over us – The apostles, prophets, 
evangelists, pastors, and teachers) 
•  Learn to speak the truth in love… (Jesus 
came “Full of grace and truth.”) 
 
When we get up under the authority of Jesus 
and His delegated authorities, the apostles, 

prophets, evangelists, pastors, and teachers, we 
become equipped to do the work of the 
ministry! Sounds like a healthy, effective and 
powerful church to me! 
 
While the First-Century Church was a healthy, 
effective and powerful church, I am afraid the 
modern-day, westernized, American church has 
become a sick, anemic and weak church, that 
has no power and little influence in culture. In 
the early 1800’s almost all the universities were 
religious institutions of higher learning, and 
along with the church, they set the culture – The 
educational system was surrendered to 
modernism and far left think tanks, while the 
church retreated into isolation and forfeited its 
ministry model of the apostles, prophets, 
evangelists, pastors and teachers – in so doing, 
the church surrendered its right to have a say in 
culture! The results? 
•  Separation of church and state… 
•  Prayer has been taken out of the educational 
system… 

•  The Bible is no longer welcome in the 
public square… 
•  Abortion is not just the law of the land, late 
term and partial birth abortion is about to 
become the wave of the future… 
•  The name Jesus has been extricated from 
American culture… 
•  The ten commandments have become an 
affront to the American way of life… 
•  The gay lifestyle and agenda has become 
mainstream on main street… 
 
All the while the Westernized American church 
sits idly by with no say, no seat at the table, and 
no influence. 
 
Why? And how could this happen? I believe it 
is at least in part due to the denial of the five-
fold ministry offered to the New Testament 
Church by Jesus, but rejected by ministers, 
churches and denominations. (We are a non-
profit organization) 
 

Even those who claim to embrace the five-fold 
ministry of the church, have little idea of what it 
should look like in the church and absolutely no 
idea of what it should look like in the 
marketplace. 
 
We, the westernized American Church have 
decided: 
•  Apostles are not for the church today… 
•  Prophets are not for the church today… 
 
This means we are not five-fold, we are three-
fold! 
 
We have defined the evangelist as someone who 
travels around the country conducting revival 
crusades. Then we have decided not to have 
revivals!!! These people are not evangelists, 
they are revivalists! I only know two, what I 
believe to be true evangelists, who are full time, 
traveling around conducting soul winning 
crusades! 
 

We are not five-fold – We are also not three-
fold – We are two-fold! 
 
The office of the teacher has become almost 
extinct in the modern church. Most churches no 
longer have Sunday School, and I am not 
advocating we go back there. (Most of what I 
knew when I entered the ministry I learned in a 
Sunday School class watching Bible characters 
being placed on a flannelgraph board) Most 
churches also do not have a discipleship 
program, whether it be a training hour sometime 
during the week, or small groups where people 
gather and receive instruction! 
 
We are not five-fold – We are not three-fold – 
We are not two-fold – We are one-fold! The 
power of the modern-day church can be likened 
to a five-cylinder motor running on one cylinder 
– As a result, we should not be too surprised 
that the westernized American church doesn’t 
have enough power to pull the hat off of our 
heads! 

Please allow me to ask the following questions: 
If the office of Prophet and Apostle ended with 
the with the First Century Church… 
•  Are there no twenty-first century 
evangelists? 
•  Are there no twenty-first century pastors? 
•  Are there no twenty-first century teachers? 
•  Are there no twenty-first century Christians? 
 
If God had not replaced each of the Patriarchs as 
they died off, Isaac would not have followed 
Abraham, Jacob would not have followed Isaac, 
etc… Joshua would not have followed Moses, 
David would not have followed Saul, or how 
about the prophets, Elisha would never have felt 
the cloak of Elijah fall upon his shoulders. 
 
The problem is that we have made the office of 
Apostle and Prophet something of a shrine, 
bigger than what we can fill outside of the 
original apostles. While there is no way to place 

too much importance upon the work the original 
Apostles did in establishing the Church, we are 
not to worship them. In fact, anyone who 
attempted to worship them because of the 
miracles, signs and wonders flowing from their 
hands, they made they made them rise up and 
treat them as mere men. We are not to worship 
these offices nor those who are supernaturally 
gifted in these offices, we are to worship God 
alone. 
 
I stand to remind all who believe the office of 
Apostle and Prophet ceased in the First-Century 
Church that the original day of Pentecost is 
believed to have occurred between 29 and 33 
A.D. According to scholars, Paul wrote 
Ephesians (The text I am preaching out of) 
somewhere around 62 A.D. I would think if the 
office of Apostle and Prophet were done away 
with that Paul would not be submitting these 
offices to the Church so late in the church’s 
history. 

What would it be like if God failed to replace 
these offices with each generation? To answer 
this question all one must do is study the dark 
ages between the Old Testament and the New 
Testament. The dark ages was a four hundred 
year span where Israel did not hear from God, 
there was no open vision from God, and there 
were no ministry offices that were functioning 
among them. This continued until the angel 
appeared the father of John the Baptist in the 
Temple with the announcement that Messiah 
would come to bring light into the darkness. 
 
I am afraid the refusal among church leaders to 
embrace a five-fold ministry model, has all but 
plunged the twenty-first century church into a 
very dark age. The model of the modern-day 
church is a Pastor (s) who leads the church and 
does all the work while the people assume the 
position of spectators and cry FEED ME! We 
have truly become a sick, anemic, weak church 

that cannot save ourselves, let alone anyone 
else.

Most church buildings are used one time on 
Sunday and MAYBE on Wednesday! This is a 
terrible use of real estate! I’m not suggesting 
that we sell our buildings, I’m advocating that 
we use them – Church property should be used 
seven days a week (PLAY BETA VIDEO) – 
Find a way to get the community onto the 
property multiple times per week. 
 
On the other hand, if the only thing we are using 
the property for is a service or two a week, the 
property should be sold and the church should 
rent a building and use the extra revenue to give 

to missions, send evangelists, and plant 
churches! 
 
In 2001 Peter Wagner predicted “The five-fold 
ministry gifts will be restored to the twenty-first 
century church.” (Sister Eubanks) 
 
(Describe the five-fold ministry gifts using the 
hand – Start with the thumb – Apostle – Prophet 
– Evangelist – Pastor - Teacher) 
•  The thumb represents the apostle – The 
thumb is the shortest and largest of all the 
fingers. The thumb is the only finger that 
can comfortably touch all the other fingers. 
The thumb enables the hand to grip, and 
when the hand is closed in the formation of 
a fist (Defensive or offensive posture), the 
thumb covers all the fingers. The (Thumb) 
apostle has a role in literally everything the 
(Hand) church does. Apostles set up 
government structures – Apostles form 
kingdom culture. Apostles have authority 
over churches, cities, regions and countries – 

Apostles attract resources – Doors 
automatically open to apostles – Apostles 
can make decisions with limited information 
and they are quick to make such decisions. 
Apostles bring revelation. Apostles in the 
generic sense are those sent to pioneer 
something new. For example, they are often 
called in to turn failing ministries around. 
They are missional from the word go. They 
plant churches and ministries almost 
effortlessly. They are either launching 
something new or tinkering with old systems 
to give new life to it. If they can’t find a way 
to give new life to an old system they boldly 
take it out behind the barn and shoot it! 
Apostles start movements. They become the 
architects of systems. In the marketplace 
they turn around failing businesses, invent 
things, and start entrepreneurial business 
ventures. 
•  The index finger represents the prophet. The 
index finger is involved in turning the page 
and is also involved in direction, as in 

pointing the way. The prophet is the gift that 
should be utilized in giving direction to the 
church for forward motion. Prophets have a 
keen sense of knowing and can see things no 
one else can see. Prophets bring 
proclamation. When prophets speak to the 
corporate body they change the atmosphere 
and take the church to a new dimension. 
When prophets speak, yesterday’s ceiling 
becomes today’s floor. Prophets inform 
kingdom culture. Prophets hear what others 
do not hear, and see what others tend to 
miss. They foretell and forthtell. Prophets 
inspire in the people faithfulness to the 
Word of God, and adherence to the promises 
of God. Prophets bring hope for holding 
onto a promise until its delivery date. The 
prophet must be submitted to the authority 
of a local church and a local pastor. A 
prophet who is willing to do this is an 
inhouse prophet. Any prophet who is 
unwilling to submit in this way is an 
outhouse prophet. In the marketplace 

prophets speak hopeful messages to those 
who don’t have a clue about the things of 
God. As they do this repeatedly, the 
recipients begin to wonder and question how 
this believer can know these things, often 
before they happen. This gives the prophet 
an opportunity to point them to Jesus and 
disciple the individual. 
•  The middle finger represents the evangelist. 
(If you are a pastor you have wanted to 
show a so-called evangelist your middle 
finger a time or two – Brother Brankel, 
“I’ve only been a pastor three months and I 
already hate evangelists”) The third finger 
on the hand is the longest finger. The reach 
of the evangelist should be greater than any 
other gift. Evangelists are gatherers. 
Evangelists not only win souls, they equip 
the local church to do the same. An 
evangelist has to win souls or they are not 
fulfilled. Rebecca said, “Give me children 
or I might as well die.” This is the cry of 
every true evangelist. An evangelist is a 

gatherer of people. A true evangelist is a 
natural born recruiter to the cause. Most of 
the time an evangelist is a naturally 
infectious person who is able to win people 
to the Kingdom by finding unique ways of 
presenting the gospel. In the church a true 
evangelist inspires churches to have soul 
winning meetings where dozens if not 
hundreds come to faith in Christ. They build 
systems called nets that can be easily 
implemented in the church and utilize 
people to do so who do not have an 
evangelistic bone in their body. The gift of 
the evangelist is an indoor/outdoor gift – it is 
needed in the church but should primarily 
operate outside the four walls of the church. 
In the marketplace evangelists are 
particularly gifted at enthusing others about 
what they stand for, selling the significance 
of their work, company, or product outside 
the group itself. Evangelists are 
salespersons. The key is to get them, in the 
course of their work and relationships in the 

world, to use their sales skills to introduce a 
lost world to Jesus. We must not forget the 
middle finger is strategically located 
between the index and ring finger. The 
evangelist should be submitted and 
accountable to a local church and pastor. I 
refuse to have an evangelist who is not a 
part of a local church and submitted to the 
authority of a pastor. It is imperative that the 
gift of the evangelist be intimately 
connected to the authority of the prophet and 
pastor. Every evangelist should have a 
pastor and prophet speaking into their lives. 
•  The ring finger represents the pastor. The 
pastor is married to the church. The pastor is 
the protector of the church. The pastor 
brings nutrition (Feeds). The pastor loves 
the church as his own body. The pastor sets 
the spiritual temperature in the church. The 
pastor NEVER runs from danger (My 
experience with a guy who said “The Lord 
told me that it is time for you to move on.”) 
Pastors are called to nurture spiritual 

development, maintain communal health, 
and engender loving community among the 
people of God. Pastors are natural born care 
givers. They have a gentle bedside manner. 
Pastors can sit patiently and listen to people 
drone on and on about their issues, and 
never lose interest! When someone sneeze’s 
a pastor is there to wipe their nose. Pastors 
have a unique ability to affirm people and 
hold them up as they walk through 
weaknesses. Pastors create community and 
bring healing to the body of Christ. The ring 
finger is strategically located between the 
middle finger (Evangelist), and the little 
finger (The teacher). No pastor will be 
effective without being connected to those 
who reach people and those who disciple 
people. Without an evangelist and teacher 
there will be no one to pastor. In the 
marketplace pastors encircle themselves 
with needy people and figure out a way to 
bring hope, healing and health to the 
situation. They gather people around causes. 

They give leadership in difficult times and 
situations. They are always a part of the 
solution and never a part of the problem. 
They use their abilities to point people to 
Christ and His church. 
•  The little finger (Pinky) represents the 
teacher. The pinky, though small is 
extremely important. The pinky can go 
where none of the other fingers are 
comfortable going. (Ear – Eye – Nose – Just 
kidding) When reading, we often use the 
pinky to mark our place or to highlight a 
passage. The teacher is vital to the success 
of the church (Training – Discipleship – 
Small groups). The teacher brings 
explanation. Teachers are those who are 
effective trainers and inspirers of learning. 
They are philosophers, thinkers, people who 
understand ideas and how they shape human 
life. The teacher mediates wisdom and 
understanding. In the church this 
philosophical type brings comprehensive 
understanding of the revelation of God’s 

Word that has been bequeathed to the 
church. They spend incredible amounts of 
time digging out unique passages and 
revelatory words. Then they simplistically 
deliver these truths to the body of Christ in a 
way that is easy to understand. The pinky is 
ALWAYS overshadowed and inferior 
(Submissive) to the ring finger. (Story of a 
teacher who decided to give the church a 
new revelation and was struck dead at 47 
years old – In his class – In front of his 
students) In the marketplace these 
individuals find ways to utilize their gifts to 
deliver all kinds of educational material. 
They also find ways to unwrap the mysteries 
of God and His Gospel to a world that is 
Biblically illiterate. 
 
A tragic truth is that most churches and 
denominations do not adequately embrace each 
of these ministry gifts. If this is not being 
highlighted in the church there is a zero percent 
chance that these gifts are operating outside the 

church. I believe these gifts were intended for 
the modern-day church, but I am equally 
convinced that they are intended for the 
marketplace. These gifts were given to the 
church, “For the perfecting of the saints, for 
the work of the ministry.” These gifts are 
indoor/outdoor gifts. They perfect the saints – 
The saints are to learn to function in them and 
then take them on the road - in the marketplace! 
 

CONCLUSION: 
I believe: 
•  Apostles beget apostles… 
•  Prophets beget prophets… 
•  Evangelists beget evangelists… 
•  Pastors beget pastors… 
•  Teachers beget teachers… 
 
Procreation was a good idea God had for filling 
up the world and I believe the procreation of 
these gifts in the church and marketplace will 
fill up the church!
