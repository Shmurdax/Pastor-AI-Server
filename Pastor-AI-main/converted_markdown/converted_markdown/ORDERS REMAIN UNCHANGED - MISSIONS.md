INTRO: In 1921, an unknown World War I American soldier was buried
in Arlington National Cemetery. Later the bodies of unknown soldiers
from World War II, the Korean Conflict, and the Vietnam War were
entombed at this location as well.

The burial site of these soldiers is known as the "Tomb of the Unknown
Soldier" and has become a major tourist attraction in our Nations
Capital. I have always been intrigued by the pomp and circumstance that
surrounds this tourist attraction.

Several years ago I had the opportunity to visit Arlington National
Cemetery and the "Tomb of the Unknown Soldier." (VIDEO CLIP)

At the "Tomb of the Unknown Soldier," every hour on the hour, 365 days
a year, a new soldier reports for duty. After an hour a relief soldier
comes up to take over for the next hour. They face each other and the
soldier going off duty says something to the new guard who is coming
onto duty.

As I sat in awed silence and watched the ceremony that surrounds the
changing of the guard I was struck by the fact that the soldier going
off duty whispers something to the soldier coming on duty. As a result
of my curiosity I did a little research and discovered that only three
words are spoken, and they are "Orders Remain Unchanged." (PLACE
GRAPHIC FOR SERMON ON ALL THREE SCREENS - LEAVE THIS SLIDE UP UNTIL TIME
FOR THE NEXT SLIDE)

The Tomb of the Unknown Soldier was dedicated on Armistice Day, November
11, 1921, and has had a military guard continually since 1937. Think of
this for the past 76 plus years this Tomb has been guarded:

- Day and night...

- Rain or shine...

- Hot or cold...

- Storm and calm...

When hurricane Isabel hit the eastern seaboard in September of 2003 the
Soldiers who were on duty to guard this Tomb faithfully carried out
their duties in spite of the fact that for the first time in 66 years,
the military powers that be, gave their permission for the tomb to go
unguarded during the time the storm was at its peak.

The original orders were given on November 11, 1921. The orders were to;
"Guard with your life the memory and honor of these particular
soldiers and generally all the soldiers in Arlington National
Cemetery!" This order does not need to be restated; it is simply to be
carried out as each soldier relays the message to the next, Orders
Remain Unchanged. (LEAVE THIS SLIDE UP UNTIL TIME FOR THE NEXT
SLIDE)

suggestion, it is a directive! A military order is not to be voted on by
the enlisted men or the officers, it is an edict that if not carried
out, bears severe consequences for those who are derelict in their
duties!

carried out by those of us who have enlisted in His service!

## II. ORDERS REMAIN...

In the military, once an order has been given it remains in effect until
another order is given that alters, rescinds or supersedes the original
order! One order can last minutes or decades depending on the magnitude
and scope of the order and on whether it is followed by additional
orders. The order to guard the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier has continued
for more than three quarters of a century, ninety-two years, two months,
and eight days to be exact!

Remember, this tomb has had a constant military guard since November 11,

1937. Think of this, there has not been one moment pass that this tomb
has not been guarded for the past: (PLACE ON THE SCREEN ONE AT A
TIME)

- 950 months...

- 4,420 weeks...

- 28,840 days...

- 692,150 hours...

- 41,528,900 minutes... (M)

- 2,491,733,400 seconds... (T)

Our Commander In Chief gave the original orders to eleven disciples who
were, in turn, supposed to pass along the orders to succeeding
generations! If the orders of the Commander In Chief have remained the
same since they were given, every generation has been responsible for
carrying out the orders in their day.

Never before in the history of the world has one generation been given

## III. ORDERS REMAIN UNCHANGED!

In the military, when an order is given no person of an inferior ranking
has the power to alter or change the original order! Once the order is
given, for it to be altered, rescinded, or superseded an officer of
higher rank must give the revised order. The Captain of the Lords Host
gave us our orders and it is not possible for a higher ranking person to
speak into our lives! Mark 16:15 (KJV) "And he said unto them, Go ye
into all the world, and preach the gospel to every creature."
Matthew 28:19 (KJV) "Go ye therefore, and teach all nations,
baptizing them in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the
Holy Ghost:" No one has the rank or authority to change the Orders;
they are to "Remain Unchanged!" Whether we carry out His orders or
not, they remain unchanged.
