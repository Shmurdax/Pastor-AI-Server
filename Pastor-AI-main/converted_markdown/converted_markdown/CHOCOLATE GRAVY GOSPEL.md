THE BLIND MAN

As a scriptural backdrop for talking about the goodness of God, I would like to use the passage out of John 9 where Jesus healed the man who was born blind -- This is a great example of the goodness of God.

Let me break this Scripture down for us:

- This man was born blind -- He had never seen the light of day

- The disciples wanted to know whether this man being born blind was the fault of the blind man or his parents -- Human nature is to blame every issue on someone -- How could the blindness have been the fault of the blind man? He was born blind -- Religious spirits always confuse the issues

- Jesus said, "Neither this man nor his parents sinned, but that the works of God should be revealed in him."

- Jesus spit on the ground and made clay of the spittle and placed it in his eyes and said, "Go wash in the pool of Siloam." Experiencing the goodness of God oftentimes requires radical obedience on our part  

- The blind man did as he was instructed -- As ridiculous as it seemed -- And the Bible says that He came forth seeing!" Sometimes you have to do the ridiculous to receive the miraculous

- End of the story -- Right? -- The goodness of God manifested itself in the blind man receiving his sight -- Everyone ends up living happily ever after -- Right? -- Not so!

- As soon as the blind man was healed the questions started -- Those who previously knew him said, "Is this in fact the blind man?" They said, "He is like him, but this is not him." The goodness of God will bring about such a change that those who know us best, will have a difficult time recognizing us!

- The blind man said, "I am in fact the blind man!" -- This is how the fight got started -This is how it always starts -- Everyone is okay with us while we are in our struggle -- The goodness of God makes religious people nervous!

- Those who previously knew the blind man said, "How were your eyes opened?" ^11 ^He answered and said, "A Man called Jesus made clay and anointed my eyes and said to me, 'Go to the pool of Siloam and wash.' So I went and washed, and I received sight." ^12 ^Then they said to him, "Where is He?" He said, "I do not know." ^13 ^They brought him who formerly was blind to the Pharisees." The goodness of God caused the friends of the blind man to turn him into the religious authorities! The very people who should have been celebrating with him were setting him up for prosecution -- We shouldn't be too surprised when the goodness of God is revealed in our lives, that the people who should be celebrating with us are disgusted by us!

- Rather than celebrating the goodness of God with the blind man, the Pharisees accused Jesus of breaking the law and started calling Jesus names including "Sinner"

- The Pharisees asked the blind man how Jesus had healed him and what he thought of Jesus - The blind man told them the story of his healing and declared Jesus to be "A prophet"

- The Pharisees believed they were involved in a bait and switch -- They did not believe that this was actually the blind man -- They thought he was a lookalike -- So they called the blind man's parents into an inquisition - "Is this your son, who you say was born blind? How then does he now see? ^20 ^His parents answered them and said, "We know that this is our son, and that he was born blind; ^21 ^but by what means he now sees we do not know, or who opened his eyes we do not know. - When we are convinced of the goodness of God, this is a great way to get crossways with religious people -- "So they again called the man who was blind, and said to him, "Give God the glory! We know that this Man is a sinner." ^25 ^He answered and said, "Whether He is a sinner or not I do not know. - This ratcheted up the temperature of the conversation -- when we do not back off of our claims of the goodness of God, we run the risk of raising the ire of truly religious people

- "Then they reviled (Insulted -- Berated -- Badgered) him and said, "You are His disciple, but we are Moses' disciples. ^29 ^We know that God spoke to Moses; as for this fellow, we do not know where He is from." The Pharisees were more interested in their history and theology that the goodness of God -- This is always true of religious spirits

- "The man answered and said to them, "Why, this is a marvelous thing, that you do not know where He is from; yet He has opened my eyes! ^31 ^Now we know that God does not hear sinners; but if anyone is a worshiper of God and does His will, He hears him. ^32 ^Since the world began it has been unheard of that anyone opened the eyes of one who was born blind. ^33 ^If this Man were not from God, He could do nothing." The sighted blind man knew more about rightly dividing the goodness of God that the educated idiots known as the Pharisees

The issue here is that the blind man, though he tried desperately, could not adequately describe the goodness of God! The goodness of God must be experienced! "Oh, taste and see that the Lord is good."
