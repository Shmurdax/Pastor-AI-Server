INTRO: December is my favorite time of year to preach a series. There is so much material in the Christmas story, that I don't know if one could ever exhaust the supply of information.

Always before, I have focused on the events surrounding the birth of Jesus, but this year I want to take a walk through His life -- Beginning with His conception, and ending with His office as eternal intercessor. In doing so, I want to focus on His life using the word, "GREATER".

THE CONCEPTION OF THE CHRISTCHILD WAS GREAT

The Prophet Isaiah writing seven hundred years before the Birth of Christ made the bold prediction that... "A virgin shall conceive..."

What? You heard it right... "A virgin shall conceive..." Can you imagine what the people to whom Isaiah was speaking, thought of such a comment? Never before in the history of the world was the conception of a child initiated in such a manner.

After such an outlandish prediction, Isaiah wouldn't be proven correct in his lifetime -- In fact, this prophecy wouldn't be fulfilled for more than seven centuries! (I like my prophecies to come to pass a little sooner)

This Christmas story begins with the miracle of the conception of the Christ Child, and ends with His Glorious Resurrection. Paul tells the Corinthian Christians, "If Christ be not raised, your preaching is in vain, your faith is in vain, and you are yet in your sins."

Listen to what the Angel said to Mary:

The virgin's name was Mary. ^28 ^And having come in, the angel said to her, "Rejoice, highly favored one, the Lord is with you; blessed are you among women!" ^29 ^But when she saw him, she was troubled at his saying, and considered what manner of greeting this was. ^30 ^Then the angel said to her, "Do not be afraid, Mary, for you have found favor with God. ^31 ^And behold, you will conceive in your womb and bring forth a Son, and shall call His name Jesus. ^32 ^He will be great, and will be called the Son of the Highest; and the Lord God will give Him the throne of His father David. ^33 ^And He will reign over the house of Jacob forever, and of His kingdom there will be no end." ^34 ^Then Mary said to the angel, "How can this be, since I do not know a man?" ^35 ^And the angel answered and said to her, "The Holy Spirit will come upon you, and the power of the Highest will overshadow you; therefore, also, that Holy One who is to be born will be called the Son of God. ^36 ^Now indeed, Elizabeth your relative has also conceived a son in her old age; and this is now the sixth month for her who was called barren. ^37 ^For with God nothing will be impossible."

- Matthew 1:20-21 (NKJV) "An angel of the Lord appeared to him in a dream, saying, "Joseph, son of David, do not be afraid to take to you Mary your wife, for that which is conceived in her is of the Holy Spirit. ^21 ^And she will bring forth a Son, and you shall call His name Jesus, for He will save His people from their sins."

Of the twenty billion people who have called planet Earth home, only one baby has ever been born without the DNA of an earthly father.

As insane as it sounds, Jesus was born of a Virgin Birth.

To begin with, this very detail sets Him apart from every other imposter who has ever claimed to be, or will ever claim to be, the Messiah. In the words of this current culture, "You just can't make this stuff up."

The Virgin Birth is the initial flash point of a life that would be marked by the miraculous and the supernatural.

To many people the Virgin Birth is too fantastic for them to believe. In such a case, those who can't get their head around the Virgin Birth, can never believe His sacrifice on Calvary can forgive sin. The bedrock of our faith is believing in His Virgin Birth.

INTRO: Last week we talked about the Conception of the Christ Child being GREAT. We also talked about the Birth of the Christ Child being GREATER than His Conception.

HIS BIRTH WAS GREAT -- BUT HIS SINLESS LIFE WAS GREATER

Think of this... 33 ½ sinless years! Hebrews 4:15 "He was tempted in all points, as we are. Yet, He did not sin!"

Tell me if anyone in here has been able to live to this point in 2023 without committing a sin? How about the last six months? How about the last thirty days? How about the last week?

33 ½ sinless years! His conception and Virgin Birth was great, but 33 ½ sinless years had to be GREATER!

Through His sinless life we witness:

- All the strength of His Deity residing in His spirit...

- All the power of God being invested into His hands...

- All the people of God being committed to His safe keeping...

- All the direction of God for road weary travelers being found in His steps...

- All the timelessness of God being seen in His deliberate life....

- All the forgiveness of God was on display through His sinless life...

Look at the results of His sinless Life:

- He prevented a wedding reception in Cana of Galilee from self-destructing, by turning the water into wine...

- He revived a nobleman's son, who had been given a terminal diagnosis...

- He told a group of bankrupt fishermen where to fish, and the fishing business boomed...

- He set free a demoniac in the synagogue...

- He healed ten lepers who had been banished to the leper colony...

- He healed the fevered brow of Peter's mother-in-law...

- He healed a man who was in the final stages of cerebral palsy...

- He healed a man with a withered hand...

- He stopped a funeral procession, to heal a widow woman's dead son...

- He delivered a demon-possessed man who had terrorized his community...

- He raised up the dead daughter of Jairus...

- He stopped the flow of blood for a woman who had been hemorrhaging for twelve straight years...

- He quenched the hunger of a crowd of thousands, with the lunch of a young boy...

- He restored the sight of a man born blind, using dirt and saliva...

- He paid the IRS debt of the disciples, with money He predicted would be in the mouth of a fish...

- He reestablished the life of a woman who had been married five times, and was shacking up with her current lover...

- He restored the speech and hearing of a deaf mute, by spitting on his fingers, and placing them in the ears, and on the tongue of the deaf mute...

- He raised Lazarus from the dead by simply speaking the word...

- On the eve of His crucifixion, He reattached the ear of Malchus, after Simon Peter cut it off...

- He counseled a thief into the Kingdom of God, while he was hanging on a cross...

His Birth was no doubt GREAT but His sinless life was GREATER!

HIS SINLESS LIFE WAS GREAT BUT HIS SUBSTITUTIONARY DEATH WAS GREATER

Look at what His substitutionary death produced: Isaiah 53:5 (NKJV) "But He was wounded for our transgressions, He was\ bruised for our iniquities; The chastisement for our peace was upon Him, And by His stripes we are healed."

- He was bruised and brought healing...

- He was pierced and eased pain...

- He was persecuted and brought freedom...

- He was dead and brought life...

The ordeal of the cross seems to be a crushing defeat! The Son of God, hanged on a tree until dead! It appears that so much was lost at the cross. The Disciples and the Christ followers of the day certainly felt that way.

Jesus willingly gave up His earthly life on the cross, but look at what He didn't give up on the cross:

- His dignity... Luke 23:28 (KJV) "But Jesus turning unto them said, Daughters of Jerusalem, weep not for me, but weep for yourselves, and for your children." Luke 23:34 (KJV) "Then said Jesus, Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do..." He was a class act; He kept his dignity!

- His deity... Matthew 27:54 (KJV) "Now when the centurion, and they that were with him, watching Jesus, saw the earthquake, and those things that were done, they feared greatly, saying, Truly this was the Son of God." Not one time during His life, death, burial or resurrection did Jesus lay down His deity; not even for one minute.

- His destiny... 1 John 3:8 (KJV) "...For this purpose the Son of God was manifested, that he might destroy the works of the devil." Colossians 2:15 (KJV) "And having spoiled principalities and powers, he made a show of them openly, triumphing over them in it."

CONCLUSION:

The crucifixion of Jesus changed the symbol of the cross from an instrument of capital punishment, to a worldwide pictogram of freedom and victory.
