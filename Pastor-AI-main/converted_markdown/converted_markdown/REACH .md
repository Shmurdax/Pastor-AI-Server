# REACH IN
 
REACHING INWARD TOWARD ONE 
ANOTHER 
 

TEXT: 1 Corinthians 12:12-14, 26 “For as the 
body is one and has many members, but all the 
members of that one body, being many, are one 
body, so also is Christ. 13 For by one Spirit we 
were all baptized into one body—whether Jews 
or Greeks, whether slaves or free—and have 
all been made to drink into one Spirit. 14 For 
in fact the body is not one member but many 
… 26 And if one member suffers, all the 
members suffer with it; or if one member is 
honored, all the members rejoice with it.” 
 
(Talk about the love of God & the fact that Jesus 
died to build a church, that would facilitate 

doing life as the children of God, and reaching a 
world that does not yet know Him) 
 
What does that look like in real time in 2018? 
•  Servant leadership… 
•  Giving our time, talent and treasures… 
•  Small groups… 
•  Cultivating community… 
•  Protection from the enemy and world can be 
found at the church… (City of refuge) 
•  Provision of all we need can be found in 
Jesus and His church… 
 
Christianity is about responding to God’s 
outreach for a restored relationship with Him. 
 

1.  
God has reached out to us and saved us 
for Jesus’ sake! 

2.  
If we are to share the heart of Jesus and 
capture the vision of the Father we must 
reach in to others, and out to others! 
 
Today I want to talk about reaching in toward 
one another in the body of Christ! Make no 
mistake about it, the Christian community – is 
an essential part of our own personal experience 
with God. (People who say I don’t have to go to 
church to be a Christian – They don’t have to 
put fuel in their car to own it – A person who 
owns an aircraft doesn’t have to get it out of the 
hangar – You do not have to raise a child to be a 
biological parent) Everything is better when it is 
in alignment and properly utilized. 
 
Paul considered all worldly achievement 
secondary to a higher, loftier calling of: 
(Philippians 3) 

•  Knowing Christ… Reaching in requires us 
to know (Intimately) Christ and to spend the 
time and effort necessary, to get to know His 
people (The church) 
•  Experiencing the Power of His 
Resurrection… The resurrection of Jesus 
from the dead is the crescendo event of the 
demonstration of the power of God. The 
resurrection is not only an historical event of 
epic proportion, but speaks to us about that 
same power operating in us 24/7. As we 
reach in, we learn to operate and walk in this 
power and bring others along with us on this 
spiritual journey. The supernatural 
capability of the Holy Spirit is best utilized 
under the umbrella of the local church. 
•  Sharing in His sufferings… There must be 
some suffering (Resistance – Opposition) 
before there can be any reigning! “If we 
suffer with Him we shall also be glorified 

with Him.” There is no such thing as a 
perfect church! (Story of a man on an island 
– 3 huts) 
•  Being conformed to His death… In order to 
truly follow Christ and do life in a Christian 
community called a church, we must – Die 
to sin – Die to self – Die to the deeds of 
others! You can’t hurt a dead persons 
feelings! 
 
We must keep these things in mind as we reach 
out to one another in the Body of Christ.

Each individual part of our body is necessary to 
form the whole. And … If one part of your body 
is injured, it calls for the attention of the whole. 
 
Even something as small as our little toe, when 
stubbed against a table leg in the night, becomes 
a focal point of attention for the entire body.

engage, one must:

and serve others through ministry… 
(Vertical and horizontal service - Connect
